# CLINICAL REFERENCE: Diabetic Kidney Disease (DKD) Management

**Disclaimer:** *This document is a clinical reference for healthcare professionals at The Kidney Experts (TKE). It does not constitute patient-specific medical advice. Clinical judgment should be applied to all patient care decisions.*

---

## 1. SCREENING & DIAGNOSIS
Based on ADA 2024 and KDIGO 2024 Guidelines.

### Screening Schedule
*   **Type 2 Diabetes (T2DM):** Annual UACR + eGFR starting **at the time of diagnosis**.
*   **Type 1 Diabetes (T1DM):** Annual UACR + eGFR starting **5 years after diagnosis**.

### Diagnostic Confirmation
*   Confirm abnormal UACR with repeat testing.
*   **Requirement:** 2 out of 3 positive UACR tests within a 3-to-6-month period.
*   *Clinical Pearl:* Rule out transient causes of albuminuria before diagnosing DKD (e.g., heavy exercise, UTI, febrile illness, menstruation, heart failure exacerbation).

## 3. THE KDIGO PILLARS OF DKD MANAGEMENT
Based on KDIGO 2022/2024 Diabetes in CKD Guidelines. Management is multifactorial and prioritized as follows:

1.  **Foundation (Lifestyle):** Nutrition, weight management, exercise, smoking cessation.
2.  **First-Line Pharmacotherapy:** 
    *   SGLT2 inhibitors (Primary kidney/CV protection)
    *   Metformin (Glycemic control, if eGFR ≥30)
    *   RAAS Blockade (ACEi/ARB for BP and proteinuria)
    *   Statins (Lipid management)
3.  **Additional Risk-Based Therapy:**
    *   GLP-1 Receptor Agonists (Glycemic/weight/CV/kidney benefits)
    *   nsMRA (Kerendia (finerenone) for residual proteinuria)

## 4. MEDICATION STACKING FLOWCHART (DECISION TREE)
Use this step-wise algorithm to maximize kidney protection in DKD.

*   **STEP 1: RAAS Blockade**
    *   *Action:* Start ACEi or ARB (e.g., lisinopril (Prinivil) or losartan (Cozaar)).
    *   *Target:* Maximize to highest tolerated dose for proteinuria reduction.
    *   *Stop/Hold:* AKI, symptomatic hypotension, or intractable hyperkalemia (K+ >5.5 mEq/L).
*   **STEP 2: SGLT2 Inhibitor**
    *   *Action:* Add Farxiga (dapagliflozin) or Jardiance (empagliflozin) regardless of A1c.
    *   *Threshold:* Initiate if eGFR ≥20. Continue until dialysis initiation.
*   **STEP 3: GLP-1 Receptor Agonist**
    *   *Action:* Add Ozempic (semaglutide) or Trulicity (dulaglutide) if A1c remains above target, for weight loss, or for added CV/kidney benefit (per FLOW trial).
*   **STEP 4: Non-steroidal MRA (nsMRA)**
    *   *Action:* Add Kerendia (finerenone) if UACR remains ≥30 mg/g despite max tolerated RAASi + SGLT2i.
    *   *Threshold:* Initiate if eGFR ≥25 and Potassium ≤5.0 mEq/L.

## 5. MULTIFACTORIAL MANAGEMENT PROTOCOLS

### 5.1 Glycemic Control
*   **A1c Target:** Generally **<7.0%**. Individualize to <8.0% for patients with high hypoglycemia risk, frailty, or limited life expectancy.
*   **Monitoring in Advanced CKD:** A1c becomes unreliable in G4-G5 CKD due to shortened RBC lifespan and ESA use. 
    *   *Alternative:* Consider Continuous Glucose Monitoring (CGM) or fructosamine. [TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED: Preferred CGM ordering workflow].

**Glycemic Medication Dosing & eGFR Adjustments:**

| Drug Class | Brand (Generic) | Dosing & Titration | eGFR Adjustments & Rules |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Biguanide** | Glucophage (metformin) | Start 500mg daily, titrate to max 2000mg/day. | **eGFR ≥45:** Full dose.<br>**eGFR 30-44:** Max 1000mg/day. Do not initiate new.<br>**eGFR <30:** STOP therapy. |
| **SGLT2i** | Farxiga (dapagliflozin)<br>Jardiance (empagliflozin) | 10mg PO daily. No titration needed for CKD benefit. | **Initiate if eGFR ≥20.**<br>Continue until dialysis.<br>*Note: Warn patient of expected 3-5 point eGFR "dip" upon initiation.* |
| **GLP-1 RA** | Ozempic (semaglutide) | 0.25mg SQ weekly x4 wks, then 0.5mg. Max 2.0mg weekly. | No eGFR dose adjustment required. Monitor for volume depletion from GI side effects. |
| **GLP-1 RA** | Trulicity (dulaglutide) | 0.75mg SQ weekly x4 wks, then 1.5mg. Max 4.5mg weekly. | No eGFR dose adjustment required. |
| **Insulin** | Lantus (glargine), Humalog (lispro), etc. | Individualized. | **eGFR 10-50:** Reduce total daily dose by 25%.<br>**eGFR <10:** Reduce total daily dose by 50%. |

### 5.2 Blood Pressure & Proteinuria
*   **BP Target:** **<130/80 mmHg** (KDIGO 2024). Consider **<120 mmHg systolic** if tolerated without orthostasis (per SPRINT-CKD data).
*   **Proteinuria Target:** UACR **<30 mg/g** OR a **≥30% reduction** from baseline.
*   **First-Line:** ACEi or ARB. Maximize dose before adding secondary agents. Do not combine ACEi and ARB.
*   **Residual Proteinuria Management:**
    *   If UACR remains ≥30 mg/g on max RAASi + SGLT2i, initiate **Kerendia (finerenone)**.
    *   *Dosing:* 10mg daily if eGFR 25-59; 20mg daily if eGFR ≥60.
    *   *Monitoring:* Check Potassium 4 weeks after initiation or dose change. Hold if K+ >5.5 mEq/L.

### 5.3 Lipid Management
*   **Guideline:** KDIGO Lipid Guidelines.
*   **Indication:** Statin therapy is recommended for ALL CKD patients aged ≥50 years, regardless of LDL level, for primary/secondary CV prevention.
*   **Preferred Agents:**
    *   Lipitor (atorvastatin) 20mg daily.
    *   Crestor (rosuvastatin) 10mg daily.
*   **Dialysis Exception:** Do NOT initiate statins *after* a patient starts dialysis. If they are already on a statin prior to dialysis, continue it.

### 5.4 Weight Management
*   Obesity exacerbates hyperfiltration and proteinuria.
*   **First-line:** GLP-1 RA (e.g., Wegovy/Ozempic (semaglutide)) for dual glucose/weight benefit.
*   **Second-line/Escalation:** Consider dual GIP/GLP-1 RA **Zepbound/Mounjaro (tirzepatide)** for significant obesity + DKD. 
    *   *Titration:* Start 2.5mg SQ weekly x4 weeks, increase by 2.5mg increments every 4 weeks (Max 15mg weekly). No eGFR adjustment needed.

### 5.5 Smoking Cessation
*   Mandatory counseling at every visit. Smoking accelerates DKD progression to ESRD by 30%.
*   Offer pharmacotherapy (e.g., Chantix (varenicline), Nicotine Replacement) and behavioral support.

## 6. SAFETY, MONITORING, & CONTRAINDICATIONS

### Sick Day Rules (SADMANS)
Educate patients to **HOLD** the following medications during acute dehydrating illnesses (vomiting, severe diarrhea, fever, inability to drink fluids) to prevent AKI and euglycemic DKA:
*   **S**ulfonylureas
*   **A**CE inhibitors
*   **D**iuretics
*   **M**etformin
*   **A**RBs
*   **N**SAIDs
*   **S**GLT2 inhibitors

### Hyperkalemia Management
*   Do not prematurely discontinue RAASi or nsMRA for mild hyperkalemia (5.1 - 5.4 mEq/L).
*   Consider adding potassium binders to maintain kidney-protective therapies:
    *   **Lokelma (sodium zirconium cyclosilicate):** 10g TID x 48 hours, then 5-10g daily.
    *   **Veltassa (patiromer):** 8.4g daily, titrate up to 25.2g daily.

## 7. PATIENT EDUCATION TALKING POINTS
*   **The "Kidney Shield" Concept:** *"Medications like Farxiga/Jardiance and Lisinopril/Losartan are not just for blood sugar or blood pressure—they are 'kidney shields' that reduce pressure inside the kidney filters."*
*   **The eGFR Dip:** *"When you start an SGLT2 inhibitor or blood pressure pill, your kidney number (eGFR) might drop slightly on the first lab draw. This is expected and means the medication is working to take pressure off your kidneys."*
*   **Sick Day Protocol:** Provide the TKE Sick Day Handout. *"If you have a stomach bug and can't keep fluids down, stop your blood pressure, water pills, and diabetes pills until you are eating and drinking normally."*
