# CLINICAL REFERENCE: Proteinuria Treatment Escalation Algorithm

**Disclaimer:** *This document is a clinical reference for healthcare professionals at The Kidney Experts (TKE). It does not constitute patient-specific medical advice. Clinical judgment should be applied to all individual patient scenarios.*

**Guideline Basis:** 
* KDIGO 2024 Clinical Practice Guideline for the Evaluation and Management of CKD
* ADA 2024 Standards of Medical Care in Diabetes
* ACC/AHA 2022 Guidelines for the Management of Heart Failure

---

## 1. Diagnostic Confirmation & Baseline Targets

Before initiating the escalation pathway, confirm the diagnosis and establish baseline metrics.

*   **Confirm Proteinuria:** Urine Albumin-to-Creatinine Ratio (UACR) **>30 mg/g** on at least **2 out of 3** first-morning void samples over a 3-month period.
    *   *Rule out transient causes:* UTI, acute febrile illness, vigorous exercise, menstruation, or decompensated heart failure.
*   **KDIGO Treatment Targets:**
    *   **Ideal:** UACR **<30 mg/g**
    *   **Acceptable Clinical Target:** **≥30% reduction** in UACR from baseline.
*   **Non-Pharmacologic Foundation:**
    *   **Sodium restriction:** <2 g/day (<90 mmol/day).
    *   **Protein intake:** 0.8 g/kg/day (avoid high-protein diets; consider plant-based protein sources).

## 2. Step-by-Step Decision Tree: Proteinuria Escalation

*Evaluate UACR every 3 months. If the target (UACR <30 mg/g or ≥30% reduction) is NOT met, proceed to the next step.*

### STEP 1: Maximize RAAS Blockade
**Action:** Initiate and titrate ACE inhibitor (ACEi) or Angiotensin Receptor Blocker (ARB) to the **maximum tolerated dose**.
*   **Check:** Basic Metabolic Panel (BMP) for Creatinine and Potassium (K+) at **1 to 2 weeks** post-initiation or dose increase. [TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED: Standardize lab order workflow for 1-2 week follow-up].
*   **DECISION POINT: Creatinine Rise**
    *   **YES (Rise ≤30% from baseline):** Acceptable hemodynamic effect. Continue therapy.
    *   **YES (Rise >30% from baseline):** **STOP/REDUCE** dose. Investigate for volume depletion, NSAID use, or bilateral renal artery stenosis.
    *   **NO (Stable):** Continue and titrate up if not at max dose.
*   **DECISION POINT: Hyperkalemia (K+ >5.5 mEq/L)**
    *   **YES:** **DO NOT STOP RAASi.** Add a potassium binder: Veltassa (patiromer) 8.4g daily OR Lokelma (sodium zirconium cyclosilicate) 10g TID x 48hrs, then 5-10g daily. Counsel on low K+ diet.
    *   **NO:** Continue therapy.
*   **Recheck UACR at 3 months.** Target met?
    *   **YES:** Maintain therapy. Monitor q3-6 months.
    *   **NO:** Proceed to Step 2.

### STEP 2: Add SGLT2 Inhibitor
**Action:** Add Farxiga (dapagliflozin) 10 mg daily OR Jardiance (empagliflozin) 10 mg daily.
*   **Condition:** eGFR must be **≥20 mL/min/1.73m²** at initiation. (If eGFR drops below 20 *after* initiation, continue therapy until dialysis/transplant).
*   **DECISION POINT: eGFR Dip**
    *   *Expected:* An acute eGFR dip of 3-5 mL/min/1.73m² is a normal hemodynamic response.
    *   **DO NOT STOP** SGLT2i for this expected dip.
*   **Recheck UACR at 3 months.** Target met?
    *   **YES:** Maintain therapy. Monitor q3-6 months.
    *   **NO:** Proceed to Step 3.

### STEP 3: Add Non-Steroidal MRA (nsMRA)
**Action:** Add Kerendia (finerenone).
*   **Conditions for Initiation:** 
    *   Patient has Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (T2DM).
    *   UACR remains **≥30 mg/g**.
    *   Serum K+ is **<5.0 mEq/L**.
    *   eGFR is **≥25 mL/min/1.73m²**.
*   **Dosing:** 10 mg daily if eGFR 25-59; 20 mg daily if eGFR ≥60.
*   **Check:** K+ at 4 weeks. If K+ >5.5, hold Kerendia, manage K+, and restart at 10 mg when K+ ≤5.0.
*   **Recheck UACR at 3 months.** Target met?
    *   **YES:** Maintain therapy. Monitor q3-6 months.
    *   **NO:** Proceed to Step 4.

### STEP 4: Consider Disease-Specific Advanced Therapies
**Action:** Evaluate underlying pathology. Is there suspected or biopsy-proven IgA Nephropathy (IgAN)?
*   **YES (IgAN confirmed):** Consider switching from ACEi/ARB to **Filspari (sparsentan)**.
    *   *CRITICAL:* Filspari is a dual endothelin/angiotensin receptor antagonist (DEARA). **You MUST discontinue current ACEi/ARB before starting Filspari.**
*   **NO / Still Refractory:** Reassess diagnosis. 
    *   Consider medication non-adherence.
    *   Consider high sodium intake (check 24-hour urine sodium).
    *   Consider kidney biopsy if not previously done to rule out secondary glomerulonephritis. [TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED: Criteria for internal vs. external biopsy referral].

## 4. ⚠️ Safety Concerns, Contraindications & Interactions

*   **ACEi/ARB:** 
    *   *Contraindications:* Pregnancy (teratogenic), history of angioedema, bilateral renal artery stenosis.
    *   *Safety:* Never combine an ACEi with an ARB.
*   **SGLT2i (Farxiga/Jardiance):**
    *   *Contraindications:* Type 1 Diabetes (high risk of euglycemic DKA), prior history of DKA, polycystic kidney disease (PKD - excluded from trials).
    *   *Safety:* Hold 3-4 days prior to scheduled surgeries or during acute severe illness to prevent DKA. Warn patients about genital mycotic infections.
*   **nsMRA (Kerendia):**
    *   *Contraindications:* Concomitant use with strong CYP3A4 inhibitors (e.g., ketoconazole, clarithromycin), adrenal insufficiency.
*   **DEARA (Filspari):**
    *   *Contraindications:* Pregnancy (requires REMS program enrollment), concomitant use with ARBs, ERAs, or aliskiren.
    *   *Safety:* Hepatotoxicity risk. Requires baseline and ongoing AST/ALT and bilirubin monitoring.

## 5. Patient Education Talking Points

*   **"The Kidney Protective Dip":** *"When we start these medications (like lisinopril or Farxiga), your kidney function number (eGFR) might drop slightly on your next lab test. This is expected and means the drug is working to take pressure off your kidney filters. It protects your kidneys in the long run."*
*   **Sick Day Rules (SADMANS):** *"If you have a severe stomach bug, vomiting, or diarrhea, stop taking your blood pressure pills, water pills, and Farxiga/Jardiance until you are eating and drinking normally again. This prevents dehydration and kidney injury."*
*   **Dietary Sodium:** *"These medications will not work effectively to lower your urine protein if you are eating too much salt. Keep sodium under 2,000 mg per day."*
*   **Hygiene (SGLT2i):** *"Farxiga/Jardiance puts extra sugar in your urine. To prevent yeast infections, practice good daily hygiene and drink plenty of water."*
