# CLINICAL REFERENCE: Heart Failure GDMT Optimization in CKD Patients

**Primary Guidelines Referenced:** 
* 2022 AHA/ACC/HFSA Guideline for the Management of Heart Failure
* 2024 KDIGO Clinical Practice Guideline for the Evaluation and Management of CKD
* 2024 ADA Standards of Care in Diabetes

---

## 1. HEART FAILURE CLASSIFICATION & TREATMENT APPROACH

Heart failure classification dictates the Guideline-Directed Medical Therapy (GDMT) approach. In CKD patients, optimizing GDMT reduces both cardiovascular mortality and CKD progression.

*   **HFrEF (Heart Failure with Reduced Ejection Fraction, EF ≤40%):** 
    *   **Target:** ALL 4 pillars of GDMT are strongly recommended (Class 1).
*   **HFmrEF (Heart Failure with Mildly Reduced Ejection Fraction, EF 41-49%):** 
    *   **Target:** SGLT2i (Class 1). Consider ARNI/ACEi/ARB, Beta-blockers, and MRAs (Class 2b).
*   **HFpEF (Heart Failure with Preserved Ejection Fraction, EF ≥50%):** 
    *   **Target:** SGLT2i (Class 1). Diuretics for congestion management. Aggressive management of comorbidities (hypertension, AFib, CKD).

---

## 2. THE FOUR PILLARS OF GDMT FOR HFrEF

**Clinical Strategy:** Start ALL four pillars at low doses simultaneously (or within 1-2 weeks of each other) rather than maximizing one drug before starting the next. Rapid initiation improves outcomes.

### PILLAR 1: ARNI or ACEi/ARB
**First-line:** Entresto (sacubitril/valsartan) is preferred over ACEi/ARB.
*   **Dosing:** Start 24/26 mg BID → titrate to 49/51 mg BID → target 97/103 mg BID.
*   **CKD Adjustments:** 
    *   eGFR ≥30 mL/min/1.73m²: Start 49/51 mg BID.
    *   eGFR <30 mL/min/1.73m²: Start 24/26 mg BID.
*   **Alternative:** If Entresto is not tolerated or cost-prohibitive, use ACEi or ARB (e.g., Vasotec (enalapril) or Diovan (valsartan)).
*   🚨 **SAFETY & CONTRAINDICATIONS:**
    *   **36-Hour Washout:** MUST have a 36-hour washout period when switching from an ACEi to Entresto to prevent life-threatening angioedema. (No washout needed if switching from an ARB).
    *   **Hyperkalemia:** If K+ >5.5 mEq/L, hold therapy, manage K+ (consider potassium binders), and restart when K+ <5.0 mEq/L.

### PILLAR 2: Beta-Blockers
Only three beta-blockers have proven mortality benefits in HFrEF.
*   **Coreg (carvedilol):** Start 3.125 mg BID → 6.25 mg BID → 12.5 mg BID → target 25 mg BID (Target 50 mg BID if weight >85 kg).
*   **Toprol-XL (metoprolol succinate):** Start 12.5–25 mg daily → 50 mg → 100 mg → target 200 mg daily.
*   **Zebeta (bisoprolol):** Start 1.25 mg daily → 2.5 mg → 5 mg → target 10 mg daily.
*   **Titration Rules:** Uptitrate every 2 weeks IF resting Heart Rate (HR) >60 bpm and Systolic Blood Pressure (SBP) >100 mmHg.
*   🚨 **SAFETY:** Do not initiate or uptitrate during acute decompensated HF (volume overload requiring IV diuretics).

### PILLAR 3: Mineralocorticoid Receptor Antagonists (MRA)
*   **Aldactone (spironolactone):** Start 12.5–25 mg daily. Target 25–50 mg daily.
*   **Inspra (eplerenone):** Start 25 mg daily. Target 50 mg daily.
*   **Kerendia (finerenone):** Non-steroidal MRA indicated specifically for patients with T2DM + CKD (addresses both HF risk and kidney progression). Target UACR <30 mg/g.
*   🚨 **SAFETY & CKD THRESHOLDS:**
    *   Do not start if baseline K+ >5.0 mEq/L or eGFR <25 mL/min/1.73m².
    *   **Monitoring:** Check BMP (specifically K+ and SCr) at 1 week and 4 weeks after initiation or dose change.

### PILLAR 4: SGLT2 Inhibitors
Highly effective across the entire EF spectrum (proven in DAPA-HF, EMPEROR-Reduced, DELIVER, EMPEROR-Preserved).
*   **Farxiga (dapagliflozin):** 10 mg daily.
*   **Jardiance (empagliflozin):** 10 mg daily.
*   **CKD Adjustments:** Can be initiated at any eGFR ≥20 mL/min/1.73m². No dose titration required.
*   🚨 **SAFETY:** Warn patients about genital mycotic infections. Hold 3-4 days prior to surgery or during acute severe illness to prevent euglycemic diabetic ketoacidosis (euDKA).

## 4. ADDITIONAL THERAPIES & DIURETICS

### Adjunctive GDMT
*   **Verquvo (vericiguat):** Consider for patients with recent HF hospitalization or need for outpatient IV diuretics despite maximal GDMT.
*   **Corlanor (ivabradine):** Consider if HR ≥70 bpm on maximally tolerated beta-blocker, AND patient is in normal sinus rhythm.
*   **BiDil (hydralazine/isosorbide dinitrate):** Recommended for African American patients with HFrEF (NYHA class III-IV) on optimal GDMT, OR for any patient who cannot tolerate ARNI/ACEi/ARB due to severe renal impairment or hyperkalemia.

### Diuretics (Symptom Management ONLY)
*Diuretics do not reduce mortality and are not considered GDMT. Use lowest effective dose to maintain euvolemia.*
*   **Loop Diuretics:** Lasix (furosemide), Bumex (bumetanide), Demadex (torsemide).
*   **CKD Pearl:** Demadex (torsemide) or Bumex (bumetanide) are often preferred in advanced CKD due to more predictable, higher oral bioavailability compared to Lasix (furosemide).
*   **Dosing:** Adjust based on daily weights and clinical congestion, NOT by a static protocol.

## 5. CKD-SPECIFIC PITFALLS & DECISION TREES

### Cardiorenal Syndrome & SCr Bumps
*   **The Scenario:** SCr rises after initiating ARNI/SGLT2i or aggressive diuresis.
*   **Decision Tree:**
    *   **If SCr rise is <30% from baseline:** **CONTINUE THERAPY.** This is an expected hemodynamic effect, not true tubular injury.
    *   **If SCr rise is >30% from baseline:** **EVALUATE.** Check volume status. If over-diuresed, reduce loop diuretic. If euvolemic, consider reducing ARNI dose. Do NOT stop SGLT2i for an initial SCr bump.
    *   **Cardiorenal Syndrome:** Often, accepting a slightly higher, stable creatinine is necessary to achieve optimal cardiac decongestion and long-term survival.

### Hyperkalemia Management
*   **The Scenario:** K+ rises to 5.1 - 5.9 mEq/L on ARNI + MRA.
*   **Decision Tree:**
    *   **Do NOT immediately stop GDMT.**
    *   **Step 1:** Review diet and stop potassium supplements/NSAIDs.
    *   **Step 2:** Add a potassium binder: Lokelma (sodium zirconium cyclosilicate) 5-10g daily OR Veltassa (patiromer) 8.4g daily.
    *   **Step 3:** If K+ remains >5.5 mEq/L despite binders, reduce MRA dose by half. Stop MRA if K+ >6.0 mEq/L.

### Hypotension
*   **The Scenario:** SBP <90 mmHg or symptomatic orthostasis.
*   **Decision Tree:**
    *   **Step 1:** Are they volume depleted? If yes, reduce loop diuretic (keep GDMT).
    *   **Step 2:** Stagger medication dosing (e.g., take Beta-blocker in AM, ARNI in PM).
    *   **Step 3:** Discontinue non-GDMT blood pressure meds (e.g., amlodipine, doxazosin).
    *   **Step 4:** If still hypotensive, down-titrate ARNI or Beta-blocker.

## 6. PATIENT EDUCATION TALKING POINTS

*   **The "Four Pillars":** *"We are putting you on four specific medications. They work together like four legs of a chair to support your heart and protect your kidneys."*
*   **Expected Changes:** *"Your kidney numbers might look slightly worse on your first lab draw, and your blood pressure will be lower. This is expected and shows the medications are taking pressure off your organs."*
*   **Daily Weights:** *"Weigh yourself every morning after peeing. Call us if you gain 3 lbs in one day or 5 lbs in a week."*
*   **Sick Day Rules:** *"If you have severe vomiting or diarrhea, stop your water pill (diuretic), your blood pressure pill (ARNI), and your sugar pill (SGLT2i) until you can eat and drink normally again."*
