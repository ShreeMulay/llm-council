### Clinical Lab Targets
*   **Corrected Calcium:** Maintain within normal laboratory range (typically 8.4 – 10.2 mg/dL). Avoid hypercalcemia.
*   **Phosphorus:** 
    *   *CKD 3-5 (Non-Dialysis):* Maintain within normal range (2.5 – 4.5 mg/dL).
    *   *CKD 5D (Dialysis):* Lower toward normal range; avoid hyperphosphatemia.
*   **Intact PTH:**
    *   *CKD 3-5 (Non-Dialysis):* Target is not strictly defined; evaluate for progressive rising trends.
    *   *CKD 5D (Dialysis):* Maintain at **2 to 9 times the upper limit of normal** (approx. 130 – 600 pg/mL).
*   **25(OH) Vitamin D:** Target **>30 ng/mL**.

## 3. Step-by-Step Clinical Decision Algorithm

### STEP 1: Nutritional Vitamin D Repletion
**Trigger:** 25(OH)D level < 30 ng/mL.
*   **Initiate:** Drisdol (ergocalciferol) or generic cholecalciferol **50,000 IU weekly for 8–12 weeks**.
*   **Maintenance:** Transition to 50,000 IU monthly or 1,000–2,000 IU daily.
*   **Follow-up:** Recheck 25(OH)D, Calcium, and PTH in 3 months.
*   **Decision Point:** If PTH remains progressively elevated despite replete 25(OH)D (>30 ng/mL), **proceed to Step 2**.

### STEP 2: Phosphorus Management
**Trigger:** Elevated serum phosphorus despite dietary restriction.
*   **Dietary Counseling:** Restrict dietary phosphorus. Focus on avoiding inorganic phosphate additives (processed foods, dark colas, fast food). *[TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED: Insert local workflow for TKE renal dietitian referral].*
*   **Phosphate Binders:** Initiate based on calcium levels, pill burden, and insurance coverage. **All binders must be taken WITH meals.**
    *   **Renvela (sevelamer carbonate):** 800 mg TID with meals. Titrate by 800 mg/meal q2-4 weeks. *Pros: No calcium load, lowers LDL.*
    *   **Velphoro (sucroferric oxyhydroxide):** 500 mg TID with meals (chewable). *Pros: Lower pill burden, provides some iron absorption.*
    *   **Auryxia (ferric citrate):** 2 tablets (210 mg elemental iron each) TID with meals. *Pros: Dual action (binds phosphorus + treats iron deficiency anemia).*
    *   **PhosLo (calcium acetate):** 667 mg (2 capsules) TID with meals. *Pros: Inexpensive. Cons: High risk of calcium load. Limit use if corrected Calcium > 9.5 mg/dL or if PTH is low.*
*   **Decision Point:** If PTH is progressively rising despite normalized 25(OH)D and controlled phosphorus, **proceed to Step 3**.

### STEP 3: Secondary Hyperparathyroidism (SHPT) Management
**Trigger:** Progressively rising PTH despite Steps 1 & 2.

#### For CKD 3-5 (Non-Dialysis)
*   **Initiate Active Vitamin D Analogs:**
    *   **Rocaltrol (calcitriol):** 0.25 mcg daily or every other day.
    *   **Zemplar (paricalcitol):** 1–2 mcg daily.
    *   **Hectorol (doxercalciferol):** 1 mcg daily.
*   **Monitoring:** Check Ca and Phos 2-4 weeks after initiation. 
*   **Stop/Hold:** Discontinue if hypercalcemia (Ca > 10.2 mg/dL) or severe hyperphosphatemia develops.

#### For CKD 5D (Dialysis)
*   **Initiate Calcimimetics:**
    *   **Sensipar (cinacalcet):** Start 30 mg daily with food. Titrate by 30 mg increments every 2-4 weeks. Max dose: 180 mg/day. Target PTH 2-9x upper normal.
    *   **Parsabiv (etelcalcetide):** 5 mg IV three times weekly at the end of hemodialysis.
*   **Monitoring:** Check serum calcium within 1 week of initiation or dose titration.
*   **Stop/Hold:** Do NOT initiate if corrected Calcium < 8.4 mg/dL.

### STEP 4: Refractory Hyperparathyroidism
**Trigger:** Intact PTH > 800 pg/mL persistently despite maximal medical therapy (Steps 1-3), especially if accompanied by intractable hypercalcemia and/or hyperphosphatemia.
*   **Action:** Refer for surgical parathyroidectomy.

## 4. Bone Density & Osteoporosis Management in CKD

Management of bone density in CKD requires careful consideration of eGFR to avoid adynamic bone disease and toxicity.

*   **Bisphosphonates (e.g., Fosamax (alendronate), Reclast (zoledronic acid)):**
    *   **eGFR > 30:** Generally safe to use per standard osteoporosis guidelines.
    *   **eGFR < 30:** **AVOID.** High risk of adynamic bone disease and potential nephrotoxicity (especially with IV formulations).
*   **RANKL Inhibitors:**
    *   **Prolia (denosumab):** 60 mg SQ every 6 months. Can be used in eGFR < 30 and dialysis, **BUT** carries severe risks.
    *   🚨 **SEVERE HYPOCALCEMIA RISK:** Patients with advanced CKD/ESRD are at extreme risk for life-threatening hypocalcemia. **Must** ensure 25(OH)D is replete and patient is on adequate calcium supplementation *before* administration.
    *   🚨 **REBOUND FRACTURE RISK:** If Prolia is stopped or delayed, there is a severe risk of rebound vertebral fractures. **Do not discontinue abruptly.** If stopping, transition to another antiresorptive agent (which is difficult in eGFR <30).

## 5. Safety Flags & Contraindications 🚨

*   **Hypercalcemia + Calcium Binders:** Do NOT use PhosLo (calcium acetate) or Tums (calcium carbonate) if corrected calcium is > 9.5 mg/dL or if the patient has adynamic bone disease (low PTH).
*   **Active Vitamin D + Hyperphosphatemia:** Active Vitamin D (Rocaltrol, Zemplar) increases intestinal absorption of BOTH calcium and phosphorus. Use with extreme caution if phosphorus is uncontrolled.
*   **Sensipar (cinacalcet) + Hypocalcemia:** Sensipar lowers serum calcium. Do not initiate if corrected calcium is < 8.4 mg/dL. Monitor for signs of hypocalcemia (paresthesias, Chvostek/Trousseau signs, QT prolongation).
*   **Drug Interactions:** Sensipar (cinacalcet) is a strong CYP2D6 inhibitor. Adjust doses of concomitant CYP2D6 substrates (e.g., Coreg (carvedilol), metoprolol, certain SSRIs/tricyclics).

## 6. Patient Education Talking Points

*   **Taking Binders Correctly:** *"Phosphate binders act like a sponge. They only work if the sponge is in your stomach at the exact same time as the food. Take your binders with the first bite of your meal or snack."*
*   **Hidden Phosphorus:** *"Avoid foods with ingredients containing 'PHOS' (e.g., phosphoric acid, hexametaphosphate). These are common in dark sodas, processed meats, and fast food, and are absorbed 100% by your body."*
*   **Sensipar (cinacalcet) Compliance:** *"This medication can cause nausea. Taking it with your largest meal of the day can significantly reduce stomach upset."*
*   **Vitamin D:** *"Even if you spend time in the sun, kidney disease prevents your body from activating Vitamin D properly. This is why you need the specific prescription forms we provide."*
