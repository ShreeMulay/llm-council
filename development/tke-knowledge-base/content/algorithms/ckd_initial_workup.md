# Initial CKD Workup and Staging
**Clinical Reference Guide for The Kidney Experts (TKE)**
*Based on KDIGO 2024 CKD Guidelines, KDIGO 2021 BP Guidelines, and ADA 2024 Standards of Care.*

---

## 1. When to Suspect CKD
CKD is often asymptomatic in early stages. Screening and high clinical suspicion are required for at-risk populations.

### High-Risk Populations (Screen Annually)
*   **Comorbidities:** Diabetes mellitus (Type 1 >5 years, Type 2 at diagnosis), Hypertension, Cardiovascular disease, Heart failure, Obesity (BMI >30).
*   **History:** Prior Acute Kidney Injury (AKI), recurrent nephrolithiasis, recurrent UTIs, childhood kidney disease.
*   **Demographics/Genetics:** Family history of CKD or End-Stage Kidney Disease (ESKD), systemic autoimmune diseases (SLE, RA).
*   **Exposures:** Chronic NSAID use, lithium, calcineurin inhibitors, history of nephrotoxic chemotherapy.

### Incidental Findings Requiring Investigation
*   Elevated serum creatinine on routine basic metabolic panel (BMP).
*   Incidental proteinuria or hematuria on routine urinalysis.
*   Structural abnormalities on imaging (e.g., small echogenic kidneys, polycystic appearance, hydronephrosis).

---

## 2. Confirmation & Diagnostic Criteria
**Definition of CKD:** Abnormalities of kidney structure or function, present for **>3 months**, with implications for health.

### The 3-Month Rule
A single abnormal lab value does NOT diagnose CKD. 
*   **Action:** Repeat eGFR and Urine Albumin-to-Creatinine Ratio (UACR) at **3 months** to confirm chronicity.
*   *Exception:* If the patient has known structural kidney disease (e.g., polycystic kidneys on ultrasound) or a documented history of kidney disease >3 months prior, immediate diagnosis can be made.

### Diagnostic Thresholds (Must persist >3 months)
*   **eGFR:** <60 mL/min/1.73 m²
*   **UACR:** ≥30 mg/g
*   **Urine Sediment:** Persistent hematuria (dysmorphic RBCs, RBC casts) or WBC casts.
*   **Structural:** Abnormal imaging or histology.

## 3. eGFR Equations & Cystatin C Usage
TKE utilizes the **CKD-EPI 2021 (race-free)** equation for standard eGFR reporting. 

### When to Order Cystatin C (eGFRcys or eGFRcr-cys)
Cystatin C is less affected by muscle mass and diet than creatinine. Order Cystatin C in the following scenarios:
1.  **Borderline eGFR (45–59 mL/min/1.73 m²):** To confirm CKD diagnosis in patients *without* albuminuria or other markers of kidney damage. If eGFRcys is >60, the patient does *not* have CKD.
2.  **Extremes of Muscle Mass:** Amputees, paraplegia/quadriplegia, severe malnutrition, bodybuilders, or extreme obesity.
3.  **Strict Medication Dosing:** When highly accurate GFR is required for narrow therapeutic index drugs (e.g., chemotherapy).
4.  **Vegetarian/Vegan Diets:** Where low dietary creatine intake artificially lowers serum creatinine.

## 5. Etiology Workup
Determine the underlying cause to guide targeted therapy. *[TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED: Standardized EMR order sets for "Initial CKD Workup"]*

### Core Initial Labs (All Patients)
*   **Blood:** Comprehensive Metabolic Panel (CMP), Complete Blood Count (CBC), Lipid Panel, Hemoglobin A1c.
*   **Urine:** Urinalysis with microscopy, spot UACR.
*   **Imaging:** Renal Ultrasound (assess size, echogenicity, rule out obstruction/cysts).

### Targeted Workup (Based on Clinical Suspicion)
*   **Suspected Glomerulonephritis (GN):** ANA, ANCA, Anti-GBM, C3/C4 complements, Hepatitis B/C, HIV, Syphilis.
*   **Age >40 with unexplained CKD/Proteinuria:** Serum Protein Electrophoresis (SPEP), Urine Protein Electrophoresis (UPEP), Serum Free Light Chains (SFLC) to rule out Multiple Myeloma/Monoclonal Gammopathy of Renal Significance (MGRS).
*   **Unexplained Hematuria:** Urine cytology, CT Urogram, Urology referral for cystoscopy.
*   **Family History of Cystic Disease:** Genetic testing panel (e.g., Natera Renasight).

## 6. Nephrology Referral Criteria
Primary care should refer to TKE when patients meet any of the following criteria:
*   **eGFR <30 mL/min/1.73 m²** (Stage G4 or G5).
*   **UACR >300 mg/g** (Stage A3).
*   **Rapid eGFR Decline:** Sustained drop of >5 mL/min/1.73 m² in one year.
*   **Unexplained Hematuria:** Especially with dysmorphic RBCs or RBC casts (suspected GN).
*   **Refractory Hypertension:** BP >130/80 despite ≥3 antihypertensives (including a diuretic).
*   **Electrolyte Abnormalities:** Refractory hyperkalemia, hyponatremia, or severe metabolic acidosis.
*   **Suspected Genetic Kidney Disease:** e.g., Autosomal Dominant Polycystic Kidney Disease (ADPKD).

## 7. Initial Management Plan by Stage
**Primary Goals:** Delay progression, minimize cardiovascular risk, and manage complications.
**Targets:** BP <120 mm Hg systolic (KDIGO 2021); UACR reduction by >30% (ideally <30 mg/g).

### Step 1: Hemodynamic & Proteinuria Management (ACEi/ARB)
*   **Indication:** First-line for HTN in CKD with UACR ≥30 mg/g (DM) or UACR ≥300 mg/g (non-DM).
*   **Agents:** Lisinopril (Prinivil/Zestril) 10-40 mg daily OR Losartan (Cozaar) 50-100 mg daily.
*   **Titration:** Titrate to maximum tolerated dose. 
*   **Monitoring:** Check BMP 2-4 weeks after initiation/titration. 
*   **Decision Tree:** 
    *   *Stop/Hold if:* Serum creatinine rises >30% from baseline, or refractory hyperkalemia (K+ >5.5 mEq/L) occurs.

### Step 2: SGLT2 Inhibitors
*   **Indication:** CKD with eGFR ≥20 mL/min/1.73 m² and UACR ≥200 mg/g (or heart failure/T2D regardless of UACR).
*   **Agents:** Farxiga (dapagliflozin) 10 mg PO daily OR Jardiance (empagliflozin) 10 mg PO daily.
*   **Dosing Note:** No titration needed. Do not initiate if eGFR <20, but *continue* if eGFR drops below 20 until dialysis initiation.
*   **Safety Flag:** 🛑 Withhold during acute illness, fasting, or surgery to prevent euglycemic diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA).

### Step 3: Non-Steroidal Mineralocorticoid Receptor Antagonists (nsMRA)
*   **Indication:** T2D with eGFR ≥25 mL/min/1.73 m², normal potassium, and residual UACR ≥30 mg/g despite max tolerated ACEi/ARB.
*   **Agent:** Kerendia (finerenone).
*   **Dosing:** 
    *   eGFR ≥60: Start 20 mg PO daily.
    *   eGFR 25-59: Start 10 mg PO daily; titrate to 20 mg after 4 weeks if K+ ≤4.8 mEq/L.
*   **Monitoring:** Check K+ at 4 weeks. Hold if K+ >5.5 mEq/L.

### Step 4: GLP-1 Receptor Agonists
*   **Indication:** T2D and CKD (especially with obesity) who have not achieved glycemic targets or need further CV/kidney protection.
*   **Agents:** Ozempic (semaglutide) 0.25-2.0 mg SQ weekly, Trulicity (dulaglutide) 0.75-4.5 mg SQ weekly.
*   **Dosing Note:** No specific eGFR cutoffs for initiation, but use caution in severe GI distress to avoid AKI from volume depletion.

### Step 5: Cardiovascular Risk Reduction
*   **Statins:** Initiate moderate-to-high intensity statin—e.g., Lipitor (atorvastatin) 20-40 mg or Crestor (rosuvastatin) 10-20 mg—in ALL CKD patients aged ≥50 years, regardless of LDL level.

## 10. Patient Education Talking Points
*   **What is CKD?** "Your kidneys have sustained some damage and aren't filtering waste as well as they should. Our goal is to protect the function you have left."
*   **Dietary Changes:** 
    *   Limit sodium to <2,000 mg/day.
    *   Adopt a plant-dominant, whole-food diet (e.g., DASH or Mediterranean).
    *   *Do not* restrict potassium or phosphorus unless specifically instructed based on lab results.
*   **Medication Safety (The "Triple Whammy"):** 
    *   🛑 **AVOID NSAIDs:** Advil (ibuprofen), Aleve (naproxen), Motrin, Celebrex (celecoxib). Use Tylenol (acetaminophen) for pain.
    *   Combining NSAIDs with blood pressure meds (ACEi/ARB) and water pills (diuretics) can cause sudden and severe kidney failure.
*   **Sick Day Rules:** If experiencing severe vomiting or diarrhea, temporarily hold blood pressure meds, water pills, and SGLT2 inhibitors (Farxiga/Jardiance) to prevent dehydration and AKI. Call the clinic for guidance. *[TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED: Standardized sick day handout]*
