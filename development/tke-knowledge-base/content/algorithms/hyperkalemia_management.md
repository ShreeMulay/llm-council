# Clinical Decision Algorithm: Hyperkalemia Management in CKD

**Primary Reference Guidelines:** 
* KDIGO 2024 Clinical Practice Guideline for the Evaluation and Management of CKD
* KDIGO 2022 Clinical Practice Guideline for Diabetes Management in CKD
* ACC/AHA/HFSA 2022 Guideline for the Management of Heart Failure

---

## 1. ACUTE Management (K+ ≥6.0 mEq/L or ECG Changes)
**Goal:** Immediate membrane stabilization, intracellular potassium shifting, and total body potassium elimination. 
**Setting:** Emergency Department or direct admission. [TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED: Clinic-to-ED transfer workflow for critical lab values].

### Step 1: Cardiac Monitoring & Evaluation
* **STAT ECG:** Evaluate for peaked T waves, PR prolongation, loss of P waves, QRS widening, or sine wave pattern.
* **Confirm Lab:** Rule out pseudohyperkalemia (hemolysis, prolonged tourniquet time, severe leukocytosis/thrombocytosis), but *do not delay treatment if ECG changes are present*.

### Step 2: Membrane Stabilization (If ECG changes present)
* **Calcium gluconate 10%:** 10 mL IV push over 2-3 minutes.
    * *Onset:* 1-3 minutes. *Duration:* 30-60 minutes.
    * *Repeat:* May repeat in 5-10 minutes if ECG changes persist.
    * ⚠️ **SAFETY WARNING:** Use with extreme caution in patients on Lanoxin (digoxin); risk of precipitating digoxin toxicity. Consider slow infusion (over 20-30 mins) if required.

### Step 3: Intracellular Shifting
* **Insulin + Glucose:** Humulin R / Novolin R (regular insulin) 10 units IV push + D50W 25g (1 ampule) IV push.
    * *CKD Adjustment:* For eGFR <30 mL/min/1.73m² or ESKD, use **5 units** of regular insulin to reduce the risk of prolonged hypoglycemia.
    * *Note:* Withhold D50W if pre-treatment blood glucose is >250 mg/dL.
    * *Monitoring:* Capillary blood glucose hourly x 4-6 hours.
* **Proventil / Ventolin (albuterol):** 10-20 mg nebulized over 10 minutes.
    * *Note:* Additive effect with insulin. Avoid in active ischemia/tachyarrhythmias.
* **Sodium Bicarbonate 8.4%:** 50 mEq (1 ampule) IV push.
    * *Note:* Limited utility as monotherapy for acute shifts. Reserve for patients with concurrent severe metabolic acidosis (pH < 7.2).

### Step 4: Potassium Elimination
* **Kayexalate / Kalexate (sodium polystyrene sulfonate - SPS):** 15-30g PO or PR.
    * *Onset:* 2-24 hours (slower onset, GI binding).
    * ⚠️ **CONTRAINDICATION:** Do not use in patients with bowel obstruction, ileus, or recent bowel surgery due to the risk of fatal intestinal necrosis.
* **Loop Diuretics:** Lasix (furosemide) 40-80 mg IV (if patient makes urine and is not volume depleted).
* **Emergent Hemodialysis:** Consider immediately if hyperkalemia is refractory to medical therapy, accompanied by severe oliguria/anuria, or if life-threatening arrhythmias occur.

## 2. SUBACUTE Management (K+ 5.5–5.9 mEq/L, No ECG Changes)
**Goal:** Safely lower potassium to <5.0 mEq/L within 48-72 hours while avoiding the premature discontinuation of nephroprotective medications.

### Step 1: Identify and Remove Reversible Causes
* **Dietary Review:** Screen for high-potassium foods (tomatoes, potatoes, bananas, citrus, melons) and hidden sources (salt substitutes containing potassium chloride).
* **Medication Review:** Hold non-essential potassium-elevating drugs:
    * NSAIDs (e.g., Motrin (ibuprofen), Celebrex (celecoxib))
    * Bactrim (trimethoprim/sulfamethoxazole)
    * Heparin
    * Potassium supplements

### Step 2: RAAS Blockade Decision
* **KDIGO 2024 Guideline:** *Do not routinely discontinue* RAAS inhibitors (ACEi/ARB/ARNI) or MRAs for hyperkalemia without first attempting dietary modification and potassium binders.
* **Action:** Temporarily hold RAAS blockade *only* if K+ >5.5 mEq/L despite initial interventions, or if the patient is acutely ill/AKI.

### Step 3: Initiate Oral Potassium Binders
Choose ONE of the following novel potassium binders:

* **Option A: Lokelma (sodium zirconium cyclosilicate - SZC)**
    * *Correction Dose:* 10g PO TID for up to 48 hours.
    * *Maintenance Transition:* Once K+ <5.0, reduce to 5g or 10g PO once daily.
* **Option B: Veltassa (patiromer)**
    * *Correction Dose:* 8.4g PO once daily.
    * *Titration:* Increase by 8.4g weekly as needed (Max: 25.2g/day).
    * *Maintenance Transition:* Continue effective dose daily.

### Step 4: Re-evaluate and Resume
* Recheck BMP in 3-7 days.
* **RESUME RAAS blockade** once K+ is confirmed <5.0 mEq/L.

## 3. CHRONIC Management (Enabling RAASi / MRA Therapy)
**Goal:** Maintain K+ <5.0 mEq/L to enable maximum tolerated doses of guideline-directed medical therapy (GDMT) including ACEi/ARB, Aldactone (spironolactone), and Kerendia (finerenone).

### Chronic Interventions
1. **Standing Potassium Binders:** Maintain patient on daily Veltassa (patiromer) or Lokelma (sodium zirconium cyclosilicate).
2. **Correct Metabolic Acidosis:** Target serum bicarbonate ≥22 mEq/L.
    * Start sodium bicarbonate 650 mg PO BID-TID if bicarb <22 mEq/L.
3. **Optimize Diuretics:** Ensure adequate loop or thiazide diuretic dosing to promote distal tubular flow and kaliuresis.
4. **Dietary Counseling:** Ongoing medical nutrition therapy (MNT) with a renal dietitian.

### Thresholds for RAASi and Kerendia (finerenone)
| Medication Class | Initiation Threshold | Holding Threshold | Restarting / Titration Protocol |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **ACEi / ARB** | K+ ≤ 5.0 mEq/L | K+ > 5.5 mEq/L | Restart when K+ < 5.0. Add binder to maintain therapy. |
| **Aldactone (spironolactone)** | K+ ≤ 5.0 mEq/L | K+ > 5.5 mEq/L | Restart at lower dose when K+ < 5.0. Monitor K+ at 1 week, 4 weeks. |
| **Kerendia (finerenone)** | K+ ≤ 5.0 mEq/L | K+ > 5.5 mEq/L | Withhold. Restart at 10 mg daily when K+ ≤ 5.0 mEq/L. |

## 4. Potassium Binders Comparison Table

| Feature | Lokelma (sodium zirconium cyclosilicate) | Veltassa (patiromer) | Kayexalate (sodium polystyrene sulfonate) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Mechanism** | Inorganic crystal, exchanges K+ for Na+ and H+ | Non-absorbed polymer, exchanges K+ for Calcium | Resin, exchanges K+ for Sodium |
| **Onset of Action** | 1 hour (Fastest oral option) | 7 hours | 2 - 24 hours |
| **Correction Dosing** | 10g PO TID x 48 hours | 8.4g PO Daily | 15-30g PO/PR |
| **Maintenance Dosing**| 5g to 15g PO Daily | 8.4g to 25.2g PO Daily | Not recommended for chronic use |
| **Drug Interactions** | Space 2 hours from narrow therapeutic index drugs (e.g., tacrolimus) | Space 3 hours from ALL other oral medications | Binds many drugs; space 3 hours from other meds |
| **Adverse Effects** | Edema (contains ~400mg Na+ per 10g dose) | Hypomagnesemia, constipation, GI upset | **Bowel necrosis**, GI bleeding, hypernatremia |
| **Cost / Access** | High (Prior Auth usually required) | High (Prior Auth usually required) | Low (Generic available) |

* **ICD-10 Codes:**
    * `E87.5` Hyperkalemia
    * `N18.x` Chronic kidney disease (code to specific stage)
    * `E87.2` Acidosis (if concurrent metabolic acidosis)
* **CPT Codes:**
    * `93000` Electrocardiogram, routine ECG with at least 12 leads; with interpretation and report.
    * `99291` Critical care, evaluation and management of the critically ill or critically injured patient; first 30-74 minutes (Use only if acute hyperkalemia requires critical care interventions).
    * `99214` / `99215` Standard E&M codes for complex medical decision making.

## 6. Patient Education Talking Points

* **The "Silent" Danger:** "High potassium usually has no symptoms until it causes a dangerous, sometimes fatal, irregular heartbeat. This is why we monitor your blood work so closely."
* **Hidden Potassium:** "Avoid salt substitutes (like Nu-Salt or No-Salt). They replace sodium with potassium, which is extremely dangerous for your kidneys."
* **Medication Adherence:** "Do not stop your heart or kidney protection medications (like your blood pressure pills) without calling us first. If your potassium is high, we have specific medications (binders) to fix the potassium so you can keep taking your kidney-saving drugs."
* **Taking Binders Correctly:** 
    * *If on Veltassa:* "Take this powder mixed with water or soft food. You must take it at least 3 hours before or 3 hours after your other pills."
    * *If on Lokelma:* "Mix this powder with water. If you take medications with narrow safety windows, we will tell you how to space them out."
* **When to go to the ER:** "If you experience sudden muscle weakness, numbness, tingling, or feel like your heart is skipping beats, go to the nearest emergency room immediately."
