# Clinical Decision Algorithm: SGLT2 Inhibitor Initiation in CKD

**Document Type:** Clinical Reference Guide  
**Target Audience:** Nephrologists, Advanced Practice Providers (APPs), and Clinical Staff at The Kidney Experts (TKE)  
**Guideline Basis:** KDIGO 2024 CKD Guidelines, ADA 2024 Standards of Care, ACC/AHA 2022 Heart Failure Guidelines  

*Disclaimer: This document is for clinical reference and educational purposes only. It does not constitute patient-specific medical advice. Clinical judgment must be applied to individual patient scenarios.*

---

## 1. ELIGIBILITY CHECK & CONTRAINDICATIONS

According to KDIGO 2024 and ADA 2024 guidelines, SGLT2 inhibitors are recommended as foundational therapy for patients with CKD and an eGFR ≥20 mL/min/1.73m², with or without Type 2 Diabetes (T2DM).

### ✅ Inclusion Criteria (May Initiate)
*   **eGFR:** ≥20 mL/min/1.73m²
*   **UACR:** Benefit is most pronounced in UACR ≥200 mg/g, but indicated for all CKD patients at risk of progression (per EMPA-KIDNEY data).
*   **Diagnosis:** CKD with T2DM, non-diabetic CKD, or Heart Failure (HFrEF/HFpEF).

### 🛑 Absolute Contraindications (Do NOT Initiate)
*   Type 1 Diabetes Mellitus (T1DM)
*   History of recurrent Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA)
*   Pregnancy, planned pregnancy, or actively lactating
*   Polycystic Kidney Disease (PKD) or patients requiring active immunosuppression for immune-mediated kidney disease (excluded from major trials; use requires individualized nephrologist discretion).

### ⚠️ Precautions (Assess Risk vs. Benefit)
*   Active foot ulcers, severe peripheral arterial disease (PAD), or high amputation risk.
*   History of recurrent severe genital mycotic infections.
*   Frail elderly patients at high risk for volume depletion/falls.

## 3. PRE-INITIATION PROTOCOL

Before writing the prescription, complete the following checklist to mitigate risks of hypoglycemia and hypovolemia.

### Step 1: Baseline Labs
*   Check eGFR, BMP (Serum Creatinine, Potassium), Fasting Glucose, and HbA1c within the last 30 days.
*   Check baseline UACR to establish a starting point for efficacy monitoring.

### Step 2: Medication Adjustments (Crucial Step)
*   **Insulin or Sulfonylureas** (e.g., Amaryl (glimepiride), Glucotrol (glipizide)): 
    *   **Action:** REDUCE dose by **20-30%** upon SGLT2i initiation to prevent severe hypoglycemia.
*   **Loop Diuretics** (e.g., Lasix (furosemide), Bumex (bumetanide), Demadex (torsemide)):
    *   **Action:** If the patient is euvolemic or elderly, consider reducing the loop diuretic dose by **25-50%** to prevent volume depletion, orthostatic hypotension, and AKI.

### Step 3: Patient Counseling
*   **Genital Hygiene:** Counsel on daily washing and thorough drying of the genital area to prevent mycotic infections and the rare risk of Fournier's gangrene.
*   **Hydration:** Advise drinking water to thirst to prevent dehydration.

## 4. POST-INITIATION MONITORING & LAB TARGETS

### 1-Month Follow-Up
*   **Labs:** BMP (eGFR, SCr, K+).
*   **The "eGFR Dip":** Expect a **3-5 mL/min/1.73m² drop** (up to 10-30% from baseline) in eGFR. 
    *   *Clinical Pearl:* **DO NOT STOP THERAPY.** This is a benign, reversible hemodynamic effect (afferent arteriole constriction) that indicates the drug is successfully reducing intraglomerular pressure. 
    *   *Action:* If eGFR drops >30%, evaluate for other causes of AKI (volume depletion, NSAID use, urinary retention) before discontinuing the SGLT2i.

### 3-Month Follow-Up
*   **Labs:** eGFR, UACR.
*   **Targets:** 
    *   UACR reduction of **>30%** from baseline.
    *   Ultimate target UACR **<30 mg/g** (though any sustained reduction is renoprotective).

### Routine Monitoring
*   Every 3-6 months per standard CKD protocols. [TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED: Standardized lab order sets for SGLT2i follow-up].

## 5. WHEN TO STOP OR HOLD THERAPY

### Permanent Discontinuation
*   **Dialysis Initiation:** Stop the SGLT2i when the patient transitions to maintenance dialysis (hemodialysis or peritoneal dialysis). There is currently insufficient data to support continued use.
*   **Pregnancy:** Stop immediately if the patient becomes pregnant.
*   **Recurrent DKA:** Stop permanently if the patient experiences an episode of Diabetic Ketoacidosis.
*   **DO NOT STOP for eGFR Decline:** If eGFR naturally declines **below 20 mL/min/1.73m²** while on therapy, **CONTINUE** the medication until dialysis initiation. The renoprotective and cardiovascular benefits persist at lower eGFRs.

### Temporary Holds ("Sick Day Rules")
Educate patients to hold their SGLT2i (along with other SADMANS medications: Sulfonylureas, ACEis, Diuretics, Metformin, ARBs, NSAIDs, SGLT2is) in the following scenarios:
*   **Acute Illness:** Vomiting, severe diarrhea, or inability to maintain normal oral fluid intake.
*   **Perioperative:** Hold **3 to 4 days prior** to major scheduled surgery or procedures requiring fasting/bowel prep.
*   **Resumption:** Resume Farxiga (dapagliflozin) or Jardiance (empagliflozin) only when the patient is clinically stable, eating, and drinking normally.

## 6. PATIENT EDUCATION TALKING POINTS

*   *"This medication protects your kidneys and heart, even if you don't have diabetes. It works by removing extra sugar and salt through your urine."*
*   *"You may notice you need to urinate more often. This is normal. Drink water when you feel thirsty."*
*   *"Because there is sugar in your urine, yeast infections can happen. Good daily hygiene—washing with soap and water and drying completely—greatly reduces this risk."*
*   *"If you get sick with a stomach bug and can't keep fluids down, stop taking this pill until you are eating and drinking normally again."*
*   *"Watch for signs of too much acid in your blood (DKA): nausea, vomiting, stomach pain, or fruity-smelling breath. Go to the ER if this happens, even if your blood sugar numbers look normal."*
