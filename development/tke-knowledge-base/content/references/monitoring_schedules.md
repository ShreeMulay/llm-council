# Comprehensive Medication Monitoring Schedules Quick Reference

**Disclaimer:** *This document is for clinical reference and educational purposes only. It does not constitute patient-specific medical advice. Clinical judgment should always supersede reference guidelines.*

**Primary Guideline Sources:** KDIGO 2024 CKD Evaluation & Management, KDIGO 2022 Diabetes Management in CKD, ADA 2024 Standards of Care, ACC/AHA 2018/2022 Cholesterol/Heart Failure Guidelines, ACR 2020 Gout Guidelines.

## 1. Renoprotective & Diabetic Kidney Disease (DKD) Agents

### RAAS Blockade: ACE Inhibitors & ARBs
*Examples: Lisinopril (Prinivil/Zestril), Losartan (Cozaar), Valsartan (Diovan)*
* **Guideline:** KDIGO 2024 CKD
* **Target:** Maximize tolerated dose for proteinuria (target UACR <30 mg/g or >50% reduction).

| Drug Class | Baseline Labs | After Start/Change | Routine Monitoring | Alert Values & Clinical Actions |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **ACEi / ARB** | BMP (Cr, K+) | 1–2 weeks | q3–6 months | **K+ > 5.5 mEq/L:** Start low-K+ diet, add K+ binder, or reduce dose.<br>**Cr rise > 30%:** Assess volume status, hold diuretics if dry. Do *not* stop RAASi if rise is <30% and stabilizes. |

### SGLT2 Inhibitors
*Examples: Dapagliflozin (Farxiga), Empagliflozin (Jardiance)*
* **Guideline:** KDIGO 2024 CKD, ADA 2024
* **Dosing:** Farxiga 10 mg daily; Jardiance 10 mg daily. Initiate if eGFR ≥ 20 mL/min/1.73m². Continue until dialysis.

| Drug Class | Baseline Labs | After Start/Change | Routine Monitoring | Alert Values & Clinical Actions |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **SGLT2i** | BMP (eGFR), A1c (if DM) | 1 month | q3–6 months | **eGFR drop < 30%:** Expected hemodynamic dip; *continue therapy*.<br>**eGFR drop > 30%:** Evaluate for volume depletion/AKI; hold temporarily.<br>**Symptoms of UTI/DKA:** Hold immediately, check UA/ketones. |

### Non-Steroidal MRAs (nsMRA)
*Examples: Finerenone (Kerendia)*
* **Guideline:** KDIGO 2022 Diabetes in CKD, ADA 2024
* **Dosing:** Initiate at 10 mg daily if eGFR 25-59; 20 mg daily if eGFR ≥ 60. Target 20 mg daily.

| Drug Class | Baseline Labs | After Start/Change | Routine Monitoring | Alert Values & Clinical Actions |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Kerendia (finerenone)** | BMP (K+, eGFR) | **Strictly at 4 weeks** | q4 months | **K+ 4.8–5.0:** Maintain dose.<br>**K+ 5.1–5.5:** Maintain 10mg or hold 20mg until K+ ≤ 5.0, then restart 10mg.<br>**K+ > 5.5:** Hold until K+ ≤ 5.0, restart at 10mg. |

### GLP-1 Receptor Agonists
*Examples: Semaglutide (Ozempic), Dulaglutide (Trulicity), Tirzepatide (Mounjaro - dual GIP/GLP-1)*
* **Guideline:** ADA 2024, KDIGO 2022

| Drug Class | Baseline Labs | After Start/Change | Routine Monitoring | Alert Values & Clinical Actions |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **GLP-1 RAs** | A1c, BMP, Weight | 4 weeks (titration) | A1c q3 months, Weight | **Severe N/V/D:** Hold/reduce dose to prevent AKI from volume depletion.<br>**Abdominal pain:** Evaluate for pancreatitis/gallbladder disease. |

### Biguanides
*Examples: Metformin (Glucophage)*
* **Guideline:** KDIGO 2022, ADA 2024

| Drug Class | Baseline Labs | After Start/Change | Routine Monitoring | Alert Values & Clinical Actions |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Metformin** | BMP, B12, A1c | 3 months (A1c) | BMP q3–6 months, B12 annually | **eGFR 30-44:** Max dose 1000 mg/day.<br>**eGFR < 30:** STOP therapy.<br>**Contrast Procedures:** Hold 24h prior and 48h post-procedure; check Cr before resuming. |

## 2. Anemia Management

### Erythropoiesis-Stimulating Agents (ESAs)
*Examples: Darbepoetin alfa (Aranesp), Epoetin alfa (Epogen/Procrit)*
* **Guideline:** KDIGO 2012 Anemia in CKD
* **Target:** Hb 10.0 - 11.5 g/dL. Do not target > 11.5 g/dL due to stroke/thrombosis risk.

| Drug Class | Baseline Labs | After Start/Change | Routine Monitoring | Alert Values & Clinical Actions |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **ESAs** | CBC, Iron Panel, B12/Folate | 2–4 weeks | CBC monthly, Iron q3 months | **Hb > 11.5:** Reduce dose by 25% or hold.<br>**Hb rise > 1 g/dL in 2 wks:** Reduce dose by 25%.<br>**Poor response:** Check reticulocyte count, evaluate for bleeding, infection, or iron deficiency. |

### Intravenous Iron
*Examples: Iron sucrose (Venofer), Ferric carboxymaltose (Injectafer)*
* **Target:** TSAT > 20% (ideally 30%), Ferritin > 200 ng/mL (CKD ND) or > 500 ng/mL (CKD 5D).

| Drug Class | Baseline Labs | After Start/Change | Routine Monitoring | Alert Values & Clinical Actions |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **IV Iron** | Ferritin, TSAT | 4–8 weeks post-course | q3 months | **TSAT > 50% or Ferritin > 800:** Hold iron therapy.<br>**Active systemic infection:** Hold IV iron until resolved. |

*Examples: Sevelamer carbonate (Renvela), Calcium acetate (PhosLo), Sucroferric oxyhydroxide (Velphoro)*
* **Guideline:** KDIGO 2017 CKD-MBD
* **Target:** Maintain serum phosphorus toward the normal range (2.5 - 4.5 mg/dL).

| Drug Class | Baseline Labs | After Start/Change | Routine Monitoring | Alert Values & Clinical Actions |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |

## 4. Hyperkalemia Management

### Potassium Binders
*Examples: Patiromer (Veltassa), Sodium zirconium cyclosilicate (Lokelma)*
* **Target:** Serum K+ 4.0 - 5.0 mEq/L to enable optimization of RAASi/nsMRA therapy.

| Drug Class | Baseline Labs | After Start/Change | Routine Monitoring | Alert Values & Clinical Actions |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **K+ Binders** | BMP (K+, Mg) | 1–4 weeks | q1–3 months | **K+ < 3.5:** Decrease dose or stop.<br>**K+ > 5.5:** Increase dose.<br>**Mg < 1.4:** (Veltassa only) Supplement Mg. |

## 5. Gout & Hyperuricemia Management

### Urate-Lowering Therapy (ULT)
*Examples: Allopurinol (Zyloprim), Febuxostat (Uloric)*
* **Guideline:** ACR 2020 Gout Guidelines
* **Target:** Serum Uric Acid < 6.0 mg/dL.

| Drug Class | Baseline Labs | After Start/Change | Routine Monitoring | Alert Values & Clinical Actions |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Allopurinol / Febuxostat** | Uric Acid, LFTs, BMP | q2–4 weeks (during titration) | q6 months | **UA > 6.0:** Titrate dose up.<br>**LFTs > 3x ULN:** Hold Febuxostat.<br>**Rash:** Stop Allopurinol immediately (risk of SJS/TEN; screen HLA-B*5801 in high-risk populations). |
*Note: Allopurinol starting dose must be reduced in CKD (e.g., 50-100 mg/day if eGFR < 30), but can be titrated up slowly to achieve target.*

## 8. SICK DAY RULES (SADMANS Protocol)

Patients must be educated to **HOLD** certain medications during acute illness involving volume depletion (vomiting, severe diarrhea, high fever, inability to maintain oral intake) to prevent Acute Kidney Injury (AKI), severe hypoglycemia, or Ketoacidosis.

Use the **SADMANS** mnemonic:
*   **S** - Sulfonylureas (Glipizide, Glimepiride)
*   **A** - ACE inhibitors (Lisinopril)
*   **D** - Diuretics (Furosemide, Torsemide, HCTZ)
*   **M** - Metformin
*   **A** - ARBs (Losartan, Valsartan)
*   **N** - NSAIDs (Ibuprofen, Naproxen - *should be avoided in CKD generally*)
*   **S** - SGLT2 inhibitors (Farxiga, Jardiance)

### When to Resume:
Instruct patients to resume these medications only after they have been eating and drinking normally for **24 to 48 hours** and acute symptoms have fully resolved. If symptoms persist >48 hours, they should contact the clinic.

*   **Z79.899:** Other long term (current) drug therapy (e.g., ESAs, Immunosuppressants)

*   **99495 / 99496:** Transitional Care Management (TCM) - post-discharge medication reconciliation

## 10. Patient Education Talking Points

*   **Lab Adherence:** *"Many of your kidney medications require us to check your blood work 1 to 4 weeks after starting. This is to ensure the medication is protecting your kidneys without causing your potassium or kidney numbers to jump to unsafe levels."*
*   **SGLT2i Expectations:** *"When you start Farxiga/Jardiance, your kidney function number (eGFR) might drop slightly on your first lab draw. This is a normal, expected reaction that shows the drug is reducing pressure inside your kidneys. Do not stop taking it unless we tell you to."*
*   **Sick Days:** Provide the patient with a printed "Sick Day Rules" card. *"If you get a stomach bug and can't keep fluids down, stop your blood pressure pills, water pills, and diabetes pills until you are eating and drinking normally again."*
*   **Safety Alerts:** *"Call our office immediately if you develop severe muscle pain on statins, a new rash on allopurinol, or signs of a urinary tract infection on your SGLT2 inhibitor."*
