## 1. CKD Staging (KDIGO 2024)

Accurate staging is required for risk stratification, medication dosing, and determining the frequency of clinical monitoring. KDIGO 2024 emphasizes using both GFR and Albuminuria categories, alongside the Kidney Failure Risk Equation (KFRE), to guide nephrology referrals and multidisciplinary care.

### GFR Categories
| Category | eGFR (mL/min/1.73 m²) | Description | Clinical Action / Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **G1** | ≥ 90 | Normal or high | Requires evidence of kidney damage (e.g., proteinuria, structural) |
| **G2** | 60–89 | Mildly decreased | Requires evidence of kidney damage |
| **G3a** | 45–59 | Mildly to moderately decreased | Monitor complications; adjust renally cleared meds |
| **G3b** | 30–44 | Moderately to severely decreased | High risk for CKD-MBD, anemia; strict BP/UACR control |
| **G4** | 15–29 | Severely decreased | Prepare for RRT/transplant; vascular access planning |
| **G5** | < 15 | Kidney failure | Initiate RRT, preemptive transplant, or conservative care |

### Albuminuria Categories
| Category | UACR (mg/g) | UACR (mg/mmol) | Description | Clinical Action / Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **A1** | < 30 | < 3 | Normal to mildly increased | Routine annual screening if diabetic/hypertensive |
| **A2** | 30–300 | 3–30 | Moderately increased | Initiate RASi, SGLT2i; target <30 mg/g |
| **A3** | > 300 | > 30 | Severely increased | High risk for progression; consider nsMRA addition |

## 3. Treatment Targets

| Parameter | Target | Guideline Source | Clinical Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Blood Pressure** | **< 120 systolic** (if tolerated) | KDIGO 2024 / SPRINT | Standardized office BP measurement required. Relax target to <130 or <140 in frail/elderly with orthostasis. |
| **UACR** | **< 30 mg/g** OR **≥ 30% reduction** | KDIGO 2024 | Maximize ACEi/ARB; add SGLT2i, nsMRA, or GLP-1 RA to achieve target. |
| **A1c** | **< 7.0%** (individualized) | ADA 2024 / KDIGO 2022 | Target <6.5% if young/healthy; <8.0% if frail/hypoglycemia risk. Note: A1c may be falsely low in advanced CKD/ESRD due to altered RBC lifespan. |
| **Hemoglobin** | **10.0 – 11.5 g/dL** | KDIGO 2012 | **WARNING:** Do not target >11.5 g/dL with ESAs (Epogen/Retacrit) due to increased stroke/thrombosis risk. |
| **Ferritin & TSAT** | **Ferritin 200–500 ng/mL**<br>**TSAT 20–30%** | KDIGO 2012 | Replete iron (IV preferred in CKD 4-5/5D) before initiating ESAs. Upper limit of Ferritin for IV iron is typically 500-800 ng/mL. |
| **Potassium** | **3.5 – 5.0 mEq/L** | KDIGO 2024 | Use potassium binders (Lokelma, Veltassa) to maintain RASi therapy rather than stopping RASi. |
| **Bicarbonate** | **≥ 22 mEq/L** | KDIGO 2024 | Supplement with sodium bicarbonate to slow CKD progression and prevent muscle wasting. |
| **Phosphorus** | **Normal range by lab** (typically 2.5–4.5 mg/dL) | KDIGO 2017 | Treat toward normal range. Restrict dietary inorganic phosphates; use binders if hyperphosphatemia persists. |
| **Intact PTH** | **CKD 3-5:** Evaluate trends<br>**CKD 5D:** 2–9x upper limit of normal | KDIGO 2017 | Do not treat a single elevated value in CKD 3-5. In 5D, target ~150-600 pg/mL depending on lab assay. |
| **25(OH) Vitamin D** | **≥ 30 ng/mL** | KDIGO 2017 | Replete with ergocalciferol or cholecalciferol as in the general population. |
| **Uric Acid** | **< 6.0 mg/dL** (only if on ULT for gout) | ACR 2020 | Asymptomatic hyperuricemia does *not* require treatment to slow CKD progression. |
| **LDL Cholesterol** | **No specific LDL target** | KDIGO 2013 / ACC/AHA 2018 | Use statins based on cardiovascular risk. **Do not initiate** statins in incident dialysis patients, but continue if already on them. |

## 5. Patient Education Talking Points

Use these simple explanations when discussing lab targets with patients:
*   **eGFR (Kidney Function):** "This is a percentage of how well your kidney filters are working. We want to keep this number as stable as possible."
*   **UACR (Protein in Urine):** "Protein in the urine acts like sandpaper on your kidney filters. Our goal is to lower this number by at least 30% using medications like Farxiga or Kerendia."
*   **Potassium:** "Kidneys clear potassium from your blood. If it gets too high, it can cause dangerous heart rhythms. We monitor this closely when we start heart and kidney protective medications."
*   **Phosphorus & PTH:** "When kidneys fail, phosphorus builds up and pulls calcium from your bones, making them weak. We check these to see if you need a special diet or 'binder' medications with your meals."
*   **Hemoglobin:** "Your kidneys make a hormone that tells your bones to make red blood cells. If your hemoglobin is low, you may feel tired, and we may need to give you iron or a special injection to boost it."

*   **82570:** Creatinine, other source (UACR component)
*   **83970:** Parathormone (PTH), intact
*   **82306:** Vitamin D, 25 hydroxy
*   **82728:** Ferritin
*   **83540 / 84466:** Iron / Transferrin (TSAT components)
