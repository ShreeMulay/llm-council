## 2. CLINICAL DECISION TREES & PROTOCOLS

### RAAS Blockade (ACEi/ARB) Management
*   **Initiation:** Start in all patients with UACR ≥30 mg/g (KDIGO 2024), regardless of BP, to delay CKD progression.
*   **Monitoring:** Check BMP 2-4 weeks after initiation or dose escalation.
*   **When to STOP or HOLD:**
    *   **Hyperkalemia:** Do not stop for K+ <5.5 mEq/L. If K+ ≥5.5, initiate dietary restriction, add oral K+ binders (Veltassa/Lokelma), or add diuretics before stopping RAASi.
    *   **AKI:** Accept up to a 30% rise in SCr from baseline. If SCr rises >30%, evaluate for volume depletion, NSAID use, or bilateral renal artery stenosis. Hold if patient is actively volume depleted.
*   **[TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED]:** Local clinic workflow for standing lab orders 2 weeks post-RAASi titration.

### SGLT2 Inhibitor (Farxiga/Jardiance) Management
*   **Initiation:** Start in CKD patients with eGFR ≥20 mL/min/1.73m² with UACR ≥200 mg/g, or heart failure, or Type 2 DM (KDIGO 2024).
*   **Expected Effect:** An initial "dip" in eGFR (up to 30%) is expected and hemodynamic; *do not stop the drug*.
*   **When to STOP:**
    *   Do **NOT** stop if eGFR falls below 20. Continue until initiation of dialysis or transplant.
    *   **HOLD** 3-4 days prior to scheduled surgery or during acute severe medical illness (risk of euglycemic DKA).

### Anemia Management (ESAs)
*   **Target:** Hemoglobin 10.0 - 11.5 g/dL.
*   **Iron First:** Ensure TSAT >30% and Ferritin >500 ng/mL before escalating ESAs.
*   **Escalation:** Increase ESA dose by 25% if Hb increases <1.0 g/dL after 4 weeks.
*   **De-escalation:** Decrease dose by 25% if Hb approaches 11.5 g/dL or rises >1.0 g/dL in any 2-week period.

## 4. DRUGS TO AVOID IN CKD (eGFR <60)

🚨 **FLAGGED SAFETY CONCERNS**

*   **NSAIDs (Ibuprofen, Naproxen, Ketorolac, Celecoxib, Meloxicam, Diclofenac):**
    *   *Why:* Block prostaglandin-mediated afferent arteriole vasodilation, causing acute renal ischemia and AKI. Can also cause acute interstitial nephritis (AIN) and worsen hypertension/edema.
    *   *Alternative:* Acetaminophen, topical NSAIDs (Voltaren gel - minimal systemic absorption), short-term Tramadol.
*   **Bactrim (Trimethoprim/Sulfamethoxazole):**
    *   *Why:* Trimethoprim blocks distal tubule potassium excretion (causing severe hyperkalemia) and blocks tubular secretion of creatinine (causing a "pseudo-AKI" rise in SCr without true GFR drop). Can also cause true AIN.
*   **Nitrofurantoin (Macrobid):**
    *   *Why:* Ineffective for UTI if eGFR <30 due to lack of excretion into the urinary tract. High risk of peripheral neuropathy and pulmonary toxicity in advanced CKD.
*   **Bisphosphonates (Alendronate, Zoledronic acid):**
    *   *Why:* Contraindicated if eGFR <35 mL/min. Risk of accumulation, severe hypocalcemia, and worsening of adynamic bone disease.
    *   *Alternative:* Prolia (denosumab) can be used in CKD but requires strict calcium monitoring (high risk of severe hypocalcemia).
*   **Sodium Phosphate Bowel Preps (OsmoPrep, Fleet Enema):**
    *   *Why:* Causes acute phosphate nephropathy (irreversible AKI due to calcium-phosphate crystal deposition in tubules).
    *   *Alternative:* PEG-based preps (GoLytely, Miralax).
*   **Baclofen:**
    *   *Why:* Primarily renally cleared. Accumulation causes severe encephalopathy, coma, and respiratory depression in CKD/ESRD.
*   **Meperidine (Demerol):**
    *   *Why:* Toxic metabolite (normeperidine) accumulates in CKD, causing seizures.

## 5. PATIENT EDUCATION TALKING POINTS

**The "SADMANS" Sick Day Rules**
Educate all CKD patients to temporarily **HOLD** the following medications during acute dehydrating illnesses (vomiting, severe diarrhea, high fever, inability to drink fluids) to prevent AKI:
*   **S**ulfonylureas (Glipizide, Glimepiride) - *Hypoglycemia risk*
*   **A**CE Inhibitors (Lisinopril) - *AKI risk*
*   **D**iuretics (Lasix, HCTZ) - *Volume depletion*
*   **M**etformin - *Lactic acidosis risk*
*   **A**RBs (Losartan) - *AKI risk*
*   **N**SAIDs (Ibuprofen) - *AKI risk (Should be avoided generally)*
*   **S**GLT2 Inhibitors (Farxiga, Jardiance) - *Euglycemic DKA and volume depletion risk*

**General Education:**
*   *"Do not take over-the-counter pain relievers like Advil, Motrin, or Aleve. Tylenol is safe for your kidneys."*
*   *"Your blood pressure medication (ACE/ARB) protects your kidneys, but we expect a small bump in your kidney numbers when we start it. This means it is working to lower the pressure inside the kidney filters."*
*   *"If you are scheduled for a colonoscopy or surgery, call our office to review which medications to hold."*

## 6. CODING & BILLING GUIDANCE

*Note: Ensure documentation supports medical necessity and time spent.*
*   **CKD ICD-10 Codes:** N18.1 (Stage 1) through N18.6 (ESRD). *Always code the underlying cause (e.g., I12.9 for Hypertensive CKD, E11.22 for Diabetic CKD).*
*   **E/M Codes (99213 - 99215):** Medication management (e.g., adjusting RAASi, starting SGLT2i, managing hyperkalemia) qualifies as Moderate to High complexity Medical Decision Making (MDM).
*   **Care Management:** Consider billing Chronic Care Management (CCM - CPT 99490) for clinical staff time spent coordinating care, managing medication prior authorizations (e.g., for GLP-1s or Binders), and patient education between visits.
