## PART 2: PRACTICE-CHANGING SUMMARY (Top 10 Impacts for TKE)

### 1. The SGLT2i Revolution (DAPA-CKD & EMPA-KIDNEY)
*   **The Paradigm Shift:** SGLT2 inhibitors are no longer just diabetes drugs; they are primary nephroprotective agents.
*   **Decision Tree:** 
    *   **Start:** eGFR ≥20 mL/min/1.73m² (per KDIGO 2024).
    *   **Stop:** Do NOT stop if eGFR falls below 20; continue until initiation of dialysis or transplant.
*   **Dosing:** Farxiga (dapagliflozin) 10 mg daily OR Jardiance (empagliflozin) 10 mg daily. No titration needed.
*   **Monitoring:** Expect a reversible "eGFR dip" of up to 30% in the first 2-4 weeks. *Do not stop the drug unless the dip exceeds 30% or the patient is volume depleted.*
*   **Patient Education:** "This medication protects your kidney filters. It will make you urinate sugar, so you must maintain good hygiene to prevent yeast infections. Stop taking it temporarily if you are sick, vomiting, or fasting for a procedure (Sick Day Rules)."

### 2. The GLP-1 RA Expansion (FLOW)
*   **The Paradigm Shift:** Ozempic (semaglutide) significantly reduces kidney failure and death in diabetic kidney disease.
*   **Target:** UACR <30 mg/g.
*   **Dosing:** Ozempic (semaglutide) 0.25 mg weekly x 4 weeks, then 0.5 mg weekly x 4 weeks, then target 1.0 mg weekly.
*   **Safety Flag:** Contraindicated in personal/family history of Medullary Thyroid Cancer or MEN2. Monitor for GI side effects and AKI secondary to volume depletion from vomiting.

### 3. Non-Steroidal MRAs for DKD (FIDELIO & FIGARO)
*   **The Paradigm Shift:** Kerendia (finerenone) provides additive kidney and CV protection *on top of* maximum tolerated RASi and SGLT2i.
*   **Dosing:** 
    *   eGFR ≥60: Start 20 mg daily.
    *   eGFR 25-59: Start 10 mg daily.
    *   Titrate to 20 mg after 4 weeks if Potassium ≤4.8 mEq/L.
*   **Monitoring:** Check Potassium at 4 weeks. Do not initiate if baseline K+ >5.0 mEq/L.

### 4. Banning Dual RAAS Blockade (ONTARGET & VA NEPHRON-D)
*   **The Paradigm Shift:** Combining an ACEi with an ARB causes harm (AKI, hyperkalemia) without CV or renal benefit.
*   **Actionable Rule:** **NEVER** prescribe an ACEi and ARB together. If switching between Entresto (sacubitril/valsartan) and an ACEi, mandate a 36-hour washout period to prevent angioedema.

### 5. Intensive BP Control in CKD (SPRINT-CKD)
*   **The Paradigm Shift:** Lower is better for survival and CV protection in non-diabetic CKD.
*   **Target:** KDIGO 2021 recommends targeting Systolic BP <120 mmHg using standardized office BP measurement.
*   **Caveat:** Individualize for frail, elderly patients or those with severe orthostasis.

### 6. Rituximab as 1st Line for Membranous Nephropathy (MENTOR)
*   **The Paradigm Shift:** Rituxan (rituximab) is superior to calcineurin inhibitors (cyclosporine) for maintaining long-term remission in Primary Membranous Nephropathy.
*   **Dosing:** 1000 mg IV on Days 1 and 15 (repeated at 6 months if partial response).
*   **Monitoring:** Monitor CD19 B-cell counts and PLA2R antibody titers.

### 7. Steroid-Sparing in ANCA Vasculitis (ADVOCATE)
*   **The Paradigm Shift:** Tavneos (avacopan), an oral C5a receptor antagonist, replaces high-dose prednisone tapers, reducing steroid toxicity while improving 52-week remission rates.
*   **Dosing:** Tavneos (avacopan) 30 mg PO BID with food.
*   **Safety Flag:** Monitor LFTs (hepatotoxicity risk).
