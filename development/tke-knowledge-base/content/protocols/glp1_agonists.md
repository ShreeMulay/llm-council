# CLINICAL REFERENCE: GLP-1 Receptor Agonists & Dual GIP/GLP-1 Agonists in CKD

## 1. Overview & Guideline Recommendations
Glucagon-like peptide-1 receptor agonists (GLP-1 RAs) and dual GIP/GLP-1 RAs are foundational therapies for patients with Type 2 Diabetes (T2D), Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD), and obesity. 

**Guideline Alignment:**
*   **KDIGO (2024):** Recommends a GLP-1 RA for patients with T2D and CKD who have not achieved individualized glycemic targets despite use of metformin and an SGLT2 inhibitor, or who are unable to use those medications.
*   **ADA (2024):** Recommends GLP-1 RAs with proven cardiovascular (CV) benefit for patients with T2D and established ASCVD, high CV risk, or CKD, independent of baseline A1c.

**Clinical Targets:**
*   **UACR:** Target <30 mg/g (or >30% reduction from baseline).
*   **A1c:** Individualized (generally <7.0% for early CKD, <8.0% for advanced CKD/high hypoglycemia risk).
*   **Weight:** Target >10-15% weight loss for obese patients with CKD to reduce intraglomerular pressure and proteinuria.

## 2. Clinical Evidence & Kidney-Specific Benefits

### The FLOW Trial (2024) - *Practice Changing*
The **FLOW trial** is the first dedicated kidney outcomes trial for a GLP-1 RA. 
*   **Agent:** Ozempic (semaglutide) 1.0 mg weekly vs. placebo.
*   **Population:** T2D and CKD (eGFR 50-75 with UACR 300-5000 mg/g, or eGFR 25-50 with UACR 100-5000 mg/g).
*   **Results:** Trial stopped early for overwhelming efficacy. Demonstrated a **24% risk reduction** in the primary composite endpoint (kidney failure, >50% eGFR decline, or death from kidney/CV causes). 

### Cardiovascular Outcome Trials (CVOTs)
Secondary kidney endpoints (primarily driven by reduction in new-onset macroalbuminuria) were demonstrated in:
*   **SUSTAIN-6:** Ozempic (semaglutide)
*   **LEADER:** Victoza (liraglutide)
*   **REWIND:** Trulicity (dulaglutide)

### Tirzepatide (Dual GIP/GLP-1 RA) Evidence
*   **SURPASS (T2D) & SURMOUNT (Obesity):** Mounjaro/Zepbound (tirzepatide) demonstrated superior A1c reduction and weight loss compared to selective GLP-1 RAs. Post-hoc analyses show significant slowing of eGFR decline and reduction in UACR.

### Kidney-Specific Benefits
1.  **Proteinuria Reduction:** Direct anti-inflammatory and anti-fibrotic effects independent of glycemic control.
2.  **eGFR Preservation:** Slows the annual rate of eGFR decline.
3.  **Weight Loss:** Reduces obesity-related hyperfiltration and intraglomerular hypertension.

## 4. Decision Tree: Initiation, Escalation, and Switching

### When to START
*   T2D + CKD patients needing further A1c lowering despite metformin/SGLT2i.
*   T2D + CKD patients with UACR >30 mg/g needing further proteinuria reduction (especially Ozempic (semaglutide) post-FLOW trial).
*   Obese patients (BMI >30, or >27 with comorbidities) with CKD to reduce hyperfiltration.

### When to ESCALATE
*   Escalate dose strictly according to the 4-week schedules to mitigate GI side effects.
*   Do **not** escalate if the patient is experiencing significant nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea. Maintain current dose until symptoms resolve.

### When to SWITCH
*   **From DPP-4 Inhibitors:** Discontinue DPP-4i (e.g., Januvia (sitagliptin), Tradjenta (linagliptin)) before starting a GLP-1 RA. They work on the same pathway; combining them offers no additional benefit and increases cost/risk.
*   **Between GLP-1 RAs:** If switching due to shortage or intolerance, generally start at the lowest dose of the new agent to prevent severe GI distress. [TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED: Consult local pharmacy guidelines for equivalent dose switching if required by shortages].

### When to STOP
*   Suspected or confirmed acute pancreatitis.
*   Severe, intractable gastroparesis.
*   Acute Kidney Injury (AKI) secondary to severe volume depletion from vomiting/diarrhea.

## 5. Combination Therapy: SGLT2i and Insulin

### Combining with SGLT2 Inhibitors (e.g., Farxiga, Jardiance)
*   **Highly Recommended:** SGLT2i and GLP-1 RAs have complementary mechanisms. SGLT2i primarily reduces intraglomerular pressure via tubuloglomerular feedback; GLP-1 RAs reduce systemic inflammation, weight, and atherosclerosis.
*   **Dosing:** No dose adjustment of either class is required when used together.

### Combining with Insulin or Sulfonylureas (SU)
*   **Hypoglycemia Risk:** GLP-1 RAs alone have a very low risk of hypoglycemia. However, adding them to insulin or SUs significantly increases risk.
*   **Action:** When initiating a GLP-1 RA in a patient on insulin, **proactively reduce the basal insulin dose by 20%**. 
*   **Action:** Discontinue or halve the dose of sulfonylureas (e.g., glipizide, glimepiride).

## 6. Safety Concerns & Contraindications 🚨

*   **Thyroid C-Cell Tumors (Absolute Contraindication):** Do NOT use in patients with a personal or family history of Medullary Thyroid Carcinoma (MTC) or Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN 2).
*   **Pancreatitis:** Discontinue immediately if severe, persistent abdominal pain radiating to the back occurs.
*   **Gastroparesis:** GLP-1 RAs delay gastric emptying. Use with extreme caution in patients with pre-existing severe diabetic gastroparesis.
*   **AKI Risk:** GLP-1 RAs are not inherently nephrotoxic, but severe nausea/vomiting/diarrhea can lead to volume depletion and pre-renal AKI. Educate patients to hold the drug and call the clinic if they cannot keep fluids down.
*   **Perioperative Risk:** Due to delayed gastric emptying and aspiration risk under anesthesia, the ASA recommends holding daily GLP-1 RAs on the day of surgery, and holding weekly GLP-1 RAs for **one week prior** to elective procedures.

## 7. Weight Management in CKD

Obesity is an independent risk factor for CKD progression (obesity-related glomerulopathy). 
*   **T2D + Obesity:** Utilize Ozempic (semaglutide) or Mounjaro (tirzepatide) to maximize dual A1c and weight benefits.
*   **Non-Diabetic CKD + Obesity:** Utilize Wegovy (semaglutide) or Zepbound (tirzepatide). While not yet explicitly FDA-approved for CKD without diabetes, weight loss of >15% profoundly reduces proteinuria and slows eGFR decline.

## 8. Patient Education Talking Points

*   **Injection Technique:** Inject subcutaneously into the abdomen, thigh, or back of the upper arm. Rotate sites weekly.
*   **Managing GI Side Effects:**
    *   *“Nausea is common but usually temporary. It means the drug is working to slow your stomach.”*
    *   Eat much smaller meals. Stop eating the moment you feel full.
    *   Avoid high-fat, greasy, or very spicy foods.
    *   Stay hydrated. If you are vomiting and cannot keep fluids down, stop the medication and call the clinic.
*   **Storage:** Store unused pens in the refrigerator. Once in use, pens can be kept at room temperature for up to 21-56 days (depending on the specific drug—check the label).
*   **Oral Rybelsus Specifics:** Must be taken on an empty stomach immediately upon waking, with no more than 4 ounces of plain water. Wait exactly 30 minutes before eating, drinking, or taking other medications.

## 9. Billing, Coding, and Prior Authorization (PA)

### Relevant ICD-10 Codes
*   **E11.22:** Type 2 diabetes mellitus with diabetic chronic kidney disease
*   **E11.21:** Type 2 diabetes mellitus with diabetic nephropathy
*   **N18.x:** Chronic kidney disease (N18.31 for Stage 3a, N18.32 for Stage 3b, N18.4 for Stage 4, etc.)
*   **E66.01:** Morbid (severe) obesity due to excess calories (Required for Wegovy/Zepbound)
*   **Z68.xx:** BMI codes (e.g., Z68.35 for BMI 35.0-35.9)

### Prior Authorization Strategies [TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED]
*   **For T2D Agents (Ozempic, Mounjaro, Trulicity):** 
    *   Always code E11.22 as the primary indication.
    *   Document failure, intolerance, or contraindication to Metformin.
    *   Cite the **FLOW trial** in PA appeals for Ozempic (semaglutide) to justify use for kidney protection in DKD.
*   **For Obesity Agents (Wegovy, Zepbound):**
    *   Ensure the patient does *not* have a T2D diagnosis on the specific PA form (many insurers will reject Wegovy if T2D is coded, demanding Ozempic instead).
    *   Document participation in a comprehensive weight management program (diet/exercise counseling) for at least 3-6 months prior.
    *   Document comorbid conditions (CKD, hypertension, sleep apnea) to justify use if BMI is between 27 and 30.
