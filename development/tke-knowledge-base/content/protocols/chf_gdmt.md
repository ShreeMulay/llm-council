# CLINICAL REFERENCE: Heart Failure GDMT in CKD Patients

**Target Audience:** Nephrologists, Advanced Practice Providers (APPs), and Clinical Staff at The Kidney Experts (TKE)
**Source Guidelines:** 2022 AHA/ACC/HFSA Guidelines for the Management of Heart Failure; 2024 KDIGO Clinical Practice Guideline for the Evaluation and Management of CKD.

---

## 1. Introduction & Key Trials
Guideline-Directed Medical Therapy (GDMT) significantly reduces mortality and hospitalizations in patients with Heart Failure with reduced Ejection Fraction (HFrEF, EF ≤40%). In patients with concurrent Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD), GDMT is highly underutilized due to fears of acute kidney injury (AKI) and hyperkalemia. 

**Key Trials Supporting GDMT:**
*   **PARADIGM-HF:** Established Entresto (sacubitril/valsartan) superiority over enalapril.
*   **DAPA-HF & EMPEROR-Reduced:** Established SGLT2i efficacy in HFrEF regardless of diabetes status.
*   **RALES & EMPHASIS-HF:** Established MRA mortality benefits in HFrEF.
*   **MERIT-HF:** Established Toprol-XL (metoprolol succinate) mortality benefits.
*   **DELIVER & EMPEROR-Preserved:** Established SGLT2i efficacy in HFpEF.
*   **FINEARTS-HF:** Established Kerendia (finerenone) efficacy in HFmrEF/HFpEF.

## 2. HFrEF Management: The Four Pillars of GDMT
Initiate all four pillars as rapidly and safely as possible. Simultaneous initiation at low doses is preferred over sequential maximization of individual agents.

### Pillar 1: ARNI (Angiotensin Receptor-Neprilysin Inhibitor)
*Replaces ACEi/ARB. Do NOT use concomitantly with ACEi/ARB.*

| Drug | Starting Dose | Target Dose | CKD Adjustments & Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Entresto** (sacubitril/valsartan) | 49/51 mg BID | 97/103 mg BID | **eGFR <30 mL/min:** Start 24/26 mg BID.<br>⚠️ **CRITICAL:** Require 36-hour washout if switching from an ACE inhibitor to prevent angioedema. No washout needed if switching from an ARB. |

### Pillar 2: Beta-Blockers
*Only three beta-blockers have proven mortality benefit in HFrEF. Do not substitute with others (e.g., metoprolol tartrate).*

| Drug | Starting Dose | Target Dose | CKD Adjustments & Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Coreg** (carvedilol) | 3.125 mg BID | 25 mg BID (50 mg BID if >85 kg) | No renal adjustment. Preferred if concurrent uncontrolled hypertension (alpha-blocking properties). |
| **Toprol-XL** (metoprolol succinate) | 12.5 - 25 mg Daily | 200 mg Daily | No renal adjustment. Cardioselective; preferred in severe COPD/asthma. |
| **Zebeta** (bisoprolol) | 1.25 mg Daily | 10 mg Daily | **eGFR <40 mL/min:** Max dose 2.5 mg Daily. |

### Pillar 3: Mineralocorticoid Receptor Antagonists (MRA)
*Crucial for mitigating cardiac fibrosis and sudden cardiac death.*

| Drug | Starting Dose | Target Dose | CKD Adjustments & Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Aldactone** (spironolactone) | 12.5 - 25 mg Daily | 50 mg Daily | **eGFR 30-49:** Start 12.5 mg Daily or QOD.<br>**eGFR <30:** Generally avoid per FDA, but may be used with extreme caution by nephrology. |
| **Inspra** (eplerenone) | 25 mg Daily | 50 mg Daily | **eGFR <50:** Contraindicated per FDA label. Less gynecomastia than spironolactone. |

### Pillar 4: SGLT2 Inhibitors
*Provides independent cardiovascular and renal protection. Diuretic-sparing effect.*

| Drug | Starting Dose | Target Dose | CKD Adjustments & Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Farxiga** (dapagliflozin) | 10 mg Daily | 10 mg Daily | **eGFR <25 mL/min:** Do not initiate. If already on therapy and eGFR drops <25, continue until dialysis. |
| **Jardiance** (empagliflozin) | 10 mg Daily | 10 mg Daily | **eGFR <20 mL/min:** Do not initiate. If already on therapy and eGFR drops <20, continue until dialysis. |

## 3. HFpEF (EF ≥50%) & HFmrEF (EF 41-49%) Management
*   **SGLT2i:** **Farxiga** (dapagliflozin) or **Jardiance** (empagliflozin) 10 mg daily are Class 1 recommendations for HFpEF/HFmrEF to reduce hospitalizations.
*   **MRAs:** **Kerendia** (finerenone) 10-20 mg daily (based on FINEARTS-HF) is emerging as a primary therapy for HFmrEF/HFpEF, particularly in patients with concurrent CKD/DKD.
*   **ARNI/ARB/MRAs:** May be considered to decrease hospitalizations, particularly in HFmrEF or those with EF on the lower end of the preserved spectrum (EF 50-55%).

## 4. Diuretic Management & Cardiorenal Syndrome
Loop diuretics are **not** GDMT (no mortality benefit) but are essential for symptom control and allowing GDMT initiation. 

### Loop Diuretic Equivalencies & Pharmacokinetics
| Drug | IV:PO Ratio | Oral Bioavailability | Half-Life | Notes in CKD |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Lasix** (furosemide) | 1:2 | 10-90% (Variable) | 1.5-2 hrs | Erratic absorption in gut edema. |
| **Bumex** (bumetanide) | 1:1 | 80-90% (Reliable) | 1-1.5 hrs | Preferred in severe right heart failure/gut edema. |
| **Demadex** (torsemide) | 1:1 | 80-100% (Reliable) | 3-4 hrs | Longer half-life, reliable absorption. |

### Managing Cardiorenal Syndrome (Balancing Diuresis vs. AKI)
*   **Permissive Creatinine Elevation:** A rise in serum creatinine of up to **30%** during active diuresis is acceptable and does *not* require stopping diuretics or GDMT, provided the patient is clinically decongesting and asymptomatic.
*   **True AKI vs. Hemodynamic Shift:** If BUN/Cr ratio rises but the patient is still volume overloaded, continue diuresis. Venous congestion (high CVP) is a primary driver of worsening renal function in HF.
*   **Diuretic Resistance:** Add a thiazide-like diuretic (e.g., **Zaroxolyn** (metolazone) 2.5-5 mg or **Diuril** (chlorothiazide) 250-500 mg IV) 30 minutes *prior* to the loop diuretic for sequential nephron blockade.

## 5. Additional Therapies (Second-Line / Adjuncts)

*   **BiDil** (hydralazine 37.5 mg / isosorbide dinitrate 20 mg): 
    *   *Indication:* African American patients with HFrEF already optimized on GDMT, or patients of any race who cannot tolerate ARNI/ACEi/ARB due to severe hyperkalemia or advanced CKD (eGFR <15).
    *   *Dosing:* 1 tab TID, titrate to 2 tabs TID.
*   **Corlanor** (ivabradine):
    *   *Indication:* HFrEF, maxed on beta-blockers, in sinus rhythm with resting HR ≥70 bpm.
    *   *Dosing:* 5 mg BID, titrate to 7.5 mg BID. Target HR 50-60 bpm.
*   **Verquvo** (vericiguat):
    *   *Indication:* High-risk HFrEF with recent HF hospitalization or need for IV diuretics.
    *   *Dosing:* 2.5 mg Daily → 5 mg → 10 mg Daily. Avoid if eGFR <15.

## 6. Titration Strategy & Decision Tree

**Goal:** Achieve target doses within 3-6 months of diagnosis.

1.  **START:** Initiate SGLT2i + low-dose Beta-Blocker + low-dose ARNI + low-dose MRA simultaneously (if hemodynamically stable and euvolemic/mildly hypervolemic).
2.  **ESCALATE (Every 1-2 weeks):**
    *   *Step 1:* Maximize Beta-Blocker (if HR >60 and euvolemic).
    *   *Step 2:* Maximize ARNI (if BP >90/60 and eGFR stable).
    *   *Step 3:* Maximize MRA (if K+ <5.0 mEq/L).
3.  **SWITCH:** If on ACEi/ARB, switch to ARNI (remember 36h washout for ACEi).
4.  **STOP/HOLD:** 
    *   Hold ARNI/MRA if K+ >5.5 mEq/L (initiate potassium binders first, see Safety).
    *   Hold Beta-Blocker in *decompensated* cardiogenic shock (do not hold for mild exacerbations).

## 7. Monitoring & Lab Targets

| Parameter | Target / Threshold | Action if off-target |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Potassium (K+)** | 4.0 - 5.0 mEq/L | If >5.0: Review diet, consider K+ binder.<br>If >5.5: Hold MRA/ARNI. |
| **Blood Pressure** | >90/60 mmHg | If asymptomatic hypotension: Continue GDMT. If symptomatic: Reduce loop diuretic first, then ARNI/BB. |
| **Heart Rate** | 50 - 60 bpm | If >70 bpm on max BB: Add Corlanor (ivabradine). |
| **Renal Function** | Cr rise <30% from baseline | If Cr rises >30%: Assess volume status. If dry, reduce diuretic. If wet, continue diuresis. |
| **UACR** | <30 mg/g | Optimize SGLT2i, ARNI, and consider Kerendia (finerenone) if DKD. |
| **NT-proBNP** | >30% reduction from baseline | If elevated/rising: Assess for hidden congestion, non-compliance, or need for advanced therapies. |

## 8. Safety Concerns & Contraindications ⚠️

*   **Hyperkalemia Management:** Do not prematurely discontinue GDMT for mild hyperkalemia (K+ 5.1-5.5). 
    *   *Action:* Initiate a potassium binder: **Lokelma** (sodium zirconium cyclosilicate) 10g TID x 48h then 5-10g Daily, OR **Veltassa** (patiromer) 8.4g Daily.
    *   *[TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED]:* Standardize clinic workflow for rapid K+ rechecks (within 3-5 days) when initiating MRAs or binders.
*   **Hypoglycemia/DKA:** SGLT2i carry a risk of euglycemic DKA. Hold SGLT2i 3 days prior to scheduled surgeries or during acute severe illness/fasting.
*   **Hypotension:** Asymptomatic BP 90-100 systolic is acceptable in HFrEF. Do not stop GDMT for "low numbers" unless the patient has orthostatic symptoms, syncope, or hypoperfusion.

## 9. Nephrology vs. Cardiology Co-Management

**When to Refer to/Consult Cardiology:**
*   Refractory volume overload despite sequential nephron blockade.
*   Need for inotropes (milrinone, dobutamine).
*   Evaluation for advanced therapies (LVAD, cardiac transplant).
*   Recurrent ICD shocks or sustained ventricular arrhythmias.
*   Severe valvular disease requiring intervention.
*   *[TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED]:* Define local cardiology groups for expedited referral pathways.

## 10. Patient Education Talking Points

*   **Daily Weights:** "Weigh yourself every morning after urinating and before eating. Call the clinic if you gain **more than 2-3 lbs in one day or 5 lbs in a week**."
*   **Sodium Restriction:** "Limit sodium to **less than 2,000 mg (2 grams) per day**. Avoid canned soups, processed meats, and fast food."
*   **Fluid Management:** "Unless instructed otherwise, limit total fluids to **1.5 to 2 Liters per day** (especially if you have low sodium levels or severe swelling)."
*   **Medication Adherence:** "These medications (Entresto, Farxiga, etc.) are designed to make your heart stronger and help you live longer, not just to make you feel better today. Do not stop them even if you feel perfectly fine."

## 11. ICD-10 & CPT Coding

**ICD-10 Codes:**
*   **I13.0** - Hypertensive heart and chronic kidney disease with heart failure and stage 1-4 CKD, or unspecified CKD.
*   **I13.11** - Hypertensive heart and CKD without heart failure, with stage 5 CKD, or ESRD.
*   **I13.2** - Hypertensive heart and CKD with heart failure and with stage 5 CKD, or ESRD.
*   **I50.20 / I50.21 / I50.22 / I50.23** - Unspecified / Acute / Chronic / Acute on chronic systolic (HFrEF) heart failure.
*   **I50.30 / I50.31 / I50.32 / I50.33** - Unspecified / Acute / Chronic / Acute on chronic diastolic (HFpEF) heart failure.

**CPT Codes for Complex Chronic Care:**
*   **99490 / 99439:** Chronic Care Management (CCM) services (first 20 mins / additional 20 mins).
