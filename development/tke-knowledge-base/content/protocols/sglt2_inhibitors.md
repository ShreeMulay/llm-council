# SGLT2 Inhibitors in Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)
**The Kidney Experts (TKE) Clinical Reference Guide**

## 1. Guideline Recommendations & Initiation Criteria
Based on the **KDIGO 2024 Clinical Practice Guideline for the Evaluation and Management of CKD** and the **ADA 2024 Standards of Care**, SGLT2 inhibitors are recommended as **first-line therapy** (alongside maximum tolerated ACEi/ARB) to slow CKD progression and reduce cardiovascular risk.

### Initiation Criteria
*   **eGFR Threshold:** Initiate when eGFR is **≥ 20 mL/min/1.73 m²**.
*   **Target Populations:**
    *   CKD with Type 2 Diabetes (T2DM).
    *   CKD without diabetes with albuminuria (target UACR < 30 mg/g, though benefits are seen across the albuminuria spectrum).
    *   CKD with Heart Failure (HFrEF or HFpEF), regardless of diabetes status.
*   **Continuation Threshold:** Once initiated, **CONTINUE therapy even if eGFR drops below 20 mL/min/1.73 m²**. Discontinue only upon initiation of dialysis or kidney transplantation.

## 2. Available Agents & Dosing
Dosing for CKD and Heart Failure indications is simplified. **No titration is required, and no dose adjustments are needed for declining eGFR once therapy is initiated.**

| Drug Name | Standard CKD Dose | eGFR Initiation Threshold | eGFR Continuation Threshold |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Farxiga (dapagliflozin)** | 10 mg PO daily | ≥ 20 mL/min/1.73 m² | Continue until dialysis/transplant |
| **Jardiance (empagliflozin)** | 10 mg PO daily | ≥ 20 mL/min/1.73 m² | Continue until dialysis/transplant |
| **Invokana (canagliflozin)** | 100 mg PO daily* | ≥ 30 mL/min/1.73 m² | Continue until dialysis/transplant |

*\*Note: Invokana (canagliflozin) is less commonly used at TKE for non-diabetic CKD due to trial design (CREDENCE was T2DM only), but remains a valid option for diabetic nephropathy.*

## 3. The Expected eGFR "Dip" (CRITICAL CLINICAL PEARL)
SGLT2 inhibitors cause afferent arteriole vasoconstriction, reducing intraglomerular pressure. This leads to an acute, reversible hemodynamic drop in eGFR.

*   **Expected Decline:** A drop of **3 to 5 mL/min/1.73 m²** (or up to 10-15% from baseline) within the first 2-4 weeks is **EXPECTED and indicates the drug is working.**
*   **Action:** **DO NOT STOP THERAPY.** The initial dip correlates with long-term kidney preservation.
*   **When to investigate:** If eGFR drops by >30% from baseline, evaluate for volume depletion, NSAID use, or concurrent acute illness.

---

## 4. Clinical Decision Tree: Start, Escalate, Switch, Stop

*   **START:** Patient has eGFR ≥ 20 and UACR ≥ 200 mg/g (or T2DM, or Heart Failure).
*   **ESCALATE:** SGLT2i dosing is fixed (10 mg). To further reduce albuminuria (Target UACR < 30 mg/g), escalate by adding a non-steroidal MRA (e.g., Kerendia (finerenone)) or GLP-1 RA if indicated.
*   **SWITCH:** If a patient experiences recurrent, severe genital mycotic infections on Farxiga (dapagliflozin), a switch to Jardiance (empagliflozin) is rarely effective (class effect). Consider switching to alternative renoprotective agents (GLP-1 RA or nsMRA).
*   **STOP:** 
    *   Initiation of maintenance dialysis.
    *   Kidney transplantation.
    *   Recurrent euglycemic DKA.
    *   Fournier's gangrene (absolute contraindication for future use).

## 6. Safety Concerns, Contraindications & Drug Interactions

### 🚨 Prominent Safety Flags
*   **Genital Mycotic Infections:** Most common adverse effect (approx. 5-10%). More common in females and uncircumcised males. Easily treated with topical or oral antifungals (e.g., fluconazole) without stopping the SGLT2i.
*   **Euglycemic Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA):** Rare but life-threatening. Blood glucose may be normal (< 250 mg/dL). Suspect if patient has nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. Check serum ketones/beta-hydroxybutyrate.
*   **Volume Depletion:** Risk of orthostatic hypotension, especially in the elderly or those on high-dose loop diuretics.

### Contraindications
*   Type 1 Diabetes (due to high DKA risk; off-label use requires specialized endocrinology management).
*   Polycystic Kidney Disease (PCKD) or patients requiring intensive immunosuppression for glomerular disease (excluded from major trials; use requires attending nephrologist approval).
*   Prior history of SGLT2i-induced DKA or Fournier's gangrene.

### Drug Interactions
*   **Insulin & Sulfonylureas (e.g., glipizide, glimepiride):** High risk of hypoglycemia. **Action:** Proactively reduce insulin or sulfonylurea dose by 10-20% upon SGLT2i initiation.
*   **Loop/Thiazide Diuretics:** Additive diuretic effect. **Action:** Consider reducing diuretic dose if the patient is euvolemic or prone to hypotension at baseline.

## 7. Perioperative & Sick Day Management
*   **Perioperative Hold:** To prevent euglycemic DKA, the FDA recommends holding SGLT2 inhibitors prior to major surgery.
    *   Hold **Farxiga (dapagliflozin)** and **Jardiance (empagliflozin)** for **3 days** prior to surgery.
    *   Hold **Invokana (canagliflozin)** for **4 days** prior to surgery.
*   **Sick Day Rules (SADMANS Protocol):** Instruct patients to HOLD their SGLT2 inhibitor during acute illness involving vomiting, diarrhea, or inability to maintain oral hydration.

## 8. Key Clinical Trials & Evidence Base (For Clinician Reference)
*   **DAPA-CKD (2020):** Farxiga (dapagliflozin). Showed a **39% reduction** in the primary composite endpoint (≥50% eGFR decline, ESKD, or renal/CV death). Included both diabetic and non-diabetic CKD.
*   **EMPA-KIDNEY (2022):** Jardiance (empagliflozin). Showed a **28% reduction** in kidney disease progression or CV death. Notably included patients with lower levels of albuminuria and lower eGFRs.
*   **CREDENCE (2019):** Invokana (canagliflozin). Showed a **30% reduction** in the primary composite endpoint specifically in patients with T2DM and albuminuric CKD.
*   **Cardiovascular Benefits:** DAPA-HF, DELIVER, EMPEROR-Reduced, and EMPEROR-Preserved demonstrated profound reductions in heart failure hospitalizations and CV mortality across the LVEF spectrum (both HFrEF and HFpEF).

## 9. Patient Education Talking Points
When prescribing, ensure the patient understands the following:
*   **The "Why":** *"This medication protects your kidneys and heart. It acts like a shield to slow down kidney damage, even if you don't have diabetes."*
*   **The eGFR Dip:** *"Your next lab draw might show a slight drop in your kidney function number. This is normal, expected, and means the drug is taking pressure off your kidneys. Do not let another doctor stop this medication without talking to us first."*
*   **Hygiene:** *"Because this drug flushes sugar out through your urine, yeast infections can happen. Wash your genital area daily with soap and water, and dry completely."*
*   **Hydration:** *"You may urinate a little more often. Drink water when you are thirsty to stay hydrated."*
*   **Sick Days:** *"If you get the stomach bug, are vomiting, or have severe diarrhea, stop taking this pill until you are eating and drinking normally again."*

## 10. Billing, ICD-10 Codes & Prior Authorization Tips

### Relevant ICD-10 Codes
To establish medical necessity for CKD/Heart Failure indications (especially for non-diabetics):
*   **N18.2 - N18.4:** Chronic kidney disease, stages 2 through 4
*   **E11.22:** Type 2 diabetes mellitus with diabetic chronic kidney disease
*   **I50.20 - I50.40:** Heart failure (systolic/diastolic, unspecified)
*   **R80.9:** Proteinuria, unspecified (use to justify albuminuria indication)

### CPT Coding Note
Counseling on SGLT2i initiation, sick day rules, and expected eGFR changes supports billing based on **Medical Decision Making (MDM) complexity** or **Time**. 
*   *High complexity* (99215) may be supported if managing multiple drug interactions (e.g., adjusting insulin/diuretics) alongside severe CKD progression.
*   Consider Chronic Care Management (CCM) codes (99490, 99491) for staff time spent on PA acquisition and follow-up lab monitoring.
