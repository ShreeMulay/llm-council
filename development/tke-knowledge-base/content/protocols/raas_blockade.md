# CLINICAL REFERENCE: RAAS Blockade in Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)

## 1. Guidelines & Indications
Based on the **KDIGO 2024 Clinical Practice Guideline for the Evaluation and Management of CKD**, Renin-Angiotensin-Aldosterone System (RAAS) blockade with an Angiotensin-Converting Enzyme inhibitor (ACEi) or Angiotensin II Receptor Blocker (ARB) is the cornerstone of renoprotective therapy.

### Primary Indications
*   **Diabetic Kidney Disease (DKD):** Recommended for all patients with diabetes, hypertension, and albuminuria (UACR ≥30 mg/g).
*   **Non-Diabetic CKD:** Recommended for patients with hypertension and severely increased albuminuria (UACR ≥300 mg/g). Suggested for patients with moderately increased albuminuria (UACR 30-299 mg/g).
*   **Hypertension in CKD:** First-line antihypertensive agent for patients with CKD and hypertension, regardless of albuminuria status (per ACC/AHA and KDIGO guidelines).

### Clinical Targets
*   **Urine Albumin-Creatinine Ratio (UACR):** Target **<30 mg/g** (or >30% reduction from baseline).
*   **Blood Pressure:** Target systolic BP **<120 mm Hg** (when tolerated, using standardized office BP measurement).

## 3. Initiation & Titration Protocol
The renoprotective (anti-proteinuric) benefits of RAAS blockade are **dose-dependent**. The goal is the *maximum tolerated dose*, not just the dose required for blood pressure control.

### Titration Decision Tree
1.  **START:** Initiate at the lowest starting dose (adjust for eGFR if <30 mL/min/1.73m²).
2.  **MONITOR:** Check basic metabolic panel (BMP) 1-2 weeks after initiation.
3.  **ESCALATE:** If BP allows (SBP >110), potassium is <5.0 mEq/L, and creatinine rise is <30%, double the dose every 2-4 weeks until the maximum target dose is reached.
4.  **MAINTAIN:** Once at max tolerated dose, monitor BMP and UACR every 3-6 months.

### eGFR Considerations (Advanced CKD)
*   **Do NOT routinely stop RAAS blockade for declining eGFR.** 
*   Per the STOP-ACEi trial, discontinuing ACEi/ARB in advanced CKD (eGFR <30) does *not* improve kidney function or delay ESKD.
*   Continue therapy unless the patient develops refractory hyperkalemia, symptomatic hypotension, or an acute creatinine rise >30% that does not stabilize.

## 4. Monitoring & Acceptable Creatinine Rise
Hemodynamic changes in the glomerulus (efferent arteriole vasodilation) cause an expected, benign drop in eGFR upon initiation.

*   **Monitoring Schedule:** Check Serum Creatinine (SCr) and Potassium (K+) **1 to 2 weeks** after starting AND after every dose increase.
*   **The 30% Rule:** A rise in SCr of **up to 30% from baseline** is expected and acceptable. 
    *   *Example:* Baseline SCr 1.5 mg/dL → rises to 1.9 mg/dL (26% increase). **Action:** Continue current dose.
    *   *If SCr rises >30%:* Evaluate for volume depletion, NSAID use, or bilateral renal artery stenosis. Reduce dose or hold temporarily.

## 5. Hyperkalemia Management
Hyperkalemia is the most common barrier to achieving maximum RAAS blockade. KDIGO 2024 recommends treating hyperkalemia to *enable* continuation of RAASi rather than immediately stopping the RAASi.

### Hyperkalemia Decision Tree
*   **K+ 5.1 - 5.5 mEq/L:** 
    *   Continue RAASi dose. 
    *   Initiate low-potassium diet education. 
    *   Review meds (stop NSAIDs, TMP-SMX, OTC supplements). 
    *   Consider adding a non-potassium sparing diuretic (e.g., Lasix (furosemide) or chlorthalidone) if volume overloaded.
*   **K+ 5.6 - 5.9 mEq/L:** 
    *   Hold or reduce RAASi dose temporarily OR initiate a potassium binder to maintain RAASi.
*   **K+ ≥ 6.0 mEq/L:** 
    *   **HOLD RAASi immediately.** 
    *   Evaluate for acute ECG changes and manage acute hyperkalemia per protocol.

## 6. Special Clinical Scenarios & Safety Flags

### 🛑 DUAL RAAS BLOCKADE IS CONTRAINDICATED
*   Combining an ACEi + ARB, or ACEi/ARB + Tekturna (aliskiren) is **strictly contraindicated**.
*   *Evidence:* The ONTARGET and VA NEPHRON-D trials demonstrated that dual therapy provides no additional cardiovascular or renal benefit but significantly increases the risk of Acute Kidney Injury (AKI) and severe hyperkalemia.

### 🛑 PREGNANCY
*   ACEi and ARBs are **absolute contraindications** in pregnancy due to severe fetal renal toxicity and teratogenicity (oligohydramnios, fetal skull hypoplasia).
*   *Action:* Counsel all females of childbearing potential. If a patient is planning pregnancy, switch to safe alternatives (e.g., Procardia (nifedipine), Trandate (labetalol)). Stop immediately if pregnancy is detected.

### ⚠️ Transitioning to Entresto (sacubitril/valsartan)
*   **Indication:** Patients with CKD and Heart Failure with reduced Ejection Fraction (HFrEF).
*   **Safety Rule:** If switching from an ACEi to Entresto, you **MUST mandate a 36-hour washout period** before starting Entresto to prevent life-threatening angioedema.
*   *Note:* No washout period is required if switching from an ARB to Entresto.

## 8. Patient Education Talking Points
When initiating or titrating ACEi/ARBs, clinical staff should cover the following with the patient:

*   **The "Kidney Shield" Concept:** *"We are prescribing this medication not just for your blood pressure, but to act as a shield for your kidneys. It reduces pressure inside the kidney filters and lowers protein in your urine."*
*   **The Expected Lab Changes:** *"When you get your blood drawn next week, your kidney numbers might look slightly worse. This is a normal, expected reaction as the medication relaxes your kidney filters. Do not stop the medication unless we tell you to."*
*   **Potassium Awareness:** *"This medication can cause your body to hold onto potassium. Avoid salt substitutes (which are made of potassium) and be mindful of high-potassium foods like bananas, potatoes, and tomatoes."*
*   **Sick Day Rules:** *"If you become severely ill with vomiting, diarrhea, or cannot drink fluids, **hold this medication** and call our office. Taking it while severely dehydrated can harm your kidneys."*
*   **Pregnancy Warning (if applicable):** *"This medication is not safe for a developing baby. If you plan to become pregnant or find out you are pregnant, stop the medication and call us immediately."*
