# Clinical Reference: Finerenone and MRAs in CKD

**Disclaimer:** *This document is a clinical reference for healthcare professionals at The Kidney Experts (TKE). It does not constitute patient-specific medical advice. Clinical judgment must be applied to individual patient scenarios.*

---

## 1. Overview & Mechanism of Action

Mineralocorticoid receptor overactivation drives inflammation and fibrosis in the kidneys and heart. While traditional steroidal MRAs have been used for decades, their use in advanced Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) has been limited by hyperkalemia and off-target side effects.

**Kerendia (finerenone)** is a novel, nonsteroidal, selective mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist (MRA). 

### Nonsteroidal vs. Steroidal MRAs
*   **Structure:** Kerendia (finerenone) has a bulky, nonsteroidal structure that alters receptor binding, resulting in a distinct gene expression profile compared to steroidal MRAs like Aldactone (spironolactone) and Inspra (eplerenone).
*   **Tissue Distribution:** Kerendia (finerenone) distributes equally between the heart and kidneys, whereas steroidal MRAs accumulate preferentially in the kidneys (increasing hyperkalemia risk).
*   **Half-life:** Kerendia (finerenone) has a short half-life (~2-3 hours) with no active metabolites, allowing for rapid clearance and faster resolution of hyperkalemia if it occurs.
*   **Side Effects:** Lacks affinity for androgen and progesterone receptors, virtually eliminating the risk of gynecomastia and impotence seen with Aldactone (spironolactone).

## 2. Guideline Positioning & Eligibility Criteria

### KDIGO 2024 Positioning
The **KDIGO 2024 Clinical Practice Guideline for the Evaluation and Management of CKD** positions Kerendia (finerenone) as a foundational pillar of therapy for diabetic kidney disease. 
*   **Recommendation:** Initiate a nonsteroidal MRA for patients with Type 2 Diabetes (T2D) and CKD who meet eligibility criteria, as an **add-on** to maximized Renin-Angiotensin System (RAS) blockade (ACEi/ARB) and an SGLT2 inhibitor (e.g., Farxiga (dapagliflozin) or Jardiance (empagliflozin)).

### Patient Eligibility Criteria
Initiate Kerendia (finerenone) when the patient meets **ALL** of the following:
*   Diagnosis of Type 2 Diabetes (T2D)
*   Diagnosis of CKD with eGFR ≥25 mL/min/1.73 m²
*   Persistent albuminuria: UACR ≥30 mg/g (Target: reduce UACR to <30 mg/g)
*   Baseline serum potassium (K+) <5.0 mEq/L
*   Currently on maximally tolerated FDA-approved dose of an ACEi or ARB

## 3. Key Clinical Trials

The approval and guideline positioning of Kerendia (finerenone) are based on a robust clinical trial program:

*   **FIDELIO-DKD (2020):** Evaluated patients with advanced CKD and T2D. Demonstrated an **18% relative risk reduction** in the primary kidney composite endpoint (kidney failure, sustained ≥40% decrease in eGFR, or renal death).
*   **FIGARO-DKD (2021):** Evaluated patients with earlier stage CKD and T2D. Demonstrated a **13% relative risk reduction** in the primary cardiovascular composite endpoint (CV death, non-fatal MI, non-fatal stroke, or hospitalization for heart failure).
*   **FIDELITY Analysis (2021):** A pre-specified pooled analysis of FIDELIO and FIGARO (n > 13,000) confirming robust, consistent benefits across the spectrum of CKD stages 1-4 in reducing both CV events and kidney disease progression.
*   **FINEARTS-HF (2024):** Evaluated Kerendia (finerenone) in patients with heart failure with mildly reduced or preserved ejection fraction (HFmrEF/HFpEF, LVEF ≥40%). Demonstrated a significant reduction in worsening heart failure events and CV death, expanding the clinical utility of finerenone in the cardiorenal space.

## 4. Dosing & Administration

Dosing is strictly guided by eGFR and serum potassium levels. 

### Initial Dosing
| Baseline eGFR (mL/min/1.73 m²) | Starting Dose of Kerendia (finerenone) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **≥ 60** | 20 mg orally once daily |
| **25 to 59** | 10 mg orally once daily |
| **< 25** | Do not initiate |

### Titration Schedule
*   **Target Dose:** 20 mg once daily.
*   **Timing:** Evaluate for uptitration 4 weeks after initiation.
*   **Criteria:** If starting at 10 mg, increase to 20 mg daily **ONLY IF** serum K+ is ≤4.8 mEq/L and eGFR remains stable.

## 6. Stacking with SGLT2 Inhibitors

KDIGO 2024 strongly advocates for the concurrent use of Kerendia (finerenone) and an SGLT2 inhibitor (e.g., Farxiga (dapagliflozin) or Jardiance (empagliflozin)).

*   **Synergistic Benefit:** SGLT2is reduce intraglomerular pressure (hemodynamic benefit), while finerenone reduces tissue inflammation and fibrosis. Together, they provide additive renoprotection and UACR reduction.
*   **Potassium Mitigation:** SGLT2 inhibitors promote distal tubular flow and mild kaliuresis. Clinical trial data (FIDELITY) shows that concurrent use of an SGLT2i **significantly reduces the risk of hyperkalemia** associated with Kerendia (finerenone).
*   **Clinical Pearl:** If a patient is not already on an SGLT2i, consider initiating the SGLT2i first, establishing tolerability, and then adding Kerendia (finerenone) to minimize hyperkalemia risk.

## 7. Comparison: Finerenone vs. Steroidal MRAs

| Feature | Kerendia (finerenone) | Aldactone (spironolactone) | Inspra (eplerenone) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **MRA Type** | Nonsteroidal, highly selective | Steroidal, non-selective | Steroidal, selective |
| **Half-life** | 2–3 hours | 1.4 hours (active metabolites 10-35 hrs) | 4–6 hours |
| **Hyperkalemia Risk** | Moderate (lower than spironolactone) | High | Moderate-High |
| **Gynecomastia Risk** | None | High (~10%) | Low |
| **Primary CKD Use** | T2D with albuminuric CKD (FDA approved) | Resistant hypertension, nephrotic edema | Post-MI HF, resistant hypertension |
| **BP Lowering Effect**| Minimal (not a primary antihypertensive) | Potent | Moderate |

**When to use which:**
*   Use **Kerendia (finerenone)** for targeted reduction of CKD progression and CV events in diabetic kidney disease.
*   Use **Aldactone (spironolactone)** when potent blood pressure reduction is needed for resistant hypertension, or for volume management in nephrotic syndrome/cirrhosis.
*   Use **Inspra (eplerenone)** if spironolactone causes gynecomastia but a steroidal MRA is still desired for BP/HF management.

## 8. Safety, Contraindications & Drug Interactions

### ⚠️ Contraindications
*   Concomitant use with **strong CYP3A4 inhibitors**.
*   Patients with adrenal insufficiency.

### ⚠️ Drug Interactions (CYP3A4 Pathway)
Kerendia (finerenone) is primarily metabolized by CYP3A4.
*   **Strong CYP3A4 Inhibitors (AVOID/CONTRAINDICATED):** Ketoconazole, itraconazole, ritonavir, clarithromycin, grapefruit/grapefruit juice. *Action: Do not co-administer.*
*   **Moderate/Weak CYP3A4 Inhibitors (CAUTION):** Erythromycin, diltiazem, verapamil, amiodarone. *Action: Monitor serum K+ more frequently during initiation and dose changes.*
*   **Strong CYP3A4 Inducers (AVOID):** Rifampin, carbamazepine, phenytoin, St. John's Wort. *Action: Avoid use, as they significantly decrease finerenone efficacy.*
