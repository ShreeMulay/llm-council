# GLOMERULONEPHRITIS (GN) IMMUNOSUPPRESSION & MANAGEMENT
**Based on KDIGO 2021 Clinical Practice Guideline for the Management of Glomerular Diseases**

## 1. General Principles & KDIGO 2021 Framework
The KDIGO 2021 guidelines emphasize that immunosuppression should be guided by a **specific histologic diagnosis** via kidney biopsy, rather than clinical syndromes alone. 
*   **Decision to Treat:** Weigh the risk of end-stage kidney disease (ESKD) against the risk of immunosuppression toxicity (infection, malignancy, metabolic complications).
*   **Foundation of Care:** Maximum tolerated supportive care (RAAS blockade, SGLT2 inhibitors, BP control) is required for *all* GN patients before or concurrent with immunosuppression.

## 2. IgA Nephropathy (IgAN)
**Goal:** Prevent progressive CKD. Target BP < 120/80 mmHg. Target proteinuria < 1 g/day (ideally < 0.3 g/day).

### Step 1: Maximum Supportive Care (First-Line for ALL)
*   **RAAS Blockade:** Maximize ACEi or ARB to maximum tolerated dose.
*   **SGLT2 Inhibitors:** Add Farxiga (dapagliflozin) 10 mg daily or Jardiance (empagliflozin) 10 mg daily (per DAPA-CKD/EMPA-KIDNEY) if eGFR ≥ 20 mL/min/1.73m².

### Step 2: Disease-Specific & Immunosuppressive Therapies
*Evaluate after 3-6 months of optimized supportive care. If proteinuria remains > 0.75 - 1.0 g/day, escalate therapy.*

#### Sparsentan (Filspari) - *PROTECT Trial*
*   **Mechanism:** Dual endothelin (ETA) and angiotensin II type 1 (AT1) receptor antagonist. First non-immunosuppressive disease-specific therapy.
*   **Dosing:** 200 mg PO daily x 14 days, then increase to 400 mg PO daily. 
*   **Decision Tree:** Start if high risk of progression despite max supportive care. **STOP** ACEi/ARB before starting (do not combine).
*   **Safety/Monitoring:** 
    *   **REMS Program:** Requires monthly LFT monitoring (hepatotoxicity risk) and pregnancy testing (embryo-fetal toxicity).
    *   Avoid if eGFR < 30 mL/min/1.73m².

#### Targeted-Release Budesonide (Tarpeyo / Nefecon) - *NEFIGARD Trial*
*   **Mechanism:** Gut-targeted corticosteroid acting on Peyer's patches to reduce mucosal production of galactose-deficient IgA1. Less systemic absorption.
*   **Dosing:** 16 mg PO daily (take whole, do not crush) for 9 months, then taper/stop.
*   **Decision Tree:** Start if UPCR ≥ 1.5 g/g and eGFR ≥ 35 mL/min/1.73m². 

#### Systemic Corticosteroids - *TESTING Trial Protocol*
*   **Decision Tree:** Reserve for patients with progressive disease/high risk of ESKD who cannot access or fail Tarpeyo/Filspari.
*   **Dosing:** Reduced-dose protocol is preferred to minimize toxicity.
    *   Methylprednisolone 0.4 mg/kg/day (max 32 mg/day) for 2 months.
    *   Taper by 4 mg/day every month over 6-9 months.
*   **Safety:** MUST prescribe PCP prophylaxis (e.g., Bactrim (sulfamethoxazole/trimethoprim) SS daily) and GI prophylaxis (PPI/H2 blocker).

## 3. Membranous Nephropathy (MN)
**Goal:** Induce immunologic remission (PLA2R depletion) followed by clinical remission (proteinuria < 0.3 g/day).

### Anti-PLA2R Antibody-Guided Treatment
*   **Monitoring:** Check anti-PLA2R antibodies at baseline, then every 3-6 months. 
*   **Decision Tree:** Immunologic remission (PLA2R disappearance) precedes clinical remission (proteinuria reduction) by months. Do not escalate therapy if PLA2R is negative but proteinuria persists (wait for healing).

### First-Line: Rituximab (Rituxan) - *MENTOR Trial*
*   **Efficacy:** Non-inferior to cyclosporine at 12 months, superior for sustained remission at 24 months.
*   **Dosing:** 1,000 mg IV on Days 1 and 15 (Rheumatology dosing) OR 375 mg/m² IV weekly x 4 weeks.
*   **Pre-medication:** Tylenol (acetaminophen), Benadryl (diphenhydramine), and Solu-Medrol (methylprednisolone) 100mg IV.

### Alternative / Severe Disease: Cyclophosphamide (Cytoxan)
*   **Protocol:** Modified Ponticelli (Alternating months x 6 months).
    *   *Months 1, 3, 5:* IV Solu-Medrol 1g x 3 days, then PO Prednisone 0.5 mg/kg/day x 27 days.
    *   *Months 2, 4, 6:* PO Cytoxan (cyclophosphamide) 2-2.5 mg/kg/day.
*   **Decision Tree:** Reserve for very high-risk patients (rapid eGFR decline, life-threatening nephrotic syndrome) due to high toxicity (infertility, malignancy, infection). Adjust Cytoxan dose for eGFR < 30.

### Calcineurin Inhibitors (CNIs)
*   **Drugs:** Prograf (tacrolimus) 0.05 mg/kg/day or Neoral (cyclosporine) 3-5 mg/kg/day.
*   **Decision Tree:** Alternative for moderate risk. **Warning:** High relapse rate upon discontinuation. Taper very slowly over 6-12 months once remission is achieved.

## 4. Lupus Nephritis (LN) - Class III, IV, V
**Goal:** Complete renal response (UPCR < 0.5 g/g, normal/stable eGFR) by 12 months.

### Induction Therapy (Choose One Base + Consider Add-ons)
| Regimen | Dosing | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Mycophenolate (CellCept)** | 2 to 3 g PO daily (divided BID) | Preferred for most, especially Hispanic/African American patients. Teratogenic. |
| **IV Cyclophosphamide (Cytoxan)** | *Euro-Lupus:* 500 mg IV every 2 weeks x 6 doses | Preferred for severe crescentic disease or non-adherence. Less gonadal toxicity than NIH protocol. |

*Note: All induction regimens include IV pulse steroids followed by a rapid PO prednisone taper (target ≤ 5-7.5 mg/day by 3-6 months).*

### Novel Add-On Therapies (For Refractory or High-Risk Disease)
#### Voclosporin (Lupkynis) - *AURORA Trial*
*   **Mechanism:** Novel CNI with predictable pharmacokinetics (no trough monitoring required).
*   **Dosing:** 23.7 mg PO BID. Added to CellCept + low-dose steroids.
*   **Decision Tree:** Add for faster proteinuria reduction. 
*   **Safety:** Do not start if baseline eGFR < 45. Monitor BP and eGFR closely. If eGFR drops > 20% from baseline, reduce dose to 15.8 mg BID.

#### Belimumab (Benlysta) - *BLISS-LN Trial*
*   **Mechanism:** BLyS-specific inhibitor (reduces B-cell survival).
*   **Dosing:** 10 mg/kg IV on Days 0, 14, 28, then every 4 weeks.
*   **Decision Tree:** Add to standard induction to reduce risk of renal flares and improve overall response.

### Maintenance Therapy
*   **First-line:** CellCept (mycophenolate) 1-2 g/day.
*   **Alternative (or for pregnancy):** Imuran (azathioprine) 1.5-2 mg/kg/day.
*   **Duration:** Minimum 3-5 years. Do not taper until complete clinical and serologic remission.

## 5. ANCA-Associated Vasculitis (AAV)
**Goal:** Rapid induction of remission to prevent irreversible nephron loss.

### Induction Therapy
*   **Rituximab (Rituxan) - *RAVE Trial*:** 375 mg/m² IV weekly x 4 weeks. Preferred for most patients, especially relapsing disease or PR3-ANCA.
*   **Cyclophosphamide (Cytoxan):** IV pulse or PO daily. Preferred for severe life-threatening disease (e.g., eGFR < 15, requiring dialysis). Often transitioned to Rituxan for maintenance.

### Steroid-Sparing: Avacopan (Tavneos) - *ADVOCATE Trial*
*   **Mechanism:** Oral C5a receptor inhibitor.
*   **Dosing:** 30 mg PO BID.
*   **Decision Tree:** Use to replace or drastically reduce systemic glucocorticoids during induction. Highly recommended for patients with high steroid-toxicity risk (diabetes, osteoporosis, severe obesity).
*   **Monitoring:** Monitor LFTs (hepatotoxicity risk).

### Maintenance Therapy
*   **Rituximab (Rituxan) - *MAINRITSAN Trial*:** 500 mg IV every 6 months for 18-24 months. Superior to azathioprine for relapse prevention.
*   **Alternative:** Imuran (azathioprine) 2 mg/kg/day.

### Plasma Exchange (PLEX) - *PEXIVAS Trial*
*   **Decision Tree:** Routine PLEX is **NO LONGER** recommended for dialysis-dependent AAV. 
*   **Indications:** Reserve for concurrent anti-GBM disease or severe pulmonary alveolar hemorrhage with hypoxemia.

## 6. Focal Segmental Glomerulosclerosis (FSGS)
*Crucial: Differentiate Primary (immunologic) from Secondary (adaptive/genetic) FSGS. Immunosuppression is ONLY for Primary FSGS.*

### Primary FSGS Treatment
*   **First-Line:** High-dose Corticosteroids.
    *   Prednisone 1 mg/kg/day (max 80 mg/day) for 4 to 16 weeks.
    *   If remission occurs, taper slowly over 6 months. Partial response rates vary widely.
*   **Steroid-Resistant or Steroid-Dependent:**
    *   **CNIs:** Prograf (tacrolimus) target trough 4-8 ng/mL OR Neoral (cyclosporine) target trough 100-175 ng/mL. Treat for at least 4-6 months before declaring failure.
    *   **Rituximab (Rituxan):** Emerging data supports use in *steroid-dependent* FSGS to maintain remission off steroids.

## 7. Pre-Immunosuppression Screening & General Monitoring
**[TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED]:** Standardized lab panel for pre-immunosuppression screening.

### Infection Screening (MANDATORY before starting)
*   **Hepatitis:** HBsAg, HBcAb (total), HBsAb, HCV Ab. (If HBcAb+, monitor HBV DNA or consult GI for prophylaxis).
*   **Tuberculosis:** QuantiFERON-TB Gold or T-SPOT.
*   **HIV:** HIV 1/2 Ag/Ab screen.
*   **Syphilis:** RPR (especially for membranous).

### Vaccination Protocol
*   Update all vaccines **BEFORE** starting immunotherapy (ideally 2-4 weeks prior).
*   **Required:** Pneumococcal (PCV20), Influenza (injectable), COVID-19, Shingrix (recombinant zoster).
*   **CONTRAINDICATION:** **NO LIVE VACCINES** (e.g., MMR, Yellow Fever, live attenuated flu) while on immunosuppression and for 6 months after stopping.

### Routine Monitoring on Therapy
*   **Labs:** CBC with diff, CMP (monitor eGFR, K+, LFTs), Urinalysis, UPCR/UACR.
*   **Rituximab specific:** Quantitative Immunoglobulins (IgG) prior to each dose. If IgG < 400 mg/dL and patient has recurrent infections, consider holding dose or IVIG replacement.
*   **Infection Surveillance:** High index of suspicion for opportunistic infections (PCP, CMV, fungal).

## 8. Billing & Coding Reference
*Ensure documentation supports medical necessity for high-cost biologics.*

**Common ICD-10 Codes:**

## 9. Patient Education Talking Points
*   **The "Why":** "Your immune system is confused and attacking your kidney filters. These medications calm the immune system to protect your kidneys from failing."
*   **Infection Risk:** "Because we are lowering your immune system, you are at higher risk for infections. Call the clinic immediately for any fever > 100.4°F, chills, or productive cough. Do not wait."
*   **Vaccines:** "Family members should get vaccinated to protect you. You cannot receive any 'live' vaccines while on these medications."
*   **Adherence:** "Do not stop these medications suddenly, even if you feel better. Stopping suddenly can cause a severe flare of your kidney disease or a dangerous drop in blood pressure (if on steroids)."
*   **BP & Swelling:** "Monitor your blood pressure at home. Call us if your BP is consistently > 130/80 or if you notice sudden weight gain or swelling in your legs."
