## 1. POTASSIUM MANAGEMENT

### 1.1 Targets and Pathophysiology
*   **Normal Range:** 3.5 – 5.0 mEq/L
*   **CKD-Specific Target:** Maintain within normal range.
*   **Clinical Concern:** > 5.5 mEq/L (Moderate to Severe Hyperkalemia).
*   **Causes of Hyperkalemia in CKD:**
    *   Reduced renal excretion (eGFR < 30 mL/min/1.73m²).
    *   RAAS blockade: ACE inhibitors, ARBs, MRAs (e.g., Aldactone (spironolactone), Kerendia (finerenone)), ARNIs.
    *   Metabolic acidosis (causes extracellular potassium shift).
    *   Dietary indiscretion (high potassium intake).
    *   Medications: NSAIDs, Calcineurin inhibitors (tacrolimus, cyclosporine), Bactrim (trimethoprim/sulfamethoxazole).

### 1.3 Chronic Hyperkalemia Management & Enabling RAAS Blockade
Per KDIGO 2024 guidelines, **do not prematurely discontinue RAAS inhibitors** due to hyperkalemia. Instead, utilize dietary restriction, correct acidosis, add loop diuretics, and initiate gastrointestinal potassium binders to enable life-saving RAASi therapy.

#### Potassium Binder Comparison & Dosing
| Drug | Lokelma (sodium zirconium cyclosilicate / SZC) | Veltassa (patiromer) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Mechanism** | Non-absorbed, highly selective inorganic cation exchanger | Non-absorbed, cation exchange polymer (exchanges K+ for Calcium) |
| **Correction Dose** | 10g PO TID for up to 48 hours | N/A (Not for rapid correction) |
| **Maintenance Dose** | 5g to 15g PO Daily | 8.4g PO Daily |
| **Titration** | Adjust by 5g daily at 1-week intervals | Adjust by 8.4g daily at 1-week intervals (Max 25.2g/day) |
| **Drug Interactions** | Separate from pH-dependent drugs by 2 hours | **Separate from ALL other oral meds by 3 hours** |
| **Adverse Effects** | Edema (contains ~400mg sodium per 5g dose) | Constipation, hypomagnesemia |
| **Clinical Pearl** | May require increasing loop diuretic due to sodium load. | Good option if patient is prone to volume overload. |

#### Decision Tree: Managing K+ 5.5 - 6.0 mEq/L (Asymptomatic)
1.  **Review Meds:** Stop NSAIDs, potassium supplements, and Bactrim.
2.  **Assess Acid-Base:** If serum HCO3 < 22 mEq/L, start Sodium Bicarbonate.
3.  **Assess Volume:** If hypervolemic, initiate/increase loop diuretic (e.g., Lasix (furosemide) or Bumex (bumetanide)).
4.  **Initiate Binder:** Start Veltassa (patiromer) 8.4g daily OR Lokelma (SZC) 5g daily.
5.  **Recheck:** BMP in 1-2 weeks.
6.  **Escalate:** If K+ remains > 5.0, titrate binder up. *Only reduce/stop RAASi if max binder therapy fails.*

### 1.4 Hypokalemia in CKD
*   **Causes:** Aggressive loop/thiazide diuretic use, GI losses (diarrhea/vomiting), malnutrition.
*   **Management:** 
    *   Klor-Con (potassium chloride) 10-20 mEq PO daily to BID. 
    *   *Caution:* Replete conservatively in CKD 4-5. Recheck BMP within 3-5 days of starting supplementation to avoid rebound hyperkalemia.

### 1.5 Patient Education: Potassium
*   **Talking Points:** "Your kidneys are losing their ability to filter out potassium. Too much potassium can cause dangerous heart rhythms."
*   **High-Potassium Foods to Moderate/Avoid:** Bananas, oranges, tomatoes, potatoes, avocados, spinach, and salt substitutes (which are often made of potassium chloride).
*   **Preparation Tip:** Leaching potatoes (soaking in water before cooking) reduces potassium content.

## 2. SODIUM & VOLUME MANAGEMENT

### 2.1 Hyponatremia in CKD
Hyponatremia in CKD is almost always a water handling issue rather than a true sodium deficit.
*   **Hypervolemic Hyponatremia (Advanced CKD, Heart Failure, Cirrhosis):**
    *   *Pathophysiology:* Decreased effective arterial blood volume leads to ADH release and water retention.
    *   *Management:* Fluid restriction (< 1.5 L/day), loop diuretics.
*   **Euvolemic Hyponatremia (SIADH):**
    *   *Management:* Fluid restriction, oral urea. 
    *   *Note on Vaptans:* Samsca (tolvaptan) is generally avoided or requires extreme caution in advanced CKD; it is contraindicated in anuria and not recommended for eGFR < 10 mL/min.

### 2.2 Sodium Restriction
*   **Target:** KDIGO 2024 and ACC/AHA guidelines strongly recommend sodium intake **< 2 g/day** (< 90 mmol/day), which equates to < 5 g of table salt (NaCl) per day.
*   **Clinical Benefits:** Enhances the anti-proteinuric and anti-hypertensive effects of ACEi/ARBs. Reduces extracellular volume expansion, edema, and left ventricular hypertrophy.
*   **Patient Education:** "Avoid processed foods, canned soups, and fast food. Do not use salt substitutes without asking, as they contain potassium."

## 3. BICARBONATE & METABOLIC ACIDOSIS

### 3.1 Targets and Pathophysiology
*   **Target:** KDIGO 2024 recommends treating metabolic acidosis to maintain serum bicarbonate **≥ 22 mEq/L**.
*   **Pathophysiology:** As nephron mass declines, the kidney's ability to excrete daily dietary acid loads (primarily from animal proteins) and synthesize ammonia decreases, leading to non-anion gap or high-anion gap metabolic acidosis.
*   **Benefits of Correction:**
    *   Slows the rate of CKD progression.
    *   Reduces bone demineralization (acidosis buffers via bone calcium).
    *   Reduces muscle protein catabolism/wasting.
    *   Improves cellular potassium uptake (lowers serum K+).

### 3.2 Treatment Strategies
*   **First-line:** Sodium Bicarbonate tablets.
    *   *Starting Dose:* 650 mg PO BID or TID.
    *   *Titration:* Increase to 1300 mg PO BID or TID to achieve HCO3 ≥ 22 mEq/L.
    *   *Monitoring:* Watch for worsening hypertension or fluid overload due to the sodium load (650 mg tablet contains ~180 mg of sodium). May require increasing loop diuretics.
*   **Alternative:** Sodium citrate/citric acid (Bicitra) - use with caution in CKD due to risk of increased aluminum absorption if patient is on aluminum-based binders.
*   **Future Therapies:** *Veverimer (TRC101)* is a non-absorbed, hydrochloric acid-binding polymer designed to treat metabolic acidosis without adding sodium. (Note: Currently rejected by FDA, but remains in nephrology literature as a potential future class).
*   **Dietary Modification:** Encourage plant-based diets, which are base-producing, compared to animal proteins, which are acid-producing.

## 4. MONITORING & FOLLOW-UP

### 4.1 Lab Monitoring Frequency
Routine monitoring of a Basic Metabolic Panel (BMP) is dictated by CKD stage and medication changes.

| CKD Stage | eGFR (mL/min/1.73m²) | Minimum BMP Frequency |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **CKD 3a** | 45 - 59 | Every 6 months |
| **CKD 3b** | 30 - 44 | Every 3 - 6 months |
| **CKD 4** | 15 - 29 | Every 1 - 3 months |
| **CKD 5** | < 15 | Every 1 month |

**[TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED]: Post-Medication Change Protocol**
*   **Initiation/Titration of RAASi, MRAs, or Diuretics:** Check BMP within **1 to 2 weeks**.
*   **Initiation/Titration of Potassium Binders:** Check BMP within **1 to 2 weeks**.
