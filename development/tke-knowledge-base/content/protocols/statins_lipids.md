# CLINICAL REFERENCE: Lipid Management in Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)

**Target Audience:** Nephrologists, Advanced Practice Providers (APPs), and Clinical Staff at The Kidney Experts (TKE)
**Reference Basis:** KDIGO Clinical Practice Guideline for Lipid Management in CKD (2013/2024 updates), ACC/AHA Blood Cholesterol Guidelines (2018/2022)

---

## 1. Guideline Overview & LDL Targets

Patients with CKD are at a significantly elevated risk for Atherosclerotic Cardiovascular Disease (ASCVD). Cardiovascular mortality is the leading cause of death in the CKD population. 

*   **KDIGO Approach ("Treat-to-Risk"):** KDIGO guidelines traditionally recommend a "fire-and-forget" strategy based on cardiovascular risk rather than specific LDL targets. Statin intensity is dictated by age and CKD stage.
*   **ACC/AHA Approach ("Treat-to-Target"):** For patients with known ASCVD or very high risk, ACC/AHA guidelines recommend a target **LDL-C < 70 mg/dL** (and < 55 mg/dL for extreme risk). 
*   **TKE Clinical Consensus:** Utilize the KDIGO risk-based approach for initiation, but adopt the ACC/AHA target of **LDL-C < 70 mg/dL** to guide escalation therapy (ezetimibe, PCSK9i) in high-risk patients.

---

## 2. Initiation Decision Tree

The decision to initiate lipid-lowering therapy in CKD depends heavily on age, eGFR, and dialysis status.

### A. Non-Dialysis CKD (Stages 1-5 ND)
*   **Age ≥ 50 years with CKD:** **INITIATE** statin or statin + Zetia (ezetimibe) regardless of baseline LDL-C level.
*   **Age 18–49 years with CKD:** **INITIATE** statin if the patient has one or more of the following:
    *   Known Coronary Artery Disease (CAD)
    *   Diabetes Mellitus
    *   Prior ischemic stroke
    *   Estimated 10-year ASCVD risk > 10%

### B. Dialysis Patients (ESKD)
*   **DO NOT INITIATE** statins in patients newly starting or currently on dialysis. The *4D* and *AURORA* trials demonstrated no cardiovascular mortality benefit for statin initiation in the hemodialysis population.
*   **CONTINUE** statin therapy if the patient was already receiving a statin prior to dialysis initiation.

### C. Kidney Transplant Recipients
*   **INITIATE** statin therapy in all adult kidney transplant recipients due to high ASCVD risk, but specific agent selection is required due to calcineurin inhibitor (CNI) interactions (see Section 6).

## 3. Pharmacotherapy: Dosing & CKD Adjustments

Always prescribe the maximum tolerated dose of a preferred statin before escalating to non-statin therapies.

### First-Line: Statins & Cholesterol Absorption Inhibitors
Based on the *SHARP* trial, statin monotherapy or statin + Zetia (ezetimibe) is the standard of care for CKD.

| Drug (Brand/Generic) | Standard Dosing | CKD-Specific Adjustments & Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Lipitor (atorvastatin)** | 20–80 mg PO daily | **Preferred in CKD.** No renal dose adjustment needed. Not removed by dialysis. |
| **Crestor (rosuvastatin)** | 5–20 mg PO daily | **eGFR < 30 mL/min:** Start at 5 mg; **MAX dose 10 mg daily.** Excreted renally; higher doses increase myopathy/proteinuria risk. |
| **Zetia (ezetimibe)** | 10 mg PO daily | No renal dose adjustment needed. Add to statin for additional ~20% LDL lowering (*IMPROVE-IT* trial). |

### Second-Line: PCSK9 Inhibitors & ATP Citrate Lyase Inhibitors
Indicated for statin-intolerant patients or those not at LDL goal (< 70 mg/dL) despite maximized statin + Zetia (ezetimibe).

| Drug (Brand/Generic) | Standard Dosing | CKD-Specific Adjustments & Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Repatha (evolocumab)** | 140 mg SC every 2 weeks OR 420 mg SC monthly | No renal dose adjustment. Patient self-administered. Proven CV benefit in *FOURIER* trial. |
| **Leqvio (inclisiran)** | 284 mg SC at Day 0, Month 3, then every 6 months | No renal dose adjustment. **Administered by HCP in clinic.** Highly convenient for adherence (*ORION* trials). |
| **Nexletol (bempedoic acid)** | 180 mg PO daily | No renal dose adjustment. Great for statin-intolerant patients (*CLEAR* trial). Do not use with Zocor (simvastatin) >20mg. |

## 4. Treatment Escalation Pathway

1.  **Step 1:** Initiate **Lipitor (atorvastatin)** 20-40 mg OR **Crestor (rosuvastatin)** 5-10 mg.
2.  **Step 2:** If LDL-C remains > 70 mg/dL after 6-12 weeks, maximize statin dose (respecting eGFR limits for Crestor).
3.  **Step 3:** If LDL-C remains > 70 mg/dL, add **Zetia (ezetimibe)** 10 mg daily.
4.  **Step 4:** If LDL-C remains > 70 mg/dL OR patient is statin-intolerant, initiate a PCSK9 inhibitor (**Repatha (evolocumab)** or **Leqvio (inclisiran)**) or **Nexletol (bempedoic acid)**.

---

## 5. Monitoring Schedule

*   **Baseline:** Fasting Lipid Panel, ALT/AST, CK (if high risk for myopathy or baseline muscle symptoms).
*   **Post-Initiation/Titration:** Repeat Fasting Lipid Panel **6 to 12 weeks** after starting or changing therapy to assess response.
*   **Maintenance:** Annually, or more frequently if clinically indicated.
*   **Routine CK/LFTs:** Routine monitoring of CK or LFTs is *not* recommended unless the patient becomes symptomatic (e.g., unexplained muscle pain, weakness, jaundice).

---

## 6. Safety, Contraindications & Drug Interactions

### ⚠️ Myopathy Risk in CKD
*   Decreased eGFR increases the serum concentration of renally cleared statins (e.g., Crestor (rosuvastatin)), increasing the risk of statin-associated muscle symptoms (SAMS) and rhabdomyolysis.
*   **Action:** If severe muscle pain or dark urine occurs, hold statin, check CK and serum creatinine. 

### ⚠️ Transplant & Immunosuppressant Interactions
Calcineurin inhibitors (especially **cyclosporine**) significantly increase statin exposure by inhibiting CYP3A4 and OATP1B1 transporters.
*   **Preferred Statins in Transplant:** **Pravachol (pravastatin)** 10-20 mg or **Lescol (fluvastatin)** 20-40 mg.
*   **If using Lipitor (atorvastatin) with cyclosporine:** MAX dose is 10 mg daily.
*   **If using Crestor (rosuvastatin) with cyclosporine:** MAX dose is 5 mg daily.
*   *Note:* Tacrolimus has fewer statin interactions than cyclosporine, but caution and lowest effective dosing are still advised.

## 7. Prior Authorization Tips for PCSK9 Inhibitors

PCSK9 inhibitors (**Repatha**, **Leqvio**) frequently require rigorous Prior Authorization (PA). 
*[TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED]: Ensure clinical staff route all PCSK9i PA requests through the designated centralized pharmacy tech/team.*

**Required Documentation for PA Success:**
1.  **Diagnosis:** Document ASCVD, HeFH, or high-risk CKD.
2.  **Statin Failure/Intolerance:** Must document failure of at least TWO statins (e.g., Lipitor and Crestor), with at least one at the lowest available dose, due to intolerable side effects (e.g., severe myalgia with elevated CK).
3.  **Ezetimibe Failure:** Document that Zetia (ezetimibe) was tried and failed, or is contraindicated.
4.  **Current Labs:** Include a recent lipid panel (within 30-60 days) showing LDL-C > 70 mg/dL despite maximal tolerated therapy.

## 8. Patient Education Talking Points

*   **"Lifetime Therapy":** Explain that statins are long-term medications to protect the heart and blood vessels, not a short-term cure. Stopping the medication will cause cholesterol to rise again.
*   **"Heart Protection":** Emphasize that in kidney disease, the heart works harder. This medication is primarily to prevent heart attacks and strokes, even if their cholesterol "doesn't look that high."
*   **Side Effect Reporting:** Instruct patients to report any new, unexplained muscle aches, weakness, or dark-colored urine immediately.
*   **Consistency:** Take the medication at the same time every day. (Note: Lipitor and Crestor have long half-lives and can be taken morning or night; shorter-acting statins should be taken at bedtime).
*   **Leqvio Convenience:** If prescribing Leqvio (inclisiran), explain: *"This is an injection we give you right here in the clinic just twice a year after your initial doses, so you don't have to remember to take another daily pill."*
