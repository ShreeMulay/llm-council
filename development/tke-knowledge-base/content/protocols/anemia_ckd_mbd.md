## PART 1: ANEMIA OF CHRONIC KIDNEY DISEASE

**Primary Guideline References:** KDIGO 2012 Clinical Practice Guideline for Anemia in CKD (with 2024 updates).

### 1. Clinical Targets & The "Iron-First" Approach
Before initiating Erythropoiesis-Stimulating Agents (ESAs), iron stores must be repleted. ESAs are ineffective in the setting of iron deficiency.

*   **Hemoglobin (Hb) Target:** 10.0 to 11.5 g/dL.
    *   **🛑 SAFETY WARNING:** Do NOT intentionally exceed Hb 13.0 g/dL. Higher targets are associated with increased risk of stroke, cardiovascular events, and vascular access thrombosis (CHOIR and CREATE trials).
*   **Iron Targets:**
    *   **Ferritin:** Target 200 – 500 ng/mL. (Routine administration of IV iron is not recommended if ferritin > 500 ng/mL without clinical justification).
    *   **TSAT (Transferrin Saturation):** Target 20% – 30%.

### 2. Iron Replacement Therapy
*[TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED]: Preferred IV iron formulation based on current clinic inventory and local infusion center contracts.*

#### Oral Iron Options
Best for CKD Stage 3-4. **Clinical Pearl:** Alternate-day dosing (QOD) improves absorption and reduces GI side effects by minimizing hepcidin-mediated blockade.
*   **Ferrous sulfate:** 325 mg (65 mg elemental iron) PO daily or QOD. Take on an empty stomach; avoid taking with phosphate binders or PPIs/H2RAs.
*   **Auryxia (ferric citrate):** 210 mg elemental iron per tablet. Acts as BOTH a phosphate binder and iron supplement. Dose: 1-2 tablets PO TID with meals.

#### Intravenous (IV) Iron Options
Preferred for CKD Stage 5, dialysis patients, or those intolerant/unresponsive to oral iron.
| Drug Name | Standard Dosing | Clinical Notes & Safety |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Venofer (iron sucrose)** | 200 mg IV x 5 doses (Total 1000 mg) | Low risk of anaphylaxis. Standard for in-center HD. |
| **Injectafer (ferric carboxymaltose)** | 750 mg IV x 2 doses (7 days apart) | High elemental iron per dose. Risk of severe hypophosphatemia. |
| **Feraheme (ferumoxytol)** | 510 mg IV x 2 doses (3-8 days apart) | **🛑 BBW:** Fatal anaphylaxis risk. Monitor for 30 mins post-infusion. Alters MRI imaging for up to 3 months. |

### 3. Erythropoiesis-Stimulating Agents (ESAs)
**Decision Tree: ESA Therapy**
*   **START:** Hb < 10.0 g/dL AND iron stores are replete (TSAT > 20%, Ferritin > 200).
*   **ESCALATE:** Increase dose by 25% if Hb increases < 1.0 g/dL after 4 weeks.
*   **DE-ESCALATE:** Decrease dose by 25% if Hb rises > 1.0 g/dL in any 2-week period, or if Hb approaches 11.5 g/dL.
*   **STOP/HOLD:** Hb > 12.0 g/dL (or > 11.5 g/dL depending on patient trajectory). Resume at 25% lower dose when Hb falls back to target range.

| Drug Name | Starting Dose (CKD Non-Dialysis) | Starting Dose (Dialysis) | Route / Frequency |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Epogen / Procrit (epoetin alfa)** | 50-100 units/kg | 50-100 units/kg | SQ or IV / 1-3x weekly |
| **Aranesp (darbepoetin alfa)** | 0.45 mcg/kg q4 weeks OR 0.75 mcg/kg q2 weeks | 0.45 mcg/kg weekly OR 0.75 mcg/kg q2 weeks | SQ or IV / Weekly to Monthly |

### 4. HIF-PHI Therapy: Jesduvroq (daprodustat)
Based on the ASCEND trials, Hypoxia-Inducible Factor Prolyl Hydroxylase Inhibitors (HIF-PHIs) stimulate endogenous erythropoietin production and improve iron mobilization.
*   **Indication:** FDA-approved **ONLY for adults on dialysis** for at least 4 months. Not approved for non-dialysis CKD in the US.
*   **Dosing:** Oral daily tablet. Starting dose depends on prior ESA dose (refer to package insert).
*   **🛑 SAFETY WARNING (BBW):** Increased risk of death, myocardial infarction, stroke, venous thromboembolism (VTE), and vascular access thrombosis. Do not use with ESAs.

### 5. ESA Resistance Workup
**Definition:** Failure to reach target Hb despite >300 IU/kg/week of Epogen (epoetin alfa) or >1.5 mcg/kg/week of Aranesp (darbepoetin alfa).
**Workup Checklist:**
1.  **Absolute/Functional Iron Deficiency:** Check Ferritin/TSAT.
2.  **Inflammation/Infection:** Check CRP. Look for occult infection (e.g., diabetic foot ulcer, line infection).
3.  **Bleeding:** Check stool for occult blood, review GI history.
4.  **Hyperparathyroidism:** Check intact PTH (severe SHPT causes bone marrow fibrosis).
5.  **Nutritional:** Check Vitamin B12 and Folate levels.
6.  **Malignancy:** Consider age-appropriate cancer screenings.

### 6. Anemia Monitoring Schedule
*   **Not on ESA:** CBC every 3 months (CKD 3-5). Iron panel annually.
*   **Initiating/Titrating ESA:** CBC every 2-4 weeks until stable.
*   **Stable on ESA:** CBC monthly.
*   **Iron Panel (Ferritin/TSAT):** Every 3 months for all patients on ESAs or active iron therapy.

## PART 2: CKD-MBD MANAGEMENT

**Primary Guideline References:** KDIGO 2017 Clinical Practice Guideline Update for the Diagnosis, Evaluation, Prevention, and Treatment of CKD-MBD (with 2024 updates).

### 7. KDIGO CKD-MBD Targets
| Parameter | CKD Stage 3-5 (Non-Dialysis) | CKD Stage 5D (Dialysis) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Phosphorus** | Maintain normal range (e.g., 2.5 - 4.5 mg/dL) | Lower toward normal range |
| **Calcium (corrected)** | Avoid hypercalcemia | Avoid hypercalcemia |
| **Intact PTH** | Progressively rising; no exact target. Treat if consistently rising above normal. | Maintain at **2 to 9 times** the upper limit of normal (approx. 130 - 600 pg/mL) |
| **25(OH) Vitamin D** | Replete if < 30 ng/mL | Replete if < 30 ng/mL |

### 8. Phosphate Binders
**Decision Tree:** Start binders if phosphorus is elevated despite dietary restriction. Choose non-calcium binders if the patient has hypercalcemia, vascular calcifications, or adynamic bone disease.

| Class | Drug Name | Standard Dosing | Clinical Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Calcium-Based** | **PhosLo / Phoslyra (calcium acetate)** | 1334 mg (2 caps) PO TID with meals | Max 1500 mg elemental Ca/day from all sources. Avoid if Ca > 9.5 mg/dL. |
| **Non-Calcium** | **Renvela (sevelamer carbonate)** | 800-1600 mg PO TID with meals | Can lower LDL cholesterol. High pill burden. Can cause GI upset. |
| **Non-Calcium** | **Fosrenol (lanthanum carbonate)** | 500-1000 mg PO TID with meals | **Must be chewed completely** to prevent GI obstruction. |
| **Non-Calcium** | **Velphoro (sucroferric oxyhydroxide)** | 500 mg PO TID with meals | Chewable. Darkens stool. Low pill burden. |
| **Iron-Based** | **Auryxia (ferric citrate)** | 2 tabs (420 mg iron) PO TID with meals | Treats both hyperphosphatemia and iron deficiency. Monitor ferritin. |

### 9. Vitamin D and Secondary Hyperparathyroidism (SHPT)
*[TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED]: Step therapy requirements for active Vitamin D vs. Calcimimetics based on local payer preferences.*

#### Step 1: Nutritional Vitamin D Repletion (if 25-OH Vit D < 30 ng/mL)
*   **Ergocalciferol (Vit D2) or Cholecalciferol (Vit D3):** 50,000 IU weekly for 8 weeks, followed by 1,000-2,000 IU daily maintenance.

#### Step 2: Active Vitamin D Analogs (for elevated PTH)
*Do not start if Phosphorus > 5.5 mg/dL or Calcium > 9.5 mg/dL (increases GI absorption of both).*
*   **Rocaltrol (calcitriol):** Start 0.25 mcg PO daily or TIW.
*   **Zemplar (paricalcitol):** Start 1-2 mcg PO daily or TIW. (Less hypercalcemia risk than calcitriol).
*   **Hectorol (doxercalciferol):** Start 1 mcg PO daily or TIW.

#### Step 3: Calcimimetics (for uncontrolled SHPT or hypercalcemia)
*Increases sensitivity of calcium-sensing receptors on the parathyroid gland, lowering PTH and Calcium.*
*   **Sensipar (cinacalcet):** Start 30 mg PO daily. Titrate every 2-4 weeks (max 180 mg/day). Take with food.
    *   **🛑 SAFETY WARNING:** Can cause severe hypocalcemia. Do not start if corrected Calcium < 8.4 mg/dL. Monitor Ca 1 week after initiation or dose change.
*   **Parsabiv (etelcalcetide):** IV calcimimetic for hemodialysis patients only.

### 10. Bone Density Considerations in CKD
*   **Bisphosphonates** (e.g., Fosamax/alendronate): **Contraindicated if eGFR < 30 mL/min** due to risk of adynamic bone disease and toxicity.
*   **Prolia (denosumab):** Can be used in advanced CKD, but carries a **high risk of severe, prolonged hypocalcemia** in CKD 4-5. Must ensure normal calcium and replete Vitamin D prior to injection. Close monitoring required.

## PART 4: PATIENT EDUCATION TALKING POINTS

### Anemia Education
*   **Oral Iron:** "Take this every other day on an empty stomach. Do not take it at the same time as your antacids or phosphate binders, as they will block the iron from absorbing."
*   **ESA Injections:** "This medication tells your bone marrow to make more red blood cells. We will check your blood levels regularly because pushing your blood count too high can be dangerous for your heart."
*   **Dark Stools:** "Iron supplements (including Auryxia and Velphoro) will make your stool look very dark or black. This is normal and expected."

### CKD-MBD Education
*   **Phosphate Binders:** "These medications act like a sponge. You must take them **with your meals** (ideally the first bite of food). If you skip a meal, skip the binder."
*   **Dietary Phosphorus:** "Avoid 'hidden' or inorganic phosphorus found in processed foods and dark colas. Your body absorbs 100% of this artificial phosphorus. Read ingredient labels and look for words containing **'PHOS'** (like phosphoric acid or hexametaphosphate) and avoid them."
*   **Sensipar (cinacalcet):** "Take this medication with food to help it absorb better and reduce stomach upset. We will be checking your blood calcium levels closely while you are on this."
