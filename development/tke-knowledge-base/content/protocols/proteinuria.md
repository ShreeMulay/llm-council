# PROTEINURIA MANAGEMENT IN CKD: Clinical Reference Guide

## 1. KDIGO 2024 Albuminuria Staging
Proteinuria is a primary driver of chronic kidney disease (CKD) progression and an independent risk factor for cardiovascular events. KDIGO 2024 guidelines classify albuminuria into three stages based on the Urine Albumin-to-Creatinine Ratio (UACR).

| KDIGO Stage | Description | UACR (mg/g) | UACR (mg/mmol) | Clinical Implication |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **A1** | Normal to mildly increased | < 30 mg/g | < 3 mg/mmol | Low risk of progression (if eGFR normal) |
| **A2** | Moderately increased | 30 – 299 mg/g | 3 – 30 mg/mmol | Moderate risk; requires intervention |
| **A3** | Severely increased | ≥ 300 mg/g | > 30 mg/mmol | High risk; nephrology referral indicated |

## 2. Measurement & Diagnosis: UACR vs. UPCR
Accurate quantification is essential for staging and monitoring treatment response.

*   **Preferred Test:** **UACR** (Urine Albumin-to-Creatinine Ratio) is the preferred initial test for all patients with CKD, as it is more sensitive to low levels of glomerular damage than UPCR.
*   **When to use UPCR** (Urine Protein-to-Creatinine Ratio): 
    *   When UACR is severely elevated (> 500–1000 mg/g) to quantify total protein.
    *   When suspecting non-albumin proteinuria (e.g., tubular proteinuria, multiple myeloma/Bence Jones proteins).
*   **Timing:** **First morning void** is strongly preferred to minimize the effects of orthostatic proteinuria and hydration status. If unavailable, a random spot urine is acceptable but should be noted.
*   **Confirmation:** Confirm abnormal UACR with **2 out of 3 positive tests over a 3-to-6-month period**. Rule out transient causes before diagnosing persistent albuminuria (e.g., UTI, acute febrile illness, vigorous exercise, menstruation, decompensated heart failure).

## 3. Treatment Targets & Clinical Impact
Reducing proteinuria directly correlates with improved renal and cardiovascular survival. 

*   **Absolute Target:** UACR **< 30 mg/g** (normalization).
*   **Relative Target:** If absolute normalization is not possible, target a **> 30% to 50% reduction** from baseline UACR.
*   **Clinical Impact:** A 30% reduction in UACR at 6 months is associated with an approximate 30% relative risk reduction for progression to End-Stage Kidney Disease (ESKD).

## 4. Pharmacologic Management Decision Tree
Follow this step-wise approach for patients with persistent albuminuria (A2 or A3), guided by KDIGO 2024 and ADA 2024 guidelines.

### STEP 1: First-Line Therapy — RAAS Blockade
Initiate an ACE inhibitor (ACEi) or Angiotensin Receptor Blocker (ARB) in patients with high blood pressure and UACR ≥ 30 mg/g, or normotensive patients with UACR ≥ 300 mg/g.

*   **Common Agents:** Lisinopril (Prinivil/Zestril), Losartan (Cozaar), Irbesartan (Avapro).
*   **Titration:** Titrate every 2-4 weeks to the **maximum tolerated dose** (e.g., Losartan 100 mg daily) or until blood pressure targets are met.
*   **Monitoring:** Check BMP 2-4 weeks after initiation or dose increase.
*   **Safety & Stopping Rules:**
    *   *Permissive decline:* A serum creatinine increase of **up to 30%** from baseline is hemodynamically expected and acceptable. Do NOT stop therapy.
    *   *Stop/Reduce if:* SCr increases > 30%, or if hyperkalemia (K+ > 5.5 mEq/L) occurs and cannot be managed with dietary restriction or potassium binders (e.g., Lokelma (sodium zirconium cyclosilicate)).
    *   *Contraindications:* Pregnancy, history of angioedema, bilateral renal artery stenosis.

### STEP 2: Add-On Therapy — SGLT2 Inhibitors
Add an SGLT2i for patients with CKD (eGFR ≥ 20 mL/min/1.73m²) and UACR ≥ 200 mg/g (KDIGO 2024 strongly recommends; suggests for UACR 20-199 mg/g), regardless of diabetes status.

*   **Agents:** Farxiga (dapagliflozin) 10 mg daily OR Jardiance (empagliflozin) 10 mg daily.
*   **eGFR Thresholds:** Initiate if eGFR ≥ 20. If eGFR drops below 20 after initiation, *continue* therapy until initiation of dialysis or transplantation.
*   **Safety & Stopping Rules:**
    *   *Sick Day Rules:* Hold during acute illness, fasting, or perioperatively to prevent euglycemic diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA).
    *   *Contraindications:* Type 1 Diabetes (due to DKA risk), polycystic kidney disease (PKD), or patients on immunosuppression for recent kidney transplant.

### STEP 3: Add-On Therapy — nsMRA
For patients with Type 2 Diabetes, eGFR ≥ 25 mL/min/1.73m², normal potassium, and persistent UACR ≥ 30 mg/g despite maximum tolerated RAASi and SGLT2i.

*   **Agent:** Kerendia (finerenone) - a non-steroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist.
*   **Dosing:** 
    *   eGFR ≥ 60: Start 20 mg daily.
    *   eGFR 25–59: Start 10 mg daily. Titrate to 20 mg after 4 weeks if K+ ≤ 4.8 mEq/L.
*   **Safety & Stopping Rules:**
    *   Check K+ at baseline, 4 weeks after initiation/titration, and periodically.
    *   Hold if K+ > 5.5 mEq/L. Resume at 10 mg when K+ ≤ 5.0 mEq/L.

### STEP 4: Specialty/Targeted Therapy — Sparsentan (Filspari)
For patients with primary IgA Nephropathy (IgAN) at high risk of progression (UPCR ≥ 1.5 g/g) despite maximal RAAS blockade. 

*   **Mechanism:** Dual endothelin angiotensin receptor antagonist (DEARA) - blocks both ETA and AT1R.
*   **PROTECT Trial Data:** Demonstrated a rapid and sustained reduction in proteinuria (~50% reduction vs. irbesartan active control) and preservation of eGFR in IgAN.
*   **Dosing:** 200 mg daily for 14 days, then increase to 400 mg daily as tolerated. (Must discontinue prior ACEi/ARB before starting).
*   **Safety & REMS Program:**
    *   **Hepatotoxicity:** Requires baseline LFTs, then monthly for the first 12 months, then every 3 months. Hold if ALT/AST > 3x ULN.
    *   **Embryo-Fetal Toxicity:** Strict pregnancy testing requirements.

## 5. Non-Pharmacologic Management
Lifestyle modifications are foundational to reducing intraglomerular pressure and proteinuria.

*   **Sodium Restriction:** Target < 2 grams (2000 mg) of sodium per day. Enhances the antiproteinuric effect of RAASi.
*   **Protein Intake:** Target 0.8 g/kg body weight/day for non-dialysis CKD (KDIGO 2024). Avoid high-protein diets (e.g., keto), which cause glomerular hyperfiltration.
*   **Weight Management:** Target BMI < 25 kg/m². Weight loss directly reduces obesity-related hyperfiltration and secondary FSGS.
*   **Smoking Cessation:** Strongly advised to reduce CV risk and CKD progression.

## 7. Nephrology Referral Guidelines
Primary care providers should refer patients to TKE when:
*   UACR ≥ 300 mg/g (Stage A3) or UPCR ≥ 0.5 g/g.
*   Proteinuria accompanied by hematuria (suspect nephritic syndrome/glomerulonephritis).
*   Unexplained rapid decline in eGFR (> 5 mL/min/1.73m² per year).
*   Inability to titrate RAASi due to refractory hyperkalemia or hypotension.

## 8. Patient Education Talking Points
*   **The "Filter Leak" Analogy:** Explain that the kidneys are like coffee filters. When damaged, they let "coffee grounds" (protein) leak into the urine. Medications help tighten the filter and reduce the pressure pushing the protein through.
*   **Silent Damage:** Emphasize that proteinuria usually has no symptoms (unless severe enough to cause edema/foamy urine), making routine lab tests and daily medication adherence critical.
*   **Sick Day Rules (SADMANS):** Educate patients to temporarily stop certain meds during severe vomiting/diarrhea to prevent kidney injury or DKA. (SADMANS: **S**ulfonylureas, **A**CEi, **D**iuretics, **M**etformin, **A**RBs, **N**SAIDs, **S**GLT2i).
*   **Dietary Impact:** Remind patients that eating too much salt directly fights against their kidney medications.

## 9. Coding & Billing Considerations
Accurate coding ensures appropriate risk stratification and reimbursement.

**ICD-10 Diagnosis Codes:**
*   **R80.9** – Isolated proteinuria, unspecified
*   **R80.1** – Persistent proteinuria, unspecified
*   **R80.2** – Orthostatic proteinuria, unspecified
*   **E11.21** – Type 2 diabetes mellitus with diabetic nephropathy (Use if proteinuria is attributed to T2D)
*   **N18.x** – Chronic kidney disease (N18.1 to N18.6, N18.9) - *Always code the CKD stage alongside the proteinuria code.*
*   **N02.8** – Recurrent and persistent hematuria with other morphologic changes (Often used for suspected IgAN prior to biopsy confirmation).

**CPT Codes (Lab):**
*   **82043** – Microalbumin, quantitative
*   **82570** – Creatinine, other source (urine)
