# General CKD Management Clinical Reference

**Target Audience:** Nephrologists, Advanced Practice Providers (APPs), and Clinical Staff at The Kidney Experts (TKE)
**Source Guidelines:** KDIGO 2024 CKD Evaluation and Management, KDIGO 2024 BP Management, ADA 2024 Standards of Care, ACC/AHA 2017/2023 BP Guidelines.

---

## 1. CKD Definition, Staging, and eGFR Calculation

Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) is defined as abnormalities of kidney structure or function, present for **>3 months**, with implications for health. 

### eGFR Calculation
*   **Standard:** Use the **CKD-EPI 2021** equation (race-free). 
*   **Confirmatory Testing:** Consider Cystatin C (CKD-EPI 2012 or 2021 Cr-CysC equation) for patients with abnormal muscle mass, extreme diets (e.g., strict vegan, high protein), or when eGFR is borderline (e.g., confirming G3a vs. G2).

### KDIGO CKD Classification (Heat Map)
Risk of progression, cardiovascular events, and mortality increases with higher G and A stages.

| eGFR Category (G Stage) | eGFR (mL/min/1.73m²) | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **G1** | ≥ 90 | Normal or high |
| **G2** | 60–89 | Mildly decreased |
| **G3a** | 45–59 | Mildly to moderately decreased |
| **G3b** | 30–44 | Moderately to severely decreased |
| **G4** | 15–29 | Severely decreased |
| **G5** | < 15 | Kidney failure |

| Albuminuria Category (A Stage) | UACR (mg/g) | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **A1** | < 30 | Normal to mildly increased |
| **A2** | 30–300 | Moderately increased (Microalbuminuria) |
| **A3** | > 300 | Severely increased (Macroalbuminuria) |

## 2. Nephrology Referral Criteria

Primary care providers should refer patients to TKE based on the following criteria. *[TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED: Internal triage workflow for urgent vs. routine referrals]*

*   **Advanced Disease:** eGFR < 30 mL/min/1.73m² (G4-G5).
*   **Severe Proteinuria:** UACR > 300 mg/g persistently, or Urine Protein-to-Creatinine Ratio (UPCR) > 0.5 g/g.
*   **Rapid Progression:** eGFR decline of > 5 mL/min/1.73m² within 1 year.
*   **Hematuria:** Unexplained microscopic hematuria accompanied by proteinuria (suspected glomerulonephritis).
*   **Refractory Hypertension:** BP above target despite ≥ 3 antihypertensive agents of different classes (including a diuretic).
*   **Electrolyte/Acid-Base Disorders:** Refractory hyperkalemia, hyponatremia, or metabolic acidosis.
*   **Hereditary Risk:** Suspicion of Polycystic Kidney Disease (PKD) or other hereditary nephropathies.
*   **Recurrent Nephrolithiasis:** Especially with declining eGFR.

## 3. Clinical Targets & Lifestyle Modifications

### Blood Pressure Targets (KDIGO 2024 / ACC/AHA)
*   **Target:** Systolic BP **< 120 mmHg** (when tolerated, using standardized office BP measurement).
*   *Rationale:* Based on SPRINT-CKD data demonstrating reduced CV events and mortality.
*   *Exceptions:* Frail elderly, patients with severe orthostatic hypotension, or limited life expectancy (individualize target to < 130/80 or < 140/90 mmHg).

### Lifestyle & Dietary Modifications
*   **Sodium:** < 2 g/day (< 90 mmol/day). Crucial for BP control and maximizing antiproteinuric effects of RAAS inhibitors.
*   **Protein:** 
    *   Non-dialysis CKD (G3-G5): **0.8 g/kg/day**. Avoid high-protein diets (> 1.3 g/kg/day).
    *   Dialysis patients: **1.0 - 1.2 g/kg/day** (to compensate for dialysate losses).
*   **Physical Activity:** Moderate-intensity exercise ≥ 150 minutes/week.
*   **Weight Management:** Target BMI 20-25 kg/m². Consider GLP-1 RAs (e.g., Ozempic (semaglutide), Wegovy (semaglutide)) for obese patients with CKD/T2DM.

## 4. Foundational CKD Pharmacotherapy & Decision Trees

### Decision Tree: Initiating & Escalating Therapy
1.  **Step 1: RAAS Inhibition (ACEi/ARB)**
    *   *Start:* First-line for hypertension with CKD, especially if UACR > 30 mg/g. Strongly recommended if UACR > 300 mg/g.
    *   *Titration:* Titrate to maximum tolerated dose (e.g., Cozaar (losartan) 100 mg daily or Prinivil/Zestril (lisinopril) 40 mg daily).
    *   *Monitor:* Check BMP 2-4 weeks after start/dose change. Accept up to 30% rise in SCr.
2.  **Step 2: SGLT2 Inhibitors**
    *   *Start:* Add for CKD patients with eGFR ≥ 20 mL/min/1.73m² and UACR ≥ 200 mg/g (regardless of diabetes status), or any CKD patient with Heart Failure/T2DM.
    *   *Agents:* Farxiga (dapagliflozin) 10 mg daily OR Jardiance (empagliflozin) 10 mg daily.
    *   *Stop/Hold:* Do not stop if eGFR drops below 20; continue until dialysis initiation. Hold for sick days/surgery (DKA risk).
3.  **Step 3: Non-steroidal MRA (nsMRA)**
    *   *Start:* Add Kerendia (finerenone) for T2DM + CKD patients with eGFR ≥ 25 mL/min/1.73m², normal potassium (< 4.8 mEq/L), and residual albuminuria (UACR ≥ 30 mg/g) despite max tolerated RAASi.
    *   *Dosing:* 10 mg daily if eGFR 25-59; 20 mg daily if eGFR ≥ 60.
4.  **Step 4: GLP-1 Receptor Agonists**
    *   *Start:* Add for T2DM + CKD if A1c is not at target or for cardiovascular/weight benefits (e.g., Ozempic (semaglutide), Trulicity (dulaglutide)).

### 🚨 Safety Flags & Contraindications
*   **NSAIDs:** Strictly avoid (e.g., Advil/Motrin (ibuprofen), Aleve (naproxen), Celebrex (celecoxib)) in eGFR < 60 due to AKI risk.
*   **Dual RAASi:** NEVER combine an ACEi with an ARB (increased risk of AKI and hyperkalemia).
*   **Hyperkalemia:** If K+ > 5.5 mEq/L, optimize diet, stop offending agents (NSAIDs, Bactrim), add oral potassium binders (e.g., Lokelma (sodium zirconium cyclosilicate), Veltassa (patiromer)) before stopping RAASi.

## 5. Monitoring & Progression Tracking

### Frequency of Monitoring (KDIGO)
Based on the G and A stages:
*   **G1-G2 / A1-A2:** Annually
*   **G3a-G3b / A2-A3:** Every 3-6 months
*   **G4-G5 / A2-A3:** Every 1-3 months

### Progression Metrics
*   **eGFR Slope:** Track trajectory. Rapid progression = sustained decline of > 5 mL/min/1.73m²/year.
*   **Proteinuria Trends:** Target UACR reduction of > 30% from baseline, ideally < 30 mg/g.

## 6. Advanced CKD (G4-G5) & Transitions of Care

### Dialysis Preparation
*   **eGFR < 20 mL/min/1.73m²:** Initiate formal modality education (Hemodialysis, Peritoneal Dialysis, Kidney Transplant, Conservative Care).
*   **eGFR < 15-20 mL/min/1.73m²:** Refer for permanent access creation (AV Fistula/Graft) or PD catheter placement. *[TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED: Preferred vascular surgery referral partners]*
*   **Transplant Referral:** Refer to transplant center when eGFR ≤ 20 mL/min/1.73m² for pre-emptive listing.

### Conservative Management
*   **Indication:** Elderly, frail, or highly comorbid patients who decline dialysis or where dialysis would not improve quality of life/survival.
*   **Focus:** Active symptom management (fluid overload, anemia, pain, pruritus, uremia), advance care planning, and palliative care integration.

## 7. AKI in CKD (Acute-on-Chronic Kidney Disease)

### Recognition
*   Increase in SCr by ≥ 0.3 mg/dL within 48 hours, OR
*   Increase in SCr to ≥ 1.5 times baseline within the prior 7 days.

### Prevention ("Sick Day Rules")
Educate patients to temporarily HOLD the following medications during acute dehydrating illnesses (vomiting, diarrhea, high fever):
*   **S**GLT2 inhibitors (Farxiga, Jardiance) - *DKA and AKI risk*
*   **A**CE inhibitors / ARBs (Lisinopril, Losartan)
*   **D**iuretics (Lasix (furosemide), Microzide (hydrochlorothiazide))
*   **M**etformin (Glucophage) - *Lactic acidosis risk*
*   **A**RBs
*   **N**SAIDs (Always avoid, but especially during illness)

### Recovery Monitoring
*   Recheck SCr and electrolytes 1-2 weeks post-AKI to ensure return to baseline.
*   Reinitiate held medications cautiously once euvolemic and SCr stabilizes.

## 8. Preventive Care & Vaccinations

CKD patients are immunocompromised. Ensure up-to-date vaccinations:
*   **Influenza:** Annually (High-dose Fluzone for ≥ 65 years).
*   **Pneumococcal:** 
    *   Prevnar 20 (PCV20) once, OR
    *   Vaxneuvance (PCV15) followed by Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23) one year later (8 weeks later if immunocompromised/nephrotic).
*   **Hepatitis B:** Check HBsAb. If < 10 mIU/mL, vaccinate. 
    *   *Note:* Advanced CKD/Dialysis requires high-dose formulations (e.g., Engerix-B 40 mcg, Recombivax HB 40 mcg) or adjuvanted Heplisav-B.
*   **COVID-19:** Follow current CDC guidelines for updated boosters.
*   **Cancer Screening:** Continue age-appropriate screening (colon, breast, prostate) unless life expectancy is < 10 years or patient is on dialysis/conservative care where risks outweigh benefits.

## 9. Medication Dose Adjustments (General Principles)

Always check eGFR before prescribing or adjusting medications.
*   **Metformin (Glucophage):** Max dose 1000 mg/day if eGFR 30-45. Discontinue if eGFR < 30.
*   **Gabapentin (Neurontin):** Highly renally cleared. 
    *   eGFR 30-59: Max 1400 mg/day
    *   eGFR 15-29: Max 700 mg/day
    *   eGFR < 15: Max 300 mg/day (or every other day)
*   **DOACs:** 
    *   Xarelto (rivaroxaban): Avoid if eGFR < 15.
    *   Eliquis (apixaban): Adjust to 2.5 mg BID if ≥ 2 of: Age ≥ 80, Weight ≤ 60 kg, SCr ≥ 1.5 mg/dL. (Often used off-label in ESRD per hematology/cardiology guidance).
*   **Contrast Media:** Use IV hydration protocols for eGFR < 30 requiring iodinated contrast. Avoid Gadolinium in eGFR < 30 due to risk of Nephrogenic Systemic Fibrosis (NSF), though newer Group II agents (e.g., Gadavist) have very low risk.

## 11. Patient Education Talking Points

*   **What is CKD?** "Your kidneys are like filters. Right now, your filters are working at about [X]% capacity. Our goal is to protect the remaining function and prevent that number from dropping."
*   **Diet:** "Avoid the salt shaker and processed foods. Too much salt raises your blood pressure and makes your kidney medications less effective. Keep protein portions to the size of a deck of cards."
*   **Medications:** "Some of your medications, like [Farxiga/Lisinopril], are specifically prescribed to protect your kidneys, even if you don't feel different taking them."
*   **Sick Days:** "If you get a stomach bug and can't keep fluids down, stop taking your blood pressure pills and water pills until you feel better. Call our office so we can guide you."
*   **Over-the-counter meds:** "Never take NSAIDs like Advil, Motrin, or Aleve for pain. They reduce blood flow to your kidneys. Use Tylenol (acetaminophen) instead."
