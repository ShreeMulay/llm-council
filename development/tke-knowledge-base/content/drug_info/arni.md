# Angiotensin Receptor-Neprilysin Inhibitor (ARNI) Clinical Monograph

## Entresto (sacubitril/valsartan)

### Mechanism of Action
Entresto (sacubitril/valsartan) is a first-in-class Angiotensin Receptor-Neprilysin Inhibitor (ARNI) containing two active components:
*   **Sacubitril:** A prodrug that inhibits neprilysin (neutral endopeptidase). Neprilysin degrades vasoactive peptides, including natriuretic peptides (ANP, BNP), bradykinin, and adrenomedullin. Inhibition leads to increased levels of these peptides, promoting vasodilation, natriuresis, diuresis, and inhibition of pathologic cardiac remodeling.
*   **Valsartan:** An Angiotensin II Receptor Blocker (ARB) that selectively blocks the AT1 receptor, preventing angiotensin II-induced vasoconstriction, aldosterone release, and renal sodium reabsorption.

### Indications in Nephrology
Based on the **2022 AHA/ACC/HFSA Heart Failure Guidelines** and **KDIGO 2024 CKD Guidelines**, Entresto (sacubitril/valsartan) is highly relevant for CKD patients with concomitant heart failure.

**FDA-Approved Indications:**
*   **Heart Failure with Reduced Ejection Fraction (HFrEF):** To reduce the risk of cardiovascular death and heart failure hospitalization in adult patients with chronic heart failure (LVEF $\le$ 40%). *Class 1a recommendation to replace ACEi/ARB.*
*   **Heart Failure with Preserved Ejection Fraction (HFpEF):** To reduce the risk of cardiovascular death and heart failure hospitalization in adult patients with chronic heart failure. Benefits are most evident in patients with LVEF below normal (e.g., LVEF 41-50%).
*   **Pediatric Heart Failure:** Symptomatic heart failure with systemic left ventricular systolic dysfunction in children $\ge$ 1 year old.

**Key Supporting Clinical Trials:**
*   **PARADIGM-HF (2014):** Compared Entresto to enalapril in HFrEF. Stopped early due to overwhelming benefit. Showed a **20% relative risk reduction** in the primary composite endpoint of CV death or HF hospitalization.
*   **PARAGON-HF (2019):** Evaluated Entresto vs. valsartan in HFpEF (LVEF $\ge$ 45%). Narrowly missed statistical significance for the primary endpoint, but subgroup analysis showed significant benefit in patients with LVEF $\le$ 57% and in women.
*   **UK HARP-III (2018):** Evaluated safety in CKD (eGFR 20-60 mL/min/1.73m²). Showed Entresto (sacubitril/valsartan) had similar effects on kidney function and risk of hyperkalemia compared to irbesartan, establishing its safety profile in advanced CKD.

### Dosing
Entresto (sacubitril/valsartan) is available in three tablet strengths: 
*   **24/26 mg** (sacubitril 24 mg / valsartan 26 mg)
*   **49/51 mg** (sacubitril 49 mg / valsartan 51 mg)
*   **97/103 mg** (sacubitril 97 mg / valsartan 103 mg)

**Standard Dosing & Titration:**
*   **Starting Dose (Standard):** 49/51 mg PO BID.
*   **Starting Dose (ACEi/ARB naive or low-dose):** 24/26 mg PO BID.
*   **Titration:** Double the dose every 2 to 4 weeks to the target maintenance dose of 97/103 mg PO BID, as tolerated by blood pressure, serum potassium, and renal function.

**CKD Dose Adjustments by eGFR Range:**
*   **eGFR $\ge$ 30 mL/min/1.73m²:** Standard dosing (Start 49/51 mg BID; or 24/26 mg BID if ACEi/ARB naive).
*   **eGFR 15-29 mL/min/1.73m²:** Start at **24/26 mg PO BID**. Titrate slowly.
*   **eGFR < 15 mL/min/1.73m² / Dialysis:** Not formally FDA-approved for ESRD, but used off-label in clinical practice for severe HFrEF. Start at **24/26 mg PO BID**. *[TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED: Consult cardiology prior to initiation in ESRD patients].*

**Hepatic Adjustments:**
*   Mild impairment (Child-Pugh A): No adjustment.
*   Moderate impairment (Child-Pugh B): Start at 24/26 mg PO BID.
*   Severe impairment (Child-Pugh C): **Contraindicated**.

### Monitoring
**Baseline Labs Required:**
*   BMP (Serum creatinine, eGFR, Potassium)
*   Blood pressure (Target systolic > 100 mmHg before starting)
*   Pregnancy test (in women of childbearing potential)

**Follow-up Lab Schedule:**
*   **Timing:** Check BMP 1 to 2 weeks after initiation and after *every* dose titration.
*   **Potassium Target:** Maintain K⁺ < 5.0 mEq/L. If K⁺ is 5.1-5.5 mEq/L, consider dietary restriction or potassium binders (e.g., Lokelma, Veltassa) before down-titrating. If K⁺ > 5.5 mEq/L, hold or reduce dose.
*   **Creatinine Target:** Permissive rise in SCr up to **30% from baseline** is acceptable, provided it stabilizes. If SCr rises > 30%, evaluate for volume depletion, hold concomitant NSAIDs/diuretics, or reduce Entresto dose.

**Biomarker Monitoring (CRITICAL NEPHROLOGY PEARL):**
*   **DO NOT USE BNP** to monitor heart failure status in patients on Entresto. Sacubitril inhibits neprilysin, which degrades BNP; therefore, BNP will be falsely elevated.
*   **MUST USE NT-proBNP** for heart failure monitoring, as it is not a substrate for neprilysin.

### Adverse Effects & Safety
**Common Side Effects (>5%):**
*   Hypotension (18%) - *Most common reason for dose reduction*
*   Hyperkalemia (12%)
*   Elevated serum creatinine/AKI (3-5%)
*   Dizziness (6%)
*   Cough (9%)

**Serious/Rare Side Effects:**
*   **Angioedema (0.5%):** Higher risk in Black patients and those with a history of ACEi-induced angioedema.

**Black Box Warnings:**
*   **Fetal Toxicity:** Drugs that act directly on the renin-angiotensin system can cause injury and death to the developing fetus. Discontinue as soon as pregnancy is detected.

**Contraindications:**
*   **Absolute:** History of angioedema related to previous ACEi or ARB therapy.
*   **Absolute:** Concomitant use with ACE inhibitors. **Requires a 36-hour washout period** when switching from an ACEi to Entresto (sacubitril/valsartan) to prevent life-threatening angioedema.
*   **Absolute:** Concomitant use with aliskiren in patients with diabetes.
*   **Relative:** Severe hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh C).

**CKD-Specific Safety Concerns:**
*   Patients with advanced CKD are at significantly higher risk for hyperkalemia and hypotension.
*   Aggressive diuresis combined with Entresto can precipitate pre-renal AKI. Adjust loop diuretics carefully when initiating Entresto, as Entresto itself has mild diuretic properties.

### Drug Interactions
*   **ACE Inhibitors:** **CONTRAINDICATED.** 36-hour washout required.
*   **ARBs:** Duplicate therapy; discontinue prior ARB before starting Entresto.
*   **Potassium-Sparing Diuretics / MRAs (Spironolactone, Eplerenone, Finerenone):** Increased risk of severe hyperkalemia. Monitor K⁺ closely.
*   **NSAIDs:** Increased risk of AKI, especially in volume-depleted or elderly patients. Avoid concomitant use.
*   **Lithium:** Entresto can decrease renal lithium clearance, leading to lithium toxicity. Monitor lithium levels closely.
*   **SGLT2 Inhibitors (Farxiga, Jardiance):** Synergistic benefits for HF/CKD, but combined use increases the risk of volume depletion and hypotension. Monitor volume status.

### Special Populations
*   **Pregnancy:** Category D/X equivalent. **Contraindicated.**
*   **Elderly:** No specific dose adjustment based on age alone, but elderly patients are more prone to hypotension and AKI. Start at 24/26 mg BID if frail or eGFR < 60.
*   **Transplant:** Use with extreme caution. Calcineurin inhibitors (Tacrolimus, Cyclosporine) already cause afferent arteriolar vasoconstriction and hyperkalemia. Adding Entresto (sacubitril/valsartan) significantly increases the risk of hyperkalemia and AKI. *[TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED: Consult transplant center before initiating in renal transplant recipients].*

### Patient Counseling Points
*   **The 36-Hour Rule:** If switching from an ACE inhibitor (e.g., lisinopril), you MUST wait 36 hours after your last ACE inhibitor dose before taking your first Entresto pill.
*   **Warning Signs:** Go to the ER immediately if you experience swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat, or have difficulty breathing (Angioedema).
*   **Hypotension:** You may feel dizzy or lightheaded when standing up. Change positions slowly.
*   **Diet:** Avoid salt substitutes containing potassium unless directed by your nephrologist.
*   **Pregnancy:** Use effective contraception. Stop the medication and call the clinic immediately if you become pregnant.

## Class Comparison: ARNI vs. Traditional ACEi/ARB Therapy

While Entresto (sacubitril/valsartan) is currently the only ARNI on the market, the primary clinical decision in nephrology/cardiology is determining **when to switch a patient from a traditional ACEi/ARB to an ARNI**.

| Feature | Traditional ACEi/ARB (e.g., Lisinopril, Losartan) | ARNI (Entresto - sacubitril/valsartan) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primary Indication** | HTN, CKD proteinuria, HFrEF | HFrEF (superiority), HFpEF (lower EF range) |
| **eGFR Thresholds** | Can be used down to ESRD | Start 24/26 mg if eGFR < 30. Caution in ESRD. |
| **Washout Required?** | No | **YES (36 hours if switching from ACEi)** |
| **Biomarker Monitoring** | BNP or NT-proBNP | **NT-proBNP ONLY** (BNP falsely elevated) |
| **BP Lowering Effect** | Moderate | Potent (higher risk of symptomatic hypotension) |
| **Cost** | Generic (Very Low) | Brand Only (High) |

### Decision Tree: When to Switch to ARNI
Consider switching a CKD patient from an ACEi/ARB to Entresto (sacubitril/valsartan) when:
1.  **Diagnosis:** Patient has symptomatic HFrEF (LVEF $\le$ 40%) despite stable doses of ACEi/ARB and beta-blockers. *(AHA/ACC Class 1a recommendation)*
2.  **Hemodynamics:** Patient has adequate blood pressure (Systolic consistently > 100 mmHg) to tolerate the switch, as Entresto causes more profound vasodilation.
3.  **Potassium:** Patient's baseline potassium is well-controlled (< 5.0 mEq/L).

### Decision Tree: How to Switch Safely
*   **If patient is on an ARB (e.g., Losartan):** Stop ARB. Start Entresto at the next scheduled dose time. No washout needed.
*   **If patient is on an ACEi (e.g., Lisinopril):** Stop ACEi. **WAIT 36 HOURS.** Start Entresto. *(Failure to observe this washout can result in life-threatening angioedema).*
*   **Dose Selection on Switch:**
    *   If on *high-dose* ACEi/ARB (e.g., lisinopril >10mg/day or losartan >50mg/day) AND eGFR $\ge$ 30: Start Entresto 49/51 mg BID.
    *   If on *low-dose* ACEi/ARB, OR ACEi/ARB naive, OR eGFR < 30: Start Entresto 24/26 mg BID.
