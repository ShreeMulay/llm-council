# Dual Endothelin/Angiotensin Receptor Antagonists (DEARA)

The DEARA class represents a novel therapeutic approach in nephrology, combining endothelin type A (ETA) receptor antagonism and angiotensin II type 1 (AT1) receptor antagonism into a single molecule. Currently, **Filspari (sparsentan)** is the first and only FDA-approved medication in this class.

---

## Filspari (sparsentan)

### Mechanism of Action
Filspari (sparsentan) is a single molecule that functions as a high-affinity, dual antagonist of both the endothelin type A (ETA) and angiotensin II type 1 (AT1) receptors. 
*   **ETA Blockade:** Prevents endothelin-1 mediated vasoconstriction, cellular proliferation, podocyte injury, and renal inflammation/fibrosis.
*   **AT1 Blockade:** Prevents angiotensin II-mediated efferent arteriolar vasoconstriction and aldosterone release.
*   **Synergy:** The dual blockade provides a synergistic reduction in intraglomerular pressure and proteinuria while mitigating the fluid retention (edema) typically seen with selective endothelin receptor antagonists (ERAs).

### Indications in Nephrology
*   **FDA-Approved Indication:** To reduce proteinuria in adults with primary Immunoglobulin A nephropathy (IgAN) at risk of rapid disease progression. *(Note: FDA granted full approval in September 2024, removing the previous accelerated approval requirement of UPCR $\ge$ 1.5 g/g, broadening access to patients at risk of progression).*
*   **Off-Label Uses:** Focal Segmental Glomerulosclerosis (FSGS) – currently under investigation (DUPLEX trial).
*   **Key Supporting Clinical Trial:** 
    *   **PROTECT Trial (Phase 3):** Compared Filspari (sparsentan) 400 mg daily vs. irbesartan 300 mg daily in 404 patients with IgAN. 
    *   *Outcomes:* At Week 36, Filspari achieved a 49.8% reduction in UPCR vs. 15.1% with irbesartan. At Week 110, the eGFR slope was significantly preserved with Filspari (-2.7 mL/min/1.73m² per year) compared to irbesartan (-3.8 mL/min/1.73m² per year).

### Dosing & Titration
**CRITICAL: Patients MUST discontinue their current ACE inhibitor (ACEi) or Angiotensin Receptor Blocker (ARB) before starting Filspari (sparsentan).** Do not overlap therapy.

*   **Standard Starting Dose:** 200 mg orally once daily.
*   **Titration Schedule:** After 14 days, increase to the maximum target dose of 400 mg orally once daily, provided the 200 mg dose is well-tolerated (stable blood pressure, serum potassium $\le$ 5.0 mEq/L, and no significant fluid retention).

**CKD Dose Adjustments by eGFR Range:**
*   **eGFR $\ge$ 60 mL/min/1.73m²:** Standard dosing (Start 200 mg, titrate to 400 mg).
*   **eGFR 30-59 mL/min/1.73m²:** Standard dosing.
*   **eGFR 15-29 mL/min/1.73m²:** Standard dosing. *Use with caution; monitor closely for hyperkalemia and acute kidney injury (AKI).*
*   **eGFR < 15 mL/min/1.73m² / Dialysis:** Not recommended (has not been adequately studied).

**Hepatic Adjustments:**
*   Mild to Moderate (Child-Pugh A or B): No adjustment required.
*   Severe (Child-Pugh C): Avoid use.

### Monitoring & REMS Program
Filspari (sparsentan) is available ONLY through a restricted program called the **Filspari REMS** due to the risks of hepatotoxicity and embryo-fetal toxicity. [TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED: Ensure provider and pharmacy are REMS-certified before prescribing].

**Baseline Labs Required:**
*   Comprehensive Metabolic Panel (specifically AST, ALT, Total Bilirubin, Potassium, SCr/eGFR).
*   Urine Protein-to-Creatinine Ratio (UPCR) and Urine Albumin-to-Creatinine Ratio (UACR).
*   Pregnancy test (females of reproductive potential).
*   Blood pressure and baseline weight/fluid status.

**Follow-Up Lab Schedule:**
*   **Liver Function Tests (ALT, AST, Bilirubin):** Monthly for the first 12 months, then every 3 months during treatment.
*   **Pregnancy Testing:** Monthly during treatment, and 1 month after discontinuation.
*   **Potassium & eGFR:** Check 1-2 weeks after initiation and 1-2 weeks after dose titration, then periodically (every 3 months or as clinically indicated).
*   **Proteinuria:** Check UPCR/UACR every 3 months.
    *   *Target:* UPCR < 0.3 g/g or a > 50% reduction from baseline.

**Decision Tree: Hepatotoxicity Management**
*   *ALT/AST > 3x ULN but $\le$ 5x ULN:* Pause Filspari. Recheck labs weekly. May restart at 200 mg if levels return to baseline and no alternative cause is found.
*   *ALT/AST > 5x ULN but $\le$ 8x ULN:* Pause Filspari. Recheck labs weekly. Consider permanent discontinuation.
*   *ALT/AST > 8x ULN OR > 3x ULN with Bilirubin > 2x ULN:* **STOP immediately and permanently discontinue.**

### Adverse Effects & Safety
*   **Black Box Warnings:** 
    1.  **Hepatotoxicity:** Elevations in aminotransferases.
    2.  **Embryo-Fetal Toxicity:** Major birth defects if used during pregnancy.
*   **Common Side Effects (>5%):** Hypotension (14%), peripheral edema (13%), hyperkalemia (13%), dizziness (11%), anemia (5%), acute kidney injury.
*   **Contraindications:**
    *   Pregnancy.
    *   Co-administration with ARBs, ACE inhibitors, ERAs, or aliskiren.
*   **CKD-Specific Safety Concerns:**
    *   *Hyperkalemia:* Risk is elevated in advanced CKD (eGFR < 30). Manage with dietary restriction, potassium binders (e.g., Lokelma, Veltassa), or diuretics before abandoning DEARA therapy.
    *   *Hemodynamic AKI:* An initial dip in eGFR (up to 10-15%) is expected due to reduced intraglomerular pressure. Do not stop therapy unless eGFR decline is > 30% from baseline.

### Drug Interactions
*   **RAAS Inhibitors:** Contraindicated with ACEi, ARB, direct renin inhibitors, and other ERAs.
*   **CYP3A4 Substrate:** 
    *   *Strong CYP3A Inhibitors (e.g., ketoconazole, clarithromycin, ritonavir):* Avoid concomitant use.
    *   *Strong CYP3A Inducers (e.g., rifampin, phenytoin):* Avoid concomitant use (decreases sparsentan efficacy).
*   **Antacids:** Administer Filspari (sparsentan) at least 2 hours before or 2 hours after antacids.
*   **Potassium-Sparing Diuretics / Supplements:** Increases risk of severe hyperkalemia. Use together only with extreme caution and frequent monitoring.
*   **NSAIDs:** Avoid use, as the combination significantly increases the risk of AKI.

### Special Populations
*   **Pregnancy:** Category X equivalent. Absolutely contraindicated. Females of reproductive potential must use effective contraception prior to, during, and for 1 month after treatment.
*   **Elderly:** No specific dose adjustment, but older adults may be more sensitive to hypotensive effects.
*   **Transplant:** Not formally studied in kidney transplant recipients. Use requires careful consideration of drug interactions (e.g., calcineurin inhibitors are CYP3A4 substrates/inhibitors).

### Patient Counseling Points
*   **The "Washout" Rule:** "You must completely stop taking your current blood pressure pill [insert ACEi/ARB name] before starting Filspari. Taking both can cause dangerously low blood pressure and kidney damage."
*   **REMS & Blood Draws:** "Because of rare but serious liver risks, you are required to get blood work done every month for the first year. We cannot send your next refill until this blood work is completed."
*   **Pregnancy Prevention:** "This medication causes severe birth defects. Strict birth control is required, and you will need monthly pregnancy tests."
*   **Warning Signs to Report:** Instruct patients to call the clinic immediately if they develop jaundice (yellowing of skin/eyes), dark "tea-colored" urine, severe right upper stomach pain, unexplained swelling in the legs, or extreme dizziness upon standing.
*   **Administration:** Take whole with water. Can be taken with or without food. Do not take antacids within 2 hours of this medication.

### Prior Authorization & Coding
*   **Common ICD-10 Codes:** 
    *   `N02.8` (Recurrent and persistent hematuria with other morphologic changes - commonly used for IgA Nephropathy)
    *   `N08` (Glomerular disorders in diseases classified elsewhere)
*   **CPT Codes:** `99214` or `99215` (Appropriate for the high medical decision-making complexity involved in REMS counseling, stopping ACEi/ARB, and initiating DEARA therapy).
*   **Tips for PA Success:** [TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED: Route all Filspari PAs through the dedicated specialty pharmacy liaison]. Ensure clinical notes explicitly document:
    1. Biopsy-proven primary IgA Nephropathy.
    2. Patient is at risk of rapid disease progression (document UPCR/UACR levels).
    3. Patient has discontinued standard ACEi/ARB therapy.
*   **Cost & Access:** High-cost specialty medication. Utilize the *Travere Patient Support* program for copay assistance and bridge supply if PA is delayed.

## Class Comparison: DEARA vs. Standard of Care

Because Filspari (sparsentan) is currently the *only* FDA-approved DEARA, the table below compares it to the traditional standard of care (ACEi/ARB monotherapy) to guide clinical decision-making.

| Feature | Filspari (sparsentan) | Standard ACEi / ARB (e.g., lisinopril, losartan) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Mechanism** | Dual ETA and AT1 receptor antagonist | Single pathway (ACE inhibition or AT1 antagonism) |
| **Primary Indication** | Primary IgA Nephropathy at risk of progression | Hypertension, Diabetic Kidney Disease, general CKD proteinuria |
| **Proteinuria Reduction** | ~50% reduction (PROTECT trial) | ~15-20% reduction |
| **eGFR Threshold** | Studied down to eGFR 15 | Used across all eGFRs (caution < 15) |
| **Monitoring** | **REMS required:** Monthly LFTs (x12 mo), monthly pregnancy tests | Routine BMP (K+, SCr) |
| **Edema Risk** | Moderate (13%) - mitigated by dual blockade vs pure ERA | Low |
| **Cost / Access** | High (Specialty Pharmacy, PA required) | Low (Generic, Tier 1) |

### Decision Tree: When to Switch to Filspari (sparsentan)
1.  **Assess Diagnosis:** Does the patient have biopsy-proven primary IgAN?
2.  **Assess Risk:** Is the patient at risk of rapid progression (e.g., UPCR remains > 0.5 - 1.0 g/g despite 3-6 months of maximally tolerated ACEi/ARB)?
3.  **Assess Safety:** Is the patient pregnant or planning pregnancy? Do they have severe hepatic impairment? (If yes $\rightarrow$ DO NOT USE).
4.  **Action:** If high risk and no contraindications, **STOP** ACEi/ARB. Wait 24-48 hours. **START** Filspari 200 mg daily. Enroll in REMS.
