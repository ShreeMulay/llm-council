# Erythropoiesis-Stimulating Agents (ESAs) Clinical Monograph

**Guidelines Reference:** KDIGO 2012 Clinical Practice Guideline for Anemia in Chronic Kidney Disease; FDA Prescribing Information. 
**Class Overview:** Erythropoiesis-stimulating agents (ESAs) are synthetic forms of erythropoietin used to treat anemia of chronic kidney disease (CKD). They stimulate the bone marrow to produce red blood cells (RBCs), reducing the need for blood transfusions. 

---

## Aranesp (darbepoetin alfa)

### Mechanism of Action
Aranesp (darbepoetin alfa) is a recombinant human erythropoiesis-stimulating protein. It binds to the erythropoietin receptor on erythroid progenitor cells in the bone marrow, stimulating RBC production. It has been engineered to contain five N-linked oligosaccharide chains (compared to three in endogenous EPO), which decreases its receptor affinity but significantly prolongs its half-life, allowing for less frequent dosing.

### Indications in Nephrology
*   **FDA-Approved:** Treatment of anemia due to CKD, including patients on dialysis and patients not on dialysis.
*   **Key Supporting Clinical Trials:**
    *   **TREAT Trial (2009):** Evaluated Aranesp (darbepoetin alfa) in 4,038 patients with type 2 diabetes, CKD, and anemia. Comparing a target Hemoglobin (Hb) of 13.0 g/dL vs. placebo (rescue ESA if Hb < 9.0 g/dL). 
    *   *Outcomes:* The higher Hb target did not reduce the risk of death or cardiovascular (CV) events but significantly increased the risk of fatal or nonfatal stroke (HR 1.92). This trial fundamentally changed FDA and KDIGO guidelines to target lower Hb levels (10-11 g/dL).

### Dosing
**Prerequisite:** Evaluate iron status before starting. Ensure Transferrin Saturation (TSAT) >20% and Ferritin >100 ng/mL (ND-CKD) or >200 ng/mL (Dialysis).

*   **Standard Starting Dose (Non-Dialysis CKD):** 0.45 mcg/kg subcutaneously (SQ) or intravenously (IV) once every 4 weeks.
*   **Standard Starting Dose (Dialysis):** 0.45 mcg/kg IV or SQ once weekly, OR 0.75 mcg/kg once every 2 weeks.
*   **Titration Schedule:**
    *   Do not increase dose more frequently than once every 4 weeks.
    *   If Hb rises < 1.0 g/dL in 4 weeks and remains < 10 g/dL: Increase dose by 25%.
    *   If Hb rises > 1.0 g/dL in any 2-week period: Decrease dose by 25% or more.
    *   If Hb approaches or exceeds 11.0 g/dL: Reduce dose or interrupt therapy.

**CKD Dose Adjustments by eGFR Range:**
*Note: Aranesp (darbepoetin alfa) clearance is not dependent on renal function. Dosing is dictated by dialysis status and Hb trajectory, not specific eGFR cutoffs.*
*   **eGFR ≥60:** Standard ND-CKD dosing (0.45 mcg/kg q4 weeks). No renal adjustment.
*   **eGFR 30-59:** Standard ND-CKD dosing (0.45 mcg/kg q4 weeks). No renal adjustment.
*   **eGFR 15-29:** Standard ND-CKD dosing (0.45 mcg/kg q4 weeks). No renal adjustment.
*   **eGFR <15 / Dialysis:** Switch to Dialysis dosing (0.45 mcg/kg weekly or 0.75 mcg/kg q2 weeks). IV route preferred for hemodialysis patients.

**Hepatic Adjustments:** No specific dosage adjustments provided in the manufacturer's labeling; use with caution.

### Monitoring
*   **Baseline Labs:** CBC (specifically Hb/Hct), Iron panel (TSAT, Ferritin), Reticulocyte count, Folate, Vitamin B12.
*   **Follow-up Schedule:**
    *   **Initiation/Titration Phase:** Monitor Hb weekly to biweekly until stable.
    *   **Maintenance Phase (ND-CKD):** Monitor Hb at least monthly.
    *   **Maintenance Phase (Dialysis):** Monitor Hb at least monthly (often checked biweekly in HD units).
    *   **Iron Stores:** Monitor TSAT and Ferritin every 3 months (or monthly if actively replacing iron).
*   **Therapeutic Targets:**
    *   **Hb Target:** 10.0 - 11.0 g/dL (Do NOT exceed 11.5 g/dL).
    *   **TSAT Target:** > 20% (ideally 20-30%).
    *   **Ferritin Target:** > 100 ng/mL (ND-CKD) or > 200 ng/mL (Dialysis).

### Adverse Effects & Safety
*   **Common Side Effects (>5%):** Hypertension, dyspnea, peripheral edema, cough, procedural hypotension (in dialysis).
*   **Serious/Rare Side Effects:** Seizures, pure red cell aplasia (PRCA) due to neutralizing antibodies, severe cutaneous reactions (SJS/TEN).
*   **Black Box Warnings:**
    *   **Cardiovascular Events:** Increased risk of death, myocardial infarction, stroke, venous thromboembolism, and thrombosis of vascular access when Hb targeted > 11 g/dL.
    *   **Cancer:** May promote tumor progression and shorten overall survival in patients with certain cancers (e.g., breast, non-small cell lung, head and neck, lymphoid, cervical).
*   **Contraindications:** Uncontrolled hypertension, pure red cell aplasia (PRCA) that begins after treatment with any ESA, serious allergic reactions to Aranesp (darbepoetin alfa).
*   **CKD-Specific Safety Concerns:** Increased risk of vascular access (AV fistula/graft) thrombosis. Rapid rises in Hb (>1 g/dL over 2 weeks) are strongly associated with adverse CV events.

### Drug Interactions
*   **Major Interactions:** No clinically significant pharmacokinetic drug interactions identified. However, concurrent use with ACE inhibitors or ARBs may slightly blunt the erythropoietic response, requiring higher ESA doses.
*   **CYP450 Interactions:** None.
*   **Food Interactions:** None.

### Special Populations
*   **Pregnancy:** Category C (prior system). Use only if potential benefit justifies the potential risk to the fetus.
*   **Elderly:** No specific dose adjustments. Monitor closely for hypertension and CV events.
*   **Transplant:** Used off-label for post-transplant anemia. Monitor closely as calcineurin inhibitors (tacrolimus/cyclosporine) can exacerbate hypertension when combined with ESAs.

### Patient Counseling Points
*   **Expectations:** Aranesp (darbepoetin alfa) will not work immediately; it takes several weeks to see an improvement in energy levels and anemia symptoms.
*   **Warning Signs:** Seek immediate emergency care for signs of stroke (face drooping, arm weakness, speech difficulty), heart attack (chest pain, shortness of breath), or blood clots (leg pain/swelling).
*   **Blood Pressure:** This medication can increase blood pressure. Monitor BP at home daily and report readings > 140/90 mmHg (or patient-specific target).
*   **Storage:** Store in the refrigerator (36°F to 46°F). Do not freeze. Protect from light. Keep in original carton until use.

### Prior Authorization & Coding
*   **ICD-10 Codes:** 
    *   D63.1 (Anemia in chronic kidney disease)
    *   N18.3, N18.4, N18.5, N18.6 (CKD stages 3, 4, 5, ESRD)
*   **CPT/HCPCS Codes:**
    *   J0881 (Injection, darbepoetin alfa, 1 mcg - Non-ESRD use)
    *   J0882 (Injection, darbepoetin alfa, 1 mcg - ESRD on dialysis use)
*   **PA Tips:** Most payers require documentation of baseline Hb < 10 g/dL, adequate iron stores (TSAT > 20%, Ferritin > 100 ng/mL), and documentation that blood pressure is controlled. [TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED: Standardize PA template with local lab integration].

## Epogen, Procrit (epoetin alfa)

### Mechanism of Action
Epogen/Procrit (epoetin alfa) is a 165-amino acid glycoprotein manufactured by recombinant DNA technology. It has the identical amino acid sequence as endogenous human erythropoietin. It binds to the erythropoietin receptor on erythroid progenitor cells, stimulating proliferation and differentiation into mature red blood cells.

### Indications in Nephrology
*   **FDA-Approved:** Treatment of anemia due to CKD, including patients on dialysis and patients not on dialysis.
*   **Key Supporting Clinical Trials:**
    *   **CHOIR Trial (2006):** Evaluated Epogen/Procrit (epoetin alfa) in 1,432 ND-CKD patients. Target Hb 13.5 g/dL vs. 11.3 g/dL. 
    *   *Outcomes:* The higher Hb target group experienced a significantly higher rate of the composite endpoint (death, MI, hospitalization for CHF, stroke) (HR 1.34). 
    *   **CREATE Trial (2006):** Target Hb 13.0-15.0 g/dL vs. 10.5-11.5 g/dL. Showed no CV benefit and a trend toward faster progression to dialysis in the higher target group.

### Dosing
**Prerequisite:** Evaluate iron status before starting. Ensure TSAT >20% and Ferritin >100 ng/mL (ND-CKD) or >200 ng/mL (Dialysis).

*   **Standard Starting Dose (Non-Dialysis CKD):** 50 to 100 Units/kg IV or SQ 3 times weekly. *(Clinical Note: In ND-CKD practice, this is frequently adapted off-label to 10,000 Units SQ once weekly or 20,000 Units every 2 weeks to improve compliance).*
*   **Standard Starting Dose (Dialysis):** 50 to 100 Units/kg IV or SQ 3 times weekly.
*   **Titration Schedule:**
    *   Do not increase dose more frequently than once every 4 weeks.
    *   If Hb rises < 1.0 g/dL in 4 weeks and remains < 10 g/dL: Increase dose by 25%.
    *   If Hb rises > 1.0 g/dL in any 2-week period: Decrease dose by 25% or more.
    *   If Hb approaches or exceeds 11.0 g/dL: Reduce dose or interrupt therapy.

**CKD Dose Adjustments by eGFR Range:**
*Note: Epogen/Procrit (epoetin alfa) clearance is not dependent on renal function. Dosing is dictated by dialysis status and Hb trajectory.*
*   **eGFR ≥60:** Standard ND-CKD dosing. No renal adjustment.
*   **eGFR 30-59:** Standard ND-CKD dosing. No renal adjustment.
*   **eGFR 15-29:** Standard ND-CKD dosing. No renal adjustment.
*   **eGFR <15 / Dialysis:** Switch to Dialysis dosing (50-100 Units/kg 3x weekly). IV route preferred for hemodialysis patients.

**Hepatic Adjustments:** No specific dosage adjustments provided; use with caution.

### Monitoring
*   **Baseline Labs:** CBC (Hb/Hct), Iron panel (TSAT, Ferritin), Reticulocyte count, Folate, Vitamin B12.
*   **Follow-up Schedule:**
    *   **Initiation/Titration Phase:** Monitor Hb weekly to biweekly until stable.
    *   **Maintenance Phase:** Monitor Hb at least monthly.
    *   **Iron Stores:** Monitor TSAT and Ferritin every 3 months.
*   **Therapeutic Targets:**
    *   **Hb Target:** 10.0 - 11.0 g/dL.
    *   **TSAT Target:** > 20%.
    *   **Ferritin Target:** > 100 ng/mL (ND-CKD) or > 200 ng/mL (Dialysis).

### Adverse Effects & Safety
*   **Common Side Effects (>5%):** Hypertension, joint pain, muscle spasm, pyrexia, dizziness, vascular access thrombosis.
*   **Serious/Rare Side Effects:** Seizures, pure red cell aplasia (PRCA), severe allergic reactions.
*   **Black Box Warnings:**
    *   **Cardiovascular Events:** Increased risk of death, MI, stroke, VTE, and thrombosis of vascular access when Hb targeted > 11 g/dL.
    *   **Cancer:** May promote tumor progression and shorten overall survival.
*   **Contraindications:** Uncontrolled hypertension, pure red cell aplasia (PRCA) that begins after treatment with any ESA, serious allergic reactions. Multi-dose vials contain benzyl alcohol and are contraindicated in neonates, infants, pregnant women, and nursing mothers.
*   **CKD-Specific Safety Concerns:** High risk of AV fistula/graft clotting. Monitor dialysis access closely.

### Drug Interactions
*   **Major Interactions:** ACE inhibitors or ARBs may blunt the erythropoietic response.
*   **CYP450 Interactions:** None.
*   **Food Interactions:** None.

### Special Populations
*   **Pregnancy:** Category C. Multi-dose vials contain benzyl alcohol (associated with fatal "gasping syndrome" in premature infants) and MUST be avoided. If necessary, use single-dose, preservative-free vials.
*   **Elderly:** Monitor closely for hypertension and CV events.
*   **Transplant:** Post-transplant anemia use is common but off-label.

### Patient Counseling Points
*   **Expectations:** Epogen/Procrit (epoetin alfa) requires frequent dosing initially. It will take weeks to feel the full effect on fatigue.
*   **Warning Signs:** Report severe headaches, sudden confusion, chest pain, or shortness of breath immediately.
*   **Blood Pressure:** Daily home BP monitoring is required. Do not administer if BP is severely elevated (e.g., >160/100 mmHg) without consulting the clinic.
*   **Storage:** Store in the refrigerator (36°F to 46°F). Do not freeze or shake vigorously.

### Prior Authorization & Coding
*   **ICD-10 Codes:** 
    *   D63.1 (Anemia in chronic kidney disease)
    *   N18.x (CKD stages)
*   **CPT/HCPCS Codes:**
    *   J0885 (Injection, epoetin alfa, 1000 units - Non-ESRD use)
    *   J0886 (Injection, epoetin alfa, 1000 units - ESRD on dialysis use)
*   **PA Tips:** Similar to Aranesp. Ensure iron studies are up to date within the last 30-90 days depending on payer.

### Decision Tree: Starting, Escalating, and Stopping Therapy

**1. When to START an ESA:**
*   Hb < 10.0 g/dL **AND**
*   Adequate iron stores confirmed (TSAT > 20%, Ferritin > 100/200 ng/mL) **AND**
*   Other causes of anemia ruled out (B12/Folate deficiency, GI bleed, hemolysis) **AND**
*   Blood pressure is adequately controlled.

**2. When to ESCALATE therapy:**
*   Hb increase is < 1.0 g/dL after 4 weeks of therapy.
*   *Action:* Increase dose by 25%. Ensure iron stores haven't depleted (ESAs consume iron rapidly).

**3. When to SWITCH agents:**
*   **Reason for switch:** Usually driven by insurance formulary preferences or patient compliance (switching from Epogen to Aranesp for less frequent injections).
*   **Conversion Ratio:** 
    *   *Rule of thumb:* 200 Units of epoetin alfa = 1 mcg of darbepoetin alfa.
    *   *Example:* A patient on Epogen 10,000 Units weekly (40,000 Units/month) would convert to approximately Aranesp 200 mcg per month (e.g., 100 mcg q2 weeks or 200 mcg q4 weeks).

**4. When to STOP or HOLD therapy:**
*   Hb approaches or exceeds 11.0 g/dL (Hold or reduce dose by 25%).
*   Rapid Hb rise (> 1.0 g/dL in a 2-week period).
*   Patient develops uncontrolled hypertension or hypertensive encephalopathy.
*   Patient experiences a stroke, MI, or severe VTE.
*   Suspected Pure Red Cell Aplasia (PRCA) - characterized by sudden loss of efficacy and severe drop in reticulocyte count.

### Choice of Agent
*   **In-Center Hemodialysis:** Epogen/Procrit (epoetin alfa) is historically preferred due to the convenience of 3x weekly dosing matching the dialysis schedule, though Aranesp is also widely used.
*   **Non-Dialysis CKD (ND-CKD) & Peritoneal Dialysis (PD):** Aranesp (darbepoetin alfa) is strongly preferred due to its longer half-life, allowing for once-monthly or bi-weekly subcutaneous injections, significantly improving patient compliance and reducing clinic visits.
