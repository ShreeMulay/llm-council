# Soluble Guanylate Cyclase (sGC) Stimulators

Soluble guanylate cyclase (sGC) stimulators are a novel class of medications increasingly relevant in cardiorenal medicine. In nephrology practice, these agents are primarily encountered in the management of high-risk patients with chronic kidney disease (CKD) and concomitant heart failure with reduced ejection fraction (HFrEF) who have experienced a recent worsening heart failure event. 

## Verquvo (vericiguat)

### Mechanism of Action
Verquvo (vericiguat) is a direct stimulator of soluble guanylate cyclase (sGC), an important enzyme in the nitric oxide (NO) signaling pathway. In patients with heart failure and CKD, endothelial dysfunction leads to decreased NO availability and subsequent sGC insufficiency. Verquvo (vericiguat) works via a dual mechanism:
1. Directly stimulates sGC independently of NO.
2. Sensitizes sGC to endogenous NO.
This restores the NO-sGC-cGMP pathway, leading to increased intracellular cyclic guanosine monophosphate (cGMP), which promotes smooth muscle relaxation, vasodilation, and potentially reduces cardiac remodeling and fibrosis.

### Indications in Nephrology
**FDA-Approved Indications:**
*   To reduce the risk of cardiovascular death and heart failure (HF) hospitalization following a hospitalization for HF or need for outpatient intravenous (IV) diuretics, in adults with symptomatic chronic HF and ejection fraction <45%.

**Relevance to Nephrology Practice:**
*   **Cardiorenal Syndrome:** Highly relevant for CKD patients with HFrEF who remain symptomatic or have recurrent volume overload requiring IV diuretics despite optimal Guideline-Directed Medical Therapy (GDMT).
*   **Guideline Support:** The **2022 AHA/ACC/HFSA Heart Failure Guidelines** assign a Class 2b recommendation for Verquvo (vericiguat) in select high-risk patients with HFrEF and recent worsening HF already on foundational GDMT.

**Key Supporting Clinical Trials:**
*   **VICTORIA Trial (2020):** Evaluated Verquvo (vericiguat) vs. placebo in 5,050 patients with HFrEF (EF <45%) and a recent worsening HF event. 
    *   *Renal Inclusion:* Included patients with eGFR down to 15 mL/min/1.73 m².
    *   *Outcomes:* Demonstrated a 10% relative risk reduction (HR 0.90; 95% CI, 0.82-0.98) in the primary composite endpoint of CV death or HF hospitalization.
    *   *CKD Subgroup:* Efficacy was maintained across eGFR categories, making it a viable option for advanced CKD patients where other GDMT (like ARNIs or MRAs) might be limited by hyperkalemia or severe renal dysfunction.

### Dosing
**Standard Dosing & Titration Schedule:**
*   **Starting Dose:** 2.5 mg orally once daily with food.
*   **Titration:** Double the dose approximately every 2 weeks as tolerated by blood pressure and symptoms.
*   **Target Dose:** 10 mg orally once daily.

**CKD Dose Adjustments by eGFR Range:**
*   **eGFR ≥60 mL/min/1.73 m²:** 2.5 mg daily, titrate to 10 mg daily.
*   **eGFR 30-59 mL/min/1.73 m²:** 2.5 mg daily, titrate to 10 mg daily (No adjustment needed).
*   **eGFR 15-29 mL/min/1.73 m²:** 2.5 mg daily, titrate to 10 mg daily (No adjustment needed).
*   **eGFR <15 mL/min/1.73 m² / Dialysis:** Not recommended (Safety and efficacy have not been established in this population).

**Hepatic Adjustments:**
*   Mild to moderate hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh A or B): No adjustment required.
*   Severe hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh C): Not recommended (not studied).

### Monitoring
**Baseline Labs & Vitals Required Before Starting:**
*   Blood pressure (Do not initiate if symptomatic hypotension or SBP <90 mmHg).
*   Pregnancy test (in females of reproductive potential).
*   Complete Blood Count (CBC) to assess baseline hemoglobin/hematocrit.
*   Comprehensive Metabolic Panel (CMP) for baseline renal and hepatic function.

**Follow-up Schedule:**
*   **2 Weeks (at each titration step):** Check BP and assess for orthostatic symptoms.
*   **3 Months:** Check CBC (monitor for anemia).
*   **Routine CKD Care:** Continue standard monitoring of eGFR, potassium, and target UACR (<30 mg/g) as part of comprehensive cardiorenal care.

**Therapeutic Drug Monitoring:**
*   None required. Efficacy is monitored via clinical response (reduction in HF symptoms, stable weight, absence of hospitalizations).

**Decision Tree: When to Start, Escalate, or Stop [TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED]**
*   **START:** Patient with HFrEF (EF <45%) + eGFR ≥15 + recent HF hospitalization or outpatient IV diuretic use + already on maximally tolerated GDMT (SGLT2i, beta-blocker, ARNI/ACEi, MRA).
*   **ESCALATE:** Increase dose every 2 weeks if SBP remains >90 mmHg and patient is asymptomatic for hypotension.
*   **STOP:** Discontinue if pregnancy occurs. Hold or reduce dose if severe symptomatic hypotension or unexplained significant anemia develops.

### Adverse Effects & Safety
**Common Side Effects (>5%):**
*   Hypotension (16% vs 15% placebo in VICTORIA)
*   Anemia (10% vs 7% placebo in VICTORIA)

**Serious/Rare Side Effects:**
*   Syncope
*   Severe symptomatic hypotension

**Black Box Warnings:**
*   **⚠️ EMBRYO-FETAL TOXICITY:** Verquvo (vericiguat) is contraindicated in pregnancy. Females of reproductive potential must use effective contraception during treatment and for 1 month after stopping the medication.

**Contraindications:**
*   **Absolute:** Pregnancy.
*   **Absolute:** Concomitant use of other sGC stimulators (e.g., Adempas [riociguat]).
*   **Relative:** Concomitant use of PDE-5 inhibitors (e.g., sildenafil, tadalafil) is not recommended due to the potential for severe hypotension.

**CKD-Specific Safety Concerns:**
*   Unlike RAAS inhibitors, Verquvo (vericiguat) does *not* typically cause hyperkalemia or acute declines in eGFR, making it an attractive add-on therapy in advanced CKD.
*   CKD patients are at higher baseline risk for anemia (due to decreased EPO production); the addition of Verquvo (vericiguat) may exacerbate renal anemia, requiring closer monitoring of hemoglobin and potential adjustment of Erythropoiesis-Stimulating Agents (ESAs).

### Drug Interactions
*   **PDE-5 Inhibitors (sildenafil, tadalafil, vardenafil):** Concomitant use is not recommended due to synergistic vasodilation and risk of severe hypotension.
*   **Other sGC Stimulators (Adempas [riociguat]):** Contraindicated.
*   **Nitrates (short- and long-acting):** Co-administration may lead to increased risk of hypotension. Use with caution.
*   **CYP450 Interactions:** None clinically significant. Verquvo (vericiguat) is primarily metabolized by UGT1A9 and UGT1A1 (glucuronidation).
*   **Food Interactions:** **Must be taken with food.** Food increases the bioavailability and reduces pharmacokinetic variability.

### Special Populations
*   **Pregnancy:** Contraindicated (Category X equivalent). Exclude pregnancy before starting.
*   **Lactation:** Not recommended.
*   **Elderly in CKD:** No specific dose adjustments needed based on age. However, elderly patients with CKD may be more susceptible to orthostatic hypotension; titrate cautiously.
*   **Transplant Considerations:** No specific contraindications, but monitor BP closely as concomitant use with antihypertensives and calcineurin inhibitors (which affect vascular tone) requires careful hemodynamic balancing.

### Patient Counseling Points
*   **Administration:** "Take this medication exactly as prescribed, once a day, **with food**."
*   **Pregnancy Warning:** "This medication can cause severe birth defects. You must use reliable birth control while taking this and for one month after stopping."
*   **Warning Signs to Report:** "Call the clinic if you experience dizziness, lightheadedness, fainting, or extreme fatigue (signs of low blood pressure or anemia)."
*   **Missed Dose:** "If you miss a dose, take it as soon as you remember on the same day. Do not take two doses on the same day to make up for a missed dose."
*   **Medication Interactions:** "Do not take medications for erectile dysfunction (like Viagra or Cialis) while on this drug, as it can cause a dangerous drop in your blood pressure."

*   Document that the patient is already on, or has contraindications to, foundational GDMT (beta-blockers, ARNIs, SGLT2is, MRAs).

## Class Comparison: sGC Stimulators

While Verquvo (vericiguat) is the primary sGC stimulator utilized in cardiorenal/HFrEF management, it is important to distinguish it from Adempas (riociguat), which belongs to the same class but has entirely different indications (pulmonary hypertension). 

**Table: Comparison of sGC Stimulators**

| Feature | Verquvo (vericiguat) | Adempas (riociguat) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primary Indication** | HFrEF (EF <45%) post-worsening HF event | PAH (Group 1) and CTEPH (Group 4) |
| **Relevance to Nephrology** | High (Cardiorenal syndrome, HFrEF in CKD) | Low (Primarily managed by Pulmonology/Cardiology) |
| **Starting Dose** | 2.5 mg once daily (with food) | 1 mg three times daily |
| **Target Dose** | 10 mg once daily | 2.5 mg three times daily |
| **eGFR Thresholds** | Safe down to eGFR 15 mL/min/1.73 m² | No specific cutoff, but use caution in severe CKD |
| **Key Clinical Trial** | VICTORIA (2020) | PATENT-1, CHEST-1 |
| **Black Box Warning** | Embryo-Fetal Toxicity | Embryo-Fetal Toxicity (Requires REMS program) |
| **Cost / Availability** | High / Brand only | High / Brand only (Restricted distribution) |

### When to Choose One Agent Over Another
*   There is **no clinical overlap** in the indications for these two drugs. 
*   **Verquvo (vericiguat)** is chosen strictly for patients with left-sided heart failure (HFrEF) to reduce cardiovascular mortality and HF hospitalizations.
*   **Adempas (riociguat)** is chosen strictly for pulmonary arterial hypertension (PAH) or chronic thromboembolic pulmonary hypertension (CTEPH). 

### Switching Between Agents Within the Class
*   **Do not switch** between these agents, as they are not interchangeable and treat entirely different disease states. 
*   **Absolute Contraindication:** Never use Verquvo (vericiguat) and Adempas (riociguat) together due to the risk of profound, life-threatening hypotension.
