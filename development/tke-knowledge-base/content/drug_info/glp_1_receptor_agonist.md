# Clinical Drug Monograph: GLP-1 Receptor Agonists
**Reference Guide for The Kidney Experts (TKE)**

**Guideline Context:** Per the *KDIGO 2022/2024 Clinical Practice Guideline for Diabetes Management in CKD* and *ADA 2024 Standards of Care*, Glucagon-Like Peptide-1 Receptor Agonists (GLP-1 RAs) are recommended for patients with Type 2 Diabetes (T2DM) and Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) who have not achieved individualized glycemic targets despite the use of metformin and SGLT2 inhibitors, or who are unable to use those medications. GLP-1 RAs with proven cardiovascular (CV) benefit are strongly preferred. 

**General Lab Targets:** 
*   **HbA1c:** Individualized; generally <6.5% to <8.0% depending on age, comorbidities, and hypoglycemia risk.
*   **UACR:** Target <30 mg/g (GLP-1 RAs have demonstrated robust albuminuria-lowering effects).

## Ozempic, Wegovy, Rybelsus (semaglutide)

### Mechanism of Action
Semaglutide is a long-acting GLP-1 receptor agonist. It selectively binds to and activates the GLP-1 receptor, increasing glucose-dependent insulin secretion, decreasing inappropriate glucagon secretion, and slowing gastric emptying. It also acts centrally to decrease appetite and food intake.

### Indications in Nephrology
*   **FDA-Approved:** 
    *   T2DM glycemic control (Ozempic, Rybelsus).
    *   Reduction of major adverse cardiovascular events (MACE) in adults with T2DM and established CVD (Ozempic).
    *   Chronic weight management in adults with BMI ≥30 or ≥27 with weight-related comorbidities (Wegovy).
    *   Reduction of MACE in adults with established CVD and obesity/overweight (Wegovy).
*   **Off-Label in Nephrology:** Proteinuria reduction in non-diabetic obesity-related glomerulopathy (emerging).
*   **Key Clinical Trials:**
    *   **FLOW (2024):** Stopped early for overwhelming efficacy. Demonstrated a 24% reduction in the composite endpoint of kidney disease progression (≥50% eGFR decline, onset of ESRD), CV death, or kidney death in patients with T2DM and CKD.
    *   **SUSTAIN-6 (2016):** Showed 26% reduction in MACE and 36% reduction in new or worsening nephropathy (primarily driven by macroalbuminuria reduction).
    *   **STEP trials:** Demonstrated ~15% body weight reduction (Wegovy).

### Dosing
*   **Ozempic (SubQ weekly):** Start 0.25 mg weekly x 4 weeks. Increase to 0.5 mg weekly. If target A1c not met after 4 weeks, increase to 1 mg, then up to max 2 mg weekly.
*   **Wegovy (SubQ weekly):** Start 0.25 mg weekly. Titrate every 4 weeks (0.5 mg → 1 mg → 1.7 mg → 2.4 mg). Max 2.4 mg weekly.
*   **Rybelsus (Oral daily):** Start 3 mg once daily x 30 days. Increase to 7 mg daily. If target A1c not met after 30 days, increase to max 14 mg daily. *Must be taken on an empty stomach with ≤4 oz water, 30 mins before other food/meds.*

**CKD Dose Adjustments by eGFR Range:**
*   **eGFR ≥60:** No adjustment.
*   **eGFR 30-59:** No adjustment.
*   **eGFR 15-29:** No adjustment.
*   **eGFR <15 / Dialysis:** No adjustment required by label, but use with extreme caution. GI side effects can cause severe volume depletion and hemodynamic instability on dialysis. [TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED: Standardize initiation dose for ESRD patients].
*   **Hepatic:** No adjustment required.

### Monitoring
*   **Baseline:** HbA1c, CMP (SCr, eGFR, LFTs), UACR, Amylase/Lipase (if history of pancreatitis).
*   **Follow-up:** Recheck CMP and A1c 3 months after initiation or dose changes. Monitor weight and BP at every clinic visit.
*   **TDM:** Not applicable.

### Adverse Effects & Safety
*   **Common (>5%):** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, constipation. (GI effects are dose-dependent and usually transient).
*   **Serious/Rare:** Acute pancreatitis, gallbladder disease (cholelithiasis), diabetic retinopathy complications (transient worsening with rapid A1c drop).
*   **Black Box Warning:** Risk of thyroid C-cell tumors. 
*   **Contraindications:** Personal or family history of Medullary Thyroid Carcinoma (MTC) or Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN 2). Prior hypersensitivity.
*   **CKD-Specific Safety Concerns:** **Acute Kidney Injury (AKI).** AKI is almost exclusively secondary to volume depletion from severe nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea. Counsel patients heavily on hydration.

### Drug Interactions
*   **Oral Medications:** Delayed gastric emptying may impact absorption of narrow therapeutic index oral drugs.
*   **Insulin / Sulfonylureas:** Increased risk of hypoglycemia. Consider reducing insulin/SU dose by 20-50% when initiating.
*   **Food:** Rybelsus absorption is severely impaired by food/other fluids.

### Special Populations
*   **Pregnancy:** Category not assigned (formerly C). **Stop at least 2 months prior to planned pregnancy** due to long half-life.
*   **Elderly:** Higher risk of volume depletion and AKI. Titrate slowly.
*   **Transplant:** Delayed gastric emptying can cause erratic absorption of **tacrolimus, cyclosporine, and mycophenolate**. [TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED: Check CNI trough levels 1-2 weeks after starting or titrating any GLP-1 RA in transplant recipients].

### Patient Counseling Points
*   "This medication will slow your digestion, which helps you feel full but can cause nausea. Eat smaller meals and stop eating when you feel full."
*   "Stay well hydrated. If you experience severe vomiting or diarrhea, stop the medication and call our office, as this can harm your kidneys."
*   **Administration:** (Ozempic/Wegovy) Inject on the same day each week. Rotate sites (abdomen, thigh, back of arm). (Rybelsus) Take first thing in the morning with a sip of water; wait 30 minutes before eating or taking other pills.

### Prior Authorization & Coding
*   **ICD-10:** E11.22 (T2DM with diabetic CKD), E11.9 (T2DM w/o complications), E66.01 (Morbid obesity - for Wegovy), Z68.xx (BMI codes).
*   **CPT:** 99213-99215 (E&M), 98960 (Education/training for patient self-management).
*   **PA Tips:** For Ozempic/Rybelsus, document failure/intolerance to metformin. For Wegovy, document BMI and participation in a comprehensive weight management program.

## Victoza, Saxenda (liraglutide)

### Mechanism of Action
Liraglutide is a once-daily GLP-1 receptor agonist that increases glucose-dependent insulin release, decreases glucagon secretion, and delays gastric emptying.

### Indications in Nephrology
*   **FDA-Approved:** 
    *   T2DM glycemic control (Victoza).
    *   Reduction of MACE in adults with T2DM and established CVD (Victoza).
    *   Chronic weight management (Saxenda).
*   **Key Clinical Trials:**
    *   **LEADER (2016):** Demonstrated 13% reduction in MACE. Secondary renal composite outcome (new onset macroalbuminuria, doubling of SCr, ESRD, or renal death) was reduced by 22%, primarily driven by a reduction in new-onset macroalbuminuria.

### Dosing
*   **Victoza (SubQ daily):** Start 0.6 mg daily x 1 week (to reduce GI symptoms; 0.6 mg is not an effective glycemic dose). Increase to 1.2 mg daily. Max 1.8 mg daily.
*   **Saxenda (SubQ daily):** Start 0.6 mg daily. Increase by 0.6 mg weekly to target dose of 3.0 mg daily.

**CKD Dose Adjustments by eGFR Range:**
*   **eGFR ≥60:** No adjustment.
*   **eGFR 30-59:** No adjustment.
*   **eGFR 15-29:** No adjustment.
*   **eGFR <15 / Dialysis:** No adjustment required by label, but limited data exists. Use with caution due to GI-induced volume depletion risk.
*   **Hepatic:** No adjustment required.

### Monitoring
*   **Baseline:** HbA1c, CMP, UACR.
*   **Follow-up:** Recheck CMP and A1c in 3 months. 

### Adverse Effects & Safety
*   **Common (>5%):** Nausea, diarrhea, vomiting, decreased appetite, dyspepsia.
*   **Serious/Rare:** Pancreatitis, gallbladder disease.
*   **Black Box Warning:** Risk of thyroid C-cell tumors.
*   **Contraindications:** Personal/family history of MTC or MEN 2. Pregnancy (Saxenda).
*   **CKD-Specific Safety Concerns:** AKI secondary to volume depletion.

### Drug Interactions
*   Similar to semaglutide. Monitor narrow therapeutic index drugs due to delayed gastric emptying. Reduce insulin/SU doses to prevent hypoglycemia.

### Special Populations
*   **Pregnancy:** Saxenda is contraindicated. Victoza should be used only if potential benefit justifies the risk.
*   **Transplant:** Monitor CNI troughs closely upon initiation/titration.

### Patient Counseling Points
*   "Inject this medication once a day, at any time of day, independent of meals."
*   "Nausea is common in the first few weeks but usually improves. Drink plenty of water."
*   "Report any severe abdominal pain that radiates to your back (signs of pancreatitis)."

### Prior Authorization & Coding
*   **ICD-10:** E11.22, E11.21 (T2DM with nephropathy), E66.01 (for Saxenda).
*   **PA Tips:** Often a preferred formulary agent if weekly GLP-1 RAs are not covered.

## Trulicity (dulaglutide)

### Mechanism of Action
Dulaglutide is a once-weekly GLP-1 receptor agonist consisting of GLP-1 covalently linked to an IgG4-Fc heavy chain, protecting it from DPP-4 degradation and extending its half-life.

### Indications in Nephrology
*   **FDA-Approved:**
    *   T2DM glycemic control.
    *   Reduction of MACE in adults with T2DM and established CVD or multiple CV risk factors.
*   **Key Clinical Trials:**
    *   **REWIND (2019):** Showed 12% reduction in MACE. Secondary renal outcome (new macroalbuminuria, sustained ≥30% eGFR decline, or ESRD) was reduced by 15%.
    *   **AWARD-7 (2018):** Specifically studied in patients with moderate-to-severe CKD (Stages 3-4). Showed dulaglutide provided similar glycemic control to insulin glargine but with significantly less eGFR decline and reduced albuminuria over 52 weeks.

### Dosing
*   **Trulicity (SubQ weekly):** Start 0.75 mg weekly. May increase to 1.5 mg weekly after 4 weeks. If further glycemic control is needed, titrate by 1.5 mg increments every 4 weeks up to a maximum of 4.5 mg weekly.

**CKD Dose Adjustments by eGFR Range:**
*   **eGFR ≥60:** No adjustment.
*   **eGFR 30-59:** No adjustment.
*   **eGFR 15-29:** No adjustment.
*   **eGFR <15 / Dialysis:** No adjustment required by label. AWARD-7 trial included patients down to eGFR 15. Use caution in ESRD regarding volume status.
*   **Hepatic:** No adjustment required.

### Monitoring
*   **Baseline:** HbA1c, CMP, UACR.
*   **Follow-up:** Recheck CMP and A1c in 3 months.

### Adverse Effects & Safety
*   **Common (>5%):** Nausea, diarrhea, vomiting, abdominal pain, decreased appetite.
*   **Serious/Rare:** Pancreatitis, acute gallbladder disease.
*   **Black Box Warning:** Risk of thyroid C-cell tumors.
*   **Contraindications:** Personal/family history of MTC or MEN 2.
*   **CKD-Specific Safety Concerns:** AKI secondary to GI volume depletion.

### Drug Interactions
*   Delayed gastric emptying affects oral drug absorption. Reduce insulin/SU doses to prevent hypoglycemia.

### Special Populations
*   **Pregnancy:** Discontinue at least 2 months before planned pregnancy.
*   **Transplant:** Monitor CNI troughs closely.

### Patient Counseling Points
*   "The Trulicity pen is a single-use, auto-injector. You will not see the needle. Press the pen against your skin, unlock it, and press the button until you hear a click."
*   "Take it on the same day every week. If you miss a dose, take it as soon as you remember, as long as the next dose is at least 3 days away."

### Prior Authorization & Coding
*   **ICD-10:** E11.22, E11.9.
*   **PA Tips:** Highly successful PA rates for T2DM with established CKD due to AWARD-7 and REWIND data.

| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Route / Freq** | SubQ Weekly / Oral Daily | SubQ Daily | SubQ Weekly |
| **eGFR Cutoff** | None (Caution in ESRD) | None (Caution in ESRD) | None (Caution in ESRD) |
| **Key CKD Trial** | FLOW (2024), SUSTAIN-6 | LEADER | AWARD-7, REWIND |
| **CV Benefit** | Yes (MACE reduction) | Yes (MACE reduction) | Yes (MACE reduction) |
| **A1c Lowering** | High (Highest in class) | Intermediate | High |
| **Weight Loss** | High (Highest in class) | Intermediate | Intermediate |
| **Cost / Access** | $$$ (Frequent shortages) | $$ (Generic Victoza available 2024) | $$$ |

### Decision Tree: Choosing an Agent in Nephrology
1.  **Is the primary goal CKD progression delay and robust proteinuria reduction?**
    *   **1st Choice:** Semaglutide (Ozempic) based on dedicated FLOW trial data showing 24% reduction in kidney outcomes.
    *   **2nd Choice:** Dulaglutide (Trulicity) based on AWARD-7 data specifically in CKD 3-4.
2.  **Is the patient needle-phobic?**
    *   **Choice:** Rybelsus (oral semaglutide). *Note: Strict fasting administration rules apply.*
3.  **Is the patient experiencing severe supply chain/shortage issues with weekly agents?**
    *   **Choice:** Switch to Liraglutide (Victoza) daily or Rybelsus daily.
4.  **Is the primary goal weight loss (Obesity without T2DM)?**
    *   **Choice:** Wegovy or Saxenda (ensure PA is routed for weight management, not T2DM).

### Decision Tree: Switching Between Agents
*   **Daily to Weekly (e.g., Victoza to Ozempic/Trulicity):** Start the weekly GLP-1 RA the day after the last dose of the daily GLP-1 RA.
*   **Weekly to Daily (e.g., Ozempic to Victoza):** Start the daily GLP-1 RA 7 days after the last dose of the weekly GLP-1 RA.
*   **Weekly to Weekly (e.g., Trulicity to Ozempic):** Start the new weekly GLP-1 RA 7 days after the last dose of the previous weekly agent.
*   *Titration Note:* When switching due to intolerance, start at the lowest initiation dose of the new drug. When switching for formulary reasons in a patient tolerating high doses, [TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED: Consult clinical pharmacist for equivalent dose mapping to avoid loss of glycemic control].

### Decision Tree: When to STOP Therapy
*   **Stop immediately if:** Suspected pancreatitis (severe abdominal pain radiating to back), suspected gallbladder disease, or severe AKI with intractable vomiting/diarrhea.
*   **Pre-operative hold:** Per ASA guidelines, hold daily GLP-1 RAs on the day of surgery/procedure. Hold weekly GLP-1 RAs for **1 week prior** to surgery/procedure to reduce the risk of regurgitation and pulmonary aspiration under anesthesia.
