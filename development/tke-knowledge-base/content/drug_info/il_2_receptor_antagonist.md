# IL-2 Receptor Antagonists (Anti-CD25) Clinical Monograph

**Guidelines Referenced:** KDIGO Clinical Practice Guideline for the Care of Kidney Transplant Recipients (2009, with 2024 update considerations), AST Infectious Diseases Community of Practice Guidelines.

---

## Simulect (basiliximab)

### Mechanism of Action
Simulect (basiliximab) is a chimeric (murine/human) monoclonal antibody (IgG1k) that acts as an interleukin-2 receptor antagonist (IL-2RA). It specifically binds to and blocks the alpha chain (CD25) of the IL-2 receptor complex on the surface of activated T-lymphocytes. By blocking this receptor, it prevents IL-2 from binding, thereby inhibiting IL-2-mediated activation and proliferation of T-cells, which is a critical pathway in the cellular immune response involved in allograft rejection.

### Indications in Nephrology
**FDA-Approved Indications:**
*   Prophylaxis of acute organ rejection in patients receiving renal transplantation. (Used as part of an immunosuppressive regimen that includes cyclosporine or tacrolimus and corticosteroids).

**Off-Label Uses:**
*   Induction therapy in other solid organ transplants (liver, heart).
*   Treatment of steroid-refractory acute graft-versus-host disease (GVHD).

**Key Supporting Clinical Trials:**
*   **CHIMERA Study & Phase III Trials (Nashan et al., 1997; Ponticelli et al., 1997):** Demonstrated that adding Simulect (basiliximab) to a baseline regimen of cyclosporine and steroids significantly reduced the incidence of biopsy-proven acute rejection at 6 months (approx. 30% in the basiliximab group vs. 44-46% in the placebo group) without increasing the incidence of opportunistic infections or malignancies.
*   **KDIGO Guidelines:** Recommend including induction therapy with a biologic agent as part of the initial immunosuppressive regimen in kidney transplant recipients. An IL-2RA (Simulect) is recommended as the first-line induction agent for patients at **low to standard immunological risk**.

### Dosing
Simulect (basiliximab) is administered as a two-dose regimen. It is administered via central or peripheral intravenous (IV) infusion over 20-30 minutes or as an IV bolus.

*   **Standard Adult Dosing:** 20 mg IV for a total of 2 doses.
    *   **Dose 1:** 20 mg IV given within 2 hours *prior* to transplant surgery.
    *   **Dose 2:** 20 mg IV given 4 days *after* transplantation. *(Note: Withhold Dose 2 if severe hypersensitivity reactions occur or if graft is lost).*

**CKD Dose Adjustments by eGFR Range:**
Simulect (basiliximab) is a monoclonal antibody cleared via the reticuloendothelial system, not by renal excretion. **No dose adjustments are required for renal impairment.**
*   **eGFR ≥60:** 20 mg IV x 2 doses
*   **eGFR 30-59:** 20 mg IV x 2 doses
*   **eGFR 15-29:** 20 mg IV x 2 doses
*   **eGFR <15 / Dialysis:** 20 mg IV x 2 doses

**Hepatic Adjustments:**
*   No dose adjustment required in hepatic impairment.

### Monitoring
**Baseline Labs Required Before Starting:**
*   Comprehensive Metabolic Panel (CMP)
*   Complete Blood Count (CBC) with differential
*   Infectious serologies (CMV, EBV, Hep B, Hep C, HIV, Syphilis, TB screening)
*   Panel Reactive Antibodies (PRA) and crossmatch (to confirm low/standard immunologic risk)

**Follow-up Lab Schedule:**
*   Routine post-transplant monitoring (daily CBC and CMP during initial hospitalization).
*   Monitor for signs of opportunistic infections (CMV PCR, BK virus PCR) per [TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED] post-transplant schedule.

**Therapeutic Drug Monitoring (TDM):**
*   **Not applicable.** Simulect (basiliximab) does not require therapeutic drug monitoring or trough levels. A 40 mg total dose maintains IL-2 receptor saturation for approximately 30 to 45 days post-transplant.

### Adverse Effects & Safety
**Common Side Effects (>5%):**
*   Gastrointestinal: Constipation, nausea, abdominal pain, diarrhea, dyspepsia.
*   Neurologic: Headache, tremor, insomnia.
*   Cardiovascular: Hypertension, peripheral edema.
*   Surgical: Surgical site complications, impaired wound healing.

**Serious/Rare Side Effects:**
*   Severe hypersensitivity reactions / Anaphylaxis (can occur with initial or subsequent doses).
*   Increased risk of opportunistic infections.
*   Increased risk of malignancies (specifically Post-Transplant Lymphoproliferative Disorder [PTLD] and skin cancers), though risk is generally lower than with lymphocyte-depleting agents (e.g., Thymoglobulin).

**Black Box Warnings:**
*   **Immunosuppression:** Only physicians experienced in immunosuppression therapy and management of organ transplant patients should prescribe Simulect (basiliximab). Patients should be managed in facilities equipped with adequate laboratory and supportive medical resources.

**Contraindications:**
*   **Absolute:** Known hypersensitivity to basiliximab or any component of the formulation.

**CKD-Specific Safety Concerns:**
*   Does not cause direct nephrotoxicity (unlike Calcineurin Inhibitors).
*   Does not cause cytokine release syndrome (CRS) or first-dose hypotension, making it hemodynamically safe for immediate post-operative kidney perfusion.

### Drug Interactions
*   **CYP450 Interactions:** None. Monoclonal antibodies are not metabolized by the cytochrome P450 system.
*   **Major Interactions:**
    *   **Immunosuppressants:** Synergistic effect with calcineurin inhibitors (Prograf/tacrolimus, Neoral/cyclosporine), antimetabolites (CellCept/mycophenolate), and corticosteroids. This is desired for efficacy but increases the cumulative risk of infection.
    *   **Live Vaccines:** Contraindicated during and shortly after therapy due to the risk of unchecked vaccine-strain infection.
*   **Food Interactions:** None (IV administration).

### Special Populations
*   **Pregnancy:** Historically Category B. May cross the placenta. Use only if the potential benefit to the mother justifies the potential risk to the fetus.
*   **Lactation:** Unknown if excreted in human milk; breastfeeding is generally not recommended during active immunosuppression.
*   **Elderly Considerations in CKD:** No dosage adjustment necessary. Elderly patients may have a higher baseline risk for infections and malignancies; monitor closely.
*   **Pediatric:** Approved for pediatric use. Dosing: <35 kg: 10 mg IV x 2 doses; ≥35 kg: 20 mg IV x 2 doses.

### Patient Counseling Points
*   **Purpose of Medication:** Explain that Simulect (basiliximab) is an "induction" medication given only twice (during and right after surgery) to temporarily paralyze the immune system's attack cells, preventing early rejection of the new kidney.
*   **Infection Risk:** Advise patients that they will be at a higher risk for infections. They must report fevers (>100.4°F), chills, sore throat, or painful urination immediately.
*   **Allergic Reactions:** Inform patients to report any difficulty breathing, rash, or severe dizziness during or shortly after the infusion.
*   **Administration:** Reassure the patient that this is an IV medication managed entirely by the inpatient transplant team.

### Prior Authorization & Coding
*   **Common ICD-10 Codes:**
    *   `Z48.22` - Encounter for aftercare following kidney transplant
    *   `T86.10` - Unspecified complication of kidney transplant
*   **CPT Codes:**
    *   `J0480` - Injection, basiliximab, 20 mg
    *   `96365` - Intravenous infusion, for therapy, prophylaxis, or diagnosis; initial, up to 1 hour
*   **Prior Authorization Tips:** Simulect is typically administered inpatient during the transplant admission, so it is usually covered under the DRG (Diagnosis-Related Group) global payment for the transplant surgery rather than requiring a separate outpatient PA.

## Class Comparison & Decision Tree

*Note: Simulect (basiliximab) is currently the **only** IL-2 receptor antagonist available on the market. A previous agent, Zenapax (daclizumab), was withdrawn from the global market due to safety concerns (hepatic injury and immune-mediated encephalitis) when used for MS. Therefore, the clinical decision in nephrology is not between different IL-2RAs, but rather between an IL-2RA (Simulect) and a Lymphocyte-Depleting Agent (e.g., Thymoglobulin) for induction.*

### Induction Therapy Comparison Table

| Feature | IL-2 Receptor Antagonist | Lymphocyte-Depleting Agent |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Agent** | **Simulect (basiliximab)** | **Thymoglobulin (rATG)** |
| **Mechanism** | Non-depleting; blocks IL-2 receptor (CD25) | Depleting; polyclonal T-cell destruction |
| **Dosing** | 20 mg IV x 2 doses (Day 0, Day 4) | 1-1.5 mg/kg IV daily for 3-7 days |
| **eGFR Thresholds** | No renal adjustment | No renal adjustment |
| **Target Population** | **Low to standard immunologic risk** | **High immunologic risk** |
| **Side Effect Profile** | Very well tolerated; no cytokine release | Cytokine release syndrome, leukopenia, thrombocytopenia, higher infection risk |
| **T-Cell Recovery** | Function returns as drug clears (weeks) | T-cell depletion lasts months to >1 year |
| **Relative Cost** | Moderate (~$3,000 - $4,000 per dose) | High (varies by weight and duration) |

### Clinical Decision Tree: Choosing Induction Therapy

**[TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED] - Verify with local transplant center affiliations (e.g., local TN transplant centers) for specific immunologic risk stratification criteria.**

1.  **Assess Immunologic Risk:**
    *   *High Risk Criteria:* High PRA (>20-30%), positive crossmatch, repeat transplant, African American ethnicity (center-dependent), prolonged cold ischemia time, DCD (Donation after Circulatory Death) donor.
    *   *Low/Standard Risk Criteria:* First transplant, low PRA, living donor, well-matched HLA.
2.  **Select Agent:**
    *   **If LOW/STANDARD risk:** Start **Simulect (basiliximab)** 20 mg IV pre-op and on post-op Day 4.
    *   **If HIGH risk:** Start **Thymoglobulin (rATG)** (or Alemtuzumab per center protocol).
3.  **Escalation/Switching:**
    *   If a patient is started on Simulect (basiliximab) but experiences immediate severe acute cellular rejection, therapy is escalated to high-dose IV corticosteroids and/or Thymoglobulin. Simulect is *not* used for the treatment of active rejection.
