# Clinical Drug Monograph: GIP/GLP-1 Receptor Agonists

**Class Overview:** The dual glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide (GIP) and glucagon-like peptide-1 (GLP-1) receptor agonist class represents a highly potent therapeutic option for glycemic control and weight management. Currently, tirzepatide is the only FDA-approved agent in this class. In nephrology, it is increasingly utilized for its profound weight-loss benefits (often required for kidney transplant waitlisting) and secondary renoprotective effects.

---

## Mounjaro, Zepbound (tirzepatide)

### Mechanism of Action
Mounjaro (tirzepatide) and Zepbound (tirzepatide) are single-molecule dual agonists of both the GIP and GLP-1 receptors. 
*   **GLP-1 agonism:** Delays gastric emptying, increases glucose-dependent insulin secretion, suppresses inappropriate glucagon secretion, and increases satiety.
*   **GIP agonism:** Synergistically enhances insulin secretion, improves insulin sensitivity in adipose tissue, and further reduces appetite. The dual action results in significantly greater HbA1c reduction and weight loss compared to selective GLP-1 receptor agonists.

### Indications in Nephrology
**FDA-Approved Indications:**
*   **Mounjaro (tirzepatide):** Adjunct to diet and exercise to improve glycemic control in adults with Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (T2DM).
*   **Zepbound (tirzepatide):** Chronic weight management in adults with a BMI $\ge$ 30 kg/m², or BMI $\ge$ 27 kg/m² with at least one weight-related comorbidity (e.g., hypertension, dyslipidemia, T2DM, obstructive sleep apnea).

**Off-Label / Nephrology-Specific Uses:**
*   Weight reduction to achieve BMI targets for kidney transplant waitlisting.
*   Proteinuria reduction in obese patients with CKD and metabolic syndrome (secondary to weight loss and glycemic control).

**Key Supporting Clinical Trials:**
*   **SURPASS-4 (2021):** Compared Mounjaro (tirzepatide) to insulin glargine in T2DM patients with high CV risk (many with CKD). *Outcomes:* Tirzepatide slowed the rate of eGFR decline and significantly reduced UACR compared to glargine. A post-hoc analysis showed a **41% relative risk reduction** in the composite kidney outcome (eGFR decline $\ge$ 40%, ESRD, or renal death).
*   **SURMOUNT-1 (2022):** Evaluated Zepbound (tirzepatide) in adults with obesity (without diabetes). *Outcomes:* Achieved up to **20.9% body weight reduction** at the 15 mg dose over 72 weeks.

### Dosing
**Standard Dosing & Titration Schedule:**
Both Mounjaro (tirzepatide) and Zepbound (tirzepatide) follow the exact same titration schedule. Administered via once-weekly subcutaneous (SQ) injection.
*   **Initiation:** 2.5 mg SQ once weekly for 4 weeks. *(Note: 2.5 mg is for treatment initiation and is not intended for long-term glycemic control).*
*   **Titration:** Increase by 2.5 mg increments every 4 weeks as tolerated.
*   **Maintenance Doses:** 5 mg, 10 mg, or 15 mg SQ once weekly.
*   **Maximum Dose:** 15 mg SQ once weekly.

**CKD Dose Adjustments by eGFR Range:**
*Tirzepatide is not renally cleared; however, GI side effects can lead to volume depletion and AKI.*
*   **eGFR $\ge$ 60 mL/min/1.73m²:** Standard dosing. No adjustment.
*   **eGFR 30-59 mL/min/1.73m²:** Standard dosing. No adjustment. Monitor closely during titration.
*   **eGFR 15-29 mL/min/1.73m²:** Standard dosing. No adjustment. High risk for AKI if nausea/vomiting occurs.
*   **eGFR < 15 mL/min/1.73m² / Dialysis:** Standard dosing. No adjustment. Use with extreme caution due to risk of uremic GI symptom exacerbation and volume shifts.

**Hepatic Adjustments:**
*   No dosage adjustment is recommended for patients with hepatic impairment.

### Monitoring
**Baseline Labs:**
*   HbA1c, BMP (SCr, eGFR, K+), LFTs, Fasting Lipid Panel.
*   Urine Albumin-to-Creatinine Ratio (UACR).

**Follow-up Lab Schedule:**
*   **BMP/eGFR:** Check 2-4 weeks after initiation and after each dose escalation in patients with eGFR < 60 or those on concurrent diuretics/RAAS inhibitors (to rule out volume-depletion AKI).
*   **HbA1c:** Every 3 months until stable, then every 6 months. Target HbA1c < 7.0% (per ADA 2024 guidelines, individualize based on hypoglycemia risk).
*   **UACR:** Annually (or every 3-6 months if actively managing macroalbuminuria). Target UACR < 30 mg/g.

**Therapeutic Drug Monitoring:**
*   Not applicable for tirzepatide.

### Adverse Effects & Safety
**Common Side Effects (>5%):**
*   Nausea (12-18%), diarrhea (12-17%), decreased appetite (5-11%), vomiting (5-9%), constipation (6-7%), dyspepsia.
*   *Note: GI side effects are dose-dependent and most prominent during the dose-escalation phase.*

**Serious/Rare Side Effects:**
*   Acute pancreatitis.
*   Acute gallbladder disease (cholelithiasis, cholecystitis).
*   Severe gastrointestinal disease (e.g., gastroparesis, ileus).
*   Hypersensitivity reactions (anaphylaxis, angioedema).

**Black Box Warnings:**
*   **Risk of Thyroid C-Cell Tumors:** Tirzepatide causes dose-dependent and treatment-duration-dependent thyroid C-cell tumors in rats. It is unknown whether it causes medullary thyroid carcinoma (MTC) in humans.

**Contraindications:**
*   **Absolute:** Personal or family history of Medullary Thyroid Carcinoma (MTC) or Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN 2). Known hypersensitivity to tirzepatide.
*   **Relative:** History of pancreatitis, severe gastroparesis.

**CKD-Specific Safety Concerns:**
*   **Acute Kidney Injury (AKI):** Post-marketing reports of AKI and worsening of chronic renal failure, sometimes requiring hemodialysis, have occurred. This is almost exclusively driven by **volume depletion** secondary to severe nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. 
*   **Sick Day Rules:** Instruct CKD patients to hold the medication and contact the clinic if they experience severe vomiting/diarrhea to prevent AKI.

### Drug Interactions
*   **Oral Medications (Delayed Gastric Emptying):** Tirzepatide delays gastric emptying, which can impact the absorption of concomitantly administered oral medications. 
    *   *Nephrology Pearl:* **Calcineurin Inhibitors (Tacrolimus, Cyclosporine).** Delayed absorption can lead to erratic trough levels. **[TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED: Increased frequency of CNI trough monitoring is required when initiating or titrating tirzepatide in transplant recipients.]**
*   **Insulin and Insulin Secretagogues (Sulfonylureas):** Increased risk of hypoglycemia. When initiating Mounjaro (tirzepatide), consider reducing the dose of sulfonylureas by 50% or reducing basal insulin by 10-20%.
*   **Diuretics / RAAS Inhibitors:** Concurrent use increases the risk of AKI if the patient becomes volume depleted from GI side effects.
*   **CYP450:** No clinically significant CYP450 interactions.

### Special Populations
*   **Pregnancy:** Weight loss offers no benefit to a pregnant patient and may cause fetal harm. Discontinue Mounjaro/Zepbound (tirzepatide) at least **2 months prior** to a planned pregnancy. If a patient becomes pregnant, discontinue immediately.
*   **Elderly in CKD:** No specific dose adjustment based on age, but older adults with CKD are highly susceptible to volume depletion and AKI. Titrate cautiously.
*   **Transplant Considerations:** Highly effective for post-transplant weight gain or pre-transplant weight loss. However, strict monitoring of immunosuppressant levels is mandatory due to altered gastric motility.

### Patient Counseling Points
*   **Administration:** Inject subcutaneously once weekly, on the same day each week, at any time of day, with or without meals. Rotate injection sites (abdomen, thigh, back of upper arm).
*   **GI Mitigation:** To reduce nausea, eat smaller meals, stop eating when full, avoid high-fat or spicy foods, and stay well-hydrated.
*   **Hydration & AKI Warning:** "Because this medication can cause nausea or diarrhea, you must drink plenty of water. If you cannot keep fluids down for more than 24 hours, call our office immediately, as dehydration can severely damage your kidneys."
*   **Storage:** Store pens in the refrigerator (36°F to 46°F). They can be kept at room temperature (up to 86°F) for up to 21 days. Do not freeze.

### Prior Authorization & Coding
**Common ICD-10 Codes:**
*   *For Mounjaro:* E11.9 (T2DM without complications), E11.22 (T2DM with diabetic chronic kidney disease), E11.29 (T2DM with other diabetic kidney complication).
*   *For Zepbound:* E66.01 (Morbid (severe) obesity due to excess calories), E66.9 (Obesity, unspecified).

**Tips for Prior Authorization (PA) Success:**
*   **Mounjaro:** Most payers require a documented diagnosis of T2DM. Off-label use for weight loss without T2DM will almost universally be denied. Step therapy (requiring trial of metformin) is common.
*   **Zepbound:** Requires the patient's plan to have a specific "weight loss carve-out" benefit. Document baseline BMI, weight, and participation in a comprehensive lifestyle modification program. **[TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED: Utilize standard TKE PA templates for Zepbound documenting transplant waitlist BMI requirements if applicable.]**

## Class Comparison & Clinical Decision Making

While tirzepatide is currently the only GIP/GLP-1 receptor agonist, it is clinically evaluated alongside the selective GLP-1 receptor agonists (e.g., semaglutide, dulaglutide).

### Clinical Decision Tree: Start, Escalate, Switch, Stop

*   **WHEN TO START:**
    *   Patient with T2DM and CKD needing significant A1c reduction and/or weight loss (Mounjaro).
    *   Patient with CKD and obesity (BMI > 30) needing weight loss for kidney transplant waitlisting (Zepbound).
*   **WHEN TO ESCALATE:**
    *   Escalate dose every 4 weeks *only if* the patient is tolerating the current dose without significant nausea/vomiting, and target A1c (<7.0%) or weight loss goals have not been met.
    *   *Do not escalate* if the patient is experiencing moderate-to-severe GI symptoms; maintain current dose until symptoms resolve.
*   **WHEN TO SWITCH:**
    *   **From GLP-1 RA to Tirzepatide:** Consider switching if a patient on maximum tolerated dose of a GLP-1 RA (e.g., Ozempic, Trulicity) has not achieved A1c or weight loss targets. 
    *   *How to switch:* Discontinue the GLP-1 RA. Start tirzepatide at the standard initiation dose of 2.5 mg weekly. Do *not* convert to high-dose tirzepatide immediately, as this will cause severe GI distress.
    *   **From Tirzepatide to GLP-1 RA:** Consider switching to Ozempic (semaglutide) if the patient requires primary CKD progression protection (based on the FLOW trial data), as tirzepatide does not yet have a primary FDA indication for CKD.
*   **WHEN TO STOP:**
    *   Recurrent AKI secondary to volume depletion.
    *   Development of acute pancreatitis or severe gallbladder disease.
    *   Pregnancy (stop 2 months prior to conception).
    *   Inadequate weight loss (<5% body weight) after 3-6 months on maximum tolerated dose of Zepbound (re-evaluate therapy).
