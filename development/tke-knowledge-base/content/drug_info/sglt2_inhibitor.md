## Farxiga (dapagliflozin)

### Mechanism of Action
Farxiga (dapagliflozin) is a highly selective, reversible inhibitor of sodium-glucose cotransporter 2 (SGLT2), located in the S1 segment of the proximal convoluted tubule. By inhibiting SGLT2, it prevents the reabsorption of filtered glucose and sodium. 
In nephrology, the primary benefit is **tubuloglomerular feedback (TGF)**. Increased sodium delivery to the macula densa causes afferent arteriolar vasoconstriction, which reduces intraglomerular hypertension and hyperfiltration. Secondary benefits include reduced proximal tubule workload/hypoxia, decreased sympathetic nervous system tone, and reduction in systemic blood pressure and uric acid.

### Indications in Nephrology
*   **FDA-Approved Indications:**
    *   **CKD:** To reduce the risk of sustained eGFR decline, ESKD, cardiovascular death, and hospitalization for heart failure in adults with CKD at risk of progression.
    *   **Heart Failure:** To reduce the risk of cardiovascular death and hospitalization for heart failure in adults with heart failure (HFrEF and HFpEF).
    *   **Type 2 Diabetes (T2DM):** Glycemic control.
*   **Off-Label Uses:** Proteinuria reduction in IgA Nephropathy and FSGS (supported by DAPA-CKD sub-analyses).
*   **Key Clinical Trials:**
    *   **DAPA-CKD (2020):** Enrolled 4,304 patients (with and without T2DM) with eGFR 25-75 mL/min/1.73m² and UACR 200-5000 mg/g. Farxiga (dapagliflozin) reduced the primary composite outcome (≥50% eGFR decline, ESKD, or renal/CV death) by 39% (HR 0.61; NNT = 19).
    *   **DAPA-HF (2019) & DELIVER (2022):** Demonstrated significant reductions in CV death and HF hospitalizations across the spectrum of ejection fractions.

### Dosing
*   **Standard Dosing:** 10 mg orally once daily in the morning, with or without food. No titration is required for CKD or HF indications.
*   **CKD Dose Adjustments by eGFR Range:**
    *   **eGFR ≥60 mL/min/1.73m²:** 10 mg once daily.
    *   **eGFR 30-59 mL/min/1.73m²:** 10 mg once daily.
    *   **eGFR 15-29 mL/min/1.73m²:** 10 mg once daily. *Note: FDA label and KDIGO 2024 support initiation if eGFR ≥25. If eGFR falls below 25 during therapy, continue 10 mg daily.*
    *   **eGFR <15 / Dialysis:** Do not initiate. If already on therapy, continue until the initiation of dialysis or kidney transplantation. Contraindicated in patients on dialysis.
*   **Hepatic Adjustments:** Severe hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh C): Starting dose 5 mg daily; if well tolerated, may increase to 10 mg.

### Monitoring
*   **Baseline Labs:** BMP (specifically eGFR and Potassium), HbA1c, Lipid panel, UACR. Assess volume status and blood pressure.
*   **Follow-up Schedule:**
    *   **2-4 weeks post-initiation:** BMP to assess eGFR and potassium. *Clinical Pearl: Expect a benign, reversible "dip" in eGFR of 10-30% due to hemodynamic changes. Do NOT stop the drug unless the dip is >30% or accompanied by acute volume depletion.*
    *   **Every 3-6 months:** BMP, HbA1c, UACR (Target UACR <30 mg/g or >30% reduction from baseline).
*   **Therapeutic Drug Monitoring:** None required.

### Adverse Effects & Safety
*   **Common (>5%):** Mycotic genital infections (females > males), urinary tract infections, increased urination/polyuria, dyslipidemia.
*   **Serious/Rare:** Euglycemic Diabetic Ketoacidosis (euDKA), Fournier's gangrene (necrotizing fasciitis of the perineum), acute kidney injury (usually secondary to volume depletion), urosepsis.
*   **Black Box Warnings:** None.
*   **Contraindications:** Hypersensitivity to Farxiga (dapagliflozin), patients on dialysis.
*   **CKD-Specific Safety Concerns:** Patients with CKD are at higher risk for volume depletion. Concurrent use of loop diuretics may require a 25-50% dose reduction of the diuretic upon SGLT2i initiation to prevent AKI.

### Drug Interactions
*   **Diuretics (Loop and Thiazide):** Synergistic volume depletion and hypotension.
*   **Insulin and Sulfonylureas:** Increased risk of hypoglycemia. *Decision Tree: If HbA1c <7.5% at initiation, consider reducing insulin dose by 10-20% or stopping sulfonylurea.*
*   **Lithium:** SGLT2 inhibitors can increase renal lithium clearance, decreasing serum lithium levels. Monitor lithium levels closely.

### Special Populations
*   **Pregnancy:** Avoid in the 2nd and 3rd trimesters (animal data shows adverse effects on renal development). Discontinue if pregnancy is detected.
*   **Elderly:** Higher risk of volume depletion and orthostatic hypotension. Assess fall risk.
*   **Transplant:** Not FDA approved for kidney transplant recipients. Use is generally avoided due to increased risk of severe UTIs and immunosuppression, though emerging data suggests safety in highly selected stable patients [TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED for transplant use].

### Patient Counseling Points
*   **Expectations:** "This medication protects your kidneys and heart. You may see a slight bump in your kidney numbers on the first lab draw—this is normal and shows the drug is working to lower pressure in the kidney."
*   **Hygiene:** Maintain excellent perineal hygiene to prevent yeast infections. Drink water when thirsty.
*   **Sick Day Rules:** **STOP** this medication 3 days before any scheduled surgery, or if you are sick with vomiting, diarrhea, or unable to eat/drink. This prevents a rare but serious condition called diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA).
*   **Warning Signs:** Report severe pelvic/groin pain, foul-smelling urine, fever, or rapid breathing immediately.

### Prior Authorization & Coding
*   **ICD-10 Codes:** E11.22 (Type 2 DM with diabetic CKD), N18.30-N18.4 (CKD stage 3-4), I50.9 (Heart failure, unspecified).
*   **CPT Codes:** 99213, 99214, 99215 (E&M codes based on complexity of CKD/DM management).
*   **PA Tips:** Document eGFR, UACR, and failure/intolerance of ACEi/ARB if applicable. Emphasize "Guideline Directed Medical Therapy (GDMT) for CKD progression."

## Jardiance (empagliflozin)

### Mechanism of Action
Jardiance (empagliflozin) is a selective SGLT2 inhibitor. Like Farxiga, it blocks glucose and sodium reabsorption in the proximal tubule, restoring tubuloglomerular feedback, reducing intraglomerular pressure, and decreasing proteinuria and CKD progression.

### Indications in Nephrology
*   **FDA-Approved Indications:**
    *   **CKD:** To reduce the risk of sustained decline in eGFR, ESKD, cardiovascular death, and hospitalization in adults with CKD at risk of progression.
    *   **Heart Failure:** To reduce the risk of CV death and HF hospitalization (HFrEF and HFpEF).
    *   **Type 2 Diabetes (T2DM):** Glycemic control and reduction of CV death.
*   **Off-Label Uses:** Non-diabetic proteinuric kidney diseases (IgAN, FSGS).
*   **Key Clinical Trials:**
    *   **EMPA-KIDNEY (2022):** Enrolled 6,609 patients (eGFR 20-45 regardless of UACR, or eGFR 45-90 with UACR ≥200). Jardiance (empagliflozin) reduced the primary composite outcome (kidney disease progression or CV death) by 28% (HR 0.72).
    *   **EMPA-REG OUTCOME (2015):** First trial to show CV mortality benefit in T2DM.
    *   **EMPEROR-Reduced / EMPEROR-Preserved:** Demonstrated HF benefits.

### Dosing
*   **Standard Dosing:** 10 mg orally once daily in the morning. (Can be titrated to 25 mg daily for additional glycemic control in T2DM, but 10 mg is the standard GDMT dose for CKD/HF).
*   **CKD Dose Adjustments by eGFR Range:**
    *   **eGFR ≥60 mL/min/1.73m²:** 10 mg once daily (may increase to 25 mg for T2DM).
    *   **eGFR 30-59 mL/min/1.73m²:** 10 mg once daily.
    *   **eGFR 15-29 mL/min/1.73m²:** 10 mg once daily. *Note: FDA label and KDIGO 2024 support initiation if eGFR ≥20. If eGFR falls below 20, continue 10 mg daily.*
    *   **eGFR <15 / Dialysis:** Do not initiate. If already on therapy, continue until dialysis initiation. Contraindicated in patients on dialysis.
*   **Hepatic Adjustments:** No dosage adjustment required.

### Monitoring
*   **Baseline Labs:** BMP (eGFR, K), HbA1c, Lipid panel, UACR.
*   **Follow-up Schedule:** BMP at 2-4 weeks to check for the expected hemodynamic eGFR dip (10-30%). Routine BMP, HbA1c, and UACR every 3-6 months. Target UACR <30 mg/g.
*   **Therapeutic Drug Monitoring:** None required.

### Adverse Effects & Safety
*   **Common (>5%):** Genital mycotic infections, UTIs, increased urination.
*   **Serious/Rare:** euDKA, Fournier's gangrene, volume depletion/hypotension.
*   **Black Box Warnings:** None.
*   **Contraindications:** Hypersensitivity, patients on dialysis.
*   **CKD-Specific Safety Concerns:** Similar to Farxiga. High risk of hypovolemia if combined with aggressive loop diuretic therapy in advanced CKD.

### Drug Interactions
*   **Diuretics:** Increased risk of hypotension and AKI.
*   **Insulin/Secretagogues:** Hypoglycemia risk.
*   **Lithium:** Decreased serum lithium levels.

### Special Populations
*   **Pregnancy:** Avoid in 2nd/3rd trimesters.
*   **Elderly:** Monitor for orthostasis.
*   **Transplant:** Avoid routine use outside of specific [TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED] guidelines due to UTI risk.

### Patient Counseling Points
*   **Expectations:** "This pill protects your kidneys from failing and your heart from weakening."
*   **Hygiene & Hydration:** Wash the groin area daily with mild soap and water. Drink fluids to avoid dehydration, especially in hot weather.
*   **Sick Day Rules:** **STOP** Jardiance (empagliflozin) 3 days prior to surgery or during acute illness (nausea, vomiting, inability to eat).
*   **Warning Signs:** Seek ER care for severe abdominal pain, nausea/vomiting, or rapid breathing (signs of DKA).

### Prior Authorization & Coding
*   **ICD-10 Codes:** E11.22, N18.30-N18.4, I50.9.
*   **PA Tips:** EMPA-KIDNEY data allows for PA approval even in patients with lower levels of proteinuria (UACR <200) if eGFR is 20-45. Highlight this in appeals.

## Invokana (canagliflozin)

### Mechanism of Action
Invokana (canagliflozin) inhibits SGLT2 in the proximal tubule. At higher doses (300 mg), it also provides mild, transient inhibition of SGLT1 in the intestinal tract, which may delay glucose absorption. Its primary renal benefit remains the restoration of tubuloglomerular feedback.

### Indications in Nephrology
*   **FDA-Approved Indications:**
    *   **Diabetic Nephropathy:** To reduce the risk of ESKD, doubling of serum creatinine, CV death, and HF hospitalization in adults with T2DM and diabetic nephropathy with albuminuria (>300 mg/day).
    *   **Type 2 Diabetes (T2DM):** Glycemic control and CV risk reduction.
*   **Off-Label Uses:** Non-diabetic CKD (KDIGO guidelines consider SGLT2i benefits a class effect, though Invokana lacks a dedicated non-diabetic CKD trial like DAPA-CKD or EMPA-KIDNEY).
*   **Key Clinical Trials:**
    *   **CREDENCE (2019):** Enrolled 4,401 patients with T2DM, eGFR 30-90, and UACR 300-5000. Invokana (canagliflozin) reduced the primary composite outcome (ESKD, doubling of SCr, or renal/CV death) by 30% (HR 0.70).
    *   **CANVAS (2017):** Demonstrated CV safety and benefit in T2DM.

### Dosing
*   **Standard Dosing:** 100 mg orally once daily, taken before the first meal of the day.
*   **CKD Dose Adjustments by eGFR Range:**
    *   **eGFR ≥60 mL/min/1.73m²:** 100 mg once daily. May increase to 300 mg daily for additional glycemic control if 100 mg is tolerated.
    *   **eGFR 30-59 mL/min/1.73m²:** 100 mg once daily. (Do not titrate to 300 mg).
    *   **eGFR 15-29 mL/min/1.73m²:** 100 mg once daily. *Note: FDA label supports initiation if eGFR ≥30 and UACR >300. However, KDIGO 2024 supports class-effect initiation down to eGFR ≥20. If eGFR falls below 30, continue 100 mg daily.*
    *   **eGFR <15 / Dialysis:** Do not initiate. Continue 100 mg daily until dialysis initiation. Contraindicated on dialysis.
*   **Hepatic Adjustments:** Severe hepatic impairment: Not recommended.

### Monitoring
*   **Baseline Labs:** BMP, HbA1c, Lipid panel, UACR. Baseline foot exam (due to historical amputation risk).
*   **Follow-up Schedule:** BMP at 2-4 weeks for eGFR dip. Routine labs every 3-6 months.
*   **Therapeutic Drug Monitoring:** None required.

### Adverse Effects & Safety
*   **Common (>5%):** Genital mycotic infections, UTIs, polyuria.
*   **Serious/Rare:** Lower limb amputation, bone fractures, euDKA, Fournier's gangrene, hyperkalemia (more frequently noted with canagliflozin than others).
*   **Black Box Warnings:** *Historical:* A BBW for lower limb amputation was added in 2017 based on CANVAS data, but **removed by the FDA in 2020**. It remains a prominent warning in the prescribing information.
*   **Contraindications:** Hypersensitivity, dialysis.
*   **CKD-Specific Safety Concerns:** Perform routine foot exams. Discontinue if a patient develops a lower extremity skin ulcer, infection, or osteomyelitis.

### Drug Interactions
*   **UGT Enzyme Inducers:** Rifampin, phenytoin, phenobarbital, ritonavir can decrease Invokana (canagliflozin) exposure. Consider increasing dose to 300 mg if eGFR ≥60, or choose an alternative agent.
*   **Digoxin:** Canagliflozin may increase digoxin AUC. Monitor digoxin levels.
*   **Diuretics & Insulin:** Same as class (volume depletion, hypoglycemia).

### Special Populations
*   **Pregnancy:** Avoid in 2nd/3rd trimesters.
*   **Elderly:** Higher risk of volume depletion.
*   **Transplant:** Avoid routine use.

### Patient Counseling Points
*   **Expectations:** "Take this medication before your first meal of the day. It protects your kidneys from diabetes."
*   **Foot Care:** "Inspect your feet daily. If you notice any new sores, ulcers, or infections on your feet or legs, call the clinic immediately."
*   **Sick Day Rules:** **STOP** Invokana (canagliflozin) 3 days before surgery or during severe illness/dehydration.

### Prior Authorization & Coding
*   **ICD-10 Codes:** E11.22 (essential for Invokana PA, as it is strictly approved for diabetic nephropathy).
*   **PA Tips:** Must document UACR >300 mg/g and presence of T2DM for highest likelihood of approval.

## Class Comparison & Clinical Decision Making

### SGLT2 Inhibitor Class Comparison Table

| Feature | Farxiga (dapagliflozin) | Jardiance (empagliflozin) | Invokana (canagliflozin) |

| **Unique Warnings** | None | None | Amputation risk, Bone fractures |
| **Cost / PA Burden** | High / Moderate | High / Moderate | High / High (Requires DM + Proteinuria) |

### Decision Tree: When to Choose Which Agent
1.  **Non-Diabetic CKD (e.g., IgAN, FSGS, Hypertensive Nephrosclerosis):**
    *   **Choose:** Farxiga (dapagliflozin) or Jardiance (empagliflozin).
    *   *Rationale:* Both have FDA approval and dedicated trial data (DAPA-CKD, EMPA-KIDNEY) for non-diabetic CKD. Invokana lacks this specific data.
2.  **Diabetic Kidney Disease (T2DM + CKD):**
    *   **Choose:** Any of the three.
    *   *Rationale:* KDIGO 2024 considers renal protection in T2DM a class effect. Choice is often dictated by patient insurance formulary.
3.  **Low eGFR (20-25 mL/min/1.73m²):**
    *   **Choose:** Jardiance (empagliflozin).
    *   *Rationale:* EMPA-KIDNEY trial and FDA label explicitly support initiation down to eGFR 20. (KDIGO 2024 supports class initiation down to 20, but Jardiance has the strictest on-label alignment).
4.  **History of Peripheral Arterial Disease, Foot Ulcers, or Amputation:**
    *   **Choose:** Farxiga (dapagliflozin) or Jardiance (empagliflozin).
    *   *Rationale:* Avoid Invokana (canagliflozin) due to the warning regarding lower limb amputations.

### Switching Between Agents
*   **Washout Period:** No washout period is required when switching between SGLT2 inhibitors.
*   **Method:** Stop Drug A on day 1; start Drug B on day 2.
*   **Monitoring Post-Switch:** Routine monitoring. A repeat 2-week BMP is generally *not* required if the patient was already hemodynamically stable on the previous SGLT2i, as the tubuloglomerular feedback effect is already established.
