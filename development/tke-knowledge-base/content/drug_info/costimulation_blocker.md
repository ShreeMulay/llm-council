# Costimulation Blockers Clinical Monograph

This clinical reference guide covers the costimulation blocker class of immunosuppressive medications, currently represented by a single FDA-approved agent in nephrology: **Nulojix (belatacept)**. 

*Guidelines referenced: KDIGO Clinical Practice Guideline for the Care of Kidney Transplant Recipients (2009, and recent AST/ASTS consensus updates).*

---

## Nulojix (belatacept)

**Aliases:** CTLA4-Ig

### Mechanism of Action
**Nulojix (belatacept)** is a selective T-cell costimulation blocker. It is a soluble fusion protein consisting of the extracellular domain of human CTLA-4 linked to a modified Fc portion of human IgG1. 
*   **Target:** Binds to CD80 and CD86 receptors on antigen-presenting cells (APCs).
*   **Action:** Blocks the CD28-mediated "Signal 2" costimulation required for full T-cell activation. Without Signal 2, T-cells recognizing an antigen (Signal 1) undergo anergy or apoptosis rather than proliferation, thereby preventing alloimmune responses and organ rejection.

### Indications in Nephrology
**FDA-Approved Indications:**
*   Prophylaxis of organ rejection in adult patients receiving a kidney transplant.
*   *Note:* Must be used in combination with basiliximab induction, mycophenolate mofetil (MMF), and corticosteroids.

**Common Off-Label Uses in Nephrology:**
*   **CNI-Sparing/Conversion Protocol:** Late conversion from a calcineurin inhibitor (CNI) to **Nulojix (belatacept)** in kidney transplant recipients experiencing severe CNI nephrotoxicity, thrombotic microangiopathy (TMA), or intolerable CNI side effects (e.g., severe neurotoxicity). *[TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED: Standardized CNI-to-belatacept conversion schedule]*

**Key Supporting Clinical Trials:**
*   **BENEFIT Trial (Standard Criteria Donors):** Compared **Nulojix (belatacept)** to cyclosporine. At 7 years, the **Nulojix (belatacept)** group showed a **43% reduction** in the risk of death or graft loss. Mean eGFR was significantly higher in the belatacept group (~25 mL/min/1.73m² higher than cyclosporine).
*   **BENEFIT-EXT Trial (Extended Criteria Donors):** Similar design but in marginal kidneys. Showed comparable patient/graft survival to cyclosporine but with significantly better renal function (mean eGFR ~11 mL/min/1.73m² higher at 7 years).
*   *Caveat from trials:* Higher incidence of acute cellular rejection (ACR) in the first 6 months and higher risk of Post-Transplant Lymphoproliferative Disorder (PTLD), particularly in the central nervous system.

### Dosing
**Nulojix (belatacept)** is administered via intravenous (IV) infusion over 30 minutes. Dosing is based on actual body weight at the time of the dose. Do not modify the dose during the course of therapy unless there is a change in body weight of >10%.

**Standard Dosing Schedule (De Novo Kidney Transplant):**
*   **Initial Phase (10 mg/kg IV):**
    *   Day 1 (prior to implantation)
    *   Day 5
    *   End of Week 2
    *   End of Week 4
    *   End of Week 8
    *   End of Week 12
*   **Maintenance Phase (5 mg/kg IV):**
    *   End of Week 16
    *   Every 4 weeks (± 3 days) thereafter

**CKD Dose Adjustments by eGFR Range:**
Because **Nulojix (belatacept)** is a large protein cleared via the reticuloendothelial system and not by renal excretion, no dose adjustments are required for renal impairment.
*   **eGFR ≥60:** No adjustment required
*   **eGFR 30-59:** No adjustment required
*   **eGFR 15-29:** No adjustment required
*   **eGFR <15 / Dialysis:** No adjustment required

**Hepatic Adjustments:**
*   No dose adjustment required.

### Monitoring
**Baseline Labs Required Before Starting:**
*   **Epstein-Barr Virus (EBV) Serology:** MUST confirm EBV IgG positive status. (IgM or PCR alone is insufficient).
*   **Infectious Disease Panel:** Cytomegalovirus (CMV) IgG, Hepatitis B surface antigen/core antibody, Hepatitis C antibody, HIV.
*   **Tuberculosis Screening:** QuantiFERON-TB Gold or T-SPOT.
*   **Baseline Neurological Exam:** To establish a baseline for future Progressive Multifocal Leukoencephalopathy (PML) monitoring.

**Follow-up Lab Schedule:**
*   **Every Infusion Visit (Monthly):** Comprehensive Metabolic Panel (CMP) to monitor eGFR and electrolytes; Complete Blood Count (CBC) to monitor for cytopenias.
*   **Quarterly/Annually:** Lipid panel, HbA1c, routine BK virus and CMV PCR monitoring per standard transplant protocols.
*   **Neurological Checks:** Brief clinical assessment for cognitive, motor, or visual changes at each infusion visit.

**Therapeutic Drug Monitoring (TDM):**
*   Routine therapeutic drug monitoring (trough levels) is **not** clinically indicated or commercially available for **Nulojix (belatacept)**.

### Adverse Effects & Safety
**Common Side Effects (>5%):**
*   Anemia, leukopenia
*   Diarrhea, constipation, nausea
*   Urinary tract infections (UTIs), upper respiratory infections (URIs)
*   Peripheral edema
*   Hypertension
*   Hypokalemia, hypophosphatemia

**Serious/Rare Side Effects:**
*   Acute cellular rejection (highest risk in the first 3-6 months post-transplant)
*   Progressive Multifocal Leukoencephalopathy (PML)
*   Severe opportunistic infections (CMV, BK nephropathy)

**Black Box Warnings:**
1.  **Post-Transplant Lymphoproliferative Disorder (PTLD):** Increased risk of PTLD, predominantly involving the central nervous system (CNS). **Contraindicated in patients who are EBV seronegative or of unknown EBV serostatus.**
2.  **Increased Risk of Infection and Malignancy:** Increased susceptibility to infection and the possible development of malignancies (especially skin cancers) due to immunosuppression.
3.  **Liver Transplant:** Not recommended for use in liver transplant patients due to an increased risk of graft loss and death.

**Contraindications:**
*   **Absolute:** EBV seronegative status or unknown EBV status.
*   **Absolute:** Known hypersensitivity to belatacept.

**CKD-Specific Safety Concerns:**
*   While it preserves eGFR better than CNIs, patients with advanced CKD (eGFR <30) are at higher baseline risk for severe infections; monitor CBC and infectious signs meticulously.

### Drug Interactions
*   **Live Vaccines:** Avoid concurrent use. Live vaccines should be administered prior to transplantation or avoided entirely while on **Nulojix (belatacept)**.
*   **Other Immunosuppressants:** Additive risk of over-immunosuppression, infection, and malignancy. (Note: It is standard to use with MMF and steroids, but avoid overlapping with CNIs long-term unless executing a specific, short-term conversion protocol).
*   **CYP450 Interactions:** None. As a biologic, it does not inhibit or induce CYP450 enzymes.
*   **Food Interactions:** None.

### Special Populations
*   **Pregnancy:** Category C (historical). Use only if the potential benefit justifies the potential risk to the fetus. National Transplant Pregnancy Registry (NTPR) monitoring is required.
*   **Lactation:** It is unknown if belatacept is excreted in human milk. Discontinue nursing or discontinue the drug, taking into account the importance of the drug to the mother.
*   **Elderly Considerations in CKD:** No overall differences in safety or efficacy were observed between patients ≥65 years and younger patients, but older patients generally have a higher baseline risk of infections and malignancies.
*   **Transplant Considerations:** This drug is exclusively used in the transplant population.

### Patient Counseling Points
*   **EBV Status:** Explain that this medication is only safe because their blood tests show they have had a past EBV infection, which protects them from a specific type of lymphoma (PTLD).
*   **Warning Signs to Report Immediately:** 
    *   *PML/CNS PTLD signs:* New-onset memory loss, confusion, changes in vision, difficulty walking, or weakness on one side of the body.
    *   *Infection signs:* Fever, chills, painful urination, or persistent cough.
*   **Infusion Adherence:** Emphasize that **Nulojix (belatacept)** must be given via IV infusion exactly every 4 weeks. Missing an infusion significantly increases the risk of losing the kidney transplant.
*   **Sun Protection:** Advise strict sun protection (SPF 30+, protective clothing) due to the increased risk of skin cancer.

### Prior Authorization & Coding
*   **Common ICD-10 Codes:**
    *   `Z94.0` (Kidney transplant status)
    *   `T86.10` (Unspecified complication of kidney transplant - used if converting due to CNI toxicity)
    *   `T86.12` (Kidney transplant failure/rejection)
*   **CPT Codes:**
    *   `96365` (Intravenous infusion, for therapy, prophylaxis, or diagnosis; initial, up to 1 hour)
*   **HCPCS J-Code:**
    *   `J0485` (Injection, belatacept, 1 mg)
*   **Tips for PA Success:** Always include documentation of positive EBV IgG serology. If converting from a CNI, clearly document the medical necessity (e.g., "Tacrolimus-induced TMA" or "Progressive CNI nephrotoxicity with declining eGFR").
*   **Patient Assistance:** Bristol-Myers Squibb offers the Nulojix Patient Assistance Program for eligible uninsured or underinsured patients.

## Class Comparison: Costimulation Blockers vs. Standard of Care (CNIs)

*Note: Because **Nulojix (belatacept)** is currently the only FDA-approved costimulation blocker for kidney transplantation, this table compares it to the standard-of-care Calcineurin Inhibitors (CNIs) to guide clinical decision-making.*

| Feature | Nulojix (belatacept) | Tacrolimus (Prograf, Envarsus XR, Astagraf XL) | Cyclosporine (Neoral, Gengraf) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Class** | Costimulation Blocker (IV) | Calcineurin Inhibitor (PO/IV) | Calcineurin Inhibitor (PO/IV) |
| **Renal Toxicity** | **None** (Preserves eGFR) | High (Afferent arteriole vasoconstriction, chronic fibrosis) | High |
| **Dosing Route/Freq** | IV Infusion every 4 weeks | PO once or twice daily | PO twice daily |
| **TDM Required?** | No | Yes (Trough levels 4-10 ng/mL) | Yes (Trough or C2 levels) |
| **Metabolic Profile** | Neutral (No new-onset diabetes, neutral BP/lipids) | High risk of NODAT, alopecia, tremors | High risk of HTN, hyperlipidemia, hirsutism, gingival hyperplasia |
| **Key Risks** | PTLD (CNS), PML, early ACR | Nephrotoxicity, Neurotoxicity, NODAT | Nephrotoxicity, Cardiovascular risk |
| **EBV Requirement** | **MUST be EBV IgG Positive** | Can be used in EBV negative | Can be used in EBV negative |

### Decision Tree: When to Choose Belatacept
1.  **De Novo Transplant:** Consider in EBV-positive patients where long-term renal function preservation is the highest priority, and the patient has reliable transportation to an infusion center every 4 weeks.
2.  **Conversion (Off-Label but Common):** 
    *   *Start Belatacept if:* Patient is EBV-positive AND experiencing progressive CNI nephrotoxicity, CNI-induced TMA, severe neurotoxicity (tremors/seizures), or severe non-adherence to daily oral medications (infusion guarantees 100% adherence for 4 weeks).
    *   *Do NOT switch if:* Patient is EBV-negative, has a history of severe acute rejection episodes, or has active severe infections.

### Switching Between Agents (CNI to Belatacept)
*[TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED: Confirm local TKE conversion protocol]*
A standard conversion protocol typically involves initiating **Nulojix (belatacept)** at the maintenance dose (5 mg/kg) while slowly tapering the CNI over 4 to 8 weeks to prevent breakthrough acute rejection during the transition. 
*   **Example Taper:** 
    *   Week 1: Give Belatacept 5 mg/kg. Reduce CNI dose by 25-50%.
    *   Week 3: Give Belatacept 5 mg/kg. Reduce CNI dose by another 25-50%.
    *   Week 5: Give Belatacept 5 mg/kg. Discontinue CNI.
    *   Week 9 and beyond: Belatacept 5 mg/kg every 4 weeks.
