## Sensipar (cinacalcet)

### Mechanism of Action
Sensipar (cinacalcet) is a calcimimetic agent that acts as an allosteric modulator of the calcium-sensing receptor (CaSR) located on the chief cells of the parathyroid gland. By increasing the sensitivity of the CaSR to extracellular calcium, Sensipar (cinacalcet) directly suppresses the secretion of parathyroid hormone (PTH). The reduction in PTH subsequently leads to decreased serum calcium and phosphorus levels.

### Indications in Nephrology
**FDA-Approved Indications:**
*   Secondary hyperparathyroidism (sHPT) in adult patients with chronic kidney disease (CKD) on dialysis.
*   Hypercalcemia in patients with parathyroid carcinoma.
*   Severe hypercalcemia in patients with primary HPT who are unable to undergo parathyroidectomy.

**Off-Label Uses:**
*   Post-renal transplant hyperparathyroidism with refractory hypercalcemia (used to avoid or delay parathyroidectomy).

**Key Supporting Clinical Trials:**
*   **EVOLVE Trial (2012):** Evaluated Sensipar (cinacalcet) vs. placebo in 3,883 hemodialysis patients with moderate-to-severe sHPT. 
    *   *Outcomes:* The unadjusted intention-to-treat analysis did not show a statistically significant reduction in the primary composite endpoint (all-cause mortality or major cardiovascular events). However, secondary analyses adjusting for baseline characteristics and non-adherence showed significant reductions in the need for surgical parathyroidectomy (HR 0.44) and severe unremitting HPT.

### Dosing
**Standard Dosing for sHPT (Dialysis Patients):**
*   **Starting Dose:** 30 mg PO once daily.
*   **Administration:** Must be taken with food or shortly after a meal (increases absorption and reduces GI upset). Tablets must be swallowed whole; do not crush or chew.
*   **Titration Schedule:** Increase dose sequentially every 2 to 4 weeks (30 mg → 60 mg → 90 mg → 120 mg → 180 mg daily) until target intact PTH (iPTH) is achieved. 
*   **Maximum Dose:** 180 mg PO once daily.

**CKD Dose Adjustments by eGFR Range:**
*Safety Warning: Sensipar (cinacalcet) is NOT indicated for sHPT in patients with CKD who are not on dialysis due to an increased risk of severe hypocalcemia.*
*   **eGFR ≥60 mL/min/1.73 m²:** Not indicated for sHPT.
*   **eGFR 30-59 mL/min/1.73 m²:** Not indicated for sHPT.
*   **eGFR 15-29 mL/min/1.73 m²:** Not indicated for sHPT.
*   **eGFR <15 mL/min/1.73 m² / Dialysis:** 30 mg PO once daily starting dose. Titrate per protocol.

**Hepatic Adjustments:**
*   Mild to moderate impairment: No initial dose adjustment required.
*   Moderate to severe hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh B and C): Half-life is prolonged by 33% to 84%. Monitor iPTH and serum calcium closely during initiation and titration.

### Monitoring
**Baseline Labs Required:**
*   Corrected serum calcium (Must be ≥8.4 mg/dL to initiate)
*   Intact PTH (iPTH)
*   Serum phosphorus

**Follow-up Lab Schedule [TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED]:**
*   **Serum Calcium:** Check within 1 week of initiation or any dose adjustment. Once on a maintenance dose, check monthly.
*   **iPTH:** Check 1 to 4 weeks after initiation or dose adjustment. Once on a maintenance dose, check every 1 to 3 months.
*   **Phosphorus:** Monitor monthly.

**Therapeutic Targets (per KDIGO 2017 CKD-MBD Guidelines):**
*   **iPTH:** Target 2 to 9 times the upper limit of normal for the assay (typically ~130 to 600 pg/mL for dialysis patients).
*   **Calcium:** Avoid hypercalcemia; maintain corrected calcium within the normal reference range. Avoid hypocalcemia (<8.4 mg/dL).

### Adverse Effects & Safety
**Common Side Effects (>5%):**
*   Nausea (29%) and Vomiting (25%) — *Most common reason for discontinuation.*
*   Diarrhea (21%)
*   Hypocalcemia (7%)

**Serious/Rare Side Effects:**
*   **Severe Hypocalcemia:** Can lead to paresthesias, myalgias, cramping, tetany, and seizures.
*   **QT Prolongation and Ventricular Arrhythmias:** Secondary to hypocalcemia.
*   **Adynamic Bone Disease:** Risk increases if iPTH is chronically suppressed below 100 pg/mL.
*   **Upper GI Bleeding:** Especially in patients with a history of ulcer disease.

**Contraindications:**
*   **Absolute:** Serum calcium less than the lower limit of normal (typically <8.4 mg/dL).
*   **Relative:** History of seizure disorder, concurrent use of medications known to prolong the QT interval.

**CKD-Specific Safety Concerns:**
*   Do not use in pre-dialysis CKD patients.
*   If iPTH falls below 150 pg/mL, reduce the dose or discontinue Sensipar (cinacalcet) and/or vitamin D analogs to prevent adynamic bone disease.

### Drug Interactions
*   **CYP3A4 Substrate:** Sensipar (cinacalcet) is metabolized by CYP3A4. Strong CYP3A4 inhibitors (e.g., ketoconazole, itraconazole, erythromycin) will increase Sensipar (cinacalcet) levels. Dose reduction may be required.
*   **Strong CYP2D6 Inhibitor:** Sensipar (cinacalcet) strongly inhibits CYP2D6. It can significantly increase the levels of medications metabolized by this pathway, including:
    *   Beta-blockers (e.g., metoprolol, carvedilol) — *Monitor for bradycardia/hypotension.*
    *   Antiarrhythmics (e.g., flecainide, propafenone).
    *   Antidepressants (e.g., amitriptyline, desipramine).
*   **Food Interactions:** High-fat meals increase absorption by up to 82%. Must be taken consistently with food.

### Special Populations
*   **Pregnancy:** No adequate data. Use only if the potential benefit justifies the potential risk to the fetus.
*   **Elderly:** No specific dose adjustments needed, but older adults may be more sensitive to hypocalcemia and GI side effects.
*   **Transplant:** Used off-label for post-transplant hyperparathyroidism. Monitor calcium closely. Note potential indirect interactions with calcineurin inhibitors (CNIs) if concurrent CYP3A4 modulators are used.

### Patient Counseling Points
*   **Administration:** "Take this medication every day with food or right after a meal. Swallow the pill whole; do not crush, chew, or break it."
*   **GI Upset:** "Nausea and vomiting are common when starting. Taking it with your largest meal can help. Let us know if it becomes intolerable."
*   **Warning Signs of Low Calcium:** "Contact the clinic immediately if you experience tingling or numbness around your mouth or in your fingers/toes, muscle cramping, or spasms."
*   **Lab Adherence:** "We will need to draw your blood frequently (especially calcium levels) when we start or change the dose of this medication."

### Prior Authorization & Coding
*   **Common ICD-10 Codes:** 
    *   N25.81 (Secondary hyperparathyroidism of renal origin)
    *   Z99.2 (Dependence on renal dialysis)
*   **CPT Codes for Monitoring:** 83970 (Intact PTH), 82310 (Calcium, total).
*   **PA Tips:** Most Medicare Part D and commercial plans require documented failure, intolerance, or contraindication to active vitamin D analogs (e.g., calcitriol, paricalcitol) AND a documented iPTH >300 pg/mL with a corrected calcium >8.4 mg/dL.
*   **Cost/Assistance:** Sensipar is available as generic cinacalcet, which has significantly reduced costs. Patient assistance programs are available through the manufacturer for uninsured/underinsured patients.

## Class Comparison: Calcimimetics in Nephrology

While Sensipar (cinacalcet) is the primary oral agent, Parsabiv (etelcalcetide) is the IV alternative commonly utilized in the in-center hemodialysis setting. 

| Feature | Sensipar (cinacalcet) | Parsabiv (etelcalcetide) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Route / Frequency** | Oral / Daily | Intravenous / 3x weekly at end of HD |
| **Starting Dose** | 30 mg PO daily | 5 mg IV 3x weekly |
| **Max Dose** | 180 mg PO daily | 15 mg IV 3x weekly |
| **eGFR Thresholds** | Dialysis ONLY (Contraindicated in pre-dialysis CKD) | Hemodialysis ONLY (Not studied in PD or pre-dialysis) |
| **Adherence** | Patient-dependent | 100% (Administered by HD staff) |
| **GI Side Effects** | High (Nausea 29%, Vomiting 25%) | Lower (Nausea 11%, Vomiting 9%) |
| **Hypocalcemia Risk** | Moderate (7%) | Higher (64% asymptomatic, 7% symptomatic) |
| **Drug Interactions** | Strong CYP2D6 inhibitor; CYP3A4 substrate | No CYP450 interactions (cleared by dialysis) |
| **Cost / Billing** | Pharmacy Benefit (Part D) | Medical Benefit (Part B / TDAPA bundle) |

### Decision Tree: Choosing and Switching Agents [TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED]

**When to choose Parsabiv (etelcalcetide) over Sensipar (cinacalcet):**
1.  **Non-adherence:** Patient is failing oral Sensipar (cinacalcet) therapy due to missed doses.
2.  **GI Intolerance:** Patient experiences severe, dose-limiting nausea/vomiting on oral Sensipar (cinacalcet).
3.  **Polypharmacy/Interactions:** Patient is on narrow-therapeutic-index CYP2D6 substrates (e.g., flecainide) where Sensipar (cinacalcet) poses a high interaction risk.

**Protocol for Switching from Sensipar (cinacalcet) to Parsabiv (etelcalcetide):**
1.  **STOP** Sensipar (cinacalcet).
2.  **WAIT** a minimum of 7 days (washout period).
3.  **CHECK** corrected serum calcium. Ensure it is ≥8.3 mg/dL.
4.  **START** Parsabiv (etelcalcetide) at 5 mg IV three times weekly at the end of hemodialysis.

**Protocol for Switching from Parsabiv (etelcalcetide) to Sensipar (cinacalcet):**
1.  **STOP** Parsabiv (etelcalcetide).
2.  **WAIT** a minimum of 4 weeks (due to the long half-life of etelcalcetide).
3.  **CHECK** corrected serum calcium. Ensure it is ≥8.4 mg/dL.
4.  **START** Sensipar (cinacalcet) at 30 mg PO daily.
