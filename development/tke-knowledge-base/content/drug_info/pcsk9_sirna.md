## Leqvio (inclisiran)

### Mechanism of Action
Leqvio (inclisiran) is a first-in-class, double-stranded small interfering RNA (siRNA) therapeutic. 
*   **Target Specificity:** It is conjugated to triantennary N-acetylgalactosamine (GalNAc), which binds specifically to the asialoglycoprotein receptors (ASGPR) expressed almost exclusively on hepatocytes.
*   **Action:** Once inside the hepatocyte, Leqvio (inclisiran) utilizes the RNA interference mechanism to direct catalytic breakdown of mRNA for proprotein convertase subtilisin/kexin type 9 (PCSK9). 
*   **Result:** This prevents the translation of PCSK9 protein, leading to increased recycling and expression of LDL receptors on the hepatocyte surface, thereby increasing LDL-C uptake and lowering circulating LDL-C levels by approximately 50%.

### Indications in Nephrology
**FDA-Approved Indications:**
*   Adjunct to diet and maximally tolerated statin therapy for the treatment of adults with primary hyperlipidemia (including heterozygous familial hypercholesterolemia [HeFH]) to reduce LDL-C.
*   Adjunct to diet and maximally tolerated statin therapy for adults with clinical atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease (ASCVD).

**Relevance in Nephrology Practice:**
*   CKD patients are at exceptionally high risk for ASCVD. Per KDIGO 2013 and ACC/AHA 2022 guidelines, statins remain first-line. However, many CKD patients fail to reach LDL-C targets or experience statin intolerance (myalgias, transaminitis). Leqvio (inclisiran) provides a potent, infrequent dosing alternative for LDL-C lowering in CKD patients with ASCVD.

**Key Supporting Clinical Trials:**
*   **ORION-10 (ASCVD):** Reduced LDL-C by 52% at 17 months compared to placebo.
*   **ORION-11 (ASCVD or ASCVD risk equivalents):** Reduced LDL-C by 50% at 17 months.
*   **ORION-7 (Renal Impairment PK Study):** Demonstrated that while plasma exposure of inclisiran increases with worsening renal function, the pharmacodynamic effect (LDL-C reduction) remains consistent. *No dose adjustment is required for any stage of CKD.*

### Dosing
**Standard Dosing & Administration:**
*   **Dose:** 284 mg administered as a single subcutaneous (SQ) injection.
*   **Schedule:** Initial dose, followed by a second dose at 3 months, and then **every 6 months** thereafter.
*   **Administration:** Must be administered by a healthcare professional (HCP) into the abdomen, upper arm, or thigh. *Not for patient self-administration.*

**CKD Dose Adjustments by eGFR Range:**
*   **eGFR ≥60 mL/min/1.73 m²:** 284 mg SQ (Initial, 3 mos, then q6 mos)
*   **eGFR 30-59 mL/min/1.73 m²:** 284 mg SQ (Initial, 3 mos, then q6 mos)
*   **eGFR 15-29 mL/min/1.73 m²:** 284 mg SQ (Initial, 3 mos, then q6 mos)
*   **eGFR <15 / Dialysis:** 284 mg SQ (Initial, 3 mos, then q6 mos). *Note: Hemodialysis does not significantly impact inclisiran clearance. Administer after dialysis if given on a dialysis day.*

**Hepatic Adjustments:**
*   Mild to moderate hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh A or B): No adjustment.
*   Severe hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh C): Not studied; use with caution.

### Monitoring
**Baseline Labs Required:**
*   Fasting Lipid Panel (FLP)
*   Hepatic function panel (AST, ALT, Bilirubin)
*   HbA1c (to assess baseline metabolic risk)

**Follow-up Lab Schedule:**
*   **Lipids:** Recheck Fasting Lipid Panel 30 days after the first dose to assess initial response, or immediately prior to the 3-month dose. Thereafter, check FLP every 6-12 months.
*   **Target LDL-C (ACC/AHA 2022):** 
    *   Clinical ASCVD: Target LDL-C < 70 mg/dL (or < 55 mg/dL for very high-risk/multiple events).
    *   Primary severe hypercholesterolemia: Target LDL-C < 100 mg/dL.

**Therapeutic Drug Monitoring:**
*   None required. Trough levels are not monitored.

### Adverse Effects & Safety
**Common Side Effects (>5%):**
*   Injection site reactions (8.2% - pain, erythema, rash)
*   Arthralgia (5.0%)
*   Urinary tract infections (6.8%)
*   Diarrhea (5.7%)
*   Bronchitis (4.3%)

**Serious/Rare Side Effects:**
*   Severe hypersensitivity reactions (rare)

**Black Box Warnings:**
*   None.

**Contraindications:**
*   Prior severe hypersensitivity to inclisiran or any excipients.

**CKD-Specific Safety Concerns:**
*   Highly safe in CKD. Because Leqvio (inclisiran) is targeted directly to the liver via GalNAc conjugation, systemic exposure is minimal, and renal clearance is not the primary mechanism of action termination. 

### Drug Interactions
*   **Major Interactions:** None clinically significant. 
*   **CYP450 Interactions:** Leqvio (inclisiran) is not a substrate, inhibitor, or inducer of CYP450 enzymes. It does not interact with statins, calcineurin inhibitors (tacrolimus/cyclosporine), or mTOR inhibitors, making it highly favorable for kidney transplant recipients.
*   **Food Interactions:** None.

### Special Populations
*   **Pregnancy:** Discontinue Leqvio (inclisiran) when pregnancy is recognized. Alternatively, consider stopping 1-2 months before planned pregnancy. (Cholesterol is essential for fetal development).
*   **Lactation:** Not recommended due to potential for serious adverse reactions in the breastfed infant.
*   **Elderly in CKD:** No dose adjustment needed based on age.
*   **Kidney Transplant:** Safe to use. No interactions with anti-rejection medications (tacrolimus, cyclosporine, mycophenolate).

### Patient Counseling Points
*   **Administration:** "This medication is given as an injection in our clinic. You will get one today, another in 3 months, and then only twice a year after that."
*   **Expectations:** "This drug works with your liver to clear bad cholesterol (LDL) from your blood. It does not replace a healthy diet."
*   **Side Effects:** "You may experience redness, pain, or bruising at the injection site. Joint pain is also possible but usually mild."
*   **Missed Doses:** "Because this is given every 6 months, keeping your clinic appointments is critical. If you miss an appointment by less than 3 months, we will give the dose and keep your original schedule. If you miss by more than 3 months, we will restart the schedule."

## Class Comparison: PCSK9 Targeted Therapies
*Note: While Leqvio (inclisiran) is the only FDA-approved **siRNA** targeting PCSK9, it is clinically grouped and compared with the PCSK9 **monoclonal antibodies (mAbs)** for formulary and therapeutic decision-making.*

| Feature | Leqvio (inclisiran) | Repatha (evolocumab) | Praluent (alirocumab) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Drug Class** | PCSK9 siRNA | PCSK9 Monoclonal Antibody | PCSK9 Monoclonal Antibody |
| **Mechanism** | Stops *production* of PCSK9 in liver | Binds/neutralizes *circulating* PCSK9 | Binds/neutralizes *circulating* PCSK9 |
| **Administration** | **HCP Administered** (In-clinic) | **Patient Self-Administered** (At home) | **Patient Self-Administered** (At home) |
| **Dosing Schedule** | Day 1, Month 3, then **every 6 months** | Every 2 weeks OR once monthly | Every 2 weeks OR once monthly |
| **eGFR Adjustments** | None (Safe in all CKD/ESRD) | None (Safe in all CKD/ESRD) | None (Safe in all CKD/ESRD) |
| **LDL-C Reduction** | ~50 - 52% | ~50 - 60% | ~45 - 58% |
| **CV Outcomes Data** | Ongoing (ORION-4, VICTORION) | Proven (FOURIER trial) | Proven (ODYSSEY OUTCOMES) |
| **Primary Billing** | Medical Benefit (J-Code J1306) | Pharmacy Benefit | Pharmacy Benefit |

### Decision Tree: When to Choose Leqvio (inclisiran)
1.  **Assess Indication:** Patient has ASCVD or HeFH and LDL-C remains >70 mg/dL despite maximally tolerated statin + ezetimibe (or patient is statin intolerant).
2.  **Assess Patient Compliance/Preference:** 
    *   If the patient is non-compliant with daily/bi-weekly medications, or has poor dexterity/needle phobia preventing self-injection $\rightarrow$ **Choose Leqvio (inclisiran)** (administered by TKE staff twice a year).
    *   If the patient prefers home administration and has established pharmacy benefit coverage for mAbs $\rightarrow$ **Choose Repatha or Praluent**.
3.  **Assess CV Risk Urgency:** If the patient needs *proven* cardiovascular mortality/morbidity reduction data immediately, mAbs (Repatha/Praluent) have completed CVOTs. Leqvio's CVOTs are currently ongoing, though LDL-lowering efficacy is established as a surrogate marker.

### Switching Between Agents
*   **Switching from a PCSK9 mAb (Repatha/Praluent) to Leqvio (inclisiran):** 
    *   Administer the first dose of Leqvio (inclisiran) immediately after the last dose of the PCSK9 mAb is due (e.g., 2 weeks after the last every-2-week mAb dose, or 4 weeks after the last monthly mAb dose).
*   **Switching from Leqvio (inclisiran) to a PCSK9 mAb:**
    *   Start the PCSK9 mAb 6 months after the last dose of Leqvio (inclisiran).
