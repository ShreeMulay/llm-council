# Antimetabolites in Nephrology: Clinical Monograph

Antimetabolites are a cornerstone of immunosuppressive therapy in nephrology, primarily utilized in kidney transplantation and the management of immune-mediated glomerular diseases (e.g., Lupus Nephritis, ANCA-associated vasculitis). This class includes **CellCept (mycophenolate mofetil)**, **Myfortic (mycophenolic acid)**, and **Imuran (azathioprine)**.

---

## CellCept (mycophenolate mofetil)

### Mechanism of Action
CellCept (mycophenolate mofetil) is a prodrug of mycophenolic acid (MPA). It acts as a reversible, non-competitive inhibitor of inosine monophosphate dehydrogenase (IMPDH). This blocks the *de novo* pathway of guanosine nucleotide synthesis. Because T and B lymphocytes are highly dependent on this *de novo* pathway for proliferation (unlike other cells which utilize salvage pathways), CellCept (mycophenolate mofetil) exerts a potent, selective cytostatic effect on lymphocytes.

### Indications in Nephrology
*   **FDA-Approved:** Prophylaxis of organ rejection in kidney transplantation (in combination with cyclosporine/tacrolimus and corticosteroids).
*   **Off-Label (Guideline-Supported):**
    *   **Lupus Nephritis (LN):** First-line induction and maintenance therapy (KDIGO 2024 Glomerular Diseases).
    *   **ANCA-Associated Vasculitis (AAV):** Maintenance therapy (alternative to rituximab or azathioprine) (KDIGO 2024).
    *   **IgA Nephropathy:** Considered in high-risk patients, particularly in Chinese populations (KDIGO 2021).
*   **Key Clinical Trials:**
    *   *ALMS Trial (2009):* Demonstrated CellCept (mycophenolate mofetil) is non-inferior to IV cyclophosphamide for LN induction, with a better adverse event profile.
    *   *MAINTAIN Nephritis Trial (2010):* Compared CellCept (mycophenolate mofetil) vs. Imuran (azathioprine) for LN maintenance; showed similar efficacy, though MMF had numerically fewer flares.

### Dosing
*   **Standard Dosing (Lupus Nephritis Induction):** 1000 mg to 1500 mg PO BID (Target: 2000 - 3000 mg/day).
    *   *Titration:* Start 500 mg PO BID for 1 week, increase to 1000 mg PO BID for 1 week, then 1500 mg PO BID to improve GI tolerance.
*   **Standard Dosing (Transplant/Maintenance):** 1000 mg PO BID.
*   **CKD Dose Adjustments by eGFR Range:**
    *   **eGFR ≥60 mL/min:** No adjustment. Standard dosing.
    *   **eGFR 30-59 mL/min:** No adjustment. Standard dosing.
    *   **eGFR 15-29 mL/min:** Avoid doses >1000 mg BID. Max dose: 1000 mg PO BID.
    *   **eGFR <15 mL/min / Dialysis:** Max dose: 1000 mg PO BID. Monitor closely for hematologic toxicity due to accumulation of the inactive glucuronide metabolite (MPAG), which can displace active MPA from albumin.
*   **Hepatic Adjustments:** No dose adjustment required for hepatic parenchymal disease.

### Monitoring
*   **Baseline Labs:** CBC with differential, CMP, Hepatitis B/C serologies, QuantiFERON-TB Gold, serum pregnancy test (mandatory for females of reproductive potential).
*   **Follow-up Schedule [TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED]:**
    *   CBC weekly for the first month, twice monthly for months 2 and 3, then monthly through the first year.
    *   CMP monthly.
    *   *Target parameters:* Hold or reduce dose if WBC <3.0 x 10^3/µL or ANC <1.5 x 10^3/µL.
*   **Therapeutic Drug Monitoring (TDM):** Not routinely recommended for glomerular diseases. In transplant, target MPA trough levels are typically 1.5 - 3.0 mcg/mL, though AUC monitoring is more accurate.

### Adverse Effects & Safety
*   **Common (>5%):** Diarrhea (up to 30%), nausea, vomiting, leukopenia, anemia, infections (bacterial, viral, fungal).
*   **Serious/Rare:** Progressive multifocal leukoencephalopathy (PML), BK virus nephropathy, pure red cell aplasia.
*   **Black Box Warnings:**
    1.  Increased risk of first-trimester pregnancy loss and congenital malformations.
    2.  Increased susceptibility to infection.
    3.  Increased risk of malignancies (especially lymphoma and skin cancers).
*   **Contraindications:** Hypersensitivity to mycophenolate, pregnancy (unless benefits outweigh risks and no alternatives exist).
*   **CKD-Specific Safety Concerns:** Hypoalbuminemia (common in nephrotic syndrome) increases the free (active) fraction of MPA, significantly increasing the risk of leukopenia and diarrhea even at standard doses.

### Drug Interactions
*   **Antacids (Mg/Al) & Phosphate Binders (Sevelamer):** Significantly decrease absorption. Separate by at least 2 hours.
*   **Proton Pump Inhibitors (PPIs):** Decrease absorption of CellCept (mycophenolate mofetil) by altering gastric pH. (Consider switching to H2 blocker or Myfortic if PPI is mandatory).
*   **Acyclovir/Valacyclovir:** Compete for renal tubular secretion with MPAG; can increase levels of both drugs in severe CKD.

### Special Populations
*   **Pregnancy:** Category D/X equivalent. Requires enrollment in the Mycophenolate REMS program. Must use two forms of reliable contraception.
*   **Elderly:** Higher risk of adverse events, particularly infections and GI bleeding. Consider lower starting doses.

### Patient Counseling Points
*   Take on an empty stomach (1 hour before or 2 hours after meals) to ensure consistent absorption.
*   Do not crush, chew, or open capsules/tablets (teratogenic exposure risk to caregivers).
*   Report any signs of infection (fever, chills, sore throat), unusual bleeding, or severe diarrhea immediately.
*   Strict adherence to dual contraception is required for women of childbearing age.

### Prior Authorization & Coding
*   **ICD-10 Codes:** M32.14 (Glomerular disease in systemic lupus erythematosus), M32.15 (Tubulo-interstitial nephropathy in SLE), Z94.0 (Kidney transplant status).
*   **CPT Codes:** 99214/99215 (E&M for complex immunosuppression management).
*   **PA Tips:** For off-label GN use, cite KDIGO 2024 guidelines and document failure or contraindication to cyclophosphamide if required by the payer.

## Myfortic (mycophenolic acid)

### Mechanism of Action
Myfortic (mycophenolic acid) is the enteric-coated, delayed-release formulation of the active moiety of CellCept. It delivers the active drug directly to the small intestine, bypassing the stomach. The mechanism of action (IMPDH inhibition) is identical to CellCept.

### Indications in Nephrology
*   **FDA-Approved:** Prophylaxis of organ rejection in kidney transplantation.
*   **Off-Label:** Same as CellCept (Lupus Nephritis, AAV, IgAN). Primarily utilized when patients experience dose-limiting upper GI intolerance (nausea, dyspepsia) on CellCept.

### Dosing
*   *Note: 720 mg of Myfortic is equimolar to 1000 mg of CellCept.*
*   **Standard Dosing (Lupus Nephritis Induction):** 720 mg to 1080 mg PO BID.
*   **Standard Dosing (Transplant/Maintenance):** 720 mg PO BID.
*   **CKD Dose Adjustments by eGFR Range:**
    *   **eGFR ≥60 mL/min:** No adjustment.
    *   **eGFR 30-59 mL/min:** No adjustment.
    *   **eGFR 15-29 mL/min:** Max dose: 720 mg PO BID.
    *   **eGFR <15 mL/min / Dialysis:** Max dose: 720 mg PO BID. Monitor closely for toxicity.
*   **Hepatic Adjustments:** No adjustment needed.

### Monitoring
*   Identical to CellCept (mycophenolate mofetil). See above.

### Adverse Effects & Safety
*   **Common (>5%):** Lower GI side effects (diarrhea) remain common, though upper GI side effects (nausea, vomiting) are generally reduced compared to CellCept. Leukopenia, anemia.
*   **Black Box Warnings & Contraindications:** Identical to CellCept.
*   **CKD-Specific Safety Concerns:** Identical to CellCept (hypoalbuminemia increases free drug fraction).

### Drug Interactions
*   **PPIs:** Unlike CellCept, Myfortic (mycophenolic acid) absorption is **NOT** significantly affected by PPIs due to its enteric coating. This is a key clinical advantage.
*   **Antacids/Binders:** Still decrease absorption. Separate administration.
*   **Acyclovir/Valacyclovir:** Same interaction as CellCept.

### Special Populations & Patient Counseling
*   Identical to CellCept. Emphasize: **Must be swallowed whole.** Do not crush, chew, or cut, as this destroys the enteric coating.

### Prior Authorization & Coding
*   **PA Tips:** Payers often require documented GI intolerance/failure of generic CellCept (mycophenolate mofetil) before approving Myfortic (mycophenolic acid). Document specific GI symptoms (e.g., "patient experienced intractable nausea and vomiting on MMF 1000mg BID").

## Imuran (azathioprine)

### Mechanism of Action
Imuran (azathioprine) is a prodrug that is rapidly converted to 6-mercaptopurine (6-MP). 6-MP acts as a purine analogue, incorporating into cellular DNA and RNA, thereby inhibiting purine synthesis. This halts the proliferation of rapidly dividing cells, particularly T and B lymphocytes.

### Indications in Nephrology
*   **FDA-Approved:** Kidney transplantation, Rheumatoid Arthritis.
*   **Off-Label (Guideline-Supported):**
    *   **Lupus Nephritis:** Maintenance therapy (alternative to MMF) (KDIGO 2024).
    *   **ANCA-Associated Vasculitis:** Maintenance therapy (KDIGO 2024).
    *   **IgA Vasculitis (HSP):** Maintenance therapy.
*   **Key Clinical Trials:**
    *   *AZA-ANCA Trial (2003):* Established Imuran (azathioprine) as standard maintenance therapy for AAV following cyclophosphamide induction (though rituximab is now often preferred).
    *   *MAINTAIN Nephritis Trial:* Showed Imuran (azathioprine) is a viable alternative to MMF for LN maintenance.

### Dosing
*   **Standard Dosing:** 1.5 to 2.0 mg/kg/day PO (typically 100 - 150 mg daily). Given as a single daily dose or divided BID.
*   **CKD Dose Adjustments by eGFR Range:**
    *   **eGFR ≥50 mL/min:** No adjustment. Standard dosing.
    *   **eGFR 10-50 mL/min:** Administer 75% of the standard dose.
    *   **eGFR <10 mL/min:** Administer 50% of the standard dose.
    *   **Dialysis:** Administer 50% of the standard dose. Give *after* hemodialysis on dialysis days, as the drug is dialyzable.
*   **Hepatic Adjustments:** Reduce dose or avoid in severe hepatic impairment.

### Monitoring
*   **Baseline Labs:** **TPMT (thiopurine methyltransferase) and NUDT15 genotyping/phenotyping is CRITICAL prior to initiation.**
    *   *Normal TPMT:* Standard dosing.
    *   *Intermediate TPMT:* Start at 30-50% of target dose.
    *   *Low/Absent TPMT:* **DO NOT USE** (fatal myelosuppression risk).
*   **Follow-up Schedule [TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED]:**
    *   CBC weekly for the first 4 weeks, then every 2 weeks for 2 months, then monthly.
    *   CMP (specifically LFTs) monthly.
    *   *Target parameters:* Maintain WBC >3.5 x 10^3/µL.

### Adverse Effects & Safety
*   **Common (>5%):** Leukopenia, thrombocytopenia, nausea, vomiting, elevated LFTs.
*   **Serious/Rare:** Hepatotoxicity (veno-occlusive disease, nodular regenerative hyperplasia), pancreatitis, severe bone marrow suppression.
*   **Black Box Warnings:** Chronic immunosuppression increases the risk of malignancy (lymphoma, skin cancer). Mutagenic potential.
*   **Contraindications:** Hypersensitivity, prior alkylating agent therapy (relative), absent TPMT activity.

### Drug Interactions
*   **Allopurinol & Febuxostat (Xanthine Oxidase Inhibitors):** **SEVERE/FATAL INTERACTION.** Xanthine oxidase is required to metabolize 6-MP. Concomitant use leads to massive accumulation of 6-MP and fatal bone marrow suppression.
    *   *Action:* Avoid combination if possible. If mandatory, Imuran (azathioprine) dose **MUST be reduced to 25% (a 75% reduction)** of the standard dose, with extreme hematologic monitoring.
*   **ACE Inhibitors:** May increase the risk of severe leukopenia.
*   **Warfarin:** Imuran (azathioprine) may decrease the anticoagulant effect of warfarin.

### Special Populations
*   **Pregnancy:** Category D. However, it is generally considered **safer than mycophenolate** during pregnancy. It is the preferred antimetabolite for women with Lupus Nephritis who are actively planning pregnancy (KDIGO 2024).
*   **Elderly:** Increased risk of toxicity; start at the lower end of the dosing range.

### Patient Counseling Points
*   Report any unusual bleeding, bruising, severe fatigue, or signs of infection immediately.
*   Report any yellowing of the skin/eyes or severe abdominal pain (pancreatitis/hepatotoxicity).
*   Use sun protection (increased risk of skin cancer).
*   Inform all providers you are taking this medication, especially before starting any gout medications.

### Prior Authorization & Coding
*   **ICD-10 Codes:** M32.14, M31.3 (Wegener's granulomatosis), Z94.0.
*   **PA Tips:** Generally inexpensive and rarely requires PA, but TPMT testing may require justification (CPT 81401).

## Class Comparison & Decision Tree

### Antimetabolite Comparison Table

| Feature | CellCept (mycophenolate mofetil) | Myfortic (mycophenolic acid) | Imuran (azathioprine) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Standard Dose** | 1000 - 1500 mg BID | 720 - 1080 mg BID | 1.5 - 2 mg/kg/day |
| **eGFR <30 Limit** | Max 1000 mg BID | Max 720 mg BID | 50-75% dose reduction |
| **Key Advantage** | Superior efficacy in LN induction | Less upper GI toxicity; No PPI interaction | Safe in pregnancy planning |
| **Key Disadvantage** | Highly teratogenic; GI intolerance | Highly teratogenic; More expensive | Fatal interaction with allopurinol |
| **Pre-testing** | Pregnancy test | Pregnancy test | TPMT / NUDT15 testing |
| **Cost / PA** | Low cost (generic) / Easy PA | Moderate cost / PA often requires MMF failure | Very low cost / No PA usually |

### Decision Tree: Selection & Switching

**1. When to Start / Choose:**
*   **Lupus Nephritis Induction:** Start **CellCept (mycophenolate mofetil)**. It is the preferred antimetabolite per KDIGO guidelines.
*   **Patient on a mandatory PPI or experiencing severe nausea/dyspepsia on MMF:** Switch to **Myfortic (mycophenolic acid)**.
*   **Female patient planning pregnancy within 6 months:** Switch to **Imuran (azathioprine)**. (MMF/MPA must be stopped at least 6 weeks prior to conception).
*   **Patient with concomitant gout requiring allopurinol:** Choose **CellCept** or **Myfortic**. Avoid Imuran.

**2. How to Switch Within Class [TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED]:**
*   *CellCept to Myfortic:* Direct switch. 1000 mg CellCept = 720 mg Myfortic. 500 mg CellCept = 360 mg Myfortic.
*   *CellCept to Imuran (e.g., for pregnancy planning):*
    *   Ensure TPMT testing is complete and normal.
    *   Do not overlap full doses (risk of profound leukopenia).
    *   Standard protocol: Stop MMF, start Imuran 50 mg daily, titrate up to 1.5-2 mg/kg/day over 2-4 weeks while monitoring CBC weekly.

**3. When to Escalate / Stop:**
*   *Escalate:* If LN proteinuria is not improving by 3-6 months, ensure patient is at maximum tolerated dose (up to 3g/day MMF equivalent) before declaring failure.
*   *Stop/Hold:* Absolute ANC < 1.5 x 10^3/µL, severe active systemic infection, or confirmed pregnancy on MMF/MPA. Hold therapy, manage acute issue, and restart at a 25-50% dose reduction once resolved.
