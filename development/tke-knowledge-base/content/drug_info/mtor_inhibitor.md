# Clinical Drug Monograph: mTOR Inhibitors in Nephrology

**Class Overview:** Mammalian target of rapamycin (mTOR) inhibitors are potent immunosuppressive and antineoplastic agents. In nephrology, they are primarily utilized for kidney transplant rejection prophylaxis (often as calcineurin inhibitor [CNI] sparing agents) and for the treatment of tuberous sclerosis complex (TSC)-associated renal angiomyolipomas (AML). 

**Guideline Basis:** Content is based on the KDIGO Clinical Practice Guideline for the Care of Kidney Transplant Recipients (2009, with ongoing updates), AST/ASTS guidelines, and international consensus guidelines for TSC management.

---

## Rapamune (sirolimus)

### Mechanism of Action
Rapamune (sirolimus) is a macrolide immunosuppressant. It binds to the immunophilin FK Binding Protein-12 (FKBP-12) to form an immunosuppressive complex. This complex binds to and inhibits the activation of the mammalian Target of Rapamycin (mTOR) complex 1 (mTORC1). Inhibition of mTOR suppresses cytokine-driven T-cell proliferation, halting the cell cycle from the G1 to the S phase.

### Indications in Nephrology
*   **FDA-Approved:** Prophylaxis of organ rejection in kidney transplant recipients aged ≥13 years.
    *   *Key Trial:* Rapamune Maintenance Regimen (RMR) trial demonstrated successful cyclosporine withdrawal at 3 months post-transplant, improving renal function while maintaining graft survival.
*   **Off-Label Uses:** 
    *   CNI-toxicity management (switching from CNI to mTORi).
    *   Primary focal segmental glomerulosclerosis (FSGS) or membranous nephropathy (MN) in patients intolerant to standard therapies (limited evidence).
    *   Autosomal Dominant Polycystic Kidney Disease (ADPKD) – *Note: Clinical trials (e.g., SUISSE ADPKD) failed to show significant slowing of cyst growth; generally not recommended for this indication.*

### Dosing
*   **Standard Transplant Dosing (De Novo):** 
    *   Loading dose: 6 mg orally once.
    *   Maintenance: 2 mg orally once daily.
    *   *Titration:* Adjust based on therapeutic drug monitoring (TDM) to target trough levels.
*   **Standard Transplant Dosing (CNI Withdrawal at 2-4 months):**
    *   Gradually taper CNI over 4-8 weeks while titrating Rapamune (sirolimus) to higher trough targets (e.g., 12-24 ng/mL initially, then 16-22 ng/mL for the first year).
*   **CKD Dose Adjustments by eGFR Range:**
    *   Rapamune (sirolimus) is extensively metabolized by the liver; renal clearance is minimal.
    *   **eGFR ≥60 mL/min/1.73m²:** No dose adjustment required.
    *   **eGFR 30-59 mL/min/1.73m²:** No dose adjustment required.
    *   **eGFR 15-29 mL/min/1.73m²:** No dose adjustment required.
    *   **eGFR <15 / Dialysis:** No dose adjustment required. Non-dialyzable.
    *   *Clinical Caveat:* While eGFR does not dictate dose, mTOR inhibitors can exacerbate proteinuria. Use with extreme caution if baseline UACR >800 mg/g.
*   **Hepatic Adjustments:**
    *   Mild-to-Moderate (Child-Pugh A/B): Reduce maintenance dose by 33%. Loading dose remains unchanged.
    *   Severe (Child-Pugh C): Reduce maintenance dose by 50%.

### Monitoring
*   **Baseline Labs:** CMP (BUN, SCr, eGFR, LFTs), CBC with diff, Fasting Lipid Panel, Urinalysis, and UPCR/UACR.
*   **Follow-up Schedule:**
    *   *Weekly for first month*, then biweekly for month 2, then monthly.
    *   Monitor Fasting Lipid Panel at 1 month, then quarterly.
    *   Monitor UPCR/UACR monthly for the first 3 months, then quarterly. Target UACR <30 mg/g or baseline.
*   **Therapeutic Drug Monitoring (TDM):**
    *   *Timing:* Draw trough level immediately before the next scheduled dose.
    *   *Target Trough (with CNI):* 4–10 ng/mL [TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED for specific transplant center targets].
    *   *Target Trough (without CNI):* 12–20 ng/mL.

### Adverse Effects & Safety
*   **Common (>5%):** Hyperlipidemia (hypercholesterolemia and hypertriglyceridemia), peripheral edema, stomatitis/mouth ulcers, hypertension, anemia, thrombocytopenia, impaired wound healing.
*   **Serious/Rare:** Non-infectious interstitial pneumonitis, de novo FSGS, thrombotic microangiopathy (TMA) when combined with CNIs, BK virus nephropathy.
*   **Black Box Warnings:**
    *   Increased susceptibility to infection and possible development of lymphoma/malignancies.
    *   Not recommended for liver or lung transplant recipients (increased mortality and graft loss).
*   **Contraindications:** Hypersensitivity to sirolimus.
*   **CKD-Specific Safety Concerns:** 
    *   **Proteinuria:** Can cause severe, nephrotic-range proteinuria. Discontinue if UACR exceeds 800-1000 mg/g.
    *   **Wound Healing:** Avoid use immediately post-transplant (first 30-90 days) in patients with high surgical complication risks or delayed graft function (DGF).

### Drug Interactions
*   **CYP3A4 and P-gp Substrate:** 
    *   *Strong Inhibitors* (e.g., ketoconazole, voriconazole, clarithromycin, ritonavir): Avoid use; significantly increases sirolimus levels.
    *   *Strong Inducers* (e.g., rifampin, phenytoin): Avoid use; significantly decreases sirolimus levels.
*   **Calcineurin Inhibitors (Cyclosporine/Tacrolimus):** Cyclosporine increases sirolimus levels. Administer Rapamune (sirolimus) 4 hours *after* cyclosporine.
*   **Food Interactions:** High-fat meals alter absorption. Must be taken consistently *either* always with food or always without food. Avoid grapefruit and grapefruit juice.

### Special Populations
*   **Pregnancy:** Category C (historical). Avoid use. Effective contraception required during and for 12 weeks after stopping therapy.
*   **Elderly:** No specific dose adjustments, but higher risk of infections and pneumonitis.
*   **Transplant:** High risk of delayed wound healing and lymphocele formation if started immediately post-op.

### Patient Counseling Points
*   **Administration:** Take exactly as prescribed, at the same time every day. Choose to take it consistently with food OR without food. Take 4 hours after cyclosporine if applicable.
*   **Diet:** Do not consume grapefruit or grapefruit juice.
*   **Warning Signs:** Report fever, persistent cough, shortness of breath (signs of pneumonitis or infection), or severe mouth sores.
*   **Wound Healing:** Inform all surgeons/dentists you are on this medication, as it delays healing.

### Prior Authorization & Coding
*   **ICD-10 Codes:** Z94.0 (Kidney transplant status), T86.10 (Unspecified complication of kidney transplant - if used for CNI toxicity).
*   **CPT Codes:** 80195 (Sirolimus drug level).
*   **PA Tips:** Document CNI toxicity (e.g., rising SCr, tremors) or specific transplant center protocol if using as de novo therapy.

## Zortress, Afinitor (everolimus)

### Mechanism of Action
Everolimus is a derivative of sirolimus with a very similar mechanism of action (mTORC1 inhibition via FKBP-12 binding). It has a shorter half-life (approx. 30 hours) compared to sirolimus (approx. 60 hours), allowing for faster achievement of steady-state levels.

### Indications in Nephrology
*   **FDA-Approved (Zortress):** Prophylaxis of organ rejection in kidney transplant recipients at low-to-moderate immunologic risk (used in combination with basiliximab, cyclosporine, and corticosteroids).
    *   *Key Trial:* A2309 study showed Zortress + reduced-dose CNI had comparable efficacy to standard CNI regimens with better preservation of eGFR at 12 months.
*   **FDA-Approved (Afinitor):** Renal angiomyolipoma (AML) associated with tuberous sclerosis complex (TSC) not requiring immediate surgery.
    *   *Key Trial:* EXIST-2 trial showed 42% of patients had ≥50% reduction in AML volume vs. 0% on placebo.
*   **Off-Label Uses:** CNI-sparing regimens in kidney transplant.

### Dosing
*   **Zortress (Kidney Transplant):**
    *   Initial: 0.75 mg orally twice daily (BID).
    *   *Titration:* Adjust based on TDM.
*   **Afinitor (TSC-Associated Renal AML):**
    *   Initial: 10 mg orally once daily.
    *   *Titration:* Reduce to 5 mg daily if intolerable side effects occur.
*   **CKD Dose Adjustments by eGFR Range:**
    *   Hepatically cleared; no renal adjustment required.
    *   **eGFR ≥60 mL/min/1.73m²:** Standard dose.
    *   **eGFR 30-59 mL/min/1.73m²:** Standard dose.
    *   **eGFR 15-29 mL/min/1.73m²:** Standard dose.
    *   **eGFR <15 / Dialysis:** Standard dose. Non-dialyzable.
    *   *Clinical Caveat:* Monitor for worsening proteinuria.
*   **Hepatic Adjustments:**
    *   *Zortress:* Child-Pugh A (reduce dose by 33%); Child-Pugh B (reduce dose by 50%); Child-Pugh C (avoid or reduce by >50%).
    *   *Afinitor:* Child-Pugh A (7.5 mg daily); Child-Pugh B (5 mg daily); Child-Pugh C (2.5 mg daily if benefits outweigh risks).

### Monitoring
*   **Baseline Labs:** CMP, CBC, Fasting Lipid Panel, UPCR/UACR.
*   **Follow-up Schedule:**
    *   Lipids and UPCR monthly for 3 months, then quarterly.
    *   CBC and CMP bi-weekly for the first month, then monthly.
*   **Therapeutic Drug Monitoring (TDM) - *Zortress Only*:**
    *   *Timing:* Draw trough level immediately before the morning dose (steady state reached in 4-5 days).
    *   *Target Trough:* 3–8 ng/mL (when used with reduced-dose CNI) [TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED].
    *   *Note:* Afinitor for TSC-AML does not routinely require TDM, though some centers target 5-15 ng/mL if efficacy is suboptimal.

### Adverse Effects & Safety
*   **Common (>5%):** Stomatitis (very common with Afinitor, up to 70%), hyperlipidemia, peripheral edema, proteinuria, delayed wound healing, acne-like rash.
*   **Serious/Rare:** Non-infectious pneumonitis, severe immunosuppression, angioedema, TMA.
*   **Black Box Warnings (Zortress):**
    *   Increased risk of malignancies and infections.
    *   Increased risk of kidney arterial and venous thrombosis resulting in graft loss (usually within the first 30 days post-transplant).
*   **Contraindications:** Hypersensitivity to everolimus or sirolimus.
*   **CKD-Specific Safety Concerns:** 
    *   **Proteinuria:** Can induce de novo proteinuria. If UACR >800 mg/g, consider discontinuation.
    *   **Graft Thrombosis:** High risk in the immediate post-transplant period.

### Drug Interactions
*   **CYP3A4 and P-gp Substrate:** 
    *   Avoid strong CYP3A4 inhibitors (e.g., ketoconazole) and inducers (e.g., rifampin).
    *   Dose adjustments required if co-administered with moderate inhibitors (e.g., fluconazole, diltiazem, verapamil) or inducers.
*   **Angiotensin-Converting Enzyme (ACE) Inhibitors:** Co-administration increases the risk of angioedema.
*   **Food Interactions:** Take consistently with or without food. Avoid grapefruit/grapefruit juice.

### Special Populations
*   **Pregnancy:** Avoid use. Effective contraception required during and for 8 weeks after stopping therapy.
*   **Elderly:** Monitor closely for pneumonitis and infections.

### Patient Counseling Points
*   **Mouth Care:** For Afinitor, use a dexamethasone alcohol-free mouthwash to prevent/treat stomatitis. Avoid commercial mouthwashes containing alcohol or peroxide.
*   **Administration:** Take at the same time every day, consistently with or without food. Do not crush or chew tablets.
*   **Warning Signs:** Report sudden shortness of breath, dry cough, or fever immediately.

### Prior Authorization & Coding
*   **ICD-10 Codes:** 
    *   Z94.0 (Kidney transplant status)
    *   D30.00, D30.01, D30.02 (Benign neoplasm of kidney / AML)
    *   Q85.1 (Tuberous sclerosis)
*   **CPT Codes:** 80169 (Everolimus drug level).
*   **PA Tips:** For Afinitor, clearly document the diagnosis of TSC and the size/presence of renal AMLs (>3 cm is a common threshold for approval).
*   **Patient Assistance:** Novartis offers the Universal Co-pay Program for Afinitor.

### Decision Tree: Starting, Switching, and Stopping

#### When to START an mTOR Inhibitor
*   **Transplant:** Patient is 1-3 months post-kidney transplant, wound healing is complete, and there is a desire to minimize CNI exposure due to CNI-nephrotoxicity (rising SCr, bland urine sediment, biopsy showing isometric vacuolization).
*   **TSC-AML:** Patient has TSC with a renal AML >3 cm, asymptomatic or mildly symptomatic, and is not actively bleeding (which would require embolization/surgery).

#### When to SWITCH Between Agents
*   **Sirolimus to Everolimus (Zortress):** Consider if the patient struggles with the long half-life of sirolimus making TDM difficult, or if twice-daily dosing aligns better with their CNI (tacrolimus/cyclosporine) BID schedule for compliance.
    *   *Conversion:* Generally a 1:1 total daily dose conversion (e.g., Sirolimus 2 mg daily = Everolimus 1 mg BID), but monitor troughs 4-5 days after switching.
*   **Everolimus to Sirolimus:** Consider if the patient prefers once-daily dosing for adherence.

#### When to STOP or ESCALATE Therapy
*   **STOP / Discontinue if:**
    *   **Proteinuria:** UACR increases to >800-1000 mg/g or UPCR >1.0 g/g. (Switch back to CNI or alternative immunosuppression).
    *   **Pneumonitis:** Patient develops non-infectious interstitial pneumonitis (ground-glass opacities on CT, hypoxia). Stop immediately and initiate systemic corticosteroids.
    *   **Severe Stomatitis:** Grade 3/4 mouth ulcers preventing oral intake (especially with Afinitor).
    *   **Surgery:** Stop 1-2 weeks prior to major elective surgery due to wound healing delays. Bridge with alternative immunosuppression [TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED].
*   **ESCALATE / Adjust if:**
    *   Trough levels are subtherapeutic.
    *   Patient is started on a moderate CYP3A4 inducer (will require mTORi dose increase).
