# Nonsteroidal Mineralocorticoid Receptor Antagonists (ns-MRA)
**Clinical Reference Guide for The Kidney Experts (TKE)**

This monograph covers the nonsteroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist (ns-MRA) class. Currently, **Kerendia (finerenone)** is the only FDA-approved agent in this class available in the United States for nephrology indications.

---

## Kerendia (finerenone)

### Mechanism of Action
Kerendia (finerenone) is a nonsteroidal, selective mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist (ns-MRA). 
*   **Receptor Specificity:** Unlike steroidal MRAs (spironolactone, eplerenone), finerenone's bulky nonsteroidal structure binds to the mineralocorticoid receptor (MR) in a unique conformation. This specific binding prevents the recruitment of transcriptional cofactors that drive pro-inflammatory and pro-fibrotic gene expression.
*   **Tissue Distribution:** Finerenone distributes equally between the heart and the kidneys, whereas steroidal MRAs accumulate preferentially in the kidneys (which contributes to their higher hyperkalemia risk).
*   **Clinical Effect:** It reduces renal fibrosis, inflammation, and albuminuria with a significantly lower risk of hyperkalemia and minimal blood pressure lowering compared to steroidal MRAs. It has no anti-androgenic effects (no gynecomastia).

### Indications in Nephrology
**FDA-Approved Indications:**
*   To reduce the risk of sustained eGFR decline, end-stage kidney disease (ESKD), cardiovascular death, non-fatal myocardial infarction, and hospitalization for heart failure in adult patients with **chronic kidney disease (CKD) associated with type 2 diabetes (T2D)**.

**Guideline Recommendations:**
*   **KDIGO 2022/2024 & ADA 2024:** Recommend initiating a nonsteroidal MRA (finerenone) in patients with T2D, eGFR ≥25 mL/min/1.73 m², normal serum potassium, and residual albuminuria (target UACR ≥30 mg/g) despite maximum tolerated RAS inhibition (ACEi/ARB) and SGLT2 inhibitor therapy.

**Off-Label Uses Common in Nephrology:**
*   Non-diabetic CKD with proteinuria (Currently under investigation in the FIND-CKD trial).
*   Heart failure with mildly reduced or preserved ejection fraction (FINEARTS-HF trial demonstrated significant CV benefit; FDA approval for this indication is anticipated).

**Key Supporting Clinical Trials:**
*   **FIDELIO-DKD (2020):** Evaluated primary renal outcomes in T2D + CKD. Showed an 18% relative risk reduction in the primary composite renal outcome (ESKD, sustained ≥40% decrease in eGFR, or renal death).
*   **FIGARO-DKD (2021):** Evaluated primary CV outcomes in T2D + CKD (earlier stage CKD than FIDELIO). Showed a 13% relative risk reduction in the primary composite CV outcome.
*   **FIDELITY (Pooled Analysis):** Confirmed a 23% risk reduction in composite kidney outcomes and 14% reduction in CV events across the spectrum of CKD in T2D.

### Dosing
Initiation and titration are strictly dependent on eGFR and serum potassium. 
*   **Target Dose:** 20 mg PO once daily.
*   **Administration:** Take with or without food.

#### CKD Dose Adjustments by eGFR Range
*   **eGFR ≥60 mL/min/1.73 m²:** Initiate at **20 mg** once daily.
*   **eGFR 25–59 mL/min/1.73 m²:** Initiate at **10 mg** once daily.
*   **eGFR 15–24 mL/min/1.73 m²:** **Do not initiate.** If the patient is already on Kerendia (finerenone) and eGFR drops below 25, *continue* the medication unless eGFR drops <15 or hyperkalemia occurs.
*   **eGFR <15 mL/min/1.73 m² / Dialysis:** Discontinue therapy.

#### Hepatic Adjustments
*   Mild to Moderate (Child-Pugh A or B): No adjustment needed.
*   Severe (Child-Pugh C): Avoid use (not studied).

### Monitoring
#### Baseline Labs Required
*   **Serum Potassium:** Must be **≤5.0 mEq/L** to initiate.
*   **eGFR:** Must be **≥25 mL/min/1.73 m²** to initiate.
*   **UACR:** To confirm indication (target UACR ≥30 mg/g).

#### Follow-Up Lab Schedule
*   Check serum potassium and eGFR **4 weeks** after initiation and **4 weeks** after any dose adjustment.

### Adverse Effects & Safety
**Common Side Effects (>5%):**
*   Hyperkalemia (14-18% in trials, though severe hyperkalemia leading to discontinuation is low at ~2-3%)
*   Hypotension (4.8%)
*   Hyponatremia (1.4%)

**Serious/Rare Side Effects:**
*   Severe hyperkalemia requiring hospitalization.

**Contraindications:**
*   Concomitant use with strong CYP3A4 inhibitors (e.g., itraconazole, ketoconazole, ritonavir, clarithromycin).
*   Adrenal insufficiency.

**CKD-Specific Safety Concerns:**
*   **Hyperkalemia Risk Mitigation:** The risk of hyperkalemia is significantly reduced when Kerendia (finerenone) is co-administered with an SGLT2 inhibitor (e.g., Farxiga (dapagliflozin) or Jardiance (empagliflozin)). KDIGO strongly recommends maximizing SGLT2i therapy prior to or concurrent with ns-MRA initiation.

### Drug Interactions
Kerendia (finerenone) is a major substrate of **CYP3A4**.
*   **Strong CYP3A4 Inhibitors:** CONTRAINDICATED. (e.g., clarithromycin, itraconazole, ritonavir).
*   **Moderate/Weak CYP3A4 Inhibitors:** Caution required. Includes common nephrology drugs like **diltiazem, verapamil, and amiodarone**. If co-administered, monitor serum potassium more frequently.
*   **Strong CYP3A4 Inducers:** Avoid use. (e.g., rifampin, carbamazepine, phenytoin, St. John's Wort) — will significantly decrease finerenone efficacy.
*   **Other Potassium-Sparing Agents:** Avoid concomitant use with steroidal MRAs (spironolactone, eplerenone), amiloride, or triamterene. Caution with TMP-SMX (Bactrim).
*   **Food Interactions:** **Grapefruit and grapefruit juice** act as CYP3A4 inhibitors and should be strictly avoided, as they increase finerenone exposure and hyperkalemia risk.

### Special Populations
*   **Pregnancy:** Category not formally assigned, but animal studies show fetal toxicity. **Avoid in pregnancy.** Women of childbearing potential should use effective contraception during treatment.
*   **Lactation:** Discontinue drug or discontinue nursing (present in animal milk, risk to infant unknown).
*   **Elderly:** No dose adjustment required based on age alone; adjust based on eGFR.
*   **Transplant:** Not studied in kidney transplant recipients. Use with extreme caution due to interactions with calcineurin inhibitors (tacrolimus/cyclosporine are CYP3A4 substrates/inhibitors and independently cause hyperkalemia). *[TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED]: Consult transplant center before initiating in any post-transplant patient.*

### Patient Counseling Points
*   **Purpose:** "This medication protects your kidneys from scarring and lowers your risk of needing dialysis or having a heart attack."
*   **Dietary Warnings:** "Do not eat grapefruit or drink grapefruit juice while on this medication. Avoid salt substitutes, as they are made of potassium."
*   **Lab Compliance:** "We must check your blood work exactly 4 weeks after you start this medication to ensure your potassium levels are safe."
*   **Warning Signs:** "Call the clinic if you experience severe muscle weakness, numbness/tingling, or a slow/irregular heartbeat, as these can be signs of high potassium."

### Prior Authorization & Coding
*   **Common ICD-10 Codes:** 
    *   E11.22 (Type 2 diabetes mellitus with diabetic chronic kidney disease)
    *   N18.31 - N18.4 (CKD Stage 3a, 3b, 4)
    *   *Note: PA usually requires documentation of both T2D and CKD.*
*   **PA Success Tips:** Document that the patient is already on maximally tolerated ACEi/ARB and an SGLT2 inhibitor (or document contraindication/intolerance to SGLT2i). Document baseline K+ ≤5.0.
*   **Cost/Assistance:** High retail cost. Bayer offers the "Kerendia Patient Assistance Program" for uninsured/underinsured patients, and a copay card for commercial insurance (often bringing cost to $0-$10/month).

## Class Comparison: Nonsteroidal vs. Steroidal MRAs

While Kerendia (finerenone) is the only ns-MRA currently approved, nephrology providers frequently must choose between initiating an ns-MRA versus an older steroidal MRA (spironolactone or eplerenone). 

### Comparison Table

| Feature | Kerendia (finerenone) | Aldactone (spironolactone) | Inspra (eplerenone) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Class** | Nonsteroidal MRA | Steroidal MRA | Steroidal MRA |
| **Receptor Affinity** | Highly selective for MR | Non-selective (binds androgen/progesterone) | Selective for MR |
| **Tissue Distribution** | Heart = Kidney | Kidney > Heart | Kidney > Heart |
| **Half-Life** | Short (2-3 hours) | Long (active metabolites >15 hrs) | Short (4-6 hours) |
| **Primary Nephrology Indication** | CKD progression in T2D | Resistant hypertension, nephrotic edema | Post-MI HF, resistant HTN |
| **BP Lowering Effect** | Minimal (systolic drops ~2-3 mmHg) | Significant | Moderate |
| **Hyperkalemia Risk** | Moderate (shorter half-life, less kidney accumulation) | High | High |
| **Anti-Androgenic Effects** | None | High (Gynecomastia, impotence) | Minimal |
| **eGFR Cutoff for Initiation** | ≥25 mL/min/1.73 m² | Generally avoid if <30 mL/min/1.73 m² | Contraindicated if <50 (for HTN) or <30 (for HF) |
| **Cost** | High (Brand only) | Low (Generic) | Low (Generic) |

### Clinical Decision Tree: Choosing an MRA

**1. When to choose Kerendia (finerenone):**
*   Patient has T2D and CKD with residual albuminuria (UACR ≥30 mg/g) despite ACEi/ARB and SGLT2i.
*   Primary goal is slowing CKD progression and reducing CV risk.
*   Patient has a history of gynecomastia on spironolactone.
*   Patient has borderline potassium issues where spironolactone is deemed too high-risk.

**2. When to choose Aldactone (spironolactone):**
*   Patient has true resistant hypertension (BP uncontrolled on 3+ agents including a diuretic).
*   Patient requires significant diuresis/edema management (e.g., nephrotic syndrome, cirrhosis).
*   Cost is an absolute barrier to finerenone.

**3. Switching between agents:**
*   If switching from spironolactone to finerenone (e.g., due to gynecomastia or to target CKD progression), allow a washout period of at least **4 to 7 days** due to the long half-life of spironolactone's active metabolites to avoid compounding hyperkalemia risk. Recheck potassium before initiating finerenone.
*   Do NOT use finerenone and spironolactone/eplerenone concurrently.
