# Clinical Drug Monograph: Potassium Binders

**Class Overview:** 
Novel oral potassium binders are non-absorbed cation exchange polymers used to treat hyperkalemia. In nephrology, their primary utility is managing chronic hyperkalemia to enable the initiation, continuation, and up-titration of Renin-Angiotensin-Aldosterone System inhibitors (RAASi) and Mineralocorticoid Receptor Antagonists (MRAs) in patients with Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) and Heart Failure (HF). 

**Important Clinical Caveat:** Neither agent is indicated for the emergency treatment of life-threatening hyperkalemia due to delayed onset of action.

---

## Veltassa (patiromer)

### Mechanism of Action
Veltassa (patiromer) is a non-absorbed, cation exchange polymer that contains a calcium-sorbitol counterion. It increases fecal potassium excretion through the binding of potassium in the lumen of the gastrointestinal tract (primarily the distal colon), exchanging calcium for potassium. 

### Indications in Nephrology
*   **FDA-Approved Indication:** Treatment of hyperkalemia in adults.
*   **Off-Label/Clinical Practice Uses:** Facilitation of RAASi/MRA optimization in CKD and diabetic kidney disease (DKD) patients who would otherwise require discontinuation due to hyperkalemia.
*   **Key Supporting Trials:**
    *   **OPAL-HK (2015):** Demonstrated Veltassa (patiromer) significantly decreased serum K+ in CKD patients (eGFR 15-59) on RAASi therapy. 76% of patients maintained target K+ (3.8-5.0 mEq/L) and remained on RAASi.
    *   **AMETHYST-DN (2015):** Showed long-term (52-week) efficacy and safety in patients with diabetic nephropathy and hyperkalemia on RAASi.
    *   **TOURMALINE (2018):** Demonstrated Veltassa (patiromer) was equally effective whether taken with or without food.

### Dosing
*   **Standard Starting Dose:** 8.4 g orally once daily.
*   **Titration:** Adjust dose by 8.4 g daily at 1-week intervals based on serum K+ levels. 
*   **Maximum Dose:** 25.2 g orally once daily.
*   **CKD Dose Adjustments by eGFR Range:** (Because the drug is not systemically absorbed, renal impairment does not affect drug clearance).
    *   **eGFR ≥60 mL/min/1.73m²:** No adjustment (Start 8.4 g daily)
    *   **eGFR 30-59 mL/min/1.73m²:** No adjustment (Start 8.4 g daily)
    *   **eGFR 15-29 mL/min/1.73m²:** No adjustment (Start 8.4 g daily)
    *   **eGFR <15 / Dialysis:** No adjustment (Start 8.4 g daily). *Note: Use on dialysis days and non-dialysis days is generally safe, but monitor closely for hypokalemia.*
*   **Hepatic Adjustments:** None required.

### Monitoring
*   **Baseline Labs:** Serum potassium (K+), magnesium (Mg++), and calcium (Ca++).
*   **Follow-up Schedule:** 
    *   Check serum K+ 1 to 2 weeks after initiation or dose titration.
    *   [TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED]: Standard clinic workflow—order BMP 7-10 days post-initiation.
    *   **Target Lab Values:** Serum K+ 4.0 - 5.0 mEq/L.
*   **Therapeutic Drug Monitoring:** Not applicable (not systemically absorbed).

### Adverse Effects & Safety
*   **Common Side Effects (>5%):** Constipation (7.2%), hypomagnesemia (5.3%), diarrhea (4.8%), nausea (2.3%).
*   **Serious/Rare Side Effects:** Severe gastrointestinal inflammation, ischemia, or necrosis (rare, usually associated with severe constipation/impaction).
*   **Black Box Warnings:** None. (The original 2015 FDA black box warning regarding drug interactions was removed in 2016).
*   **Contraindications:** Known hypersensitivity to Veltassa (patiromer).
*   **CKD-Specific Safety Concerns:** 
    *   **Hypomagnesemia:** Veltassa (patiromer) binds magnesium. CKD patients are at risk for electrolyte shifts; monitor Mg++ and supplement if < 1.4 mg/dL.
    *   **GI Motility:** Avoid in patients with severe constipation, bowel obstruction, or impaction, as CKD patients often have uremic gastroparesis or are on constipating medications (e.g., phosphate binders).

### Drug Interactions
*   **Major Interactions:** Veltassa (patiromer) binds to many orally administered medications, reducing their absorption. 
*   **Spacing Rule:** Administer other oral medications at least **3 hours before or 3 hours after** Veltassa (patiromer).
*   **CYP450 Interactions:** None (not systemically absorbed).
*   **Food Interactions:** Can be taken with or without food.

### Special Populations
*   **Pregnancy:** Not absorbed systemically; not expected to result in fetal exposure.
*   **Elderly:** No specific dose adjustments. Monitor closely for constipation.
*   **Transplant:** Safe to use, but **CRITICAL** to adhere to the 3-hour spacing rule with immunosuppressants (tacrolimus, mycophenolate, cyclosporine) to avoid subtherapeutic anti-rejection drug levels.

### Patient Counseling Points
*   **Preparation:** Mix the powder with 1/3 cup (approx. 3 ounces) of water. Stir well and drink immediately. If powder remains, add more water, stir, and drink. Can also be mixed with applesauce or pudding. Do NOT heat or microwave.
*   **Spacing:** You MUST take this medication 3 hours apart from all other oral pills.
*   **Storage:** Store in the refrigerator (36°F to 46°F). If stored at room temperature, it must be used within 3 months.
*   **Warning Signs:** Report severe constipation, abdominal pain, or black/bloody stools immediately.

### Prior Authorization & Coding
*   **ICD-10 Codes:** E87.5 (Hyperkalemia), N18.3 - N18.6 (CKD stages), I50.9 (Heart failure, unspecified).
*   **PA Tips:** Document that the patient requires continuation of RAASi/MRA for cardio-renal protection but is limited by hyperkalemia. Document failure or intolerance of dietary K+ restriction.
*   **Cost/Assistance:** Veltassa RelyPS program available for copay assistance. [TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED]: Route to TKE pharmacy tech for PA submission.

## Lokelma (sodium zirconium cyclosilicate)

### Mechanism of Action
Lokelma (sodium zirconium cyclosilicate) is a non-absorbed, highly selective inorganic cation exchanger. It preferentially captures potassium in exchange for hydrogen and sodium throughout the entire gastrointestinal tract.

### Indications in Nephrology
*   **FDA-Approved Indication:** Treatment of hyperkalemia in adults.
*   **Off-Label/Clinical Practice Uses:** RAASi/MRA optimization in CKD/HF. Management of hyperkalemia in ESRD/Hemodialysis patients (often dosed on non-dialysis days).
*   **Key Supporting Trials:**
    *   **HARMONIZE (2014):** Demonstrated rapid K+ lowering (within 48 hours) in patients with K+ >5.1 mEq/L, maintaining normokalemia over 28 days.
    *   **DIALIZE (2019):** First trial specifically in hemodialysis patients. Showed Lokelma (sodium zirconium cyclosilicate) 5g daily on non-dialysis days significantly maintained pre-dialysis K+ between 4.0-5.0 mEq/L without requiring rescue therapy.

### Dosing
*   **Correction Phase (Initial):** 10 g orally three times daily (TID) for up to 48 hours.
*   **Maintenance Phase:** 10 g orally once daily. 
*   **Titration:** Adjust by 5 g daily at 1-week intervals. Dose can range from 5 g every other day up to a maximum of 15 g daily.
*   **Hemodialysis Dosing:** Start 5 g orally once daily on **non-dialysis days**. Titrate up to 15 g daily on non-dialysis days based on pre-dialysis K+ levels.
*   **CKD Dose Adjustments by eGFR Range:**
    *   **eGFR ≥60 mL/min/1.73m²:** No adjustment
    *   **eGFR 30-59 mL/min/1.73m²:** No adjustment
    *   **eGFR 15-29 mL/min/1.73m²:** No adjustment
    *   **eGFR <15 / Dialysis:** No adjustment (See Hemodialysis specific dosing above).
*   **Hepatic Adjustments:** None required.

### Monitoring
*   **Baseline Labs:** Serum K+.
*   **Follow-up Schedule:** 
    *   Check serum K+ 2-3 days after starting the correction phase.
    *   Check serum K+ 1-2 weeks after adjusting the maintenance dose.
    *   **Target Lab Values:** Serum K+ 4.0 - 5.0 mEq/L.
*   **Therapeutic Drug Monitoring:** Not applicable.

### Adverse Effects & Safety
*   **Common Side Effects (>5%):** Mild to moderate edema (5.7% to 16.1%, dose-dependent).
*   **Serious/Rare Side Effects:** Severe hypokalemia if over-titrated.
*   **Black Box Warnings:** None.
*   **Contraindications:** Known hypersensitivity.
*   **CKD-Specific Safety Concerns:** 
    *   **Sodium Load:** Each 5 g dose contains approximately 400 mg of sodium. This can exacerbate hypertension, fluid retention, and heart failure in CKD patients. Monitor weight, blood pressure, and edema closely. Diuretic adjustment may be necessary.
    *   **GI Motility:** Avoid in severe constipation, bowel obstruction, or post-operative ileus.

### Drug Interactions
*   **Major Interactions:** Lokelma (sodium zirconium cyclosilicate) transiently increases gastric pH. It can alter the absorption of drugs that exhibit pH-dependent solubility (e.g., azole antifungals, dabigatran, atazanavir).
*   **Spacing Rule:** Administer pH-dependent oral medications at least **2 hours before or 2 hours after** Lokelma (sodium zirconium cyclosilicate). It does *not* bind to other medications like Veltassa does.
*   **CYP450 Interactions:** None.
*   **Food Interactions:** Can be taken with or without food.

### Special Populations
*   **Pregnancy:** Not absorbed systemically; expected to be safe.
*   **Elderly:** Monitor closely for fluid overload/edema due to sodium content.
*   **Heart Failure:** Use with caution. The sodium load may precipitate HF exacerbations. Consider increasing loop diuretics if edema develops.

### Patient Counseling Points
*   **Preparation:** Empty the packet into a glass with approximately 3 tablespoons (1.5 ounces) of water. Stir well and drink immediately. The powder will not dissolve completely (it will look cloudy). Add a little more water to the glass, swirl, and drink to ensure the full dose is taken.
*   **Warning Signs:** Report sudden weight gain, swelling in the legs/ankles, or shortness of breath immediately (signs of fluid overload).
*   **Storage:** Store at room temperature. No refrigeration required.

### Prior Authorization & Coding
*   **ICD-10 Codes:** E87.5 (Hyperkalemia), N18.x (CKD stages).
*   **PA Tips:** Emphasize the need for RAASi continuation. If the patient has failed Veltassa, note the reason (e.g., inability to adhere to 3-hour spacing rule, GI intolerance).
*   **Cost/Assistance:** AstraZeneca offers the Lokelma Copay Savings Program.

## Class Comparison & Decision Trees

### Comparison Table

| Feature | Veltassa (patiromer) | Lokelma (sodium zirconium cyclosilicate) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Active Exchange Ion** | Calcium | Sodium and Hydrogen |
| **Onset of Action** | ~7 hours | ~1 hour |
| **Correction Dosing** | N/A (Start 8.4 g daily) | 10 g TID for up to 48 hours |
| **Maintenance Dosing** | 8.4 g to 25.2 g daily | 5 g QOD to 15 g daily |
| **eGFR Thresholds** | No adjustments needed | No adjustments needed |
| **Drug Spacing Rule** | **3 hours** for ALL oral meds | **2 hours** for pH-dependent meds only |
| **Key Adverse Effect** | Constipation, Hypomagnesemia | Edema, Fluid retention (due to Na+ load) |
| **Storage** | Refrigerator (Room temp for 3 mos) | Room temperature |
| **Formulation** | Powder (mix with water/food) | Powder (mix with water only) |

### Decision Tree: When to Choose Which Agent

**Choose Veltassa (patiromer) IF:**
1.  Patient has concurrent Heart Failure or severe edema/volume overload (avoids the sodium load of Lokelma).
2.  Patient has resistant hypertension sensitive to sodium intake.
3.  Patient has baseline hypocalcemia (Veltassa releases small amounts of calcium).

**Choose Lokelma (sodium zirconium cyclosilicate) IF:**
1.  Faster K+ lowering is desired (e.g., K+ is 5.8 - 6.0 mEq/L in clinic and you want to avoid ER send-out, though still not for true emergencies).
2.  Patient has a complex medication regimen making a blanket 3-hour spacing rule impossible to adhere to.
3.  Patient lacks reliable refrigeration.
4.  Patient has a history of severe constipation or baseline hypomagnesemia.

### Switching Between Agents
If a patient experiences adverse effects (e.g., severe edema on Lokelma, or severe constipation on Veltassa) or insurance mandates a switch:
1.  **Stop** the current agent.
2.  **Start** the alternative agent the following day at the standard starting maintenance dose (Veltassa 8.4 g daily OR Lokelma 5-10 g daily).
3.  **Monitor:** [TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED] Order a BMP 1 week after the switch to ensure K+ remains in the target range (4.0 - 5.0 mEq/L) and titrate the new agent as needed.
