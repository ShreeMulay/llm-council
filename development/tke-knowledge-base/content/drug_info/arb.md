## Cozaar (losartan)

### Mechanism of Action
Cozaar (losartan) is a selective, competitive angiotensin II receptor type 1 (AT1) antagonist. By blocking the binding of angiotensin II to AT1 receptors in vascular smooth muscle and the adrenal gland, it prevents vasoconstriction and aldosterone-secreting effects. In the kidney, it preferentially dilates the efferent arteriole, reducing intraglomerular pressure and subsequently reducing proteinuria. It also has a unique, mild uricosuric effect.

### Indications in Nephrology
*   **FDA-Approved:** 
    *   Treatment of diabetic nephropathy with an elevated serum creatinine and proteinuria (UACR ≥300 mg/g) in patients with type 2 diabetes and hypertension.
    *   Hypertension.
    *   Stroke risk reduction in patients with hypertension and left ventricular hypertrophy (LVH).
*   **Off-Label:** Proteinuric non-diabetic CKD, IgA nephropathy.
*   **Key Clinical Trials:**
    *   **RENAAL (2001):** Demonstrated a 16% risk reduction in the composite endpoint of doubling of serum creatinine, ESRD, or death in type 2 diabetics with nephropathy.
    *   **LIFE (2002):** Showed superiority over atenolol in reducing cardiovascular morbidity and mortality (specifically stroke) in patients with LVH.

### Dosing
*   **Standard Dosing:** Start 25–50 mg PO daily. Titrate every 2–4 weeks to a maximum of 100 mg PO daily (can be given as 50 mg BID for better 24-hour BP coverage).
*   **CKD Dose Adjustments by eGFR Range:**
    *   **eGFR ≥60:** 50 mg daily; titrate to 100 mg daily.
    *   **eGFR 30-59:** 50 mg daily; titrate to 100 mg daily.
    *   **eGFR 15-29:** Start 25 mg daily; titrate cautiously to 50-100 mg daily based on BP and potassium.
    *   **eGFR <15 / Dialysis:** Start 25 mg daily. Dialysis does not clear Cozaar (losartan). Administer post-dialysis if intradialytic hypotension is a concern.
*   **Hepatic Adjustments:** Start at 25 mg daily in mild-to-moderate hepatic impairment.

### Monitoring
*   **Baseline Labs:** BMP (Serum creatinine, eGFR, Potassium), UACR.
*   **Follow-Up Schedule:** Check BMP 2–4 weeks after initiation or dose titration. [TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED: Standardize lab orders to "TKE ARB Initiation Panel"].
*   **Lab Targets:** 
    *   BP <120 mm Hg systolic (per KDIGO 2021).
    *   UACR <30 mg/g (or >30% reduction from baseline).
    *   Potassium <5.0 mEq/L.

### Adverse Effects & Safety
*   **Common (>5%):** Dizziness, fatigue, hyperkalemia, hypotension.
*   **Serious/Rare:** Angioedema (lower risk than ACE inhibitors), acute kidney injury (AKI).
*   **Black Box Warning:** **Fetal Toxicity.** Discontinue immediately if pregnancy is detected.
*   **Contraindications:** Hypersensitivity, co-administration with aliskiren in patients with diabetes.
*   **CKD-Specific Safety Concerns:** 
    *   **Permissive SCr Bump:** Per KDIGO 2024, an asymptomatic increase in SCr up to 30% within 4 weeks of starting is hemodynamically expected and *not* a reason to stop therapy.
    *   **Hyperkalemia:** Risk increases significantly at eGFR <30.

### Drug Interactions
*   **Major Nephrology Interactions:** 
    *   **NSAIDs:** Increased risk of AKI and blunted antihypertensive effect.
    *   **Potassium-sparing diuretics / MRAs (e.g., Aldactone (spironolactone), Kerendia (finerenone)):** Additive hyperkalemia risk.
    *   **Lithium:** ARBs decrease lithium clearance, risking toxicity.
*   **CYP450:** Metabolized by CYP2C9 and CYP3A4; inhibitors (e.g., fluconazole) may decrease conversion to its active metabolite.

### Special Populations
*   **Pregnancy:** Category D equivalent (Contraindicated).
*   **Elderly:** Start at 25 mg daily due to increased risk of orthostatic hypotension.
*   **Transplant:** Safe, but monitor closely for hyperkalemia when used with calcineurin inhibitors (Prograf (tacrolimus), Gengraf (cyclosporine)).

### Patient Counseling Points
*   **Expectations:** "This medication protects your kidneys by lowering the pressure inside the kidney filters."
*   **Warning Signs:** Report dizziness upon standing, lip/tongue swelling, or severe diarrhea/vomiting.
*   **Sick Day Rules:** [TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED: Distribute TKE Sick Day Handout]. Hold this medication if you are experiencing severe vomiting, diarrhea, or inability to drink fluids to prevent acute kidney injury.
*   **Diet:** Avoid salt substitutes, as they are largely made of potassium chloride and can cause dangerous potassium spikes.

### Prior Authorization & Coding
*   **ICD-10 Codes:** I12.9 (Hypertensive CKD stage 1-4), I13.0 (Hypertensive heart and CKD), E11.21 (Type 2 DM with diabetic nephropathy).
*   **CPT Codes:** 99213/99214 for E&M management of CKD/HTN.
*   **Cost:** Generic losartan is Tier 1 on almost all formularies ($4-$10/month). PA is rarely required.

## Diovan (valsartan)

### Mechanism of Action
Diovan (valsartan) is a potent, highly selective AT1 receptor antagonist. It blocks the vasoconstrictor and aldosterone-secreting effects of angiotensin II. It has a slightly longer half-life than losartan (~6 hours vs 2 hours for losartan's active metabolite).

### Indications in Nephrology
*   **FDA-Approved:** 
    *   Hypertension.
    *   Heart Failure (HFrEF) to reduce cardiovascular mortality.
    *   Post-Myocardial Infarction (to reduce CV mortality in patients with LV dysfunction).
*   **Off-Label:** Proteinuric CKD (diabetic and non-diabetic).
*   **Key Clinical Trials:**
    *   **Val-HeFT (2001):** Demonstrated significant reduction in heart failure hospitalizations.
    *   **VALIANT (2003):** Proved valsartan was non-inferior to captopril in post-MI patients with LV dysfunction.
    *   **MARVAL (2002):** Showed valsartan reduced urinary albumin excretion in type 2 diabetics more effectively than amlodipine, despite similar BP reductions.

### Dosing
*   **Standard Dosing:** HTN: Start 80-160 mg PO daily; max 320 mg daily. Heart Failure: Start 40 mg PO BID; target 160 mg PO BID.
*   **CKD Dose Adjustments by eGFR Range:**
    *   **eGFR ≥60:** 80-160 mg daily.
    *   **eGFR 30-59:** 80 mg daily.
    *   **eGFR 15-29:** Start 40-80 mg daily; titrate cautiously.
    *   **eGFR <15 / Dialysis:** Start 40 mg daily. Not dialyzable.
*   **Hepatic Adjustments:** Maximum dose 80 mg daily in mild-to-moderate hepatic impairment.

### Monitoring
*   **Baseline Labs:** BMP, UACR.
*   **Follow-Up Schedule:** BMP 2–4 weeks post-initiation/titration.
*   **Lab Targets:** BP <120 mm Hg systolic, UACR <30 mg/g.

### Adverse Effects & Safety
*   **Common (>5%):** Viral infection, fatigue, abdominal pain, hyperkalemia.
*   **Serious/Rare:** Angioedema, AKI.
*   **Black Box Warning:** **Fetal Toxicity.**
*   **Contraindications:** Hypersensitivity, concurrent aliskiren in diabetics.
*   **CKD-Specific Safety Concerns:** High risk of hyperkalemia in advanced CKD, especially when dosed BID for heart failure.

### Drug Interactions
*   **Major Nephrology Interactions:** NSAIDs, Potassium-sparing diuretics, Lithium.
*   **Entresto (sacubitril/valsartan):** Do NOT combine Diovan (valsartan) with Entresto, as Entresto already contains valsartan.
*   **Food Interactions:** Food decreases absorption by 40%; however, it can be taken with or without food as long as it is consistent.

### Special Populations
*   **Pregnancy:** Category D equivalent (Contraindicated).
*   **Elderly:** No specific adjustment needed, but start at lower end of dosing range.
*   **Transplant:** Safe; monitor potassium.

### Patient Counseling Points
*   **Administration:** Take at the same time every day. If taking for heart failure, it must be taken twice daily.
*   **Warning Signs:** Report severe dizziness, especially when standing up quickly.
*   **Sick Day Rules:** Hold for severe volume depletion (vomiting/diarrhea).

### Prior Authorization & Coding
*   **ICD-10 Codes:** I11.0 (Hypertensive heart disease with heart failure), I50.20 (Unspecified systolic (congestive) heart failure), I12.9.
*   **Cost:** Generic valsartan is widely available and inexpensive.

## Avapro (irbesartan)

### Mechanism of Action
Avapro (irbesartan) is a non-competitive AT1 receptor antagonist with a high affinity for the receptor. It has excellent oral bioavailability (60-80%) and a long half-life (11-15 hours), allowing for true once-daily dosing and sustained 24-hour blood pressure control.

### Indications in Nephrology
*   **FDA-Approved:** 
    *   Hypertension.
    *   Diabetic nephropathy (treatment of diabetic nephropathy with an elevated serum creatinine and proteinuria in patients with type 2 diabetes and hypertension).
*   **Off-Label:** Non-diabetic proteinuric CKD.
*   **Key Clinical Trials:**
    *   **IDNT (2001):** Demonstrated a 20% risk reduction in the composite of doubling of SCr, ESRD, or death compared to amlodipine or placebo in type 2 diabetics.
    *   **IRMA-2 (2001):** Showed that irbesartan delayed the progression from microalbuminuria to macroalbuminuria in type 2 diabetics.

### Dosing
*   **Standard Dosing:** Start 150 mg PO daily; titrate to maximum 300 mg PO daily.
*   **CKD Dose Adjustments by eGFR Range:**
    *   **eGFR ≥60:** 150 mg daily; titrate to 300 mg daily.
    *   **eGFR 30-59:** 150 mg daily; titrate to 300 mg daily.
    *   **eGFR 15-29:** Start 75 mg daily (use half-tablet of 150mg); titrate to 150-300 mg based on tolerance.
    *   **eGFR <15 / Dialysis:** Start 75 mg daily. Not dialyzable.
*   **Hepatic Adjustments:** No dosage adjustment necessary.

### Monitoring
*   **Baseline Labs:** BMP, UACR.
*   **Follow-Up Schedule:** BMP 2–4 weeks post-initiation/titration.
*   **Lab Targets:** BP <120 mm Hg systolic, UACR <30 mg/g.

### Adverse Effects & Safety
*   **Common (>5%):** Hyperkalemia, orthostatic hypotension, dizziness.
*   **Serious/Rare:** Angioedema, AKI.
*   **Black Box Warning:** **Fetal Toxicity.**
*   **Contraindications:** Hypersensitivity, concurrent aliskiren in diabetics.
*   **CKD-Specific Safety Concerns:** Same as class (permissive 30% SCr bump, hyperkalemia).

### Drug Interactions
*   **Major Nephrology Interactions:** NSAIDs, Potassium-sparing diuretics, Lithium.
*   **CYP2C9:** Metabolized primarily by CYP2C9; inhibitors (e.g., amiodarone, fluconazole) may increase irbesartan levels.

### Special Populations
*   **Pregnancy:** Category D equivalent (Contraindicated).
*   **Elderly:** Start at 75 mg daily in patients ≥75 years old or those who are volume depleted.
*   **Transplant:** Safe; monitor potassium.

### Patient Counseling Points
*   **Expectations:** "This drug has strong evidence for protecting kidneys in patients with diabetes."
*   **Administration:** Can be taken with or without food.
*   **Sick Day Rules:** Hold for severe volume depletion.

### Prior Authorization & Coding
*   **ICD-10 Codes:** E11.21 (Type 2 DM with diabetic nephropathy), I12.9.
*   **Cost:** Generic irbesartan is Tier 1/Tier 2 on most formularies.

## Micardis (telmisartan)

### Mechanism of Action
Micardis (telmisartan) is a highly lipophilic AT1 receptor antagonist with the longest half-life in the ARB class (~24 hours). Uniquely, it also acts as a partial agonist of PPAR-gamma (similar to thiazolidinediones), which may improve insulin sensitivity and endothelial function, making it highly attractive for metabolic syndrome and diabetic CKD.

### Indications in Nephrology
*   **FDA-Approved:** 
    *   Hypertension.
    *   Cardiovascular risk reduction in patients unable to take ACE inhibitors.
*   **Off-Label:** Diabetic nephropathy, proteinuric CKD.
*   **Key Clinical Trials:**
    *   **ONTARGET (2008):** Proved telmisartan was non-inferior to ramipril for CV outcomes but with less cough/angioedema. (Also proved that combining ACEi + ARB causes harm: more AKI and hyperkalemia).
    *   **AMADEO (2008):** Showed telmisartan 80 mg provided significantly greater reduction in proteinuria compared to losartan 100 mg in diabetic nephropathy.
    *   **TRANSCEND (2008):** Showed CV benefits in patients intolerant to ACE inhibitors.

### Dosing
*   **Standard Dosing:** Start 40 mg PO daily; titrate to maximum 80 mg PO daily.
*   **CKD Dose Adjustments by eGFR Range:**
    *   **eGFR ≥60:** 40 mg daily; titrate to 80 mg daily.
    *   **eGFR 30-59:** 40 mg daily; titrate to 80 mg daily.
    *   **eGFR 15-29:** Start 20 mg daily; titrate cautiously.
    *   **eGFR <15 / Dialysis:** Start 20 mg daily. Not dialyzable.
*   **Hepatic Adjustments:** Start 20 mg daily in mild-to-moderate hepatic impairment. Highly cleared by the liver.

### Monitoring
*   **Baseline Labs:** BMP, UACR.
*   **Follow-Up Schedule:** BMP 2–4 weeks post-initiation/titration.
*   **Lab Targets:** BP <120 mm Hg systolic, UACR <30 mg/g.

### Adverse Effects & Safety
*   **Common (>5%):** Back pain, sinusitis, diarrhea, hyperkalemia.
*   **Serious/Rare:** Angioedema, AKI.
*   **Black Box Warning:** **Fetal Toxicity.**
*   **Contraindications:** Hypersensitivity, concurrent aliskiren in diabetics.
*   **CKD-Specific Safety Concerns:** Due to its long half-life, if hyperkalemia or AKI occurs, it may take longer to resolve after stopping the medication compared to losartan.

### Drug Interactions
*   **Major Nephrology Interactions:** NSAIDs, Potassium-sparing diuretics, Lithium.
*   **Digoxin:** Telmisartan can increase peak digoxin levels by up to 49%; monitor digoxin trough levels closely.

### Special Populations
*   **Pregnancy:** Category D equivalent (Contraindicated).
*   **Elderly:** No specific adjustment needed.
*   **Transplant:** Safe; monitor potassium.

### Patient Counseling Points
*   **Storage Warning:** **Must be kept in its original blister pack until administration.** The tablets are highly hygroscopic (absorb moisture from the air) and will degrade if placed in a standard pillbox.
*   **Expectations:** Provides excellent 24-hour blood pressure control, preventing morning BP spikes.

### Prior Authorization & Coding
*   **ICD-10 Codes:** I10 (Essential hypertension), I25.10 (ASHD), E11.21.
*   **Cost:** Generic telmisartan is widely available but may be slightly more expensive or placed on a higher tier than losartan/valsartan depending on the PBM.

## Class Comparison & Clinical Decision Making

### ARB Class Comparison Table

| Drug | Starting Dose | Max Dose | Half-Life | Key Nephrology/CV Trials | Unique Features |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cozaar (losartan)** | 25-50 mg daily | 100 mg daily | 2 hrs (metabolite 6-9 hrs) | RENAAL, LIFE | Mild uricosuric effect (good for gout); shortest half-life. |
| **Diovan (valsartan)** | 80 mg daily | 320 mg daily | 6 hrs | Val-HeFT, VALIANT, MARVAL | Strong HFrEF data; requires BID dosing for heart failure. |
| **Avapro (irbesartan)** | 150 mg daily | 300 mg daily | 11-15 hrs | IDNT, IRMA-2 | Excellent bioavailability; strong diabetic nephropathy data. |
| **Micardis (telmisartan)**| 40 mg daily | 80 mg daily | 24 hrs | ONTARGET, AMADEO | Longest half-life (true 24h BP control); PPAR-gamma agonist; keep in blister pack. |

### When to Choose One Agent Over Another
*   **For Gout/Hyperuricemia:** Choose **Cozaar (losartan)** due to its unique uricosuric properties.
*   **For Concurrent HFrEF:** Choose **Diovan (valsartan)** (or switch to Entresto per cardiology).
*   **For Poor Medication Adherence / Morning BP Spikes:** Choose **Micardis (telmisartan)** due to its 24-hour half-life.
*   **For Severe Diabetic Nephropathy:** **Avapro (irbesartan)** and **Cozaar (losartan)** have the most robust, FDA-approved historical trial data (IDNT, RENAAL), though **Micardis (telmisartan)** showed superior proteinuria reduction in AMADEO.
*   **For Pillbox Users:** Avoid **Micardis (telmisartan)** due to blister-pack requirements.

### Switching Between Agents
When switching due to formulary issues or side effects (other than angioedema/hyperkalemia), use the following approximate dose equivalents:
*   Losartan 50 mg ≈ Valsartan 80 mg ≈ Irbesartan 150 mg ≈ Telmisartan 40 mg.
*   *Note:* If a patient develops a cough on an ACE inhibitor, it is safe to switch to an ARB. If a patient develops angioedema on an ACE inhibitor, use ARBs with extreme caution (cross-reactivity is <10%, but risk remains).

### TKE Clinical Decision Tree: ARB Management in CKD
*(Based on KDIGO 2024 CKD and 2021 BP Guidelines)*

1.  **WHEN TO START:**
    *   Hypertension with CKD (Target BP <120 systolic).
    *   Normotensive patients with UACR >300 mg/g (strongly recommended) or >30 mg/g (suggested).
2.  **HOW TO ESCALATE:**
    *   Titrate dose every 2–4 weeks until maximum tolerated dose is reached, BP is <120 systolic, or UACR is <30 mg/g.
    *   *Maximally tolerated dose* is defined as the highest dose that does not cause symptomatic hypotension or unmanageable hyperkalemia.
3.  **WHEN TO HOLD / STOP:**
    *   **Do NOT stop** for an asymptomatic SCr increase of ≤30% from baseline.
    *   **Hold/Stop if:** 
        *   SCr increases >30% from baseline (evaluate for bilateral renal artery stenosis or volume depletion).
        *   Potassium >5.5 mEq/L *despite* dietary restriction, diuretic optimization, and use of potassium binders (e.g., Lokelma, Veltassa). [TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED: Refer to TKE Hyperkalemia Management Pathway].
        *   Symptomatic hypotension occurs.
        *   Patient becomes pregnant.
    *   *KDIGO 2024 Pearl:* Continue RAS inhibitors even as patients progress to Stage 4/5 CKD unless specific contraindications (refractory hyperkalemia) arise, as discontinuation is associated with higher cardiovascular mortality.
