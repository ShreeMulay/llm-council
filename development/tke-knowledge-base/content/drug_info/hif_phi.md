# HIF-PHI Class Clinical Monograph: Jesduvroq (daprodustat)

**Target Audience:** Nephrologists, Advanced Practice Providers (APPs), and Clinical Staff at The Kidney Experts (TKE).
**Reference Guidelines:** KDIGO 2012 Clinical Practice Guideline for Anemia in CKD (and subsequent controversies conferences), FDA Prescribing Information (2023).

---

## Jesduvroq (daprodustat)

### Mechanism of Action
Jesduvroq (daprodustat) is an oral hypoxia-inducible factor prolyl hydroxylase inhibitor (HIF-PHI). By reversibly inhibiting HIF-PH enzymes (PH1, PH2, and PH3), daprodustat stabilizes HIF-alpha subunits. This simulates a physiologic response to hypoxia, leading to the increased transcription of erythropoietin (EPO) and genes involved in iron metabolism (e.g., transferrin, transferrin receptors, divalent metal transporter 1), thereby increasing red blood cell production and improving iron utilization.

### Indications in Nephrology
*   **FDA-Approved Indication:** Treatment of anemia due to chronic kidney disease (CKD) in adults who have been receiving dialysis for at least 4 months.
*   **Off-Label Uses:** None recommended. 
*   **Important Limitation:** **NOT** approved for patients with CKD who are not on dialysis due to an increased risk of major adverse cardiovascular events (MACE) observed in this population.
*   **Key Supporting Clinical Trials:**
    *   **ASCEND-D (Dialysis):** Demonstrated non-inferiority to ESAs (epoetin alfa/darbepoetin alfa) for the primary efficacy endpoint of mean change in hemoglobin (Hb) and the primary safety endpoint of first occurrence of MACE.
    *   **ASCEND-ND (Non-Dialysis):** While non-inferior for Hb correction, the FDA determined the safety profile (MACE risk) was unacceptable for the non-dialysis population.

### Dosing
**General Administration:** Administer orally once daily, with or without food. **Must be swallowed whole** (do not cut, crush, or chew).

#### Starting Dose: ESA-Naïve Patients
*   Hb < 9.0 g/dL: 4 mg once daily
*   Hb 9.0 to 10.0 g/dL: 2 mg once daily
*   Hb > 10.0 g/dL: 1 mg once daily

#### Starting Dose: Switching from an ESA
*Based on the patient's total weekly ESA dose at the time of switch:*
*   **Epoetin alfa < 8,000 units/week** OR **Darbepoetin alfa < 40 mcg/week** OR **Mircera < 30 mcg/week:** Start Jesduvroq (daprodustat) **4 mg** once daily.
*   **Epoetin alfa 8,000 - 16,000 units/week** OR **Darbepoetin alfa 40 - 80 mcg/week** OR **Mircera 30 - 75 mcg/week:** Start Jesduvroq (daprodustat) **6 mg** once daily.
*   **Epoetin alfa > 16,000 units/week** OR **Darbepoetin alfa > 80 mcg/week** OR **Mircera > 75 mcg/week:** Start Jesduvroq (daprodustat) **8 mg** once daily.

#### Titration Schedule & Decision Tree
*   **Target Hb:** 10.0 to 11.0 g/dL. Do not target Hb > 11.0 g/dL.
*   **Frequency:** Do not increase the dose more frequently than once every 4 weeks. Decreases can occur at any time.
*   **Dose Steps:** 1 mg, 2 mg, 4 mg, 6 mg, 8 mg, 12 mg, 16 mg, 24 mg (Maximum dose: 24 mg/day).
*   **Escalate:** If Hb rises < 1.0 g/dL after 4 weeks, increase by 1 dose step.
*   **Hold/Reduce:** If Hb rises > 1.0 g/dL in 2 weeks, or if Hb > 11.0 g/dL, reduce dose by 1 step or hold therapy until Hb falls to target, then resume at 1 dose step lower.
*   **Stop:** If target Hb is not achieved after 24 weeks of dose escalation, discontinue Jesduvroq (daprodustat) and switch to an alternative anemia therapy.

#### CKD Dose Adjustments by eGFR Range
*   **eGFR ≥60:** Contraindicated / Not FDA Approved.
*   **eGFR 30-59:** Contraindicated / Not FDA Approved.
*   **eGFR 15-29:** Contraindicated / Not FDA Approved.
*   **eGFR <15 / Dialysis:** Approved **ONLY** for patients on dialysis for ≥4 months. Follow standard dosing above.

#### Hepatic Adjustments
*   **Mild (Child-Pugh A):** No adjustment.
*   **Moderate (Child-Pugh B):** Reduce starting dose by 50% (e.g., if standard starting dose is 4 mg, start at 2 mg).
*   **Severe (Child-Pugh C):** Not recommended.

### Monitoring
*   **Baseline Labs:** 
    *   Hemoglobin (Hb)
    *   Iron panel (Target TSAT > 20%, Ferritin > 100 ng/mL before starting)
    *   Liver Function Tests (ALT, AST, Alk Phos, Total Bilirubin)
*   **Follow-up Schedule:**
    *   **Hb:** Every 2 weeks following initiation or dose adjustment until stable, then monthly.
    *   **Iron Panel:** Monthly for the first 3 months, then quarterly. [TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED: Confirm local TKE standing orders for iron monitoring frequency in dialysis units].
    *   **LFTs:** Periodically as clinically indicated.
*   **Therapeutic Drug Monitoring:** Not applicable.

### Adverse Effects & Safety
*   **Common (>5%):** Hypertension, thrombotic vascular events, abdominal pain, dizziness, allergic reactions.
*   **Serious/Rare:** Hepatotoxicity, gastrointestinal hemorrhage.
*   **BLACK BOX WARNINGS:**
    *   **Increased risk of death, myocardial infarction, stroke, venous thromboembolism (VTE), and thrombosis of vascular access.** 
    *   Targeting Hb > 11.0 g/dL increases the risk of MACE.
*   **Contraindications:**
    *   Concomitant use of strong CYP2C8 inhibitors (e.g., gemfibrozil).
    *   Uncontrolled hypertension.
*   **CKD-Specific Safety Concerns:**
    *   **Vascular Access Thrombosis:** Monitor AV fistulas/grafts closely for thrills/bruits.
    *   **Dialysis Modality:** Approved for both hemodialysis (HD) and peritoneal dialysis (PD), provided the patient has been on dialysis >4 months.

### Drug Interactions
*   **CYP2C8 Inhibitors (Strong):** Gemfibrozil. **Contraindicated.** Increases daprodustat AUC by ~4-fold.
*   **CYP2C8 Inhibitors (Moderate):** Clopidogrel. Reduces starting dose of Jesduvroq (daprodustat) by half. Monitor Hb closely.
*   **CYP2C8 Inducers:** Rifampin. May decrease daprodustat efficacy. Monitor Hb and titrate up as needed.
*   **Phosphate Binders & Multivalent Cations:** Iron supplements, calcium-based binders (calcium acetate, calcium carbonate), sevelamer, lanthanum, magnesium. These bind to the drug in the gut and reduce absorption.
    *   *Rule:* Administer Jesduvroq (daprodustat) at least **1 hour before** or **4 hours after** phosphate binders or oral iron.

### Special Populations
*   **Pregnancy:** Category not formally assigned, but animal data shows fetal harm (embryo-fetal toxicity/mortality). Advise females of reproductive potential to use effective contraception.
*   **Lactation:** Discontinue breastfeeding during treatment and for 1 week after the final dose.
*   **Elderly:** No specific dose adjustments required based on age, but monitor closely for MACE and thrombotic events.
*   **Transplant:** Not studied in kidney transplant recipients. Avoid use.

### Patient Counseling Points
*   **Administration:** Take exactly as prescribed, once daily. You can take it with or without food. Swallow the tablet whole.
*   **Binder Timing:** *Critical instruction for dialysis patients:* Take this medication at least 1 hour before or 4 hours after your phosphate binders (e.g., Renvela, PhosLo) or oral iron pills.
*   **Warning Signs:** Seek immediate emergency care if you experience chest pain, shortness of breath, sudden numbness/weakness (signs of stroke), or pain/swelling in your legs.
*   **Access Care:** Check your dialysis access (fistula or graft) daily for your "buzz" or "thrill." Call the clinic immediately if it stops.
*   **Blood Pressure:** This medication can increase your blood pressure. Monitor your BP at home and take your BP medications as prescribed.

### Prior Authorization & Coding
*   **ICD-10 Codes:** 
    *   `D63.1` (Anemia in chronic kidney disease)
    *   `N18.6` (End-stage renal disease)
    *   `Z99.2` (Dependence on renal dialysis)
*   **PA Tips:** Ensure documentation clearly states the patient has been on dialysis for >4 months. Document baseline Hb, iron indices (showing iron replete status), and rationale for using HIF-PHI over standard ESA (e.g., patient preference for oral therapy, ESA hyporesponsiveness).
*   **Cost & Assistance:** High cost (specialty tier). GSK offers the "GSK For You" patient assistance program for eligible uninsured/underinsured patients. [TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED: Route all Jesduvroq PAs through the central TKE pharmacy technician/biologics coordinator].

## Class Comparison: HIF-PHIs vs. Standard of Care

*Note: While multiple HIF-PHIs have been developed (roxadustat, vadadustat), **Jesduvroq (daprodustat)** is currently the ONLY agent in this class FDA-approved in the United States. The table below compares the HIF-PHI class to standard ESAs to guide clinical decision-making.*

| Feature | Jesduvroq (daprodustat) | Roxadustat / Vadadustat | Standard ESAs (Epogen, Retacrit, Aranesp, Mircera) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Class** | HIF-PHI (Oral) | HIF-PHI (Oral) | Erythropoiesis-Stimulating Agents (Injectable) |
| **FDA Status** | **Approved** (Dialysis >4 mos only) | **Not FDA Approved** in the US (Rejected due to MACE risk) | **Approved** (Dialysis and Non-Dialysis CKD) |
| **Route** | Oral tablet (Daily) | Oral tablet | Subcutaneous or Intravenous (Weekly to Monthly) |
| **eGFR Thresholds** | Dialysis only (<15) | N/A in US | All eGFR ranges (ND-CKD and Dialysis) |
| **Key Trials** | ASCEND-D, ASCEND-ND | OLYMPUS, ROCKIES, INNO2VATE | CREATE, CHOIR, TREAT |
| **Iron Utilization** | Improves endogenous iron transport; may require less IV iron | Similar to daprodustat | Requires aggressive IV/oral iron supplementation |
| **Phosphate Binder Interaction** | **Yes** (Separate by 1hr before / 4hr after) | Yes | **No** |
| **Cost/Access** | High / Specialty Pharmacy | N/A | Moderate to High / Often bundled in dialysis center Medicare PPS (bundle) |

### When to Choose a HIF-PHI over an ESA
Consider Jesduvroq (daprodustat) for patients who:
1.  Are on **Peritoneal Dialysis (PD)** or **Home Hemodialysis (HHD)** where avoiding frequent subcutaneous injections or clinic visits for IV ESAs is highly desirable.
2.  Have demonstrated **ESA hyporesponsiveness** (requiring massive doses of ESAs to maintain Hb), as HIF-PHIs may overcome inflammatory blocks to erythropoiesis.
3.  Have severe needle phobia.

### When to AVOID a HIF-PHI
1.  **Non-dialysis CKD patients** (Absolute contraindication).
2.  Patients in their first 4 months of dialysis.
3.  Patients with a recent history of MACE, stroke, or severe unprovoked VTE.
4.  Patients with severe hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh C).
5.  Patients who are highly non-adherent to complex oral medication regimens (due to the strict separation required from phosphate binders).

### Switching Between Agents
*   **From ESA to HIF-PHI:** Follow the dose conversion table in the Dosing section above. Stop the ESA and start Jesduvroq the day the next ESA dose would have been due.
*   **From HIF-PHI to ESA:** If Jesduvroq is discontinued due to lack of efficacy or adverse events, wait at least 2 days after the last Jesduvroq dose before initiating an ESA. Start the ESA at the standard weight-based starting dose per the ESA package insert.
