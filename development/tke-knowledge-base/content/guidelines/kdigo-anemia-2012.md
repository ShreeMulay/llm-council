# KDIGO Clinical Practice Guideline for Anemia in CKD (2012 with 2024 Updates)

## Overview
The KDIGO Clinical Practice Guideline for Anemia in Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) provides a comprehensive framework for the diagnosis, evaluation, and management of anemia in both non-dialysis-dependent (ND-CKD) and dialysis-dependent (DD-CKD) patients. Anemia is a ubiquitous complication of advanced CKD, driven primarily by decreased renal erythropoietin production and altered iron homeostasis (hepcidin excess). 

Proper management of CKD-associated anemia is critical in nephrology practice because it directly impacts patient quality of life, cardiovascular morbidity, and transfusion requirements. Historically, normalizing hemoglobin (Hb) was the goal; however, landmark trials demonstrated increased cardiovascular and thrombotic risks with high-target Erythropoiesis-Stimulating Agent (ESA) therapy. 

**Target Population:** Adult and pediatric patients with CKD stages 3-5, including those on hemodialysis (HD) and peritoneal dialysis (PD), as well as kidney transplant recipients.
**Intended Audience:** Nephrologists, Advanced Practice Providers (APPs), dialysis unit staff, and CKD clinic nurses.
**Guideline Evolution:** This reference integrates the foundational 2012 KDIGO guidelines with the 2024 KDIGO updates, which notably introduce Hypoxia-Inducible Factor Prolyl Hydroxylase Inhibitors (HIF-PHIs) into the treatment armamentarium and refine iron management strategies.

## Key Recommendations

### Diagnosis and Evaluation
*   **Diagnostic Thresholds:** Diagnose anemia when Hb is **< 13.0 g/dL in adult males** and **< 12.0 g/dL in adult females** (1A).
*   **Testing Frequency:** 
    *   CKD 3: Annually
    *   CKD 4-5 (ND): Twice annually
    *   CKD 5HD/5PD: Monthly
*   **Initial Workup:** Prior to initiating ESAs or iron, evaluate for other causes of anemia. Required labs include: CBC with indices, absolute reticulocyte count, serum ferritin, serum transferrin saturation (TSAT), vitamin B12, and folate.

### Iron Therapy
Iron supplementation is the first-line therapy for CKD anemia and must be optimized before or alongside ESA/HIF-PHI therapy.
*   **Targets:** Administer iron if **TSAT is ≤ 30% AND Ferritin is ≤ 500 ng/mL** (2C).
*   **ND-CKD Route:** A 1-3 month trial of oral iron is acceptable, but IV iron is preferred if oral is poorly tolerated, ineffective, or if rapid repletion is needed.
*   **DD-CKD Route:** IV iron is strongly recommended over oral iron for hemodialysis patients (2C).
*   **Maintenance:** Once replete, maintenance iron is often required to offset ongoing losses (especially in HD).

**Common Iron Therapies & Dosing:**
| Drug (Brand/Generic) | Route | Typical Repletion Dose | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Venofer (iron sucrose)** | IV | ND-CKD: 200 mg IV x 5 doses<br>HD: 100 mg IV x 10 doses | Preferred in many infusion centers. |
| **Injectafer (ferric carboxymaltose)** | IV | 750 mg IV x 2 doses (separated by 7 days) | Risk of severe hypophosphatemia. |
| **Feraheme (ferumoxytol)** | IV | 510 mg IV x 2 doses (separated by 3-8 days) | Black Box Warning for fatal anaphylaxis. |
| **Auryxia (ferric citrate)** | PO | 2 tablets (420 mg iron) PO TID with meals | Also acts as a phosphate binder. |

### Erythropoiesis-Stimulating Agents (ESAs)
*   **Initiation Threshold:** Consider starting ESA when **Hb < 10.0 g/dL** (2A). For ND-CKD, individualize therapy if Hb is > 10.0 g/dL but patient has severe ischemic symptoms.
*   **Target Hb:** Maintain Hb between **10.0 and 11.5 g/dL**. **Do NOT target Hb > 13.0 g/dL** due to increased risk of stroke, thrombosis, and mortality (1A).
*   **Titration:** Adjust dose based on Hb trajectory. Do not increase dose more frequently than every 4 weeks. If Hb rises > 1.0 g/dL in 2 weeks, decrease dose by 25%.

**Common ESA Therapies & Dosing:**
| Drug (Brand/Generic) | Route | Starting Dose | Titration |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Epogen / Retacrit (epoetin alfa)** | IV/SQ | HD: 50-100 U/kg TIW<br>ND-CKD: 50-100 U/kg weekly | Adjust by 25% based on Hb response. |
| **Aranesp (darbepoetin alfa)** | IV/SQ | HD: 0.45 mcg/kg weekly<br>ND-CKD: 0.45 mcg/kg q4 weeks | Adjust by 25% based on Hb response. |
| **Mircera (methoxy PEG-epoetin beta)** | IV/SQ | 0.6 mcg/kg every 2 weeks | Once stable, can transition to monthly dosing. |

### Hypoxia-Inducible Factor Prolyl Hydroxylase Inhibitors (HIF-PHIs)
*   **Indication:** The 2024 update includes HIF-PHIs as an alternative to ESAs. In the US, **Jesduvroq (daprodustat)** is FDA-approved **ONLY for dialysis patients** who have been receiving dialysis for at least 4 months.
*   **Dosing:** Starting dose depends on whether the patient is ESA-naïve or converting from an ESA. Typical starting dose is 2 mg to 4 mg PO daily.
*   **Safety:** Black Box Warning for increased risk of death, myocardial infarction, stroke, venous thromboembolism, and thrombosis of vascular access.

### Red Blood Cell Transfusion
*   **Recommendation:** Avoid transfusions when possible to minimize alloimmunization (critical for transplant candidates) and iron overload (1B).
*   **Indications:** Reserve for acute hemorrhage, severe hemodynamic instability, or severe ESA resistance where Hb cannot be maintained above 7.0 g/dL.

## Practice Points

*   **The "Iron First" Rule:** Always replete iron before escalating ESA doses. An ESA will not work effectively if the bone marrow lacks the iron substrate to build hemoglobin. 
*   **ESA Hyporesponsiveness:** Defined as requiring > 300 U/kg/week of epoetin alfa (or equivalent) to maintain Hb. If encountered, evaluate for:
    *   Iron deficiency (most common)
    *   Infection / Inflammation (check CRP)
    *   Occult blood loss (GI bleed)
    *   Hyperparathyroidism (severe CKD-MBD)
    *   Aluminum toxicity or pure red cell aplasia (PRCA - rare).
*   **Infection and Iron:** Avoid administering IV iron during active systemic infections, as iron can promote bacterial growth and exacerbate inflammation.
*   **Patient Education Talking Points:**
    *   *Why aren't we aiming for a "normal" hemoglobin?* "Studies show that pushing your blood count to normal levels with medications actually increases your risk for strokes and heart attacks. We aim for a 'safe zone' that improves your energy without putting your heart at risk."
    *   *Oral Iron:* "Take oral iron on an empty stomach or with Vitamin C (like orange juice) to boost absorption. It will likely make your stools dark or black—this is normal. If it causes severe constipation, let us know."

## Evidence Summary

The conservative Hb targets in modern nephrology are driven by three landmark trials that overturned previous practices of normalizing hemoglobin:
*   **CHOIR (2006):** ND-CKD patients targeted to Hb 13.5 vs 11.3 g/dL. The high-target group had a significantly higher risk of the composite cardiovascular endpoint.
*   **CREATE (2006):** ND-CKD patients targeted to Hb 13.0-15.0 vs 10.5-11.5 g/dL. No improvement in cardiovascular events, and a trend toward faster progression to dialysis in the high-target group.
*   **TREAT (2009):** Diabetic ND-CKD patients targeted to Hb 13.0 vs placebo (rescue ESA if Hb < 9.0). The high-target group had a significantly increased risk of fatal or nonfatal stroke (HR 1.92).
*   **ASCEND-D (2021):** Evaluated Jesduvroq (daprodustat) vs ESA in dialysis patients. Showed non-inferiority for MACE (Major Adverse Cardiovascular Events). This led to FDA approval for the DD-CKD population.

## What's New / Changed

*   **Introduction of HIF-PHIs:** The 2024 KDIGO update formally integrates HIF-PHIs. Unlike ESAs which are injectable, HIF-PHIs are oral agents that stimulate endogenous erythropoietin production and downregulate hepcidin, potentially improving iron utilization.
*   **Biosimilars:** The widespread adoption of ESA biosimilars (e.g., Retacrit for Epogen) has changed formulary landscapes without altering clinical targets.
*   **Higher Ferritin Tolerance:** While the 2012 guideline set a soft ceiling of Ferritin 500 ng/mL, recent trials (e.g., PIVOTAL) suggest proactive, high-dose IV iron in HD patients (targeting ferritin up to 700 ng/mL) reduces ESA requirements and cardiovascular events without increasing infection risk. *Note: KDIGO 2024 updates are incorporating these findings.*

## Quick Reference

### Target & Threshold Summary
| Parameter | Threshold / Target | Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Hemoglobin (Diagnosis)** | < 13.0 (M), < 12.0 (F) | Initiate anemia workup (Iron, B12, Folate, Retic). |
| **TSAT** | ≤ 30% | Initiate Iron (IV preferred in HD, PO/IV in ND-CKD). |
| **Ferritin** | ≤ 500 ng/mL | Initiate Iron. (May target higher in HD per PIVOTAL). |
| **Hemoglobin (ESA Start)** | < 10.0 g/dL | Initiate ESA or HIF-PHI (if HD). |
| **Hemoglobin (Target)** | 10.0 - 11.5 g/dL | Titrate ESA/HIF-PHI to maintain in this range. |
| **Hemoglobin (Ceiling)** | > 11.5 g/dL | Decrease ESA dose by 25% or hold. |

### Decision Tree: Anemia Management
1. **Diagnose:** Hb < 12 (F) or < 13 (M).
2. **Evaluate:** Check Iron panel, B12, Folate, FOBT.
3. **Replete Iron:** If TSAT ≤ 30% or Ferritin ≤ 500, give iron. 
   * *Wait 4 weeks to assess response before starting ESA.*
4. **Initiate ESA/HIF-PHI:** If Hb remains < 10.0 g/dL despite iron repletion.
5. **Monitor & Titrate:** Check Hb monthly (HD) or q1-3 months (ND-CKD). Adjust dose by 25% to keep Hb 10.0-11.5.
