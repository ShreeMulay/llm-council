# Clinical Reference Guideline: Finerenone in Diabetic Kidney Disease (Based on FIDELIO-DKD & KDIGO/ADA Guidelines)

## Overview
This clinical reference outlines the standard of care for utilizing **Kerendia (finerenone)**, a non-steroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist (nsMRA), in patients with chronic kidney disease (CKD) associated with Type 2 Diabetes (T2D). 

Based on the landmark **FIDELIO-DKD trial (NEJM, 2020)** and subsequently codified in the **KDIGO 2022/2024 Clinical Practice Guidelines for Diabetes Management in CKD** and the **ADA Standards of Care 2024**, this protocol represents a paradigm shift in nephrology. Kerendia (finerenone) effectively reduces the risk of sustained eGFR decline, end-stage kidney disease (ESKD), cardiovascular (CV) death, non-fatal myocardial infarction, and hospitalization for heart failure.

**Target Population:** Adult patients with T2D and CKD (eGFR $\ge$ 25 mL/min/1.73 m² and UACR $\ge$ 30 mg/g) who are already on maximally tolerated RAS inhibition (ACEi or ARB).
**Intended Audience:** TKE Nephrologists, Advanced Practice Providers (APPs), and Clinical Pharmacists/Nurses.

## Key Recommendations

### 1. Patient Selection and Initiation
*   **Recommendation:** Initiate Kerendia (finerenone) in patients with T2D, eGFR $\ge$ 25 mL/min/1.73 m², normal serum potassium ($\le$ 5.0 mEq/L), and albuminuria (UACR $\ge$ 30 mg/g) despite maximum tolerated doses of an ACE inhibitor or ARB. *(KDIGO Grade 1A)*
*   **Target Values:** 
    *   Target UACR: < 30 mg/g (or $\ge$ 30% reduction from baseline)
    *   Target Serum Potassium: $\le$ 4.8 mEq/L for optimal up-titration.
*   **Prerequisites:** Patients should ideally already be on an SGLT2 inhibitor (e.g., Farxiga (dapagliflozin) or Jardiance (empagliflozin)) if eGFR $\ge$ 20, as SGLT2i use reduces the risk of hyperkalemia associated with nsMRAs.

### 2. Dosing and CKD-Specific Modifications
Dosing is strictly dictated by baseline eGFR and serum potassium levels.

| Baseline eGFR (mL/min/1.73 m²) | Starting Dose of Kerendia (finerenone) | Target Dose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **$\ge$ 60** | 20 mg PO once daily | 20 mg PO once daily |
| **25 to < 60** | 10 mg PO once daily | 20 mg PO once daily (if K+ permits) |
| **< 25** | Do not initiate | N/A |

### 3. Potassium Monitoring and Titration Schedule
*   **Baseline:** Check serum potassium and eGFR prior to initiation. Do **not** initiate if K+ > 5.0 mEq/L.
*   **Follow-up:** Check serum potassium 4 weeks after initiation or any dose adjustment.

**Titration Protocol (at 4-week check):**
| Current Serum K+ (mEq/L) | Action if on 10 mg daily | Action if on 20 mg daily |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **$\le$ 4.8** | **Escalate** to 20 mg daily | **Maintain** 20 mg daily |
| **4.9 to 5.5** | **Maintain** 10 mg daily | **Maintain** 20 mg daily |
| **> 5.5** | **Hold** therapy. Recheck K+ in 72 hrs. | **Hold** therapy. Recheck K+ in 72 hrs. |

*Note: Once K+ falls to $\le$ 5.0 mEq/L after being held, restart at 10 mg daily.*

## Practice Points

### Decision Tree: Start, Escalate, Switch, Stop
*   **START:** T2D + CKD (UACR $\ge$ 30) + eGFR $\ge$ 25 + K+ $\le$ 5.0 on max tolerated ACEi/ARB.
*   **ESCALATE:** Increase from 10 mg to 20 mg after 4 weeks if K+ remains $\le$ 4.8 mEq/L and eGFR is stable.
*   **SWITCH:** If a patient is on a steroidal MRA like Aldactone (spironolactone) or Inspra (eplerenone) for resistant hypertension, evaluate the primary clinical need. Kerendia (finerenone) has less BP-lowering effect but superior renal/CV protection in T2D. Do not use both simultaneously.
*   **STOP:** Discontinue if patient develops recurrent severe hyperkalemia (> 5.5 mEq/L) despite dietary restriction and potassium binders, or if the patient starts a strong CYP3A4 inhibitor.

### ⚠️ Safety Concerns & Contraindications
*   **Contraindications:** Concomitant use with strong CYP3A4 inhibitors (e.g., itraconazole, ketoconazole, clarithromycin, ritonavir) or patients with adrenal insufficiency.
*   **Drug Interactions:** Moderate CYP3A4 inhibitors (e.g., erythromycin, verapamil, diltiazem, grapefruit juice) increase finerenone exposure. Monitor K+ more frequently.
*   **Hyperkalemia Management:** Before stopping Kerendia (finerenone) for mild hyperkalemia (5.1 - 5.4 mEq/L), consider initiating a potassium binder such as Veltassa (patiromer) or Lokelma (sodium zirconium cyclosilicate), or adding an SGLT2 inhibitor or loop diuretic if clinically indicated.

### Patient Education Talking Points
*   **Purpose:** *"This medication protects your kidneys from diabetes and reduces your risk of needing dialysis or having a heart attack."*
*   **Diet:** *"Avoid grapefruit and grapefruit juice, as they can cause this drug to build up in your body. Do not use salt substitutes containing potassium."*
*   **Labs:** *"We must check your blood work exactly 4 weeks after you start this medication to ensure your potassium levels are safe."*

## Evidence Summary

### The FIDELIO-DKD Trial (2020)
*   **Population:** 5,734 patients with T2D and CKD (UACR 30-300 with eGFR 25-60, or UACR 300-5000 with eGFR 25-75). All on max tolerated RASi.
*   **Primary Endpoint:** Composite of kidney failure, sustained $\ge$ 40% decrease in eGFR, or death from renal causes.
*   **Key Result:** Kerendia (finerenone) significantly reduced the primary endpoint compared to placebo (HR 0.82; 95% CI, 0.73 to 0.93; P=0.001). 
*   **Secondary Endpoint (CV):** Reduced risk of CV death, nonfatal MI, nonfatal stroke, or hospitalization for heart failure (HR 0.86; P=0.03).
*   **NNT / NNH:** Number Needed to Treat (NNT) to prevent one primary kidney event at 3 years = **29**. Number Needed to Harm (NNH) for hyperkalemia leading to discontinuation = **43**.
*   **Limitations:** Trial excluded patients with non-diabetic CKD (though the subsequent FIND-CKD trial is currently investigating this).

## What's New / Changed
*   **Shift from Steroidal to Non-Steroidal MRAs:** Unlike Aldactone (spironolactone), Kerendia (finerenone) has a non-steroidal structure, leading to equal distribution between heart and kidney tissue, significantly lower rates of hyperkalemia, and no anti-androgenic side effects (gynecomastia).
*   **The "Four Pillars" of Diabetic CKD:** Guidelines now recognize four foundational therapies for T2D CKD: 
    1. RASi (ACEi/ARB)
    2. SGLT2i (e.g., Farxiga (dapagliflozin))
    3. nsMRA (Kerendia (finerenone))
    4. GLP-1 RA (e.g., Ozempic (semaglutide))

### One-Page Summary: Kerendia (finerenone) in T2D CKD
*   **Who gets it:** T2D patients with eGFR $\ge$ 25 and UACR $\ge$ 30 already on an ACEi/ARB.
*   **Starting Dose:** 20 mg daily if eGFR $\ge$ 60; 10 mg daily if eGFR 25-59.
*   **Mandatory Check:** Must check Potassium at baseline and exactly 4 weeks after starting or changing dose.
*   **Titration:** Goal is 20 mg daily. Increase from 10 mg to 20 mg at 4 weeks *only if* K+ is $\le$ 4.8 mEq/L.
*   **Safety:** Hold for K+ > 5.5 mEq/L. Contraindicated with strong CYP3A4 inhibitors (e.g., clarithromycin). Warn patients against grapefruit juice and salt substitutes.
*   **Synergy:** Highly recommended to use concurrently with an SGLT2i (e.g., Jardiance (empagliflozin)) to maximize renal protection and minimize hyperkalemia risk.
