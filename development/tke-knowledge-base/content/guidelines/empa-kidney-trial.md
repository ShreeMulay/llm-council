# Clinical Reference Guideline: SGLT2 Inhibitor Use in CKD (Based on EMPA-KIDNEY & KDIGO 2024)

*Disclaimer: This document is a clinical reference for use by The Kidney Experts (TKE) providers and staff. It is not intended to replace clinical judgment or provide patient-specific medical advice.*

## Overview

**What this guideline covers and why it matters for nephrology practice**
The EMPA-KIDNEY trial (NEJM, 2023) represents a paradigm shift in nephrology, expanding the proven renoprotective and cardioprotective benefits of SGLT2 inhibitors to a much broader population of patients with Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD). Prior to this, SGLT2 inhibitors were primarily indicated for diabetic kidney disease or CKD with significant albuminuria. EMPA-KIDNEY demonstrated that Jardiance (empagliflozin) significantly reduces the risk of kidney disease progression or cardiovascular death in patients with CKD, **including those without diabetes and those with low levels of albuminuria**. 

This evidence has been formally integrated into the **KDIGO 2024 Clinical Practice Guideline for the Evaluation and Management of CKD** and the **ADA 2024 Standards of Care**, making SGLT2 inhibitors foundational, first-line therapy for CKD progression delay.

**Target population and intended audience**
*   **Target Population:** Adult patients with CKD (eGFR 20 to <90 mL/min/1.73 m²), with or without Type 2 Diabetes (T2D), with or without albuminuria (depending on eGFR).
*   **Intended Audience:** TKE Nephrologists, Advanced Practice Providers (APPs), clinical pharmacists, and nursing staff.

**What changed from previous paradigms**
Historically, SGLT2 inhibitors were restricted to patients with T2D (CREDENCE trial) or those with high albuminuria (DAPA-CKD trial). EMPA-KIDNEY lowered the eGFR initiation threshold to 20 mL/min/1.73 m² and removed the albuminuria requirement for patients with eGFR <45, vastly increasing the number of eligible patients in a standard nephrology practice.

## Key Recommendations

### Initiation and Patient Selection
Based on EMPA-KIDNEY inclusion criteria and KDIGO 2024 guidelines (Grade 1A/2B evidence):

*   **Initiate Jardiance (empagliflozin) or Farxiga (dapagliflozin) in patients with:**
    *   eGFR ≥ 20 mL/min/1.73 m² AND Urine Albumin-to-Creatinine Ratio (UACR) ≥ 200 mg/g (KDIGO Grade 1A).
    *   eGFR 20 to 45 mL/min/1.73 m² regardless of UACR (KDIGO Grade 2B).
*   **Target Values:** 
    *   Primary goal: Delay progression to ESKD.
    *   Secondary goal: Target UACR < 30 mg/g (or > 30% reduction from baseline).
*   **Contraindications (DO NOT START):**
    *   Type 1 Diabetes (T1D) due to high risk of diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA).
    *   Polycystic Kidney Disease (PKD) or patients requiring immunosuppression for immune-mediated kidney disease (excluded from trials; use requires expert individualized judgment).
    *   Prior history of DKA.
    *   Kidney transplant recipients [TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED for off-label use in transplant].

### Dosing and Titration
*   **Drug:** Jardiance (empagliflozin)
*   **Dose:** 10 mg PO once daily.
*   **Titration:** None required. The 10 mg dose provides maximal renal benefit. (Note: 25 mg dose exists for glycemic control but offers no additional renal benefit).
*   **Alternative:** Farxiga (dapagliflozin) 10 mg PO once daily (based on DAPA-CKD).
*   **CKD-Stage Modifications:** 
    *   **Initiation:** Can be started down to an eGFR of 20 mL/min/1.73 m².
    *   **Continuation:** Once started, **continue therapy until the initiation of dialysis or kidney transplantation**, even if eGFR falls below 20 mL/min/1.73 m² (KDIGO Grade 1A).

### Monitoring Parameters
*   **Baseline Labs:** BMP (eGFR, Potassium), UACR, HbA1c (if diabetic).
*   **Follow-up Labs:** Repeat BMP in 2-4 weeks after initiation.
*   **Expected Hemodynamic Effect:** Anticipate an acute, reversible "dip" in eGFR of 10-30% within the first 2-4 weeks. **Do not discontinue therapy** unless the eGFR drop exceeds 30% or is accompanied by signs of acute volume depletion.

## Practice Points

### Practical Implementation Tips for TKE Clinics
*   **The "eGFR Dip":** This is the most common reason for inappropriate discontinuation by primary care or other specialists. Educate the patient *before* prescribing that their kidney numbers will look slightly worse initially, but this indicates the drug is working to reduce intraglomerular pressure.
*   **Glycemic Impact:** In patients without diabetes, or those with eGFR <45, the glucose-lowering effect of Jardiance (empagliflozin) is negligible. Hypoglycemia is exceedingly rare unless combined with insulin or sulfonylureas.
*   **Diuretic Synergy:** SGLT2 inhibitors have mild diuretic properties. In patients on high-dose loop diuretics (e.g., Lasix (furosemide)), consider a 20-25% reduction in the loop diuretic dose upon initiation to prevent hypovolemia, especially in elderly patients.

### Decision Tree: When to Hold or Stop
*   **HOLD (Sick Day Rules):** Instruct patients to hold Jardiance (empagliflozin) during acute illness (vomiting, diarrhea, severe infection), prolonged fasting (NPO for surgery), or bowel prep for colonoscopy. Restart when eating and drinking normally.
*   **STOP:** Discontinue permanently if the patient develops DKA, Fournier's gangrene, or requires dialysis/transplant.

### Patient Education Talking Points
*   **Hygiene:** "This medication works by removing sugar through your urine. Because sugar feeds bacteria and yeast, you must maintain excellent genital hygiene to prevent yeast infections or UTIs. Drink plenty of water."
*   **Sick Days:** "If you are sick to your stomach, vomiting, or cannot eat/drink, stop taking this pill until you are better."
*   **Purpose:** "Even though this was originally a diabetes drug, we are using it as a kidney-protection drug. It acts like a blood pressure pill specifically for the filters in your kidneys."

### Relevant Billing Codes

## Evidence Summary

### EMPA-KIDNEY Trial (2023)
*   **Population:** 6,609 patients with CKD. eGFR 20 to <45 (any UACR) or eGFR 45 to <90 (UACR ≥ 200). 54% did NOT have diabetes.
*   **Intervention:** Jardiance (empagliflozin) 10 mg daily vs. Placebo.
*   **Primary Endpoint:** Composite of progression of kidney disease (ESKD, sustained eGFR <10, sustained ≥40% decline in eGFR, or renal death) or CV death.
*   **Key Result:** 28% relative risk reduction in the primary endpoint (HR 0.72; 95% CI, 0.64 to 0.82; P<0.001). 
*   **NNT:** Number Needed to Treat to prevent one primary outcome event over 2 years was approximately 26.
*   **Limitations:** The trial was stopped early due to overwhelming efficacy, which may slightly exaggerate the effect size. The event rate in the non-albuminuric subgroup was low, meaning the absolute benefit in this specific cohort takes longer to manifest.

## What's New / Changed

*   **Expansion to Non-Diabetic CKD:** Prior to DAPA-CKD and EMPA-KIDNEY, SGLT2 inhibitors were strictly for diabetic nephropathy. They are now standard of care for non-diabetic CKD (excluding PKD/immune-mediated).
*   **Lower eGFR Thresholds:** The initiation threshold has been lowered from eGFR 30 (CREDENCE) to eGFR 25 (DAPA-CKD) and now to **eGFR 20** (EMPA-KIDNEY).
*   **Inclusion of Low Albuminuria:** EMPA-KIDNEY is the first major trial to prove benefit in patients with advanced CKD (eGFR <45) *without* significant albuminuria (UACR <200).

## How TKE Applies This Guideline

**Integration with Other Guidelines**
TKE utilizes the EMPA-KIDNEY data in conjunction with the **KDIGO 2024 CKD Guidelines** and **ADA 2024 Standards of Care**. For diabetic patients, Jardiance (empagliflozin) or Farxiga (dapagliflozin) should be used alongside maximum tolerated ACEi/ARB therapy and, if UACR remains ≥30 mg/g, combined with a non-steroidal MRA like Kerendia (finerenone).

### One-Page Summary: SGLT2i Initiation in CKD
1.  **Identify Eligible Patients:** Any CKD patient with eGFR ≥20. If eGFR is 45-90, they must have UACR ≥200. Diabetes is NOT required.
2.  **Rule Out Contraindications:** T1D, prior DKA, PKD, active immunosuppression.
3.  **Prescribe:** Jardiance (empagliflozin) 10 mg PO daily OR Farxiga (dapagliflozin) 10 mg PO daily.
4.  **Adjust Concomitant Meds:** Consider reducing loop diuretics (e.g., Lasix (furosemide)) by 20% if the patient is euvolemic/elderly. Consider reducing insulin/sulfonylureas if the patient is diabetic and tightly controlled (A1c < 7.0%).
5.  **Educate:** Warn about genital mycotic infections, importance of hydration, and "Sick Day Rules" (hold for vomiting/diarrhea/fasting).
6.  **Monitor:** Check BMP in 2-4 weeks. Expect a mild bump in creatinine (eGFR dip up to 30%). Reassure patient.
7.  **Maintain:** Do not stop the medication as CKD progresses. Continue until the patient starts dialysis or receives a kidney transplant.
