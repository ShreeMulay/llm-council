## Overview

### What this guideline covers and why it matters for nephrology practice
The 2022 AHA/ACC/HFSA Heart Failure (HF) Guideline provides a comprehensive framework for the diagnosis and management of HF. For nephrology practice at The Kidney Experts (TKE), this guideline is critical because **Cardiorenal Syndrome** is ubiquitous. Nephrologists frequently manage the renal consequences of HF and the renal limitations of Guideline-Directed Medical Therapy (GDMT). The guideline establishes the "Four Pillars" of GDMT, which heavily intersect with chronic kidney disease (CKD) management, particularly regarding estimated glomerular filtration rate (eGFR) fluctuations, hyperkalemia, and diuretic resistance.

### Target population and intended audience
*   **Target Population:** Adults with Heart Failure with Reduced Ejection Fraction (HFrEF; LVEF $\le$40%), Mildly Reduced Ejection Fraction (HFmrEF; LVEF 41-49%), Preserved Ejection Fraction (HFpEF; LVEF $\ge$50%), and Improved Ejection Fraction (HFimpEF).
*   **Intended Audience:** Cardiologists, Nephrologists, Primary Care Providers, APPs, and clinical pharmacists.

### What changed from the previous version
*   **SGLT2 inhibitors** are now firmly established as the 4th pillar of HFrEF GDMT (Class 1A).
*   **SGLT2 inhibitors** received a Class 2a recommendation for HFmrEF and HFpEF (the first therapy to show definitive benefit in HFpEF).
*   **ARNI** (sacubitril/valsartan) is now preferred over ACEi/ARB for HFrEF (Class 1A).
*   New classification: **HFimpEF** (Heart Failure with Improved Ejection Fraction) for patients whose LVEF was $\le$40% but has improved to >40%. These patients should *continue* HFrEF GDMT.

## Key Recommendations

### 1. The Four Pillars of GDMT for HFrEF (LVEF $\le$40%)
All patients with HFrEF should be on all four pillars unless contraindicated. Simultaneous or rapid sequential initiation is preferred over historical slow, stepwise addition.

#### A. ARNI / ACEi / ARB (Renin-Angiotensin System Inhibition)
*   **Recommendation:** Entresto (sacubitril/valsartan) is recommended to reduce morbidity and mortality [Class 1A]. If ARNI is not feasible, use an ACEi or ARB [Class 1A].
*   **Target Values:** Maximize to target doses; monitor for symptomatic hypotension, eGFR drop >30%, and K+ >5.0 mEq/L.
*   **CKD-Specific Modifications:**
    *   *Entresto (sacubitril/valsartan):* eGFR $\ge$30 mL/min: Start 49/51 mg BID. eGFR <30 mL/min: Start 24/26 mg BID.
    *   *Caution:* Stop ACEi 36 hours before starting ARNI to prevent angioedema.

#### B. Beta-Blockers
*   **Recommendation:** Use 1 of 3 evidence-based beta-blockers to reduce mortality [Class 1A].
*   **Drugs:** Toprol XL (metoprolol succinate), Coreg (carvedilol), Zebeta (bisoprolol).
*   **CKD-Specific Modifications:** No specific renal dose adjustments required, but monitor for bradycardia as uremia can alter drug clearance/sensitivity.

#### C. Mineralocorticoid Receptor Antagonists (MRAs)
*   **Recommendation:** Recommended to reduce morbidity and mortality [Class 1A].
*   **Drugs:** Aldactone (spironolactone), Inspra (eplerenone).
*   **CKD-Specific Modifications:**
    *   **Contraindicated** if eGFR <30 mL/min/1.73m² or serum K+ >5.0 mEq/L at initiation.
    *   *Monitoring:* Check K+ and SCr at 1 week, 4 weeks, and periodically thereafter.

#### D. SGLT2 Inhibitors
*   **Recommendation:** Recommended to reduce HF hospitalization and cardiovascular mortality, irrespective of diabetes status [Class 1A].
*   **Drugs:** Farxiga (dapagliflozin) 10 mg daily, Jardiance (empagliflozin) 10 mg daily.
*   **CKD-Specific Modifications:**
    *   *Farxiga (dapagliflozin):* Initiate if eGFR $\ge$25 mL/min.
    *   *Jardiance (empagliflozin):* Initiate if eGFR $\ge$20 mL/min.
    *   *Note:* Do not stop for acute, expected eGFR dips (<30%) upon initiation.

### 2. Management of HFmrEF (41-49%) and HFpEF ($\ge$50%)
*   **SGLT2 Inhibitors:** Farxiga (dapagliflozin) or Jardiance (empagliflozin) can be beneficial to decrease HF hospitalizations and CV mortality [Class 2a].
*   **MRAs, ARBs, ARNIs:** May be considered to decrease hospitalizations, particularly in patients with LVEF on the lower end of this spectrum [Class 2b].

### 3. Congestion and Diuretic Therapy
*   **Recommendation:** Loop diuretics are recommended to relieve congestion [Class 1C].
*   **Drugs:** Lasix (furosemide), Bumex (bumetanide), Demadex (torsemide).
*   **CKD-Specific Modifications:** Patients with advanced CKD require higher threshold doses of loop diuretics (e.g., Furosemide 80-160 mg/dose) due to decreased tubular secretion of the drug.

## Practice Points

### Decision Tree: Managing eGFR Dips with GDMT
When starting Entresto (sacubitril/valsartan) or SGLT2i (Farxiga/Jardiance), a hemodynamic drop in eGFR is expected.
1.  **eGFR drop < 30% from baseline:** Continue therapy. Recheck in 2-4 weeks.
2.  **eGFR drop > 30% from baseline:**
    *   Assess volume status (is the patient over-diuresed?).
    *   Hold nephrotoxins (NSAIDs).
    *   Reduce diuretic dose if euvolemic/hypovolemic.
    *   If still >30% drop, reduce dose or temporarily hold ARNI/SGLT2i.

### Decision Tree: Managing Hyperkalemia to Enable GDMT
Hyperkalemia often limits ARNI and MRA use in CKD patients.
1.  **K+ 5.1 - 5.5 mEq/L:** Educate on low K+ diet. Consider adding/optimizing SGLT2i or loop diuretic (which promote kaliuresis).
2.  **K+ > 5.5 mEq/L:** Consider initiation of potassium binders: Lokelma (sodium zirconium cyclosilicate) or Veltassa (patiromer) to allow continuation of life-saving MRA/ARNI therapy. [TKE PROTOCOL NEEDED]

### Diuretic Resistance in Advanced CKD
*   Switch from Lasix (furosemide) to Demadex (torsemide) or Bumex (bumetanide) for better oral bioavailability.
*   **Sequential Nephron Blockade:** Add a thiazide-like diuretic 30-60 minutes *before* the loop diuretic.
    *   Zaroxolyn (metolazone) 2.5-5 mg PO.
    *   Diuril (chlorothiazide) 250-500 mg IV.

## Evidence Summary

*   **DAPA-HF (2019) & EMPEROR-Reduced (2020):** Demonstrated that Farxiga (dapagliflozin) and Jardiance (empagliflozin) reduced CV death and HF hospitalizations in HFrEF by ~25%, regardless of diabetes status. NNT ~20 over 1.5 years.
*   **PARADIGM-HF (2014):** Showed Entresto (sacubitril/valsartan) was superior to enalapril in reducing CV death and HF hospitalizations in HFrEF.
*   **EMPEROR-Preserved (2021) & DELIVER (2022):** Proved Jardiance and Farxiga significantly reduce the combined endpoint of CV death or HF hospitalization in patients with HFpEF (LVEF >40%).
*   **Limitations:** Most major HF trials excluded patients with eGFR <20-30 mL/min. Therefore, GDMT in Stage 4/5 CKD and ESRD relies heavily on clinical judgment and extrapolations.

## What's New / Changed

*   **SGLT2i Expansion:** The most significant change is the elevation of SGLT2 inhibitors to Class 1A for HFrEF and Class 2a for HFpEF/HFmrEF.
*   **HFimpEF:** Recognition that patients whose EF recovers must stay on GDMT to prevent relapse. Do not stop Entresto or Coreg just because the EF is now 45%.
*   **Finerenone (Kerendia):** Mentioned for patients with T2D and CKD to reduce the risk of HF hospitalizations (based on FIDELIO-DKD and FIGARO-DKD).

*   **The "Four Pillars":** Explain that HF requires a team of 4 medications working together, like 4 tires on a car. Missing one makes the ride unstable.
*   **Kidney Numbers:** "Your kidney numbers (creatinine) might look a little worse when we start these drugs (Entresto, Farxiga). This is actually a sign the drug is working to take pressure off the kidney filters. We will monitor it closely."
*   **Weight Monitoring:** Instruct patients to weigh themselves daily. Call the clinic for a weight gain of >3 lbs in a day or >5 lbs in a week.

### One-Page Summary of Most Important Recommendations
1.  **Diagnose and Classify:** Determine LVEF (HFrEF $\le$40%, HFmrEF 41-49%, HFpEF $\ge$50%).
2.  **Initiate the 4 Pillars for HFrEF:** Do not wait to max out one drug before starting the next. Start low-dose Entresto, a Beta-blocker, an MRA, and an SGLT2i simultaneously or in rapid sequence.
3.  **Use SGLT2i for HFpEF:** Farxiga or Jardiance should be initiated in patients with preserved EF to prevent hospitalizations.
4.  **Manage the Kidneys to Save the Heart:** Tolerate mild-to-moderate eGFR drops (up to 30%) and use potassium binders to keep patients on life-saving MRAs and ARNIs.
5.  **Decongest Effectively:** Use loop diuretics to achieve euvolemia. If resistant, switch to torsemide/bumetanide or add a thiazide (metolazone) 30 mins prior to the loop diuretic.
