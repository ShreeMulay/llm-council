# KDIGO 2024 Clinical Practice Guideline for Lipid Management in CKD

**Organization:** KDIGO (Kidney Disease: Improving Global Outcomes)
**Publication Date:** 2024-01-01
**Relevant Domains:** statins_lipids, cardiovascular_risk_reduction, ckd_management

## Overview

### Scope and Importance for Nephrology
Cardiovascular disease (CVD) remains the leading cause of morbidity and mortality in patients with Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD). The KDIGO 2024 Lipid Management Guideline provides an updated framework for assessing atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease (ASCVD) risk and managing dyslipidemia across the spectrum of kidney disease. For nephrology practice, this guideline bridges the gap between traditional "fire-and-forget" statin strategies and modern, target-driven lipid lowering therapies (LLTs) utilized by cardiology.

### Target Population and Intended Audience
- **Population:** Adults and children with CKD (non-dialysis), patients on chronic dialysis (HD/PD), and kidney transplant recipients (KTRs).
- **Audience:** Nephrologists, Advanced Practice Providers (APPs), clinical pharmacists, and primary care physicians co-managing CKD patients.

### Paradigm Shift
This update marks a significant shift from the 2013 KDIGO guidelines by re-introducing specific LDL-C targets (aligning with ACC/AHA and ESC guidelines) and formally incorporating newer non-statin therapies, including PCSK9 inhibitors and ATP citrate lyase (ACL) inhibitors, for patients with advanced CKD.

## Key Recommendations

### 1. Primary Prevention in Non-Dialysis CKD (eGFR <60 mL/min/1.73m²)
- **Recommendation:** Initiate statin or statin/ezetimibe combination therapy in all adults aged ≥50 years with eGFR <60 mL/min/1.73m² without chronic dialysis. *(Grade 1A)*
- **Adults 18-49 years with CKD:** Initiate statin therapy if they have known coronary disease, diabetes mellitus, prior ischemic stroke, or an estimated 10-year ASCVD risk >10%. *(Grade 2B)*
- **Target:** Achieve a ≥50% reduction in LDL-C from baseline OR an absolute LDL-C <70 mg/dL.
- **First-line Agents:** 
  - Lipitor (atorvastatin) 20-40 mg daily
  - Crestor (rosuvastatin) 5-10 mg daily (Note CKD dosing limits)

### 2. Secondary Prevention (Known ASCVD + CKD)
- **Recommendation:** For patients with CKD and established ASCVD, utilize high-intensity statin therapy. If targets are not met, escalate to combination therapy. *(Grade 1A)*
- **Target:** LDL-C <55 mg/dL (aligning with extreme-risk cardiology guidelines).
- **Escalation Pathway:** 
  1. Maximize tolerated statin.
  2. Add Zetia (ezetimibe) 10 mg daily.
  3. Add PCSK9 inhibitor: Repatha (evolocumab) or Praluent (alirocumab).

### 3. Patients on Maintenance Dialysis (G5D)
- **Recommendation (Initiation):** Do NOT routinely initiate statin or statin/ezetimibe therapy in patients with dialysis-dependent CKD for *primary* prevention of cardiovascular events. *(Grade 1A)*
- **Recommendation (Continuation):** If a patient is already receiving statin or statin/ezetimibe therapy at the time of dialysis initiation, CONTINUE the therapy. *(Grade 2C)*
- **Secondary Prevention in G5D:** Initiation of LLT for *secondary* prevention (e.g., post-MI) should be individualized, weighing competing mortality risks.

### 4. Kidney Transplant Recipients (KTRs)
- **Recommendation:** Initiate statin therapy in all adult kidney transplant recipients. *(Grade 1B)*
- **Target:** LDL-C <70 mg/dL.
- **Caution:** High risk of drug-drug interactions (DDIs) between statins and calcineurin inhibitors (CNIs), particularly Sandimmune/Neoral (cyclosporine).

| **Praluent (alirocumab)** | 75-150 mg SQ q2 weeks | **No adjustment needed.** | Safe in severe CKD/dialysis. |
| **Nexletol (bempedoic acid)**| 180 mg daily | **No adjustment needed.** | **Warning:** Can increase serum uric acid; risk of gout. |

### ⚠️ Safety Concerns & Contraindications
- **Fibrates:** Lopid (gemfibrozil) is **CONTRAINDICATED** with statin therapy due to a severe risk of rhabdomyolysis. Tricor (fenofibrate) requires dose reduction in CKD and should be avoided if eGFR <30 mL/min/1.73m².
- **Transplant Interactions:** Sandimmune/Neoral (cyclosporine) significantly increases statin levels. If a patient is on cyclosporine, **avoid** Crestor (rosuvastatin) >5mg, Lipitor (atorvastatin) >10mg, and Zocor (simvastatin) entirely. Prograf (tacrolimus) has fewer statin interactions.

## Decision Tree: Lipid Management in CKD

**1. Assess Patient Status:**
- *Is the patient on dialysis?* 
  - YES: Are they already on a statin? 
    - YES: Continue. 
    - NO: Do not start for primary prevention. (Stop tree).
  - NO: Proceed to Step 2.

**2. Start Therapy (Primary or Secondary Prevention):**
- Initiate Lipitor (atorvastatin) 20 mg OR Crestor (rosuvastatin) 5-10 mg.
- Check baseline Lipid Panel, LFTs (AST/ALT).

**3. Monitor & Escalate (at 8-12 weeks):**
- *Is LDL-C at target (<70 mg/dL for primary, <55 mg/dL for secondary)?*
  - YES: Maintain therapy. Check lipids annually.
  - NO: Maximize statin dose (respecting eGFR limits).
  
**4. Switch / Add-On (Refractory or Intolerant):**
- *If LDL-C remains above target on max tolerated statin:* Add Zetia (ezetimibe) 10 mg.
- *If still above target:* Initiate PCSK9i (Repatha (evolocumab) or Praluent (alirocumab)).
- *If statin intolerant (myalgias):* Stop statin. Wait for symptom resolution. Re-challenge with alternative statin (e.g., Crestor to Lipitor) at a lower dose or every-other-day dosing. If still intolerant, use Zetia (ezetimibe) + Nexletol (bempedoic acid) or PCSK9i.

## Practice Points & Patient Education

### Clinical Implementation
- **Muscle Symptoms:** Statin-associated muscle symptoms (SAMS) are common but often not truly pharmacological. Check CPK *only* if the patient reports severe, unexplained muscle pain or weakness. Routine CPK monitoring is not recommended.
- **Proteinuria:** Statins do not significantly reduce proteinuria, nor do they slow the progression of CKD to ESKD. Their sole purpose is ASCVD risk reduction.
- **Timing:** While older statins (Zocor) must be taken at night, Lipitor (atorvastatin) and Crestor (rosuvastatin) have long half-lives and can be taken at any time of day, improving adherence.

### Patient Education Talking Points
- *"Kidney disease acts as an accelerator for heart disease. We are prescribing this medication to protect your heart and blood vessels, not necessarily to fix your kidneys."*
- *"If you experience severe, flu-like muscle aches or dark urine, stop the medication and call our clinic. However, mild aches are common and often resolve."*
- *"Do not stop this medication if you start dialysis without talking to us first."*

## Evidence Summary

- **SHARP Trial (2011):** Demonstrated that Zocor (simvastatin) + Zetia (ezetimibe) safely reduced major atherosclerotic events by 17% in patients with moderate-to-severe CKD compared to placebo.
- **4D and AURORA Trials:** Showed that initiating statins in patients already on hemodialysis did *not* significantly reduce cardiovascular mortality, forming the basis for the "do not initiate on dialysis" recommendation.
- **FOURIER & ODYSSEY OUTCOMES (CKD Subgroups):** Demonstrated that PCSK9 inhibitors (Repatha and Praluent) are safe and highly effective at lowering LDL-C and reducing ASCVD events in patients with eGFR down to 20 mL/min/1.73m².
- **CLEAR Outcomes (2023):** Showed Nexletol (bempedoic acid) reduced CV events in statin-intolerant patients, including a robust subgroup of patients with CKD, though uric acid monitoring is required.

---

## What's New / Changed in 2024

1. **Return of LDL Targets:** The 2013 guideline famously abandoned LDL-C targets in favor of a "fire-and-forget" statin dose based on age and eGFR. The 2024 update aligns with cardiology by reinstating specific targets (<70 mg/dL and <55 mg/dL) to guide escalation therapy.
2. **Integration of PCSK9 Inhibitors:** Formal endorsement of Repatha (evolocumab) and Praluent (alirocumab) for secondary prevention in CKD patients who do not meet targets on statin + ezetimibe.
3. **Inclusion of Bempedoic Acid:** Nexletol (bempedoic acid) is now recognized as a viable alternative for statin-intolerant CKD patients.
4. **Leqvio (inclisiran):** Acknowledged as an emerging twice-yearly option, though long-term CKD data is still maturing.

## How TKE Applies This Guideline

### Integration with Other Guidelines
- **Diabetic CKD:** We integrate this guideline with the **ADA 2024 Standards of Care**. For patients with Diabetic Kidney Disease (DKD), aggressive lipid management is paired with SGLT2 inhibitors (e.g., Farxiga (dapagliflozin) or Jardiance (empagliflozin)) and GLP-1 RAs (e.g., Ozempic (semaglutide)) for comprehensive cardiorenal protection.
- **Cardiology Co-Management:** TKE providers should defer to or collaborate with the patient's cardiologist if the patient has recent acute coronary syndrome (ACS), ensuring we do not inadvertently decrease statin doses unless dictated by eGFR thresholds (e.g., Crestor (rosuvastatin) limits).

### 5-Point TKE Summary
1. **Start early:** Treat almost all non-dialysis CKD patients >50 years old with a statin.
2. **Watch the kidneys:** Cap Crestor (rosuvastatin) at 10mg and Zocor (simvastatin) at 20mg if eGFR <30. Lipitor (atorvastatin) requires no renal adjustment.
3. **Don't start on dialysis:** Do not initiate statins for primary prevention once a patient is on dialysis, but do not stop them if they were already taking them.
4. **Treat to target:** Aim for LDL <70 mg/dL (primary) or <55 mg/dL (secondary/ASCVD). 
5. **Escalate boldly:** Use Zetia (ezetimibe) and PCSK9 inhibitors (Repatha/Praluent) if targets are not met; they are safe and effective in advanced CKD.
