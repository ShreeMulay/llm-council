# Clinical Reference: Dapagliflozin in Patients with Chronic Kidney Disease (DAPA-CKD) & KDIGO Integration

**Primary Source:** DAPA-CKD Trial (NEJM, 2020-10-08) | DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa2024816
**Guideline Integrations:** KDIGO 2024 CKD Evaluation & Management, KDIGO 2022 Diabetes in CKD, ADA 2024 Standards of Care
**Relevant Domains:** SGLT2 Inhibitors, Diabetic Kidney Disease, Non-Diabetic Proteinuric CKD

---

## Overview

The DAPA-CKD trial represents a paradigm shift in nephrology, establishing SGLT2 inhibitors as foundational therapy for Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) *independent* of diabetes status. Prior to this landmark trial, SGLT2 inhibitors were primarily viewed as glycemic control agents. DAPA-CKD demonstrated that Farxiga (dapagliflozin) significantly reduces the risk of kidney function decline, end-stage kidney disease (ESKD), and cardiovascular or renal death in patients with CKD stages 2-4 with albuminuria, regardless of the presence of Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (T2DM).

**Target Population and Intended Audience:**
This reference is intended for TKE Nephrologists, Advanced Practice Providers (APPs), and clinical pharmacists managing adult patients with proteinuric CKD (eGFR 25–75 mL/min/1.73 m² and Urine Albumin-to-Creatinine Ratio [UACR] 200–5000 mg/g). 

**What Changed from Previous Paradigms:**
Historically, RAAS blockade (ACEi/ARB) was the sole pillar of nephroprotection for proteinuric CKD. DAPA-CKD, subsequently codified in KDIGO 2024 and ADA 2024 guidelines, established Farxiga (dapagliflozin) as a mandatory second pillar of therapy. It explicitly expanded the indication to non-diabetic kidney diseases (e.g., IgA Nephropathy, hypertensive nephrosclerosis), fundamentally changing the nephrology standard of care.

## Key Recommendations

The following recommendations synthesize the DAPA-CKD trial protocol with current KDIGO 2024 and ADA 2024 guidelines.

### Initiation and Patient Selection
*   **KDIGO 2024 Recommendation:** Treat adult patients with CKD and heart failure or an eGFR $\ge$ 20 mL/min/1.73 m² with UACR $\ge$ 200 mg/g with an SGLT2 inhibitor (Grade 1A).
*   **DAPA-CKD Specific Criteria:** Initiate Farxiga (dapagliflozin) in patients with eGFR $\ge$ 25 mL/min/1.73 m² and UACR $\ge$ 200 mg/g. 
*   **Target Values:** 
    *   Primary therapeutic goal: Reduce UACR by $\ge$ 30% from baseline.
    *   Ultimate clinical target: UACR < 30 mg/g (though any reduction is nephroprotective).
*   **Background Therapy:** Patients should be on a maximally tolerated dose of an ACE inhibitor or ARB for at least 4 weeks prior to initiation, unless contraindicated.

### Dosing and Administration
*   **Drug:** Farxiga (dapagliflozin)
*   **Dose:** 10 mg PO once daily.
*   **Titration:** None required. This is a flat-dosed therapy.
*   **CKD-Stage Modifications:** 
    *   **eGFR $\ge$ 25:** Initiate 10 mg daily.
    *   **eGFR < 25:** Do not *initiate* per FDA label for Farxiga (dapagliflozin), though KDIGO 2024 supports class initiation down to eGFR 20.
    *   **eGFR decline after initiation:** If eGFR falls below 25 mL/min/1.73 m² *after* starting therapy, **CONTINUE** the medication until the initiation of maintenance dialysis or kidney transplantation.

### Contraindications and Exclusions
*   **Absolute Contraindications:** Hypersensitivity to Farxiga (dapagliflozin), Type 1 Diabetes Mellitus (due to high risk of euglycemic DKA), Polycystic Kidney Disease (PCKD), and patients currently on maintenance dialysis.
*   **Trial Exclusions (Use Clinical Judgment):** Lupus nephritis, ANCA-associated vasculitis, and patients requiring recent cytotoxic or immunosuppressive therapy (within 6 months).

## Practice Points

### Decision Tree: SGLT2i Management in CKD
*   **START:** Patient has eGFR $\ge$ 25 and UACR $\ge$ 200 mg/g (with or without T2DM). Ensure patient is euvolemic and not at high risk for DKA.
*   **ESCALATE:** N/A (Fixed 10 mg dose). Optimize concurrent RAAS blockade or add Kerendia (finerenone) if T2DM is present and UACR remains elevated.
*   **SWITCH:** If patient experiences recurrent genital mycotic infections or intolerance to Farxiga (dapagliflozin), consider switching to Jardiance (empagliflozin) 10 mg daily, though cross-reactivity/class-effect side effects are likely.
*   **STOP:** Discontinue upon initiation of hemodialysis/peritoneal dialysis, kidney transplantation, pregnancy, or during acute critical illness/major surgery (hold 3 days prior).

### The "eGFR Dip" (Hemodynamic Effect)
*   **Mechanism:** SGLT2 inhibitors cause afferent arteriolar vasoconstriction, reducing intraglomerular pressure.
*   **Clinical Action:** Expect an acute drop in eGFR of 10-20% within the first 2-4 weeks. 
*   **Threshold:** Do **NOT** stop the drug unless the eGFR decline is > 30% from baseline. If > 30%, evaluate for volume depletion, NSAID use, or bilateral renal artery stenosis.

### Patient Education Talking Points
*   **"The Plumbing Analogy":** Explain that Farxiga (dapagliflozin) reduces the "pressure in the kidney filters," which protects them long-term, even if the lab numbers (creatinine) look slightly worse at first.
*   **Hygiene:** Emphasize daily genital hygiene to prevent fungal infections (the most common side effect).
*   **Sick Day Rules:** Instruct patients to STOP taking Farxiga (dapagliflozin) if they are vomiting, have severe diarrhea, or cannot eat/drink, to prevent dehydration and Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA).
*   **Urination:** Warn patients they will urinate more frequently, especially in the first few weeks.

## Evidence Summary

**Landmark Trial:** DAPA-CKD (Dapagliflozin and Prevention of Adverse Outcomes in Chronic Kidney Disease)
*   **Population:** 4,304 patients; eGFR 25-75; UACR 200-5000. Notably, 32.5% of participants did **not** have diabetes.
*   **Primary Endpoint:** Composite of sustained $\ge$ 50% eGFR decline, ESKD, or renal/CV death.
*   **Key Results:** 
    *   Farxiga (dapagliflozin) reduced the primary composite endpoint by **39%** (Hazard Ratio 0.61; 95% CI, 0.51 to 0.72; P<0.001).
    *   All-cause mortality was reduced by 31% (HR 0.69).
    *   Results were consistent regardless of T2DM status.
*   **Number Needed to Treat (NNT):** 19 patients treated over 2.4 years to prevent one primary outcome event.
*   **Limitations:** The trial excluded patients with PCKD, lupus nephritis, and ANCA vasculitis. Efficacy in these specific populations remains unproven.

## What's New / Changed

*   **Decoupling from Diabetes:** Prior to DAPA-CKD, SGLT2 inhibitors were strictly anti-hyperglycemic agents. This trial established them as primary nephroprotective agents.
*   **Lower eGFR Thresholds:** Historically, SGLT2 inhibitors were contraindicated in eGFR < 45. DAPA-CKD pushed the initiation threshold down to 25. (Subsequent trials like EMPA-KIDNEY pushed class guidelines down to 20).
*   **Continuation to Dialysis:** Guidelines now explicitly state that once initiated, Farxiga (dapagliflozin) should be continued even if eGFR drops below 25, stopping only when dialysis or transplant occurs.

### One-Page Summary: Farxiga (dapagliflozin) in CKD
1.  **Who gets it?** Any CKD patient (eGFR $\ge$ 25, UACR $\ge$ 200), with or without diabetes, excluding Type 1 DM and PCKD.
2.  **What is the dose?** Farxiga (dapagliflozin) 10 mg PO daily. No titration.
3.  **What to expect?** A mild, reversible drop in eGFR (up to 30%) within the first month. Do not stop the drug for this.
4.  **When to stop?** Dialysis initiation, kidney transplant, acute dehydrating illness (sick days), or major surgery (hold 3 days prior).
5.  **What to monitor?** BMP at 2-4 weeks (to check eGFR dip and ensure no severe AKI), UACR at 3 months to assess therapeutic response. Monitor clinically for signs of genital mycotic infections or volume depletion.
