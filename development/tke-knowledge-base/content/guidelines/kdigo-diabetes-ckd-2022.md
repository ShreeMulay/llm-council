# KDIGO 2022 Clinical Practice Guideline for Diabetes Management in CKD

**Guideline:** KDIGO 2022 Clinical Practice Guideline for Diabetes Management in CKD
**Organization:** Kidney Disease: Improving Global Outcomes (KDIGO)
**Publication Date:** 2022-11-01
**Relevant Domains:** Diabetes, SGLT2 Inhibitors, GLP-1 Receptor Agonists, nsMRAs

---

## Overview

The KDIGO 2022 guideline provides a comprehensive framework for managing patients with diabetes and chronic kidney disease (CKD). For nephrology practice, this guideline represents a paradigm shift from purely glucose-centric management to a holistic, organ-protection strategy aimed at delaying CKD progression and reducing cardiovascular (CV) events. 

**Target Population:** Patients with Type 1 Diabetes (T1D) or Type 2 Diabetes (T2D) and concomitant CKD (eGFR <60 mL/min/1.73 m² or UACR $\ge$30 mg/g).
**Intended Audience:** Nephrologists, Advanced Practice Providers (APPs), Primary Care Providers, and Endocrinologists.

**Why it matters for TKE:** Nephrologists are now primary prescribers of disease-modifying therapies (SGLT2is, GLP-1 RAs, nsMRAs). This guideline provides the evidence-based foundation for aggressive, multi-drug pharmacological intervention to preserve kidney function.

## Key Recommendations

### 1. Glycemic Monitoring and Targets
*   **Recommendation:** Use HbA1c to monitor glycemic control (1A). Continuous Glucose Monitoring (CGM) is recommended to assess time in range and hypoglycemia, especially in advanced CKD where HbA1c is less reliable (2B).
*   **Target Values:** Individualized HbA1c target ranging from **<6.5% to <8.0%**.
    *   *Aim lower (<6.5%):* CKD G1-G2, young age, long life expectancy, no prior severe hypoglycemia.
    *   *Aim higher (<8.0%):* CKD G4-G5, high hypoglycemia risk, limited life expectancy, multiple comorbidities.
*   **CGM Targets:** Time in Range (TIR) >70%, Time Below Range (TBR) <4%.

### 2. First-Line Pharmacotherapy: SGLT2 Inhibitors
*   **Recommendation:** Treat patients with T2D, CKD, and an eGFR $\ge$20 mL/min/1.73 m² with an SGLT2 inhibitor (1A).
*   **Key Rule:** Once initiated, **continue the SGLT2 inhibitor even if eGFR falls below 20 mL/min/1.73 m²**, unless the patient initiates dialysis or receives a kidney transplant.
*   **Drug Options & Dosing:**
    *   **Farxiga (dapagliflozin):** 10 mg PO daily. No titration needed.
    *   **Jardiance (empagliflozin):** 10 mg PO daily. No titration needed for CKD benefit.
    *   **Invokana (canagliflozin):** 100 mg PO daily. (May increase to 300 mg if eGFR >60 and additional glycemic control needed).

### 3. First-Line Pharmacotherapy: Metformin
*   **Recommendation:** Treat patients with T2D, CKD, and an eGFR $\ge$30 mL/min/1.73 m² with metformin (1B).
*   **Drug Options & Dosing:**
    *   **Glucophage (metformin):** Start 500 mg PO daily, titrate to 1000-2000 mg daily based on eGFR.
*   **CKD-Specific Modifications:**
    *   eGFR $\ge$45: Max dose 2000 mg/day.
    *   eGFR 30-44: Max dose 1000 mg/day. Do not initiate new starts in this range, but can continue if already on it.
    *   eGFR <30: **Discontinue** (Contraindicated due to lactic acidosis risk).

### 4. Additional Pharmacotherapy: GLP-1 Receptor Agonists
*   **Recommendation:** In patients with T2D and CKD who have not achieved individualized glycemic targets despite use of metformin and SGLT2i, or who are unable to use those medications, recommend a long-acting GLP-1 RA (1B).
*   **Drug Options & Dosing (Cardiovascular/Kidney benefits proven):**
    *   **Ozempic (semaglutide):** Start 0.25 mg SQ weekly x 4 weeks, then 0.5 mg weekly. Max 2.0 mg weekly. (Safe down to eGFR 15).
    *   **Trulicity (dulaglutide):** Start 0.75 mg SQ weekly, titrate up to 4.5 mg weekly. (Safe down to eGFR 15).
    *   **Victoza (liraglutide):** Start 0.6 mg SQ daily x 1 week, titrate to 1.2 - 1.8 mg daily. (Safe down to eGFR 15).

### 5. Additional Pharmacotherapy: Non-steroidal MRAs (nsMRA)
*   **Recommendation:** Recommend a non-steroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist with proven kidney/CV benefit for patients with T2D, eGFR $\ge$25 mL/min/1.73 m², normal serum potassium, and UACR $\ge$30 mg/g despite maximum tolerated RASi (2A).
*   **Drug Options & Dosing:**
    *   **Kerendia (finerenone):** 
        *   eGFR $\ge$60: Start 20 mg PO daily.
        *   eGFR 25-59: Start 10 mg PO daily.
        *   Titrate to 20 mg after 4 weeks if K+ $\le$4.8 mEq/L and eGFR stable.

### 6. Renin-Angiotensin System Inhibitors (RASi)
*   **Recommendation:** Recommend an ACEi or ARB for patients with diabetes, hypertension, and albuminuria (UACR $\ge$30 mg/g) (1B). Titrate to the highest approved dose that is tolerated.
*   **Target:** UACR <30 mg/g.

## Practice Points

### Decision Tree: Pharmacotherapy in Diabetic CKD
1.  **START:** All patients with T2D and CKD (eGFR $\ge$20) should be on **Farxiga (dapagliflozin)** or **Jardiance (empagliflozin)** AND **Glucophage (metformin)** (if eGFR $\ge$30). Add ACEi/ARB if UACR $\ge$30 mg/g.
2.  **ESCALATE (Residual Albuminuria):** If UACR remains $\ge$30 mg/g despite max tolerated ACEi/ARB + SGLT2i, and K+ is $\le$4.8, ADD **Kerendia (finerenone)**.
3.  **ESCALATE (Glycemic/Weight/ASCVD):** If HbA1c remains above target, BMI is high, or patient has high ASCVD risk, ADD **Ozempic (semaglutide)** or **Trulicity (dulaglutide)**.
4.  **SWITCH:** If SGLT2i is not tolerated (e.g., recurrent mycotic infections), switch to GLP-1 RA as primary disease-modifying agent.
5.  **STOP:** Stop Metformin if eGFR <30. Stop SGLT2i if patient starts dialysis. Hold SGLT2i/Metformin during acute illness (SADMANS).

### ⚠️ Safety Concerns & Contraindications
*   **SGLT2i:** Risk of euglycemic Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA). Hold 3-4 days prior to scheduled surgeries or during acute illness/fasting. Genital mycotic infections are common. *Note: A transient dip in eGFR (up to 30%) upon initiation is expected and is NOT a reason to stop the drug.*
*   **GLP-1 RA:** Contraindicated in personal/family history of Medullary Thyroid Carcinoma (MTC) or Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN2). High risk of GI side effects (nausea/vomiting) which can lead to AKI via volume depletion.
*   **nsMRA (Kerendia):** Hyperkalemia risk. Do not initiate if K+ >5.0 mEq/L. Monitor K+ at 4 weeks after initiation or dose change.

### Patient Education Talking Points
*   *"We are starting this medication (SGLT2i) not just for your blood sugar, but because it acts like a shield for your kidneys and your heart."*
*   *"You may notice a slight drop in your kidney numbers on your next lab test. This is expected and actually shows the medication is working to reduce pressure inside your kidneys."*
*   *"If you get sick with vomiting, diarrhea, or cannot eat/drink normally, stop taking your SGLT2i and Metformin until you are eating and drinking normally again."*

## Evidence Summary

*   **CREDENCE (2019):** **Invokana (canagliflozin)** vs placebo in T2D + CKD (eGFR 30-90, UACR >300). Resulted in a 30% relative risk reduction in the primary composite outcome (ESRD, doubling of serum creatinine, or renal/CV death). NNT = 22 over 2.5 years.
*   **DAPA-CKD (2020):** **Farxiga (dapagliflozin)** vs placebo in CKD (eGFR 25-75, UACR 200-5000) *with and without diabetes*. Showed a 39% reduction in sustained $\ge$50% eGFR decline, ESRD, or renal/CV death.
*   **EMPA-KIDNEY (2022):** **Jardiance (empagliflozin)** vs placebo in CKD (eGFR 20-45, or 45-90 with UACR $\ge$200). Showed a 28% reduction in kidney disease progression or CV death.
*   **FIDELIO-DKD & FIGARO-DKD (2020/2021):** **Kerendia (finerenone)** vs placebo in T2D + CKD. Showed significant reduction in kidney progression and CV events, leading to the new nsMRA recommendations.

## What's New / Changed (vs. 2020 Guidelines)

1.  **Lowered eGFR Threshold for SGLT2i:** Reduced from $\ge$30 to **$\ge$20** mL/min/1.73 m² based on EMPA-KIDNEY and DAPA-CKD data.
2.  **Addition of nsMRAs:** **Kerendia (finerenone)** is now formally recommended (Grade 2A) for residual albuminuria.
3.  **GLP-1 RA Elevation:** Stronger emphasis on GLP-1 RAs for cardiovascular protection and weight management in CKD, independent of glycemic control needs.

### The "SADMANS" Sick Day Rule
Instruct patients to hold the following medications during acute dehydrating illness (vomiting, diarrhea, severe fever) to prevent AKI and DKA:
*   **S**ulfonylureas
*   **A**CE inhibitors
*   **D**iuretics
*   **M**etformin
*   **A**RBs
*   **N**SAIDs
*   **S**GLT2 inhibitors
