# KDIGO 2024 Clinical Practice Guideline for the Evaluation and Management of CKD

**Organization:** KDIGO (Kidney Disease: Improving Global Outcomes)
**Publication Date:** March 2024
**Version:** 2024
**URL:** [KDIGO 2024 CKD Guideline](https://kdigo.org/guidelines/ckd-evaluation-and-management/)

---

## Overview

The KDIGO 2024 Clinical Practice Guideline for the Evaluation and Management of Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) represents a massive paradigm shift in nephrology. It updates the foundational 2012 guidelines to incorporate a decade of breakthrough therapies, fundamentally changing CKD management from a reactive, complication-driven approach to a proactive, disease-modifying strategy. 

**Why it matters for TKE practice:**
This guideline establishes the new standard of care for comprehensive CKD management. It mandates the use of multi-pillar therapy—combining Renin-Angiotensin-Aldosterone System (RAAS) blockade, SGLT2 inhibitors, non-steroidal Mineralocorticoid Receptor Antagonists (ns-MRAs), and GLP-1 Receptor Agonists (GLP-1 RAs)—to delay progression to end-stage kidney disease (ESKD) and reduce cardiovascular mortality. 

**Target Population & Intended Audience:**
*   **Population:** Adults and children with CKD (eGFR < 60 mL/min/1.73 m² or markers of kidney damage like UACR $\ge$ 30 mg/g for > 3 months).
*   **Audience:** Nephrologists, Advanced Practice Providers (APPs), clinical pharmacists, and nursing staff at TKE.

**What Changed:**
The 2024 update introduces the routine use of the Kidney Failure Risk Equation (KFRE), expands indications for SGLT2 inhibitors to non-diabetic CKD, introduces Kerendia (finerenone) as a foundational therapy, and aligns blood pressure targets to a strict systolic < 120 mmHg.

## Key Recommendations

### 1. Evaluation and Risk Stratification
*   **Diagnosis:** Confirm CKD using eGFR and Urine Albumin-to-Creatinine Ratio (UACR).
*   **Cystatin C:** KDIGO recommends using a combination of serum creatinine and cystatin C (eGFRcr-cys) for more accurate GFR estimation, especially when eGFRcr is 45-59 mL/min/1.73 m² and UACR is < 30 mg/g (1B).
*   **Risk Prediction:** Use the 4-variable Kidney Failure Risk Equation (KFRE) to predict the 2-year and 5-year risk of ESKD. 
    *   *Target:* Refer to multidisciplinary care when 5-year KFRE > 10%.

### 2. RAAS Blockade (ACEi / ARB)
*   **Recommendation:** Initiate an ACEi or ARB for patients with CKD, hypertension, and UACR $\ge$ 300 mg/g (1B) or UACR 30–299 mg/g (2C).
*   **Drugs:** Cozaar (losartan), Diovan (valsartan), Altace (ramipril), Prinivil/Zestril (lisinopril).
*   **Titration:** Titrate to the highest approved dose that is tolerated (1B).
*   **Monitoring:** Check basic metabolic panel (BMP) 2-4 weeks after initiation or dose increase.
*   **CKD Modifications:** 
    *   Do *not* routinely stop RAASi when eGFR falls below 30 mL/min/1.73 m² (based on STOP-ACEi trial).
    *   Accept up to a 30% reversible decline in eGFR upon initiation.

### 3. SGLT2 Inhibitors
*   **Recommendation:** Treat adults with CKD and heart failure or eGFR $\ge$ 20 mL/min/1.73 m² with UACR $\ge$ 200 mg/g with an SGLT2i (1A). Also recommended for T2D and CKD with eGFR $\ge$ 20 (1A).
*   **Drugs & Dosing:**
    *   Farxiga (dapagliflozin): 10 mg PO daily.
    *   Jardiance (empagliflozin): 10 mg PO daily.
    *   Invokana (canagliflozin): 100 mg PO daily.
*   **CKD Modifications:** Initiate if eGFR $\ge$ 20. If eGFR drops below 20 *after* initiation, **continue therapy** until initiation of dialysis or kidney transplantation.
*   **Monitoring:** No routine dose titration needed. No routine eGFR/K+ monitoring required solely for SGLT2i initiation.

### 4. Non-steroidal MRA (ns-MRA)
*   **Recommendation:** Treat adults with T2D, eGFR $\ge$ 25 mL/min/1.73 m², normal serum potassium, and UACR $\ge$ 30 mg/g with an ns-MRA despite maximum tolerated RAASi (1A).
*   **Drugs & Dosing:** Kerendia (finerenone).
    *   eGFR $\ge$ 60: Start 20 mg PO daily.
    *   eGFR 25-59: Start 10 mg PO daily.
*   **Titration:** Increase from 10 mg to 20 mg after 4 weeks if K+ $\le$ 4.8 mEq/L and eGFR remains stable.
*   **Monitoring:** Check K+ at 1 month after initiation or dose change.

### 5. GLP-1 Receptor Agonists
*   **Recommendation:** Treat adults with T2D and CKD who have not achieved individualized glycemic targets with a GLP-1 RA (1B). (Note: The FLOW trial now supports GLP-1 RAs for direct kidney protection).
*   **Drugs:** Ozempic/Wegovy (semaglutide), Trulicity (dulaglutide), Mounjaro/Zepbound (tirzepatide - dual GIP/GLP-1).
*   **CKD Modifications:** Ozempic (semaglutide) requires no renal dose adjustment. Trulicity (dulaglutide) requires no renal dose adjustment.

### 6. Blood Pressure Management
*   **Recommendation:** Target systolic blood pressure (SBP) to < 120 mmHg, when tolerated, using standardized office BP measurement (2B).

## Decision Trees: Start, Escalate, Switch, Stop

### Multi-Pillar Therapy Escalation in Diabetic CKD
1.  **START:** Maximize RAASi (e.g., Cozaar (losartan) 100mg) + SGLT2i (e.g., Farxiga (dapagliflozin) 10mg) if eGFR $\ge$ 20.
2.  **ESCALATE (Add-on):** If UACR remains $\ge$ 30 mg/g and K+ $\le$ 5.0, add Kerendia (finerenone).
3.  **ESCALATE (Add-on):** If BMI > 27 or A1c not at target, add GLP-1 RA (e.g., Ozempic (semaglutide)).
4.  **STOP/HOLD:** Hold SGLT2i during prolonged fasting, surgery, or critical illness (DKA risk). Hold RAASi/ns-MRA if K+ > 5.5 mEq/L despite dietary restriction and potassium binders (e.g., Lokelma (sodium zirconium cyclosilicate)).

## Practice Points & Clinical Implementation

*   **The "Permissive eGFR Dip":** Educate staff and patients that a 10-30% drop in eGFR is expected and hemodynamically mediated when starting RAASi, SGLT2i, or ns-MRAs. Do not stop the drug unless the drop is > 30% or accompanied by severe AKI/volume depletion.
*   **Hyperkalemia Management:** Do not prematurely discontinue RAASi or Kerendia (finerenone) for mild hyperkalemia (5.1-5.4 mEq/L). Utilize low-potassium diets, diuretics (if volume overloaded), or potassium binders like Lokelma (sodium zirconium cyclosilicate) or Veltassa (patiromer) to enable life-saving CKD therapies.
*   **Sick Day Rules:** Patients must be educated to hold "SADMANS" medications (Sulfonylureas, ACEi, Diuretics, Metformin, ARBs, NSAIDs, SGLT2i) during acute dehydrating illnesses (vomiting/diarrhea) to prevent AKI and euglycemic DKA.

## Safety Flags, Contraindications, & Interactions

🚨 **SGLT2 Inhibitors (Farxiga, Jardiance, Invokana):**
*   **Safety:** Risk of euglycemic Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA), especially in insulin-deficient patients, during fasting, or acute illness. Risk of genital mycotic infections.
*   **Contraindication:** Type 1 Diabetes (due to high DKA risk), Polycystic Kidney Disease (PKD), or patients on active immunosuppression for glomerular disease (limited data).

🚨 **ns-MRAs (Kerendia / finerenone):**
*   **Safety:** Hyperkalemia. 
*   **Contraindication:** Do not initiate if K+ > 5.0 mEq/L. 
*   **Interactions:** Strong CYP3A4 inhibitors (e.g., grapefruit juice, ketoconazole, clarithromycin) significantly increase finerenone exposure.

🚨 **RAAS Blockade (ACEi/ARB):**
*   **Safety:** Hyperkalemia, AKI, teratogenicity.
*   **Contraindication:** Pregnancy, history of angioedema (ACEi), bilateral renal artery stenosis.

## Evidence Summary

*   **DAPA-CKD & EMPA-KIDNEY:** Demonstrated that Farxiga (dapagliflozin) and Jardiance (empagliflozin) reduce the risk of CKD progression and cardiovascular death by ~30-40% in both diabetic and non-diabetic CKD. *NNT to prevent one primary outcome event over 2.4 years is ~19.*
*   **FIDELIO-DKD & FIGARO-DKD:** Showed that Kerendia (finerenone) reduces kidney failure and cardiovascular events in T2D with CKD.
*   **FLOW Trial (2024):** Ozempic (semaglutide) 1.0 mg significantly reduced the risk of major kidney disease events, cardiovascular events, and all-cause death in patients with T2D and CKD.
*   **STOP-ACEi:** Showed no significant benefit in eGFR recovery when stopping ACEi/ARBs in advanced CKD (Stage 4/5), reinforcing the recommendation to continue these agents.

## What's New / Changed from Prior Guidelines

1.  **SGLT2i for All:** Expanded from just diabetic kidney disease to almost all proteinuric CKD (excluding PKD and active GN).
2.  **Introduction of ns-MRAs:** Kerendia (finerenone) is now a foundational pillar, not a salvage therapy.
3.  **Strict BP Targets:** Shifted from < 130/80 to < 120 SBP (standardized office reading).
4.  **KFRE Integration:** Shift from using eGFR alone for referral/planning to using the 4-variable KFRE.
5.  **Cystatin C:** Stronger push for Cystatin C to confirm eGFR, especially to avoid over-diagnosing CKD in patients with borderline creatinine-based eGFRs.

## Patient Education Talking Points

*   **The "Four Pillars":** *"We are putting you on a combination of medications—like a shield with four layers—to protect your kidneys. These include blood pressure pills, a sugar pill that protects kidneys, a hormone blocker, and sometimes a weight-loss/diabetes shot."*
*   **The eGFR Dip:** *"When you start [Farxiga / Kerendia / Lisinopril], your kidney numbers might look slightly worse on your next lab test. This is expected. It means the medication is taking the pressure off your kidney filters."*
*   **Sick Days:** *"If you get a stomach bug and can't keep fluids down, stop taking your [Farxiga/Jardiance] and your blood pressure pills immediately, and call our clinic. Taking them while dehydrated can harm your kidneys."*
*   **Hygiene (SGLT2i):** *"Medications like [Farxiga/Jardiance] cause you to urinate out extra sugar. You must practice excellent hygiene to prevent yeast infections."*

### One-Page Summary: The TKE Approach to KDIGO 2024
1.  **Confirm & Stage:** Use eGFR (consider Cystatin C) and UACR. Calculate KFRE.
2.  **Foundation:** Maximize RAASi (ACEi/ARB) for all patients with UACR > 30 mg/g. Do not stop for late-stage CKD.
3.  **Add SGLT2i:** Start Farxiga (dapagliflozin) 10mg or Jardiance (empagliflozin) 10mg for eGFR $\ge$ 20 and UACR $\ge$ 200 (or any UACR if diabetic/heart failure).
4.  **Add ns-MRA:** Start Kerendia (finerenone) 10-20mg for diabetic CKD with UACR $\ge$ 30 and eGFR $\ge$ 25, provided K+ is normal.
5.  **Add GLP-1 RA:** Utilize Ozempic (semaglutide) or similar for diabetic CKD to control A1c, weight, and provide direct kidney protection.
6.  **Manage Complications:** Treat BP to < 120 SBP. Use potassium binders (Lokelma/Veltassa) to manage hyperkalemia rather than stopping protective CKD meds. Educate on sick day rules.
