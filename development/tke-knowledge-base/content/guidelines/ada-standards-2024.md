# ADA Standards of Care in Diabetes 2024: Nephrology Clinical Guideline Summary

## Overview
The **American Diabetes Association (ADA) Standards of Care in Diabetes 2024** is the premier comprehensive guideline for the management of diabetes mellitus. For nephrology practice, Chapter 11 ("Chronic Kidney Disease and Risk Management") and Chapter 9 ("Pharmacologic Approaches to Glycemic Treatment") are the most critical. 

Diabetic kidney disease (DKD) occurs in 20–40% of patients with diabetes and is the leading cause of end-stage kidney disease (ESKD). This guideline provides the framework for slowing CKD progression, reducing cardiovascular (CV) events, and managing complex pharmacotherapy in the setting of reduced kidney function. 

**Target Population:** Adult patients with Type 1 or Type 2 Diabetes (T1D/T2D) and Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD).
**Intended Audience:** Nephrologists, APPs, diabetes care and education specialists, and clinical pharmacists.
**Why it matters for TKE:** Nephrologists are increasingly the primary prescribers of disease-modifying therapies (SGLT2 inhibitors, GLP-1 receptor agonists, and nsMRAs). This guideline establishes the standard of care for multi-pillar therapy in diabetic nephropathy, shifting the focus from purely glycemic control to cardiorenal risk reduction.

## Key Recommendations

### 1. Screening and Diagnosis
*   **Screening Frequency:** Assess urinary albumin-to-creatinine ratio (UACR) and estimated glomerular filtration rate (eGFR) at least annually in all patients with T2D and patients with T1D of ≥5 years duration (Grade A).
*   **High-Risk Screening:** Screen twice annually if UACR is >30 mg/g or eGFR is <60 mL/min/1.73 m² (Grade B).
*   **Diagnosis:** Requires two of three elevated UACR specimens (≥30 mg/g) collected within a 3–6 month period, or persistently reduced eGFR (<60 mL/min/1.73 m²) in the absence of other primary causes of kidney damage.

### 2. Glycemic and Blood Pressure Targets
*   **A1C Target:** Generally <7.0% for non-pregnant adults (Grade A). 
    *   *CKD Modification:* Relax to <8.0% for patients with advanced CKD, limited life expectancy, or high hypoglycemia risk (Grade B).
*   **Time in Range (TIR):** For patients on Continuous Glucose Monitors (CGM), target >70% time in range (70-180 mg/dL) and <4% time below range (<70 mg/dL).
*   **Blood Pressure Target:** <130/80 mmHg for patients with diabetes and CKD, if it can be safely achieved (Grade A).
*   **Albuminuria Target:** Target a UACR reduction of ≥30% from baseline to slow CKD progression (Grade B).

### 3. Pharmacologic Management (The "Four Pillars")

#### Pillar 1: RAS Inhibition (ACEi / ARB)
*   **Recommendation:** First-line therapy for hypertension in patients with diabetes and UACR ≥300 mg/g (Grade A) or UACR 30–299 mg/g (Grade B).
*   **Dosing:** Titrate to the maximum tolerated approved dose.
*   **Monitoring:** Check serum creatinine and potassium 2–4 weeks after initiation or dose escalation. A creatinine bump of up to 30% is acceptable and does not warrant discontinuation.

#### Pillar 2: SGLT2 Inhibitors
*   **Recommendation:** Recommended for patients with T2D and CKD (eGFR ≥20 mL/min/1.73 m²) with UACR ≥200 mg/g to reduce CKD progression and CV events (Grade A). Also recommended for UACR 20-199 mg/g (Grade B).
*   **Drugs & Dosing:**
    *   **Farxiga (dapagliflozin):** 10 mg PO daily.
    *   **Jardiance (empagliflozin):** 10 mg PO daily.
    *   **Invokana (canagliflozin):** 100 mg PO daily.
*   **CKD Modification:** Initiate if eGFR ≥20. If eGFR drops below 20 during treatment, *continue* the medication until the initiation of dialysis or kidney transplantation.

#### Pillar 3: Non-Steroidal Mineralocorticoid Receptor Antagonists (nsMRA)
*   **Recommendation:** Recommended for patients with T2D and CKD with albuminuria (UACR ≥30 mg/g) and normal potassium, who are on maximum tolerated RAS inhibition, to reduce CV events and CKD progression (Grade A).
*   **Drug & Dosing:** **Kerendia (finerenone)**
    *   eGFR ≥60: Start 20 mg PO daily.
    *   eGFR 25–59: Start 10 mg PO daily.
    *   Titrate from 10 mg to 20 mg after 4 weeks if potassium is ≤4.8 mEq/L.
*   **Monitoring:** Check potassium 4 weeks after initiation or dose change. Do not initiate if K+ >5.0 mEq/L.

#### Pillar 4: GLP-1 Receptor Agonists & Dual GIP/GLP-1 RA
*   **Recommendation:** Recommended for patients with T2D and CKD who require further glycemic control, weight management, or have high CV risk (Grade A). They also reduce albuminuria and slow eGFR decline.
*   **Drugs & Dosing:**
    *   **Ozempic (semaglutide):** Start 0.25 mg SQ weekly x 4 weeks, then 0.5 mg weekly. Max 2.0 mg weekly.
    *   **Trulicity (dulaglutide):** Start 0.75 mg SQ weekly. Max 4.5 mg weekly.
    *   **Mounjaro (tirzepatide) [GIP/GLP-1]:** Start 2.5 mg SQ weekly x 4 weeks, then 5 mg weekly. Max 15 mg weekly.
*   **CKD Modification:** No dose adjustment required for eGFR decline, but use caution in severe GI distress to avoid volume depletion and AKI.

## Practice Points

### Therapy Escalation Decision Tree
1.  **Baseline:** Patient has T2D, eGFR 45, UACR 400 mg/g, BP 140/90.
2.  **Step 1:** Maximize ACEi/ARB (e.g., Lisinopril 40 mg or Losartan 100 mg).
3.  **Step 2:** Add SGLT2i (e.g., Farxiga (dapagliflozin) 10 mg daily).
4.  **Step 3:** Assess at 4-8 weeks. If UACR remains >30 mg/g and K+ is ≤5.0, add nsMRA (e.g., Kerendia (finerenone) 10 mg daily).
5.  **Step 4:** If BMI >27, A1C >7%, or ASCVD present, add GLP-1 RA (e.g., Ozempic (semaglutide)).

### Sick Day Rules (SADMANS)
Educate patients to hold the following medications during acute illness involving vomiting, diarrhea, or inability to maintain oral intake to prevent AKI and euglycemic DKA:
*   **S**ulfonylureas
*   **A**CE inhibitors
*   **D**iuretics
*   **M**etformin
*   **A**RBs
*   **N**SAIDs
*   **S**GLT2 inhibitors

### Patient Education Talking Points
*   *"SGLT2 inhibitors like Farxiga or Jardiance are not just diabetes drugs; they are kidney-protecting drugs. They act like a pressure-relief valve for your kidney filters."*
*   *"You may see a slight bump in your creatinine (kidney number) when we start these medications. This is expected and shows the drug is working to lower pressure in the kidney."*
*   *"If you take an SGLT2 inhibitor, you must practice excellent genital hygiene to prevent yeast infections, and stop the medication immediately if you are sick and cannot drink fluids."*

### Relevant Coding
*   **ICD-10:** 
    *   E11.22 (Type 2 diabetes mellitus with diabetic chronic kidney disease)
    *   N18.x (CKD stage, e.g., N18.3a for Stage 3a, N18.4 for Stage 4)
*   **CPT:** 99214 or 99215 (depending on medical decision making complexity, which is typically high when managing multi-pillar therapy and CKD progression risk).

## Evidence Summary

The ADA 2024 guidelines are heavily informed by landmark cardiorenal outcome trials:

*   **DAPA-CKD (Farxiga/dapagliflozin):** Included patients with and without diabetes (eGFR 25-75, UACR 200-5000). Showed a 39% relative risk reduction in the primary composite endpoint (sustained ≥50% eGFR decline, ESKD, or renal/CV death). NNT = 19 over 2.4 years.
*   **EMPA-KIDNEY (Jardiance/empagliflozin):** Included patients with eGFR 20-45 (regardless of UACR) or eGFR 45-90 (UACR ≥200). Showed a 28% reduction in kidney disease progression or CV death.
*   **CREDENCE (Invokana/canagliflozin):** T2D patients with eGFR 30-90 and UACR >300. Showed a 30% reduction in the primary composite renal endpoint.
*   **FIDELIO-DKD & FIGARO-DKD (Kerendia/finerenone):** T2D patients with CKD. Demonstrated significant reductions in CKD progression and CV events (especially heart failure hospitalizations) with a lower risk of hyperkalemia compared to steroidal MRAs (spironolactone).
*   **Limitations:** Most trials excluded patients with eGFR <20 at initiation, non-albuminuric advanced CKD, and Type 1 Diabetes. Evidence for SGLT2i and nsMRA in T1D remains limited due to DKA risks.

## What's New / Changed in 2024

1.  **Stricter Blood Pressure Targets:** The target was lowered from <140/90 to **<130/80 mmHg** for all patients with diabetes, aligning with KDIGO and ACC/AHA guidelines.
2.  **Heart Failure Screening:** New emphasis on screening for asymptomatic heart failure in patients with diabetes and CKD, given the high overlap.
3.  **Weight Management as Primary Goal:** Dual GIP/GLP-1 RAs (Mounjaro/tirzepatide) are now prominently featured for their profound weight loss benefits, which indirectly improve renal hemodynamics and glycemic control.
4.  **CKD Screening:** Stronger language mandating *both* eGFR and UACR for screening; missing UACR is highlighted as a major gap in primary care that nephrology must address.

### The "One-Minute" Summary for TKE Providers
1.  **Screen:** Check UACR and eGFR at every new consult and annually.
2.  **Block RAS:** Maximize ACEi/ARB first. Accept up to a 30% rise in serum creatinine.
3.  **Add SGLT2i:** If eGFR ≥20, start Farxiga or Jardiance. Do not stop if eGFR drops below 20; ride it out to dialysis.
4.  **Add nsMRA:** If UACR remains ≥30 and K+ is ≤5.0, add Kerendia.
5.  **Manage Weight/Risk:** Utilize GLP-1 RAs (Ozempic/Trulicity/Mounjaro) for obesity, ASCVD, and further albuminuria reduction.
6.  **Educate:** Teach SADMANS sick-day rules to prevent AKI.
