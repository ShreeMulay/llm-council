# Clinical Reference: MENTOR Trial & KDIGO 2021 Management of Primary Membranous Nephropathy

**Source Material:** MENTOR Trial (NEJM, 2019) integrated with KDIGO 2021 Clinical Practice Guideline for the Management of Glomerular Diseases
**Publication Date:** July 4, 2019 (MENTOR) / October 2021 (KDIGO)
**Relevant Domains:** gn_immunosuppression, glomerular_disease

---

## Overview

The MENTOR (Membranous Nephropathy Trial Of Rituximab) trial is a landmark study that fundamentally shifted the treatment paradigm for primary Membranous Nephropathy (pMN). Prior to MENTOR, calcineurin inhibitors (CNIs) like Neoral/Gengraf (cyclosporine) or alkylating agents (cyclophosphamide) were standard first-line therapies. MENTOR demonstrated that B-cell depletion with Rituxan (rituximab) is non-inferior to cyclosporine at 12 months and **superior** at 24 months for maintaining remission, largely due to the high relapse rate when CNIs are discontinued.

**Why it matters for nephrology practice:** 
This trial established Rituxan (rituximab) as a preferred, first-line, steroid-sparing immunosuppressive agent for moderate-to-high-risk primary MN. It provides a safer alternative to the modified Ponticelli regimen (cyclophosphamide/steroids) and a more durable alternative to CNIs.

**Target Population:** 
Adult patients with primary Membranous Nephropathy (typically PLA2R-positive) exhibiting nephrotic-range proteinuria (>3.5 g/day) despite 3-6 months of maximum tolerated conservative antiproteinuric therapy (ACEi/ARB, SGLT2i), with an eGFR generally >40 mL/min/1.73m².

## Key Recommendations

Based on the MENTOR trial protocol and subsequent KDIGO 2021 guidelines (Grade 1B evidence for Rituximab in moderate/high-risk MN).

### 1. Risk Stratification & When to Start Therapy
Do not start immunosuppression immediately upon diagnosis unless the patient is at high/very high risk of rapid progression.
*   **Low Risk:** Normal eGFR, proteinuria <3.5 g/d. *Recommendation:* Conservative care only.
*   **Moderate Risk:** Normal eGFR, proteinuria >3.5 g/d but <8.0 g/d, no decrease in eGFR over 6 months. *Recommendation:* Wait 3-6 months on conservative care. If proteinuria remains >3.5 g/d or PLA2R remains >50 RU/mL, initiate Rituxan (rituximab).
*   **High Risk:** eGFR <60 mL/min/1.73m², proteinuria >8.0 g/d, or PLA2R >50 RU/mL. *Recommendation:* Initiate Rituxan (rituximab) or cyclophosphamide immediately.
*   **Very High Risk:** Life-threatening nephrotic syndrome or rapid eGFR decline. *Recommendation:* Cyclophosphamide + steroids (Rituximab monotherapy acts too slowly for very high risk).

### 2. Rituxan (rituximab) Dosing & Administration (MENTOR Protocol)
*   **Initial Dosing:** Rituxan (rituximab) 1,000 mg IV on Day 1 and Day 15.
*   **Pre-medication:** Acetaminophen 650 mg PO, diphenhydramine 50 mg PO/IV, and methylprednisolone 100 mg IV 30-60 minutes prior to infusion.
*   **Month 6 Assessment & Escalation:** 
    *   If *Complete Remission* (Proteinuria <0.3 g/d): Stop therapy.
    *   If *Partial Remission* (Proteinuria reduced by >50% and is 0.3 - 3.5 g/d): Administer a second course of Rituxan (rituximab) 1,000 mg IV on Day 1 and Day 15.
    *   If *No Response* (Proteinuria reduction <25%): Declare treatment failure; switch to alternative therapy (e.g., cyclophosphamide or CNI).
*   **CKD Modifications:** No dose adjustment required for eGFR. However, efficacy is reduced, and infection risk is higher in advanced CKD (eGFR <30).

### 3. Neoral/Gengraf (cyclosporine) Dosing (Alternative/Comparator)
*   **Initial Dosing:** Neoral/Gengraf (cyclosporine) 3.5 mg/kg/day PO divided into two daily doses (BID).
*   **Target Trough:** 125 to 175 ng/mL (measured 12 hours post-dose).
*   **CKD Modifications:** 
    *   If serum creatinine increases by >30% from baseline, reduce dose by 50%.
    *   If creatinine does not return to within 30% of baseline within 2 weeks, discontinue cyclosporine.
*   **Duration:** Treat for 12 months, then taper slowly over 2-3 months to prevent relapse.

### 4. Lab Targets & Monitoring
*   **Immunologic Remission:** Anti-PLA2R antibody titer <2 RU/mL (often precedes clinical remission by 3-6 months).
*   **Clinical Complete Remission:** Urine Protein-Creatinine Ratio (UPCR) <0.3 g/g (or <300 mg/g) and stable serum albumin >3.5 g/dL.
*   **Clinical Partial Remission:** UPCR <3.5 g/g with a >50% reduction from baseline, stable eGFR.
*   **B-Cell Depletion Target:** CD19 count <5 cells/µL (measured 2-4 weeks post-infusion to confirm depletion).

## Practice Points

### Clinical Decision Tree
*   **Start:** Primary MN patient with UPCR > 3.5 g/g after 3-6 months of maximally tolerated lisinopril/losartan + Farxiga (dapagliflozin)/Jardiance (empagliflozin).
*   **Escalate/Repeat:** At 6 months, check UPCR and PLA2R. If PLA2R is negative but UPCR is 2.0 g/g (partial remission), give the second 6-month Rituxan (rituximab) course.
*   **Switch:** If at 6 months PLA2R is still highly elevated and UPCR has not improved, consider switching to cyclophosphamide or adding a CNI (combination therapy).
*   **Stop:** If complete remission is achieved at 6 months, do not give the 6-month Rituxan (rituximab) doses. Monitor quarterly.

### Practical Implementation Tips
*   **The "Lag" Phenomenon:** Educate patients that Rituxan (rituximab) takes time. Immunologic remission (PLA2R turning negative) happens at 3-6 months, but clinical remission (proteinuria dropping) may take 12-24 months as the damaged podocytes slowly heal. Do not prematurely declare treatment failure at 3 months.
*   **Infection Prophylaxis:** Consider Bactrim (sulfamethoxazole/trimethoprim) DS three times weekly for PCP prophylaxis during active B-cell depletion, especially if combined with any steroids.
*   **Vaccinations:** Administer pneumococcal, influenza, and COVID-19 vaccines at least 4 weeks *before* Rituxan (rituximab) infusion, as vaccine response will be blunted for 6+ months post-infusion.

### Safety Concerns & Contraindications
*   **Hepatitis B:** **Black Box Warning.** Rituxan (rituximab) can cause fatal HBV reactivation. Screen all patients for HBsAg and anti-HBc before administration.
*   **Hypogammaglobulinemia:** Monitor quantitative immunoglobulins (IgG). If IgG falls <400 mg/dL with recurrent infections, IVIG replacement may be necessary.
*   **CNI Nephrotoxicity:** If using Neoral (cyclosporine), monitor BP closely and check SCr every 2 weeks for the first 2 months.

### Patient Education Talking Points
*   *"We are using a medication called Rituxan to target the specific immune cells (B-cells) that are attacking your kidney filters."*
*   *"This is not a quick fix. It may take 6 to 12 months to see your protein levels drop, even if the medication is working perfectly."*
*   *"You will be at a higher risk for infections. Call the clinic immediately if you develop a fever, chills, or a severe cough."*

## Evidence Summary

**The MENTOR Trial (NEJM 2019)**
*   **Population:** 130 patients with primary MN, proteinuria >5 g/d, and eGFR >40.
*   **Intervention:** Rituximab (1g days 1 & 15, repeated at 6 months if partial response) vs. Cyclosporine (3.5 mg/kg/d for 12 months).
*   **Primary Endpoint:** Composite of complete or partial remission at 24 months.
*   **Key Results:** 
    *   At 12 months: Rituximab 60% vs. Cyclosporine 52% (Non-inferiority met).
    *   At 24 months: Rituximab 60% vs. Cyclosporine 20% (Superiority met, p<0.001).
    *   *Why the drop in Cyclosporine?* High relapse rate once the drug was stopped at 12 months.
*   **Safety:** Adverse events were significantly lower in the Rituximab group (17% vs 31%).
*   **Limitations:** Excluded patients with eGFR <40 mL/min/1.73m². Did not compare Rituximab to the cyclophosphamide (Ponticelli) regimen, which remains the gold standard for *very high-risk* patients.

## What's New / Changed

*   **Shift from CNIs:** MENTOR proved that while CNIs (cyclosporine/tacrolimus) reduce proteinuria quickly, they do not alter the underlying immunologic disease process in MN, leading to near-universal relapse upon withdrawal. Rituximab provides durable immunologic remission.
*   **PLA2R Monitoring:** The trial and subsequent KDIGO guidelines cemented the role of anti-PLA2R antibodies not just for diagnosis, but for monitoring treatment response and guiding the 6-month re-dosing decision.
*   **First-Line Status:** Rituximab is now formally recognized by KDIGO 2021 as a first-line agent for moderate and high-risk pMN.
