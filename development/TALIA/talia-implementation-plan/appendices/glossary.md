# TALIA Glossary

## Medical Terms, Abbreviations, and TALIA-Specific Terminology

---

## A

**A1C (Hemoglobin A1C)**
A blood test that shows average blood sugar over the past 2-3 months. Target for most diabetics: <7%.

**ACE Inhibitor (ACEi)**
Angiotensin-Converting Enzyme Inhibitor. Blood pressure medication that also protects kidneys. Examples: lisinopril, enalapril, ramipril.

**AKI (Acute Kidney Injury)**
Sudden decrease in kidney function, often reversible.

**ARB (Angiotensin Receptor Blocker)**
Blood pressure medication similar to ACEi. Examples: losartan, valsartan, olmesartan.

**ARNI (Angiotensin Receptor-Neprilysin Inhibitor)**
Heart failure medication. Example: sacubitril/valsartan (Entresto).

**AVF (Arteriovenous Fistula)**
Surgically created connection between artery and vein in the arm for hemodialysis access. Best type of access.

**AVG (Arteriovenous Graft)**
Synthetic tube connecting artery to vein for hemodialysis access.

---

## B

**Barista Model**
TALIA's care delivery philosophy where the patient sits in one location (like a coffee shop customer) and all services come to them.

**BMP (Basic Metabolic Panel)**
Blood test including electrolytes, kidney function, and glucose.

**BNP (B-type Natriuretic Peptide)**
Blood test for heart failure. Elevated levels suggest heart strain.

**BP (Blood Pressure)**
Force of blood against artery walls. Measured in mmHg (e.g., 120/80).

---

## C

**Card System**
TALIA's physical documentation system using color-coded cards for structured data capture.

**CKD (Chronic Kidney Disease)**
Long-term kidney damage or decreased function. Staged 1-5 based on GFR.

**CMP (Comprehensive Metabolic Panel)**
Extended blood test including BMP plus liver function.

**CPRA (Calculated Panel Reactive Antibody)**
Measure of antibodies that could reject a transplant. Higher = harder to find match.

**Creatinine**
Waste product filtered by kidneys. Higher levels indicate worse kidney function.

---

## D

**Dapagliflozin (Farxiga)**
SGLT2 inhibitor medication for kidney and heart protection.

**Dialysis**
Treatment that filters blood when kidneys can't. Types: hemodialysis, peritoneal dialysis.

**Dry Weight**
Target weight when patient has no excess fluid. Goal for heart failure management.

**Dyspnea**
Medical term for shortness of breath.

---

## E

**Edema**
Swelling caused by fluid accumulation in tissues.

**eGFR (Estimated Glomerular Filtration Rate)**
Measure of kidney function. Normal: >90 mL/min/1.73m².

**Empagliflozin (Jardiance)**
SGLT2 inhibitor medication for kidney and heart protection.

**EPO (Erythropoietin)**
Hormone made by kidneys that stimulates red blood cell production.

**ESA (Erythropoiesis-Stimulating Agent)**
Medication replacing EPO. Examples: Epogen, Procrit, Aranesp.

**ESKD/ESRD (End-Stage Kidney/Renal Disease)**
Kidney failure requiring dialysis or transplant. GFR <15.

---

## F

**Ferritin**
Blood test for iron stores. Target in CKD: 100-500 ng/mL.

**Finerenone (Kerendia)**
Non-steroidal MRA for diabetic kidney disease.

**Fistula**
See AVF.

---

## G

**GDMT (Guideline-Directed Medical Therapy)**
Evidence-based medications proven to improve outcomes. For CKD: ACEi/ARB, SGLT2i, MRA.

**GFR (Glomerular Filtration Rate)**
Rate at which kidneys filter blood. Primary measure of kidney function.

**GLP-1 (Glucagon-Like Peptide-1) Agonist**
Diabetes/weight loss medication. Examples: Ozempic, Trulicity, Mounjaro.

**Gout**
Painful joint condition caused by high uric acid crystals.

---

## H

**HD (Hemodialysis)**
Dialysis using a machine to filter blood outside the body.

**Hgb (Hemoglobin)**
Protein in red blood cells carrying oxygen. Target in CKD anemia: 10-11 g/dL.

**HF (Heart Failure)**
Heart can't pump blood effectively. Types: HFrEF, HFmrEF, HFpEF.

**HFpEF**
Heart Failure with Preserved Ejection Fraction (EF ≥50%).

**HFrEF**
Heart Failure with Reduced Ejection Fraction (EF <40%).

**HTN (Hypertension)**
High blood pressure.

**Hyperkalemia**
High potassium level (>5.5 mEq/L).

**Hypokalemia**
Low potassium level (<3.5 mEq/L).

---

## I

**Intervention Card**
TALIA's orange card documenting provider decisions and treatment plan.

**IV (Intravenous)**
Administered directly into a vein.

---

## J-K

**JVP (Jugular Venous Pressure)**
Visible neck vein distension indicating fluid overload.

**K+ (Potassium)**
Electrolyte critical for heart function. Normal: 3.5-5.0 mEq/L.

**Krystexxa (Pegloticase)**
IV medication for refractory gout that breaks down uric acid.

---

## L-M

**MA (Medical Assistant)**
In TALIA: "Care Flow Specialist" responsible for Stations 1, 2, and 5.

**Measurement Card**
TALIA's blue card for vital signs and objective measurements.

**MRA (Mineralocorticoid Receptor Antagonist)**
Medication class including spironolactone and finerenone.

**MRN (Medical Record Number)**
Patient's unique identifier in the healthcare system.

**Module Card**
TALIA's condition-specific cards activated based on patient diagnoses.

---

## N

**NSAID (Non-Steroidal Anti-Inflammatory Drug)**
Pain medication that can harm kidneys. Examples: ibuprofen (Advil), naproxen (Aleve).

**NYHA Class**
New York Heart Association functional classification for heart failure (I-IV).

---

## O-P

**Orthopnea**
Shortness of breath when lying flat.

**Patient Summary Card**
TALIA's purple card given to patient to take home.

**PD (Peritoneal Dialysis)**
Dialysis using the lining of the abdomen to filter blood.

**Pitting Edema**
Swelling that leaves an indentation when pressed.

**PND (Paroxysmal Nocturnal Dyspnea)**
Waking up suddenly at night gasping for breath.

**Pod**
Patient station in TALIA model where patient remains for entire visit.

**Proteinuria**
Protein in urine, indicating kidney damage.

---

## Q-R

**QD**
Once daily (medication dosing).

**QoL (Quality of Life)**
Patient's overall well-being and satisfaction.

**RAS (Renal Artery Stenosis)**
Narrowing of arteries supplying kidneys.

---

## S

**Scribe**
In TALIA: "Intelligence Preparer" responsible for Station 3 and 4 documentation.

**SGLT2i (Sodium-Glucose Cotransporter-2 Inhibitor)**
Medication class providing major kidney protection. Examples: Jardiance, Farxiga.

**SOB (Shortness of Breath)**
Difficulty breathing.

**Spironolactone (Aldactone)**
MRA medication used for heart failure and kidney protection.

**Station**
One of five workflow points in TALIA visit model.

---

## T

**TALIA**
The Application Layer Intelligence Artificial. The Kidney Experts' care delivery system.

**TSAT (Transferrin Saturation)**
Blood test measuring iron availability. Target in CKD: 20-30%.

**Teach-Back**
Education technique where patient repeats information in their own words.

**Tophi**
Hard uric acid crystal deposits under skin in chronic gout.

---

## U-V

**UA (Urinalysis)**
Urine test for various substances.

**ULT (Urate-Lowering Therapy)**
Medications that reduce uric acid. Examples: allopurinol, febuxostat.

**Uric Acid**
Waste product causing gout. Target: <6.0 mg/dL.

**Uremia**
Symptoms of kidney failure (nausea, confusion, itching, etc.).

---

## W-Z

**95% Rule**
TALIA principle that 95% of care is delivered before provider arrives.

---

## CKD Stage Reference

| Stage | GFR (mL/min/1.73m²) | Description |
|-------|---------------------|-------------|
| 1 | ≥ 90 | Normal or high, with kidney damage markers |
| 2 | 60-89 | Mildly decreased |
| 3a | 45-59 | Mildly to moderately decreased |
| 3b | 30-44 | Moderately to severely decreased |
| 4 | 15-29 | Severely decreased |
| 5 | < 15 | Kidney failure |
