# Patient Communication Scripts

## Standard Scripts for TALIA Care Model

---

## Section 1: Arrival & Check-In Scripts

### Front Desk Greeting

**Standard Greeting:**
> "Welcome to The Kidney Experts! May I have your name and date of birth?"

**After Verification:**
> "Great, thank you [Name]. Please step on the scale right over there, and then have a seat in Pod [Number]. [MA Name] will be with you in just a moment."

**New Patient Welcome:**
> "Welcome to The Kidney Experts! We're glad you're here. I have a few forms for you to complete. Once you're done, please step on the scale and have a seat in Pod [Number]. Our team will take good care of you."

### Explaining the Pod Model (if patient asks)

> "We do things a little differently here. Instead of moving from room to room, you'll sit in one comfortable spot and our team will come to you. First our medical assistant, then our scribe and doctor, and finally our medical assistant again to go over everything. It's like a coffee shop - you sit, we serve!"

---

## Section 2: Station 1 Scripts (Vitals)

### Entering the Pod

> "Good [morning/afternoon], [Name]! I'm [Your Name] and I'll be taking care of you today. How are you doing?"

### Confirming Weight

> "I see you weighed [X] pounds at the scale. Is that right?"

**If weight changed significantly:**
> "I notice your weight has [gone up/gone down] by [X] pounds since your last visit. Have you noticed any changes? Any swelling or feeling bloated?"

### Blood Pressure

**Before Taking:**
> "I'm going to check your blood pressure now. Please sit quietly and try not to talk for a moment."

**If BP is High:**
> "Your blood pressure is a bit higher than we'd like today. Sometimes it's higher at the doctor's office than at home. Do you check it at home? We'll definitely discuss this with the doctor."

**If BP is Very High (>180/110):**
> "Your blood pressure is quite elevated today. I'm going to let the doctor know right away. Are you having any headache, chest pain, or blurry vision?"

### Edema Check

> "Now I'm going to check your legs and ankles for any swelling. May I take a look?"

**If Edema Present:**
> "I'm noticing some swelling here. Have you seen this? Is it worse than usual?"

### Transitioning to Station 2

> "Great, your vital signs are done. Now I'm going to ask you some questions about how you've been doing since your last visit."

---

## Section 3: Station 2 Scripts (History)

### Chief Concern

> "What brings you in today? What's the main thing you want to talk about with the doctor?"

**If Patient Says "Just a Follow-Up":**
> "Got it. Any particular concerns or symptoms you've noticed? Anything new or different?"

### Interval History

**Hospitalizations:**
> "Have you been in the hospital since we saw you last?"

**If Yes:**
> "I'm sorry to hear that. Can you tell me which hospital, what happened, and how long you were there?"

**ER Visits:**
> "Have you been to the emergency room or urgent care?"

**Medications:**
> "Let's go through your medications. Are you taking all of them as prescribed?"

**If Non-Adherent:**
> "I understand - sometimes medications can be tricky. What's getting in the way? Is it cost, side effects, or just hard to remember?"

### Symptom Review

> "I'm going to ask about some common symptoms. Just tell me yes or no, and we'll talk more about any that bother you."

**For Each Symptom:**
> "Any [fatigue/shortness of breath/swelling/nausea/itching/etc.]?"

**If Yes:**
> "How bad is it on a scale of 0 to 10? When did it start? Is it getting better or worse?"

### Transitioning to Wait for Provider

> "Thank you for all that information. The doctor will be in shortly to see you. Do you have any questions while we wait?"

---

## Section 4: Station 4 Scripts (Provider Encounter)

### Provider Greeting

> "Hello [Name], I'm Dr. [Name]. I've reviewed your information and I'd like to talk about a few things today."

### Explaining Test Results

**Lab Results (General):**
> "I have your lab results here. Let me go through the important ones with you."

**GFR/Kidney Function:**
> "Your kidney function number, called GFR, is [X]. This tells us how well your kidneys are filtering. [Normal is above 90 / Your stage is X, which means...]"

**If GFR Declined:**
> "Your kidney function has gone down a bit since last time. This is something we want to slow down as much as possible. The good news is we have medications that can really help protect your kidneys."

### Starting New Medications

**SGLT2 Inhibitor:**
> "I'd like to start you on a medication called [Jardiance/Farxiga]. It's been shown to protect your kidneys and slow down kidney disease. It may make you urinate a bit more, and it's important to stay hydrated and keep the genital area clean. But the kidney protection it provides is significant."

**ACE Inhibitor/ARB:**
> "I'm starting you on a blood pressure medication that also protects your kidneys. It's called [medication name]. It helps by reducing the pressure inside your kidneys."

### Discussing Difficult Topics

**Dialysis Preparation:**
> "Your kidney function is at a point where we need to start planning ahead. This doesn't mean you need dialysis now, but I want to make sure you have choices when the time comes. Let's talk about your options."

**Transplant Referral:**
> "Based on your kidney function, I'd like to refer you for a transplant evaluation. A transplant is the best treatment for kidney failure - better than dialysis. You don't have to be on dialysis first. Would you be interested in learning more?"

### Closing the Encounter

> "Do you have any questions for me? [MA Name] will come back to go over everything we talked about and give you a summary to take home."

## Section 5: Station 5 Scripts (Education)

### Returning to Pod

> "Hi [Name], I'm back to go over everything with you before you leave."

### Reviewing the Summary Card

> "I have a summary card here with all the important information from your visit. Let me go through it with you."

**Your Numbers:**
> "Your blood pressure today was [X/Y]. Your goal is below 130/80. Your weight was [X] pounds. And your kidney function number, your GFR, is [X], which means you're at stage [X]."

**What We Did:**
> "Today we [reviewed your kidney health / adjusted your medications / ordered some labs / etc.]."

**New Medications:**
> "You're starting a new medication called [name]. It [what it does]. Take it [when and how]. You might notice [common side effects], but [reassurance]."

### Action Items

> "Here are the things I need you to do:
> 1. [First action item]
> 2. [Second action item]
> 3. [Third action item]
> Can you tell me those back to me?"

### When to Call

> "If you have any of these warning signs, I want you to call us right away: [specific symptoms based on condition]. Our number is right here on the card: 731-300-6155."

### Scheduling Follow-Up

> "The doctor wants to see you back in [timeframe]. Let me schedule that now... Your next appointment is [date and time]. If labs are needed, go [X days] before your appointment."

### Closing

> "This card is yours to take home. It has everything we talked about. Do you have any questions before you go?"

> "Take care, and we'll see you on [next appointment date]!"

## Section 6: Difficult Conversation Scripts

### Patient Upset About Wait

> "I'm really sorry for the wait. I know your time is valuable. We're doing everything we can to see you as quickly as possible. Thank you for your patience."

### Patient Refusing Recommended Treatment

> "I understand this is a lot to think about. Can you tell me more about your concerns? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make the best decision for yourself."

**If Still Refusing:**
> "I respect your decision. Let's make sure to talk about this again at your next visit. In the meantime, please call us if you change your mind or have questions."

### Breaking Bad News (GFR Declined Significantly)

> "I need to share something important with you. Your kidney function has changed since your last visit. It's now at [X], which is lower than before. I know this can be concerning. Let's talk about what this means and what we can do."

### Discussing End of Life/Conservative Care

> "For some patients, dialysis may not be the right choice. It's okay to decide not to do dialysis. What's most important is that you understand your options and that we respect your wishes. Would you like to talk about what matters most to you?"

## Section 7: TALIA-Specific Explanations

### Explaining Why We Use Cards

**If Patient Asks:**
> "These cards help us make sure we don't miss anything. Every patient gets the same thorough care. And you'll take one home with all your information, so you don't have to remember everything yourself."

### Explaining the Team Approach

> "You'll notice we work as a team here. Our medical assistants gather your information and provide education. Our scribes help the doctor with documentation so the doctor can focus entirely on you. And the doctor makes all the medical decisions. Everyone has a role, and it's all focused on taking the best care of you."

### Explaining Why They Stay in One Spot

> "We designed our clinic so you don't have to keep moving. You stay right here, and we come to you. It's more comfortable and more efficient. You're the VIP!"

## Tips for Effective Communication

1. **Use the patient's name** - It personalizes the interaction
2. **Speak slowly and clearly** - Medical information is complex
3. **Use plain language** - Avoid jargon
4. **Pause for questions** - Give them time to process
5. **Use teach-back** - "Can you tell me in your own words..."
6. **Show empathy** - "I understand this can be overwhelming"
7. **Be honest** - Even when news is difficult
8. **End positively** - Focus on what we CAN do
