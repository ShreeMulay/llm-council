# Scribe Training Guide

## TALIA Intelligence Preparer Training

**Role Title:** Intelligence Preparer (Scribe)  
**Training Duration:** 2 weeks (40 hours)  
**Certification Required:** Complete all modules + competency demonstration

---

## Welcome to TALIA

As an Intelligence Preparer, you transform raw data into actionable intelligence. You do the cognitive work of chart review so providers can focus on patient care and decision-making.

**Your Mission:** When the provider walks into the pod, they should have everything they need to make decisions. No hunting through charts. No surprises. Complete context.

---

## Your Stations

| Station | Time | Your Role | Cards Used |
|---------|------|-----------|------------|
| **Station 2** | Min 8-15 | Support MA, begin chart prep | Assessment Card (assist) |
| **Station 3** | Min 16-20 | Prepare provider briefing | Briefing preparation |
| **Station 4** | Min 21-32 | Real-time documentation | Intervention Card (Orange) |

## Module 1: Chart Review & Context Preparation

### Before Patient Arrives

**Review in this order:**
1. **Problem List** - Active diagnoses, CKD stage
2. **Recent Labs** - Creatinine, GFR, potassium, hemoglobin
3. **Medication List** - Current meds, recent changes
4. **Last Visit Note** - What was the plan?
5. **Recent Events** - Hospital discharges, consult notes

### What to Extract

| Category | Look For | Note For Provider |
|----------|----------|-------------------|
| **Kidney Function** | GFR trend (up/down/stable) | "GFR 28→25 over 6 months" |
| **Labs Due** | What needs to be ordered | "CMP due, last 3 months ago" |
| **GDMT Status** | ACEi/ARB, SGLT2i, MRA | "Not on SGLT2i, GFR 35" |
| **Recent Events** | Hospitalizations, ER visits | "Admitted 2 weeks ago for HF" |
| **Pending Items** | Referrals, tests ordered | "Transplant eval pending" |

### Creating the Provider Briefing

**Format:**
```
PATIENT: [Name], [Age] [Sex]
CKD Stage: [X] | GFR: [XX] (trend: ↑/↓/→)

KEY ISSUES:
1. [Most important issue]
2. [Second issue]
3. [Third issue]

RECENT EVENTS:
• [Hospital/ER visits]
• [Significant changes]

GDMT STATUS:
• ACEi/ARB: [On/Off/Contraindicated]
• SGLT2i: [On/Off/Opportunity]
• MRA: [On/Off/K+ status]

LABS: [Due/Recent key values]

TODAY'S AGENDA: [Visit type, patient concerns]
```

## Module 2: Station 3 - Intelligence Preparation

**Time Budget:** 5 minutes (Minutes 16-20)

### Your Tasks

1. **Review MA's cards** - Measurement and Assessment complete?
2. **Pull relevant data** - Labs, trends, prior decisions
3. **Prepare briefing** - Verbal summary ready for provider
4. **Identify decision points** - What does provider need to decide?
5. **Stage Intervention Card** - Have it ready with patient info

### Pre-Populated Information

Before provider arrives, have ready:
- Patient name, DOB, MRN on Intervention Card
- CKD stage identified
- Current GDMT status noted
- Relevant Module Cards activated

### The 30-Second Briefing

When provider approaches, deliver concise briefing:

**Template:**
> "[Name] is a [age]-year-old with CKD stage [X], GFR [XX]. Here for [visit type]. Key issues today: [1], [2], [3]. GDMT: [status]. [Any urgent concerns]."

**Example:**
> "Mrs. Johnson is a 67-year-old with CKD stage 4, GFR 22. Here for routine follow-up. Key issues: blood pressure not at goal (152/88 today), not on SGLT2i, and pre-dialysis planning needed. She was hospitalized 2 weeks ago for heart failure exacerbation."

## Module 3: Station 4 - Real-Time Documentation

**Time Budget:** 12 minutes (Minutes 21-32)

### Your Role During Provider Encounter

1. **Listen actively** - Capture key decisions
2. **Document in real-time** - Fill Intervention Card as provider speaks
3. **Clarify if needed** - "Did you want to start Farxiga 10mg?"
4. **Track GDMT** - Ensure all three classes addressed
5. **Capture discussion points** - What patient needs to know

### Intervention Card Sections

**Assessment Section:**
- Primary diagnosis (be specific: "CKD Stage 4 secondary to diabetic nephropathy")
- CKD Stage (circle correct one)
- Secondary diagnoses (up to 3)

**GDMT Status:**
For each medication class, document:
- Current: On or Off
- Action: Continue, Start, Increase, Hold, or Contraindicated

| Class | Document |
|-------|----------|
| ACEi/ARB | Drug name, dose, action taken |
| SGLT2i | Drug name (Jardiance/Farxiga), dose, action |
| MRA | Drug name (spironolactone/finerenone), dose, action |

**Medication Changes:**
- STARTED: New medications with dose
- STOPPED: Discontinued medications with reason
- DOSE CHANGED: Drug, old dose → new dose

**Labs Ordered:**
- Check all applicable boxes
- Add timing (today vs. before next visit)

**Referrals:**
- Transplant center (specify which one)
- Vascular access
- Dietitian
- Other specialists

**Follow-up:**
- Time interval selected
- Any special instructions

**Discussion Points:**
- 3 key points for Patient Summary Card
- Write in PLAIN LANGUAGE (patient will see this)

### Documentation Standards

**Be Specific:**
- ❌ "Blood pressure medication"
- ✓ "Lisinopril 20mg daily"

**Be Complete:**
- ❌ "Labs ordered"
- ✓ "CMP, CBC ordered, to be done 3 days before next visit"

**Be Clear:**
- ❌ "Discussed GDMT"
- ✓ "Started Farxiga 10mg daily for kidney protection"

### Getting Provider Signature

Before provider leaves Station 4:
1. Review card briefly with provider
2. Confirm medication changes are correct
3. Obtain signature and date
4. Add your initials

## Module 4: GDMT Knowledge

### Why GDMT Matters

Guideline-Directed Medical Therapy (GDMT) for CKD includes three medication classes that slow kidney disease progression:

| Class | Example Drugs | Kidney Benefit |
|-------|---------------|----------------|
| ACEi/ARB | Lisinopril, Losartan | Reduces proteinuria, slows progression |
| SGLT2i | Jardiance, Farxiga | Major kidney protection, even without diabetes |
| MRA | Spironolactone, Finerenone | Additional protection, especially with diabetes |

### SGLT2i - The Big Opportunity

SGLT2 inhibitors are the biggest advance in CKD treatment in decades. Your job is to ensure the provider addresses this every visit.

**Eligible Patients:**
- GFR ≥ 20 to initiate
- Can continue if started at higher GFR
- With or without diabetes

**Red Flags if NOT on SGLT2i:**
- GFR > 20 and no documented contraindication
- Make sure provider addresses this!

### Contraindications to Know

| Medication | Contraindications |
|------------|-------------------|
| ACEi/ARB | Bilateral renal artery stenosis, angioedema history, pregnancy, K+ > 5.5 |
| SGLT2i | Type 1 diabetes (relative), frequent UTI, DKA history |
| MRA | K+ ≥ 5.0, severe renal impairment |

## Module 5: Medical Terminology

### Common Abbreviations

| Abbreviation | Meaning |
|--------------|---------|
| CKD | Chronic Kidney Disease |
| GFR/eGFR | (Estimated) Glomerular Filtration Rate |
| ESRD/ESKD | End-Stage Renal/Kidney Disease |
| ACEi | ACE Inhibitor |
| ARB | Angiotensin Receptor Blocker |
| SGLT2i | Sodium-Glucose Cotransporter-2 Inhibitor |
| MRA | Mineralocorticoid Receptor Antagonist |
| HD | Hemodialysis |
| PD | Peritoneal Dialysis |
| AVF | Arteriovenous Fistula |
| AVG | Arteriovenous Graft |
| BNP | B-type Natriuretic Peptide |
| HF | Heart Failure |
| HFrEF | Heart Failure with Reduced Ejection Fraction |
| DM | Diabetes Mellitus |
| HTN | Hypertension |
| SOB | Shortness of Breath |

### Lab Values to Know

| Lab | Normal Range | CKD Targets |
|-----|--------------|-------------|
| Creatinine | 0.6-1.2 mg/dL | Varies (trend matters more) |
| GFR | > 90 mL/min | Slow the decline |
| Potassium | 3.5-5.0 mEq/L | < 5.5 (watch with ACEi/MRA) |
| Hemoglobin | 12-16 g/dL | 10-11 g/dL target |
| A1C | < 5.7% | < 7-8% (varies by patient) |
| Uric Acid | 2.5-7.0 mg/dL | < 6.0 mg/dL for gout |

## Module 6: Workflow Integration

### Timing Coordination

```
0:00-0:07   Patient arrives, Station 1 (MA)
            → You: Chart review, briefing prep

0:08-0:15   Station 2 (MA + you assist)
            → You: Verify history, support data gathering

0:16-0:20   Station 3 (You alone)
            → Complete briefing prep, stage Intervention Card

0:21        Provider arrives
            → Deliver 30-second briefing

0:21-0:32   Station 4 (Provider + You)
            → Real-time documentation on Intervention Card

0:32        Provider leaves
            → Finalize card, ensure signature
            → Hand off Discussion Points to MA for Station 5
```

### Communication with MA

**Before Station 4:**
- Confirm Assessment Card is complete
- Verify Module Cards are pulled
- Any urgent findings?

**After Station 4:**
- Share Discussion Points with MA
- Highlight key education needs
- Any special instructions for patient?

### Communication with Provider

**Do:**
- Be concise (30-second briefing)
- Highlight decision points
- Clarify orders in real-time
- Ask if you missed anything before they sign

**Don't:**
- Interrupt during patient conversation (unless critical)
- Make clinical recommendations
- Fill in information you're not sure about

## Module 8: Common Errors to Avoid

| Error | Why It's Bad | How to Avoid |
|-------|--------------|--------------|
| Vague documentation | Can't verify what was done | Be specific - drug names, doses |
| Missing GDMT status | Missed opportunity | Address all 3 classes every visit |
| Illegible handwriting | Can't be scanned, errors occur | Print clearly |
| No provider signature | Incomplete documentation | Get signature before they leave |
| Generic discussion points | Patient doesn't understand | Write in plain language |
| Forgetting to hand off | MA doesn't know the plan | Always communicate before Station 5 |
