# Medical Assistant (MA) Training Guide

## TALIA Care Flow Specialist Training

**Role Title:** Care Flow Specialist (Medical Assistant)  
**Training Duration:** 2 weeks (40 hours)  
**Certification Required:** Complete all modules + competency demonstration

---

## Welcome to TALIA

You are the most important person in the TALIA system. As a Care Flow Specialist, you are the conductor of the patient's visit - ensuring smooth flow, complete data capture, and excellent patient experience.

**The 95% Rule:** 95% of care is delivered by YOU before the provider ever enters the pod. You are not "just taking vitals" - you are delivering care.

**The Barista Model:** Like a coffee shop where customers sit and everything comes to them, our patients sit in ONE pod and you bring all services to them.

## Module 1: The Card System

### Why Cards?

Cards are NOT just forms. Cards ARE the documentation. They ensure:
- **Nothing gets missed** - every field is a prompt
- **Consistency** - every patient gets the same thoroughness
- **Efficiency** - you don't have to think about what to ask
- **Education** - teaching is built into every card back

### Card Handling

1. **Before patient arrives:**
   - Pull Core Cards (Blue, Green, Orange, Purple)
   - Check schedule for known conditions
   - Pre-pull Module Cards based on problem list

2. **During visit:**
   - Fill in cards completely - no blank fields
   - Use checkboxes (circles ○) correctly
   - Write legibly - cards may be scanned

3. **After visit:**
   - Patient Summary Card goes HOME with patient
   - Other cards go to scanning station
   - Never throw away a card - even if mistakes

### Card Colors - Know Them Instantly

| Color | Card | Your Role |
|-------|------|-----------|
| 🔵 Blue | Measurement | You complete at Station 1 |
| 🟢 Green | Assessment | You complete at Station 2 |
| 🟠 Orange | Intervention | Scribe completes (you review) |
| 🟣 Purple | Patient Summary | You complete at Station 5 |

## Module 2: Station 1 - Vitals & Measurements

**Time Budget:** 4 minutes (Minutes 3-7)

### Sequence

1. **Greet patient** in pod (they've already weighed at self-service station)
2. **Confirm weight** - transcribe from self-service or re-weigh if needed
3. **Calculate weight change** from last visit
4. **Blood pressure** - proper technique (see below)
5. **Heart rate and rhythm** - note if regular or irregular
6. **Edema assessment** - look AND press
7. **Complete Measurement Card**
8. **Flag any urgent values**

### Blood Pressure Technique (CRITICAL)

**Preparation:**
- Patient seated for 5 minutes (can be while you gather supplies)
- Feet flat on floor, uncrossed
- Back supported
- Arm supported at heart level
- No talking during measurement

**Cuff Placement:**
- Correct size (bladder encircles 80% of arm)
- Lower edge 2-3 cm above elbow crease
- Artery marker over brachial artery

**Common Errors to Avoid:**
| Error | Impact | Correction |
|-------|--------|------------|
| Cuff too small | Falsely HIGH reading | Use larger cuff |
| Cuff too large | Falsely LOW reading | Use smaller cuff |
| Arm unsupported | Falsely HIGH (+10 mmHg) | Rest arm on table |
| Talking during | Falsely HIGH (+10 mmHg) | Silence during reading |
| Full bladder | Falsely HIGH (+15 mmHg) | Offer bathroom first |
| Legs crossed | Falsely HIGH (+8 mmHg) | Feet flat, uncrossed |

### Edema Assessment

**Visual Check:**
- Compare ankles to each other
- Look for sock marks
- Check if shoes feel tight (ask patient)

**Pitting Test:**
- Press firmly on shin (not ankle) for 5 seconds
- Release and observe
- Grade the depth of the pit:

| Grade | Depth | Recovery Time |
|-------|-------|---------------|
| Trace | Barely visible | Immediate |
| 1+ | 2mm | < 10 seconds |
| 2+ | 4mm | 10-15 seconds |
| 3+ | 6mm | 15-30 seconds |
| 4+ | 8mm+ | > 30 seconds |

**Check Multiple Locations:**
- Ankles (most common)
- Shins
- Sacral area (if bedridden)
- Periorbital (around eyes - morning)

### Urgent Values - FLAG IMMEDIATELY

| Finding | Action |
|---------|--------|
| BP > 180/110 | Notify provider IMMEDIATELY |
| BP < 90/60 | Notify provider IMMEDIATELY |
| Weight gain > 5 lbs | Flag on card, alert provider |
| New/worsening edema | Flag on card, alert provider |
| Heart rate > 120 or < 50 | Notify provider |

## Module 3: Station 2 - Data Gathering

**Time Budget:** 7 minutes (Minutes 8-15)

### Sequence

1. **Verify visit type** - Follow-up? New problem? Urgent?
2. **Chief concern** - Use patient's exact words
3. **Interval history** - What happened since last visit?
4. **Symptom review** - Go through checklist systematically
5. **Medication review** - Changes, adherence, concerns
6. **Activate modules** - Select based on conditions
7. **Complete Assessment Card**

### Asking the Right Questions

**Chief Concern:**
- "What brings you in today?"
- "What's the main thing you want us to address?"
- Write their EXACT words - don't interpret

**Interval History:**
- "Have you been to the hospital since we saw you last?"
- "Have you been to the ER or urgent care?"
- "Any new diagnoses or health issues?"
- "Any surgeries or procedures?"

**Symptoms - Don't Just Read the List:**
| Symptom | How to Ask |
|---------|------------|
| Fatigue | "How's your energy level? Scale of 0-10?" |
| SOB | "Do you get short of breath? With what activity?" |
| Swelling | "Any swelling in your legs or ankles?" |
| Nausea | "Any nausea or vomiting? Loss of appetite?" |
| Itching | "Any itching? Where? How bad?" |
| Sleep | "How are you sleeping? Any problems?" |

**Medication Reconciliation:**
- "Are you taking all your medications as prescribed?"
- "Any new medications from other doctors?"
- "Did you stop or change any medications?"
- "Any trouble affording your medications?"
- "Any side effects bothering you?"

### Activating Module Cards

Based on patient conditions, pull the appropriate Module Cards:

| Condition | Module Card | Color |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Takes NSAIDs | NSAIDs Module | Coral |
| Has gout | Gout/Krystexxa Module | Burgundy |
| Diabetic | Diabetes Module | Yellow |
| Heart failure | Heart Failure Module | Red |
| BP not at goal | BP Control Module | Dark Blue |
| Anemia (Hgb < 10) | Anemia Module | Pink |
| GFR < 20 | Pre-Dialysis Module | Navy |
| GFR < 25 | Transplant Module | Teal |

## Module 4: Station 5 - Wrap-Up & Education

**Time Budget:** 5 minutes (Minutes 33-38)

### Sequence

1. **Return to pod** after provider leaves
2. **Review Intervention Card** - understand what was decided
3. **Complete Patient Summary Card** - translate to plain language
4. **Deliver education** - use card backs as guides
5. **Confirm understanding** - teach-back method
6. **Schedule follow-up** - before patient leaves
7. **Send patient home** with Summary Card

### Translating Medical to Plain Language

| Medical Term | Plain Language |
|--------------|----------------|
| ACEi/ARB | Blood pressure medicine that also protects your kidneys |
| SGLT2 inhibitor | Medicine that protects your kidneys and lowers blood sugar |
| Diuretic | Water pill |
| Edema | Swelling |
| GFR | Kidney function number |
| CKD | Chronic kidney disease |
| Creatinine | Waste product that shows kidney function |

### The Teach-Back Method

Don't ask: "Do you understand?"
Instead ask: "Can you tell me in your own words..."

**Examples:**
- "Can you tell me what your 3 action items are?"
- "Can you explain when you should call us?"
- "What will you do differently with your medications?"

If patient can't explain back correctly, re-teach and try again.

### Education Topics by Module

| Module | Key Education Points |
|--------|---------------------|
| NSAIDs | Why they hurt kidneys, safe alternatives |
| Gout | Uric acid goal, dietary triggers, medication importance |
| Diabetes | A1C goal, SGLT2i benefits, sick day rules |
| Heart Failure | Daily weights, fluid restriction, when to call |
| BP Control | Goal BP, home monitoring, medication timing |
| Anemia | Why it happens, iron therapy, symptoms to watch |
| Pre-Dialysis | Modality options, access protection, preparation |
| Transplant | Benefits, evaluation process, living donor |

## Module 5: Communication Skills

### Creating a Positive Experience

**First Impressions:**
- Make eye contact
- Use patient's name
- Warm, genuine greeting
- Explain what you're doing and why

**During the Visit:**
- Position yourself at patient's level (don't stand over them)
- Listen more than you talk
- Acknowledge concerns before moving on
- Never rush - even if you're busy

**Non-Verbal Communication:**
- Open body posture
- Nodding to show understanding
- Appropriate facial expressions
- Don't look at clock or appear hurried

### Handling Difficult Situations

**Patient is upset:**
- Acknowledge their feelings: "I can see you're frustrated"
- Listen fully before responding
- Don't get defensive
- Escalate to provider if needed

**Patient doesn't want to answer questions:**
- Explain why the information matters
- "This helps us take better care of your kidneys"
- Respect their choice but document refusal

**Patient has cognitive issues:**
- Speak slowly and clearly
- Involve family/caregiver
- Write things down
- Be patient - repeat as needed

## Module 7: Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It's Bad | How to Avoid |
|---------|--------------|--------------|
| Skipping BP technique | Inaccurate readings lead to wrong treatment | Follow protocol every time |
| Writing your interpretation instead of patient's words | Loses important nuance | Quote exactly |
| Not asking about OTC meds | Missing NSAIDs, supplements | Ask specifically about "anything you buy at the store" |
| Rushing education | Patient doesn't understand | Use teach-back |
| Forgetting to schedule follow-up | Patient doesn't return | Make appointment before wrap-up |
| Not pulling Module Cards | Missing important assessments | Check problem list every visit |
| Leaving blank fields | Incomplete documentation | Every field must be completed |
