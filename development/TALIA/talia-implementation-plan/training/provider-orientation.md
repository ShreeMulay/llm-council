# Provider Orientation Guide

## TALIA System Overview for Providers

**Document Purpose:** Orient providers to the TALIA care delivery model  
**Time to Review:** 30 minutes  
**Key Concept:** You focus on decisions; the team handles everything else

---

## The TALIA Philosophy

### The 95% Rule

When you walk into a patient's pod, 95% of the work is already done:
- Vitals captured and flagged
- History gathered and documented
- Labs reviewed and trends identified
- GDMT status prepared
- Decision points identified

**Your job:** Make decisions, connect with the patient, and move on.

### The Barista Model

Patients sit in ONE location (their pod) for the entire visit. Everything comes to them:
- MA comes for vitals
- MA comes for history
- You come for the encounter
- MA comes for education and wrap-up

No waiting rooms. No wandering. No wasted time.

## Your Workflow

### Arriving at the Pod

1. **Receive briefing** from Scribe (30 seconds)
   - Patient summary
   - Key issues
   - GDMT status
   - Decision points

2. **Review cards briefly**
   - Measurement Card: Any flags?
   - Assessment Card: Patient concerns?
   - Module Cards: What needs addressing?

### During the Encounter

1. **Greet patient** - They've been waiting; acknowledge them
2. **Address chief concern** - What brought them in?
3. **Examine as needed** - Cards have objective data; add your clinical findings
4. **Make decisions** - Verbalize clearly for Scribe
5. **Explain key points** - What patient needs to understand

### Documenting Through the Scribe

**Speak your decisions clearly:**

❌ "Let's adjust the medications"  
✓ "Start Farxiga 10mg daily, continue lisinopril 20mg"

❌ "Order the usual labs"  
✓ "CMP and CBC, do labs 3 days before next visit"

❌ "See them back"  
✓ "Follow up in 2 months"

### The GDMT Checklist

**Address all three classes every visit:**

1. **ACEi/ARB**
   - On max tolerated dose?
   - If not on: contraindication documented?

2. **SGLT2i** (CRITICAL)
   - If GFR ≥ 20 and not on SGLT2i: Start it or document why not
   - This is the biggest opportunity in CKD care

3. **MRA**
   - K+ < 5.0? Consider adding
   - Watch potassium closely

### Providing Discussion Points

Before leaving, give Scribe 3 key points for Patient Summary Card:

**Make them:**
- Plain language (patient will read this)
- Actionable (what should patient do?)
- Important (what matters most?)

**Example:**
1. "We're starting a new kidney-protecting medication called Farxiga"
2. "Keep your blood pressure below 130/80 - check at home"
3. "Get labs done 3 days before your next visit in 2 months"

## Working with Your Team

### The MA (Care Flow Specialist)

**They handle:**
- Vitals and measurements
- History gathering
- Symptom review
- Medication reconciliation
- Patient education
- Scheduling

**Your interaction:**
- Review their cards
- Provide Discussion Points

### The Scribe (Intelligence Preparer)

**They handle:**
- Chart review
- Provider briefing
- Real-time documentation
- Intervention Card completion

**Your interaction:**
- Receive briefing
- Verbalize decisions clearly
- Review and sign Intervention Card

### Communication Protocol

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Urgent value on Measurement Card | MA should have already alerted you |

## GDMT Quick Reference

### ACEi/ARB

**Target:** Maximum tolerated dose

**Start/Increase if:**
- Not at max dose
- BP allows
- K+ < 5.5
- No contraindications

**Hold/Reduce if:**
- K+ > 5.5
- AKI
- Symptomatic hypotension

**Contraindications:**
- Bilateral renal artery stenosis
- Angioedema history
- Pregnancy

### SGLT2i

**Agents:** Dapagliflozin (Farxiga) 10mg, Empagliflozin (Jardiance) 10mg

**Start if:**
- GFR ≥ 20 (can continue if already on and GFR drops)
- No contraindications

**Benefits:**
- Slows CKD progression (independent of diabetes)
- Reduces heart failure hospitalization
- Reduces cardiovascular death

**Counseling points:**
- May increase urination
- Genital hygiene important
- Hold during acute illness (sick day rules)

**Relative contraindications:**
- Type 1 diabetes
- Frequent UTI/yeast infections
- DKA history

### MRA

**Agents:** Spironolactone 12.5-25mg, Finerenone 10-20mg

**Start if:**
- K+ < 5.0
- Additional proteinuria reduction needed
- Heart failure with CKD

**Monitor:**
- K+ within 1 week of starting
- Hold if K+ ≥ 5.5

## Common Scenarios

### Patient Not on SGLT2i, GFR 35

**Decision needed:** Start SGLT2i or document contraindication

**Script:**
> "Based on the latest guidelines, there's a medication that can help protect your kidneys - it's called Farxiga. It's been shown to slow down kidney disease significantly. I'd like to start you on this today."

### Blood Pressure 158/92, On 3 Medications

**Decision needed:** Add medication, increase dose, or assess adherence

**Questions to ask:**
- "Are you taking all your BP medications?"
- "Any side effects?"
- "What does your BP look like at home?"

### GFR Declining, Now 22

**Decisions needed:**
1. Optimize GDMT
2. Discuss modality options (if not done)
3. Ensure transplant referral in progress
4. Consider vascular access referral

### Patient Asks About Prognosis

**Key points:**
- CKD is manageable, not a death sentence
- Our job is to slow it down
- Many patients never need dialysis
- Transplant is an option for many
- Lifestyle and medications make a real difference

## Tips for Success

### Maximize Your 12 Minutes

1. **Trust the team** - They've gathered what you need
2. **Review cards quickly** - Don't re-ask what's documented
3. **Decide, don't debate** - You know what to do
4. **Speak clearly for documentation** - Scribe is listening
5. **Connect with the patient** - This is your moment

### Build Team Trust

- Thank the MA and Scribe
- Give feedback (positive and constructive)
- Don't undermine them in front of patients
- Debrief after difficult cases

### Handle Exceptions Gracefully

Not every visit fits the 40-minute model. When you need more time:
- Complex new patient
- Bad news conversation
- Acute issue

Signal to the team and adjust. The model is a guide, not a prison.
