# Pod Workflow SOP

## Standard Operating Procedure: TALIA Pod-Based Patient Visit

**Document ID:** SOP-002  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective Date:** [Implementation Date]  
**Owner:** Clinical Operations Manager  
**Review Cycle:** Quarterly

---

## Purpose

Define the standardized workflow for patient visits within the TALIA pod-based care delivery model, ensuring consistent, high-quality, efficient care.

---

## Scope

This SOP covers the complete patient visit from pod arrival to departure, encompassing all five stations.

---

## Station 1: Vitals & Measurements

**Time:** Minutes 3-7 (4 minutes)  
**Staff:** MA  
**Card:** Measurement Card (Blue)

### Procedure

#### 1.1 Greet Patient
- Enter pod with warm greeting
- Use patient's name
- "Good morning, [Name]! I'm [Your Name] and I'll be taking care of you today."

#### 1.2 Confirm Weight
- "I see you weighed in at [X] pounds. Is that correct?"
- If discrepancy, re-weigh in pod
- Calculate change from last visit

#### 1.3 Blood Pressure
**Preparation (30 seconds):**
- Patient seated, feet flat, back supported
- Arm at heart level on table/armrest
- Correct cuff size selected

**Measurement:**
- Apply cuff snugly, 2-3 cm above elbow
- "Please sit quietly for a moment"
- Take reading
- Document: Systolic/Diastolic, position, arm used

#### 1.4 Heart Rate and Rhythm
- Note rate from BP device or count manually
- Assess rhythm: regular or irregular
- Document both

#### 1.5 Additional Vitals (if indicated)
- Temperature (if symptomatic or per protocol)
- Oxygen saturation (if respiratory symptoms)

#### 1.6 Edema Assessment
**Visual Inspection:**
- Both ankles/legs
- Note sock marks, shoe tightness

**Pitting Test:**
- Press shin for 5 seconds
- Grade depth (Trace, 1+, 2+, 3+, 4+)
- Note location(s)
- Compare to last visit

#### 1.7 Complete Measurement Card
- Fill all fields
- Calculate weight change
- Check appropriate boxes
- Flag urgent values:
  - BP > 180/110 or < 90/60 → Notify provider IMMEDIATELY
  - Weight change > 5 lbs → Flag for provider
  - Edema worsening → Flag for provider

#### 1.8 Transition to Station 2
- "Great, your vital signs are done. Now I'm going to ask you some questions about how you've been doing."

## Station 2: Data Gathering

**Time:** Minutes 8-15 (7 minutes)  
**Staff:** MA (Scribe may assist)  
**Cards:** Assessment Card (Green) + Module Cards

### Procedure

#### 2.1 Visit Type
- Confirm reason for visit
- Check appropriate box: Follow-up / New Problem / Urgent / Annual

#### 2.2 Chief Concern
- "What brings you in today?"
- "What's the main thing you want us to talk about?"
- Document in PATIENT'S OWN WORDS
- Don't interpret or summarize

#### 2.3 Interval History
Ask about events since last visit:

**Hospitalizations:**
- "Have you been in the hospital since we saw you last?"
- If yes: Where? When? Why? How long?

**ER Visits:**
- "Have you been to the emergency room?"
- If yes: Where? When? Why?

**New Diagnoses:**
- "Have any other doctors told you about new health problems?"

**Surgeries/Procedures:**
- "Any surgeries or procedures?"

#### 2.4 Symptom Review
Go through checklist systematically:

| Symptom | Question to Ask |
|---------|-----------------|
| Fatigue | "How's your energy level lately? Scale 0-10?" |
| SOB | "Any shortness of breath? With activity? At rest?" |
| Swelling | "Any swelling? Where? Getting better or worse?" |
| Nausea/Vomiting | "Any nausea or vomiting? Loss of appetite?" |
| Itching | "Any itching? Where? How bad?" |
| Cramps | "Any muscle cramps? When do they happen?" |
| Sleep | "How are you sleeping?" |
| Confusion | "Any trouble with memory or thinking?" |
| Urinary | "Any changes in urination? Blood? Foam?" |

#### 2.5 Medication Review
- "Let's go through your medications."
- "Are you taking all your medications as prescribed?"

**If non-adherence:**
- "What's getting in the way?"
- Document barrier: Cost / Side effects / Forgot / Other

**Changes:**
- "Any new medications from other doctors?"
- "Did you stop any medications?"
- "Any dose changes?"

#### 2.6 Activate Module Cards
Based on conditions identified, pull appropriate modules:

| If This... | Pull This Module |
|------------|------------------|
| NSAID use | NSAIDs (Coral) |
| Gout / elevated uric acid | Gout/Krystexxa (Burgundy) |
| Diabetes | Diabetes (Yellow) |
| Heart failure | Heart Failure (Red) |
| BP not at goal | BP Control (Dark Blue) |
| Hgb < 10 or on ESA | Anemia (Pink) |
| GFR < 20 | Pre-Dialysis (Navy) |
| GFR < 25 | Transplant (Teal) |

Complete relevant sections of module cards.

#### 2.7 Complete Assessment Card
- All fields filled
- Modules checked
- Initials and time

#### 2.8 Handoff to Scribe
- "I'm going to let [Scribe] know you're ready for the doctor."
- Ensure cards are complete and visible for Scribe

## Station 3: Intelligence Preparation

**Time:** Minutes 16-20 (5 minutes)  
**Staff:** Scribe  
**Cards:** Review all completed cards, prepare Intervention Card

### Procedure

#### 3.1 Review Completed Cards
- Measurement Card: Any flags?
- Assessment Card: Key concerns?
- Module Cards: What needs addressing?

#### 3.2 Chart Review
Pull key information:
- Recent labs (GFR, K+, Hgb)
- Lab trends
- GDMT status
- Pending referrals/tests

#### 3.3 Prepare Provider Briefing
Create mental or written summary:
- Patient name, age, CKD stage, GFR
- Visit type and chief concern
- Key issues (3 max)
- GDMT status and opportunities
- Recent events/changes

#### 3.4 Stage Intervention Card
- Patient info pre-populated
- Provider name ready
- Have pen ready

#### 3.5 Alert Provider
- Signal that patient is ready
- Be prepared to deliver 30-second briefing

---

## Station 4: Provider Encounter

**Time:** Minutes 21-32 (12 minutes)  
**Staff:** Provider + Scribe  
**Card:** Intervention Card (Orange)

### Procedure

#### 4.1 Provider Arrival
**Scribe delivers briefing (30 seconds):**
> "[Name] is a [age]-year-old with CKD stage [X], GFR [XX]. Here for [visit type]. Key issues: [1, 2, 3]. GDMT: [status]. [Any urgent concerns]."

#### 4.2 Provider Reviews Cards
- Quick scan of Measurement and Assessment Cards
- Note any flags or concerns
- Review Module Cards if applicable

#### 4.3 Patient Encounter
Provider conducts visit:
- Greets patient
- Addresses chief concern
- Examines as needed
- Makes clinical decisions
- Explains key points to patient

#### 4.4 Scribe Documents
Real-time documentation on Intervention Card:

**Assessment:**
- Primary diagnosis (specific)
- CKD stage
- Secondary diagnoses

**GDMT Status:**
- ACEi/ARB: Current status + action
- SGLT2i: Current status + action
- MRA: Current status + action

**Medication Changes:**
- Started (drug, dose)
- Stopped (drug, reason)
- Dose changed (drug, old → new)

**Orders:**
- Labs (specific tests + timing)
- Referrals (where + why)

**Follow-up:**
- Time interval

#### 4.5 Discussion Points
Provider gives Scribe 3 key points for Patient Summary:
- In plain language
- Actionable for patient
- Most important takeaways

#### 4.6 Provider Signature
- Provider reviews card briefly
- Signs and dates
- Scribe adds initials

#### 4.7 Provider Exits
- Provider moves to next patient
- Scribe finalizes documentation
- Scribe communicates Discussion Points to MA

---

## Station 5: Wrap-Up & Education

**Time:** Minutes 33-38 (5 minutes)  
**Staff:** MA  
**Card:** Patient Summary Card (Purple)

### Procedure

#### 5.1 Return to Pod
- MA returns after receiving Discussion Points from Scribe
- Review Intervention Card for plan

#### 5.2 Complete Patient Summary Card
Transfer key information in plain language:

**Your Numbers Today:**
- BP (with goal)
- Weight (with change)
- GFR/Stage

**What We Did Today:**
- Check appropriate boxes
- Fill in specifics

**Your Action Items:**
- 3 specific, actionable items for patient

**Medications to Know:**
- Key medications with simple purposes

**Next Appointment:**
- Schedule before completing card
- Note lab timing if needed

#### 5.3 Deliver Education
Using card back and module card backs:

**Cover:**
- What the visit findings mean
- Why medications matter
- What symptoms to watch for
- When to call us

**Use teach-back:**
- "Can you tell me in your own words what your action items are?"
- If patient can't repeat, re-teach and try again

#### 5.4 Module-Specific Education
Based on activated modules:

| Module | Key Education Points |
|--------|---------------------|
| NSAIDs | Kidney damage, safe alternatives |
| Gout | Uric acid goal, dietary triggers |
| Diabetes | SGLT2i benefits, sick day rules |
| Heart Failure | Daily weights, fluid restriction, warning signs |
| BP Control | Home monitoring, goal BP |
| Anemia | Why it happens, iron importance |
| Pre-Dialysis | Modality options, access care |
| Transplant | Benefits, process, living donor |

#### 5.5 Confirm Understanding
- "Do you have any questions?"
- "Is there anything you're not sure about?"
- Answer questions or escalate if needed

#### 5.6 Provide Summary Card
- "This card is yours to take home."
- "It has everything we talked about today."
- "Our phone number is right here if you have questions."

---

## Departure

**Time:** Minutes 38-40  
**Staff:** None required

### Procedure

1. Patient stands from pod
2. Patient walks directly to exit
3. No checkout desk needed (appointment already scheduled)
4. Patient leaves with Summary Card

## Quality Standards

### Time Management
| Station | Target | Maximum |
|---------|--------|---------|
| Station 1 | 4 min | 5 min |
| Station 2 | 7 min | 8 min |
| Station 3 | 5 min | 5 min |
| Station 4 | 12 min | 15 min |
| Station 5 | 5 min | 6 min |

### Card Completion
- 100% of fields completed
- Legible handwriting
- All urgent values flagged
- Patient leaves with Summary Card

### Patient Experience
- Warm greeting at each station
- Minimal wait between stations
- Plain language explanations
- Questions answered

---

## Exception Handling

### Provider Running Late
- MA can extend Station 2 (additional education, comfort)
- Scribe ensures patient is informed
- Don't start next patient until current one completes Station 5

### Patient Needs More Time
- Complex cases may need extended Station 4
- Signal team to adjust
- Next patient may need brief delay

### Urgent Finding
- Any urgent value: notify provider IMMEDIATELY
- Don't wait for normal workflow
- Document notification on card

### Patient Requests to Leave Early
- Offer to reschedule
- If leaving: ensure critical education delivered
- Document incomplete visit
- Follow up by phone
