# GOUT/KRYSTEXXA MODULE

## Card Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Type** | Module Card |
| **Color** | Burgundy (#922B21) |
| **Size** | 5" × 7" |
| **Paper** | 80-100 lb cardstock, matte |
| **Used By** | MA at Station 2, Provider at Station 4 |
| **Priority** | 2 (Revenue Opportunity) |
| **Phase** | Phase 1 (Shadow) |

## Activation Trigger

- Gout diagnosis in problem list
- Elevated uric acid (>6.0 mg/dL)
- Current or potential Krystexxa candidate
- Patient on urate-lowering therapy
- Active gout flare

## Purpose

Manage gout effectively, optimize urate-lowering therapy, and identify candidates for Krystexxa (pegloticase) infusion therapy. Gout accelerates kidney disease progression, and effective management protects kidney function while generating practice revenue through Krystexxa administration.

## Training Notes

**For MAs:**
1. Ask about gout flares at every visit - patients may not mention them
2. Check if uric acid has been tested recently
3. Note visible tophi on hands, elbows, ears
4. Assess medication adherence - ULT must be taken daily
5. Identify Krystexxa candidates proactively

**For Providers:**
1. Review Krystexxa criteria systematically
2. Order G6PD before starting Krystexxa
3. Document contraindications clearly
4. Set uric acid goal: <6.0 (or <5.0 with tophi)
5. Consider referral to rheumatology if complex

**Key Conversation Points:**
- "How many gout attacks have you had this year?"
- "Are you taking your allopurinol/febuxostat every day, even when you feel fine?"
- "Have you noticed any lumps under your skin around your joints?"
- "There's a newer treatment called Krystexxa that might help if your gout isn't controlled with pills"
