# METABOLIC ACIDOSIS MODULE

## Card Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Type** | Module Card |
| **Color** | Yellow-Green (#9ACD32) |
| **Size** | 5" × 7" |
| **Paper** | 80-100 lb cardstock, matte |
| **Used By** | MA at Station 2 |
| **Priority** | 2 |
| **Phase** | Phase 2 (Full Pilot) |

## Activation Trigger

- Bicarbonate (CO2) < 22 mEq/L on any recent lab
- History of metabolic acidosis
- On sodium bicarbonate therapy
- GFR < 30 mL/min (uremic acidosis common)
- Diabetic with history of DKA
- Diarrhea or GI bicarbonate loss

## Purpose

Identify and manage metabolic acidosis, a common complication of CKD that accelerates kidney disease progression, causes muscle wasting, and worsens bone disease. Treatment with bicarbonate supplementation can slow CKD progression.

## Training Notes

### For MAs
1. **CO2 is the key number** - easy to find on basic metabolic panel
2. **Anion gap helps identify cause** - calculate if electrolytes available
3. **CKD is the most common cause** in our patient population
4. **Bicarbonate has sodium** - flag heart failure patients
5. **Goal is 22-26** - not trying to make it "perfect"

### Dosing Guide (for reference)
| CO2 Level | Starting Dose | Notes |
|-----------|---------------|-------|
| 18-21 | 650 mg (1 tab) BID | Mild |
| 15-17 | 650 mg (1 tab) TID | Moderate |
| <15 | Discuss with provider | Severe |

### Red Flags - Notify Provider Immediately
- CO2 < 12 mEq/L
- Kussmaul breathing (deep, rapid breathing)
- Altered mental status
- New elevated anion gap in diabetic (possible DKA)

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Don't forget to check potassium - it may drop with treatment
- Don't start high dose in heart failure patients
- Don't assume stable if patient missed doses
- Don't overlook diarrhea as a cause (especially new onset)
