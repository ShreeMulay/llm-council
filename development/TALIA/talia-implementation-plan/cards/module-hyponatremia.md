# HYPONATREMIA MODULE

## Card Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Type** | Module Card |
| **Color** | Light Blue (#87CEEB) |
| **Size** | 5" × 7" |
| **Paper** | 80-100 lb cardstock, matte |
| **Used By** | MA at Station 2 |
| **Priority** | 2 (Moderate - Can be serious) |
| **Phase** | Phase 2 (Full Pilot) |

## Activation Trigger

- Sodium < 135 mEq/L on any recent lab
- History of hyponatremia
- On medications that cause hyponatremia (thiazides, SSRIs, desmopressin)
- Heart failure with sodium issues
- Excessive water intake reported
- SIADH diagnosis

## Purpose

Identify, manage, and prevent hyponatremia (low sodium). Severe hyponatremia can cause seizures and brain swelling. Proper management requires understanding the cause and correcting at the appropriate rate.

## Training Notes

### For MAs
1. **Volume status is KEY** - determines the cause and treatment
2. **Check medication list carefully** - thiazides are the most common cause
3. **Ask about water intake** - many patients drink too much
4. **Edema + low sodium = hypervolemic** (heart failure, cirrhosis)
5. **Dry + low sodium = hypovolemic** (dehydration, diuretic overuse)

### Volume Status Clues
| Finding | Hypovolemic | Euvolemic | Hypervolemic |
|---------|-------------|-----------|--------------|
| BP | Low | Normal | Normal/High |
| Skin turgor | Poor | Normal | Normal |
| Edema | None | None | Present |
| JVD | Flat | Normal | Elevated |
| Cause | Dehydration, GI loss | SIADH, hypothyroid | CHF, cirrhosis |

### Red Flags - Notify Provider Immediately
- Na+ < 120 mEq/L
- Any neurological symptoms (confusion, seizures)
- Rapid drop in sodium (>10 mEq/L in <24 hrs on record)
- Fall related to low sodium

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Don't assume chronic just because patient "looks fine"
- Don't forget to ask about OTC medications and herbal supplements
- Don't overlook beer potomania in patients who drink heavily
- Don't skip fluid intake assessment - it's often the cause
