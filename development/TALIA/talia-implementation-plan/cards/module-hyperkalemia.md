# HYPERKALEMIA MODULE

## Card Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Type** | Module Card |
| **Color** | Orange-Red (#FF5722) |
| **Size** | 5" × 7" |
| **Paper** | 80-100 lb cardstock, matte |
| **Used By** | MA at Station 2 |
| **Priority** | 1 (High - Life Threatening) |
| **Phase** | Phase 2 (Full Pilot) |

## Activation Trigger

- Potassium > 5.0 mEq/L on any recent lab
- History of hyperkalemia episodes
- On potassium-sparing medications (spironolactone, amiloride)
- GFR < 30 mL/min
- Currently on potassium binder (Lokelma, Veltassa, Kayexalate)

## Purpose

Identify, manage, and prevent life-threatening hyperkalemia. High potassium can cause fatal heart rhythms. Early detection and systematic management saves lives.

## Training Notes

### For MAs
1. **Check TODAY's labs first** - potassium changes daily
2. **Severity determines urgency**:
   - >6.0 = TELL provider before continuing
   - 5.6-6.0 = Complete card, flag for provider
   - 5.1-5.5 = Complete card normally
3. **Always ask about salt substitutes** - patients often don't know they contain potassium
4. **Medication timing matters** - binders must be separated from other meds
5. **Diet is crucial** - spend time on food education

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Don't assume hemolyzed sample without checking
- Don't forget to ask about over-the-counter supplements
- Don't skip dietary assessment - it's often the cause
- Don't wait until provider sees patient if K+ >6.0

### Emergency Protocol
If K+ > 6.5 mEq/L with symptoms:
1. Stop card completion
2. Alert provider IMMEDIATELY
3. Prepare for potential ECG
4. Stay with patient until provider arrives
