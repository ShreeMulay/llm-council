YOUR HEART AND KIDNEYS

HOW YOUR HEART AND KIDNEYS ARE CONNECTED

Your heart pumps blood to your kidneys. If your heart is weak, less blood gets to your kidneys. This can make kidney function worse.

Your kidneys control fluid in your body. Too much fluid makes your heart work harder.

Protecting one protects the other!

DAILY WEIGHT IS CRITICAL

  • Weigh yourself every morning BEFORE eating or drinking
  • Use the same scale, same clothes
  • Write it down or use an app
  • Call us if you gain 3+ lbs in one day or 5+ lbs in one week

My dry weight goal: _______ lbs

FLUID AND SALT LIMITS

  • Fluid limit: Usually 6-8 cups (48-64 oz) per day
  • Sodium limit: Less than 2,000 mg per day
  • Avoid canned soups, processed foods, fast food
  • Read labels - sodium adds up fast!

WATER PILLS (DIURETICS)

  • Help remove extra fluid from your body
  • Take in the morning to avoid nighttime bathroom trips
  • You'll urinate more - this is expected
  • Don't skip doses even if you feel fine

WHEN TO CALL US RIGHT AWAY

  • Weight gain of 3+ lbs in one day or 5+ lbs in one week
  • Increased swelling in legs, ankles, or belly
  • Trouble breathing, especially when lying down
  • Waking up at night short of breath
