YOUR BONES AND KIDNEYS

WHY KIDNEY DISEASE AFFECTS BONES

Your kidneys help control calcium, phosphorus, and vitamin D - all important for strong bones.

When kidneys don't work well:

  • Phosphorus builds up in blood
  • Vitamin D isn't activated properly
  • Calcium drops, making bones release calcium
  • Bones become weak over time

PHOSPHORUS BINDERS

These medicines grab phosphorus from your food before it enters your blood.

CRITICAL: Take WITH meals and snacks, not on empty stomach!

  • Chew tablets completely or take with first bite of food
  • Take the right number of pills based on meal size
  • Carry some with you when eating out

VITAMIN D

  • Helps your body use calcium for strong bones
  • Take as prescribed - some forms need monitoring
  • Get some sunlight when possible

WHEN TO CALL US

  • Bone pain or muscle weakness
  • Muscle cramps or twitching
  • Numbness around mouth or fingertips
  • Trouble taking your phosphorus binders
