AFTER ACUTE KIDNEY INJURY (AKI)

WHAT HAPPENED TO YOUR KIDNEYS

You had an Acute Kidney Injury (AKI) - a sudden drop in how well your kidneys work. This can happen from:

  • Severe infection or illness
  • Dehydration
  • Certain medications
  • Surgery or procedures

WHY FOLLOW-UP MATTERS

Even if your kidneys seem better, AKI increases your risk for:

  • Chronic kidney disease (CKD)
  • Another AKI episode
  • Heart problems

Regular monitoring helps catch problems early!

PROTECT YOUR KIDNEYS GOING FORWARD

  • Avoid NSAIDs (ibuprofen, Advil, Aleve, Motrin)
  • Stay hydrated - drink water throughout the day
  • Tell all doctors about your AKI history
  • Ask before any contrast dye for CT or heart cath
  • Take medications as prescribed

BEFORE ANY PROCEDURE

Tell your doctors:

  • "I had acute kidney injury on [date]"
  • "My kidney doctor is at The Kidney Experts"
  • "Please contact them before giving contrast dye"

WHEN TO CALL US

  • Decreased urination
  • Swelling in legs or ankles
  • Severe fatigue
  • Before any surgery or procedure with contrast
