CONTRAST DYE AND YOUR KIDNEYS

WHAT IS CONTRAST?

Contrast is a special dye used in imaging tests (like CT scans or heart catheterizations) to help doctors see blood vessels and organs better.

In people with kidney disease, contrast can sometimes cause temporary or permanent kidney damage.

YOUR PROTECTION PLAN

We've created a plan to protect your kidneys:

  • Before: IV fluids to keep you well hydrated
  • During: Use lowest amount of safest contrast
  • After: Check kidney function to make sure it's okay

MEDICATIONS TO HOLD

STOP these before your procedure:

  • Metformin: Stop 48 hours before, restart when creatinine okay
  • SGLT2 inhibitors (Jardiance, Farxiga): Stop day of procedure
  • NSAIDs: Stop 2-3 days before
  • Diuretics: May need to hold - ask us

AFTER YOUR PROCEDURE

  • Drink plenty of water to flush out the contrast
  • Get your blood work done when scheduled
  • Don't restart held medications until we confirm your kidneys are okay

CALL US AFTER YOUR PROCEDURE IF:

  • Decreased urination
  • Swelling in legs or ankles
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Unusual fatigue
