LUPUS AND YOUR KIDNEYS

WHAT IS LUPUS NEPHRITIS?

Lupus is an autoimmune disease where your immune system attacks healthy tissues. When it attacks your kidneys, it's called lupus nephritis.

About half of people with lupus develop kidney involvement at some point.

MEDICATIONS ARE CRITICAL

Hydroxychloroquine (Plaquenil)

  • Take EVERY DAY - cornerstone of lupus treatment
  • Protects kidneys and reduces flares
  • Get yearly eye exams

Immunosuppressants

  • Calm your immune system to protect kidneys
  • Never stop suddenly - taper under guidance

PREVENTING FLARES

  • Sun protection - UV light triggers flares; use SPF 50+
  • Avoid infections - wash hands, call early if sick
  • Rest when needed - fatigue is real
  • Take medications as prescribed
  • Reduce stress - stress can trigger flares

SIGNS OF A FLARE

  • Increased fatigue
  • New or worsening joint pain
  • Rash (especially butterfly rash on face)
  • Foamy urine or decreased urination
  • New swelling in legs or face

WHEN TO CALL US

  • Signs of a flare (above)
  • Fever over 100.4°F
  • Blood in urine
  • Increased swelling
  • Trouble taking medications
