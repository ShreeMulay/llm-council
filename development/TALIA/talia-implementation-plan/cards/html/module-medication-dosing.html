MEDICATIONS AND YOUR KIDNEYS

WHY KIDNEY FUNCTION AFFECTS MEDICATIONS

Many medications are removed from your body through your kidneys. When kidneys don't work as well, medications can build up and cause side effects.

Some medications need lower doses in CKD. Some should be avoided entirely.

MEDICATIONS TO ALWAYS AVOID

NSAIDsIbuprofen (Advil, Motrin), Naproxen (Aleve)
Antacids with MgMilk of Magnesia, some Maalox
Some laxativesMagnesium citrate, Fleet enemas

SAFE FOR PAIN

  • Acetaminophen (Tylenol): Safe up to 3 grams/day
  • Topical creams: Lidocaine, capsaicin
  • Heat/ice

Ask us before taking any new medication!

HERBAL SUPPLEMENTS - BE CAREFUL!

Many "natural" products can harm kidneys:

  • Not regulated by FDA
  • May contain hidden ingredients
  • Can interact with your medications
  • Always tell us what supplements you take

BEFORE TAKING ANY NEW MEDICATION

  • Call us first - even over-the-counter
  • Tell pharmacist you have kidney disease
  • Ask your other doctors to check with us
  • Read labels for kidney warnings
