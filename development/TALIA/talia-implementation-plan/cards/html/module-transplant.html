CARING FOR YOUR TRANSPLANT

YOUR ANTI-REJECTION MEDICATIONS

These medications keep your body from attacking your new kidney.

  • Take EXACTLY as prescribed - same time every day
  • NEVER skip doses - even one missed dose can trigger rejection
  • If you miss a dose, call us for guidance
  • Don't run out - refill before you're low

INFECTION PREVENTION

Your immune system is lowered to protect your kidney. This means you're more prone to infections.

  • Wash hands frequently
  • Avoid sick people when possible
  • Stay up to date on vaccines (ask us which ones are safe)
  • Practice food safety - avoid raw/undercooked foods
  • Call us early if you feel sick

SUN AND SKIN PROTECTION

Transplant patients have higher skin cancer risk.

  • Use SPF 30+ sunscreen daily
  • Wear hats and protective clothing
  • Avoid peak sun hours (10am-4pm)
  • Get yearly skin checks with dermatologist

SIGNS OF REJECTION

  • Fever over 100.4°F
  • Pain or swelling over the transplant
  • Decreased urine output
  • Rapid weight gain (fluid retention)
  • Feeling unwell without explanation

CALL US IMMEDIATELY IF:

  • Fever over 100.4°F
  • Pain over your transplant kidney
  • Blood in urine or decreased output
  • Missed anti-rejection medication
