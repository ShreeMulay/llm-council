ANEMIA AND YOUR KIDNEYS

WHY KIDNEY PATIENTS GET ANEMIA

Healthy kidneys make a hormone called EPO that tells your bone marrow to make red blood cells.

When kidneys don't work as well, they make less EPO, so you make fewer red blood cells.

Red blood cells carry oxygen to give you energy. Fewer red blood cells = less energy.

SYMPTOMS OF ANEMIA

  • Feeling tired or weak
  • Shortness of breath
  • Dizziness or lightheadedness
  • Pale skin
  • Feeling cold
  • Trouble concentrating

IRON IS ESSENTIAL

Your body needs iron to make red blood cells. Without enough iron, even the best medications won't help your anemia.

If taking oral iron:

  • Take on an empty stomach if you can
  • Vitamin C (orange juice) helps absorption
  • Don't take with calcium or antacids
  • May cause constipation or dark stools - this is normal

ESA MEDICATIONS

ESA (Erythropoietin-Stimulating Agent) is a shot that helps your body make more red blood cells.

  • Works like the natural hormone your kidneys used to make
  • Given as a shot under the skin or in dialysis
  • Takes weeks to see full effect

WHEN TO CALL US

  • Severe fatigue that limits your activities
  • Shortness of breath at rest
  • Chest pain
  • Blood in stool or very dark stools (not from iron)
