DIALYSIS PATIENT GUIDE

YOUR DRY WEIGHT

Your dry weight is your weight when all extra fluid is removed.

  • Too much fluid = swelling, high BP, shortness of breath
  • Too little fluid = cramps, dizziness, low BP
  • Report weight changes or symptoms to dialysis staff

My dry weight goal: _______ kg

FLUID AND DIET LIMITS

  • Fluid: Usually 32-48 oz/day (ask your team)
  • Sodium: Less than 2,000 mg/day
  • Potassium: Limit high-K+ foods
  • Phosphorus: Take binders WITH meals

ACCESS CARE

Your access is your lifeline!

  • Check your fistula/graft daily for the "thrill" (buzzing feeling)
  • Keep it clean - wash before dialysis
  • No blood draws, BP, or sleeping on access arm
  • Report any redness, swelling, or drainage immediately

ATTEND ALL TREATMENTS

  • Missing dialysis is dangerous
  • Shortened treatments don't clean well enough
  • Stay the full time - your life depends on it
  • Plan ahead for holidays and travel

WHEN TO CALL US

  • Access problems (no thrill, swelling, fever)
  • Severe shortness of breath
  • Chest pain
  • Can't attend dialysis
