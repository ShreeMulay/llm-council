UNDERSTANDING GLOMERULONEPHRITIS

WHAT IS GLOMERULONEPHRITIS?

Glomerulonephritis (GN) means inflammation of the kidney's tiny filters (glomeruli). This causes protein and blood to leak into your urine.

There are many types with different causes - some from the immune system attacking the kidneys.

YOUR TREATMENT

Immunosuppressive medications calm your immune system to reduce kidney inflammation.

  • Take exactly as prescribed - timing and dose matter
  • Never stop suddenly without calling us
  • These medications lower your immune system

INFECTION PREVENTION

With a lowered immune system, you're more prone to infections:

  • Wash hands frequently
  • Avoid sick people when possible
  • Call us early if you feel sick
  • Get recommended vaccines (ask which are safe)
  • Practice food safety

MONITORING YOUR DISEASE

We watch for:

  • Protein in urine - should decrease with treatment
  • Blood in urine - should improve
  • Kidney function (GFR) - should stabilize

Report any foamy urine or dark/red urine immediately.

CALL US IMMEDIATELY IF:

  • Fever over 100.4°F
  • Blood in urine (pink, red, or brown)
  • Increased swelling
  • Unable to take your medications
