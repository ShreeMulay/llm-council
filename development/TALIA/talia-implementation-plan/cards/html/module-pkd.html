POLYCYSTIC KIDNEY DISEASE

WHAT IS PKD?

PKD is an inherited condition where fluid-filled cysts grow in your kidneys. Over time, these cysts can crowd out healthy kidney tissue.

ADPKD is the most common type - it runs in families (50% chance of passing to children).

SLOWING PKD PROGRESSION

  • Control blood pressure - goal usually <130/80
  • Drink lots of water - at least 3 liters/day to suppress cyst growth hormone
  • Limit caffeine - may stimulate cyst growth
  • Avoid NSAIDs - ibuprofen, Aleve
  • Consider Jynarque if you qualify - slows cyst growth

ABOUT JYNARQUE (TOLVAPTAN)

Jynarque is an FDA-approved medication that can slow PKD progression.

  • Causes increased urination (expected)
  • Must drink lots of water
  • Requires monthly liver tests (rare risk of liver injury)
  • Not for everyone - talk to us about whether you qualify

WATCHING FOR COMPLICATIONS

  • Cyst infection: Fever, flank pain - needs specific antibiotics
  • Cyst bleeding: Blood in urine, pain - usually rest and fluids
  • Kidney stones: More common in PKD
  • Brain aneurysm: Family screening if history

WHEN TO CALL US

  • Blood in urine (especially with pain or fever)
  • Fever over 100.4°F
  • Severe flank or abdominal pain
  • Sudden severe headache (could indicate aneurysm)
