BLOOD PRESSURE AND YOUR KIDNEYS

WHY BLOOD PRESSURE MATTERS

High blood pressure is the #2 cause of kidney failure in America (after diabetes).

Think of it like a garden hose: too much pressure damages the hose over time. The same happens to the tiny blood vessels in your kidneys.

Controlling blood pressure can slow kidney disease by 30-40%!

YOUR BLOOD PRESSURE GOAL

For most kidney patients: Below 130/80

TAKING YOUR BP AT HOME

  • Check at the same time each day (morning is best)
  • Sit quietly for 5 minutes before checking
  • Feet flat on floor, arm supported at heart level
  • Don't talk during measurement
  • Write down your numbers to bring to visits

LIFESTYLE TIPS

  • Limit sodium to less than 2,000 mg/day
  • Exercise 30 minutes most days
  • Limit alcohol
  • Manage stress
  • Take medications at the same time every day

WHEN TO CALL US

  • BP over 180/110 (even without symptoms)
  • BP under 90/60 with dizziness
  • Severe headache with high BP
  • Side effects from BP medications
