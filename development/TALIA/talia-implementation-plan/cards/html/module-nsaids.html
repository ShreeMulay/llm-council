NSAIDS AND YOUR KIDNEYS

WHAT ARE NSAIDS?

NSAIDs (Non-Steroidal Anti-Inflammatory Drugs) are common pain relievers found in many stores:

  • Ibuprofen: Advil, Motrin, Nuprin
  • Naproxen: Aleve, Naprosyn
  • Aspirin: Bayer, Bufferin (high doses)
  • Others: Meloxicam, Diclofenac, Celecoxib

WHY NSAIDS HURT KIDNEYS

NSAIDs reduce blood flow to your kidneys. For people with kidney disease, this can:

  • Cause sudden kidney damage
  • Make chronic kidney disease worse
  • Interfere with blood pressure medications
  • Cause fluid retention and swelling

Even "just a few" can cause harm with kidney disease!

SAFER PAIN OPTIONS

  • Acetaminophen (Tylenol): Safe for kidneys when used as directed (max 3g/day)
  • Topical creams: Lidocaine patches, capsaicin cream
  • Physical therapy: Stretching, strengthening exercises
  • Heat/ice: For muscle and joint pain
  • Talk to us: About prescription options if needed

CHECK YOUR MEDICINE CABINET

Many products contain hidden NSAIDs. Read labels for:

  • Ibuprofen
  • Naproxen sodium
  • Aspirin (except low-dose 81mg for heart)

When in doubt, call us before taking!

WHEN TO CALL US

  • Before taking any NSAID
  • If you accidentally took an NSAID
  • If you need help finding pain alternatives
  • If another doctor wants to prescribe an NSAID
