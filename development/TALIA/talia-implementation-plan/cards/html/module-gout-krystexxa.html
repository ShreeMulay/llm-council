GOUT AND YOUR KIDNEYS

WHAT IS GOUT?

Gout happens when uric acid crystals build up in your joints, causing sudden, severe pain and swelling.

Gout and kidneys are connected:

  • Kidneys remove uric acid from your blood
  • When kidneys don't work well, uric acid builds up
  • High uric acid can also damage kidneys over time

FOODS TO LIMIT

  • Red meat and organ meats
  • Shellfish (shrimp, lobster, scallops)
  • Beer and liquor (wine is lower risk)
  • Sugary drinks with high-fructose corn syrup

Drink plenty of water - helps flush uric acid

YOUR GOUT MEDICATION

Allopurinol or Febuxostat:

  • Lowers uric acid production
  • Take EVERY DAY even when feeling fine
  • May cause more flares at first (this is normal)

Krystexxa:

  • IV infusion for severe gout that doesn't respond to pills
  • Breaks down uric acid quickly
  • Requires monitoring before each infusion

IF YOU HAVE A GOUT FLARE

  • Continue your regular gout medication
  • Ice the affected joint
  • Rest and elevate the joint
  • Call us for flare treatment options
  • Avoid NSAIDs - they can hurt kidneys!

WHEN TO CALL US

  • Gout flare that doesn't improve
  • New or worsening joint swelling
  • Side effects from gout medications
  • Questions about diet and triggers
