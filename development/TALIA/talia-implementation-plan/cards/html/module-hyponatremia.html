LOW SODIUM (HYPONATREMIA)

WHAT IS LOW SODIUM?

Sodium helps control fluid balance in your body. When sodium is too low, water moves into your cells and causes swelling - including in your brain.

Normal sodium: 136-145 mEq/L

SYMPTOMS OF LOW SODIUM

  • Mild: Nausea, headache, muscle cramps
  • Moderate: Confusion, difficulty concentrating
  • Severe: Seizures, loss of consciousness (EMERGENCY)

Many people have no symptoms, especially if sodium dropped slowly.

FLUID RESTRICTION

If you're told to limit fluids:

  • Your limit: _______ cups/day (_______ mL)
  • ALL liquids count - water, coffee, soup, ice cream
  • Spread fluids throughout the day
  • Use small cups to control portions
  • Suck on ice chips or hard candy for thirst

TIPS FOR MANAGING THIRST

  • Keep mouth moist with lip balm
  • Chew sugar-free gum
  • Rinse mouth without swallowing
  • Avoid salty foods (they make you thirsty)
  • Stay cool - heat increases thirst

CALL US OR GO TO ER IF:

  • Severe headache
  • Confusion or unusual behavior
  • Severe nausea and vomiting
  • Seizure or loss of consciousness
