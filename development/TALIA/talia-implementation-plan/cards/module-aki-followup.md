# AKI FOLLOW-UP MODULE

## Card Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Type** | Module Card |
| **Color** | Red-Orange (#E74C3C) |
| **Size** | 5" × 7" |
| **Paper** | 80-100 lb cardstock, matte |
| **Used By** | MA at Station 2 |
| **Priority** | 1 (High Priority Follow-Up) |
| **Phase** | Phase 2 (Full Pilot) |

## Activation Trigger

- Recent hospitalization with acute kidney injury (AKI)
- Creatinine rise >0.3 mg/dL in last 48 hours
- Post-AKI follow-up visit (within 90 days of AKI episode)
- Recovery from dialysis-requiring AKI
- Nephrotoxic medication exposure with creatinine rise

## Purpose

Ensure proper follow-up after acute kidney injury. AKI survivors have high risk of developing CKD, progressing to ESKD, and recurrent AKI. Close monitoring in the first 90 days post-AKI is critical for long-term kidney preservation.

## Training Notes

### For MAs
1. **Know the baseline** - Always have pre-AKI creatinine for comparison
2. **90-day window is critical** - close monitoring during this period
3. **Medication reconciliation essential** - what was held, what was restarted
4. **NSAIDs are the enemy** - reinforce at every visit
5. **Recovery trajectory matters** - track if improving, stable, or declining

### Follow-Up Schedule Post-AKI
| Days Post-AKI | Lab Frequency | Visit |
|---------------|---------------|-------|
| 0-7 | Every 2-3 days | Weekly |
| 7-30 | Weekly | Every 2 weeks |
| 30-90 | Every 2-4 weeks | Monthly |
| >90 | Based on recovery | Transition to CKD schedule |

### Red Flags - Notify Provider
- Creatinine rising again after initial improvement
- New oliguria (<500 mL/day)
- Failure to improve after 7 days
- New symptoms (edema, SOB, confusion)

### When to Restart Held Medications
- ACE/ARB: When Cr within 30% of baseline and stable x 2 checks
- Diuretics: Based on volume status
- Metformin: When GFR >30 and stable
- SGLT2i: When fully recovered and stable
- NSAIDs: Ideally NEVER in AKI survivors
