# DIALYSIS MODULE

## Card Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Type** | Module Card |
| **Color** | Navy Blue (#34495E) |
| **Size** | 5" × 7" |
| **Paper** | 80-100 lb cardstock, matte |
| **Used By** | MA at Station 2 |
| **Priority** | 1 (Critical Population) |
| **Phase** | Phase 2 (Full Pilot) |

## Activation Trigger

- Currently on hemodialysis (HD)
- Currently on peritoneal dialysis (PD)
- On home hemodialysis (HHD)
- Dialysis clinic monthly visit

## Purpose

Monthly comprehensive assessment for dialysis patients focusing on adequacy, access function, anemia/bone disease management, volume status, and quality of life. Dialysis patients require careful monitoring to prevent complications and maintain quality of life.

## Training Notes

### For MAs
1. **Access exam is critical** - every visit, check thrill and bruit
2. **Know their dry weight** - weight gain tells us about fluid control
3. **Kt/V >1.2 is the goal** for HD (>1.7 weekly for PD)
4. **Transplant should be discussed** - many patients don't know they're candidates
5. **Quality of life matters** - ask how they're actually feeling

### Access Exam Quick Reference
| Finding | Concern | Action |
|---------|---------|--------|
| Weak thrill | Possible stenosis | Schedule fistulogram |
| High-pitched bruit | Stenosis | Schedule fistulogram |
| No thrill | Clotted access | URGENT - notify provider |
| Exit site drainage | Catheter infection | URGENT - culture, antibiotics |

### Adequacy Goals
| Measure | Goal |
|---------|------|
| Kt/V (HD) | ≥1.2 |
| Kt/V (PD weekly) | ≥1.7 |
| URR | ≥65% |
| IDWG | <5% body weight |

### Red Flags - Notify Provider
- No thrill in access (possible clot)
- Fever with catheter (line infection)
- Kt/V <1.0 (inadequate dialysis)
- IDWG >4 kg consistently
- Severe depression/poor quality of life
