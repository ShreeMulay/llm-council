# CKD-MBD (MINERAL BONE DISEASE) MODULE

## Card Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Type** | Module Card |
| **Color** | Bone/Tan (#D2B48C) |
| **Size** | 5" × 7" |
| **Paper** | 80-100 lb cardstock, matte |
| **Used By** | MA at Station 2 |
| **Priority** | 3 |
| **Phase** | Phase 2 (Full Pilot) |

## Activation Trigger

- CKD Stage 3b or higher (GFR < 45)
- Elevated PTH (parathyroid hormone)
- Low or high calcium
- High phosphorus
- On phosphate binders
- On Vitamin D therapy (calcitriol, paricalcitol, ergocalciferol)
- History of bone fractures
- On Sensipar/Parsabiv (cinacalcet/etelcalcetide)

## Purpose

Manage the mineral and bone complications of CKD that affect bone health, cardiovascular calcification, and overall mortality. CKD-MBD is a systemic disorder that affects nearly all advanced CKD patients.

## Training Notes

### For MAs
1. **Labs are the foundation** - can't complete card without recent PTH, Ca, Phos
2. **Binder timing is crucial** - emphasize "with first bite" every visit
3. **Look for hidden phosphorus** - ask about processed foods, fast food
4. **Bone pain + high PTH = concern** - flag for provider
5. **Calciphylaxis is rare but serious** - note if patient at high risk

### Common Lab Interpretation
| PTH | Calcium | Phosphorus | Likely Issue |
|-----|---------|------------|--------------|
| High | Normal | High | Secondary hyperparathyroidism - needs binders |
| High | High | Normal | Tertiary hyperparathyroidism - may need surgery |
| Low | High | Normal | Over-suppressed - reduce Vitamin D/calcimimetic |
| High | Low | High | Severe CKD-MBD - needs aggressive management |

### Red Flags
- PTH > 1000 pg/mL → Consider parathyroidectomy referral
- Calcium > 10.5 + high PTH → Tertiary hyperparathyroidism
- Skin lesions + severe CKD-MBD → Calciphylaxis (urgent)
