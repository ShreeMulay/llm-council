# CONTRAST NEPHROPATHY PREVENTION MODULE

## Card Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Type** | Module Card |
| **Color** | Yellow/Warning (#F39C12) |
| **Size** | 5" × 7" |
| **Paper** | 80-100 lb cardstock, matte |
| **Used By** | MA at Station 2 or Nurse for Pre-Procedure |
| **Priority** | 1 (Pre-Procedure) |
| **Phase** | Phase 2 (Full Pilot) |

## Activation Trigger

- Scheduled for procedure with IV contrast (CT with contrast, cardiac cath, angiogram)
- Pre-procedure nephrology consultation
- CKD patient needing contrast-enhanced imaging
- History of contrast-induced nephropathy
- GFR < 60 mL/min

## Purpose

Prevent contrast-induced acute kidney injury (CI-AKI) through risk assessment, pre-procedure hydration protocols, medication management, and post-procedure monitoring. CI-AKI can accelerate CKD progression or cause acute dialysis need.

## Training Notes

### For MAs
1. **Calculate risk score** - determines precautions needed
2. **Metformin hold is critical** - can cause lactic acidosis if kidneys fail
3. **Hydration is key prevention** - volume expansion protects kidneys
4. **Post-procedure labs are essential** - catch CI-AKI early
5. **Document allergy history** - allergic reactions are different from kidney injury

### Risk Score Quick Reference
| Score | Risk Level | CI-AKI Risk | Recommendation |
|-------|------------|-------------|----------------|
| 0-5 | Low | <2% | Oral hydration |
| 6-10 | Moderate | 2-5% | IV hydration |
| 11-15 | High | 5-10% | IV hydration, close monitoring |
| >15 | Very High | >15% | Consider alternative imaging |

### Prevention Protocol
| Risk | Pre-Procedure | Post-Procedure |
|------|---------------|----------------|
| Low | Oral hydration | Labs in 48-72 hrs |
| Moderate | IV NS 1 mL/kg/hr x 6 hrs | Labs in 24-48 hrs |
| High | IV NS 1 mL/kg/hr x 12 hrs pre/post | Labs in 24 hrs, daily x 3 |

### Red Flags Post-Procedure
- Cr rise >0.5 mg/dL from baseline
- Cr rise >25% from baseline
- Oliguria (<500 mL/day)
- Symptoms (edema, SOB)
