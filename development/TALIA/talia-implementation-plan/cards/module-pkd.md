# POLYCYSTIC KIDNEY DISEASE (PKD) MODULE

## Card Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Type** | Module Card |
| **Color** | Purple/Magenta (#9B59B6) |
| **Size** | 5" × 7" |
| **Paper** | 80-100 lb cardstock, matte |
| **Used By** | MA at Station 2 |
| **Priority** | 2 |
| **Phase** | Phase 2 (Full Pilot) |

## Activation Trigger

- Diagnosis of ADPKD (Autosomal Dominant Polycystic Kidney Disease)
- Diagnosis of ARPKD (Autosomal Recessive PKD - rare)
- Known kidney cysts with family history of PKD
- On Tolvaptan (Jynarque) therapy
- History of cyst hemorrhage or infection

## Purpose

Specialized management of PKD patients including Tolvaptan therapy monitoring, cyst complications, liver involvement, and family screening. PKD is a genetic disease requiring unique monitoring and treatment approaches.

## Training Notes

### For MAs
1. **Family history is crucial** - PKD is genetic, affects counseling
2. **BP control is #1 priority** - target <130/80
3. **Tolvaptan requires REMS monitoring** - LFTs are mandatory
4. **Ask about hematuria** - cyst hemorrhage is common
5. **NSAIDs are especially dangerous** in PKD

### Tolvaptan (Jynarque) Monitoring
| Time | LFT Frequency |
|------|---------------|
| First 18 months | Monthly |
| After 18 months | Every 3 months |

### Mayo Classification Quick Reference
| Class | Risk | Description |
|-------|------|-------------|
| 1A | Low | Typical slow progression |
| 1B | Intermediate | |
| 1C | High | Rapid progression - Tolvaptan candidate |
| 1D | High | Rapid progression - Tolvaptan candidate |
| 1E | Very High | Very rapid progression - Tolvaptan candidate |

### Red Flags
- Gross hematuria lasting >1 week (cyst hemorrhage)
- Fever + flank pain (cyst infection - needs IV antibiotics)
- ALT/AST >3x ULN on Tolvaptan (STOP drug, notify provider)
- Sudden severe headache (possible aneurysm rupture)
