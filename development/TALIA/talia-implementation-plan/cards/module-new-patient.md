# NEW PATIENT MODULE

## Card Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Type** | Module Card |
| **Color** | Bright Blue (#3498DB) |
| **Size** | 5" × 7" |
| **Paper** | 80-100 lb cardstock, matte |
| **Used By** | MA at Station 1 (Check-in) |
| **Priority** | 1 (First Visit) |
| **Phase** | Phase 1 (Pilot Launch) |

## Activation Trigger

- First visit to The Kidney Experts
- New patient consultation
- Transfer of care from another nephrologist
- Hospital follow-up for patient not previously in our practice

## Purpose

Comprehensive intake for new patients to gather complete medical history, understand their kidney disease journey, establish baseline measurements, and set expectations for the TALIA care model. First impressions matter - this sets the tone for the entire patient relationship.

## Training Notes

### For MAs
1. **This is the patient's FIRST impression** - be welcoming and thorough
2. **Get the medication list completely** - scan or document all meds
3. **Flag NSAIDs immediately** - these need to stop
4. **Explain our care model** - it's different, prepare them
5. **Mission statement matters** - share it, they need to hear it
6. **CKD stage should be calculated** - use GFR to determine stage

### CKD Staging Quick Reference
| GFR | Stage | Description |
|-----|-------|-------------|
| ≥90 | 1 | Normal or high |
| 60-89 | 2 | Mildly decreased |
| 45-59 | 3a | Mildly to moderately decreased |
| 30-44 | 3b | Moderately to severely decreased |
| 15-29 | 4 | Severely decreased |
| <15 | 5 | Kidney failure |
| Dialysis | 5D | On dialysis |

### Welcome Script (suggested)
"Welcome to The Kidney Experts! Our mission is to help you avoid dialysis if possible, or at least delay it as long as we can. Today we'll spend time getting to know your health history and creating a plan together. You'll notice we do things a little differently here - you'll stay in one spot and our team will come to you. We want to make sure you understand everything about your kidney health, so please ask questions at any time!"

### Red Flags on New Patient Intake
- GFR < 15 without prior nephrology care → urgent evaluation
- No ACE/ARB on diabetic with proteinuria → needs to start
- Active NSAID use → needs to stop today
- Contrast allergy not documented → critical for future imaging
- Prior dialysis access never used → needs assessment
