# DIABETES MODULE

## Card Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Type** | Module Card |
| **Color** | Yellow (#F1C40F) |
| **Size** | 5" × 7" |
| **Paper** | 80-100 lb cardstock, matte |
| **Used By** | MA at Station 2 |
| **Priority** | 3 |
| **Phase** | Phase 2 (Full Pilot) |

## Activation Trigger

- Diabetes diagnosis (Type 1, Type 2, or other)
- A1C > 6.5%
- On any diabetes medications
- History of diabetic kidney disease

## Purpose

Optimize diabetes management in the context of kidney disease, ensure SGLT2 inhibitor therapy is maximized, and provide diabetes-specific education. Diabetes is the #1 cause of kidney failure in the US.

```

          │

          │
    ┌─────┴─────┐
    │           │
    ▼           ▼
 GFR ≥20     GFR <20
    │           │
    ▼           ▼
 START      May continue if
 SGLT2i     already on; avoid
            initiating new
```

**Preferred SGLT2 Inhibitors for CKD:**
- Dapagliflozin (Farxiga) 10mg daily
- Empagliflozin (Jardiance) 10mg daily

**Counseling Points:**
- Genital yeast infections - keep area clean and dry
- Increased urination initially
- Stay well hydrated
- Hold during acute illness, surgery, dehydration

## Training Notes

**For MAs:**
1. SGLT2i status is the most important field - every diabetic should be on one if GFR >20
2. Ask about hypoglycemia specifically - patients may not mention it
3. Check if A1C is due (every 3 months if uncontrolled, every 6 months if stable)
4. Ask about home glucose monitoring - many patients stop checking
5. Foot exam can be visual - look for wounds, deformities

**Key Conversation Points:**
- "When was your last A1C checked?"
- "Are you taking a kidney-protecting diabetes medication like Jardiance or Farxiga?"
- "Have you had any episodes of low blood sugar?"
- "When was your last eye exam?"
