## Version: 1.0

## Owner: Shree Mulay, MD

## Evidence Base: EWGSOP2 (2019), Fried Frailty Phenotype, KDIGO CKD-Sarcopenia

---

## 1. Purpose

Establish a standardized Tier 1 physical performance screening protocol that MAs perform at every CKD clinic visit. This adds approximately 2 minutes to the vitals intake workflow and captures three objective measurements that correlate with cardiovascular mortality, falls, hospitalization, and CKD progression.

**Why This Matters**: CKD patients lose muscle mass 3-5x faster than age-matched controls. Grip strength below threshold independently predicts CV mortality (HR 1.7-2.3). Early detection enables intervention before functional decline becomes irreversible.

## 3. The Three Tier 1 Tests

### 3.1 Grip Strength (Jamar Dynamometer)

**Time**: ~30 seconds

**Setup**:
1. Patient seated in chair with feet flat on floor
2. Shoulder adducted (arm at side), elbow flexed at 90 degrees
3. Forearm in neutral position (thumb up), wrist between 0-30 degrees extension
4. Dynamometer handle in position II (second notch)

**Procedure**:
1. Demonstrate the squeeze motion to the patient
2. Say: "Squeeze as hard as you can... squeeze... squeeze... and relax."
3. Record the peak value in kg (or lbs -- convert: lbs / 2.205 = kg)
4. Test the DOMINANT hand
5. Perform ONE trial (sufficient for screening; research uses best of 3)
6. If patient has AV fistula/graft in dominant arm, test non-dominant arm and note it

**Thresholds (EWGSOP2 Cutoffs)**:

| | Normal | Low (Flag for Provider) |
|---|--------|------------------------|
| **Men** | >= 27 kg (59.5 lbs) | < 27 kg (< 59.5 lbs) |
| **Women** | >= 16 kg (35.3 lbs) | < 16 kg (< 35.3 lbs) |

**Recording**: Enter value in kg. Circle "LOW" if below threshold.

**Special Considerations**:
- AV fistula/graft: test opposite hand, note on form
- Arthritis/hand pain: note "unable -- pain" and skip to next test
- Dialysis patients: test non-access arm
- Recent hand surgery: skip, note reason

---

### 3.2 30-Second Sit-to-Stand (STS)

**Time**: ~45 seconds (including setup)

**Setup**:
1. Armless chair against a wall (so it does not slide)
2. Patient sits in middle of chair, feet flat on floor, arms crossed over chest
3. Demonstrate: "Stand all the way up, then sit all the way down. That's one."

**Procedure**:
1. Say: "When I say go, stand up and sit down as many times as you can in 30 seconds. Ready? Go."
2. Start stopwatch on "Go"
3. Count each FULL stand (patient reaches full upright position)
4. If patient is partway up at 30 seconds, count it if they are more than halfway up
5. Say "Stop" at 30 seconds

**Thresholds**:

| | Normal | Low (Flag for Provider) |
|---|--------|------------------------|
| **All adults** | >= 10 repetitions in 30 sec | < 10 repetitions in 30 sec |

**Age-adjusted reference ranges** (for provider interpretation, not MA flagging):

| Age | Below Average | Average | Above Average |
|-----|--------------|---------|---------------|
| 60-64 | < 12 | 12-17 | > 17 |
| 65-69 | < 11 | 11-16 | > 16 |
| 70-74 | < 10 | 10-15 | > 15 |
| 75-79 | < 9 | 9-14 | > 14 |
| 80+ | < 8 | 8-13 | > 13 |

**Recording**: Enter number of complete repetitions. Circle "LOW" if <10.

**Special Considerations**:
- Wheelchair-bound: skip, note "unable -- wheelchair"
- Knee/hip replacement <6 months: skip, note reason
- If patient needs arms to stand: note "used arms" and still count reps
- Fall risk patients: have second staff member standing nearby (do not assist unless needed)

---

### 3.3 Gait Speed (4-Meter Walk)

**Time**: ~30-45 seconds (including setup)

**Setup**:
1. Mark a 4-meter (13.1 ft) straight path on floor with tape
2. Add 1-meter acceleration zone BEFORE the start line (patient begins walking before timing starts)
3. Add 1-meter deceleration zone AFTER the end line (patient continues past the mark)
4. Total path needed: 6 meters (19.7 ft) -- hallway or long exam room

**Procedure**:
1. Patient stands 1 meter behind the start line
2. Say: "Walk at your normal comfortable speed toward the mark at the end."
3. Start stopwatch when patient's FIRST FOOT crosses the start line
4. Stop stopwatch when patient's FIRST FOOT crosses the end line
5. Calculate: **4 meters / time in seconds = gait speed (m/s)**
6. Perform ONE trial

**Thresholds (EWGSOP2)**:

| | Normal | Low (Flag for Provider) |
|---|--------|------------------------|
| **All adults** | >= 0.8 m/s | < 0.8 m/s |

**Quick conversion guide for MAs**:

| Time (seconds) | Gait Speed (m/s) | Status |
|----------------|-------------------|--------|
| 4.0 sec | 1.00 | Normal |
| 4.5 sec | 0.89 | Normal |
| 5.0 sec | 0.80 | Borderline |
| 5.5 sec | 0.73 | LOW |
| 6.0 sec | 0.67 | LOW |
| 7.0 sec | 0.57 | LOW |
| 8.0 sec | 0.50 | LOW |
| 10.0 sec | 0.40 | LOW |

**Rule of thumb**: If the patient takes more than 5 seconds to walk 4 meters, it's LOW.

**Recording**: Enter time in seconds AND calculated gait speed in m/s. Circle "LOW" if <0.8 m/s.

**Special Considerations**:
- Walker/cane: patient may use their usual assistive device. Note device used.
- Wheelchair: skip, note "unable -- wheelchair"
- If hallway is not available: a 3-meter path is acceptable (adjust calculation: 3 / time = speed)
- Amputee: skip gait speed, note reason

## 4. Integrated Workflow

### When in the Visit

```
Patient arrives
  |
  v
Check-in (front desk)
  |
  v
Vitals (MA) -- weight, BP, HR, O2 sat, temp
  |
  v
[NEW] Physical Performance Screening -- grip, STS, gait speed (~2 min)
  |
  v
Room patient for provider
```

### Step-by-Step MA Workflow

1. **After completing standard vitals**, say to patient: "We're going to do three quick physical tests today. These help us track your strength and mobility."

2. **Grip strength** (patient still seated from vitals):
   - Hand patient the dynamometer
   - Position arm: shoulder at side, elbow 90 degrees
   - "Squeeze as hard as you can... squeeze... and relax."
   - Record value. Note if non-dominant hand used (fistula).

3. **Sit-to-stand** (patient still in chair):
   - "Now please cross your arms over your chest."
   - "When I say go, stand up and sit down as many times as you can in 30 seconds."
   - Count reps. Record.

4. **Gait speed** (walk to exam room):
   - If hallway tape is in place: "Please walk at your normal speed to the mark."
   - Time the 4-meter segment. Calculate speed. Record.
   - If no hallway tape, perform in exam room or skip with note.

5. **Record all three values** on the screening form (see Section 5).

6. **Flag for provider** if ANY value is below threshold:
   - Place a flag sticker on the encounter form, OR
   - Note in Epic rooming flowsheet, OR
   - Verbally alert provider: "Grip/STS/gait speed was low today."

## 7. Provider Interpretation Guide

When the MA flags a low value, the provider reviews during the encounter.

### Interpretation Framework

| Test | Below Threshold | Clinical Implication | Action |
|------|----------------|---------------------|--------|
| Grip strength | M <27kg / F <16kg | Probable sarcopenia (EWGSOP2). Independent predictor of CV mortality (HR 1.7-2.3). | Confirm with DXA/BIA for muscle mass (Tier 2). Dietary protein review. Consider PT referral. |
| STS <10 reps | <10 in 30 sec | Lower extremity weakness. Fall risk. Reduced functional capacity. | Fall risk assessment (Section 30). PT referral. Home exercise program. |
| Gait speed | <0.8 m/s | Mobility limitation. Predicts hospitalization, cognitive decline, mortality. | Comprehensive geriatric assessment if age >65. Consider PT. Rule out neurological causes. |

### Severity Grading

| Grade | Criteria | Action Level |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| **Mild** | 1 of 3 tests low, close to threshold | Monitor. Repeat next visit. Education on exercise. |
| **Moderate** | 2 of 3 tests low, OR 1 test significantly below threshold | PT referral. Nutrition consult. Tier 2 assessment consideration. |
| **Severe** | All 3 tests low, OR grip <20kg (M) / <10kg (F), OR gait speed <0.4 m/s | Urgent PT referral. Comprehensive sarcopenia workup. Tier 2 assessment. Consider frailty screening (CFS). |

### Trending

Track values visit-over-visit. A decline of >5% per year in grip strength or gait speed is clinically significant even if still above threshold.

## 8. Training Plan

### Initial Training (1 hour)

| Module | Duration | Content |
|--------|----------|---------|
| Why we screen | 10 min | CKD-sarcopenia link, mortality data, TKE's mission |
| Equipment demo | 10 min | Jamar dynamometer setup, calibration check, positioning |
| Grip strength practice | 10 min | Each MA practices on 2-3 colleagues |
| STS practice | 10 min | Counting, timing, edge cases (arms used, partial reps) |
| Gait speed practice | 10 min | Marking the course, timing technique, conversion table |
| Recording & flagging | 10 min | Form completion, when to flag, how to communicate to provider |

### Competency Check

After training, each MA demonstrates all 3 tests on a colleague. Trainer verifies:
- [ ] Correct dynamometer positioning (handle II, elbow 90 degrees)
- [ ] Correct STS instructions and counting
- [ ] Correct gait speed timing (start/stop at lines, not at patient movement)
- [ ] Correct recording on paper form
- [ ] Knows thresholds and when to flag
- [ ] Knows special considerations (fistula, wheelchair, assistive devices)

### Ongoing

- Monthly: spot-check one MA performing screening (10 min observation)
- Quarterly: review screening compliance rate (target >90%)
- Annual: dynamometer calibration + refresher training

## 9. Pilot Plan

### Week 1-2: Soft Launch

| Day | Activity |
|-----|----------|
| Day 1 | Equipment arrives. Set up gait speed tape in hallway. |
| Day 2 | MA training session (1 hour, during lunch or before clinic). |
| Day 3-5 | Pilot: 1 MA screens 3-5 patients per day. Dr. Mulay observes. |
| Day 6-10 | Expand: all MAs screen all CKD patients. Collect timing data. |

### Week 3-4: Full Implementation

- All MAs screening all CKD patients at every visit
- Measure: time added per patient (target <2 min)
- Measure: MA compliance rate (target >90%)
- Measure: provider finds flag useful (informal feedback)
- Adjust workflow based on feedback

### Go/No-Go Criteria

| Criteria | Threshold | Action if Not Met |
|----------|-----------|-------------------|
| Average screening time | <= 2.5 min | Simplify to grip-only (30 sec) |
| MA compliance | >= 80% by week 4 | Re-train, identify barriers |
| Provider finds value | Positive feedback from Dr. Mulay | Continue |
| Patient complaints | < 5% | Review communication script |

## 10. Patient Communication Script

**MA says**: "We're going to do three quick physical tests today. This helps Dr. Mulay track your strength and balance over time, which is important for your kidney health."

**If patient asks why**: "Research shows that grip strength and walking speed are connected to heart and kidney health. By measuring these regularly, we can catch changes early and get you help if needed."

**If patient declines**: "That's okay. I'll make a note that you preferred not to do the screening today. We can try again next visit if you'd like."

**If value is low**: (MA does NOT tell the patient the value is "low" or abnormal.) "Dr. Mulay will review your results during your visit today."
