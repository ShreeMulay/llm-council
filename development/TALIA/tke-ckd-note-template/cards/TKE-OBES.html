WHAT THIS MEANS FOR YOU
Understanding Weight & Kidney Health
Extra weight puts stress on your kidneys and can speed up kidney disease. It also raises your risk for diabetes and high blood pressure. Losing even a small amount of weight can help protect your kidneys.
YOUR TARGETS
  • BMI: Less than 30 kg/m² (or as set by your doctor)
  • Weight Loss Goal: 5-10% of body weight over 6-12 months
  • Activity: 150 minutes per week of moderate exercise
WHAT YOU CAN DO
  • Set small, realistic goals - even 5% weight loss helps
  • Eat more vegetables, lean proteins, and whole grains
  • Limit sugary drinks, fried foods, and large portions
  • Move more - walking counts!
  • Take weight medications as prescribed
WHEN TO CALL US
  • Rapid weight gain (more than 3 lbs in a day)
  • Swelling in legs or feet
  • Side effects from weight medications
  • Questions about diet or exercise with kidney disease
