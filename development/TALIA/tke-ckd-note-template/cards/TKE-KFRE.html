WHAT THIS MEANS FOR YOU
Understanding Your Kidney Risk Score
The KFRE (Kidney Failure Risk Equation) uses your age, kidney function, and protein in your urine to estimate your chance of needing dialysis or a transplant in the next 2-5 years. This helps us plan your care.
YOUR RISK LEVEL
  • Low risk (<5%): Continue current care, regular follow-up
  • Moderate risk (5-15%): More frequent monitoring, optimize medications
  • High risk (15-40%): Start planning for future kidney care options
  • Very high risk (>40%): Active preparation for dialysis or transplant
WHAT YOU CAN DO
  • Take all your kidney-protective medicines as prescribed
  • Keep your blood pressure and blood sugar in target range
  • Avoid NSAIDs (ibuprofen, naproxen) and other kidney-harmful medicines
  • Follow a kidney-friendly diet (low salt, appropriate protein)
  • Ask about transplant options early - living donors are best
WHEN TO CALL US
  • New or worsening swelling, shortness of breath
  • Nausea, loss of appetite, or metallic taste
  • Confusion or difficulty concentrating
  • Questions about transplant or dialysis options
