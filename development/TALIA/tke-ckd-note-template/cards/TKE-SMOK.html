WHAT THIS MEANS FOR YOU
Smoking & Your Kidneys
Smoking damages blood vessels throughout your body, including in your kidneys. It speeds up kidney disease and raises your risk of heart attack and stroke. Quitting is one of the best things you can do for your health.
YOUR GOAL
  • Target: Tobacco-free (no cigarettes, no vaping)
  • Benefits start immediately: Blood pressure drops within 20 minutes of quitting
  • Long-term: Kidney function decline slows significantly
WHAT YOU CAN DO
  • Set a quit date and tell family/friends
  • Ask about nicotine patches, gum, or medications
  • Call the Quitline: 1-800-QUIT-NOW (free coaching)
  • Avoid triggers - change routines that make you want to smoke
  • Don't give up if you slip - most people try several times before quitting for good
