WHAT THIS MEANS FOR YOU
Understanding Kidney Biopsy
A kidney biopsy is a procedure where a small piece of kidney tissue is removed and examined under a microscope. This helps us understand exactly what is causing your kidney disease.
WHY THIS MATTERS
  • Provides a definite diagnosis of your kidney condition
  • Helps us choose the best treatment for your specific disease
  • Shows how much scarring is present in your kidneys
  • Helps predict how your kidney disease may progress
WHAT YOU CAN DO
  • Follow all pre-procedure instructions carefully
  • Rest for 24 hours after the biopsy
  • Take all prescribed medications as directed
  • Attend all follow-up appointments
WHEN TO CALL US
  • Blood in urine that doesn't clear within 24 hours
  • Severe pain at the biopsy site
  • Fever or chills after the procedure
  • Dizziness or feeling faint
