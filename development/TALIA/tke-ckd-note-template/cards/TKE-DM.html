WHAT THIS MEANS FOR YOU
Understanding Diabetes & Kidney Health
Diabetes is one of the leading causes of kidney disease. High blood sugar over time damages the tiny filters in your kidneys. Good blood sugar control can slow or stop this damage.
YOUR TARGETS
  • HbA1c: Usually less than 7%, but your doctor may set a different goal
  • Fasting Blood Sugar: 80-130 mg/dL
  • After Meals: Less than 180 mg/dL
WHAT YOU CAN DO
  • Take your diabetes medicines as prescribed
  • Check your blood sugar as directed
  • Eat regular, balanced meals - limit sweets and sugary drinks
  • Stay active - even short walks help
  • Get your annual eye and foot exams
WHEN TO CALL US
  • Blood sugar stays above 300 mg/dL
  • Blood sugar drops below 70 mg/dL (low blood sugar)
  • Feeling very thirsty, urinating a lot, or losing weight
  • Numbness or tingling in hands or feet
