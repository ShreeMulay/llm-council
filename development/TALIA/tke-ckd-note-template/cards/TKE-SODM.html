WHAT THIS MEANS FOR YOU
Why Limit Sodium?
Too much sodium (salt) raises blood pressure and causes fluid retention. Both of these stress your kidneys and heart. Limiting sodium helps your blood pressure medicines work better and reduces swelling.
YOUR TARGET
  • Less than 2,000 mg sodium per day
  • That's about 1 teaspoon of table salt
  • Most sodium comes from processed and restaurant foods, not the salt shaker
HIGH-SODIUM FOODS TO LIMIT
  • Canned soups, vegetables, and sauces
  • Deli meats, bacon, sausage, hot dogs
  • Fast food and restaurant meals
  • Chips, pretzels, salted snacks
  • Frozen dinners and pizza
  • Pickles, olives, soy sauce
TIPS FOR SUCCESS
  • Read labels - look for <140mg sodium per serving
  • Cook at home more often
  • Use herbs, spices, lemon, and vinegar for flavor
  • Ask for sauces/dressings on the side when eating out
  • Choose "low sodium" or "no salt added" products
