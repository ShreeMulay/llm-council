WHAT THIS MEANS FOR YOU
Understanding Protein in Your Urine
Your kidneys filter your blood and normally keep protein inside your body. When protein leaks into your urine, it can be a sign that your kidneys need extra care. Finding and treating this early helps protect your kidneys.
YOUR TARGETS
  • UACR: Less than 30 mg/g is the goal
  • Trend: We want to see your numbers going down or staying stable
WHAT YOU CAN DO
  • Take your blood pressure and kidney medicines every day
  • Keep your blood pressure under control (goal: less than 120/80)
  • If you have diabetes, keep your blood sugar in target range
  • Limit salt in your diet (less than 2 grams per day)
  • Stay active - aim for 30 minutes of walking most days
WHEN TO CALL US
  • Swelling in your legs, ankles, or around your eyes
  • Foamy or bubbly urine
  • Sudden weight gain (more than 3 pounds in a day)
  • Feeling very tired or short of breath
