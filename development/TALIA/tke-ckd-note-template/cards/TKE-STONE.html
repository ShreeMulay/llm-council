WHAT THIS MEANS FOR YOU
Understanding Kidney Stones
Kidney stones are hard deposits made of minerals and salts that form inside your kidneys. Once you've had one stone, you're more likely to have another. Prevention is key!
YOUR TARGETS
  • Urine output: At least 2.5 liters (about 10 cups) per day
  • Urine color: Light yellow to clear
  • Goal: No new stones!
WHAT YOU CAN DO
  • Drink plenty of water throughout the day
  • Limit salt intake (less than 2,300 mg/day)
  • Eat moderate amounts of protein
  • Get enough calcium from food (not supplements)
  • Limit oxalate-rich foods if you have calcium oxalate stones
WHEN TO CALL US
  • Severe pain in your back or side
  • Blood in your urine
  • Fever with pain (may indicate infection)
  • Unable to urinate
