WHAT THIS MEANS FOR YOU
Understanding Electrolytes
Electrolytes are minerals in your blood that your body needs to work properly. Your kidneys normally keep these balanced. When kidneys don't work well, potassium can get too high and bicarbonate can get too low.
YOUR TARGETS
  • Potassium: 3.5-5.0 mEq/L (too high is dangerous)
  • Bicarbonate: 22 or higher (prevents acid buildup)
  • Sodium: 136-145 mEq/L
WHAT YOU CAN DO
  • Limit high-potassium foods if your K+ is high: bananas, oranges, potatoes, tomatoes
  • Take bicarbonate supplements as prescribed
  • Take potassium binders with meals if prescribed
  • Get regular lab work to monitor levels
  • Tell us about itchy skin (pruritus)
WHEN TO CALL US
  • Muscle weakness, numbness, or tingling
  • Irregular heartbeat or palpitations
  • Severe nausea or vomiting
  • Confusion or difficulty breathing
