WHAT THIS MEANS FOR YOU
Understanding PPIs & Kidneys
PPIs (proton pump inhibitors) like omeprazole and pantoprazole reduce stomach acid. While helpful for heartburn, long-term use may affect your kidneys. We want to make sure you're only taking them if truly needed.
COMMON PPIs
  • Omeprazole: Prilosec
  • Pantoprazole: Protonix
  • Esomeprazole: Nexium
  • Lansoprazole: Prevacid
WHAT YOU CAN DO
  • Don't stop your PPI suddenly without talking to us first
  • Ask if you still need it - some people can safely stop
  • Try lifestyle changes: smaller meals, avoid late eating, elevate head of bed
  • Avoid trigger foods: spicy, fatty, acidic foods, caffeine
  • Consider H2 blockers (like famotidine) as a safer alternative
WHEN TO CALL US
  • Severe heartburn not controlled by medication
  • Difficulty swallowing
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Blood in stool or vomit
