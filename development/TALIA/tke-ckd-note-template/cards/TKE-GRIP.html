WHAT THIS MEANS FOR YOU
Why We Measure Grip Strength
Grip strength is a simple way to check your overall muscle strength. Weak muscles can be a sign of frailty, which is common in kidney disease. Tracking this helps us catch problems early and keep you strong.
YOUR TARGETS
  • Men: 27 kg or higher
  • Women: 16 kg or higher
  • Goal: Maintain or improve over time
HOW TO BUILD STRENGTH
  • Squeeze a stress ball or tennis ball daily
  • Do resistance exercises (even with soup cans!)
  • Eat enough protein to build muscle
  • Stay active - walking helps overall strength
  • Ask about physical therapy or renal rehab
WHY IT MATTERS
  • Strong muscles help you stay independent
  • Better strength = lower fall risk
  • Muscle health affects kidney health
  • Strength can improve at any age!
