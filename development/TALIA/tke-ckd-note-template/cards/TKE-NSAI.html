WHAT THIS MEANS FOR YOU
Why Avoid NSAIDs?
NSAIDs (like ibuprofen and naproxen) can hurt your kidneys, especially when you already have kidney disease. They reduce blood flow to your kidneys and can cause sudden kidney damage.
MEDICINES TO AVOID
  • Ibuprofen: Advil, Motrin, Nuprin
  • Naproxen: Aleve, Naprosyn
  • Others: Celebrex, Mobic, Voltaren, Indocin
  • Combination products: Excedrin, Midol, some cold medicines
SAFER ALTERNATIVES FOR PAIN
  • Acetaminophen (Tylenol): Safe for kidneys when used as directed
  • Topical creams: Lidocaine patches, capsaicin cream
  • Heat/ice therapy
  • Physical therapy
  • Ask your doctor about prescription options if needed
IMPORTANT TIPS
  • Always check labels - NSAIDs are in many products
  • Tell all your doctors you have kidney disease
  • Ask the pharmacist before taking any new OTC medicine
  • Call us before taking any pain medicine you're unsure about
