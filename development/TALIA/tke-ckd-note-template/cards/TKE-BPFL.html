WHAT THIS MEANS FOR YOU
Understanding Blood Pressure & Fluid
High blood pressure is one of the main causes of kidney disease and can make it worse. Controlling your blood pressure is one of the most important things you can do to protect your kidneys.
YOUR TARGETS
  • Blood Pressure: Less than 120/80 mmHg
  • Sodium: Less than 2 grams (2000 mg) per day
  • Fluid: No swelling in legs or ankles
WHAT YOU CAN DO
  • Take blood pressure medicines every day, even when you feel fine
  • Check your blood pressure at home and keep a log
  • Limit salt in your diet - avoid processed foods
  • Weigh yourself daily - sudden weight gain means fluid
  • Stay active - aim for 30 minutes of walking most days
WHEN TO CALL US
  • Blood pressure consistently above 140/90 at home
  • New or worsening swelling in legs, ankles, or feet
  • Sudden weight gain (more than 3 pounds in a day)
  • Shortness of breath, especially when lying down
  • Dizziness or lightheadedness
