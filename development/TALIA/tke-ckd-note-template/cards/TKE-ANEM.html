WHAT THIS MEANS FOR YOU
Understanding Anemia & Kidney Disease
Your kidneys make a hormone called EPO that tells your body to make red blood cells. When kidneys are damaged, they make less EPO, which can cause anemia (low blood count). This makes you feel tired and weak.
YOUR TARGETS
  • Hemoglobin: 10-12 g/dL (not too low, not too high)
  • Ferritin: Greater than 100 ng/mL
  • TSAT: 20-50%
WHAT YOU CAN DO
  • Take iron supplements as directed (with vitamin C, not with dairy)
  • Eat iron-rich foods: lean red meat, beans, spinach, fortified cereals
  • Keep all appointments for IV iron or ESA injections
  • Get lab work done as scheduled
  • Report increased fatigue or shortness of breath
WHEN TO CALL US
  • Severe fatigue or weakness
  • Shortness of breath with normal activities
  • Chest pain or rapid heartbeat
  • Black or bloody stools
