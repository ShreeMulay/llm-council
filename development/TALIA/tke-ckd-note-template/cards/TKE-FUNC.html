WHAT THIS MEANS FOR YOU
Why We Test Your Function
These simple tests tell us how strong and steady you are. Kidney disease can affect your muscles and balance. By checking these regularly, we can catch problems early and help you stay active and independent.
YOUR TARGETS
  • Walking speed: 0.8 meters/second or faster
  • Sit-to-stand: 10 or more times in 30 seconds
  • TUG test: Less than 12 seconds
  • 6-minute walk: 350 meters or more
WHAT YOU CAN DO
  • Walk daily - even 10 minutes helps
  • Practice standing from a chair without using hands
  • Do balance exercises (stand on one foot)
  • Eat enough protein to maintain muscle
  • Ask about renal rehabilitation programs
WHEN TO CALL US
  • You notice sudden weakness or fatigue
  • You have a fall or near-fall
  • Walking becomes much harder
  • You feel unsteady on your feet
