WHAT THIS MEANS FOR YOU
Why Vaccines Matter for Kidney Patients
Kidney disease can weaken your immune system, making infections more dangerous. Vaccines help protect you from serious illnesses. Some vaccines are especially important if you're considering a transplant.
RECOMMENDED VACCINES
  • Flu shot: Every year in the fall
  • Pneumonia (PCV20): One dose for most adults
  • Hepatitis B: Series of 3-4 shots (required for transplant)
  • COVID: Stay up to date with boosters
  • Shingles (Shingrix): 2 doses if age 50+
  • Tdap: Every 10 years
HEPATITIS B - IMPORTANT!
  • Kidney patients are at higher risk for Hepatitis B
  • You need immunity BEFORE a transplant
  • May need higher doses or more shots
  • We check your blood to confirm immunity
WHERE TO GET VACCINES
  • At our office during your visit
  • Your primary care doctor
  • Local pharmacy (CVS, Walgreens, etc.)
  • Health department
