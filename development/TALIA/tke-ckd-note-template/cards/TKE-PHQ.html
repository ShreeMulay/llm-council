WHAT THIS MEANS FOR YOU
Understanding Depression & Kidney Disease
Depression is common in people with kidney disease - you're not alone. It's a real medical condition that can be treated. Taking care of your mental health is just as important as taking care of your kidneys.
SIGNS OF DEPRESSION
  • Feeling sad, hopeless, or empty
  • Loss of interest in things you used to enjoy
  • Changes in sleep or appetite
  • Feeling tired all the time
  • Trouble concentrating
  • Thoughts of death or suicide
WHAT YOU CAN DO
  • Talk to your doctor - treatment helps
  • Stay connected with family and friends
  • Try to stay active, even short walks help
  • Join a support group for kidney patients
  • Consider counseling or therapy
