WHAT THIS MEANS FOR YOU
Understanding Gout & Kidney Health
Gout happens when uric acid builds up in your blood and forms crystals in your joints. High uric acid can also harm your kidneys. Keeping uric acid low protects both your joints and kidneys.
YOUR TARGETS
  • Uric Acid: Less than 6.0 mg/dL
  • Goal: No gout flares
  • Tophi: Shrinking or gone
WHAT YOU CAN DO
  • Take your gout medicine every day, even when you feel fine
  • Drink plenty of water (unless you have fluid restrictions)
  • Limit red meat, organ meats, and shellfish
  • Avoid sugary drinks and alcohol (especially beer)
  • Keep a healthy weight
WHEN TO CALL US
  • Sudden severe joint pain, redness, or swelling
  • Frequent gout flares despite treatment
  • New lumps under your skin (tophi)
  • Side effects from gout medications
