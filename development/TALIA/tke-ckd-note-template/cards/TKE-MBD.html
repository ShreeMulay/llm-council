WHAT THIS MEANS FOR YOU
Understanding Bone Health & Kidneys
Your kidneys help keep your bones strong by balancing calcium, phosphorus, and vitamin D. When kidneys don't work well, these get out of balance, which can weaken your bones and cause other problems.
YOUR TARGETS
  • Vitamin D: 30-50 ng/mL
  • Calcium: 8.4-10.2 mg/dL
  • Phosphorus: 2.5-4.5 mg/dL
  • PTH: Depends on your CKD stage
WHAT YOU CAN DO
  • Take vitamin D and phosphate binders as prescribed
  • Limit high-phosphorus foods: dairy, processed foods, dark colas
  • Take phosphate binders WITH meals (they bind phosphorus in food)
  • Get regular lab work to monitor levels
  • Stay active to keep bones strong
WHEN TO CALL US
  • Bone pain or muscle cramps
  • Itchy skin that won't go away
  • Muscle weakness or twitching
  • Confusion or memory problems
