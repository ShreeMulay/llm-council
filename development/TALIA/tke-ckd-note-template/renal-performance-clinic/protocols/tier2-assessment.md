## 3. Short Physical Performance Battery (SPPB)

**Total time**: ~15 minutes
**Scoring**: 0-12 points (sum of 3 components)

### 3.1 Balance Tests (0-4 points)

#### Side-by-Side Stand
1. Demonstrate the position: feet together, side by side
2. Say: "Try to stand with your feet together, side by side, for 10 seconds."
3. Start timing when patient achieves position
4. Stop if patient moves feet or grabs support

**Scoring**:
- Held 10 seconds: proceed to semi-tandem
- Held <10 seconds or unable: 0 points for balance, skip to gait speed

#### Semi-Tandem Stand
1. Demonstrate: heel of one foot beside big toe of other foot
2. Say: "Now try to stand with the heel of one foot next to the big toe of the other foot for 10 seconds."
3. Patient chooses which foot is forward
4. Time for 10 seconds

**Scoring**:
- Held 10 seconds: proceed to tandem
- Held <10 seconds: 1 point for balance, skip to gait speed

#### Tandem Stand (Heel-to-Toe)
1. Demonstrate: heel of one foot directly in front of and touching toes of other foot
2. Say: "Now try to stand with the heel of one foot directly in front of the other foot for 10 seconds."
3. Patient chooses which foot is forward
4. Time for 10 seconds

**Balance Scoring Summary**:

| Result | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Unable to hold side-by-side for 10 sec | 0 |
| Side-by-side only (not semi-tandem) | 1 |
| Semi-tandem but not tandem | 2 |
| Tandem 3.00-9.99 seconds | 3 |
| Tandem 10.00 seconds | 4 |

---

### 3.2 Gait Speed Test (0-4 points)

**Course**: 4 meters (13.1 feet) with 1-meter acceleration and deceleration zones

#### Procedure
1. Patient stands with toes at start line
2. Say: "Walk at your usual pace to the other end of the course."
3. Start timing when first foot crosses start line
4. Stop timing when first foot crosses finish line
5. Perform TWO trials
6. Record BEST time

**Gait Speed Scoring**:

| Time (seconds) | Gait Speed (m/s) | Points |
|----------------|------------------|--------|
| Unable | - | 0 |
| >8.70 sec | <0.46 m/s | 1 |
| 6.21-8.70 sec | 0.46-0.64 m/s | 2 |
| 4.82-6.20 sec | 0.65-0.83 m/s | 3 |
| <4.82 sec | >0.83 m/s | 4 |

**Recording**:
- Time 1: _______ seconds
- Time 2: _______ seconds
- Best time: _______ seconds
- Gait speed: _______ m/s (= 4 / best time)
- Points: _______

---

### 3.3 Chair Stand Test (0-4 points)

**Equipment**: Armless chair against wall

#### Pre-Test (Single Chair Stand)
1. Patient sits in middle of chair, feet flat, arms crossed over chest
2. Say: "Stand up one time without using your arms."
3. If unable: score 0 for chair stands, end SPPB

#### Timed 5x Chair Stand
1. If single stand successful, say: "Now stand up and sit down 5 times as quickly as you can."
2. Start timing on "Go"
3. Stop timing when patient stands the 5th time
4. Patient must fully stand and fully sit each time

**Chair Stand Scoring**:

| Time (seconds) | Points |
|----------------|--------|
| Unable or >60 sec | 0 |
| >16.70 sec | 1 |
| 13.70-16.69 sec | 2 |
| 11.20-13.69 sec | 3 |
| <11.20 sec | 4 |

**Recording**:
- Single stand: [ ] Successful [ ] Unable
- 5x time: _______ seconds
- Points: _______

## 4. 6-Minute Walk Test (6MWT)

**Purpose**: Assess functional exercise capacity
**Time**: ~10 minutes (including rest and vitals)

### 4.1 Contraindications

**Absolute**:
- Unstable angina in past month
- MI in past month
- Resting HR >120 bpm
- Resting BP >180/100 mmHg
- Resting SpO2 <85% on room air

**Relative** (proceed with caution):
- Resting HR >100 bpm
- Resting BP >160/90 mmHg
- SpO2 <90% on room air

### 4.2 Equipment & Setup

- 30-meter (100 ft) flat, straight corridor
- Cones at each end
- Stopwatch
- Pulse oximeter
- Blood pressure cuff
- Chair (for rest if needed)
- Borg RPE scale
- Supplemental O2 if patient uses it

### 4.3 Pre-Test

1. Patient rests seated for 10 minutes
2. Record baseline vitals:
   - HR: _______ bpm
   - BP: _______/_______ mmHg
   - SpO2: _______% on [ ] RA [ ] O2 at _____ L/min
   - Borg dyspnea: _______/10
   - Borg fatigue: _______/10

### 4.4 Instructions to Patient

"The object of this test is to walk as far as possible for 6 minutes. You will walk back and forth in this hallway. Six minutes is a long time to walk, so you will be exerting yourself. You are permitted to slow down, to stop, and to rest as necessary. You may lean against the wall while resting, but resume walking as soon as you are able.

You will be walking back and forth around the cones. You should pivot briskly around the cones and continue back the other way without hesitation. Now I'm going to show you. Please watch the way I turn without hesitation."

**Demonstrate one lap.**

"Are you ready to do that? I am going to use this counter to keep track of the number of laps you complete. Remember that the object is to walk AS FAR AS POSSIBLE for 6 minutes, but don't run or jog.

Start now, or whenever you are ready."

### 4.5 During Test

- Start timer when patient begins walking
- Do NOT walk with patient
- Use standardized encouragement phrases ONLY at each minute:
  - 1 min: "You are doing well. You have 5 minutes to go."
  - 2 min: "Keep up the good work. You have 4 minutes to go."
  - 3 min: "You are doing well. You are halfway done."
  - 4 min: "Keep up the good work. You have only 2 minutes left."
  - 5 min: "You are doing well. You have only 1 minute to go."
  - 15 sec before end: "In a moment I'm going to tell you to stop. When I do, just stop right where you are."
  - 6 min: "Stop!"

- Record number of laps and distance of partial lap
- If patient stops: note time stopped, reason, and when resumed
- If patient cannot continue: record distance at stop point

### 4.6 Post-Test

Immediately after stopping:
- HR: _______ bpm
- SpO2: _______% 
- Borg dyspnea: _______/10
- Borg fatigue: _______/10

After 1 minute rest:
- HR: _______ bpm
- SpO2: _______%

### 4.7 Distance Calculation

- Number of complete laps: _______
- Distance per lap: 60 meters (30m x 2)
- Partial lap distance: _______ meters
- **Total distance: _______ meters**

### 4.8 Interpretation

| Distance | Interpretation |
|----------|----------------|
| >450 m | Normal functional capacity |
| 350-450 m | Mild limitation |
| 200-349 m | Moderate limitation |
| <200 m | Severe limitation |

**CKD-specific thresholds**:
- <350 m: Poor prognosis, correlates with CV mortality
- <300 m: Consider cardiac evaluation
- <200 m: Significant functional impairment

## 5. Timed Up and Go (TUG)

**Purpose**: Assess mobility and fall risk
**Time**: ~2 minutes

### 5.1 Setup
- Armless chair with seat height ~46 cm (18 inches)
- Cone or tape mark 3 meters (10 feet) from chair front legs
- Clear path between chair and mark

### 5.2 Procedure
1. Patient sits with back against chair, arms on armrests
2. Say: "When I say 'Go,' stand up, walk to the line on the floor at your normal pace, turn around, walk back to the chair, and sit down."
3. Start timing on "Go"
4. Stop timing when patient's buttocks touch the seat

### 5.3 Scoring

| Time | Interpretation |
|------|----------------|
| <10 sec | Normal mobility |
| 10-12 sec | Borderline |
| 12-20 sec | Increased fall risk |
| >20 sec | High fall risk, impaired mobility |

**TUG Time: _______ seconds**

## 6. Frailty Assessment

### 6.1 Fried Frailty Phenotype (5 criteria)

| Criterion | Definition | Present? |
|-----------|------------|----------|
| **Weight loss** | Unintentional loss >10 lbs (4.5 kg) in past year | [ ] Yes [ ] No |
| **Exhaustion** | Self-reported: "Everything I did was an effort" or "I could not get going" >=3 days/week | [ ] Yes [ ] No |
| **Low physical activity** | Men: <383 kcal/week; Women: <270 kcal/week | [ ] Yes [ ] No |
| **Slow walking speed** | 4m gait speed in lowest 20% by gender/height | [ ] Yes [ ] No |
| **Weakness** | Grip strength in lowest 20% by gender/BMI | [ ] Yes [ ] No |

**Scoring**:
- 0 criteria: Robust
- 1-2 criteria: Pre-frail
- 3+ criteria: Frail

**Fried Score: _______/5 = [ ] Robust [ ] Pre-frail [ ] Frail**

### 6.2 Clinical Frailty Scale (CFS)

| Score | Category | Description |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | Very fit | Robust, active, energetic, motivated. Exercise regularly. |
| 2 | Well | No active disease symptoms but less fit than category 1. |
| 3 | Managing well | Medical problems well controlled but not regularly active. |
| 4 | Vulnerable | Not dependent but symptoms limit activities. "Slowed up." |
| 5 | Mildly frail | More evident slowing, need help with high-order IADLs. |
| 6 | Moderately frail | Need help with all outside activities and housekeeping. |
| 7 | Severely frail | Completely dependent for personal care. |
| 8 | Very severely frail | Completely dependent, approaching end of life. |
| 9 | Terminally ill | Life expectancy <6 months. |
