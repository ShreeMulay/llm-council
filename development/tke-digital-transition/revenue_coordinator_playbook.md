## WEEKLY RHYTHM

### Monday: PIPELINE REVIEW (1 hour)
1. Open the TKE Command Center → CCM Pipeline tab
2. Review current enrollment numbers vs. weekly target
3. Identify patients who were flagged as "eligible but not enrolled" in the past week
4. Prioritize call list: newest eligible patients first (warm from recent visit)
5. Check for any CCM patients who missed their monthly touchpoint (at risk of billing gap)

### Tuesday-Thursday: ENROLLMENT & TOUCHPOINT CALLS (3-4 hours/day)

**For NEW CCM Enrollments:**
1. Call patient. Script:
   > "Hi [Patient Name], this is [Your Name] from The Kidney Experts. Dr. Mulay's team has identified that you may benefit from our Chronic Care Management program. This is a free service covered by Medicare where we check in with you monthly to help manage your kidney health, medications, and any concerns. Can I tell you a little more about it?"
2. If interested: Explain the program, obtain verbal consent (document date/time), schedule first CCM call
3. If declined: Note reason in CCM Pipeline tab. Flag for re-approach in 90 days.
4. Goal: **Minimum 10 new enrollments per week across all locations**

**For EXISTING CCM Patients (Monthly Touchpoints):**
1. Call each enrolled patient at least once per month (minimum 20 minutes of billable time)
2. Document: medication adherence, new symptoms, barriers to care, social determinants
3. Log time in CCM tracking system (minimum 20 min for 99490, 40 min for 99439)
4. Flag clinical concerns for APP/MD review

**For TCM Capture:**
1. Check hospital discharge notifications daily (from local hospitals, fax system, or EHR alerts)
2. For every TKE patient discharged: schedule follow-up within 2 business days (phone) and in-person within 7-14 days
3. Ensure TCM codes (99495 high-complexity / 99496 moderate) are billed
4. Document: discharge summary reviewed, medication reconciliation completed, follow-up plan confirmed

### Friday: REPORTING & PLANNING (1 hour)
1. Update CCM Pipeline tab with weekly numbers
2. Calculate: new enrollments, total enrolled, enrollment rate, minutes logged, TCM captures
3. Flag any patients at risk of dropping out
4. Prepare 3-sentence summary for Monday leadership huddle
5. Review RPM device deployment status (once program launches)

## CCM ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

A patient qualifies for CCM if ALL of the following are true:
1. **Diagnosis:** CKD Stage 3-5 (or any 2+ chronic conditions expected to last 12+ months)
2. **Risk:** Conditions place patient at significant risk of death, acute exacerbation, or functional decline
3. **Consent:** Patient (or caregiver) has provided verbal or written consent
4. **No Conflicts:** Patient is not enrolled in another practice's CCM program
5. **Medicare:** Patient has Medicare Part B (or commercial payer that covers CCM codes)

## COMPLIANCE GUARDRAILS

1. **ALWAYS obtain consent before enrolling.** Document date, time, method (verbal/written), and who provided consent.
2. **NEVER bill CCM without documented, billable clinical activity.** Time spent on administrative tasks (scheduling, filing) does not count.
3. **ALWAYS perform medication reconciliation** as part of CCM touchpoints.
4. **NEVER enroll a patient in CCM who is already enrolled with another provider's CCM program.** Ask the patient.
5. **ALWAYS document at least 20 minutes of clinical staff time** per month per CCM patient before submitting a claim.
6. **TCM billing requires:** (a) discharge summary review, (b) medication reconciliation, (c) face-to-face visit within 7 or 14 days.
7. **RPM billing requires:** (a) device transmitting data for at least 16 days per month, (b) at least 20 minutes of clinical staff time reviewing/acting on data.

## ESCALATION TRIGGERS

Contact Brandy Carr immediately if:
- A patient reports a new acute symptom during a CCM call
- A patient is non-adherent to critical medications (RAASi, SGLT2i, Finerenone)
- A patient's BP reading (via RPM) is >180/120 or <90/60
- A patient expresses suicidal ideation or severe depression
- A patient requests to leave the practice

Contact Ashley Carter immediately if:
- A CCM claim is denied by a payer
- You discover a patient is enrolled in another provider's CCM
- A payer requests documentation for a CCM/TCM audit
