## PHASE 1: INITIAL APPROACH & VALUATION (2-4 Weeks)

### Step 1: The First Conversation
**Who:** Shree and/or Anna, in person (not email, not phone)
**Where:** Lunch or coffee, their turf
**Tone:** Peer-to-peer, not buyer-to-seller

**Key messages:**
- "We know how hard solo practice is. We've been there."
- "Your patients deserve continuity. We can provide that."
- "Your staff can keep their jobs. We need good people."
- "We're not PE. We're two nephrologists who want to keep your patients off dialysis."

**What to learn:**
- Why are they considering selling/retiring?
- What is the patient panel composition (CKD vs ESRD)?
- Which staff would stay?
- What are the lease terms?
- Any pending litigation or compliance issues?
- What EHR are they using?
- What is their relationship with local hospitals and dialysis units?

### Step 2: Valuation Framework

**Method 1: Revenue Multiple (Primary)**
- Distressed/retiring practice: **0.5-0.8x trailing 12-month revenue**
- Healthy practice: **0.8-1.2x revenue**
- Example: $1M revenue practice = $500K-$800K acquisition price

**Method 2: EBITDA Multiple (Validation)**
- Small practice: **3-5x adjusted EBITDA**
- Adjust EBITDA: Add back owner compensation above market rate ($400K benchmark)
- Example: $1M revenue, $200K owner comp above market, $100K EBITDA → Adjusted EBITDA $300K → Price $900K-$1.5M

**Method 3: Patient Value (Sanity Check)**
- Value per active CKD patient: **$500-$1,000**
- Value per active ESRD patient: **$300-$500** (declining value for TKE's model)
- Example: 800 CKD + 200 ESRD patients → $400K-$900K

**Use all three methods. Offer at the lower end. Negotiate.**

### Step 3: Letter of Intent (LOI)
**Draft with healthcare M&A attorney.** Budget: $2,500-$5,000 for LOI + initial legal.

**LOI must include:**
- Purchase price range (or formula)
- Asset purchase vs. stock purchase (PREFER ASSET PURCHASE — cleaner, less liability)
- Transition period: Selling physician available for 60-90 days for patient introductions
- Staff retention commitment: TKE intends to retain all willing staff
- Exclusivity period: 60-90 days for due diligence
- Confidentiality clause

## PHASE 2: DUE DILIGENCE (4-6 Weeks)

### Financial Due Diligence

- [ ] 3 years of tax returns (personal if sole proprietor, corporate if entity)
- [ ] 3 years of P&L statements
- [ ] Trailing 12-month revenue by CPT code
- [ ] Trailing 12-month revenue by payer
- [ ] Accounts receivable aging report (>90 days = risk)
- [ ] Collections rate (good: >95%, concerning: <90%)
- [ ] Outstanding debt or liens
- [ ] Lease terms and assignment provisions
- [ ] Equipment list and condition
- [ ] Malpractice insurance history and tail coverage requirements

### Clinical Due Diligence

- [ ] Patient panel breakdown by CKD stage
- [ ] ESRD patient count and dialysis unit relationships
- [ ] Active medication protocols (how far from TKE standards?)
- [ ] Any active clinical trials?
- [ ] Hospital privileging and call coverage arrangements
- [ ] Any quality complaints or state board actions?

### Operational Due Diligence

- [ ] Staff roster with tenure, compensation, and roles
- [ ] Benefits currently provided (health insurance, PTO, retirement)
- [ ] Which staff are likely to stay? (Ask directly — have Brandy interview them)
- [ ] Referral source analysis (who sends patients? Will they continue?)
- [ ] Current EHR and data migration feasibility
- [ ] Vendor contracts (labs, supplies, cleaning, IT)
- [ ] HIPAA compliance status

### Legal Due Diligence

- [ ] Corporate structure and ownership
- [ ] Any pending or past litigation
- [ ] Non-compete agreements (selling physician AND staff)
- [ ] Medicare/Medicaid enrollment status
- [ ] State licensure and facility certifications
- [ ] OSHA compliance

**Engage a healthcare M&A attorney ($10K-$20K for full due diligence and closing docs).**
**Engage a healthcare-specific CPA for financial due diligence ($3K-$5K).**

## PHASE 3: CLOSE THE DEAL (2-4 Weeks)

### Financing Options

| Source | Amount | Terms | Pros | Cons |
|--------|--------|-------|------|------|
| **SBA 7(a) Loan** | Up to $5M | 10-year, ~7-8% interest, 10-20% down | Government-backed, long term | Slow (60-90 days), personal guarantee |
| **Bank Term Loan** | $250K-$2M | 5-7 year, 8-10% | Faster than SBA | Higher rate, shorter term |
| **Seller Financing** | 20-50% of price | 3-5 year, 5-7% | Aligns seller interest in transition | Seller may want more upfront |
| **Cash** | Variable | N/A | Fastest, cheapest | Depletes reserves |

**Recommended: 50% seller financing + 50% SBA or bank loan.** This keeps the selling physician invested in a smooth transition and preserves your cash.

### Closing Day Checklist
- [ ] Asset Purchase Agreement executed
- [ ] Bill of sale for equipment and supplies
- [ ] Assignment of lease (or new lease negotiation)
- [ ] Patient notification letters mailed (joint letter from both physicians)
- [ ] Staff retention letters issued (with new compensation terms)
- [ ] Medicare/Medicaid enrollment transfer or new application filed
- [ ] Malpractice tail coverage confirmed (seller's responsibility)
- [ ] EHR data export/migration plan activated
- [ ] Marketing materials updated (website, Google Business listing)

## GATE CHECK: Ready for Acquisition #2?

**All must be GREEN before pursuing a second deal:**

| Gate Criterion | Measurement | Threshold |
|----------------|-------------|-----------|
| Location Profitability | Monthly P&L | Positive within 6 months |
| Standardization Rate | Report card completion | >50% within 90 days |
| Staff Retention | Headcount vs. Day 1 | >80% at 6 months |
| Patient Retention | Return visits / acquired panel | >70% at 6 months |
| Integration Timeline | Days from close to full reporting | <90 days |
| Leadership Bandwidth | Can Shree/Brandy handle another? | Honest assessment |
| Cash Position | Post-acquisition reserves | >3 months operating expenses |

**If any criterion is RED, fix it before acquiring again. Scaling a broken integration model just scales the breakage.**
