## Part 2: The 6 Levels of AI Literacy

AI literacy isn't binary (you either "get it" or you don't). It's a spectrum. Everyone starts somewhere, and the goal is **continuous growth**, not perfection.

### Level 0: Unaware

- **Characteristics:** No exposure to AI; may fear job replacement; unfamiliar with tools like ChatGPT or Gemini
- **Mindset:** "AI is going to take my job" or "I don't understand what AI even is"
- **Training Focus:** Foundational mindset workshop; address fears with facts
- **Signs you're at this level:**
  - You've never used an AI chatbot
  - You think AI is only for tech companies
  - You're worried AI will eliminate your position

### Level 1: Aware

- **Characteristics:** Knows AI exists; has used basic tools (voice assistants, autocomplete); understands AI won't replace their role
- **Mindset:** "I've heard of ChatGPT but haven't really used it for work"
- **Training Focus:** Introduction to practical applications; see AI as helpful, not threatening
- **Signs you're at this level:**
  - You use Siri/Alexa/Google Assistant occasionally
  - You've seen AI in the news but haven't tried it yourself
  - You're curious but unsure where to start

### Level 2: Explorer

- **Characteristics:** Experiments with AI (Gemini, Claude, ChatGPT); can write basic prompts; understands AI makes mistakes
- **Mindset:** "I've tried it a few times—sometimes it's helpful, sometimes not"
- **Training Focus:** Structured prompting techniques; when/how to verify outputs
- **Signs you're at this level:**
  - You've asked ChatGPT or Gemini questions a few times
  - You sometimes get good results, sometimes not
  - You're not sure why some prompts work better than others

### Level 3: Practitioner

- **Characteristics:** Uses AI regularly in work; crafts effective prompts; knows when to verify; integrates into some workflows
- **Mindset:** "AI is part of my toolkit—I use it for drafting, summarizing, and brainstorming"
- **Training Focus:** Advanced workflow integration; role-specific applications
- **Signs you're at this level:**
  - You use AI at least weekly for work tasks
  - You can write prompts that usually get good results
  - You know to double-check clinical information
  - You've saved time on specific tasks using AI

### Level 4: Integrator

- **Characteristics:** Embeds AI deeply into daily workflows; teaches others; identifies new use cases; improves team efficiency
- **Mindset:** "I can't imagine doing my job without AI now. Let me show you how I use it."
- **Training Focus:** Mentorship skills; custom prompt development; cross-functional applications
- **Signs you're at this level:**
  - AI is part of your daily routine
  - Colleagues ask you for AI tips
  - You've created your own prompts or templates
  - You actively look for new ways to use AI

### Level 5: Champion

- **Characteristics:** Drives AI adoption across the practice; develops custom workflows; contributes to practice AI strategy; represents TKE externally
- **Mindset:** "How can we use AI to transform kidney care delivery?"
- **Training Focus:** Strategic AI initiatives; innovation projects; external speaking/publishing
- **Signs you're at this level:**
  - You lead AI training sessions
  - You contribute to the TKE prompt library
  - You help evaluate new AI tools
  - You present on AI at conferences or meetings

## Part 3: The Three Dimensions of AI Literacy

AI literacy isn't just about knowing which buttons to click. It's a combination of **mindset**, **capability**, and **application**.

### Dimension 1: AI Mindset

This is the most important dimension—and where most people get stuck.

#### The Wrong Mindset: AI as Tool

- "Just tell me what to type"
- "If it gives me a wrong answer, it's useless"
- "I don't have time for this"
- "AI will replace my job eventually"

**Result:** 10× less effective outcomes

#### The Right Mindset: AI as Teammate

- "How can we solve this together?"
- "Let me refine my question to get a better answer"
- "What context does AI need from me to help?"
- "AI makes my expertise more valuable, not less"

**Result:** 10× better outcomes

> **Research from Stanford University shows that people who treat AI as a collaborative teammate achieve 10× better outcomes than those who treat it as a simple tool.**

#### The Mindset Shift Explained

| Tool Users | Teammate Collaborators |
|------------|------------------------|
| Give commands and expect perfect answers | Provide context, iterate, and verify together |
| Blame AI when outputs are wrong | Refine prompts to get better outputs |
| Use AI occasionally when forced | Integrate AI into daily practice |
| See AI as threat | See AI as amplifier |

At TKE, we expect **teammate collaboration**. AI is not a vending machine. It's more like a very smart, very fast research assistant who needs direction and verification.

---

### Dimension 2: AI Capability

These are the practical skills you need to work effectively with AI:

#### Core Skills

1. **Effective Prompting** — Writing clear, context-rich requests that get useful outputs
2. **Output Verification** — Critically evaluating AI responses (especially for clinical content)
3. **Iterative Refinement** — Providing feedback to improve AI outputs
4. **Appropriate Tool Selection** — Knowing when to use AI vs. when not to

#### The PACT Framework for Prompting

| Element | Description | Example |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **P**urpose | What do you need? | "Write a prior authorization letter" |
| **A**udience | Who is this for? | "...to Blue Cross Blue Shield" |
| **C**ontent | What details are needed? | "...for Krystexxa for a CKD Stage 4 patient with chronic tophaceous gout who failed allopurinol and febuxostat" |
| **T**one | How should it sound? | "...professional and persuasive, citing medical necessity" |

#### Verification Checklist

Before using any AI output in clinical or professional work:

- [ ] Are drug names, dosages, and interactions correct?
- [ ] Are clinical recommendations consistent with guidelines (KDIGO/KDOQI)?
- [ ] Are patient-specific details accurate (not invented)?
- [ ] Are dates, lab values, and numbers correct?
- [ ] Does this make sense based on my clinical judgment?

## Part 4: The 5 Rules for AI Prompting at TKE

Print this. Tape it to your monitor. Refer to it daily.

### Rule 1: Be Specific

**Bad:** "Write a letter"

**Good:** "Write a prior authorization letter for Krystexxa for a CKD Stage 4 patient with chronic tophaceous gout who has failed allopurinol and febuxostat"

The more specific your request, the better the output. Include:
- What you need (letter, summary, list, explanation)
- For whom (insurance company, patient, referring physician)
- Key clinical details (diagnosis, medications, lab values)
- Desired format or length

---

### Rule 2: Provide Context

AI doesn't know what you know. Give it the information it needs:

**Example prompt with context:**

> "As a nephrology practice writing to Blue Cross Blue Shield, draft a prior authorization request for Krystexxa infusion. The patient has CKD Stage 4 (eGFR 18), chronic tophaceous gout with visible tophi on both hands, and has failed maximum-dose allopurinol (300mg daily for 6 months with uric acid remaining >9) and febuxostat (discontinued due to elevated LFTs). Request approval for the full 6-month course per manufacturer protocol."

**Context elements to include:**
- Your role/perspective ("As a nephrology practice...")
- The recipient ("...writing to Blue Cross Blue Shield...")
- Relevant clinical details
- Desired outcome

---

### Rule 3: TKE Workspace Only

**This is critical for HIPAA compliance.**

The Kidney Experts has a **Business Associate Agreement (BAA) with Google**. This means you **CAN** use Protected Health Information (PHI) when:

✅ You are logged into your **@thekidneyexperts.com** Google account
✅ You are using **Google Gemini** within our Workspace
✅ You are on TKE-approved devices and networks

**You CANNOT use PHI when:**

❌ Using personal ChatGPT, Claude, or other AI accounts
❌ Using AI tools on personal devices
❌ Sharing patient information with non-BAA-covered platforms

> **Simple rule:** If you're using your TKE Google account and TKE Gemini, you're safe. If you're anywhere else, **de-identify everything** or don't use patient data at all.

---

### Rule 4: Verify Everything

AI is a teammate, not the final signer. **You are always responsible for the output.**

**Always verify:**
- Drug names, dosages, and interactions (AI can hallucinate medications)
- Clinical recommendations (cross-reference with guidelines)
- Patient-specific details (AI may confuse or invent information)
- Dates, lab values, and numerical data
- Any factual claims or statistics

**The rule:** Trust, but verify. Every time.

**What "verification" looks like:**
- Quick spot-check for obvious errors
- Cross-reference with known sources (UpToDate, KDIGO, etc.)
- Apply your clinical/professional judgment
- If something seems wrong, it probably is—dig deeper

---

### Rule 5: Iterate and Refine

Your first prompt rarely produces the perfect output. That's normal and expected.

**Effective iteration looks like:**
- "Make this more concise—aim for 2 paragraphs"
- "Add a section about the patient's transplant candidacy"
- "Rewrite this at an 8th-grade reading level for the patient"
- "The tone is too formal—make it warmer and more conversational"
- "Include the specific lab values I mentioned"
- "Format this as a bulleted list instead"

Think of it as a conversation, not a command. The more you guide the AI, the better the result.

**Pro tip:** Save successful prompts as templates for future use.

## Part 7: Common Questions and Concerns

### "Will AI replace my job?"

**No.** AI will change your job—by taking over the parts you probably don't enjoy anyway (repetitive documentation, routine communications, data gathering). Your human skills (clinical judgment, patient relationships, empathy, complex problem-solving) become MORE valuable, not less.

### "What if AI makes a mistake and I use it?"

**You are always responsible for the final output.** That's why verification is Rule #4. AI is a teammate that drafts, suggests, and accelerates—but you are the professional who reviews, edits, and signs off.

### "I'm not good with technology. Can I still learn this?"

**Absolutely.** AI literacy is more about mindset than technical skill. If you can have a conversation, you can use AI. We'll start at your level and build from there.

### "Is it safe to use patient information with AI?"

**Within TKE's Google Workspace and Gemini, yes.** We have a Business Associate Agreement (BAA) with Google that covers HIPAA compliance. Just never use patient information on personal accounts or non-approved platforms.

### "How much time will this take from my day?"

**Initially, training takes a few hours.** Once you're proficient, AI *saves* you time—often 30-60 minutes per day on documentation, communication, and research tasks.

### "What if I already know how to use AI?"

**Great!** Take the assessment to confirm your level, and consider becoming an AI Champion to help train others.
