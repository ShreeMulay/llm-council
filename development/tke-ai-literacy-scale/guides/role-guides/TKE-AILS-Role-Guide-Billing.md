## Your Role in the AI-Powered Practice

### The Billing Team's Mission

At The Kidney Experts, the billing team is essential to our mission. Without successful reimbursement, we can't sustain the innovative care that keeps patients off dialysis.

With AI, your role evolves from **form-filler** to **Revenue Cycle Strategist**:

| Traditional Billing | AI-Augmented Billing |
|--------------------|---------------------|
| Fill out PA forms manually | AI drafts clinical narratives |
| Write appeals from scratch | AI generates compelling appeals |
| Hope for approval | Anticipate and address denial reasons |

At Level 3 (Practitioner), you:

## Top 10 AI Use Cases

### Use Case 1: Prior Authorization - Standard Request

**Scenario:** Patient needs SGLT2 inhibitor; insurance requires PA.

**Prompt Template:**
```
Draft a prior authorization letter for:

Medication: [Drug name, dose, frequency]
Insurance: [Payer name]

Patient information:
- Diagnosis: CKD Stage [X], eGFR [X], UACR [X]
- Comorbidities: [Diabetes, HTN, Heart Failure, etc.]
- Reason for request: [Why this medication is needed]

Clinical justification:
- Failed alternatives: [List prior medications tried, why failed/inadequate]
- Guideline support: [KDIGO 2024 recommends SGLT2i for CKD with UACR >200]
- Expected benefit: [Slow CKD progression, reduce cardiovascular events]

Include:
1. Clear statement of medical necessity
2. Clinical evidence supporting the request
3. Guideline citations
4. Request for expedited review if urgent

Format: Professional prior authorization letter
Tone: Factual, compelling, evidence-based
```

**Verification:** Confirm all clinical details match the chart; verify guideline citations are accurate.

### Use Case 2: Prior Authorization - High-Cost Specialty Drug

**Scenario:** Patient needs Krystexxa (pegloticase) for refractory gout.

**Prompt Template:**
```
Draft a comprehensive prior authorization for a high-cost biologic:

Medication: Krystexxa (pegloticase) infusion per FDA-approved protocol
Insurance: [Payer]

Patient profile:
- Diagnosis: Chronic refractory tophaceous gout
- CKD Stage: [X] (eGFR [X])
- Duration of gout: [X] years
- Number and location of tophi: [Describe]
- Uric acid level: [Current, target]

Failed conventional therapy (REQUIRED):
1. Allopurinol: [Max dose tried, duration, reason for failure]
2. Febuxostat: [Dose, duration, reason for failure/discontinuation]
3. Other ULTs: [If any]

Why Krystexxa is medically necessary:
- Refractory to oral therapy despite maximum doses
- Ongoing joint destruction / tophi burden
- CKD limits options (contraindications to probenecid, etc.)
- Quality of life significantly impacted

Supporting evidence:
- KDIGO gout guidelines
- ACR guidelines for gout management
- FDA indication for refractory chronic gout

Request:
- Full 6-month treatment course per manufacturer protocol
- Infusions every 2 weeks at certified infusion center

Include request for peer-to-peer if initially denied.
```

**Verification:** Ensure all failed therapies are documented in the chart; confirm clinical details.

### Use Case 3: Step Therapy Override

**Scenario:** Insurance requires step therapy; patient has clinical reason to bypass.

**Prompt Template:**
```
Draft a step therapy exception request:

Requested medication: [Drug name]
Insurance-required step: [What insurance wants patient to try first]
Reason for exception: [Why patient should skip the required step]

Clinical justification:
- Patient has already tried [required step medication]: [Dates, doses, outcome]
- OR: [Required step medication] is contraindicated because: [Reason - allergy, interaction, clinical status]
- OR: Delay in treatment would cause: [Irreversible harm, progression, etc.]

Supporting documentation:
- [Reference relevant labs, notes, or prior records]
- [Cite guidelines if they support skipping the step]

Request: Approve [requested medication] without requiring [step therapy drug]

Format: Exception request letter
Cite: Insurance policy exception criteria if known
```

**Verification:** Confirm the clinical reason for exception is documented in the chart.

### Use Case 4: Denial Appeal - Initial Appeal

**Scenario:** Prior authorization was denied; need to appeal.

**Prompt Template:**
```
Draft an appeal for a denied prior authorization:

Denied medication: [Drug name, dose]
Denial date: [Date]
Denial reason: [Quote or summarize the denial letter]
Insurance: [Payer]
Appeal level: [First appeal / Second appeal / External review]

Our counter-argument:
[Address each denial reason specifically]

Additional evidence to include:
- [New clinical information since initial request]
- [More detailed documentation of prior treatment failures]
- [Updated guidelines or literature supporting use]
- [Specialist opinion if available]

Structure:
1. Reference the denial and our disagreement
2. Address each denial reason point by point
3. Provide additional clinical evidence
4. Cite relevant guidelines and literature
5. Request approval based on medical necessity
6. Request peer-to-peer review

Tone: Professional, assertive, evidence-based
Attach: [List of supporting documents to include]
```

**Verification:** Ensure counter-arguments directly address the denial reasons; verify all clinical details.

### Use Case 5: Peer-to-Peer Preparation

**Scenario:** Insurance medical director will call to discuss the case.

**Prompt Template:**
```
Prepare talking points for a peer-to-peer review:

Case summary:
- Patient: [Brief clinical profile]
- Requested treatment: [Medication/procedure]
- Denial reason: [What insurance cited]

Key points to make:
1. Why this patient specifically needs this treatment
2. What has been tried and why it failed
3. Guideline support for the request
4. What happens if the patient doesn't get this treatment
5. Why alternatives suggested by the payer won't work

Anticipate objections:
- If they say "[common objection]" → respond with [counter-argument]
- If they ask about [alternative] → explain why [it won't work for this patient]

Have ready:
- Specific lab values and dates
- Timeline of prior treatments
- Guideline citations (KDIGO, ACR, etc.)
- Any relevant specialty society statements

Goal: Approval for [specific request]
```

**Verification:** Review talking points with provider before the call; have chart open during peer-to-peer.

### Use Case 6: Transplant Referral Authorization

**Scenario:** Patient needs authorization for transplant center evaluation.

**Prompt Template:**
```
Draft a prior authorization for transplant center evaluation:

Patient profile:
- Age: [X]
- CKD Stage: [4 or 5]
- eGFR: [X] (or dialysis-dependent)
- Etiology: [Cause of kidney disease]
- Time on dialysis (if applicable): [Duration]

Transplant candidacy factors:
- No absolute contraindications: [Confirm]
- Life expectancy: [Expected to benefit from transplant]
- Functional status: [Able to undergo surgery and recovery]
- Social support: [Has support system]

Why transplant evaluation is medically necessary:
- ESRD or imminent ESRD
- Transplant is the best long-term treatment
- Pre-emptive transplant preferred when possible
- Improves survival and quality of life vs. dialysis

Requested: Authorization for comprehensive transplant evaluation at [Center Name]

Include: Reference to KDIGO transplant guidelines and CMS criteria for transplant candidacy.
```

**Verification:** Confirm patient meets general transplant criteria; verify no documented contraindications.

### Use Case 7: Infusion/Procedure Authorization

**Scenario:** Patient needs authorization for dialysis access procedure or infusion therapy.

**Prompt Template:**
```
Draft a prior authorization for a nephrology procedure:

Procedure: [Fistula creation / Catheter placement / Iron infusion / ESA therapy / Bone biopsy / etc.]
CPT code(s): [If known]
Facility: [Where procedure will be done]

Patient profile:
- Diagnosis: [ESRD, CKD Stage, anemia, etc.]
- Reason procedure is needed: [Clinical justification]

Medical necessity:
- [Why this procedure at this time]
- [What happens without the procedure]
- [Any prior related procedures]

For dialysis access:
- Dialysis modality planned: [HD/PD]
- Timeline to dialysis: [Imminent / within 6 months / etc.]
- Preferred access type and reason: [Fistula preferred per KDOQI]

For infusion therapy:
- Indication: [Iron deficiency anemia, etc.]
- Oral therapy failed: [Yes/No, details]
- Labs supporting need: [Ferritin, TSAT, Hgb, etc.]

Request: Single authorization / Series of treatments
```

**Verification:** Confirm procedure details with clinical team; verify CPT codes are current.

### Use Case 9: Coding Documentation Support

**Scenario:** Provider needs guidance on documentation to support billing level.

**Prompt Template:**
```
Review this documentation for billing code support:

Proposed code: [E/M level or procedure code]
Service date: [Date]

Documentation:
[Paste relevant portions of the note]

Evaluate:
1. Does the documentation support the proposed code?
2. What elements are present that support the code?
3. What elements might be missing or weak?
4. Suggestions for strengthening documentation (for future)

Note: This is for compliance review, not clinical advice.
```

**Verification:** Consult coding guidelines (CPT, CMS) if uncertain; escalate complex questions to coding specialist.

### Use Case 10: Denial Pattern Analysis

**Scenario:** Want to understand why we're getting denied for a particular drug/service.

**Prompt Template:**
```
Help me analyze a denial pattern:

Medication/Service: [What we keep getting denied for]
Insurance: [Payer or all payers]
Common denial reasons: [List the reasons we're seeing]

Questions:
1. What might we be missing in our submissions?
2. What information should we include proactively?
3. Are there specific criteria we need to address?
4. What documentation would make denials less likely?

Based on these denial patterns, suggest improvements to our PA submission process.
```

**Verification:** Cross-reference with actual payer policies if available; discuss with clinical team.

## Appeals & Denials

### Appeal Strategy

**Level 1 Appeal:** Address denial reason directly with more evidence
**Level 2 Appeal:** Escalate with stronger clinical argument, literature
**External Review:** Independent review if internal appeals exhausted
**Peer-to-Peer:** Request medical director discussion

### Using AI for Appeals

1. **Analyze the denial:** What specifically did they deny and why?
2. **Draft counter-arguments:** Address each denial point
3. **Add new evidence:** What can we provide that we didn't before?
4. **Cite guidelines:** What authoritative sources support our request?
5. **Prepare for peer-to-peer:** What will we say to their medical director?

## Common Pitfalls & Solutions

### Pitfall 1: Incorrect Clinical Information

**Problem:** AI drafts a letter with wrong lab values or medications.

**Solution:** ALWAYS verify clinical information against the chart. AI drafts, you verify.

**Rule:** If you didn't copy it from the chart yourself, double-check it.

---

### Pitfall 2: Outdated Guideline Citations

**Problem:** AI cites old guidelines that may have been updated.

**Solution:** Verify guideline citations are current. KDIGO 2024 is the current CKD guideline. When in doubt, check the source.

---

### Pitfall 3: Generic Letters That Don't Fit

**Problem:** AI generates a generic letter that doesn't address the specific case.

**Solution:** Include patient-specific details in your prompt. The more context you provide, the better the output.

---

### Pitfall 4: Sending AI Output Without Review

**Problem:** Errors go to the insurance company because no one reviewed the letter.

**Solution:** Every AI-generated letter must be reviewed and edited before sending. Treat AI as a first draft, not a final product.

---

### Pitfall 5: Missing Required Elements

**Problem:** AI doesn't know what the specific payer requires.

**Solution:** Use AI to draft the narrative, but YOU ensure all required fields and attachments are included based on payer requirements.

## Quick Reference Card

### The 5 Rules (Billing Version)

1. **Be Specific** — Include diagnosis, drug, dose, denial reason, payer
2. **Provide Context** — Prior treatments, lab values, why this patient needs this
3. **TKE Workspace Only** — PHI only in @thekidneyexperts.com Gemini
4. **Verify Everything** — Check every medication, every lab value, every date
5. **Iterate & Refine** — "Make it more compelling," "Add the guideline citation"

### Verification Checklist

Before sending ANY PA or appeal:

- [ ] Patient name and DOB correct?
- [ ] Insurance information accurate?
- [ ] Medication name, dose, and frequency correct?
- [ ] Clinical information matches the chart?
- [ ] Guideline citations are accurate?
- [ ] All required attachments included?

### AI Is Great For

✅ Drafting medical necessity statements
✅ Creating compelling narratives
✅ Organizing clinical evidence
✅ Citing guidelines appropriately
✅ Structuring appeals logically
✅ Preparing peer-to-peer talking points

### AI Is NOT For

❌ Replacing chart review
❌ Generating clinical information
❌ Final quality check (that's you)
❌ Knowing payer-specific requirements
❌ Submitting without review
