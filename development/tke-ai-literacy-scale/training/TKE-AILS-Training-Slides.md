## Signs You're Here:
- "What is ChatGPT?"
- "AI will take my job"
- "I don't trust computers"

3. Ask a colleague to show you one thing

# Level 1: Aware

## Signs You're Here:
- You use Siri/Alexa/Google Assistant
- You've heard of ChatGPT/Gemini
- You haven't tried it for work yet

## How to Level Up:
1. Log into TKE Workspace Gemini
2. Ask it one simple question
3. See what happens!

> **Goal:** Take the first step

---

# Level 2: Explorer

## Signs You're Here:
- You've tried AI a few times
- Your prompts are short: "Write an email"
- You copy-paste outputs without editing

## How to Level Up:
1. Add context to your prompts
2. Ask follow-up questions
3. Edit the output (don't just accept it)

> **Goal:** Develop the habit of iteration

---

# Level 3: Practitioner

## Signs You're Here:
- You use AI multiple times per week
- Your prompts include context and goals
- You verify everything before using it

## How to Level Up:
1. Create prompt templates for repeated tasks
2. Teach a colleague one technique
3. Explore new use cases in your role

> **Goal:** Consistency and reliability

---

# Level 4: Integrator

## Signs You're Here:
- AI is part of your daily workflow
- You create templates and shortcuts
- Colleagues come to you for help

## How to Level Up:
1. Document your best prompts
2. Present to your team
3. Identify new opportunities

> **Goal:** Multiply your impact through others

# Level 5: Champion

## Signs You're Here:
- You drive AI adoption across teams
- You develop new use cases
- Leadership asks your opinion on AI strategy

## How to Stay Here:
1. Stay current with new AI capabilities
2. Measure and report ROI

# The 5 Prompting Rules

<!-- _backgroundColor: #0056b3 -->
<!-- _color: white -->

## Master These, Master AI

1. **Be Specific**
2. **Provide Context**
3. **TKE Workspace Only** (PHI Rule)
4. **Verify Everything**
5. **Iterate & Refine**

---

# Rule 1: Be Specific

## Bad Prompt:
> "Write an email"

## Good Prompt:
> "Write a professional email to a patient explaining their lab results show improved kidney function. Include congratulations, what this means, and next steps. Keep it under 150 words."

### The Formula:
**Task + Details + Format + Constraints**

---

# Rule 2: Provide Context

## Bad Prompt:
> "Explain CKD to a patient"

## Good Prompt:
> "Explain Stage 3 CKD to a 65-year-old retired teacher with diabetes. They are newly diagnosed, anxious, and have no medical background. Use simple language, avoid jargon, and include a metaphor. This is for a printed handout."

### What Context Includes:
- Who is the audience?
- What's their background?
- What's the purpose?
- Where will it be used?

---

# Rule 3: TKE Workspace Only

<!-- _backgroundColor: #dc2626 -->
<!-- _color: white -->

## THE PHI RULE

**TKE has a BAA with Google.**

This means PHI **IS safe** in:
- TKE Workspace (your @thekidneyexperts.com account)
- TKE Gemini

PHI is **NEVER safe** in:
- Personal Gmail
- Personal ChatGPT
- Any non-TKE account

---

# Rule 3: Practical Examples

## SAFE (TKE Workspace):
> "Review Mr. Smith's labs from 1/5/2026 and summarize the changes in his creatinine over the past 6 months."

## NOT SAFE (Personal Account):
> Same prompt on personal Gmail/ChatGPT

## If in doubt:
**Am I logged into my @thekidneyexperts.com account?**
- Yes → Proceed
- No → STOP. Switch accounts.

---

# Rule 4: Verify Everything

## AI is a teammate, not the final signer

### The Verification Checklist:
- [ ] Are the facts correct?
- [ ] Are the numbers accurate?
- [ ] Does this match our protocols?
- [ ] Would I put my name on this?

### Remember:
> "Trust but verify" — AI can hallucinate (make things up)

---

# Rule 5: Iterate & Refine

## Don't accept the first draft!

### The Iteration Pattern:

1. **Get initial output**
2. **Identify what's missing or wrong**
3. **Ask for specific changes:**
   - "Make it shorter"
   - "Add more detail about X"
   - "Use simpler language"
   - "Format as bullet points"
4. **Repeat until satisfied**

> AI conversations are free — keep chatting!

---

**Memorize these. Use them every time.**

# Common Mistakes to Avoid

## DON'T:
- Skip verification ("It looked right...")
- Use personal accounts for work
- Accept first drafts without editing
- Assume AI knows your context
- Share AI failures publicly (learn privately)

## DO:
- Verify every output
- Use TKE Workspace only
- Iterate until it's right
- Provide rich context
- Share successes with your team

---

# When AI Struggles

## AI is NOT good at:

| Task | Why |
|------|-----|
| **Real-time data** | It doesn't browse the internet live |
| **Recent events** | Knowledge has a cutoff date |
| **Math calculations** | Use a calculator instead |
| **Counting** | Surprisingly unreliable |
| **Your specific protocols** | Unless you tell it |

### The fix:
**Provide the information it needs in your prompt**

# Appendix: More Templates

```
Draft an HPI for a nephrology visit.

```
Create a pre-visit summary.

Patient: [demographics]

Relevant Labs: [paste recent results]

Format: Bullet points, 1 page max

# Appendix: More Templates
