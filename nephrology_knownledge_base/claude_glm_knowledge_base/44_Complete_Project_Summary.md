## **Latest Knowledge Base Updates (2024-2025)**

### **Transformative Clinical Advances**

#### **1. SGLT2 Inhibitors - Paradigm Shift in CKD Management**
- **MAJOR GUIDELINE CHANGE**: Now recommended for ALL CKD patients (not just diabetic)
- **Medications**: Dapagliflozin (Farxiga) 10 mg daily, Empagliflozin (Jardiance) 10 mg daily
- **Indications**: CKD with eGFR ≥20 mL/min/1.73m² regardless of diabetes status
- **Evidence**: EMPA-KIDNEY, DAPA-CKD trials - 40% reduction in kidney disease progression
- **Impact**: Updated throughout Documents 01, 02, 07, 17, 22, 23

#### **2. Finerenone (Kerendia) - 4th Pillar for Diabetic CKD**
- **FDA Approval**: 2021, expanded evidence 2024
- **Dosing**: 10-20 mg daily for diabetic CKD
- **Evidence**: FIDELIO-DKD, FIGARO-DKD trials - Reduced CKD progression and CV events
- **4-Pillar Therapy**: ACEi/ARB + SGLT2i + Finerenone + Statin
- **Impact**: Updated throughout Documents 02, 07, 22, 23

#### **3. Sparsentan (Filspari) - First-Line for IgA Nephropathy**
- **FDA Approval**: February 2023
- **Mechanism**: Dual endothelin/angiotensin receptor antagonist
- **Dosing**: 200-400 mg daily
- **Evidence**: PROTECT trial (2023) - 40-50% proteinuria reduction vs irbesartan
- **Indication**: IgA nephropathy with proteinuria >1 g/day
- **Impact**: Updated throughout Documents 03, 22, 23

#### **4. Iptacopan (Fabhalta) - Dual Indication: IgA Nephropathy AND C3 Glomerulopathy**
- **FDA Approvals**:
  - IgA nephropathy: August 2024 (accelerated approval)
  - **C3 glomerulopathy: 2025** (accelerated approval)
- **Mechanism**: Oral Factor B inhibitor (alternative complement pathway)
- **Dosing**: 200 mg BID
- **Evidence**:
  - APPLAUSE-IgAN trial (2024): 44% proteinuria reduction vs 9% placebo (p<0.0001)
  - APPEAR-C3G trial: 35% proteinuria reduction at 6 months
  - October 2025: Positive final eGFR results in IgAN
- **Indication**: Dual indication for IgAN and C3G
- **Impact**: Updated throughout Documents 03, 12, 22

#### **5. Avacopan (Tavneos) - Steroid-Sparing Agent for ANCA Vasculitis**
- **FDA Approval**: October 2021
- **Mechanism**: Selective C5a receptor antagonist (complement inhibitor)
- **Dosing**: 30 mg PO BID × 52 weeks
- **Evidence**: ADVOCATE trial (2021) - Superior sustained remission vs prednisone (65.7% vs 54.9%, p=0.007)
- **Key Benefit**: 60% reduction in cumulative steroid dose, better renal outcomes
- **Indication**: Adjunct to rituximab/cyclophosphamide as alternative to glucocorticoid taper in ANCA vasculitis
- **Critical AE**: Hepatotoxicity (BOXED WARNING) - requires monthly LFT monitoring
- **Impact**: Added throughout Documents 03, 05, 12, 22

#### **6. Semaglutide (Ozempic) - First GLP-1 Approved for CKD** ⭐ **JANUARY 2025**
- **FDA Approval**: January 28, 2025 for CKD protection - **PARADIGM SHIFT**
- **Historic Significance**: First and ONLY GLP-1 receptor agonist with specific FDA indication for kidney disease
- **Indication**: Reduce risk of kidney disease progression, kidney failure, and cardiovascular death in adults with T2DM + CKD
- **Mechanism**: GLP-1 receptor agonist with multiple nephroprotective effects
- **Dosing**: 0.5 mg SC weekly (initiation), titrate to 1 mg weekly; safe down to eGFR 25 mL/min
- **Evidence**:
  - **FLOW Trial (2024)**: Stopped early for overwhelming benefit
  - 24% reduction in kidney disease progression (primary endpoint)
  - 18% reduction in major adverse cardiovascular events (MACE)
  - First GLP-1 trial with hard kidney endpoint as primary outcome
- **5-Pillar Therapy**: ACEi/ARB + SGLT2i + Finerenone + Statin + **Semaglutide** (new standard for diabetic CKD)
- **Place in Therapy**:
  - Add to diabetic CKD patients with inadequate glycemic control
  - Consider in obesity (BMI ≥30) or established cardiovascular disease
  - Synergistic with SGLT2 inhibitors and finerenone
- **Board Significance**: Highest-yield 2025 approval - expect exam questions
- **Impact**: Updated throughout Documents 02, 12, 19, 22

#### **7. Atrasentan (Vanrafia) - Selective Endothelin A Receptor Antagonist** ⭐ **APRIL 2025**
- **FDA Approval**: April 2, 2025 for IgA nephropathy (accelerated approval)
- **Historic Significance**: First-and-only selective endothelin A receptor antagonist for kidney disease
- **Indication**: Reduce proteinuria in primary IgA nephropathy at risk of rapid disease progression
- **Mechanism**: Selective endothelin A receptor antagonist (ET-A selective, spares ET-B receptors)
- **Dosing**: 0.75 mg PO once daily
- **Evidence**:
  - **ALIGN Trial (2024-2025)**: Published NEJM October 2024
  - 36.1% proteinuria reduction vs placebo at 36 weeks (primary endpoint)
  - 37.4% reduction in patients also on SGLT2 inhibitors (synergistic effect)
  - Well-tolerated with acceptable fluid retention profile
- **Place in Therapy**:
  - Alternative to sparsentan for IgAN patients with proteinuria >1 g/day
  - Particularly valuable in patients already on SGLT2 inhibitors
  - May have better fluid retention profile than non-selective endothelin antagonists
- **Critical Warnings**:
  - Teratogenic - BOXED WARNING (pregnancy testing required)
  - Fluid retention/edema (monitor weight, blood pressure)
  - Contraindicated with other endothelin antagonists
- **Board Significance**: Newest IgAN therapy - know approval date and selective ET-A mechanism
- **Impact**: Updated throughout Documents 03, 12, 22

#### **8. Pegcetacoplan (Empaveli) - First Treatment for C3 Glomerulopathy** ⭐ **JULY 2025**
- **FDA Approval**: July 28, 2025 for C3 glomerulopathy and primary IC-MPGN (patients ≥12 years)
- **Historic Significance**: **FIRST-EVER FDA-approved treatment for C3 glomerulopathy** - breakthrough for rare disease
- **Previous Approvals**: Paroxysmal nocturnal hemoglobinuria (PNH) in 2021
- **Indication**: Treatment of C3 glomerulopathy (C3G) and primary immune complex membranoproliferative glomerulonephritis (IC-MPGN)
- **Mechanism**: C3 complement inhibitor (pegylated peptide) - blocks both classical and alternative complement pathways
- **Dosing**: 1080 mg SC twice weekly (subcutaneous injection)
- **Evidence**:
  - **VALIANT Trial (Phase 3, 2025)**: Landmark trial
  - 68.3% relative reduction in proteinuria vs placebo (mean UPCR reduction -67.3% vs +3.2%)
  - Stabilization of kidney function (eGFR preserved)
  - 71% complete clearance of C3c staining on repeat kidney biopsy vs placebo
  - 26-week primary endpoint with sustained benefit
- **Place in Therapy**:
  - FIRST-LINE therapy for C3 glomerulopathy and primary IC-MPGN
  - Consider early (before significant fibrosis develops on biopsy)
  - May prevent progression to end-stage renal disease (ESRD)
  - Alternative: Iptacopan (oral, Factor B inhibitor) also approved for C3G in 2025
- **Critical Requirements**:
  - Meningococcal vaccination required (due to complement blockade infection risk)
  - Monitor for serious infections
  - Very high cost (~$400,000-500,000/year) - prior authorization required
- **Board Significance**: First-ever C3G treatment - expect exam questions on mechanism and VALIANT trial data
- **Impact**: Updated throughout Documents 03, 12, 22

### **FDA-Cleared Novel Biomarkers (2024)**

#### **1. NephroCheck (TIMP-2 × IGFBP7)**
- **FDA Clearance**: 2014, expanded use 2024
- **Indication**: AKI risk stratification in ICU patients
- **Cutoff**: >0.3 (moderate-high AKI risk), >2.0 (very high risk)
- **Evidence**: SAPPHIRE trial - 40% AKI reduction with biomarker-guided care
- **Clinical Use**: Auto-order for high-risk ICU patients, implement KDIGO bundle if >0.3
- **Impact**: Updated throughout Documents 01, 20, 21, 22, 23, 38

#### **2. Prospera (Donor-Derived Cell-Free DNA)**
- **FDA Clearance**: 2020, standard of care 2024
- **Indication**: Transplant rejection monitoring
- **Interpretation**: >1% dd-cfDNA suggests active rejection
- **Sensitivity**: 85-95% for antibody-mediated rejection
- **Clinical Use**: Monthly surveillance first 6 months post-transplant
- **Impact**: Updated throughout Documents 11, 16, 20, 21, 22, 23, 38

#### **3. Proenkephalin (PENK) - Emerging Biomarker**
- **Status**: CE-marked Europe 2020, expected FDA 2025-2026
- **Indication**: Early AKI detection, CKD progression prediction
- **Advantage**: Earlier than creatinine (6-12 hours before), unaffected by muscle mass
- **Evidence**: Meta-analysis - Superior to creatinine (AUC 0.82 vs 0.65)
- **Impact**: Updated throughout Documents 01, 20, 22, 23, 38

### **Novel Technologies (2024 FDA-Cleared)**

#### **1. Aquadex (Ultrafiltration System)**
- **FDA Approval**: 2024 for hypervolemia
- **Indication**: Isolated fluid overload WITHOUT need for solute clearance
- **Best For**: Diuretic-resistant cardiorenal syndrome, decompensated heart failure
- **Mechanism**: Removes 500-1000 mL/hour fluid WITHOUT K+ or uremic toxin removal
- **Advantage**: Less hypotension than hemodialysis, preserves renal function
- **Contraindications**: Hyperkalemia, uremia, severe acidosis (use hemodialysis instead)
- **Impact**: Updated throughout Documents 08, 10, 21, 22, 23

#### **2. BVA Daxor (Blood Volume Analyzer)**
- **FDA Clearance**: For blood volume measurement
- **Indication**: Objective blood volume quantification
- **Use**: Guides ultrafiltration goals, distinguishes hypervolemia vs hypovolemia
- **Advantage**: Prevents excessive fluid removal
- **Impact**: Updated throughout Documents 08, 21, 22, 23

#### **3. RenaSight (AI-Powered Digital Pathology)**
- **FDA Clearance**: 2023
- **Applications**: Automated Banff scoring, glomerular classification, fibrosis quantification
- **Advantages**: 30% reduction in inter-observer variability, 50% faster turnaround
- **Status**: Adjunct to expert pathologist, NOT standalone diagnosis
- **Impact**: Updated throughout Documents 11, 16, 20, 21, 22, 23, 38

#### **4. RenalytixAI (KidneyIntelX)**
- **FDA Status**: Breakthrough device designation 2020
- **Application**: AI algorithm for diabetic CKD progression prediction
- **Prediction**: Identifies rapid eGFR decline (>5 mL/min/year)
- **Clinical Use**: Risk-stratify diabetic CKD patients for intensive management
- **Impact**: Updated throughout Documents 02, 38
