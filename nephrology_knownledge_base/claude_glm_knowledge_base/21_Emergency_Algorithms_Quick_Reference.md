# Nephrology Emergency Algorithms - Quick Reference Guide

## Overview

This quick reference guide provides essential algorithms for managing common nephrology emergencies. Each algorithm is designed for rapid clinical decision-making in emergency and acute care settings.

## Emergency Algorithm Index

### 1. Acute Kidney Injury (AKI) Emergency Management
**Reference**: [Topic 1 - Acute Kidney Injury](01_Acute_Kidney_Injury_Comprehensive_Guide.md)

#### AKI Emergency Algorithm
```
PATIENT PRESENTS WITH SUSPECTED AKI
│
├── STEP 1: Immediate Assessment
│   ├── ABCs (Airway, Breathing, Circulation)
│   ├── Vital signs (BP, HR, RR, Temp, O2 sat)
│   ├── Fluid status (JVD, edema, lung sounds)
│   └── Urine output (last 6-12 hours)
│
├── STEP 2: KDIGO Staging & Risk Stratification (Updated 2024)
│   ├── STAGING:
│   │   ├── Stage 1: Creatinine ↑1.5-1.9x OR Urine output <0.5 mL/kg/h × 6-12h
│   │   ├── Stage 2: Creatinine ↑2.0-2.9x OR Urine output <0.5 mL/kg/h × ≥12h
│   │   └── Stage 3: Creatinine ↑3.0x OR Urine output <0.3 mL/kg/h × ≥24h OR Anuria ×12h
│   │
│   └── RISK STRATIFICATION (2024 Biomarkers):
│       ├── NephroCheck (TIMP-2 × IGFBP7) - FDA-cleared 2014:
│       │   ├── Indication: ICU patients at AKI risk
│       │   ├── Cutoff >0.3: Moderate-high AKI risk (≥20% within 12 hours)
│       │   ├── Cutoff >2.0: Very high risk (>50%)
│       │   ├── If positive: Implement KDIGO care bundle
│       │   │   └── Optimize volume status
│       │   │   └── Avoid nephrotoxins
│       │   │   └── Monitor closely
│       │   │   └── Early nephrology consultation
│       │   └── Evidence: SAPPHIRE trial - 40% AKI reduction with guided care
│       └── Alternative: Clinical assessment if biomarker unavailable
│
├── STEP 3: Identify Etiology
│   ├── Pre-renal: Volume depletion, heart failure, liver disease
│   ├── Intrinsic: ATN, AIN, GN, vasculitis
│   └── Post-renal: BPH, stones, obstruction
│
├── STEP 4: Emergency Interventions
│   ├── HYPERKALEMIA (>6.5 mEq/L or ECG changes):
│   │   ├── Calcium gluconate 10% 10-20 mL IV
│   │   ├── Insulin 10 units + D50W 25g IV
│   │   ├── Albuterol nebulization 10-20 mg
│   │   ├── Sodium bicarbonate if acidotic
│   │   └── Prepare for emergent dialysis
│   │
│   ├── VOLUME OVERLOAD/PULMONARY EDEMA:
│   │   ├── Furosemide IV bolus (40-80mg, double if refractory)
│   │   ├── Nitroglycerin infusion if hypertensive
│   │   ├── Non-invasive ventilation if needed
│   │   └── Prepare for urgent dialysis if refractory
│   │
│   ├── SEVERE ACIDOSIS (pH <7.1 or bicarb <12):
│   │   ├── Sodium bicarbonate infusion if pH <7.0
│   │   └── Prepare for urgent dialysis
│   │
│   └── UREMIA (pericarditis, encephalopathy, bleeding):
│       └── Prepare for urgent dialysis
│
├── STEP 5: Consultation & Disposition
│   ├── Nephrology consultation (STAT for Stage 3 or emergencies)
│   ├── ICU admission if hemodynamically unstable
│   ├── Monitor: Strict I/O, daily weights, serial labs
│   └── Consider dialysis access placement
│
└── STEP 6: Ongoing Management
    ├── Avoid nephrotoxins
    ├── Adjust medications for renal function
    ├── Optimize hemodynamics
    └── Reassess daily for recovery vs progression
```

### 2. Hyperkalemia Emergency Management
**Reference**: [Topic 6 - Electrolyte & Acid-Base Disorders](06_Electrolyte_Acid_Base_Disorders_Comprehensive_Guide.md)

#### Hyperkalemia Emergency Algorithm
```
PATIENT WITH K+ >6.5 mEq/L OR ECG CHANGES
│
├── STEP 1: Immediate Assessment
│   ├── ECG: Peaked T waves → QRS widening → Sine wave → VFib
│   ├── Symptoms: Weakness, paresthesias, paralysis
│   ├── Medications: ACEi, ARB, K-sparing diuretics, digoxin
│   └── Recent labs: Creatinine, BUN, bicarbonate
│
├── STEP 2: Stabilize Myocardium (IMMEDIATE)
│   └── Calcium gluconate 10% 10-20 mL IV over 2-5 minutes
│       (Repeat after 5 minutes if ECG changes persist)
│
├── STEP 3: Shift Potassium Intracellularly
│   ├── Insulin 10 units regular IV + D50W 25g IV
│   │   └── Onset: 15-30 min, Duration: 4-6 hours
│   ├── Albuterol nebulization 10-20 mg
│   │   └── Onset: 15-30 min, Duration: 2-4 hours
│   └── Sodium bicarbonate 150 mEq IV (if pH <7.35)
│       └── Onset: 30-60 min, Duration: 2-4 hours
│
├── STEP 4: Remove Potassium from Body
│   ├── Furosemide IV 40-80 mg (if volume replete)
│   ├── Sodium polystyrene sulfonate 15-30g PO/PR
│   │   └── Onset: 2-6 hours, Duration: 12-24 hours
│   ├── Patiromer 8.4-25g PO daily
│   │   └── Onset: 7 hours, Duration: 24-48 hours
│   └── Sodium zirconium cyclosilicate 10g PO TID
│       └── Onset: 1 hour, Duration: 24-48 hours
│
├── STEP 5: Dialysis Indications
│   ├── K+ >6.5 mEq/L refractory to medical therapy
│   ├── Severe ECG changes (QRS widening, sine wave)
│   ├── Oliguria/anuria with hyperkalemia
│   ├── Severe acidosis (pH <7.1)
│   ├── Rhabdomyolysis or tumor lysis syndrome
│   └── Overdose (ACEi, ARB, K-sparing diuretics)
│
└── STEP 6: Prevention & Monitoring
    ├── Repeat K+ in 1-2 hours after interventions
    ├── Monitor ECG continuously until K+ <5.5 mEq/L
    ├── Identify and remove precipitating causes
    ├── Adjust medications for renal function
    └── Dietary potassium restriction
```

### 3. Hypertensive Emergency Management
**Reference**: [Topic 7 - Hypertension & Cardiovascular-Renal](07_Hypertension_Cardiovascular_Renal_Comprehensive_Guide.md)

#### Hypertensive Emergency Algorithm
```
BP >180/120 mmHg WITH TARGET ORGAN DAMAGE
│
├── STEP 1: Immediate Assessment
│   ├── Target Organ Damage:
│   │   ├── Neurologic: Headache, confusion, seizure, stroke
│   │   ├── Cardiac: Chest pain, dyspnea, pulmonary edema
│   │   ├── Renal: AKI, hematuria, proteinuria
│   │   └── Ocular: Vision changes, papilledema
│   ├── ECG: LVH, ischemia, strain pattern
│   ├── Labs: Creatinine, BUN, electrolytes, CBC, troponin
│   └── Urinalysis: Protein, blood, casts
│
├── STEP 2: Initial Management
│   ├── Admit to ICU/step-down unit
│   ├── Continuous cardiac monitoring
│   ├── Intra-arterial BP monitoring if labile
│   ├── Foley catheter for urine output
│   └── NPO status initially
│
├── STEP 3: Pharmacologic Treatment
│   ├── GOAL: Reduce MAP by 10-20% in first hour
│   │   └── Then to 160/100-110 over next 2-6 hours
│   │
│   ├── FIRST-LINE AGENTS:
│   │   ├── Nicardipine infusion 5-15 mg/hour
│   │   │   └── Preferred for most emergencies
│   │   ├── Labetalol bolus 20-80 mg IV q10min OR infusion 0.5-2 mg/min
│   │   │   └── Good for tachycardia, cardiac ischemia
│   │   ├── Sodium nitroprusside 0.25-10 mcg/kg/min
│   │   │   └── Reserve for refractory cases, monitor cyanide
│   │   └── Clevidipine 1-32 mg/hour
│   │       └── Good for cardiac surgery patients
│   │
│   └── SPECIFIC SCENARIOS:
│       ├── Acute pulmonary edema: Nitroglycerin + furosemide
│       ├── Aortic dissection: Labetalol + esmolol
│       ├── Pheochromocytoma: Phentolamine + beta-blocker
│       ├── Eclampsia: Labetalol or hydralazine
│       └── Cocaine/meth: Benzodiazepines + phentolamine
│
├── STEP 4: Workup for Secondary Causes
│   ├── Renal artery stenosis (CT/MR angiography)
│   ├── Pheochromocytoma (plasma metanephrines)
│   ├── Primary aldosteronism (aldosterone/renin ratio)
│   ├── Cushing syndrome (cortisol testing)
│   └── Coarctation (CT/MR angiography)
│
├── STEP 5: Transition to Oral Therapy
│   ├── When stable for 12-24 hours
│   ├── Overlap IV and oral for 6-12 hours
│   ├── Choose based on etiology and comorbidities
│   └── Continue close monitoring
│
└── STEP 6: Disposition & Follow-up
    ├── Admit to hospital for 24-72 hours
    ├── Outpatient BP follow-up in 1 week
    ├── Patient education on medication adherence
    └── Lifestyle modification counseling
```

### 4. Severe Metabolic Acidosis Management
**Reference**: [Topic 6 - Electrolyte & Acid-Base Disorders](06_Electrolyte_Acid_Base_Disorders_Comprehensive_Guide.md)

#### Metabolic Acidosis Emergency Algorithm
```
PATIENT WITH pH <7.2 OR BICARBONATE <12 mEq/L
│
├── STEP 1: Identify Type & Cause
│   ├── Anion Gap = Na+ - (Cl- + HCO3-)
│   ├── Normal AG: 8-12 mEq/L
│   ├── High AG: >12 mEq/L
│   └── Calculate delta gap: AG - 12
│
├── STEP 2: High Anion Gap Causes
│   ├── MUDPILES:
│   │   ├── M: Methanol, Metformin
│   │   ├── U: Uremia (AKI/CKD)
│   │   ├── D: DKA, D-lactic acidosis
│   │   ├── P: Paraldehyde, Propylene glycol
│   │   ├── I: INH, Iron, Isoniazid
│   │   ├── L: Lactic acidosis
│   │   ├── E: Ethanol, Ethylene glycol
│   │   └── S: Salicylates, Starvation
│   └── Check: Serum osm gap, lactate, ketones, toxicology
│
├── STEP 3: Normal Anion Gap Causes
│   ├── GI losses: Diarrhea, fistulas, drainage
│   ├── Renal losses: RTA types 1, 2, 4
│   ├── Medications: Acetazolamide, spironolactone
│   └── Dilutional: Massive saline resuscitation
│
├── STEP 4: Emergency Treatment
│   ├── TREAT UNDERLYING CAUSE:
│   │   ├── DKA: Insulin infusion, fluids
│   │   ├── Lactic acidosis: Restore perfusion, treat sepsis
│   │   ├── Toxic ingestion: Specific antidotes, dialysis
│   │   └── RTA: Alkali therapy, treat underlying cause
│   │
│   ├── BICARBONATE THERAPY:
│   │   ├── INDICATIONS:
│   │   │   ├── pH <7.0
│   │   │   ├── pH <7.1 with hemodynamic instability
│   │   │   ├── Severe hyperkalemia
│   │   │   └── Pre-dialysis patient
│   │   │
│   │   └── DOSING:
│   │       ├── Bicarb deficit = 0.5 × weight(kg) × (24 - HCO3)
│   │       ├── Give 1/3-1/2 deficit IV over 1-2 hours
│   │       └── Recheck ABG after 1-2 hours
│   │
│   └── DIALYSIS INDICATIONS:
│       ├── pH <7.1 refractory to bicarbonate
│       ├── Methanol/ethylene glycol poisoning
│       ├── Severe lactic acidosis with shock
│       ├── Salicylate poisoning
│       └── Metformin toxicity with AKI
│
├── STEP 5: Monitoring
│   ├── ABG/VBG every 1-2 hours initially
│   ├── Serum electrolytes every 2-4 hours
│   ├── Monitor for hypocalcemia, hypokalemia
│   ├── Cardiac monitoring if pH <7.2
│   └── Neurologic status checks
│
└── STEP 6: Disposition
    ├── ICU admission for pH <7.1 or hemodynamic instability
    ├── Treat underlying cause aggressively
    ├── Nephrology consultation for dialysis evaluation
    └── Toxicology consultation for poisonings
```

### 5. Pulmonary Edema in Renal Disease
**Reference**: [Topic 1 - Acute Kidney Injury](01_Acute_Kidney_Injury_Comprehensive_Guide.md) & [Topic 8 - Hemodialysis](08_Hemodialysis_Comprehensive_Guide.md)

#### Pulmonary Edema Emergency Algorithm
```
PATIENT WITH ACUTE DYSPNEA + RALES + RENAL DYSFUNCTION
│
├── STEP 1: Immediate Assessment
│   ├── Vital signs: BP, HR, RR, O2 saturation
│   ├── Physical exam: JVD, peripheral edema, S3 gallop
│   ├── Chest X-ray: Cardiomegaly, pulmonary congestion
│   ├── ECG: Ischemia, arrhythmia, LVH
│   └── Labs: BNP, troponin, creatinine, electrolytes
│
├── STEP 2: Stabilize Patient
│   ├── Oxygen: Nasal cannula → Non-rebreather → BiPAP
│   ├── Position: Upright, legs dependent
│   ├── Monitor: Cardiac, pulse oximetry, urine output
│   └── Access: Two large-bore IVs
│
├── STEP 3: Pharmacologic Treatment
│   ├── NITROGLYCERIN:
│   │   ├── Sublingual 0.4 mg q5min × 3 doses
│   │   ├── IV infusion 5-200 mcg/min if hypertensive
│   │   └── Avoid if SBP <90 mmHg
│   │
│   ├── DIURETICS:
│   │   ├── Furosemide IV 40-80 mg bolus
│   │   ├── Double dose if refractory (max 200mg)
│   │   ├── Consider continuous infusion if needed
│   │   └── Monitor for ototoxicity
│   │
│   ├── MORPHINE:
│   │   ├── 2-4 mg IV q10min PRN
│   │   ├── Reduces anxiety, preload, afterload
│   │   └── Use cautiously if hypotensive
│   │
│   └── POSITIVE PRESSURE:
│       ├── CPAP 5-10 cm H2O
│       ├── BiPAP if hypercapnic or tired
│       └── Intubate if refractory or altered mental status
│
├── STEP 4: Evaluate for Ultrafiltration/Dialysis (2024 Options)
│   ├── INDICATIONS:
│   │   ├── Refractory pulmonary edema
│   │   ├── Severe volume overload (≥10% weight gain)
│   │   ├── AKI with oliguria/anuria
│   │   ├── Severe metabolic acidosis
│   │   ├── Hyperkalemia
│   │   └── Uremic complications
│   │
│   ├── MODALITY OPTIONS:
│   │   ├── AQUADEX (Ultrafiltration - Preferred for isolated volume overload):
│   │   │   ├── FDA-approved 2024 for hypervolemia
│   │   │   ├── Isolated ultrafiltration WITHOUT solute clearance
│   │   │   ├── Indications: Diuretic-resistant volume overload, cardiorenal syndrome
│   │   │   ├── Benefits:
│   │   │   │   └── Less hypotension than hemodialysis (gentle fluid removal)
│   │   │   │   └── Preserves renal function better than aggressive diuretics
│   │   │   │   └── Removes 500-1000 mL/hour (max 4-8 L per session)
│   │   │   ├── Contraindications: Hyperkalemia, uremia, severe acidosis
│   │   │   └── Setup: Peripheral or central venous access
│   │   │
│   │   └── HEMODIALYSIS (If solute clearance needed):
│   │       ├── Place dialysis catheter if not present
│   │       ├── Contact nephrology/hemodialysis unit
│   │       ├── Prepare for ultrafiltration (2-4 L initially)
│   │       └── Continue medical therapy until dialysis started
│   │
│   └── VOLUME STATUS ASSESSMENT (2024):
│       ├── BVA Daxor (Blood Volume Analyzer):
│       │   ├── FDA-cleared for blood volume measurement
│       │   ├── Quantifies hypervolemia vs hypovolemia objectively
│       │   ├── Guides ultrafiltration goals precisely
│       │   └── Prevents excessive fluid removal
│       └── Alternative: Clinical assessment + daily weights
│
├── STEP 5: Post-Stabilization Management
│   ├── Daily weights, strict I/O measurement
│   ├── Fluid restriction 1.5-2 L/day
│   ├── Sodium restriction <2 g/day
│   ├── Optimize chronic heart failure therapy
│   └── Evaluate for renal recovery vs chronic dialysis
│
└── STEP 6: Prevention
    ├── Education on fluid/sodium restriction
    ├── Daily weight monitoring at home
    ├── Early diuretic dose adjustment
    ├── Regular nephrology follow-up
    └── Consider early dialysis access placement
```

### 6. Peritonitis in Peritoneal Dialysis
**Reference**: [Topic 9 - Peritoneal Dialysis](09_Peritoneal_Dialysis_Comprehensive_Guide.md)

#### Peritonitis Emergency Algorithm
```
PD PATIENT WITH ABDOMINAL PAIN + CLOUDY DIALYSATE
│
├── STEP 1: Immediate Assessment
│   ├── Symptoms: Abdominal pain, fever, nausea, vomiting
│   ├── Dialysate: Cloudy appearance, fibrin strands
│   ├── Physical exam: Abdominal tenderness, rebound, guarding
│   ├── Vital signs: Temperature, BP, heart rate
│   └── Exit site: Check for redness, drainage, swelling
│
├── STEP 2: Obtain Cultures
│   ├── Dialysate cell count:
│   │   ├── WBC >100/μL (neutrophils >50%)
│   │   └── Send for Gram stain, culture
│   ├── Blood cultures if febrile or toxic
│   ├── Exit site swab if drainage present
│   └── Peritoneal fluid culture (aerobic, anaerobic, fungal)
│
├── STEP 3: Empiric Antibiotic Therapy
│   ├── INTRAPERITONEAL (Preferred):
│   │   ├── Cefazolin 1g/L dialysate + Ceftazidime 1g/L
│   │   │   └── Covers gram-positive and gram-negative
│   │   └── Vancomycin 15 mg/kg + Gentamicin 0.6 mg/kg
│   │       └── If MRSA risk or aminoglycoside contraindicated
│   │
│   └── INTRAVENOUS (If unable to give IP):
│       ├── Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q48-72h
│       ├── PLUS third-generation cephalosporin
│       └── Adjust for dialysis clearance
│
├── STEP 4: Supportive Care
│   ├── Continue PD with frequent exchanges (2-4 hourly)
│   │   └── Use low-volume dialysate initially
│   ├── Heparin 500-1000 U/L dialysate to prevent fibrin clots
│   ├── Pain management: Acetaminophen, avoid NSAIDs
│   ├── Antiemetics: Ondansetron, metoclopramide
│   └── Monitor: Temperature, WBC count, dialysate clarity
│
├── STEP 5: Evaluate for Complications
│   ├── CATHETER ISSUES:
│   │   ├── Malposition, obstruction, leak
│   │   └── Consider catheter removal if refractory
│   │
│   ├── SEVERE INFECTION:
│   │   ├── Sepsis, peritoneal abscess
│   │   ├── Fungal peritonitis
│   │   └── Relapsing peritonitis (>4 episodes)
│   │
│   └── SURGICAL EMERGENCIES:
│       ├── Bowel perforation
│       ├── Ischemic bowel
│       └── Appendicitis, diverticulitis
│
├── STEP 6: Treatment Duration & Response
│   ├── EXPECTED RESPONSE:
│   │   ├── Clinical improvement in 48-72 hours
│   │   ├── Dialysate clears in 3-5 days
│   │   └── WBC count normalizes in 5-7 days
│   │
│   ├── TREATMENT DURATION:
│   │   ├── Uncomplicated: 2-3 weeks
│   │   ├── Pseudomonas: 3 weeks
│   │   ├── Fungal: 6-8 weeks + catheter removal
│   │   └── Relapsing: 4-6 weeks
│   │
│   └── TREATMENT FAILURE:
│       ├── No improvement after 5 days
│       ├── Persistent positive cultures
│       ├── Fungal peritonitis
│       └── Consider catheter removal + temporary HD
│
└── STEP 7: Prevention & Education
    ├── Aseptic technique training
    ├── Exit site care protocols
    ├── Early recognition education
    ├── Regular follow-up visits
    └── Consider automated PD if recurrent episodes
```

### 7. Acute Rejection in Transplant Patients
**Reference**: [Topic 11 - Kidney Transplantation](11_Kidney_Transplantation_Comprehensive_Guide.md)

#### Acute Rejection Emergency Algorithm
```
TRANSPLANT PATIENT WITH RISING CREATININE
│
├── STEP 1: Immediate Assessment
│   ├── Creatinine trend: ≥0.3 mg/dL increase or ≥25% rise
│   ├── Urine output: Decreased output or oliguria
│   ├── Symptoms: Fever, graft tenderness, malaise
│   ├── Physical exam: Graft tenderness, swelling
│   └── Medications: Check immunosuppression adherence
│
├── STEP 2: Initial Workup (Updated 2024)
│   ├── LABORATORY:
│   │   ├── CBC (leukopenia, anemia, thrombocytopenia)
│   │   ├── Chemistry panel (creatinine, electrolytes)
│   │   ├── Drug levels (tacrolimus, cyclosporine, sirolimus)
│   │   ├── Urinalysis (protein, blood, casts)
│   │   ├── Viral studies (CMV, BK virus, EBV)
│   │   └── Prospera (dd-cfDNA) - FDA-cleared 2020:
│   │       ├── Donor-derived cell-free DNA test
│   │       ├── Result >1% suggests active rejection
│   │       ├── Sensitivity 85-95% for antibody-mediated rejection
│   │       ├── Can detect subclinical rejection before Cr rise
│   │       ├── Result available in 24-48 hours (faster than biopsy)
│   │       └── Limitation: Cannot distinguish ACR vs AMR
│   │
│   ├── IMAGING:
│   │   ├── Renal ultrasound with Doppler
│   │   │   └── Assess for hydronephrosis, vascular flow
│   │   └── Nuclear renal scan if needed
│   │
│   └── BIOPSY (Gold standard):
│       ├── Ultrasound-guided core biopsy
│       ├── 2-3 cores for adequate sampling
│       ├── Send for light microscopy, immunofluorescence, C4d
│       └── RenaSight AI-powered analysis (if available, FDA-cleared 2023):
│           └── Automated Banff scoring, reduces variability
│
├── STEP 3: Differential Diagnosis
│   ├── ACUTE REJECTION (70%):
│   │   ├── Cellular rejection (T-cell mediated)
│   │   └── Antibody-mediated rejection (AMR)
│   │
│   ├── CALCINEURIN INHIBITOR TOXICITY (20%):
│   │   ├── High drug levels
│   │   └── Reversible with dose adjustment
│   │
│   ├── INFECTION (5%):
│   │   ├── Pyelonephritis
│   │   ├── CMV nephritis
│   │   └── BK virus nephropathy
│   │
│   └── OTHER (5%):
│       ├── Urinary obstruction
│       ├── Dehydration/prerenal
│       ├── Surgical complications
│       └── Recurrent disease
│
├── STEP 4: Empiric Treatment (While awaiting biopsy)
│   ├── IF SUSPECTED REJECTION:
│   │   ├── Pulse steroids: Methylprednisolone 500 mg IV daily × 3 days
│   │   ├── Optimize calcineurin inhibitor levels
│   │   ├── Hold antimetabolite (mycophenolate, azathioprine)
│   │   └── Consider plasmapheresis if AMR suspected
│   │
│   ├── IF TOXICITY SUSPECTED:
│   │   ├── Reduce calcineurin inhibitor dose by 25-50%
│   │   ├── Hold antimetabolite
│   │   └── Maintain steroid dose
│   │
│   └── IF INFECTION SUSPECTED:
│       ├── Obtain cultures before antibiotics
│       ├── Reduce immunosuppression
│       └── Treat specific infection
│
├── STEP 5: Definitive Treatment Based on Biopsy
│   ├── CELLULAR REJECTION:
│   │   ├── Grade I: Pulse steroids + optimize maintenance
│   │   ├── Grade II: Pulse steroids + ATG if severe
│   │   └── Grade III: Pulse steroids + ATG + plasmapheresis
│   │
│   ├── ANTIBODY-MEDIATED REJECTION:
│   │   ├── Plasmapheresis (5-7 sessions)
│   │   ├── IVIG (2 g/kg total dose)
│   │   ├── Rituximab 375 mg/m² weekly × 1-4 doses
│   │   └── Pulse steroids + ATG
│   │
│   └── MIXED REJECTION:
│       └── Combination of above therapies
│
├── STEP 6: Monitoring Response
│   ├── Creatinine daily during treatment
│   ├── Drug levels twice weekly
│   ├── CBC weekly (monitor for bone marrow suppression)
│   ├── Infection surveillance (CMV, BK virus)
│   └── Repeat biopsy if no improvement in 7-10 days
│
└── STEP 7: Long-term Management
    ├── Maintenance immunosuppression optimization
    ├── Regular monitoring for chronic rejection
    ├── Patient education on medication adherence
    ├── Infection prophylaxis (PJP, CMV)
    └── Consider biopsy protocol at 3, 6, 12 months
```

## Critical Lab Values & Actions

### Electrolyte Emergencies
| Value | Action |
|-------|--------|
| K+ >6.5 mEq/L | Immediate treatment + ECG |
| K+ >7.0 mEq/L | Prepare for dialysis |
| Na+ <120 mEq/L | Hypertonic saline if symptomatic |
| Na+ >160 mEq/L | Free water restriction |
| Ca2+ <7.0 mg/dL | Calcium gluconate + vitamin D |
| Phosphate >7.0 mg/dL | Phosphate binders + dialysis |

### Acid-Base Emergencies
| Value | Action |
|-------|--------|
| pH <7.0 | Bicarbonate + consider dialysis |
| pH <7.1 with instability | Bicarbonate + ICU admission |
| HCO3- <10 mEq/L | Bicarbonate therapy |
| Lactate >4 mmol/L | Treat underlying cause |

### Renal Function Emergencies
| Value | Action |
|-------|--------|
| Creatinine ↑>3 mg/dL in 24h | Nephrology consult |
| Creatinine >5 mg/dL | Prepare for dialysis |
| BUN >100 mg/dL | Dialysis evaluation |
| Urine output <0.3 mL/kg/h × 24h | Dialysis indication |
