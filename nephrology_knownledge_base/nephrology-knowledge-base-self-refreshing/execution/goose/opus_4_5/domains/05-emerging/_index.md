# Emerging Topics

This domain covers new developments, technological advancements, and future directions in nephrology. It focuses on topics expected to impact clinical practice within the next 3-5 years, including artificial intelligence, precision medicine, novel therapeutics, and digital health innovations.

## Topics

- **[[AI and Machine Learning in Nephrology]]**: Applications of artificial intelligence and machine learning algorithms for prediction, diagnosis, and management of kidney diseases.
  - Subtopics: Predictive models for AKI and CKD progression, AI-assisted interpretation of kidney pathology and imaging, Personalized treatment algorithms, Operational applications for dialysis unit management
  - Priority: high
  - Sources: Nature Reviews Nephrology, Journal of the American Society of Nephrology (JASN), Kidney International

- **[[Precision Nephrology and Genomics]]**: The use of genetic and genomic information to tailor diagnosis, risk stratification, and treatment for individual patients with kidney disease.
  - Subtopics: Clinical application of APOL1 genetic testing, Pharmacogenomics for immunosuppressants and other drugs, Biomarker discovery for early disease detection, Polygenic risk scores for CKD
  - Priority: medium
  - Sources: Clinical Journal of the American Society of Nephrology (CJASN), National Institute of Diabetes and Digestive and Kidney Diseases (NIDDK) research summaries, Genetics in Medicine

- **[[Digital Health and Remote Patient Monitoring]]**: The integration of digital tools, telehealth platforms, and mobile applications to enhance patient care and monitoring outside of traditional clinical settings.
  - Subtopics: Telehealth models for CKD and dialysis care, Remote monitoring of blood pressure, weight, and glucose, Patient engagement and education apps, EHR integration of patient-generated health data
  - Priority: high
  - Sources: American Telemedicine Association Guidelines, KDIGO Controversies Conference Reports, npj Digital Medicine

- **[[Novel Therapeutics for Kidney Disease]]**: Emerging drug classes and therapeutic agents that are changing the standard of care for CKD, glomerular diseases, and their complications.
  - Subtopics: Expanded indications for SGLT2 inhibitors, Non-steroidal Mineralocorticoid Receptor Antagonists (nsMRAs), HIF-PH inhibitors for anemia of CKD, Complement pathway inhibitors for glomerular diseases
  - Priority: high
  - Sources: FDA New Drug Approvals, New England Journal of Medicine (NEJM), The Lancet

- **[[Clinical Trials and Pipeline Drugs]]**: An overview of ongoing late-stage clinical trials and promising drugs in the development pipeline for various kidney diseases.
  - Subtopics: Review of key Phase III trial results, Promising mechanisms of action in Phase II, Anti-fibrotic agents in development, Novel therapies for polycystic kidney disease
  - Priority: medium
  - Sources: ClinicalTrials.gov, ASN Kidney Week Abstracts, Industry-sponsored trial publications

- **[[Wearable and Implantable Kidney Devices]]**: The development of next-generation renal replacement therapies, including wearable artificial kidneys and implantable bioartificial organs.
  - Subtopics: The Kidney Project: Implantable Bioartificial Kidney, Wearable Artificial Kidney (WAK) progress, Advances in sorbent and filtration technology, Future patient selection and implementation challenges
  - Priority: low
  - Sources: The Kidney Project (UCSF/Vanderbilt), ASN Kidney News, ASAIO Journal

- **[[Health Equity in Nephrology]]**: Initiatives and research focused on understanding and eliminating disparities in kidney disease prevalence, care, and outcomes among different populations.
  - Subtopics: Impact of social determinants of health on CKD, Addressing racial and ethnic disparities in ESKD, Strategies for culturally competent care, Improving diversity in nephrology clinical trials
  - Priority: high
  - Sources: ASN Health Equity and Justice Committee resources, National Kidney Foundation (NKF) position statements, JAMA Network journals
