# Care Delivery

This domain outlines the models, processes, and strategies for delivering high-quality, efficient, and patient-centered nephrology care. It covers value-based initiatives, multidisciplinary team structures, care coordination across different settings, and the integration of technology like telehealth.

## Topics

- **[[Value-Based Care Models in Nephrology]]**: An overview of value-based care models relevant to nephrology, focusing on payment structures, quality metrics, and practice implementation.
  - Subtopics: Kidney Care Choices (KCC) Model Implementation, Accountable Care Organizations (ACOs) for Nephrology, Quality Payment Program (QPP) and MIPS Reporting, Measuring and Reporting Quality Metrics, Contracting with Payers for Value-Based Arrangements
  - Priority: high
  - Sources: CMS Kidney Care Choices (KCC) Model Website, Renal Physicians Association (RPA) VBC Resources

- **[[Home Dialysis Program Management]]**: A practical guide to establishing, managing, and growing a successful home dialysis program, including both peritoneal dialysis (PD) and home hemodialysis (HHD).
  - Subtopics: Patient Selection and Training for Home Therapies, PD Program Logistics and Staffing Models, Home Hemodialysis (HHD) Program Implementation, Monitoring, Troubleshooting, and Urgent Support for Home Patients, Billing and Coding for Home Dialysis Services
  - Priority: high
  - Sources: ISPD Guidelines for Peritoneal Dialysis, NKF KDOQI Guidelines for Home Dialysis

- **[[Multidisciplinary Care Team Model]]**: The structure and function of a multidisciplinary team (MDT) in nephrology care, including roles, responsibilities, and communication workflows.
  - Subtopics: Defining Team Roles (Nephrologist, NP/PA, RN, Dietitian, Social Worker), Integrated Care Workflows and Handoffs, Conducting Effective Patient Care Conferences, Measuring MDT Effectiveness and Patient Outcomes, Documentation Standards for Team-Based Care
  - Priority: high
  - Sources: ASN Kidney News articles on team-based care, Renal Physicians Association (RPA) Practice Management Resources

- **[[Care Coordination and Transitions of Care]]**: Strategies for managing patient transitions between care settings to reduce readmissions and improve outcomes.
  - Subtopics: Hospital Discharge Planning for CKD/ESRD Patients, Managing the Transition from Advanced CKD to Dialysis, Post-Transplant Care Coordination with Transplant Centers, Warm Handoffs to Primary Care and Other Specialists, Medication Reconciliation During Transitions
  - Priority: high
  - Sources: Agency for Healthcare Research and Quality (AHRQ) - Care Transitions, ESRD Network Program Resources

- **[[Patient Education and Engagement]]**: Methods and materials for educating patients and their families about kidney disease, treatment options, and self-management.
  - Subtopics: Comprehensive Kidney Disease Education (CKD Options Talks), Shared Decision-Making for Modality Choice, Health Literacy and Culturally Competent Education Materials, Utilizing Patient Portals and Digital Engagement Tools, Peer Mentoring Programs
  - Priority: medium
  - Sources: National Kidney Disease Education Program (NKDEP), American Association of Kidney Patients (AAKP) Educational Resources

- **[[Telehealth and Remote Patient Monitoring]]**: Implementation and best practices for using telehealth and remote monitoring technologies to deliver nephrology care.
  - Subtopics: Telehealth Visit Protocols and Clinical Workflows, Billing and Coding for Telehealth Services, Remote Patient Monitoring for Home Dialysis and CKD Management, Technology Platform Selection and HIPAA Compliance, Virtual Acute Care and Hospital-at-Home Models
  - Priority: medium
  - Sources: CMS Telehealth Policies, American Telemedicine Association (ATA) Guidelines

- **[[Quality Improvement Initiatives]]**: Frameworks and practical approaches for implementing quality improvement (QI) projects within a nephrology practice.
  - Subtopics: Plan-Do-Study-Act (PDSA) Cycles in Nephrology Settings, Root Cause Analysis for Adverse Events (e.g., infections, hospitalizations), Developing and Tracking Key Performance Indicators (KPIs), ESRD Quality Incentive Program (QIP) Compliance, Patient Safety and High-Reliability Organizing Principles
  - Priority: high
  - Sources: Institute for Healthcare Improvement (IHI), CMS ESRD Quality Incentive Program (QIP) Resources
