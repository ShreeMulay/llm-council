# Regulatory Compliance

This domain outlines the federal and state regulatory requirements governing nephrology practices and End-Stage Renal Disease (ESRD) facilities. It focuses on the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) regulations, including Conditions for Coverage (CfC), quality reporting programs like QIP, and billing compliance, to ensure patient safety and operational integrity.

## Topics

- **[[Conditions for Coverage for ESRD Facilities]]**: Federal regulations from CMS that all dialysis facilities must meet to receive Medicare and Medicaid payment.
  - Subtopics: Governance and Administration (V-Tags 400s), Quality Assessment and Performance Improvement (QAPI) (V-Tags 500s), Patient Assessment and Plan of Care (V-Tags 600s), Water Treatment and Dialysate Quality (V-Tags 200s), Infection Control (V-Tags 100s)
  - Priority: high
  - Sources: 42 CFR Part 494, CMS State Operations Manual, Appendix H

- **[[ESRD Quality Incentive Program (QIP)]]**: The CMS value-based purchasing program that links a portion of a dialysis facility's payment to its performance on quality-of-care measures.
  - Subtopics: Annual QIP Clinical and Reporting Measures, Total Performance Score (TPS) Calculation, Payment Adjustments and Penalties, Data Submission via CROWNWeb and NHSN, Review and Correction Process
  - Priority: high
  - Sources: CMS ESRD QIP Official Website, Annual ESRD Prospective Payment System (PPS) Final Rule

- **[[Medicare and Medicaid Requirements for Nephrology Practices]]**: Rules and regulations governing billing, coding, and documentation for physician services in nephrology under Medicare and Medicaid.
  - Subtopics: Monthly Capitated Payment (MCP) for ESRD Services, Merit-based Incentive Payment System (MIPS) for Nephrology, Documentation for Dialysis Rounds and Office Visits, Stark Law and Anti-Kickback Statute Compliance, Advanced Practice Provider (APP) Billing Rules
  - Priority: high
  - Sources: CMS Medicare Claims Processing Manual, Chapter 8, CMS Quality Payment Program Website

- **[[Dialysis Facility Compare and Star Ratings]]**: The CMS system for publicly reporting dialysis facility quality data and assigning a summary star rating to help patients choose a provider.
  - Subtopics: Star Rating Methodology and Domains, Quality Measures Included in Ratings, Public Reporting and Data Preview Periods, Impact on Patient Referrals and Reputation, Strategies for Improving Star Ratings
  - Priority: medium
  - Sources: CMS Dialysis Facility Compare Website, Dialysis Facility Compare Star Rating Technical Methodology Guide

- **[[HIPAA and Patient Privacy in Nephrology]]**: Compliance with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) regarding patient privacy and data security in a nephrology setting.
  - Subtopics: Management of Protected Health Information (PHI), Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) with Vendors, Patient Authorization and Release of Information, Security Risk Analysis Requirements, Breach Notification Rules and Procedures
  - Priority: high
  - Sources: HHS.gov HIPAA for Professionals, 45 CFR Parts 160, 162, and 164

- **[[Emergency Preparedness for Dialysis Facilities]]**: CMS requirements for developing and maintaining a comprehensive emergency preparedness program for dialysis facilities to ensure patient safety during disasters.
  - Subtopics: All-Hazards Risk Assessment and Planning, Emergency Communication Plan, Policies, Procedures, and Continuity of Operations, Annual Training and Testing (Drills and Exercises), Collaboration with Local Emergency Management
  - Priority: medium
  - Sources: CMS Emergency Preparedness Rule (42 CFR §494.62), Kidney Community Emergency Response (KCER) Program

- **[[State-Specific Regulations and Licensure]]**: Regulations, licensure requirements, and reporting mandates for dialysis facilities and medical practices as defined by state health departments and medical boards.
  - Subtopics: State Dialysis Facility Licensure and Surveys, Certificate of Need (CON) Laws for New Facilities, State-Mandated Adverse Event Reporting, Medical Practice Act and Scope of Practice for APPs, State-Specific Privacy and Data Security Laws
  - Priority: medium
  - Sources: State Department of Health Website, State Medical Board Regulations

- **[[Federal Register Monitoring and Rulemaking]]**: The process for monitoring, interpreting, and responding to proposed and final rules from CMS and other federal agencies that affect nephrology.
  - Subtopics: Tracking the Annual ESRD PPS and Physician Fee Schedule Rules, Analyzing Proposed Rule Impact on Operations, Public Comment Submission Process, Implementing Final Rule Changes, Subscribing to Relevant Agency Updates
  - Priority: low
  - Sources: FederalRegister.gov, CMS Newsroom and Fact Sheets
