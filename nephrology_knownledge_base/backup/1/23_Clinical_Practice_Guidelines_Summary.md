# Clinical Practice Guidelines Summary - Nephrology

## Overview

This comprehensive summary consolidates major clinical practice guidelines in nephrology, focusing on KDIGO, KDOQI, ISPD, and other society recommendations. It provides quick access to evidence-based recommendations for clinical practice.

## Section I: KDIGO Guidelines Summary

### 1. KDIGO Clinical Practice Guideline for Acute Kidney Injury (2021 Update)
**Reference**: [Topic 1 - Acute Kidney Injury](01_Acute_Kidney_Injury_Comprehensive_Guide.md)

#### Key Recommendations

**Definition & Staging:**
- **AKI Definition**: Any of the following within 48 hours:
  - ↑Serum creatinine by ≥0.3 mg/dL (≥26.5 μmol/L)
  - ↑Serum creatinine to ≥1.5 times baseline
  - Urine output <0.5 mL/kg/h for 6-12 hours

**Risk Assessment & Prevention:**
- **Identify at-risk patients**: Sepsis, shock, major surgery, contrast exposure
- **Prevention strategies**:
  - Optimize hemodynamics and volume status
  - Avoid nephrotoxic medications when possible
  - Consider alternative imaging to contrast
  - Use iso-osmolar contrast with minimal volume

**Management Recommendations:**
- **Volume management**: Individualized fluid therapy, avoid fluid overload
- **Medication adjustments**: Dose adjust for renal function, avoid nephrotoxins
- **Glycemic control**: Target 140-180 mg/dL in critically ill patients
- **Nutrition**: 20-30 kcal/kg/day, protein 0.8-1.0 g/kg/day in non-catabolic patients

**Dialysis Initiation:**
- **Consider emergent dialysis for**:
  - Refractory hyperkalemia (>6.5 mEq/L)
  - Refractory volume overload
  - Severe metabolic acidosis (pH <7.1)
  - Uremic complications (pericarditis, encephalopathy)

### 2. KDIGO Clinical Practice Guideline for Chronic Kidney Disease (2023 Update)
**Reference**: [Topic 2 - Chronic Kidney Disease](02_Chronic_Kidney_Disease_Comprehensive_Guide.md)

#### Key Recommendations

**Definition & Classification:**
- **CKD Definition**: Abnormalities of kidney structure or function present for >3 months
- **GFR Categories (G)**: G1 (≥90), G2 (60-89), G3a (45-59), G3b (30-44), G4 (15-29), G5 (<15)
- **Albuminuria Categories (A)**: A1 (<30), A2 (30-300), A3 (>300) mg/g

**Risk Factor Management (2024 UPDATE):**
- **Blood pressure targets**:
  - <140/90 mmHg for all CKD patients
  - <130/80 mmHg if albuminuria >30 mg/g
- **ACEi/ARB therapy**: Recommended for albuminuria >300 mg/g
- **SGLT2 inhibitors (2024 MAJOR UPDATE)**: Recommended for ALL CKD patients with eGFR ≥20 mL/min/1.73m²
  - NO LONGER limited to diabetic CKD only
  - Dapagliflozin (Farxiga) 10 mg daily OR Empagliflozin (Jardiance) 10 mg daily
  - Evidence: EMPA-KIDNEY, DAPA-CKD trials (40% reduction in kidney disease progression)
- **Finerenone (Kerendia) - NEW 2024**: Non-steroidal MRA for diabetic CKD
  - 10-20 mg daily for diabetic CKD patients
  - FIDELIO-DKD, FIGARO-DKD trials: Reduced CKD progression and CV events
  - Consider as part of 4-pillar therapy (ACEi/ARB + SGLT2i + Finerenone + statin)

**Complication Management:**
- **Anemia**: Evaluate when Hb <13 g/dL (men) or <12 g/dL (women)
- **CKD-MBD**: Start monitoring at eGFR <45 mL/min/1.73m²
- **Metabolic acidosis**: Treat if bicarbonate <22 mEq/L

**Referral Timing:**
- **Nephrology referral**: eGFR <30 mL/min/1.73m² or rapidly progressive
- **Dialysis access planning**: eGFR 20-25 mL/min/1.73m²

### 3. KDIGO Clinical Practice Guideline for Glomerular Diseases (2021)
**Reference**: [Topic 3 - Glomerular Diseases](03_Glomerular_Diseases_Comprehensive_Guide.md)

#### Key Recommendations

**Minimal Change Disease & FSGS:**
- **First-line therapy**: Oral prednisone 1 mg/kg/day (max 80 mg) for 4-16 weeks
- **Steroid-sparing agents**: Consider for steroid-dependent or frequently relapsing disease
- **Rituximab**: Alternative for steroid-dependent/relapsing MCD

**Membranous Nephropathy:**
- **Risk stratification**: Use anti-PLA2R antibody levels and proteinuria
- **Immunosuppression indicated for**:
  - Proteinuria >4 g/day persisting >6 months
  - Declining eGFR
  - Symptomatic nephrotic syndrome
- **First-line**: Alternating steroids + cyclophosphamide or rituximab

**IgA Nephropathy (KDIGO 2025 GUIDELINE):** ⭐ **MAJOR UPDATE**
- **Paradigm Shift**: Early immunologic intervention targeting pathophysiology (vs. supportive-care-only)
- **Foundational Therapy (ALL Patients)**:
  - ACEi/ARB at maximum tolerated dose (target BP <120/80 mmHg)
  - **SGLT2 inhibitors** (empagliflozin 10 mg or dapagliflozin 10 mg daily) - NEW STANDARD OF CARE
  - Reassess after 3-6 months before adding disease-modifying therapy
- **Risk Stratification**:
  - **Low risk** (proteinuria <0.5 g/day): Supportive care only
  - **Moderate risk** (proteinuria 0.5-1.0 g/day): Shared decision-making for disease-modifying therapy
  - **High risk** (proteinuria >1 g/day despite supportive care): Disease-modifying therapy recommended
- **Disease-Modifying Therapy Options (Moderate-High Risk)**:
  - **Option A: Iptacopan (Fabhalta®) 200 mg BID** - KDIGO 2025 PREFERRED
    - Factor B inhibitor (complement pathway)
    - 44% proteinuria reduction, slowed eGFR decline
    - **MANDATORY meningococcal vaccination** before starting
  - **Option B: Atrasentan (Vanrafia®) 0.75 mg daily** - FDA April 2025
    - Selective ET-A receptor antagonist
    - 36% proteinuria reduction, additive with SGLT2i
  - **Option C: Sparsentan (Filspari®) 200-400 mg daily**
    - Dual ERA/ARB, 50% proteinuria reduction
  - **Option D: Nefecon/Tarpeyo® 16 mg daily × 9 months**
    - Targeted-release budesonide, 31% reduction in eGFR decline
- **Systemic corticosteroids NOW SECOND-LINE** (use only if newer agents unavailable)

**Lupus Nephritis (KDIGO 2024 GUIDELINE):** ⭐ **MAJOR UPDATE**
- **Paradigm Shift**: Belimumab triple therapy NOW first-line standard of care for Class III/IV
- **First-Line Induction (Class III/IV) - TRIPLE THERAPY PREFERRED**:
  - **MMF** 2-3 g/day PO BID
  - **Prednisone** 0.5-0.75 mg/kg/day (lower initial dose vs. historical 1 mg/kg), taper to ≤5 mg/day by 6 months
  - **Belimumab (Benlysta®)** 10 mg/kg IV at weeks 0, 2, 4, then every 4 weeks
    - FDA approved December 2020 for active lupus nephritis
    - BLISS-LN Trial: 43% vs. 32% renal response, 51% reduction in renal-related events
    - **KDIGO 2024: Initiate at diagnosis** (don't wait for MMF failure)
- **Alternative Induction Options**:
  - Voclosporin (Lupkynis®) 23.7 mg BID + MMF + low-dose steroids (FDA 2021)
  - Low-dose IV cyclophosphamide (Euro-Lupus protocol)
- **Maintenance Therapy**:
  - MMF 1-2 g/day + prednisone ≤5 mg/day + **continue belimumab** (at least 24 months)
  - **Hydroxychloroquine** 200-400 mg/day in ALL patients (KDIGO 2024 strong recommendation)
- **Refractory Disease**: Rituximab 1 g × 2 doses (KDIGO 2024: recommended despite LUNAR trial negativity)
- **Duration**: Minimum 2-3 years maintenance after complete renal response

### 4. KDIGO Clinical Practice Guideline for Hypertension in CKD (2021)
**Reference**: [Topic 7 - Hypertension & Cardiovascular-Renal](07_Hypertension_Cardiovascular_Renal_Comprehensive_Guide.md)

#### Key Recommendations

**Blood Pressure Targets:**
- **General CKD population**: <140/90 mmHg
- **High-risk CKD** (albuminuria >30 mg/g): <130/80 mmHg
- **Dialysis patients**: <140/90 mmHg pre-dialysis, <130/80 mmHg home

**Medication Selection:**
- **First-line**: ACEi or ARB (especially with proteinuria)
- **Add-on therapy**:
  - Thiazide-like diuretic (eGFR >30)
  - Loop diuretic (eGFR <30)
  - Calcium channel blocker
  - Beta-blocker if cardiac disease

**Special Populations:**
- **Diabetic CKD**: ACEi/ARB + SGLT2 inhibitor
- **Transplant recipients**: ACEi/ARB if proteinuria present
- **Pregnancy**: Labetalol, nifedipine, methyldopa

## Section II: KDOQI Guidelines Summary

### 5. KDOQI Clinical Practice Guideline for Hemodialysis Adequacy (2023 Update)
**Reference**: [Topic 8 - Hemodialysis](08_Hemodialysis_Comprehensive_Guide.md)

#### Key Recommendations

**Dosing Targets:**
- **Standard Kt/V**: ≥1.2 per session (3x/week schedule)
- **URR (Urea Reduction Ratio)**: ≥65%
- **Extended hours**: Consider Kt/V ≥1.4 for high-risk patients

**Frequency Considerations:**
- **Standard**: 3 times weekly, 3-4 hours per session
- **More frequent**: Consider for residual renal function <2 mL/min/1.73m²
- **Nocturnal/home**: Consider for better volume control and quality of life

**Monitoring Requirements:**
- **Monthly**: Kt/V, URR, albumin, phosphorus
- **Quarterly**: PTH, calcium, hemoglobin
- **Annually**: Ferritin, transferrin saturation

### 6. KDOQI Clinical Practice Guideline for Peritoneal Dialysis Adequacy (2023 Update)
**Reference**: [Topic 9 - Peritoneal Dialysis](09_Peritoneal_Dialysis_Comprehensive_Guide.md)

#### Key Recommendations

**Adequacy Targets:**
- **Weekly Kt/V**: ≥1.7 (combined renal + peritoneal)
- **Creatinine clearance**: ≥45 L/week/1.73m²
- **Residual renal function**: Preserve when possible

**Prescription Optimization:**
- **CAPD**: 4 exchanges daily, 2-2.5 L per exchange
- **APD**: Individualized based on peritoneal transport
- **Icodextrin**: Consider for high transporters or fluid overload

**Monitoring Schedule:**
- **Monthly**: Kt/V, creatinine clearance, albumin
- **Quarterly**: Phosphorus, calcium, PTH
- **Peritoneal equilibration test**: Annually or if clinical change

### 7. KDOQI Clinical Practice Guideline for Nutrition in CKD (2020 Update)
**Reference**: [Topic 19 - Nutrition in Kidney Disease](19_Nutrition_Kidney_Disease_Comprehensive_Guide.md)

#### Key Recommendations

**Protein Requirements:**
- **CKD stages 3-5 (non-dialysis)**: 0.6-0.8 g/kg/day
- **Hemodialysis**: 1.0-1.2 g/kg/day
- **Peritoneal dialysis**: 1.2-1.3 g/kg/day
- **Consider ketoanalogue supplementation** if protein <0.6 g/kg/day

**Energy Requirements:**
- **<60 years**: 35 kcal/kg/day
- **≥60 years**: 30-35 kcal/kg/day
- **Adjust for physical activity level**

**Micronutrient Management:**
- **Sodium**: <2 g/day for most patients
- **Potassium**: Individualize based on labs
- **Phosphorus**: 800-1000 mg/day
- **Calcium**: 1000-1200 mg/day total

## Section III: ISPD Guidelines Summary

### 8. ISPD Guidelines for Peritonitis Treatment (2022 Update)
**Reference**: [Topic 9 - Peritoneal Dialysis](09_Peritoneal_Dialysis_Comprehensive_Guide.md)

#### Key Recommendations

**Initial Treatment:**
- **Empiric intraperitoneal antibiotics**:
  - Gram-positive coverage: Cefazolin or vancomycin
  - Gram-negative coverage: Ceftazidime or aminoglycoside
- **Duration**: 2-3 weeks for uncomplicated infections

**Treatment Modifications:**
- **Culture-positive**: Tailor to organism and sensitivities
- **Methicillin-resistant organisms**: Vancomycin
- **Pseudomonas**: Two anti-pseudomonal agents
- **Fungal**: Remove catheter + antifungal therapy

**Catheter Removal Indications:**
- **Refractory infection**: No improvement after 5 days
- **Relapsing infection**: Same organism within 4 weeks
- **Fungal peritonitis**: Always remove catheter
- **Tunnel/exit site infection**: If associated with peritonitis

### 9. ISPD Guidelines for Encapsulating Peritoneal Sclerosis (2021)
**Reference**: [Topic 9 - Peritoneal Dialysis](09_Peritoneal_Dialysis_Comprehensive_Guide.md)

#### Key Recommendations

**Risk Factors:**
- **Long PD duration**: >8 years
- **High glucose exposure**: Especially icodextrin
- **Peritonitis history**: Multiple episodes
- **Genetic predisposition**: Consider family history

**Prevention Strategies:**
- **Regular monitoring**: CT scans for high-risk patients
- **Biocompatible solutions**: Consider neutral pH solutions
- **Early transplantation**: Consider before 8 years of PD

**Treatment Approaches:**
- **Early stage**: Stop PD, consider tamoxifen
- **Advanced stage**: Surgical intervention, nutritional support
- **Transplantation**: Consider after EPS resolution

## Section IV: Transplantation Guidelines

### 10. AST Guidelines for Kidney Transplant Recipients (2023 Update)
**Reference**: [Topic 11 - Kidney Transplantation](11_Kidney_Transplantation_Comprehensive_Guide.md)

#### Key Recommendations

**Immunosuppression Protocols:**
- **Induction therapy**:
  - IL-2 receptor antagonists for low-risk recipients
  - Lymphocyte-depleting agents for high-risk recipients
- **Maintenance therapy**:
  - Triple therapy: Calcineurin inhibitor + antimetabolite + steroids
  - Consider steroid minimization for low-risk patients

**Rejection Management:**
- **Acute cellular rejection**: Pulse steroids ± ATG
- **Antibody-mediated rejection**: Plasmapheresis + IVIG ± rituximab
- **Chronic rejection**: Optimize maintenance therapy, treat risk factors

**Infection Prophylaxis:**
- **PJP prophylaxis**: TMP-SMX for 6-12 months
- **CMV prophylaxis**: Based on donor/recipient serostatus
- **UTI prophylaxis**: Consider for recurrent infections

### 11. KDIGO Transplant Guidelines (2020 Update)
**Reference**: [Topic 11 - Kidney Transplantation](11_Kidney_Transplantation_Comprehensive_Guide.md)

#### Key Recommendations

**Donor Evaluation:**
- **Living donor evaluation**: Comprehensive medical, psychosocial, and genetic assessment
- **Deceased donor allocation**: Based on KDPI score and recipient factors
- **Extended criteria donors**: Consider for recipients >60 years or with limited life expectancy

**Recipient Management:**
- **Pre-transplant evaluation**: Cardiovascular assessment, infectious disease screening
- **Post-transplant monitoring**: Regular assessment of renal function, drug levels, complications
- **Long-term follow-up**: Annual comprehensive evaluation

## Section V: Special Population Guidelines

### 12. ACOG Guidelines for Pregnancy in CKD (2022 Update)
**Reference**: [Topic 13 - Pregnancy & Kidney Disease](13_Pregnancy_Kidney_Disease_Comprehensive_Guide.md)

#### Key Recommendations

**Preconception Counseling:**
- **Optimize BP control**: Target <130/80 mmHg before conception
- **Medication adjustments**: Switch from ACEi/ARB to safer alternatives
- **Kidney function**: eGFR >40 mL/min/1.73m² associated with better outcomes

**Pregnancy Management:**
- **BP targets**: <140/90 mmHg, <130/80 mmHg if proteinuria present
- **Proteinuria monitoring**: Every 4-6 weeks
- **Fetal growth**: Ultrasound every 4 weeks starting at 20 weeks

**Delivery Planning:**
- **Timing**: 37-39 weeks for most CKD patients
- **Earlier delivery**: Consider for worsening renal function or severe hypertension
- **Postpartum care**: Close monitoring for 6 weeks postpartum

### 13. ASPEN Guidelines for Nutrition in Dialysis (2021)
**Reference**: [Topic 19 - Nutrition in Kidney Disease](19_Nutrition_Kidney_Disease_Comprehensive_Guide.md)

#### Key Recommendations

**Nutritional Assessment:**
- **Regular screening**: Every 1-3 months for dialysis patients
- **Assessment tools**: Subjective global assessment, anthropometrics, labs
- **Monitor**: Albumin, prealbumin, nPCR

**Intervention Strategies:**
- **Protein supplementation**: Oral supplements first, consider IDPN if needed
- **Appetite stimulation**: Consider megestrol acetate for anorexia
- **Exercise programs**: Combine with nutrition for optimal outcomes

**Special Considerations:**
- **Inflammation**: Address underlying inflammatory conditions
- **Dialysis adequacy**: Optimize to improve nutritional status
- **Psychosocial support**: Address depression and social barriers

## Section VIII: Future Directions & Emerging Guidelines

### 18. Emerging Topics (2024-2025 UPDATE)

#### SGLT2 Inhibitors in ALL CKD (2024 GUIDELINE CHANGE)
- **Major Update**: Now recommended for ALL CKD patients (not just diabetic)
- **Indications**: eGFR 20-90 mL/min/1.73m² regardless of diabetes status
- **Evidence**: EMPA-KIDNEY trial (2023) - benefit in non-diabetic CKD
- **Combination therapy**: With ACEi/ARB for maximal nephroprotection
- **Cardiovascular protection**: Primary prevention in high-risk CKD

#### Novel Biomarkers (2024 FDA-CLEARED)
- **NephroCheck (TIMP-2 × IGFBP7)**:
  - FDA-cleared 2014, expanded use 2024
  - AKI risk stratification in ICU patients
  - Cutoff >0.3: Implement KDIGO care bundle
  - SAPPHIRE trial: 40% AKI reduction with biomarker-guided care
- **Prospera (Donor-Derived Cell-Free DNA)**:
  - FDA-cleared 2020, standard of care 2024
  - Transplant rejection monitoring
  - >1% dd-cfDNA suggests active rejection
  - Non-invasive surveillance (monthly first 6 months)
- **Proenkephalin (PENK)**:
  - CE-marked Europe 2020, expected FDA 2025-2026
  - Early AKI detection (6-12 hours before creatinine)
  - Superior to creatinine (AUC 0.82 vs 0.65)

#### Novel Technologies (2024 FDA-CLEARED)
- **Aquadex (Ultrafiltration System)**:
  - FDA-approved 2024 for isolated fluid overload
  - Diuretic-resistant cardiorenal syndrome, decompensated heart failure
  - Removes 500-1000 mL/hour without solute clearance
  - Preferred over hemodialysis when NO hyperkalemia/uremia
- **BVA Daxor (Blood Volume Analyzer)**:
  - Objective blood volume measurement
  - Guides ultrafiltration goals precisely
  - Prevents excessive fluid removal
- **RenaSight (AI-Powered Digital Pathology)**:
  - FDA-cleared 2023 for kidney biopsy analysis
  - Automated Banff scoring, glomerular classification
  - 30% reduction in inter-observer variability, 50% faster turnaround

#### Artificial Intelligence in Nephrology (2024 EXPANSION)
- **AKI prediction**: Machine learning algorithms integrated into EMRs
- **RenaSight pathology**: AI-assisted biopsy interpretation (FDA-cleared 2023)
- **Progression risk**: Advanced biomarker integration (KFRE calculator, RenalytixAI)
- **Treatment optimization**: Personalized medicine approaches

#### Home Dialysis Expansion
- **Telehealth integration**: Remote monitoring programs
- **Training protocols**: Standardized education programs
- **Quality metrics**: Home-specific outcome measures

### 19. Guidelines in Development & Recent Updates

#### KDIGO 2024-2025 Major Guideline Updates ⭐

**KDIGO 2025 IgA Nephropathy Guideline (Published 2025):**
- **Paradigm Shift**: From supportive-care-only to early targeted immunologic intervention
- **Key Recommendations**:
  - SGLT2 inhibitors (empagliflozin, dapagliflozin) now standard of care for ALL IgAN patients
  - Target BP <120/80 mmHg (systolic <120)
  - Risk stratification guides treatment escalation (low/moderate/high risk)
  - Disease-modifying therapy for high-risk patients (proteinuria >1 g/day):
    - **Preferred: Iptacopan** (Factor B inhibitor) 200 mg BID - requires meningococcal vaccination
    - **Alternative: Atrasentan** (selective ET-A antagonist) 0.75 mg daily - FDA April 2025
    - **Alternative: Sparsentan** (dual ERA/ARB) 200-400 mg daily
    - **Alternative: Nefecon/Tarpeyo** (targeted-release budesonide) 16 mg × 9 months
  - Systemic corticosteroids demoted to second-line therapy
  - Combination therapy may be considered for highest-risk patients
- **Impact**: First major guideline update reflecting 2023-2025 FDA approvals (iptacopan, atrasentan)
- **Reference**: See Document 03 (Glomerular Diseases) for comprehensive treatment algorithm

**KDIGO 2024 Lupus Nephritis Guideline Update (Published March 2024):**
- **Paradigm Shift**: Belimumab triple therapy elevated from "optional" to "first-line standard of care"
- **Key Recommendations**:
  - **Triple therapy (MMF + steroids + belimumab)** now preferred first-line for Class III/IV LN
  - Initiate belimumab at diagnosis (don't wait for MMF failure)
  - Lower cumulative steroid doses (start 0.5-0.75 mg/kg vs. historical 1 mg/kg)
  - Hydroxychloroquine should be continued in ALL lupus nephritis patients
  - Voclosporin (Lupkynis®) is alternative first-line option for rapid proteinuria reduction
  - Rituximab recommended for refractory disease (despite LUNAR trial negativity)
  - Maintenance therapy should include belimumab continuation (≥24 months)
- **Evidence Base**: BLISS-LN Trial (2020) - 51% reduction in renal-related events with belimumab
- **Impact**: First major change in lupus nephritis treatment algorithms in over a decade
- **Reference**: See Document 03 (Glomerular Diseases) for comprehensive treatment protocol
