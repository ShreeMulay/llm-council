# Nephrology Board Examination - High-Yield Concepts Summary

## Overview

This comprehensive board preparation guide focuses on the most frequently tested and high-yield concepts in nephrology for board examinations. It is organized by topic areas with emphasis on clinical scenarios, diagnostic criteria, treatment protocols, and board-style questions.

## Section I: Core Clinical Syndromes (Highest Yield)

### 1. Acute Kidney Injury (AKI) - Board Essentials
**Reference**: [Topic 1 - Acute Kidney Injury](01_Acute_Kidney_Injury_Comprehensive_Guide.md)

#### Must-Know KDIGO Staging
| Stage | Serum Creatinine | Urine Output | Clinical Significance |
|-------|------------------|--------------|----------------------|
| Stage 1 | ↑1.5-1.9x baseline OR ↑0.3 mg/dL | <0.5 mL/kg/h × 6-12h | Early injury, reversible |
| Stage 2 | ↑2.0-2.9x baseline | <0.5 mL/kg/h × ≥12h | Moderate injury |
| Stage 3 | ↑3.0x baseline OR ↑4.0 mg/dL OR dialysis | <0.3 mL/kg/h × ≥24h OR anuria ×12h | Severe injury, often dialysis |

#### Board Questions Pattern: AKI
**Common Clinical Scenarios:**
- Postoperative patient with rising creatinine
- Sepsis-induced AKI in ICU
- Contrast-induced nephropathy
- Rhabdomyolysis after trauma
- Obstruction (BPH, stones)

**Key Diagnostic Clues:**
- **Pre-renal**: BUN/Cr ratio >20:1, FeNa <1%, FENa <1%, urine osm >500
- **Intrinsic**: BUN/Cr ratio ~10-20:1, FeNa >2%, muddy brown casts (ATN)
- **Post-renal**: BUN/Cr ratio >10:1, hydronephrosis on ultrasound

**High-Yield Treatment Points:**
- Avoid nephrotoxins in AKI
- Volume optimization in pre-renal AKI
- Early nephrology consultation for Stage 3
- Dialysis indications: hyperkalemia, volume overload, acidosis, uremia

### 2. Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) - Board Essentials
**Reference**: [Topic 2 - Chronic Kidney Disease](02_Chronic_Kidney_Disease_Comprehensive_Guide.md)

#### Must-Know KDIGO Classification
| Stage | eGFR (mL/min/1.73m²) | Description | Key Management |
|-------|----------------------|-------------|----------------|
| G1 | ≥90 | Normal/high with markers of damage | Risk factor modification |
| G2 | 60-89 | Mildly decreased | Slow progression |
| G3a | 45-59 | Mild-moderate decrease | ACEi/ARB if proteinuria |
| G3b | 30-44 | Moderate-severe decrease | Prepare for ESRD |
| G4 | 15-29 | Severe decrease | Dialysis access planning |
| G5 | <15 | Kidney failure | Dialysis or transplant |

#### Board Questions Pattern: CKD
**Common Clinical Scenarios:**
- Diabetic nephropathy progression
- Hypertensive nephrosclerosis
- Polycystic kidney disease
- Glomerulonephritis progression

**Key Diagnostic Points:**
- **Albuminuria categories**: A1 (<30 mg/g), A2 (30-300 mg/g), A3 (>300 mg/g)
- **ACEi/ARB benefits**: Reduce proteinuria, slow progression
- **SGLT2 inhibitors (2024 BOARD ALERT)**: Standard of care for ALL CKD patients (eGFR ≥20), NOT just diabetic
  - Dapagliflozin (Farxiga) 10 mg daily or Empagliflozin (Jardiance) 10 mg daily
  - EMPA-KIDNEY, DAPA-CKD trials: 40% reduction in kidney disease progression
- **Finerenone (Kerendia) - NEW 2024**: Non-steroidal MRA for diabetic CKD
  - 10-20 mg daily reduces CKD progression and CV events (FIDELIO, FIGARO trials)
- **Semaglutide (Ozempic) - 2025 BOARD ALERT** ⭐ **HIGHEST YIELD - NEWEST APPROVAL**
  - **FDA-approved January 28, 2025** for CKD - First and ONLY GLP-1 approved for kidney disease progression
  - FLOW trial: 24% reduction in kidney disease progression, 18% reduction in MACE
  - **High-Yield Board Point**: Semaglutide is the ONLY GLP-1 RA with specific CKD indication
  - Dosing: 0.5-1.0 mg subcutaneous weekly
  - Safe down to eGFR 25 mL/min
- **Complications**: Anemia, bone disease, cardiovascular disease
- **eGFR equation UPDATE (2021)**: CKD-EPI equation WITHOUT race-based correction

**High-Yield Treatment Algorithms (2025 UPDATE):**
- **Diabetic CKD (5-PILLAR APPROACH)**: ACEi/ARB + SGLT2i + Finerenone + Statin + **GLP-1 RA (Semaglutide)**
  - Use GLP-1 RA if: inadequate glycemic control, obesity (BMI ≥30), or established CVD
  - **Board Testable**: Know that semaglutide is NOW FDA-approved specifically for CKD (2025)
- **Non-diabetic CKD**: SGLT2 inhibitor for ALL + ACEi/ARB if proteinuria >300 mg/g
- **CKD-MBD**: Phosphate binders, vitamin D, calcimimetics

### 3. Glomerular Diseases - Board Essentials
**Reference**: [Topic 3 - Glomerular Diseases](03_Glomerular_Diseases_Comprehensive_Guide.md)

#### Must-Know Syndromes
| Syndrome | Key Features | Typical Causes | Treatment |
|----------|--------------|----------------|-----------|
| Nephrotic | Proteinuria >3.5g/day, edema, hypoalbuminemia, hyperlipidemia | Minimal change, FSGS, membranous | Steroids, immunosuppression |
| Nephritic | Hematuria, RBC casts, hypertension, mild proteinuria | Post-strep, IgA, lupus | Treat underlying cause |
| RPGN | Rapid GFR decline, active urinary sediment | Anti-GBM, immune complex, pauci-immune | Pulse steroids + cyclophosphamide |

#### Board Questions Pattern: Glomerular Diseases
**High-Yield Presentations (2024 UPDATE):**
- **Minimal Change Disease**: Children, selective proteinuria, responds to steroids
- **FSGS**: Adults, nephrotic syndrome, often steroid-resistant
- **Membranous Nephropathy**: Adults, thromboembolism risk, anti-PLA2R antibodies
- **IgA Nephropathy (2024-2025 BOARD ALERT)** - Multiple NEW FDA-Approved Therapies:
  - Gross hematuria after URI, mesangial IgA deposition
  - **Sparsentan (Filspari)**: FDA-approved February 2023
    - Dual endothelin/angiotensin receptor antagonist
    - Superior to irbesartan (PROTECT trial 2023): 40-50% proteinuria reduction
    - **High-Yield Board Point**: First-line for IgA nephropathy with proteinuria >1 g/day
  - **Atrasentan (Vanrafia)**: FDA-approved April 2, 2025 (accelerated) ⭐ **NEWEST IgAN DRUG**
    - **First-and-only selective endothelin A receptor antagonist**
    - ALIGN trial: 36.1% proteinuria reduction vs. placebo
    - 37.4% reduction when combined with SGLT2 inhibitors
    - Dosing: 0.75 mg PO daily
    - **High-Yield Board Point**: Selective ET-A antagonist (April 2025 approval - NEWEST for boards)
    - **Critical AE**: Fluid retention/edema (monitor weight, BP) + teratogenic (BOXED WARNING)
  - **Iptacopan (Fabhalta)**: FDA-approved August 2024 (accelerated approval)
    - First-in-class oral complement inhibitor (Factor B inhibitor)
    - APPLAUSE-IgAN trial: 44% proteinuria reduction vs. 9% placebo (p<0.0001)
    - October 2025: Positive final results showing slowing of eGFR decline
    - **High-Yield Board Point**: Oral complement inhibitor for IgA nephropathy - dual benefit (proteinuria + eGFR)
    - **Critical Monitoring**: Meningococcal vaccination mandatory before starting
- **ANCA-Associated Vasculitis (2024 BOARD ALERT)**:
  - RPGN with pauci-immune GN, c-ANCA (PR3) or p-ANCA (MPO)
  - **Avacopan (Tavneos)**: FDA-approved October 2021
  - Selective C5a receptor antagonist (complement inhibitor)
  - ADVOCATE trial 2021: Superior sustained remission vs prednisone (65.7% vs 54.9%, p=0.007)
  - 60% reduction in cumulative steroid dose, better renal outcomes
  - **High-Yield Board Point**: Steroid-sparing alternative for ANCA vasculitis (use with rituximab or cyclophosphamide)
  - **Critical AE**: Hepatotoxicity (monitor LFTs monthly) - BOXED WARNING
- **Lupus Nephritis**: Multiple classes, treat based on ISN/RPS classification

**Essential Diagnostic Tests:**
- **ANA, anti-dsDNA**: Lupus nephritis
- **Anti-GBM antibodies**: Goodpasture syndrome
- **ANCA**: Vasculitis (c-ANCA for Wegener, p-ANCA for microscopic polyangiitis)
- **Anti-PLA2R**: Membranous nephropathy
- **Complement levels**: Low in lupus, post-strep GN

**Treatment Priorities:**
- **Induction**: High-dose steroids + cyclophosphamide or mycophenolate
- **Maintenance**: Azathioprine or mycophenolate
- **RPGN**: Treat as emergency - dialysis may be needed

## Section II: Electrolytes & Acid-Base (Very High Yield)

### 4. Potassium Disorders - Board Essentials
**Reference**: [Topic 6 - Electrolyte & Acid-Base Disorders](06_Electrolyte_Acid_Base_Disorders_Comprehensive_Guide.md)

#### Must-Know Hyperkalemia Management
| Severity | K+ Level | ECG Changes | Treatment |
|----------|----------|-------------|-----------|
| Mild | 5.0-6.0 mEq/L | Peaked T waves | Remove K+ sources, adjust meds |
| Moderate | 6.0-6.5 mEq/L | Peaked T waves | Calcium gluconate + shift K+ |
| Severe | >6.5 mEq/L | QRS widening, sine wave | All above + prepare dialysis |

**Emergency Treatment Sequence:**
1. **Calcium gluconate** (membrane stabilization)
2. **Insulin + D50W** (shift intracellular)
3. **Beta-agonists** (albuterol nebulization)
4. **Bicarbonate** (if acidotic)
5. **Remove K+**: Diuretics, exchange resin, dialysis

#### Board Questions Pattern: Potassium
**Common Scenarios:**
- ACEi/ARB-induced hyperkalemia
- Hyperkalemia in CKD
- Rhabdomyolysis
- Tumor lysis syndrome

**Key Diagnostic Clues:**
- **Trans-tubular K+ gradient (TTKG)**: Distinguish renal vs extrarenal causes
- **Medication review**: Always ask about ACEi, ARB, K-sparing diuretics
- **Acid-base status**: Acidosis shifts K+ extracellularly

### 5. Sodium Disorders - Board Essentials
#### Must-Know Hyponatremia Classification
| Category | Serum Na | Osmolality | Volume Status | Common Causes |
|----------|----------|------------|---------------|---------------|
| Hypovolemic | <135 | Low | Low | Diuretics, vomiting |
| Euvolemic | <135 | Low | Normal | SIADH, hypothyroidism |
| Hypervolemic | <135 | Low | High | Heart failure, cirrhosis |

**Board Questions Pattern: Sodium**
**High-Yield Points:**
- **SIADH diagnosis**: Low osm, high urine osm >100, euvolemic, normal thyroid/adrenal
- **Treatment**: Fluid restriction first line, consider demeclocycline or tolvaptan
- **Rapid correction risk**: Osmotic demyelination - correct no more than 8-12 mEq/L in 24h
- **Central pontine myelinolysis**: Dysarthria, paralysis, seizures

### 6. Acid-Base Disorders - Board Essentials
#### Must-Kow Winter's Formula
**Expected PaCO2 = 1.5 × HCO3- + 8 ± 2**

**Board Questions Pattern: Acid-Base**
**Common Scenarios:**
- **RTA Type 1**: Distal, inability to acidify urine, nephrocalcinosis
- **RTA Type 2**: Proximal, bicarbonate wasting, Fanconi syndrome
- **RTA Type 4**: Hyperkalemic, aldosterone resistance, diabetic nephropathy

**High-Yield Calculations:**
- **Anion gap = Na+ - (Cl- + HCO3-)**
- **Delta gap = Measured AG - Normal AG (12)**
- **Expected HCO3- = 24 - Delta gap** (for pure high AG acidosis)

## Section III: Dialysis & Transplantation (High Yield)

### 7. Hemodialysis - Board Essentials
**Reference**: [Topic 8 - Hemodialysis](08_Hemodialysis_Comprehensive_Guide.md)

#### Must-Kow Dialysis Adequacy
- **Kt/V target**: ≥1.2 per session (3x/week schedule)
- **URR (Urea Reduction Ratio)**: ≥65%
- **Standard prescription**: 3-4 hours, 3 times weekly

**Board Questions Pattern: Dialysis**
**Common Scenarios:**
- **Dialysis initiation**: GFR <15 mL/min with uremic symptoms
- **Access complications**: Infection, thrombosis, steal syndrome
- **Dialysis disequilibrium**: Headache, seizures during/after dialysis

**High-Yield Complications:**
- **Hypotension**: Most common complication
- **Muscle cramps**: Due to rapid ultrafiltration
- **Access infection**: More common with catheters than fistulas

### 8. Kidney Transplantation - Board Essentials
**Reference**: [Topic 11 - Kidney Transplantation](11_Kidney_Transplantation_Comprehensive_Guide.md)

#### Must-Know Immunosuppression
| Agent | Class | Key Toxicity | Monitoring |
|-------|-------|--------------|------------|
| Prednisone | Steroid | Hyperglycemia, osteoporosis | Blood glucose, bone density |
| Tacrolimus | Calcineurin inhibitor | Nephrotoxicity, neurotoxicity | Drug levels |
| Mycophenolate | Antimetabolite | Bone marrow suppression | CBC |
| Sirolimus | mTOR inhibitor | Hyperlipidemia, delayed wound healing | Drug levels |

**Board Questions Pattern: Transplant**
**Common Scenarios:**
- **Acute rejection**: Rising creatinine, graft tenderness
- **Drug interactions**: Azole antifungals increase tacrolimus levels
- **Infection prophylaxis**: TMP-SMX for PJP, valganciclovir for CMV

**High-Yield Points:**
- **Living donor > deceased donor**: Better outcomes, longer graft survival
- **ABO incompatible transplantation**: Possible with desensitization
- **BK virus nephropathy**: Consider reducing immunosuppression

## Section IV: Novel Biomarkers & Technologies (2024 BOARD-TESTABLE)

### 11. FDA-Cleared Biomarkers - Board Essentials

#### Must-Know Novel Biomarkers (High-Yield for 2024+ Boards)

**NephroCheck (TIMP-2 × IGFBP7) - FDA-cleared 2014:**
- **Indication**: AKI risk stratification in ICU patients
- **Cutoff values**:
  - >0.3 (ng/mL)²/1000: Moderate-high AKI risk (≥20% within 12 hours)
  - >2.0: Very high risk (>50%)
- **Clinical Action**: If >0.3, implement KDIGO care bundle (optimize volume, avoid nephrotoxins)
- **Board Point**: SAPPHIRE trial showed 40% AKI reduction with biomarker-guided care

**Prospera (Donor-Derived Cell-Free DNA) - FDA-cleared 2020:**
- **Indication**: Transplant rejection monitoring
- **Interpretation**:
  - >1% dd-cfDNA: Suggests active rejection
  - Sensitivity 85-95% for antibody-mediated rejection
- **Advantage**: Detects subclinical rejection before creatinine rise
- **Limitation**: Cannot distinguish ACR vs AMR (biopsy still needed)
- **Board Point**: Non-invasive surveillance test, order monthly first 6 months post-transplant

**Proenkephalin (PENK) - CE-marked Europe 2020, Expected FDA 2025-2026:**
- **Indication**: Early AKI detection, CKD progression prediction
- **Advantage**: Earlier than creatinine (6-12 hours before), not affected by muscle mass
- **Evidence**: Meta-analysis showed superior to creatinine (AUC 0.82 vs 0.65)
- **Board Point**: Emerging biomarker, know mechanism and advantages

#### Board Questions Pattern: Biomarkers
**Common Scenarios:**
- ICU patient at risk for AKI → Use NephroCheck
- Transplant patient with rising Cr → Check Prospera before biopsy
- Early AKI detection → PENK superior to creatinine

### 12. Novel Technologies - Board Essentials (2024 TESTABLE)

**Aquadex (Ultrafiltration System) - FDA-approved 2024:**
- **Indication**: Isolated fluid overload WITHOUT need for solute clearance
- **Best for**: Diuretic-resistant cardiorenal syndrome, decompensated heart failure
- **Mechanism**: Isolated ultrafiltration (removes fluid, NOT K+ or uremic toxins)
- **Contraindications**: Hyperkalemia, uremia, severe acidosis (use hemodialysis instead)
- **Board Point**: Choose Aquadex for volume overload alone, hemodialysis if electrolyte/uremia issues

**BVA Daxor (Blood Volume Analyzer) - FDA-cleared:**
- **Indication**: Objective blood volume measurement
- **Use**: Guides ultrafiltration goals, distinguishes true hypervolemia vs hypovolemia
- **Advantage**: Prevents excessive fluid removal
- **Board Point**: Useful for complex volume status assessment

**RenaSight (AI-Powered Digital Pathology) - FDA-cleared 2023:**
- **Indication**: Kidney biopsy analysis
- **Applications**: Automated Banff scoring, glomerular classification, fibrosis quantification
- **Advantage**: Reduces inter-observer variability by 30%, faster turnaround
- **Board Point**: Adjunct to pathologist, NOT standalone diagnosis

#### High-Yield Board Points (2024 Technologies)
1. **NephroCheck >0.3** → Implement KDIGO AKI prevention bundle
2. **Prospera >1%** → Consider biopsy for rejection
3. **Aquadex** → Isolated volume overload WITHOUT hyperkalemia/uremia
4. **Hemodialysis** → Volume overload WITH hyperkalemia/uremia/acidosis
5. **RenaSight** → Standardizes pathology interpretation

## Section V: Special Situations (Medium-High Yield)

### 9. Pregnancy & Kidney Disease - Board Essentials
**Reference**: [Topic 13 - Pregnancy & Kidney Disease](13_Pregnancy_Kidney_Disease_Comprehensive_Guide.md)

#### Must-Know Physiologic Changes
- **GFR increases 50%**: Creatinine decreases to 0.5-0.7 mg/dL
- **Proteinuria**: Up to 300 mg/day considered normal
- **Serum bicarbonate**: Decreases to 18-22 mEq/L (respiratory alkalosis compensation)

**Board Questions Pattern: Pregnancy**
**High-Yield Scenarios:**
- **Preeclampsia**: New hypertension + proteinuria after 20 weeks
- **Lupus nephritis in pregnancy**: High risk of flare, continue hydroxychloroquine
- **Dialysis in pregnancy**: Increase frequency to 5-6 times weekly

**Safe Medications in Pregnancy:**
- **Antihypertensives**: Labetalol, nifedipine, methyldopa
- **Immunosuppression**: Prednisone, azathioprine, tacrolimus (monitor levels)
- **Avoid**: ACEi, ARB, mycophenolate, cyclophosphamide

### 10. Medication Dosing in Renal Failure - Board Essentials
**Reference**: [Topic 12 - Renal Pharmacology](12_Renal_Pharmacology_Comprehensive_Guide.md)

#### Must-Know Dosing Adjustments
| Medication | CrCl >50 | CrCl 10-50 | CrCl <10 | Dialysis |
|------------|----------|------------|----------|---------|
| Vancomycin | q12h | q24-48h | q48-72h | Post-HD |
| Gentamicin | q8h | q12-24h | q24-48h | Post-HD |
| Digoxin | q24h | q24-48h | q48-72h | Post-HD |
| Metformin | Avoid | Avoid | Avoid | Avoid |
| NOACs | Standard | Reduce dose | Avoid | Variable |

**Board Questions Pattern: Pharmacology**
**Common Scenarios:**
- **Antibiotic dosing**: Vancomycin, aminoglycosides, fluoroquinolones
- **Anticoagulation**: Warfarin (monitor INR), DOACs (dose adjust)
- **Diuretics**: Loop diuretics still effective in CKD

**High-Yield Toxicities:**
- **Aminoglycosides**: Nephrotoxicity, ototoxicity
- **Amphotericin B**: Nephrotoxicity, electrolyte wasting
- **Contrast media**: Prevent with hydration, possibly N-acetylcysteine

## Section V: High-Yield Board Question Patterns

### Pattern 1: "What is the next best step?"
**Strategy**: Focus on stabilization first, then diagnosis
- **AKI**: Volume status assessment → Stop nephrotoxins → Consult nephrology
- **Hyperkalemia**: ECG → Calcium gluconate → Shift K+ → Remove K+
- **Nephrotic syndrome**: Rule out secondary causes → Consider biopsy → Start steroids

### Pattern 2: "Which medication should be discontinued?"
**Strategy**: Identify nephrotoxins in renal impairment
- **ACEi/ARB**: In AKI, bilateral renal artery stenosis
- **NSAIDs**: In CKD, heart failure, cirrhosis
- **Metformin**: When eGFR <30 mL/min
- **Contrast**: In at-risk patients without prophylaxis

### Pattern 3: "What is the most likely diagnosis?"
**Strategy**: Use pattern recognition with key clinical features
- **Minimal change**: Child + nephrotic syndrome + responds to steroids
- **FSGS**: Adult + nephrotic syndrome + HIV/obesity
- **Membranous**: Adult + nephrotic syndrome + thromboembolism
- **IgA nephropathy**: Gross hematuria after URI

### Pattern 4: "What is the best initial diagnostic test?"
**Strategy**: Choose least invasive, most informative test
- **Proteinuria workup**: Urine protein/creatinine ratio
- **Hematuria workup**: Urine microscopy + urine culture
- **AKI workup**: Renal ultrasound (rule out obstruction)
- **Glomerular disease**: ANA, ANCA, complement levels

## Section VI: Must-Kow Formulas & Calculations

### Essential Formulas
1. **eGFR (CKD-EPI)**:
   - Male, Black: eGFR = 141 × min(Scr/κ,1)^α × max(Scr/κ,1)^-1.209 × 0.993^Age × 1.159
   - Male, non-Black: Same without 1.159 factor
   - Female: Multiply by 1.018 (Black) or 1.012 (non-Black)

2. **FeNa (Fractional Excretion of Sodium)**:
   - FeNa = (Urine Na × Serum Cr) / (Serum Na × Urine Cr) × 100

3. **Anion Gap**:
   - AG = Na+ - (Cl- + HCO3-)

4. **Delta Gap**:
   - Delta gap = Measured AG - Normal AG (12)

5. **Expected Bicarbonate in High AG Acidosis**:
   - Expected HCO3- = 24 - Delta gap

6. **Winter's Formula**:
   - Expected PaCO2 = 1.5 × HCO3- + 8 ± 2

7. **TTKG (Trans-Tubular K+ Gradient)**:
   - TTKG = (Urine K+ × Serum Osm) / (Serum K+ × Urine Osm)

### Critical Values to Memorize
- **Normal eGFR**: >90 mL/min/1.73m²
- **CKD Stage 3**: 30-59 mL/min/1.73m²
- **Dialysis threshold**: <15 mL/min/1.73m²
- **Normal anion gap**: 8-12 mEq/L
- **Severe hyperkalemia**: >6.5 mEq/L
- **Severe hyponatremia**: <120 mEq/L
- **Kt/V target**: ≥1.2
- **Proteinuria threshold**: >300 mg/day

## Section VII: Board Study Strategy

### 1-Week Intensive Study Plan

**Day 1: Core Syndromes**
- AKI: KDIGO staging, causes, management
- CKD: Staging, complications, progression
- Glomerular diseases: Nephrotic vs nephritic patterns

**Day 2: Electrolytes & Acid-Base**
- Potassium: Emergency management
- Sodium: Hyponatremia workup
- Acid-base: RTA types, anion gap acidosis

**Day 3: Dialysis & Transplant**
- Hemodialysis: Adequacy, complications
- Peritoneal dialysis: Peritonitis management
- Transplant: Immunosuppression, rejection

**Day 4: Pharmacology**
- Antibiotic dosing adjustments
- Antihypertensive choices
- Nephrotoxic medications

**Day 5: Special Situations**
- Pregnancy: Physiologic changes, safe medications
- Genetics: PKD, Alport syndrome
- Geriatrics: Frailty, dialysis decisions

**Day 6: Practice Questions**
- Complete 50-100 board-style questions
- Review incorrect answers thoroughly
- Identify knowledge gaps

**Day 7: Final Review**
- Review high-yield formulas
- Memorize critical values
- Review emergency algorithms

### Test-Taking Strategies

1. **Read the question stem first**: Identify the clinical scenario
2. **Look for key lab values**: Creatinine, potassium, proteinuria
3. **Consider the time course**: Acute vs chronic presentation
4. **Eliminate obviously wrong answers**: Use process of elimination
5. **Choose the most specific answer**: General vs specific management
6. **Watch for absolute words**: "Always," "never" are often incorrect
7. **Consider safety first**: Stabilize before diagnosing

### Common Board Question Traps

1. **Normal creatinine in pregnancy**: 0.5-0.7 mg/dL is normal
2. **Contrast prophylaxis**: IV hydration is most evidence-based
3. **ACEi in bilateral renal artery stenosis**: Can cause AKI
4. **Metformin in severe CKD**: Avoid when eGFR <30
5. **Steroid response in minimal change**: Usually within 8 weeks
6. **ANCA patterns**: c-ANCA (PR3) for Wegener, p-ANCA (MPO) for others

1. **Focus on patterns**: Recognize classic presentations
2. **Memorize critical values**: Know emergency thresholds
3. **Practice calculations**: eGFR, FeNa, anion gap
4. **Understand mechanisms**: Why treatments work
5. **Review guidelines**: KDIGO recommendations are frequently tested
6. **Practice time management**: Average 2 minutes per question
7. **Stay calm**: Read questions carefully, don't rush
