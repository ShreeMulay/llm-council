## 1. Executive Summary

### The Opportunity

A dedicated GLP-1 Kidney Protection Clinic represents the single most impactful new service line TKE can launch in 2026. It operationalizes quadruple therapy for diabetic CKD, directly supports the BHAG ("Ridding the World of the Need for Dialysis!"), and creates a scalable revenue stream through E/M visits, RPM, and CCM billing.

### Council Consensus (5/5 models agree)

- **Launch recommendation:** YES -- strong strategic, clinical, and financial fit
- **Model:** APP-led, physician-supervised, embedded within existing CKD workflow
- **Year 1 target:** 150-200 active patients (pilot)
- **Year 3 target:** 500-900 active patients
- **Revenue per patient/year:** $900-$3,200 (range reflects dispensing model)
- **Dispensing:** Specialty pharmacy partnership first; evaluate buy-and-bill in Phase 2
- **Key accelerator:** Medicare GLP-1 Bridge program (July 2026) -- $50/month Medicare GLP-1 access; CMS BALANCE model (January 2027)

### Why Now

1. **FLOW trial** (NEJM 2024) -- definitive evidence for GLP-1 kidney protection
2. **FDA approval** of semaglutide (Ozempic) for CKD indication (January 2025)
3. **Medicare GLP-1 Bridge** launching July 2026 ($50/mo Medicare copay); **BALANCE model** January 2027
4. **KDIGO 2024 guidelines** include GLP-1 RAs in CKD management
5. **CKM syndrome framework** positions nephrology as metabolic guardian
6. **TKE already prescribes GLP-1s** as part of quadruple therapy -- this systematizes it

### What This Is NOT

This is **not** a weight-loss clinic. This is a **Kidney Protection & CKM Clinic** that uses GLP-1 receptor agonists as evidence-based therapy to prevent dialysis. This distinction matters for:
- Brand integrity ("Big Expertise. Small-Town Heart.")
- Payer positioning (medical necessity vs. cosmetic)
- PCP referral messaging (specialist co-management)
- Regulatory posture (avoid weight-loss clinic stigma)

```
QUADRUPLE THERAPY FOR DIABETIC CKD
1. RAAS Inhibitor (ACE/ARB)
2. SGLT2 Inhibitor (Farxiga/Jardiance)
3. Nonsteroidal MRA (Kerendia/finerenone)
4. GLP-1 Receptor Agonist (Ozempic/Mounjaro) <-- THIS CLINIC
```

## 3. Clinical Evidence Foundation

### The FLOW Trial (NEJM, July 2024)

The **first dedicated kidney outcomes trial** for a GLP-1 receptor agonist. This is the clinical backbone of the entire clinic.

| Outcome | Hazard Ratio | Reduction | P-value |
|---------|-------------|-----------|---------|
| **Primary: Major kidney disease events** | 0.76 | **24% reduction** | P=0.0003 |
| Kidney-specific components | 0.79 | 21% reduction | P<0.05 |
| **CV death** | 0.71 | **29% reduction** | P<0.001 |
| **All-cause mortality** | 0.80 | **20% reduction** | P=0.01 |
| Major CV events (MACE) | 0.82 | 18% reduction | P=0.029 |
| eGFR slope (annual decline) | -- | 1.16 mL/min/1.73m2 slower | P<0.001 |

- **Population:** 3,533 patients with T2D + CKD (eGFR 25-75)
- **Drug:** Semaglutide 1.0 mg weekly vs. placebo
- **Follow-up:** Median 3.4 years (stopped early for efficacy)
- **Implication:** GLP-1s are no longer "nice to have" -- they are essential kidney-protective therapy

## 4. Clinical Model & Workflow

### Clinic Naming

**Recommended:** "TKE Metabolic Kidney Health Clinic" or "TKE CKM Clinic"

Do NOT call it a "weight loss clinic." Frame it as kidney protection.

### Provider Model: APP-Led, Physician-Supervised

| Role | Responsibility | Time Allocation |
|------|---------------|----------------|
| **NP or PA (Primary)** | Runs clinic: initiation, titration, side-effect management, education | 0.5 FTE Year 1, 1.0 FTE Year 2+ |
| **Nephrologist (Oversight)** | Defines protocols, handles complex cases, supports appeals, reviews dashboards | 2-4 hours/month |
| **MA/RN** | Pre-visit vitals/BMI, injection teaching, RPM setup, PA coordination | 0.5-1.0 FTE |
| **PA Coordinator** | Prior authorizations, appeals, pharmacy liaison | 0.5-1.0 FTE (critical role) |

**Council recommendation:** NP slightly preferred over PA for this role due to emphasis on chronic disease counseling, patient education, and longitudinal management. Choose the APP with strongest patient education skills and comfort with diabetes/obesity medications.

### Patient Eligibility Criteria

#### Tier 1 (Priority -- Start Here)
- CKD Stage 2-4 (eGFR 25-75)
- Type 2 Diabetes
- Obesity (BMI >=30) or overweight with metabolic comorbidity (BMI >=27)
- Already on RAASi + SGLT2i (or documented contraindication)
- NOT currently on GLP-1 RA
- Albuminuria (UACR >30) and/or high CV risk

#### Tier 2 (Expand To)
- CKD + obesity + established ASCVD/HF
- Diabetic CKD intolerant of other intensification
- Post-AKI metabolic optimization candidates

#### Lower Priority (Later Phases)
- Non-diabetic obesity-only patients
- Transplant recipients (protocolize separately)
- Advanced frailty/ESRD (limited benefit)

### Visit Workflow

#### Visit 1: Initial GLP-1 Evaluation (30-39 min = 99214)

```
PRE-VISIT (MA, 5-10 min)
  - Vitals: Weight, BMI, BP, HR
  - Labs review: eGFR, UACR, A1c, lipids, K+
  - RPM data pull (if applicable)
  - Verify insurance/PA status

PROVIDER VISIT (APP, 20-25 min)
  - Confirm CKD stage + CKM risk profile
  - Review current meds (pillars 1-3 of quadruple therapy)
  - Assess contraindications:
    - MTC/MEN2 family history
    - Pancreatitis history
    - Severe gastroparesis
    - Pregnancy planning
  - Shared decision-making (FLOW data, expectations)
  - Select agent: semaglutide vs tirzepatide
  - Starting dose + titration schedule
  - Set expectations: GI side effects, appetite changes
  - Hydration, protein intake, sick-day rules
  - Order labs: A1c, renal panel, lipids (q3mo)

POST-VISIT (MA/RN, 5-10 min)
  - Injection demonstration (if first-time)
  - Patient education handout
  - PA submission initiated
  - Schedule 4-week follow-up
  - RPM enrollment if eligible
```

#### Follow-Up Titration Visits (20-29 min = 99213)

Every 4-6 weeks during dose escalation:
- Current dose + adherence
- Weight change
- GI side effects (nausea, diarrhea, constipation)
- Glucose trends (if diabetic)
- BP and orthostasis risk
- Hydration/bowel status
- Next titration step
- Insulin/SU down-titration if needed

#### Maintenance Visits (20-29 min = 99213 or 99214)

Every 2-3 months once at target dose:
- Weight/BMI trend
- Labs: eGFR, UACR, A1c
- Side effect reassessment
- Quadruple therapy compliance check
- RPM data review
- Care coordination with primary nephrology visit

## 5. Billing & Coding Strategy

### E/M Coding for GLP-1 Clinic Visits

#### 99213 (Level 3 Established Patient) -- ~$63 Medicare TN-35 APP / ~$74 Physician / ~$90-110 Commercial

**Time-based:** 20-29 minutes total
**MDM-based (Low):** 2+ self-limited problems OR 1 stable chronic illness

> **TN Locality 35 Note:** Jackson, TN Medicare rates are ~15-20% below national averages due to GPCI adjustment (Work 0.964, PE 0.896, MP 0.544). APP billing at 85% of physician rate further reduces Medicare reimbursement. Commercial payers often reimburse APPs at 100% and at 120-150% of Medicare, partially offsetting this.

Use for:
- Stable monthly follow-up
- Straightforward dose increase
- Side-effect check (no management change)
- Quick adherence counseling

#### 99214 (Level 4 Established Patient) -- ~$86 Medicare TN-35 APP / ~$101 Physician / ~$120-150 Commercial

**Time-based:** 30-39 minutes total
**MDM-based (Moderate):** 1+ chronic illness with exacerbation/progression OR prescription drug management requiring monitoring

Use for:
- Initial evaluation (almost always 99214)
- Insulin/SU dose adjustment
- Complex CKD + DM + obesity management
- Side-effect troubleshooting requiring medication change
- Review of kidney + CV risk reduction plan
- Labs interpretation with management decisions

**Why most GLP-1 clinic visits qualify for 99214:**
CKD patients on GLP-1 therapy inherently meet moderate MDM because:
1. **Problems:** Multiple chronic conditions (CKD + T2D + obesity + HTN) = moderate complexity
2. **Data:** External labs reviewed + interpretation = moderate data
3. **Risk:** Prescription drug management (GLP-1 + interactions) = moderate risk

#### 99215 (Level 5 Established Patient) -- ~$129 Medicare TN-35 APP / ~$152 Physician

**Time-based:** 40-54 minutes total
**MDM-based (High):** 1+ chronic illness with severe exacerbation, progression, or side effects posing threat to life/bodily function

Use for (5-10% of visits):
- **Complex decompensation:** AKI superimposed on CKD (Cr spike), severe hyperkalemia (K>6.0) requiring hold/adjust of multiple nephroprotective agents
- **Dialysis planning visits:** eGFR approaching <15-20, discussing access placement, dialysis modality while optimizing remaining therapy
- **Cardiorenal crisis management:** Volume depletion from GLP-1 GI effects + loop diuretics, requiring multi-drug adjustment
- **Complex new referral (or bill 99205):** CKD Stage 4 + uncontrolled T2D (A1c >9) + BMI >40 + recent hospitalization + extensive external records review + discussion with hospitalist/endocrinologist

**Audit guidance:** 99215 should be <10-15% of total GLP-1 clinic visits. Document the specific MDM elements: severity of problem, extensive data reviewed (Category 2: independent lab interpretation + external physician discussion), and high-risk medication management.

#### 99205 (Level 5 New Patient) -- ~$184 Medicare TN-35 APP / ~$216 Physician

For new complex referrals from PCPs/hospitals with severe presentations requiring extensive evaluation. Most standard new referrals should be 99204 (moderate complexity, ~$145-165).

#### G2211 Add-On Code (~$13 Medicare TN-35 APP / ~$16-$40 depending on payer)

Bill WITH 99202-99215 when:
- Visit is the continuing focal point for needed services (TKE IS the medical home for CKD)
- Ongoing care for serious/complex condition (CKD + T2D qualifies)

**This should be billed on nearly every GLP-1 clinic visit.**

**Impact on TKE's Year 2-3 projections:**

**Note:** Ozempic for T2D+CKD is **already covered** by Medicare Part D under the existing diabetes indication. The Bridge/BALANCE programs add coverage for weight-focused indications (Wegovy, Zepbound) that expand the eligible patient pool.

**Action:** TKE should prepare to be a Bridge program participant provider. Monitor CMS announcements (cms.gov/innovation) and apply as soon as applications open.

## 7. Medication Dispensing Strategy

### Council Consensus: Start with Specialty Pharmacy, Evaluate Buy-and-Bill Later

The Council was split 3-2 between specialty pharmacy partnership (majority) and buy-and-bill. The synthesized recommendation:

### Phase 1 (Months 1-12): Specialty Pharmacy Partnership

**Do this first:**
- Partner with 1-2 high-touch specialty or independent pharmacies in Jackson/West TN
- Pharmacy handles: inventory, refills, patient assistance programs, manufacturer copay cards
- TKE handles: prescribing, PA submission, clinical monitoring

**Why:**
- Lower inventory risk (GLP-1s cost $800-$1,350/month wholesale)
- No refrigerated storage requirements
- No spoilage/waste risk
- Pharmacy expertise in manufacturer assistance programs
- Simpler regulatory posture
- Focus staff energy on clinical care, not supply chain

**Preferred pharmacy partner criteria:**
- Strong PA support capabilities
- Experience with specialty/injectable medications
- Adherence monitoring and refill tracking
- Local presence (patient trust in small-town market)
- Manufacturer copay card expertise

### Phase 2 (Months 12-18): Evaluate Buy-and-Bill

After establishing clinical operations, evaluate:

| Factor | Threshold to Proceed |
|--------|---------------------|
| Active patient volume | >300 patients |
| PA approval rate | >80% |
| Payer economics favorable | Confirmed with 2+ major payers |
| Storage/compliance infrastructure | Verified |
| Staff bandwidth | Dedicated inventory manager |

**Buy-and-Bill Potential (if favorable):**

| Agent | WAC/month | Medicare Reimburse (ASP+6%) | Net Margin/pt/mo |
|-------|-----------|---------------------------|-----------------|
| Semaglutide (Ozempic) | ~$935 | ~$990 | ~$55-100 |

### E. CKD Sick-Day Protocol for GLP-1 Patients

This is a key nephrology differentiator. PCPs and medspas do NOT provide this level of kidney-specific safety guidance.

**When to activate sick-day rules:** Vomiting >24 hours, diarrhea >24 hours, fever >101F, unable to maintain oral intake, acute illness requiring antibiotics

**Patient instructions (laminated card for wallet):**

```
TKE GLP-1 SICK DAY RULES
If you are vomiting, have diarrhea, or cannot eat/drink:

1. HOLD your GLP-1 injection until you can eat/drink normally
2. HOLD Farxiga/Jardiance (SGLT2i) until eating normally
3. HOLD Kerendia (finerenone) if you cannot drink fluids
4. CONTINUE your blood pressure medicine unless told otherwise
5. DRINK at least 8 oz of fluid every hour you are awake
6. CHECK your blood sugar if diabetic -- if <70, drink juice
7. CALL TKE at [XXX-XXX-XXXX] if:
   - You cannot keep fluids down for 12+ hours
   - Your urine is very dark or you stop urinating
   - You feel dizzy/lightheaded when standing
   - Your blood sugar is <60 or >300
8. GO TO ER if:
   - No urine output for 8+ hours
   - Confusion, chest pain, or trouble breathing
   - Blood sugar <50 despite juice/glucose tablets

RESUME all medications when eating/drinking normally for 24 hrs.
Call us before resuming if you were sick >48 hours.
```

**Provider protocol for sick-day triage calls:**
- Check BMP (Cr, K+, bicarb) within 24 hours of call
- Hold SGLT2i + finerenone if Cr elevated >30% above baseline
- Hold GLP-1 until GI symptoms resolve
- Consider IV fluids (urgent care or infusion center) if unable to hydrate
- Resume medications sequentially: RAASi first, then SGLT2i, then finerenone, then GLP-1 last
- Recheck BMP 5-7 days after resumption
