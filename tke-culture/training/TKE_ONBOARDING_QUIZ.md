# TKE Culture Onboarding Quiz

> Digital assessment for new hires and annual refresher. Passing score: 80% (20/25 questions).

---

## Quiz Administration

### Format Options
1. **Google Forms** — Free, easy to create and share
2. **Microsoft Forms** — If using M365 ecosystem
3. **Typeform** — More engaging user experience
4. **Custom web app** — If integrating with HRIS

### When to Administer
- **New hires**: Week 1, after reviewing culture materials
- **All staff**: Annual refresher (tie to performance review cycle)
- **Post-training**: After culture rollout sessions

### Scoring
- **25 questions total**
- **80% passing** (20 correct)
- **Immediate feedback** on incorrect answers
- **Retry allowed** after 24 hours

---

## SECTION 1: IDENTITY (5 Questions)

### Q1. What is TKE's slogan (BHAG)?

- A) "Big Expertise. Small-Town Heart."
- B) "Ridding the World of the Need for Dialysis!"
- C) "Prevention Over Profit"
- D) "Treated like family. Built for impact."

**Correct: B**

**Feedback if wrong**: Our slogan is "Ridding the World of the Need for Dialysis!" — this is our aspirational vision that drives everything we do.

---

### Q2. Which tagline is patient-facing?

- A) "Treated like family. Built for impact."
- B) "What happens to one, happens to all."
- C) "Big Expertise. Small-Town Heart."
- D) "Prevention Over Profit"

**Correct: C**

**Feedback if wrong**: "Big Expertise. Small-Town Heart." is what patients experience—clinical excellence with genuine warmth. "Treated like family. Built for impact." is our internal tagline.

---

### Q3. What are the three pillars of TKE's mission?

- A) Prevent, Treat, Bill
- B) Prevent dialysis, Prioritize transplants/home therapies, Empower through education
- C) Diagnose, Medicate, Monitor
- D) Care, Cure, Comfort

**Correct: B**

**Feedback if wrong**: Our mission is: "We transform kidney health by preventing the need for dialysis, prioritizing transplants and home therapies, and empowering patients through education."

---

### Q4. How are the internal and external taglines connected?

- A) They're the same thing
- B) The internal culture produces the external experience
- C) They're for different departments
- D) One is for marketing, one is for HR

**Correct: B**

**Feedback if wrong**: The internal culture ("Treated like family. Built for impact.") is the *engine* that produces the external experience ("Big Expertise. Small-Town Heart.").

---

### Q5. Complete this statement: "Success at TKE is measured by..."

- A) Revenue and profit margins
- B) Number of procedures performed
- C) Patients who never need dialysis
- D) Patient volume

**Correct: C**

**Feedback if wrong**: Our value "Prevention Over Profit" means success = patients who never need dialysis. This is our mission, even when it reduces revenue.

## SECTION 2: ACCOUNTABILITY SYSTEM (8 Questions)

### Q6. What is TKE's Operating Principle?

- A) "Prevention Over Profit"
- B) "What happens to one, happens to all."
- C) "Work Smart, Not Long"
- D) "Direct is Kind"

**Correct: B**

**Feedback if wrong**: Our Operating Principle is "What happens to one, happens to all." This creates shared fate and motivates peer accountability.

---

### Q7. Which of these is a "Trust Destroyer"?

- A) Direct conversation, one-on-one
- B) Direct conversation, as a team
- C) Gossiping about someone to a colleague
- D) Sharing your thought before asking a question

**Correct: C**

**Feedback if wrong**: The three Trust Destroyers are: Ignoring, Gossiping, and Escalating to the boss. Gossiping—talking ABOUT someone instead of TO them—destroys trust even if the other person agrees with you.

---

### Q8. What does "Speak for Yourself" mean?

- A) Don't share information with others
- B) Use "I" statements instead of "we think" or "everyone feels"
- C) Only talk about your own department
- D) Keep opinions private

**Correct: B**

**Feedback if wrong**: "Speak for Yourself" means using "I think" and "I believe" instead of hiding behind "we," "everyone," or "the team." This prevents the other person from feeling ganged-up on.

---

### Q9. What is "Statements Before Questions"?

- A) Always make statements, never ask questions
- B) Share the thought that led to your question before asking it
- C) Answer questions with questions
- D) Ask permission before speaking

**Correct: B**

**Feedback if wrong**: Before asking a question, share the thought that led to it. This prevents miscommunication because the other person doesn't have to guess your intent.

---

### Q10. A colleague keeps missing deadlines. What's the FIRST appropriate step?

- A) Tell your manager and let them handle it
- B) Mention it to other colleagues to see if they've noticed
- C) Have a direct, one-on-one conversation with the colleague
- D) Document the incidents for HR

**Correct: C**

**Feedback if wrong**: The first step is always direct conversation, one-on-one. Escalating to the boss or gossiping to others destroys trust.

---

### Q11. When is it appropriate to involve leadership in a peer issue?

- A) Immediately, to prevent escalation
- B) Only after at least two direct conversations haven't resolved it
- C) Whenever you feel uncomfortable
- D) Never—leadership should stay out of peer issues

**Correct: B**

**Feedback if wrong**: Leadership involvement is a LAST resort. The escalation path is: (1) Direct conversation, (2) Second direct conversation, (3) Peer facilitator, (4) Team conversation, (5) Leadership—only after steps 1-4.

---

### Q12. What should leadership ask FIRST when someone escalates a peer issue?

- A) "What's the other person's side of the story?"
- B) "How serious is this issue?"
- C) "Did you talk to them directly? What happened?"
- D) "Should I call a meeting?"

**Correct: C**

**Feedback if wrong**: Leadership should first ask "Did you talk to them directly? What happened?" and help the person have a more effective conversation, not solve it for them.

---

### Q13. Complete the Contract We Make: "I care enough about you and our mission to tell you what I see. I expect..."

- A) "...you to agree with me."
- B) "...you to do the same for me."
- C) "...leadership to handle the rest."
- D) "...things to improve immediately."

**Correct: B**

**Feedback if wrong**: The Contract We Make is: "I care enough about you and our mission to tell you what I see. I expect you to do the same for me."

## SECTION 3: VALUES & FUNDAMENTALS (8 Questions)

### Q14. What are TKE's 5 Core Values?

- A) Quality, Service, Innovation, Teamwork, Respect
- B) People First, Prevention Over Profit, Work Smart, Data Over Opinion, AI Amplifies Human Impact
- C) Honesty, Integrity, Excellence, Compassion, Growth
- D) Safety, Quality, Efficiency, Patient-Centered, Continuous Improvement

**Correct: B**

**Feedback if wrong**: TKE's 5 Core Values are: (1) People First, Always, (2) Prevention Over Profit, (3) Work Smart, Not Long, (4) Data Over Opinion, (5) AI Amplifies Human Impact.

---

### Q15. How many Fundamentals does TKE have?

- A) 5
- B) 6
- C) 9
- D) 10

**Correct: B**

**Feedback if wrong**: TKE has 6 Fundamentals: (1) Direct is Kind, (2) Listen First Then Act, (3) Fix Systems Own My Part Grow, (4) Test Learn Improve, (5) Unreasonable Hospitality, (6) AI in Everything.

---

### Q16. What does "Fix Systems, Own My Part, Grow" mean?

- A) Only fix systems, individuals aren't responsible
- B) Only hold individuals accountable, systems are fine
- C) Every failure has system gaps AND personal lessons—find both
- D) Growth is optional if systems are fixed

**Correct: C**

**Feedback if wrong**: "Fix Systems, Own My Part, Grow" means every failure reveals system gaps AND opportunities for personal growth. Both are true. "Fix systems" can't become an excuse to avoid personal accountability.

---

### Q17. What question should you ask yourself daily according to "AI in Everything"?

- A) "What did my manager do today?"
- B) "What did I do manually today that AI could help with?"
- C) "How can I avoid using AI?"
- D) "What new AI tool was released?"

**Correct: B**

**Feedback if wrong**: The daily question for "AI in Everything" is: "What did I do manually today that AI could help with?" Every repetitive task is an automation candidate.

---

### Q18. What does "Unreasonable Hospitality" mean?

- A) Being unreasonably demanding of patients
- B) Going beyond expected, anticipating needs, delivering warmth with expertise
- C) Hospitality that costs too much
- D) Only being nice to easy patients

**Correct: B**

**Feedback if wrong**: "Unreasonable Hospitality" means going beyond what's expected—anticipating questions before they're asked, remembering personal details, and providing expertise WITH heart.

---

### Q19. Which statement best represents "Listen First, Then Act"?

- A) "Let me tell you what you should do."
- B) "I understand before solving. I ask before assuming."
- C) "Just listen to me and do what I say."
- D) "Action is more important than listening."

**Correct: B**

**Feedback if wrong**: "Listen First, Then Act" means: "I understand before solving. I ask before assuming. The best care starts with hearing what matters."

---

### Q20. According to "Test, Learn, Improve," which approach is preferred?

- A) Perfect planning before any action
- B) Big bets on untested ideas
- C) Small experiments with clear success criteria
- D) Avoiding experimentation to reduce risk

**Correct: C**

**Feedback if wrong**: "Test, Learn, Improve" means running small experiments with clear success criteria, learning from both successes and failures. Progress beats perfection.

---

### Q21. What distinguishes "Direct is Kind" from being harsh?

- A) Using "we think" statements
- B) Speaking for yourself, sharing thoughts before questions, describing without blame
- C) Being indirect to avoid hurt feelings
- D) Waiting until the problem gets worse

**Correct: B**

**Feedback if wrong**: "Direct is Kind" means: I speak for myself, I share my thought before questions, I describe without blame, I coach directly. Direct ≠ harsh.

## SECTION 4: DECISION-MAKING & APPLICATION (4 Questions)

### Q22. What are the 5 questions in TKE's Decision Filter?

- A) Who, What, When, Where, Why
- B) Patient? Data? Sustainable? AI? Talked directly?
- C) Cost? Benefit? Risk? Time? Resources?
- D) Approve? Deny? Defer? Escalate? Document?

**Correct: B**

**Feedback if wrong**: The 5-Question Decision Filter is: (1) Does this serve the patient? (2) What does the data say? (3) Is this sustainable? (4) Could AI help? (5) Have I talked directly to people affected?

---

### Q23. What are Leadership's Two Jobs?

- A) Hire people and manage budget
- B) Model peer accountability and clear the organizational path
- C) Give orders and track performance
- D) Attend meetings and send emails

**Correct: B**

**Feedback if wrong**: Leadership's Two Jobs are: (1) Model peer accountability—have direct conversations yourself, and (2) Clear the organizational path—remove blockers your team can't solve.

---

### Q24. An MA wants to hold Dr. Mulay accountable for a missed commitment. According to TKE culture, this is:

- A) Inappropriate—staff shouldn't question doctors
- B) Expected and welcomed—bidirectional accountability applies to everyone
- C) Only okay if done privately
- D) Something HR should handle

**Correct: B**

**Feedback if wrong**: Bidirectional accountability means accountability flows everywhere—peer-to-peer, team-to-leadership, and leadership-to-team. An MA can (and should) hold the CEO accountable. Title doesn't shield anyone.

---

### Q25. Complete this philosophy statement: "The mark of a great leader is the ability to describe what happened..."

- A) "...in the most positive light."
- B) "...in a way that protects the team."
- C) "...without assigning blame."
- D) "...with complete accuracy."

**Correct: C**

**Feedback if wrong**: "The mark of a great leader is the ability to describe what happened without assigning blame." This is accountability at TKE—direct, kind, blameless, and growth-oriented.

## IMPLEMENTATION NOTES

### Google Forms Setup
1. Create form with "Quiz" mode enabled
2. Set passing score to 80%
3. Enable "Show correct answer" after submission
4. Require sign-in to prevent repeat attempts
5. Send results to spreadsheet for tracking

### Tracking & Reporting
- Track completion rates by department
- Track average scores over time
- Identify commonly missed questions for additional training
- Require passing before end of probation period

### Annual Refresher
- Same quiz, annual requirement
- Track improvement year-over-year
- Use results to identify areas needing reinforcement
- Consider adding new questions as culture evolves
