# TKE Culture Implementation Playbook (v3.0)

> **12-Week Rollout Plan + Coryell Accountability Training + Onboarding Curriculum**

---

## Executive Summary

This playbook provides a structured 12-week plan to implement the TKE Culture System v3.0 across all locations. The major components include **Eric Coryell's peer accountability framework** and **David Friedman's fundamentals-based approach**, now distilled into 6 core individual behaviors.

**What's New in v3.0**:
- Dual taglines (patient-facing + internal)
- Distilled from 9 fundamentals to 6 (removing redundancy with Accountability System)
- New accountability philosophy intro ("describe without blame")
- "Listen First, Then Act" fundamental (from Selena's input)
- Growth mindset integration ("Fix Systems, Own My Part, Grow")
- Expanded leadership expectations (Clear the path, Shield your team, Standards are standards, 48-hour response)
- "Shared metrics create shared fate" added to Operating Principle

**Success Criteria**:
- 100% of staff can recite 5 Core Values and Operating Principle by Week 8
- 80% of staff can demonstrate direct conversation skills by Week 12
- 100% of staff have had at least ONE real peer accountability conversation by Day 90
- Leadership commitments tracked publicly starting Week 4
- Culture language used in 100% of performance reviews by Month 4
- Measurable improvement in "psychological safety + direct feedback" survey scores by Month 6

### Training Module 1: The Coryell Framework

**Content**:
- Why leader-led accountability doesn't scale
- The psychological contract most teams have (and why we break it)
- Three paths that destroy trust (ignore, gossip, escalate)
- Two paths that build trust (direct 1:1, direct as team)
- Shared fate: "What happens to one, happens to all"
- NEW: "Shared metrics create shared fate"
- Bidirectional accountability: team holds leadership accountable too

**NEW: The Accountability Philosophy**
> "The mark of a great leader is the ability to describe what happened without assigning blame."

- Accountability is not punishment—it's trust
- Same facts, different framing: "The report was late" vs "You screwed up"
- The goal is solutions and growth, not fault-finding

**Discussion Questions**:
1. Think of a time you ignored an issue instead of confronting directly. What happened?
2. Think of a time someone gossiped to you about a colleague. How did it affect your trust?
3. What makes direct conversation scary? What would make it easier?
4. NEW: Describe a situation without assigning blame. How does it feel different?

### Training Module 2: "Direct is Kind" Skill-Building

**"Speak for Yourself" Exercises**:

*Exercise 1: Translate these statements*
| "We" Statement | Translate to "I" Statement |
|----------------|---------------------------|
| "We've noticed you've been late" | ? |
| "Everyone thinks the meeting was too long" | ? |
| "The team feels like you're not pulling your weight" | ? |
| "Leadership has decided to change the policy" | ? |

*Exercise 2: Catch yourself*
- Pair up. Have a 5-minute conversation about a workplace issue.
- Partner's job: interrupt every time you use "we," "everyone," or "the team."
- Switch roles.

**"Statements Before Questions" Exercises**:

*Exercise 1: Add the statement*
| Question Alone | With Statement First |
|----------------|---------------------|
| "Why did you do it that way?" | ? |
| "When will that be done?" | ? |
| "Why didn't you tell me about the problem?" | ? |

*Exercise 2: Practice*
- Partner shares a scenario where they'd want to ask a question
- Before asking, they must share the thought that led to the question
- Partner gives feedback: "I understood your intent" or "I still felt interrogated"

### Training Module 3: "Listen First, Then Act" (NEW)

**Why This Matters**:
- We're experts—we jump to solutions too fast
- Patients and colleagues feel unheard
- Understanding before solving prevents rework and resentment

**Exercises**:

*Exercise 1: Reflect before responding*
- Partner shares a problem (1 minute)
- Before responding, you must reflect back what you heard
- Partner confirms: "Yes, that's right" or "Not quite, what I meant was..."

*Exercise 2: Ask before assuming*
- Practice asking "What matters most to you about this?" before offering solutions
- Practice asking "What have you already tried?" before suggesting fixes

### Training Module 4: Direct Conversation Role-Plays

**Scenario 1: Peer not meeting commitments**
- Setup: Your colleague committed to having the report done by Friday. It's Monday and it's still not done. This is the third time.
- Practice: Have the direct conversation using "I" statements, share your thought before questions, describe without blame.

**Scenario 2: Colleague gossiping**
- Setup: A colleague starts venting to you about another team member who isn't present.
- Practice: Redirect them to direct conversation ("Have you talked to them?")

**Scenario 3: Someone escalates a peer issue to you**
- Setup: A team member comes to you (the manager) with an issue about a peer.
- Practice: Ask "Did you talk to them first?" and help them prepare for that conversation rather than taking it over.

**Scenario 4: Someone escalates an organizational blocker**
- Setup: A team member comes to you about a broken system/process/resource gap.
- Practice: THIS is your job to solve. Acknowledge it, commit to a timeline, and follow through.

**Scenario 5: Giving feedback to leadership**
- Setup: Your manager made a commitment two weeks ago and hasn't delivered.
- Practice: Have the direct conversation (as the team member speaking UP to leadership).

### Training Module 5: Real-Time Interventions

**Practice intervening when someone violates behavioral rules**:

| Violation | Practice Intervention |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Someone says "we think" | "Can you speak for yourself? What do YOU think?" |
| Someone asks question without statement | "Can you share the thought that led to that question?" |
| Someone talks about person not in room | "Have you talked to them directly?" |
| Someone tries to escalate peer issue | "Did you try talking to them first? What happened?" |
| Someone assigns blame | "Can you describe what happened without assigning blame?" |

**Role-play**: In groups of 3, one person violates a rule, one person practices intervening, one person gives feedback on how it felt.

### Week 4: Manager Practice

**Daily Activities**:
- Each manager practices "Direct is Kind" (catch and correct themselves)
- Each manager practices "Listen First" (reflect back before responding)
- Each manager has one direct conversation they've been avoiding
- Each manager redirects at least one escalation attempt (peer issue) OR clears one organizational blocker
- Daily 10-minute manager check-in call (share wins and challenges)

**Preparation for All-Staff Launch**:
- [ ] Each manager prepares 2-3 location-specific examples for each fundamental
- [ ] Managers practice explaining the accountability philosophy to family/friends
- [ ] Managers identify 1-2 potential Culture Champions at their location
- [ ] Managers prepare to share a personal story of when they ignored/gossiped/escalated

## Phase 3: All-Staff Launch (Week 5)

### Objective
Introduce the culture system to all employees with energy, clarity, and commitment—with special emphasis on peer accountability and the new behavioral expectations.

### Launch Event Format

**Option A**: Single all-hands meeting (if logistics allow)
**Option B**: Location-by-location rollout (same week)

**Duration**: 2 hours

**Agenda**:

| Time | Activity | Owner |
|------|----------|-------|
| 0:00-0:15 | Opening: Why this matters (personal story) | Dr. Mulay |
| 0:15-0:25 | Identity: Slogan, Mission, Dual Taglines | Dr. Mulay |
| 0:25-0:45 | The Coryell Framework: Shared Fate + Shared Metrics + Bidirectional Accountability | Dr. Mulay |
| 0:45-0:55 | NEW: The Accountability Philosophy ("describe without blame") | Brandy/Manager |
| 0:55-1:05 | Break |
| 1:05-1:20 | Live Demo: "Direct is Kind" (Speak for Yourself + Statements Before Questions) | Leadership role-play |
| 1:20-1:35 | NEW: "Listen First, Then Act" demo | Leadership role-play |
| 1:35-1:50 | The 5 Values + 6 Fundamentals (interactive) | Brandy/Manager |
| 1:50-2:00 | Q&A + Commitment: 90-day intensive focus | Leadership Panel |

### Key Messages to Emphasize

1. **Dual taglines, one culture**: "Patients experience 'Big Expertise. Small-Town Heart.' That happens because we operate as 'Treated like family. Built for impact.'"

2. **This is different because it goes both ways**: "Leadership will be held accountable too. You can and should give us feedback directly."

3. **Accountability is trust, not punishment**: "The mark of a great leader—and teammate—is describing what happened without assigning blame. We hold each other accountable because we care."

4. **We're breaking the old contract**: "Most teams have an unspoken rule: 'I won't talk about your performance if you don't talk about mine.' We're breaking that. We care enough to tell each other the truth."

5. **Listen before you leap**: "We're experts—that's dangerous. We have to slow down and listen first."

6. **This will be uncomfortable**: "Direct conversation is hard. We're choosing courage over comfort. You won't be punished for speaking up respectfully."

### Materials to Distribute

- [ ] Wallet cards (Values + Operating Principle on front, 6 Fundamentals on back)
- [ ] Break room posters (updated for v3.0)
- [ ] "Direct is Kind" reminder cards
- [ ] Full culture document access (shared drive link)

### Addressing Skepticism

| Question | Response |
|----------|----------|
| "Is this just another initiative that'll fade?" | "Leadership is tracking THEIR commitments publicly now. We're measuring this. In 90 days, you'll see this is real." |
| "What if my manager doesn't follow this?" | "You can hold them accountable too. That's what bidirectional accountability means. If your manager isn't modeling this, you can say so—and leadership will back you up." |
| "What if I try direct conversation and it goes badly?" | "We'll help you. The escalation path exists—but it starts with you trying first. If it doesn't work, we'll facilitate." |
| "This feels scary" | "It IS scary. We're asking you to be braver than most workplaces require. But the alternative—ignoring, gossiping, escalating—destroys trust. We're choosing the harder path because it's better." |

## Risk Mitigation (Updated)

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation Strategy |
|------|------------|---------------------|
| **Cynicism** ("This will fade") | High initially | Leader modeling, leadership commitment tracking, celebrate direct conversation wins |
| **Fear of direct conversation** | High | Start small, celebrate courage, promise no retaliation, leadership models receiving feedback |
| **Manager inconsistency** | High | Manager training, skip-level checks, culture in manager evals, redirect escalation tracking |
| **Leadership doesn't model** | Medium | Public commitment tracking, team feedback to leadership, Dr. Mulay models first |
| **"Direct is Kind" feels unnatural** | High | Repetition, gentle interventions, celebrate progress not perfection |
| **Direct conversation goes badly** | Medium | Provide escalation path (after trying), offer facilitation, debrief failures as learning |
| **Bidirectional accountability feels threatening** | Medium | Leadership goes first, celebrate team-to-leader feedback, no retaliation |
| **Confusion about peer vs. org escalation** | Medium | Clear distinction in training: peer issue → redirect; org blocker → manager owns |
| **"Listen First" slows things down** | Low | Emphasize it prevents rework; practice makes it faster |

## Manager Quick Reference (Updated for v3.0)

### When You See This... → Do This

| Situation | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| Employee says "we think" or "everyone feels" | "Can you speak for yourself? What do YOU think?" |
| Employee asks question without sharing thought | "Can you share the thought that led to that question?" |
| Employee assigns blame | "Can you describe what happened without assigning blame?" |
| Employee venting about colleague | "Have you talked to them directly? That's our standard." |
| Employee escalates PEER issue to you | "Did you talk to them first? What happened?" Then: "How can I help you prepare for that conversation?" (Don't take it over) |
| Employee escalates ORGANIZATIONAL blocker | "I've got this. Let me work on it. I'll update you within 48 hours." (This IS your job) |
| Employee jumps to solution without listening | "What did they say when you asked what mattered most to them?" |
| Someone made an error | "What happened? What's the system issue? What's your part? What did we learn?" |
| Great direct conversation observed | Recognize publicly: "I want to call out [Name] for having a direct conversation about [topic]. That takes courage." |
| Leadership commitment is overdue | (You can say this to Dr. Mulay): "You committed to X by Y date. What's the status?" |
