# Comprehensive Due Diligence Checklist for Acquiring a Nephrology Practice

> **LLM Council Generated** | Generic Template for Medical Practice M&A

---

## 1. CORPORATE/LEGAL

### Documents to Request:
- Articles of Incorporation, Bylaws, Operating Agreements
- Current ownership structure with percentages
- Board meeting minutes (3-5 years)
- All litigation records (pending, threatened, settled)
- Joint venture agreements (especially with dialysis centers)
- Corporate compliance certificates and minute books
- Non-compete/non-solicitation agreements

### Critical Questions:
- Are there any JV arrangements with dialysis providers that could violate Stark/Anti-Kickback?
- Do change of control provisions exist in material contracts?
- What is the enforceability of restrictive covenants?
- Have there been any Medicare audits or settlements?

### Red Flags:
- Undisclosed ownership interests or disputed ownership
- Joint ventures with inadequate compliance documentation
- Pending False Claims Act or regulatory investigations
- Frequent ownership changes or incomplete corporate records

---

## 2. FINANCIAL

### Documents to Request:
- Audited/reviewed financial statements (3-5 years)
- Tax returns (federal and state, 3-5 years)
- Current YTD financials with general ledger detail
- A/R and A/P aging reports
- Normalized EBITDA calculations with add-backs
- Debt schedules and loan agreements
- Capital expenditure history and depreciation schedules

### Critical Questions:
- What is the revenue breakdown between office visits, hospital rounds, and dialysis visits?
- Are personal expenses running through the practice?
- What are the working capital requirements?
- Are there any off-balance-sheet liabilities?

### Red Flags:
- Declining revenue/margins without clear explanation
- Revenue concentration (>30% from single source)
- High aged receivables (>15% over 90 days)
- Inconsistencies between tax returns and financials

---

## 3. REVENUE CYCLE MANAGEMENT

### Documents to Request:
- Days in A/R trending reports
- Net collection rate by payer
- Denial logs with root cause analysis
- Charge capture reports (especially dialysis rounds)
- Write-off analysis and bad debt history
- ESRD Monthly Capitation Payment (MCP) tracking

### Critical Questions:
- What is the clean claim rate?
- How are dialysis rounds tracked and billed?
- Is billing handled in-house or outsourced?
- What is the collection rate for patient responsibility?

### Red Flags:
- Days in A/R exceeding 60-70 days
- Net collection rate below 90%
- High denial rates (>10%)
- Poor tracking of dialysis rounds or missed billing opportunities

---

## 4. PAYER CONTRACTS

### Documents to Request:
- All managed care contracts with fee schedules
- Medicare/Medicaid provider agreements
- Value-based care arrangements (CKCC, ESCO)
- Contract expiration schedule
- Payer mix analysis by volume and revenue
- Credentialing files for all providers

### Critical Questions:
- Are contracts assignable without payer consent?
- How do rates compare to local market benchmarks?
- What percentage of business comes from each payer?
- Are there any exclusive arrangements or most favored nation clauses?

### Red Flags:
- Heavy concentration in low-paying payers
- Contracts personally assigned to selling physicians
- Below-market reimbursement rates
- Imminent contract expirations without renewal options

---

## 5. CLINICAL OPERATIONS

### Documents to Request:
- Clinical protocols for CKD/ESRD management
- Quality metrics (Kt/V, URR, fistula rates, hospitalization rates)
- Provider productivity reports
- Dialysis center coverage agreements
- Medical directorship agreements and time logs
- Equipment inventory and maintenance records

### Critical Questions:
- What is the patient volume per provider per day?
- How are hospital and dialysis rounds structured?
- Are there exclusive rounding agreements?
- What ancillary services are offered (infusions, vascular access)?

### Red Flags:
- Poor clinical outcomes vs. benchmarks
- Over-reliance on single physician for complex procedures
- Lack of standardized clinical protocols
- Outdated or poorly maintained equipment

---

## 6. COMPLIANCE

### Documents to Request:
- Compliance program documentation
- HIPAA risk assessments and breach history
- OIG exclusion screening records
- Stark/Anti-Kickback analysis for all arrangements
- Medical directorship FMV documentation
- Medicare/Medicaid audit results
- Billing compliance audit reports

### Critical Questions:
- How is Fair Market Value determined for medical directorships?
- When was the last compliance audit performed?
- Are there documented time logs for paid directorship duties?
- Have there been any whistleblower complaints?

### Red Flags:
- Absence of formal compliance program
- Medical directorship payments without time tracking
- History of regulatory violations or corrective action plans
- Upcoding patterns in billing audits

---

## 7. HR/STAFFING

### Documents to Request:
- Employee census with compensation details
- Physician employment agreements
- Employee handbook and benefit summaries
- Turnover analysis by role
- Workers' compensation claims history
- Pending HR litigation or complaints

### Critical Questions:
- What is the staff turnover rate, especially clinical staff?
- Are key physicians nearing retirement?
- Do employment agreements contain appropriate protections?
- Is compensation at market levels?

### Red Flags:
- High turnover (>20% annually) for clinical staff
- Key person dependencies without succession planning
- Missing or unenforceable non-compete agreements
- Pending employment litigation

---

## 8. REAL ESTATE

### Documents to Request:
- All lease agreements with amendments
- Property deeds and title reports (if owned)
- Rent rolls and CAM reconciliations
- ADA compliance documentation
- Environmental assessments
- Deferred maintenance logs

### Critical Questions:
- Are leases assignable to new ownership?
- Is the location convenient for dialysis patients?
- Are there adequate parking and accessibility features?
- What are the renewal options and rent escalation terms?

### Red Flags:
- Leases expiring within 24 months without options
- Personal guarantees that cannot be released
- Above-market rental rates
- Deferred maintenance or ADA compliance issues

---

## 9. IT SYSTEMS

### Documents to Request:
- EHR/PM system contracts and licenses
- Hardware/software inventory with ages
- Cybersecurity assessment results
- Business Associate Agreements (BAAs)
- Disaster recovery and backup procedures
- Integration documentation (labs, hospitals, dialysis)

### Critical Questions:
- Is the EHR specialized for nephrology workflows?
- Are systems cloud-based or on-premise?
- What is the history of breaches or downtime?
- Will current contracts transfer to new ownership?

### Red Flags:
- Outdated or unsupported systems
- History of ransomware or data breaches
- Poor integration requiring manual data entry
- Lack of documented disaster recovery plans

---

## 10. PATIENT BASE

### Documents to Request:
- Patient demographics and geographic analysis
- CKD stage distribution (3/4/5 vs. ESRD)
- Referral source analysis with trends
- Patient retention and attrition reports
- New patient acquisition trends
- Home vs. in-center dialysis mix

### Critical Questions:
- What is the active patient panel size and growth rate?
- Who are the top 10 referral sources?
- What percentage of patients are from each CKD stage?
- Is the patient base aging without replacement?

### Red Flags:
- Declining patient volumes or high attrition (>15%)
- >50% of referrals from single source
- Aging patient demographic without CKD pipeline
- Limited geographic reach or market penetration

---

## 11. REPUTATION

### Documents to Request:
- Online review analysis (Google, Healthgrades, Vitals)
- Patient satisfaction scores (CG-CAHPS)
- Quality Incentive Program (QIP) scores
- Referral partner feedback
- Marketing materials and community involvement

### Critical Questions:
- How does the practice rank against local competitors?
- Have any major referral sources been lost recently?
- What is the Net Promoter Score?
- Are there any negative media coverage issues?

### Red Flags:
- Consistently poor online reviews (<4 stars)
- Lost key referral relationships
- Poor dialysis center quality scores
- Negative press or social media presence

---

## 12. INSURANCE

### Documents to Request:
- Professional liability policies and claims history (5 years)
- Tail coverage quotes and provisions
- General liability, cyber, and property coverage
- Directors & Officers insurance
- Workers' compensation experience mod
- Loss runs for all policies

### Critical Questions:
- Is malpractice coverage claims-made or occurrence?
- Who is responsible for tail coverage costs?
- What is the claims frequency and severity?
- Are there adequate cyber liability limits?

### Red Flags:
- Multiple malpractice claims or large settlements
- High tail coverage costs (indicating risk)
- Coverage gaps (especially cyber liability)
- Rising premiums indicating underwriting concerns

## KEY CONSIDERATIONS FOR NEPHROLOGY ACQUISITIONS

### Nephrology-Specific Risks:

1. **Regulatory Complexity**: Focus heavily on Stark/Anti-Kickback compliance, especially regarding dialysis center relationships and medical directorships

2. **Revenue Concentration**: Assess dependency on dialysis rounds and ESRD patients

3. **Clinical Integration**: Evaluate relationships with dialysis centers and hospitals

4. **Succession Planning**: Many nephrology practices have aging physicians without clear succession

5. **Technology Requirements**: Ensure EHR can handle complex nephrology workflows and dialysis integration

### Priority Areas by Deal Phase:

| Phase | Focus Areas |
|-------|-------------|
| **LOI Stage** | Financial, Payer Contracts, Key Person Risk |
| **Early DD** | Compliance, Corporate Structure, Litigation |
| **Deep DD** | Clinical Ops, HR, IT Systems, Patient Base |
| **Pre-Close** | Insurance, Real Estate, Final Verifications |
