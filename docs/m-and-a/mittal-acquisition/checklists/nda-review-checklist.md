# M&A NDA REVIEW CHECKLIST
## The Kidney Experts, PLLC - Acquisition of Mittal Kidney & Dialysis PLLC

**Deal Context:**
- **Buyer:** The Kidney Experts, PLLC (TKE) - Jackson, TN
- **Target:** Mittal Kidney & Dialysis PLLC - Memphis/Cordova, TN  
- **Seller's Rep:** Jud Cannon, CPA - Canon Wright Blount
- **Deal Size:** ~$10M
- **NDA Direction:** FROM Seller's Rep TO Buyer (likely seller-favorable)

**Important Note:** As this NDA is drafted by the seller's CPA firm, expect aggressive terms protecting their client. Every provision should be reviewed with a buyer's lens.

---

## 1. STANDARD TERMS TO VERIFY

| Clause/Term | Ideal Language | Red Flag Language | Negotiation Approach |
|------------|----------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Definition of Confidential Information** | "Non-public information marked 'Confidential' or identified as such within 10 days if oral" | "All information disclosed regardless of marking" | Insist on marking requirement; include standard exclusions (public domain, prior knowledge, independently developed) |
| **Permitted Use** | "Solely for evaluating potential acquisition of Target by Buyer" | Vague purposes or multiple uses permitted | Narrow to single purpose - acquisition evaluation only |
| **Permitted Recipients** | "Buyer's directors, officers, employees, and Professional Advisors (legal, accounting, financing sources) on need-to-know basis" | "Only employees named in writing" or requiring prior consent | Expand to include all necessary advisors and potential lenders |
| **Standard of Care** | "Same degree of care as own confidential information, but no less than reasonable care" | "Highest degree of care" or strict liability | Push for reasonable care standard |
| **Exclusions from CI** | Full list: (1) public domain, (2) already known, (3) independently developed, (4) lawfully received from third party, (5) required by law, (6) de-identified PHI | No exclusions or limited exclusions | Insert complete standard exclusions per ABA model |

---

## 2. RED FLAGS / DEAL BREAKERS

| Clause/Term | What Makes it a Deal Breaker | Required Action |
|------------|------------------------------|-----------------|
| **Non-Circumvention/Broker Fees** | Any clause making TKE liable for Cannon Wright Blount's fees | **DELETE ENTIRELY** - TKE did not engage the CPA firm |
| **Non-Solicitation of Patients** | Prohibiting TKE from treating Mittal patients who seek care | **DELETE** - Violates patient choice in healthcare |
| **Standstill Provisions** | Restrictions on TKE acquiring other nephrology practices | **DELETE** - These belong in LOI, not initial NDA |
| **Admission of Accuracy** | "TKE acknowledges information is accurate and complete" | **DELETE** - Eliminates due diligence rights |
| **One-Way Indemnification** | TKE indemnifies Seller for any breach without reciprocity | **MUTUALIZE** or limit to gross negligence/willful misconduct |

---

## 3. NEGOTIATION POINTS

| Clause/Term | Current Position | Preferred Position | Compromise Position |
|------------|------------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Non-Solicitation of Employees** | Broad 24-month restriction | No restriction | 12 months for direct solicitation only (general postings allowed) |
| **Term/Duration** | 5+ years | 18-24 months | 2-3 years maximum |
| **Governing Law/Venue** | Seller's choice | Tennessee law, Madison County (Jackson) | Tennessee law, neutral venue (Nashville) |
| **Mutual vs One-Way** | One-way (protects Seller only) | Fully mutual | Accept one-way if TKE shares no data initially |
| **Attorney Fees** | Buyer pays all costs | Each party bears own | Prevailing party standard |

---

## 4. BUYER-PROTECTIVE PROVISIONS TO REQUEST

| Missing Provision | Required Language | Why Critical |
|------------------|-------------------|--------------|
| **No Obligation to Proceed** | "Nothing herein obligates Buyer to proceed with any transaction" | Prevents implied commitment |
| **No Warranty on Information** | "Seller makes no representation as to accuracy or completeness" | Protects against reliance claims |
| **Residual Knowledge** | "Nothing prevents use of Residual Knowledge retained in unaided memory" | Protects clinical/operational learning |
| **Return/Destruction Flexibility** | "Upon request, destroy OR return at Buyer's option, except for legal/IT archival" | Acknowledges IT backup realities |

---

## 5. HEALTHCARE-SPECIFIC CONCERNS

| Issue | Required Provision | Implementation |
|-------|-------------------|----------------|
| **HIPAA Compliance** | "All PHI handled per HIPAA; no PHI shared without BAA" | Defer BAA until post-LOI deep diligence |
| **De-Identified Data** | "Initial data shall be de-identified per HIPAA standards" | Request aggregate data only at NDA stage |
| **Clinical Protocols** | "General medical knowledge not restricted" | Include in Residual Knowledge clause |
| **Regulatory Compliance** | "May disclose to healthcare regulatory advisors" | Carve out from confidentiality |
| **Whistleblower Protection** | "Nothing prohibits reporting fraud or patient safety concerns" | Add explicit safe harbor |

**Critical:** An NDA is NOT a Business Associate Agreement (BAA). If any PHI will be shared, a separate BAA must be executed before any patient data is viewed.

## 7. RESIDUAL KNOWLEDGE CLAUSE

**Essential Language:**
```
"Notwithstanding anything herein, Buyer may use Residual Knowledge, defined as 
information retained in the unaided memories of Buyer's personnel having had 
authorized access to Confidential Information. Residual Knowledge shall include 
general ideas, concepts, know-how, techniques, and skills, but exclude specific 
patient information, detailed financial data, or deliberately memorized information."
```

**Healthcare Addition:** "Including general medical and clinical practices, operational workflows, and business practices common in nephrology"

**Why This Matters:** If TKE learns that Mittal has a highly efficient dialysis supply inventory system or billing workflow, TKE should be able to implement similar improvements even if the deal fails.

---

## 8. RETURN/DESTRUCTION OF INFORMATION

| Requirement | Acceptable Terms | Unacceptable Terms |
|------------|------------------|-------------------|
| **Trigger** | "Upon written request or deal termination" | "Immediate automatic destruction" |
| **Method** | "Destroy or return at Buyer's option" | "Must return all copies" |
| **Exceptions** | "Except: legal holds, regulatory requirements, IT backups, internal analyses" | "No exceptions" |
| **Certification** | "Officer certification within 30 days of request" | "All employees must certify" |

**IT Reality:** Deleting specific emails from server backups is technically impossible for most IT systems. The NDA must acknowledge this reality.
