# Pre-NDA Conversation Guide: Mittal Kidney & Dialysis Acquisition
## Call with Jud Cannon, CPA - Canon Wright Blount

**Prepared for:** Dr. Shree Mulay, The Kidney Experts, PLLC
**Purpose:** Pre-NDA discovery call to assess deal viability
**Target Duration:** 20-30 minutes

---

## Opening & Rapport Building

**Script:** 
> "Hi Jud, this is Dr. Shree Mulay from The Kidney Experts in Jackson. Thanks for taking my call. We're a growing nephrology group with 2 MDs and 5 APPs across 4 locations, and we're very familiar with the Memphis market. I've always respected the work Canon Wright Blount does in the healthcare space. We see strong strategic value in what Dr. Mittal has built and would like to understand if there's a potential fit."

**Key Context to Keep in Mind:**
- You're a smaller buyer (~$4.5M revenue) looking at a larger target ($10-12.5M ask)
- The DaVita Medical Director contract is a critical risk that must be probed carefully
- This is pre-NDA, so keep questions high-level and non-confidential

---

## 1. DEAL PROCESS & TIMELINE

| Question | Purpose | Red Flag Answer | Follow-up |
|----------|---------|----------------|-----------|
| "Can you walk me through how the sale process is being run? Is this a targeted outreach or broader auction?" | Gauge competition and urgency | "We have 5+ PE firms in the data room" or "We need to close in 30 days" | "What's driving the timeline?" / "How do regional buyers like us compare to other interested parties?" |
| "What's your ideal timeline from LOI through closing?" | Assess if realistic and aligned | Unrealistic rush (<60 days) or dragging (6+ months marketed) | "Are there specific events driving that timeline?" |
| "Are you working with an investment banker or handling this directly?" | Understand process sophistication | No clear process or structure | "What information will be available post-NDA?" |

---

## 2. SELLER MOTIVATION

| Question | Purpose | Red Flag Answer | Follow-up |
|----------|---------|----------------|-----------|
| "What's the primary driver for Dr. Mittal and his partners looking to sell at this time?" | Understand true motivation | "Just exploring options" or hints at partner disputes | "Is this primarily about retirement planning or seeking administrative relief?" |
| "What does success look like for the sellers post-transaction?" | Identify key decision factors | "100% financial maximization only" | "How important is ensuring continuity of care for patients?" |
| "Are all three physicians aligned on the decision to sell?" | Check for internal unity | "We haven't discussed with all partners yet" | "What are their individual visions post-sale?" |

---

## 3. BUSINESS OVERVIEW

| Question | Purpose | Red Flag Answer | Follow-up |
|----------|---------|----------------|-----------|
| "Can you give me a high-level overview of the revenue split between clinic visits, dialysis rounding, and medical directorships?" | Validate revenue mix without confidential data | "Majority from one administrative contract" | "How has that mix trended over the last few years?" |
| "Are both locations (Memphis/Cordova and Southaven) included, and do they operate under one entity?" | Understand acquisition complexity | "Multiple LLCs with complex structure" | "Would sellers prefer asset or stock purchase?" |
| "What's the approximate patient panel size and payer mix?" | Assess operational scale | Unable to provide basic metrics | "Is it typical Medicare-heavy for the market?" |

---

## 4. PHYSICIAN TRANSITION

| Question | Purpose | Red Flag Answer | Follow-up |
|----------|---------|----------------|-----------|
| "What are Dr. Mittal's plans post-closing? Is he looking for immediate retirement or a transition period?" | Assess patient retention risk | "He wants to exit completely within 3 months" | "Would he consider a 1-2 year transition or consulting arrangement?" |
| "What about Drs. Goel and Shukoor - are they planning to stay long-term?" | Evaluate practice continuity | "They're not aware of the sale" or "No employment agreements" | "Do they have non-competes or retention incentives?" |
| "How stable is the APP team?" | Ensure operational continuity | High turnover mentioned | "What would help retain them post-acquisition?" |

---

## 5. KEY RELATIONSHIPS (CRITICAL SECTION - THE DAVITA QUESTION)

**Note:** This is the most important section. The DaVita Medical Director contract transferability is the #1 risk in this deal.

| Question | Purpose | Red Flag Answer | Follow-up |
|----------|---------|----------------|-----------|
| "I understand there are relationships with dialysis providers like DaVita. Are there any Medical Director contracts, and in your experience, how have they handled ownership transitions?" | **Probe the critical risk without being too direct** | "Haven't reviewed transferability" or "DaVita has termination rights" | "Are these contracts held by the practice or individual physicians?" |
| "What portion of practice revenue or EBITDA comes from medical directorships versus clinical care?" | **Quantify the risk** | ">50% from director fees" | "Has there been any communication with DaVita about succession planning?" |
| "Are there any other key hospital or facility relationships we should know about?" | Identify other dependencies | Heavy concentration with 1-2 sources | "Any exclusive arrangements or contracts coming up for renewal?" |

**Why This Matters:**
- If DaVita contracts are non-transferable, the valuation drops by 30-50%
- If they're held by Dr. Mittal personally (not the practice LLC), they may not transfer at all
- DaVita has a history of renegotiating or terminating contracts upon ownership change

---

## 6. DEAL STRUCTURE PREFERENCES

| Question | Purpose | Red Flag Answer | Follow-up |
|----------|---------|----------------|-----------|
| "Given our respective sizes, are the sellers open to creative structures like earn-outs, seller notes, or equity rollover?" | Test flexibility for financing | "100% cash at closing only" | "What percentage would they want upfront versus deferred?" |
| "Any specific tax or structure preferences?" | Align early on mechanics | Rigid requirements that harm buyer | "Open to structures that benefit both parties?" |

**Context:** TKE ($4.5M revenue) acquiring a $10M+ target will require creative financing. If seller demands 100% cash, this may not be feasible.

---

## 7. VALUATION BASIS

| Question | Purpose | Red Flag Answer | Follow-up |
|----------|---------|----------------|-----------|
| "The $10-12.5M range implies about 6-8x EBITDA. Can you share how you arrived at that valuation?" | Validate the multiple | "That's just what Dr. Mittal wants" or no methodology | "Is that based on normalized EBITDA with market-rate physician comp?" |
| "What adjustments or add-backs are included in the $1.6M EBITDA calculation?" | Understand if EBITDA is real | Adding back full physician salaries | "What would pro-forma EBITDA look like with a replacement physician?" |

**The "Multiple Trap" to Avoid:**
- 6-8x is high for a private practice acquisition by another private practice
- Make sure EBITDA is calculated AFTER paying physicians fair market salaries
- If they're adding back Dr. Mittal's entire salary as an "add-back," the real EBITDA is much lower

---

## 8. RED FLAG PROBING

| Question | Purpose | Red Flag Answer | Follow-up |
|----------|---------|----------------|-----------|
| "Are there any compliance matters, audits, or legal issues we should be aware of?" | Surface deal killers early | Any mention of investigations or audits | "Has this been fully resolved?" |
| "Anything that's come up with other buyers that we should discuss upfront?" | Learn from others' due diligence | Multiple failed LOIs | "What were the main concerns?" |
| "What do you see as the biggest challenge for a buyer?" | Get honest assessment | Mentions contract assignments or physician retention | "How do you suggest addressing that?" |

The medical directorship relationships will obviously be something we'll want to understand better once we're under NDA. What's the best next step - should we execute an NDA and review initial materials?"

## Key Listening Points & Deal Killers to Watch For

| Warning Sign | Implication | Action |
|--------------|-------------|--------|
| ⚠️ DaVita Contract Non-Transferable | If >30% of EBITDA depends on this, valuation drops significantly | May need to walk away or restructure as earnout |
| ⚠️ Dr. Mittal's Immediate Exit | Major patient attrition risk | Require 18-24 month transition commitment |
| ⚠️ Junior Physicians Not Committed | Practice has no terminal value without Goel/Shukoor | Require retention agreements as condition |
| ⚠️ Inflated EBITDA | Using owner earnings vs. true EBITDA | Demand normalized financials with market comp |
| ⚠️ 100% Cash Requirement | May be impossible for TKE to finance | Walk away or find equity partner |
| ⚠️ Multiple Failed LOIs | Something fundamentally wrong | Understand why before investing time |
