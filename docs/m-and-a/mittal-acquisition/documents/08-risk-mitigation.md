## 1. Healthcare Regulatory Compliance

### 1.1 Stark Law (Physician Self-Referral)

**What It Is**: Federal law prohibiting physicians from referring Medicare/Medicaid patients for designated health services to entities with which they have a financial relationship.

**Risk Areas in This Deal**:
- Dr. Mittal's DaVita Medical Director agreements
- Any lab, imaging, or DME referral arrangements
- Compensation arrangements with employed physicians

**Due Diligence Requirements**:
- [ ] Obtain all physician compensation agreements
- [ ] Review Medical Director agreements for FMV documentation
- [ ] Verify written agreements pre-date referrals
- [ ] Confirm compensation is not volume-based
- [ ] Review space/equipment lease arrangements

**Mitigation Actions**:
1. Require FMV opinions for all Medical Director agreements
2. Escrow funds for potential Stark exposure (recommend 5% of purchase price)
3. Obtain seller representations and warranties regarding compliance
4. Ensure all arrangements fit within Stark exceptions (employment, personal services, etc.)

### 1.2 Anti-Kickback Statute (AKS)

**What It Is**: Criminal statute prohibiting payment or receipt of anything of value to induce referrals of federal healthcare program patients.

**Risk Areas in This Deal**:
- DaVita Medical Director compensation
- NSight RPM partnership revenue share
- Any marketing or consulting arrangements
- Dialysis facility relationships

**Red Flags to Investigate**:
- [ ] Compensation tied to referral volume
- [ ] Below-market leases from referral sources
- [ ] Free services provided to referral sources
- [ ] Exclusive referral arrangements

**Mitigation Actions**:
1. Verify all arrangements qualify for AKS safe harbors
2. Require OIG Advisory Opinion if arrangements are questionable
3. Include specific AKS representations in purchase agreement
4. Conduct interviews with compliance officer (if exists)

### 1.3 False Claims Act (FCA)

**Risk Areas**:
- Billing for services not rendered
- Upcoding E&M or dialysis services
- Billing for non-covered services
- Improper modifier usage

**Due Diligence Requirements**:
- [ ] Conduct billing audit (sample 100+ claims)
- [ ] Review Medicare RAC/MAC audit history
- [ ] Check for overpayment refunds in past 5 years
- [ ] Verify coding credentials of billing staff

**Recommended**: Engage healthcare billing compliance consultant for pre-close audit (~$15,000-25,000)

## 2. Corporate Practice of Medicine (CPOM)

### 2.1 Tennessee CPOM Rules

**Statute**: Tennessee prohibits corporations from practicing medicine. Physician practices must be owned by licensed physicians.

**Structure Requirements**:
- TKE, PLLC is physician-owned (Dr. Shree Mulay, Dr. Anna Lee-Mulay) ✓
- Acquiring entity must be physician-owned PLLC
- Non-compete enforcement requires physician ownership

**Recommended Structure**:
```
TKE Holdings, LLC (if needed for banking)
    │
    └── The Kidney Experts, PLLC (TN)
            │
            ├── Acquired: Mittal Kidney & Dialysis clinical operations
            └── Memphis/Southaven locations
```

**Action Items**:
- [ ] Confirm TKE's PLLC structure is current with TN Secretary of State
- [ ] Verify physician ownership percentages meet CPOM requirements
- [ ] Review any Management Services Organization (MSO) arrangements

### 2.2 Mississippi CPOM Rules (Southaven Location)

**Statute**: Mississippi has similar CPOM restrictions. Foreign PLLCs can operate but must register.

**Requirements**:
- [ ] Register TKE as foreign PLLC in Mississippi
- [ ] Obtain Mississippi medical practice authorization
- [ ] Verify compliance with MS Board of Medical Licensure

**Timeline**: Allow 30-45 days for Mississippi registration

## 4. Provider Retention & Non-Compete Risk

### 4.1 Physician Non-Compete Analysis

**Tennessee Non-Compete Law**:
- Enforceable if reasonable in scope, duration, geography
- Typical: 2 years, 15-25 mile radius
- Must have consideration (employment, sale proceeds)

- Must be reasonable and necessary to protect business interests

## 6. Payor Contract Assignment

### 6.1 Medicare/Medicaid

**Medicare**:
- Requires CMS-855B enrollment update
- 30-60 day processing time
- Continue billing under existing NPI during transition

**TennCare (Tennessee Medicaid)**:
- Requires new provider enrollment or ownership change notification
- 45-60 day processing typical
- May require site visit for new locations

**Mississippi Medicaid**:
- Separate enrollment required for Southaven
- 60-90 day processing
- Verify Southaven is currently enrolled

### 6.2 Commercial Payors

**Assignment Analysis**:
| Payor | Assignment Clause | Risk Level |
|-------|-------------------|------------|
| BlueCross BlueShield TN | Usually allows with notice | Low |
| Cigna | Typically requires consent | Medium |
| United Healthcare | May require re-credentialing | Medium |
| Humana | Usually allows assignment | Low |
| Aetna | Consent typically required | Medium |

**Mitigation Strategy**:
1. Identify all payor contracts during due diligence
2. Review assignment clauses in each contract
3. Submit assignment notices 60 days before close
4. Prepare for re-credentialing if required (90-120 days)

### 6.3 Fee Schedule Risk

**Concern**: Some payors may use assignment as opportunity to renegotiate rates downward.

**Protection**:
- Seller warranty that fee schedules disclosed are current
- Escrow holdback if rates decrease within 12 months
- Require seller assistance with payor negotiations
