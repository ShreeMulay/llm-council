## Executive Summary

### Integration Philosophy

**Principle**: "Day 1 Continuity, Day 90 Optimization"

The primary goal of integration is to maintain uninterrupted patient care while systematically aligning the acquired practice with TKE's operational standards. This is NOT a turnaround—it's a merger of two successful practices.

### Critical Success Factors

| Factor | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Patient Retention | >95% at 12 months | Active patient count |
| Physician Retention | 100% at 12 months | Employment status |
| Staff Retention | >85% at 12 months | Headcount |
| Revenue Continuity | >90% of baseline Month 1-3 | Monthly collections |
| No Compliance Incidents | Zero | Regulatory events |

**Decision Options**:

| Option | Pros | Cons | Cost |
|--------|------|------|------|
| **A: Maintain Both** | No migration risk | Dual systems, inefficiency | $0 upfront, ongoing cost |
| **B: Migrate Mittal to CKD EHR** | Unified system | Migration risk, training | $75-150K |

**Recommendation**: Option A for first 6 months, then evaluate Option B

## 7. Integration Risk Management

### 7.1 Key Integration Risks

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|------------|
| Physician departure | Medium | High | Retention packages, equity path |
| Patient attrition | Low | High | Communication, continuity |
| Staff turnover | Medium | Medium | Competitive comp, culture |
| EHR migration failure | Medium | High | Phased approach, parallel ops |
| DaVita contract loss | Medium | High | Earnout protection, transition |
| Revenue decline | Low | Medium | Close monitoring, quick action |
| Cultural clash | Medium | Medium | Integration events, communication |

### 7.2 Early Warning Indicators

| Indicator | Threshold | Response |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| Weekly patient no-shows | >15% increase | Patient outreach campaign |
| Provider productivity | <80% of baseline | Investigate root cause |
| Staff turnover | >2 departures/month | Compensation review, exit interviews |
| Claim denials | >15% rate | Coding audit, payor follow-up |
| Patient complaints | >3/week | Service recovery protocol |
| Physician satisfaction | <3.5/5.0 rating | Immediate leadership meeting |

### 7.3 Contingency Plans

**If Physician Leaves**:
1. Immediate: Redistribute patients to remaining physicians
2. Short-term: Engage locum tenens (budget $5K/day)
3. Medium-term: Recruit replacement (3-6 month process)
4. Impact: Activate earnout escrow if applicable

**If Revenue Drops >20%**:
1. Immediate: Analyze root cause (attrition vs. billing vs. payor)
2. Short-term: Accelerate marketing, referral outreach
3. Medium-term: Cost reduction (non-clinical staff, vendors)
4. Escalation: Engage seller for transition support

**If EHR Migration Fails**:
1. Immediate: Revert to parallel operation
2. Short-term: Extend legacy system contract
3. Medium-term: Engage new migration partner
4. Learn: Post-mortem analysis, revised timeline
