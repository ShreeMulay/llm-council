## Executive Summary

CMS is aggressively shifting nephrology reimbursement from fee-for-service to value-based models. The Kidney Care Choices (KCC) program represents the largest opportunity for nephrology practices to capture upside from quality improvement and cost reduction.

## 1. Kidney Care Choices (KCC) Program

### 1.1 Program Overview

**What is KCC?**
- CMS Innovation Center model (CMMI)
- Voluntary payment model for nephrologists
- Total cost of care accountability for CKD/ESRD patients
- Shared savings if costs below benchmark
- Risk for costs above benchmark (in some tracks)

**Program Duration**: 2022-2026 (current), likely extended/expanded

**Eligible Participants**:
- Nephrology practices with Medicare patients
- Dialysis facilities (separately)
- Combinations via "Kidney Care Entities"

### 1.2 KCC Options

| Option | Risk Level | Upside Potential | Downside Risk |
|--------|------------|------------------|---------------|
| **Kidney Care First (KCF)** | Lower | Moderate | None |
| **CKCC - Professional** | Moderate | Higher | Limited |
| **CKCC - Global** | Higher | Highest | Significant |

**Recommendation for TKE**: Start with **CKCC Professional** (Year 1), evaluate **Global** (Year 3+)

### 1.3 CKCC Professional Track Details

**Risk/Reward Structure**:
- Upside: Up to 50% of savings below benchmark
- Downside: Repayment of up to 50% of losses (capped)
- Quality adjustments: +/- 10% based on quality scores

**Benchmark Calculation**:
- Based on historical Medicare spending for attributed patients
- Adjusted for patient risk (HCC scores)
- Trend factor applied annually
- Regional adjustments

**Attribution**:
- CKD Stage 4/5 and ESRD patients
- Attributed to nephrology practice based on claims
- Minimum 11 attributed patients required
- "Alignment" with dialysis facilities optional

**Assumptions**:
- Combined TKE + Mittal CKD 4/5 + ESRD patients: 500
- Medicare eligible (FFS): 350 (70%)
- Average Medicare spend per patient: $45,000/year

## 2. Chronic Care Management (CCM)

### 2.1 Program Overview

**What is CCM?**
- Medicare program for patients with 2+ chronic conditions
- Monthly payment for non-face-to-face care management
- Requires patient consent and documented time

**Billing Codes**:
| Code | Time Requirement | 2025 Rate (Est.) |
|------|------------------|------------------|
| 99490 | 20+ minutes/month | ~$62 |
| 99439 | Each additional 20 min | ~$47 |
| 99487 | Complex CCM (60 min) | ~$135 |
| 99489 | Each additional 30 min | ~$75 |

### 2.2 CCM Opportunity for Combined Practice

- TKE CKD patients: 800
- Mittal CKD patients: 700

**Realistic Enrollment**: 40% = 600 patients

- Connected devices transmit data to practice
- Monthly reimbursement for device + monitoring

## 5. Principal Care Management (PCM)

### 5.1 Program Overview

**What is PCM?**
- Similar to CCM but for single high-risk condition
- Appropriate when CKD is the primary complex condition
- 30 minutes/month clinical staff time

**Billing Codes**:
| Code | Description | 2025 Rate (Est.) |
|------|-------------|------------------|
| 99424 | First 30 minutes | ~$83 |
| 99425 | Each additional 30 min | ~$70 |

### 5.2 PCM vs. CCM Selection

| Factor | Choose CCM | Choose PCM |
|--------|------------|------------|
| Multiple complex conditions | ✓ | |
| CKD is primary driver | | ✓ |
| Higher reimbursement need | | ✓ |
| Simpler documentation | | ✓ |

**Strategy**: Use PCM for early CKD patients, CCM for complex multi-morbid patients

### 5.3 PCM Incremental Opportunity

- Patients not suitable for CCM but eligible for PCM: 200
- Enrollment rate (40%): 80 patients
- Monthly revenue: 80 × $83 = $6,640

## 9. Strategic Considerations

### 9.1 Competitive Positioning

**VBC as Differentiation**:
- Small practices struggle with VBC infrastructure
- Combined TKE/Mittal has scale advantage
- Value-based focus strengthens referral relationships
- Positions for MA plan contracting

**Dialysis Organization Dynamics**:
- DaVita and Fresenius pursuing VBC (ESCO, KCC)
- Independent nephrology practices at risk of marginalization
- VBC capability preserves practice independence
- Potential for joint venture or partnership structures

### 9.2 Physician Engagement

**Key Success Factor**: Physician buy-in

**Strategies**:
- Tie VBC performance to compensation (20-30% of bonus)
- Transparent quality dashboards
- Monthly quality huddles
- Celebrate successes

**Quality Compensation Model**:
| Measure | Weight | Threshold | Bonus Pool |
|---------|--------|-----------|------------|
| KCC Savings | 40% | >3% savings | $50,000 |
| CCM Enrollment | 20% | >50% eligible | $25,000 |
| Quality Scores | 25% | >80% composite | $30,000 |
| Patient Satisfaction | 15% | >4.5/5.0 | $20,000 |
| **Total Pool** | | | **$125,000** |
