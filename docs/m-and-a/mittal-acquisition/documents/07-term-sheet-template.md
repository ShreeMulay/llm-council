## 1. TRANSACTION STRUCTURE

### 1.1 Form of Transaction

The proposed transaction (the "Transaction") will be structured as an asset purchase in which Buyer will acquire substantially all of the assets of the Practice, including but not limited to:

- Patient records and medical charts
- Furniture, fixtures, and equipment
- Supplies and inventory
- Trade names, trademarks, and goodwill
- Telephone numbers, website, and domain names
- All contracts assumed by Buyer (payor contracts, vendor agreements, leases)
- All accounts receivable with aging of 90 days or less

### 1.2 Excluded Assets

The following assets will be excluded from the Transaction:

- Cash and cash equivalents
- Accounts receivable with aging greater than 90 days
- Personal assets of the owners
- Corporate minute books and organizational documents
- Tax records required to be retained by Seller

### 1.3 Assumed Liabilities

Buyer will assume only those liabilities specifically identified and agreed to in the Definitive Agreement. All other liabilities, known or unknown, will remain with Seller.

---

## 2. PURCHASE PRICE

### 2.1 Total Purchase Price

The total purchase price for the Practice assets shall be **[TEN MILLION DOLLARS ($10,000,000)]**, subject to adjustments as described below (the "Purchase Price").

### 2.2 Purchase Price Allocation

The Purchase Price shall be paid as follows:

| Component | Amount | Percentage |
|-----------|--------|------------|
| Cash at Closing | $5,000,000 | 50% |
| Seller Promissory Note | $2,500,000 | 25% |
| Earnout Payments | $1,500,000 | 15% |
| Escrow Holdback | $1,000,000 | 10% |
| **Total** | **$10,000,000** | **100%** |

### 2.3 Working Capital Adjustment

The Purchase Price is based on a target working capital amount equal to the average working capital for the three (3) months immediately preceding Closing. Any excess or deficiency in working capital at Closing shall result in a dollar-for-dollar adjustment to the Purchase Price, settled within ninety (90) days post-Closing.

---

## 3. SELLER FINANCING

### 3.1 Promissory Note Terms

Seller agrees to provide financing in the form of a promissory note with the following terms:

| Term | Details |
|------|---------|
| Principal Amount | $2,500,000 |
| Interest Rate | 6.0% per annum, fixed |
| Term | Five (5) years |
| Amortization | Fully amortizing |
| Payment Frequency | Monthly |
| Prepayment | Permitted without penalty |
| Security | Subordinated to SBA financing |
| Standby Period | No payments for first twelve (12) months |

### 3.2 Subordination

The Seller Promissory Note shall be subordinated to any senior lender financing, including SBA 7(a) financing, pursuant to terms acceptable to such senior lender.

---

## 4. EARNOUT PROVISIONS

### 4.1 Earnout Payments

Seller shall be eligible to receive additional earnout payments totaling up to $1,500,000, based on the achievement of the following milestones:

| Milestone | Payment | Trigger Date |
|-----------|---------|--------------|
| DaVita contract assignment approved | $500,000 | 6 months post-Closing |
| Dr. Mittal continued employment (12 months) | $250,000 | 12 months post-Closing |
| Dr. Mittal continued employment (24 months) | $250,000 | 24 months post-Closing |
| Dr. Goel continued employment (18 months) | $250,000 | 18 months post-Closing |
| Practice EBITDA maintained at 90% of baseline | $250,000 | 18 months post-Closing |
| **Total Potential Earnout** | **$1,500,000** | |

### 4.2 Earnout Administration

Earnout calculations shall be based on mutually agreed accounting principles and shall be calculated by Buyer within thirty (30) days following each measurement date. Seller shall have fifteen (15) days to review and dispute any calculation.

### 5.1 Escrow Amount

At Closing, $1,000,000 of the Purchase Price shall be deposited with a mutually agreed escrow agent (the "Escrow Agent").

### 5.2 Escrow Allocation

| Purpose | Amount | Release Trigger |
|---------|--------|-----------------|
| Representation & Warranty Claims | $500,000 | 18 months post-Closing |
| Working Capital Adjustment | $250,000 | 90 days post-Closing |
| General Indemnification | $250,000 | 18 months post-Closing |
| **Total Escrow** | **$1,000,000** | |

### 5.3 Escrow Release

Funds shall be released from escrow to Seller upon the expiration of the applicable period, less any amounts subject to pending claims.

Buyer's obligation to close the Transaction shall be subject to satisfaction of the following conditions:

### 6.1 Due Diligence

Completion of due diligence review satisfactory to Buyer in its sole discretion.

### 6.2 DaVita Contract Assignment

Receipt of written consent from DaVita Healthcare Partners for the assignment of all medical director agreements to Buyer or its designee.

### 6.3 Provider Employment

Execution of employment agreements with Dr. Sanjeev Mittal, Dr. Arvind Goel, Dr. Shehbaz Shukoor, and both nurse practitioners on terms acceptable to Buyer.

### 6.4 Financing

Buyer obtaining financing on terms acceptable to Buyer, including SBA 7(a) financing.

### 6.5 Lease Assignment

Assignment or new lease agreements for both Practice locations (Cordova, TN and Southaven, MS).

### 6.6 Regulatory Approvals

Receipt of all required regulatory approvals, including Medicare and Medicaid provider enrollment changes.

### 6.7 No Material Adverse Change

No material adverse change in the business, operations, financial condition, or prospects of the Practice.

### 6.8 Legal Documentation

Execution of mutually acceptable Definitive Agreement and all ancillary documents.

## 8. EMPLOYMENT AND TRANSITION

### 8.1 Dr. Mittal Employment

Dr. Mittal shall enter into an employment agreement with Buyer for a minimum of twenty-four (24) months post-Closing, with terms to include:

- Competitive compensation at fair market value
- Reasonable call and clinic schedule
- Non-competition covenant (50-mile radius, 2 years post-employment)
- Non-solicitation covenant (patients and employees)
- Consulting availability post-employment

### 8.2 Other Provider Employment

Buyer intends to offer employment to all current providers on terms substantially similar to their current arrangements or as otherwise negotiated.

### 8.3 Staff Retention

Buyer intends to retain substantially all current staff, subject to standard background checks and employment requirements.

---

## 9. DUE DILIGENCE

### 9.1 Due Diligence Period

Buyer shall have sixty (60) days from execution of this LOI to complete due diligence (the "Due Diligence Period").

### 9.2 Access

During the Due Diligence Period, Seller shall provide Buyer and its representatives with reasonable access to:

- Financial records and tax returns
- Contracts and agreements
- Employee records (as permitted by law)
- Patient records (de-identified as appropriate)
- Physical facilities
- Management and key employees
- Third-party advisors (accountants, attorneys)

### 9.3 Confidentiality

All due diligence information shall be subject to the terms of the previously executed Non-Disclosure Agreement.

---

## 10. BINDING PROVISIONS

Notwithstanding the non-binding nature of this LOI, the following provisions shall be binding on the parties:

### 10.1 Exclusivity

For a period of ninety (90) days from the date hereof (the "Exclusivity Period"), Seller shall not, and shall cause its representatives not to:

- Solicit, initiate, or encourage any inquiry or proposal from any third party regarding an acquisition of the Practice
- Participate in any discussions or negotiations with any third party regarding such an acquisition
- Provide any confidential information to any third party for purposes of evaluating an acquisition

### 10.2 Confidentiality

The terms of this LOI and all related discussions shall be treated as confidential and shall not be disclosed to any third party without the prior written consent of the other party, except as required by law or to professional advisors bound by confidentiality obligations.

### 10.3 Expenses

Each party shall bear its own expenses in connection with the Transaction, including fees of attorneys, accountants, and other advisors.

### 10.4 Governing Law

This LOI shall be governed by the laws of the State of Tennessee.
