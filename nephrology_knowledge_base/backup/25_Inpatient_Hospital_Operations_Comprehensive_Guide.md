## 1. Hospital Consultation Service Structure

### Service Models Overview

#### Traditional Consultation Model
**Structure:**
- Nephrology team responds to consulting physician requests
- Recommendations provided to primary team
- Primary team maintains primary responsibility
- Billing through consultation codes (99251-99255)

**Advantages:**
- Clear responsibility delineation
- Lower liability exposure
- Flexible staffing models
- Established reimbursement patterns

**Challenges:**
- Limited control over implementation
- Potential for delayed care
- Communication gaps
- Variable quality of primary team execution

#### Co-Management Model
**Structure:**
- Shared responsibility between hospitalist and nephrology
- Joint patient care planning
- Divided clinical responsibilities
- Collaborative documentation

**Advantages:**
- Improved care coordination
- Better outcome control
- Enhanced communication
- Potential for quality improvement

**Implementation Requirements:**
- Formal co-management agreements
- Defined responsibility matrices
- Shared documentation protocols
- Quality metric tracking

#### Primary Service Model
**Structure:**
- Nephrology team assumes primary care responsibility
- Direct management of all aspects of care
- Complete clinical autonomy
- Primary admission and discharge authority

**Best For:**
- Specialized nephrology units
- Academic medical centers
- High-complexity patient populations
- Research protocols

### Team Composition & Staffing

#### Physician Staffing Models
**Single Provider Coverage:**
- One nephrologist covers all consultations
- Call responsibility 24/7 or with backup
- Patient census: 15-25 concurrent consultations
- Support from APP for routine follow-up

**Two-Provider Team:**
- Day team and night team separation
- Shared call responsibilities
- Higher patient capacity (25-35 concurrent)
- Better work-life balance
- Improved continuity of care

**Academic Service Structure:**
- Attending physician oversight
- Fellow training responsibilities
- Resident participation
- Medical student education
- Research integration

#### Advanced Practice Provider Integration
**NP/PA Roles:**
- Routine consultation follow-up
- Stable patient management
- Discharge planning coordination
- Family communication
- Documentation assistance

**Productivity Metrics:**
- 10-15 patients per day (independent)
- 15-20 patients per day (collaborative)
- 0.5-0.7 FTE per attending physician
- 24/7 coverage for large services

#### Support Staff Requirements
**Nurse Coordinator:**
- Care coordination across units
- Discharge planning facilitation
- Family education
- Outpatient follow-up scheduling
- Quality metric tracking

**Administrative Assistant:**
- Consultation triage

## 3. Clinical Protocols & Order Sets

### Standardized Order Sets

#### Acute Kidney Injury Management Order Set
**Initial Evaluation Orders:**
- Basic metabolic panel (BMP) every 6-12 hours
- Complete blood count (CBC) with differential
- Urinalysis with microscopy
- Urine electrolytes (if indicated)
- Fractional excretion calculations
- Medication review panel

**Monitoring Orders:**
- Strict intake/output measurement
- Daily weights (same scale, same time)
- Vital signs every 4 hours (or more frequently if unstable)
- Fluid balance assessment every shift
- Neurological status monitoring

**Management Interventions:**
- Nephrotoxic medication hold
- Medication dosing adjustment protocol
- Volume optimization algorithm
- Hemodynamic monitoring orders
- Nephrology consultation follow-up

#### Electrolyte Management Protocols

**Hyperkalemia Management Protocol:**
**Mild (K+ 5.0-5.5 mEq/L):**
- Remove high-potassium foods
- Review and hold potassium-containing medications
- Repeat labs in 6-12 hours
- Consider loop diuretic if volume overloaded

**Moderate (K+ 5.6-6.5 mEq/L):**
- All mild measures plus:
- Sodium polystyrene sulfonate or patiromer
- IV insulin and dextrose (if ECG changes)
- Calcium gluconate if ECG changes present
- Consider dialysis if persistent

**Severe (K+ >6.5 mEq/L or ECG changes):**
- All moderate measures plus:
- Emergency dialysis evaluation
- ICU transfer consideration
- Continuous cardiac monitoring
- Repeat labs every 2-4 hours

**Hyponatremia Management Algorithm:**
**Assessment:**
- Volume status evaluation
- Osmolality calculation
- Symptom severity assessment
- Timeline determination

**Treatment Protocols:**
- Hypovolemic: Volume repletion
- Euvolemic: Fluid restriction, consider vasopressin antagonists
- Hypervolemic: Fluid restriction, loop diuretics
- Severe symptoms: Hypertonic saline (3%)
- Monitoring frequency based on severity

### Discharge Planning Protocols

#### CKD Discharge Planning
**Pre-Discharge Assessment:**
- Renal function stabilization

**Discharge Medication Management:**

**Follow-up Coordination:**
- Nephrology clinic appointment (1-2 weeks)

#### Transition to Dialysis Discharge
**Urgent Start Dialysis Discharge:**
- Dialysis unit coordination
- Vascular access education

- Dietary restrictions education
- Medication timing adjustments
- Transportation arrangements

**Outpatient Dialysis Transition:**
- Modality selection confirmation

## 8. Emergency Response & Critical Situations

### Emergency Consultation Protocols

#### Dialysis Emergencies
**Hyperkalemia Emergency Protocol:**
1. **Immediate Assessment (within 5 minutes)**
   - Cardiac monitoring
   - ECG evaluation
   - Vital signs assessment
   - Airway evaluation

2. **Emergency Treatment (within 15 minutes)**
   - Calcium gluconate administration
   - Insulin and dextrose infusion
   - Beta-agonist nebulization
   - Sodium bicarbonate (if acidotic)

3. **Definitive Management (within 1 hour)**
   - Emergency dialysis initiation
   - Continuous cardiac monitoring
   - Serial laboratory testing
   - ICU transfer consideration

**Severe Volume Overload Protocol:**
- Respiratory assessment and support
- Diuretic resistance evaluation
- Ultrafiltration consideration
- Hemodynamic monitoring
- ICU transfer criteria

#### Critical Care Emergencies
**Acute Pulmonary Edema:**
- Immediate non-invasive ventilation
- Nitroglycerin infusion
- Furosemide bolus and infusion
- Hemodynamic monitoring
- Urgent dialysis evaluation

**Severe Metabolic Acidosis:**
- pH and bicarbonate assessment
- Ventilatory support consideration
- Bicarbonate administration criteria
- Emergency dialysis indications
- Underlying cause identification
