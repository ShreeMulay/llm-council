Staffing and workforce planning are critical components of nephrology practice success, directly impacting patient care quality, operational efficiency, and financial performance. ## Staffing Models & Ratios

### Clinical Staffing Models

#### **Physician Staffing**
1. **Outpatient Clinic Model**
   - **General Nephrology**: 1 physician per 2,500-3,000 patients
   - **Subspecialty Focus**: 1 physician per 1,500-2,000 patients
   - **New Patient Capacity**: 8-12 new patients per clinic day
   - **Follow-up Volume**: 20-25 established patients per clinic day

2. **Dialysis Program Model**
   - **Medical Director**: 0.1 FTE per 100 dialysis patients
   - **Attending Coverage**: 1 physician per 150-200 patients
   - **Round Coverage**: 0.05 FTE per 100 patients for daily rounds
   - **On-call Coverage**: 1 physician per 300-400 patients

3. **Hospital Consult Service**
   - **Base Coverage**: 1 physician per 25-30 daily consults
   - **ICU Coverage**: Additional 0.5 FTE per 15 ICU patients
   - **Weekend Coverage**: 1 physician per 50-60 weekend consults
   - **Night Coverage**: 1 physician per 100-120 overnight patients

#### **Advanced Practice Provider Staffing**
1. **Nurse Practitioner Model**
   - **Clinic Support**: 1 NP per 2 physicians (15-18 patients/day)
   - **Dialysis Care**: 1 NP per 100-150 dialysis patients
   - **Hospital Service**: 1 NP per 20-25 consults/day
   - **Transplant Coordination**: 1 NP per 50-75 transplant candidates

2. **Physician Assistant Model**
   - **Surgical Support**: 1 PA per 2 access surgeons
   - **Procedure Assistance**: 1 PA per 500-750 procedures/year
   - **Inpatient Care**: 1 PA per 20-25 patients/day
   - **Emergency Coverage**: 1 PA per 2-3 dialysis units

### Nursing Staffing Models

#### **Dialysis Nursing**
1. **In-Center Hemodialysis**
   - **Patient-to-Nurse Ratio**: 4:1 (chronic stable patients)
   - **High-Acuity Ratio**: 3:1 (complex medical patients)
   - **Charge Nurse**: 1 per 20-24 patients or 3-4 nurses
   - **Float Pool**: 10% of total nursing staff

2. **Home Dialysis**
   - **Home Training Nurse**: 1 per 20-25 home patients
   - **Home Follow-up**: 1 per 40-50 home patients
   - **Peritoneal Dialysis**: 1 nurse per 30-35 PD patients
   - **Home Hemodialysis**: 1 nurse per 15-20 HHD patients

#### **Clinic Nursing**
- **Rooming Nurse**: 1 per 2-3 physicians (10-12 patients/hour)
- **Triage Nurse**: 1 per 3-4 physicians (phone calls, urgent issues)
- **Care Coordinator**: 1 per 500-750 CKD patients
- **Diabetes Educator**: 1 per 1,000 patients with diabetes

### Administrative & Support Staffing

#### **Practice Operations**
1. **Front Desk Operations**
   - **Check-in/Check-out**: 1 per 2,000 patient visits/month
   - **Phone Triage**: 1 per 3 physicians or 15,000 calls/year
   - **Referral Coordination**: 1 per 4-5 physicians
   - **Patient Registration**: 1 per 25-30 new patients/day

2. **Billing & Revenue Cycle**
   - **Biller/Coder**: 1 per 1.5-2 physicians (8,000 charges/month)
   - **Charge Entry**: 1 per 2.5-3 physicians
   - **Accounts Receivable**: 1 per $2-3 million in collections
   - **Denial Management**: 1 per $5-7 million in charges

3. **Clinical Support**
   - **Medical Assistant**: 1 per physician (10-12 patients/hour)
   - **Lab Technician**: 1 per 500-750 point-of-care tests/month
   - **Dietitian**: 1 per 200-250 dialysis patients
   - **Social Worker**: 1 per 150-200 dialysis patients
