## 1. Dialysis Facility Structure & Organization

### Facility Types & Models

#### Freestanding Dialysis Facility
**Characteristics:**
- Independent physical location
- Dedicated to dialysis services only
- Typically owned by LDO, hospital, or physician group
- Specialized staff and equipment
- Focused operational processes

**Advantages:**
- Operational efficiency
- Specialized expertise
- Controlled environment
- Dedicated resources
- Streamlined workflows

- Transportation dependencies
- Staffing specialization requirements

## 2. Facility Design & Physical Environment

### Space Planning & Layout

#### Clinical Area Requirements
**Treatment Floor Layout:**
```
Reception → Waiting Room → Assessment Area → Treatment Floor → Education Rooms → Discharge Area
```

**Space Standards:**
- **Treatment Stations:** 80-100 sq ft per station
- **Aisle Width:** Minimum 8 feet for equipment access
- **Nurse Station:** Central location with visibility of all stations
- **Treatment Room:** 200-250 sq ft for isolation procedures
- **Education Room:** 150-200 sq ft for patient education

**Patient Capacity Planning:**
- **Standard Capacity:** 16-24 stations per shift
- **Expansion Capability:** 20% additional space for growth
- **Isolation Capacity:** Minimum 2 isolation rooms
- **Home Training:** Dedicated space for PD training
- **Storage:** 15-20% of total space for supplies

#### Support Areas Design
**Water Treatment System:**
- Dedicated room with ventilation
- Space for reverse osmosis equipment
- Storage for water treatment chemicals
- Access for maintenance and servicing
- Monitoring equipment location

**Laboratory Area:**
- Phlebotomy station
- Sample processing area
- Refrigerated storage for specimens
- Point-of-care testing equipment
- Biohazard disposal facilities

**Staff Areas:**
- Break room and lounge
- Locker rooms with showers
- Conference room for meetings
- Administrative offices
- Education and training space

## 3. Staffing Models & Human Resources

### Staffing Ratios & Requirements

#### Regulatory Minimum Standards (CMS Conditions for Coverage)
**Nursing Staff:**
- **Chronic Hemodialysis:** 1 RN per 16 patients on treatment
- **Acute/ICU Dialysis:** 1 RN per 2-3 patients
- **Home Dialysis:** 1 RN per 25-30 home patients
- **Charge Nurse:** Additional RN for administrative oversight

**Patient Care Technicians:**
- **PCT-to-Patient Ratio:** 1:3-4 (varies by state)
- **Training Requirements:** CCHT certification or equivalent
- **Supervision:** Direct RN supervision required
- **Scope of Practice:** State-specific limitations

**Professional Staff:**
- **Social Worker:** 1 FTE per 100 patients
- **Dietitian:** 1 FTE per 100 patients
- **Medical Director:** Time commitment based on patient census
- **Biomedical Technician:** 1 FTE per 50-75 machines

- **PCT Ratio:** 1:4 (maximum efficient)
- **Part-Time Staff:** Flexible scheduling
- **Shared Resources:** Multi-facility support staff

## 4. Clinical Operations & Patient Care

### Treatment Scheduling & Workflow

#### Shift Structure Design
**Standard Shift Configuration:**
- **First Shift:** 6:00 AM - 2:30 PM (16 patients)
- **Second Shift:** 2:00 PM - 10:30 PM (16 patients)
- **Third Shift:** 10:00 PM - 6:30 AM (8-12 patients, if applicable)

**Scheduling Optimization:**
- Patient assignment stability
- Continuity of care assignments
- Staff preference integration
- Transportation coordination
- Emergency slot availability

#### Patient Treatment Workflow
**Pre-Treatment Process:**
1. **Patient Arrival and Check-in**
   - Vital signs measurement
   - Weight assessment
   - Pre-dialysis evaluation
   - Access assessment

2. **Machine Setup and Priming**
   - Prescription verification
   - Machine safety checks
   - Dialysate preparation
   - Treatment parameter confirmation

3. **Cannulation and Connection**
   - Access site preparation
   - Cannulation technique
   - Connection verification
   - Initial parameter assessment

**Intradialytic Monitoring:**
- Vital signs every 30 minutes
- Machine parameter monitoring
- Patient comfort assessment
- Access site evaluation
- Complication surveillance

**Post-Treatment Process:**
- Treatment documentation
- Access site care
- Post-dialysis evaluation
- Patient education
- Discharge planning

### Clinical Protocols & Standards

#### Standardized Treatment Protocols
**Hemodialysis Prescription Standards:**
- **Dialysis Dose:** Kt/V ≥ 1.2 (single pool)
- **Treatment Time:** Minimum 3.5 hours
- **Blood Flow Rate:** 250-500 mL/min
- **Dialysate Flow Rate:** 500-800 mL/min
- **Ultrafiltration Goals:** Individualized based on weight

**Individualization Parameters:**
- Patient size and body surface area
- Residual renal function
- Clinical tolerance
- Lifestyle considerations
- Vascular access capabilities

#### Anemia Management Protocol
**Laboratory Monitoring:**
- **Hemoglobin:** Monthly
- **Ferritin:** Quarterly
- **TSAT:** Quarterly
- **CBC:** Monthly
- **Iron Studies:** As clinically indicated

**Treatment Algorithm:**
- **Hgb < 10 g/dL:** ESA dose adjustment consideration
- **Hgb 10-11.5 g/dL:** Target range maintenance
- **Hgb > 11.5 g/dL:** ESA dose reduction
- **Iron Deficiency:** IV iron supplementation protocol

#### Mineral Metabolism Management
**Monitoring Parameters:**
- **Calcium:** Monthly
- **Phosphorus:** Monthly
- **PTH:** Quarterly
- **Alkaline Phosphatase:** Quarterly
- **Vitamin D:** As clinically indicated

**Treatment Targets:**
- **Calcium:** 8.4-10.2 mg/dL
- **Phosphorus:** 3.5-5.5 mg/dL
- **PTH:** 150-600 pg/mL (facility-specific)
